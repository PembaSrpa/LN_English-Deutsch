# A Cunning Man’s Survival Tactic (2)

---

Rustle! Crumple! Lloyd’s hands became busy as they chucked clothes, gloves, boots, and bandanas into his bag, as well as some food and a water container. He never spoke a word while he crammed his bag as though he didn’t have a minute to waste. In a ferocious and feverish manner, he engrossed himself in packing.

“What… are you doing?” asked one of the delegations who came over.

Lloyd looked up to catch a glimpse of the one who called him and replied, “As you can see, I’m packing.” “Packing?” he asked further. “Packing for what?”

“It’s for when I run away tonight,” said Lloyd, pausing for a second.

“Run away…?”

“Yes.”

Rustle, rustle. Crumple, crumple. Lloyd resumed packing after giving his answer. Meanwhile, everyone who was laughing joyfully in the reception hall started to pay attention to Lloyd’s room, which was just adjoined in one corner of the hall.

“Why would you flee in the middle of the night? What do you mean, Lloyd?”

At last, Count Ventura walked over and asked.

Lloyd glanced at the count with a wry smile on his face. “It’s exactly as I said, Your Excellency. What else would I mean by running away?”

“Run away? Run away from here? Tonight?” asked the count again.

“Yes,”

“But why would you do that?”

“That’s because I found a need to do so just now,” answered Lloyd nonchalantly.

Even when he was answering, his hands kept moving. He packed rice oil to use against the desert sun and a blanket to get himself through the freezing night.

“Your Excellency,” Lloyd added, “didn’t you tell everyone earlier that the talk went much better than you expected?”

“I did.”

“That is why.” Lloyd continued to speak. “You said that the sultan admitted his responsibility for the Monster Domino incident, agreed to our demand for compensation, and willfully suggested and signed an agreement to prevent something like this from happening ever again, right?”

“Yes, as we already know,” agreed the count, still not getting Lloyd. “Is that a problem?”

“Yes. But only to me.”

A wry smile appeared on Lloyd again, who was still packing.

“The sultan isn’t a generous man. He’s not a fool anymore. Considering that, he must have an ulterior motive to agree with all our negotiation terms.”

“Wait… Are you saying that what the sultan wants has something to do with you?” asked Count Ventura.

“Yes. I know it’s hard to believe,” dismissed Lloyd.

“No, I believe you. Do elaborate.”

Count Ventura’s eyes grew serious. He now had complete trust in Lloyd’s competence. How else could he not? Without Lloyd, the negotiation would’ve never happened. Their diplomatic success from today would’ve been unimaginable.

And now, these were the words coming from Lloyd, the man behind this stellar accomplishment. The count couldn’t thoughtlessly dismiss his concerns.

“I do not know about the others, but I will believe what you say. Please elaborate for me.”

“Yes, Your Excellency. Thank you for your words.” The wry smile on Lloyd’s face deepened. “This is purely a guess, but I think the sultan is trying to hold me down in this kingdom.”

“Hold you down?”

“That’s right, Your Excellency. The sultan wants me to be his son-in-law.”

“Huh…?” The count seemed unconvinced.

“It is the truth,” emphasized Lloyd, “The sultan already assigned one of his daughters as my guard when I traveled to the Kandahar region.”

“Is that so?” The count lifted his brow.

“It happened, Your Excellency. I said nothing about it because it was a personal matter and something not to disclose so publicly.”

“I see,” the count observed. “What you are saying is that the sultan has agreed to all our conditions because he wanted to make you his son-in-law?”

“I reckon he did.”

“But why?”

“To parade his generosity,” said Lloyd expressionlessly.

“Generosity, huh.”

“All of this,” defended Lloyd, “is my personal thoughts on the matter. The sultan must want to emphasize himself as a virtuous ruler with a big heart. If my guess is right-”

“What would happen?” interrupted the count.

“He will summon me to his palace as soon as the sun rises tomorrow.”

“Is it so he can show off his generosity and virtue to you?”

“Yes. and on top of that, he will try to assign me additional work.”

The count tilted his head for clarification at Lloyd’s words.

“Work? But what if you turn him down?”

“He will pressure me, threatening to overturn the results of today’s negotiation.”

Lloyd’s toneless answer caused the count’s mustache to twitch.

“Overturn the diplomatic agreement over one man? Are you suggesting that the sultan is that covetous of you?” The count’s voice dripped with doubt.

“Well, it is uncomfortable to say it out loud. I know it sounds highly implausible. But the more I think about it, I’m troubled as such an outrageous situation seems to be coming true.”

Now, the wry smile on Lloyd’s face couldn’t get any deeper. He was agitated as the ludicrous scenario in his head was turning into reality. And what was more agitating was that his speculation was aligned with what he saw during his time with the sultan so far.

That sultan was obsessive though he tried to act cool on the outside.

It was the same last night. Lloyd had visited the palace to report to the sultan about the construction of the Qanat, but the latter explicitly expressed his desire to have Lloyd. And now he did what? Unnecessarily decided to be extremely generous during their negotiation?

Perhaps. Maybe. A strong gut feeling that the sultan would summon and pressure Lloyd gripped him hard. He was afraid that the sultan would use their negotiations as blackmail. Take up the job I have for you! Or I’ll break the deal once and for all. The fault is yours if that happens. Lloyd was afraid that the sultan would frame and accuse him of something completely outrageous. Should that happen, what could Lloyd possibly do?

I’ll have no choice but to accept his terms. That will be the only way for the deal and peace to be maintained. I have to prevent my fiefdom from getting involved in a war.

Once he fell into the sultan’s hands…

I’ll be trapped. Would the sultan let me goal after one more project? That’s a funny joke. He would never. I’ll be assigned more work one after another. That’s how he plans to trap me here.

If that happened, it was game over for him. He’d be stuck in this place, getting seduced by the sultan into being recruited. And give it a few years, he would fool himself that living in this place wasn’t so bad. That was how he would end up.

I know this is a baseless hunch, but I always end up being right about these kinds of things.

His past experiences gave him a pretty good intuition, especially from when he lived in a room rental. There were times when he was slightly short on rent because his wage was pushed back. He could only pay a few days after his rent due date. When this happened, Lloyd’s course of action was important.

I had to figure out the manager’s movement patterns. I even had to sense whether the manager was near based on other people’s changes in voice tones or behavior.

The case in point was when he headed to the communal bathroom at the end of the hallway or to the communal kitchen for some ramen. If he failed to catch on to people’s gestures and bumped into the manager, he would be scolded for not paying his rent. Then Lloyd had to plead with him to wait a few more days with the saddest expression he could muster. In short, his life experiences had sharpened his senses, and that tingly sense was on alert right now.

Ugh… I hate it.

Lloyd’s shoulders trembled as he imagined a future where he was swept up in the sultan’s artifice. Quickly shaking off his chills, he spoke.

“So, if the sultan plans on forcing his will on me, then I should respond in the same manner. That is, using the swiftest, simplest, and most efficient method.”

“And that is why you are running away in the middle of the night?”

“Yes. After all, my job here is done anyway. Even if I’m wrong and the sultan doesn’t look for me, I lose nothing from leaving tonight.”

That was equally true. He lost nothing by rushing off. If he was correct, he was outwitting the sultan. If not, he lost nothing.

“As such, Your Excellency, please do me a favor,” asked Lloyd.

Thump. Lloyd swiveled around now that he was done packing. Slinging his bag over his shoulder, he flashed a smile at the count, and the latter found himself nodding in spite of himself.

The next day, Count Ventura was sent to the palace first thing in the morning. Technically speaking, Lloyd was the one summoned, but the count went on Lloyd’s behalf to entertain the sultan.

“Dear Count Ventura, why are you here when I called for someone else?”

The palace was tiled with sleek black marble. The sultan’s voice was mixed with wonder and complaint at the same time. Count Ventura swallowed and thought to himself…

That young man from the Frontera family was correct about his prediction.

The count recalled what Lloyd had said the previous night, how the sultan would summon him to the palace the following morning. It appeared that his prediction was on point.

What a smart fellow he is.

A smile escaped the count, and he remembered what Lloyd requested before rushing off.

“The reason I arrived here in response to your order is simple, Your Majesty,” Count Ventura said.

“Simple? Do enlighten me, the head of the Magentano delegation.”

“Oh, yes, Your Majesty. Lloyd Frontera, the man whom you summoned this morning, is currently traveling back home,” politely announced the count.

“What…?”

The sultan paused. Count Ventura continued to speak.

“To be honest, Your Majesty, to everyone’s misfortune, Lloyd Frontera has contracted a serious disease. Judging that it is due to his prolonged stay in a foreign country, it was only right for him to be sent home ahead of everyone else.”

“You do not dare argue that he left to recover at home, are you?”

“Your Majesty, it is with regret to inform you that he was in urgent need of recuperation at home.”

“Do you possibly think that I will believe your excuse?” huffed the sultan.

“It is regretful to say-”

“Forget it. That is enough,” broke in the sultan as he swayed his hand. A bitter smile appeared on the sultan. 

Lloyd Frontera. Did he see through my intentions?

It was regretful. He had agreed to most of the terms demanded by the Magentano delegation on purpose and meant to use it as a pretext to assign Lloyd to new projects. According to his plan, he was going to use the deal as blackmail if Lloyd rejected his proposal. The sultan meant to tie him down here under duress and make Lloyd his official.

He predicted my intentions simply by seeing the negotiation results. And he rushed off in the middle of the night using his health as an excuse?

What a sly and sneaky person he was! The sultan couldn’t help but smile in bitterness the more he thought about it. At the same time, he raised his white flag and accepted defeat.

I lost. I was defeated. How could I bring back an official of another kingdom when he returned to recover from an illness?

The sultan could summon Lloyd back, but that would be overstepping his authority. He could also put a bounty on the man, but that would be detrimental to his public image. He would become a ruler who prevented a sick man from resting. And what if he went further and threatened to scrap the diplomatic agreement unless this sick man accepted more work? His plans would be made open to the world. Moreover, that would also trouble Lloyd at the same time because taking up his offer would be the same as announcing that Lloyd was against the Magentano kingdom. So, in short, it was risky for both him and Lloyd.

Ha.

Lloyd managed to sabotage his plan by vanishing in thin air during the night. He even made it so no one could gain anything from each other anymore. Lloyd designed a situation wherein no one needed each other.

This is exactly why I cannot stop coveting him.

Dumbfounded, the sultan finally burst into laughter and beckoned Count Ventura to leave. And all alone on the throne, he fell into deep thought about the matter.

I will quietly let you go this time. But do not think you can avoid me forever.

Lloyd Frontera. I am going to make you mine one day. Whether I make you my son-in-law or bestow to you power equal to a prince, I will make use of your abilities to create a foundation that will advance the kingdom for a thousand years. The sultan resolved over and over again. But at this moment, there was something the sultan was completely oblivious of.

Everything became bleak and hazy as he burned with passion to recruit Lloyd. It was only the next day when he realized that his greatest talent had left his presence and set off on a long journey to meet Lloyd.

“What…? Sheherazade? She is gone?”

The girl with the most potential among his children. The daughter was set to become a sword master. The sultan clutched the back of his neck as he took the letter she left for him.
