# The One Who Left and the One Who Stayed (2)

---

Clunk… Clunk… The train leisurely clunked its way along the rail, and the outside scenery passed by at a slow pace. Inside one of the cars, Javier, the knight of the Frontera County, was sitting with his arms folded against his chest. With his head down, he bit his lips. But his gaze wasn’t darted toward the ground.

He was intently staring at the side wall, where the voices of two people were coming from. Voices kept coming. Extremely familiar voices. Both belonged to Lloyd Frontera. And as Javier heard them, he was able to realize something, at last. The question he had asked at times but never mentioned aloud. The curiosity that constantly surged in him but he never spoke aloud of. And even the suspicions that shot through him, which he chose to bottle down. But now, he knew. He had the answer to everything.

The real and the fake.

The victim and the thief.

The self-indulgent and the hardworking.

The degenerate and the constructive.

The despicable and the respectable.

Javier finally understood that they were two different individuals, and no one else was aware of it. He was enlightened at last.

Master Lloyd.

Javier bit his lips until they were white. Yet, he had no idea which Lloyd he just inwardly called. He was truly lost about it. Was it the real Lloyd Frontera who lived as a hooligan? Or the fake Lloyd Frontera who lifted his family from misery with hard work and dedication? Javier was absolutely unsure which one he called for, served, and wished to stay beside. He was filled with anguish.

“…”

Should he slash down on him instead? Make the fake Lloyd pay for his sins? In principle, that seemed to be the right choice. A deathly desire flashed across Javier’s eyes for a split second. But soon enough, it disappeared much more quickly than it appeared. He shook his head hard

No.

That didn’t seem like the right judgment in any case. Was it because he liked the fake version of Lloyd Frontera? No. He wasn’t foolish enough to involve his personal feelings in a matter like this. Currently, he was judging the new situation at hand based on one criterion.

Count Arcos Frontera, the very person Javier had pledged his lifelong allegiance to. Javier considered how his decision would affect and harm his lord. He wondered which choice would be in the best interest for him. That was the only criteria with which he strove to judge this situation. He coldly evaluated the situation, excluding most of his emotions in the process. And the cold nature of his evaluation made his decision all the more painful.

Please forgive me, Master Lloyd.

Javier made up his mind to give up Ghost Frontera and spare the fake Lloyd. It was an inevitable choice. It appeared so as he heard the conversation between the two Lloyds.

It appears the real Lloyd has become a ghost.

He figured that his former young master was as good as dead. It seemed that any attempt to retrieve his body would be useless. In other words, there was no way for the real Lloyd, the hooligan, to be brought back to life. What would happen if he killed the fake Lloyd now as punishment? It would only lead to terrible results.

The situation won’t get any better. No, it’ll get even worse.

Whatever the truth was, whoever had taken over Lloyd’s body, the fake one had been acting as the real one in the most confident and natural manner up to this point. No, he had behaved in an incomparably exemplary manner and saved the family from the pit of destruction. So, killing the fake young master wouldn’t do any good for the family. It would be like losing a giant pillar holding up an entire building. Javier knew that without the fake Lloyd, his lord’s family would be back on the track of destruction.

This is the only way.

It really was the case, realistically. Who was the one that received the trust from Queen Magentano? Technically speaking, it wasn’t Count Frontera, his lord, but Lloyd, the fake. And who had billed the Lacona viscounty for water? And who was maintaining their water system? That would also be the fake young master. And what about Bibeong, who stops monsters from the opposite side of the eastern mountain range from invading his fiefdom? Bibeong, too, was a fantastical creature under the command of the fake Lloyd.

On top of everything, the fiefdom’s ondol and coal mine, the maintenance and repair of the apartment complex for the refugees, and Dragon Solitas in charge of treating the sludge… Fake Lloyd was at the center of countless businesses and projects. What then would happen if the fake Lloyd disappeared? Nothing in the Frontera fiefdom would function properly.

“…”

It was impossible for him not to admit it at this point. Even though the living Lloyd was evidently a sham, he was better than the real one. No, he had become an indispensable figure in the fiefdom. Javier loosened his grip on his sword. Of course, this conclusion didn’t settle all of his concerns and distress. There was still a big concern left in his mind.

Should I then report this to the lord?

The decision he made here wasn’t final, and it definitely wasn’t definitive. It was only a temporary judgment on whether he should kill the fake Lloyd right now. The final decision, however, had to be made by Count Frontera. And so, Javier was on the fence.

It is clearly proper for me to inform the lord right away.

But somehow, he didn’t feel like doing so. Though keeping it under his hat could be a form of betrayal and he was aware of this with absolute certainty, Javier was hesitant about telling the truth. But he couldn’t understand why. The answer soon came to him when he could hear the two Lloyds’ conversing once again.

“So, I will take good care of the count and countess. I’m sorry.”

“Forget it, bastard.”

“And I plan on revealing the truth in the future. That is, about you and me.”

“…”

Once again, Javier bit his lips. And he wondered what the imposter was planning with those words. Why in the world was he speaking that way while making Javier’s heart soften?

Is it right to give him a chance… for now?

Javier felt the sincerity in the fake Lloyd’s remarks. It could be a lie, but Javier wanted to trust the former. He wanted to trust that the fake Lloyd felt genuine guilt toward Count Arcos and Count Marbella and would come clean someday. So, it was only right for Javier to wait.

“…”

Javier wondered to himself if he had lost it. He suppressed the thought that crossed his mind for a second. At least the fake young master didn’t turn a blind eye to the real one. The former gave a helping hand to the real Lloyd, who had become a wandering ghost. The former had the decency to be apologetic and take responsibility for his wrongdoings. So, Javier thought that he should wait and observe. At the very least, it was only right to give a chance for the person directly involved in the situation to reveal the truth.

I am sorry, my lord.

Javier closed his eyes, hoping that his judgment would not harm the Frontera family. He struggled to suppress the confusion and dismay surging inside his heart. Amid his internal conflict, the train dutifully moved forward with a dull sound. It traveled consistently, slowly, and efficiently until it arrived on the platform at the edge of Hell, the final station.

“Whew.”

Lloyd got down from the train, and he massaged his butt. Just like he expected, the train ride was similar to his experience on the army truck, but he couldn’t bring himself to feel nostalgic about it. His butt was quite sore, and even his back ached.

In other words, this is a hellish train ride.

Lloyd smiled wryly, telling himself that this was good enough since the train was constructed for the purpose of transporting souls in the first place. Moreover, the comfort of the ride wasn’t what mattered. There was something significantly more important that required more of his care.

Javier couldn’t be nearby here, right?

Lloyd carefully looked around the moment he got down from the platform, fearing Javier would be near and see the ghost of Frontera. He prudently surveyed the surrounding area, his heart beating much like the time he stole some money from his mother’s purse. After much surveillance, he was put at ease.

Looks like he isn’t here.

Lloyd had instructed Javier to guard this area when he headed to Hell Castle to report to the King of Hell. Could it be that he had gone to the bathroom? Or was he taking a nap after growing tired of waiting? Whatever the reason was, Lloyd felt a rush of relief when Javier was nowhere to be found in the vast, open area of the station.

Now is my chance.

Javier could come back if he dawdled around. The situation would get complicated if Javier saw Ghost Frontera. In the worst-case scenario, he might see Lloyd as a criminal and attack him. Lloyd wiped the sweat off his forehead and hurried Ghost Frontera.

“Hey, hurry up and get out. Quickly,” urged Lloyd.

“Oh, tsk. Stop pushing me.”

“I’m not pushing you. I’m just asking you to move quickly.”

“What, you telling me to reincarnate fast now that your business with me is over?” grumbled Ghost Frontera.

“Come on! It’s not that.”

“What a coward. Are you scared that Javier might find out?”

“Haah. You are right.”

“Fine. Let’s hurry. I never liked that jerk anyway. I don’t want to bump into him.”

“You don’t like him?”

“No.” Ghost Frontera nodded at Lloyd’s question. And in the most obvious tone, Ghost Frontera retorted, “It’s not fair for a human to look like that. I hate him. I feel like life is cheating me whenever I see him, and it pisses me off. I’ve been in a bad mood ever since my father put that bastard on my side to guard me.”

“You too…?” Lloyd gaped as though he just met a comrade.

“Me too,” seconded Ghost Frontera wistfully.

“Dammit.”

For a second, a sense of camaraderie swelled in their hearts as the two berated the good-looking guys. And then they hurried up again toward the Door of Reincarnation that stood at the end of the platform. This location was chosen by design to ensure that the transported souls could enter the door through the platform as soon as they got off the train.

“Whew.”

Could it be that Ghost Frontera was jittery to stand in front of the door? Or was its heart getting the flutters in its stomach as it was about to say goodbye to its wandering life? Ghost Frontera collected its breath for a while before turning Lloyd’s way and chuckling.

“I’ll be going then.”

“Okay.”

They didn’t exchange any special goodbyes or touching words of farewell. No, in the first place, there wasn’t a need for any of it. Ghost Frontera and Lloyd knew this very well. So, they just waved their hands at each other, much like a couple parting ways at night after spending a relaxed day together and knowing they’d meet each other again the following day despite their half-hearted goodbye. Similarly, the two bid each other farewell without making a fuss in an ordinary manner. But before the final step into the Door of Reincarnation, Ghost Frontera turned and looked Lloyd’s way.

“…”

Its lips trembled very softly as though it was whispering. Lloyd couldn’t make out its words. That was it. Ghost Frontera was gone.

“…”

Lloyd gawked at the door. For some reason, his heart was perturbed. Words fell short of describing the feeling. So he just gave a soft sigh and turned, trudging back to the train and hopping onboard. And he collapsed to the ground that was rattling and stared at the area opposite of him.

It was the spot chatty Ghost Frontera had sat a moment ago. But now, only the tarp was fluttering against the wind. And the train started to move, clunking much like a while ago at a slow and consistent pace. The scenery of Hell moved just the same, and all the while, Lloyd didn’t say anything. He remained seated still and thought to himself that Hell wasn’t a good place. The air was hot, and the volcanic ash stung his eyes. Lloyd rubbed his eyes several times with his sleeve. Later, once the long journey to the Hell Castle came to an end, Lloyd got down to be welcomed by someone he was glad to see.

“Eh…? Javier?”

“Did you arrive just now, Master Lloyd.”

“What are you doing here?” asked Lloyd, feeling dazed to find Javier here. He had told the latter to stay at the other station. So what was he doing here?

While Lloyd remained confused, Javier smiled and said, “I came here because I was nervous.”

“Nervous?”

“Yes.”

“About what?”

“About you abandoning me for good,” said Javier, “That is why I ran all the way here until I discovered that you went to check on the train.”

“So, have you been waiting until I got here?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes.”

“Ha… You must have not liked the idea of swimming from the polar region to the Frontera fiefdom.”

“I did not,” Javier said as he nodded. “I was against it, but now that I am seeing you here, I am starting to change my mind.”

“Change your mind? How?”

“That I’d rather avoid seeing your face in any case.”

“…”

“It was a joke.”

“No, you sounded pretty serious.”

“Did you see through me?”

“Probably?” Lloyd scoffed.

Lloyd was relieved. Javier had done something unilaterally, so he couldn’t have seen the ghost of Frontera at the edge of Hell.

The latter had come to escort, which forced Lloyd to feign a forced smile and exchange a silly joke. It was a relief, truly.

“Then let’s go. I have to give the report about the result of the test to the king.”

“Understood.”

Lloyd walked ahead of Javier, his heart slightly lighter than a second ago. Javier started at the back of Lloyd with a meaningful gaze.

“…”

Javier was relieved that Lloyd bought his hasty lie, so he was able to treat him like usual and exchange silly banter with him. He was truly glad that he could turn a blind eye to the eyes of fake Lloyd, which were slightly swollen.

Master Lloyd. May you have a peaceful and happy new life.

Javier prayed from the bottom of his heart. The real and the fake. The victim and the thief. The self-indulgent and the hardworking. The former master and the present master. He wished that both of them would have a peaceful and happy future and that every day left in their lives will be peaceful and warm. The one who left and the one who stayed. Javier prayed in his heart for both of them. And without a word, he followed Lloyd.
