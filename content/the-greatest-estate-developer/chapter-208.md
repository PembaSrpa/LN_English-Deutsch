# A Secret Plan to Stop Forced Labor (3)

---

First love. Much like measles, it was an emotion that blossomed inside everyone at least once in their life. It was also a painful memory for most because they were too clumsy to clutch and reap its final fruits. Lloyd, too, had experienced his first love. Back in his days when stores rented out books, his heart used to pound every time he looked at the part-time worker there. He was confused as to what was happening to him, why his face kept getting red. A clench of dismay rushed through him as he couldn’t make sense of his bodily reactions. But strangely and too much of his dismay, he found himself making for the store on his own all the time, each visit causing the same terrible blush on his face and the maddening increase of heartbeat. Even so, he mysteriously ushered himself to the store, as if drawn by something enchanting, and borrowed piles of books he wouldn’t read. The reason behind his strange behavior was clarified only much later when the girl quit her job.

Tsk.

Lloyd knew neither her phone number nor her name. They never properly spoke. Just like that, his first love at 14 ended in failure. Lloyd pushed aside his old memory and spoke, looking straight into Julian’s eyes.

“You’ll lose Miss Sheherazade if you keep fumbling like this.”

“What…?”

Julian’s eyes widened like a bunny’s.

“I’m not joking,” Lloyd continued. “You do know why she crossed the desert to come here, right?”

“Oh, I do,” Julian replied. “She told me she has something to get from you. That she wants to record your lullaby…”

“What do you think will happen if I give her a recording right now?” Lloyd glanced at him meaningfully.

“Hyung…?”

“Then she will go back to her kingdom, right?”

“Oh, I… guess?” Julian’s voice trembled.

“What are you going to do about it?” echoed Lloyd heavily.

Now, Lloyd could hear the wheels in Julian’s head turning. His eyes shook when he resumed. “Um, hyung, are you really going to do that?”

His gaze seemed to beg, “Please don’t!”

“I don’t know,” Lloyd said in a dry tone. “The decision isn’t mine to make.”

“Then?”

“It’s up to Miss Sheherazade.”

His tone started to sound more nonchalant, as though he knew nothing and didn’t care what decision she made.

“The thing is,” Lloyd continued, “I laid out the choices she had a few days before you arrived. It was either to serve in the Engineering Corps for five years in exchange for the lullaby recording or go back.”

“Then…” Julian mumbled, unsure how to react.

“If she thankfully takes my offer, she will spend about five years here. If not, well, there’s nothing I can do.” Lloyd stressed his last words.

“Th-that’s-”

“You don’t want that to happen, right?” exclaimed Lloyd.

Nod. Julian nodded his head earnestly. Done. I succeeded in coaxing him, Lloyd told himself as he put on a smile that only he would know the reason behind.

“I know, you must hate to see her leave,” cooed Lloyd. “You want to always be with her. But you don’t know what to do about it. Right?”

“Yes…” Julian said, looking up at him with a more desperate gaze. “Hyung, what should I do?”

“What you should do?”

“Yeah.”

“First, I suggest you observe her feelings.”

“Her feelings?”

“Yeah, this matter involves more than just how you feel.”

It was true. You couldn’t just think about your own feelings. It was important to consider if the object of your affection liked you too. So, Julian had to be considerate of the other party and learn about her feelings as carefully as possible.

That’s what I heard. From who? A classmate of mine.

The thing was, Lloyd never had a girlfriend before. He had never had any experience in that field. Though he was sad that was the case, it hardly mattered right now.

It doesn’t matter even if I don’t have any experience in this. What matters now is helping Julian realize his feelings and coaxing him to take action.

He just needed to gently coax him. And then what? Everything would naturally move forward after that.

Sheherazade must be feeling similar to Julian.

Thinking so, Lloyd tapped Julian’s shoulders.

“It’s nothing too difficult,” Lloyd comforted. “Now, you know what you’re feeling. So, stay true to your heart and take action. Be nicer to her, and when you find a chance, how about showing some courage?”

“Showing some courage?” gasped Julian.

“Uh-huh.”

“Do you mean to tell her how I feel?” gasped Julian again.

“Of course.”

Gulp. Julian’s neck bobbed as he swallowed. The mere thought of the act made him nervous. But then, Lloyd saw in a split second the vague light of determination flashing across Julian’s eyes.

That rascal.

Lloyd desperately suppressed his laughter, swallowing hard. He figured he could leave it here with Julian. Now, it was time to gently coax Sheherazade.

“Mwahaha.”

“…”

"Mwahahaha.”

“…”

The next morning, Javier seriously questioned whether his young master was in the right state of mind.

What is happening?

Javier stared at the back of Lloyd’s chestnut-shaped head with a leery gaze. They were taking a morning stroll after eating breakfast, and the man walking ahead of him kept letting out eerie bursts of laughter for some reason.

“Mwahahahaha.”

“…”

At last, unable to hold back any longer, Javier decided to ask. “What are you so happy about?”

Javier’s experiences told him that Lloyd was laughing like that for a reason. Convinced that Lloyd must be brewing up something treacherous, he asked him a question. And just as Javier expected, Lloyd stopped and turned, batting back with a peculiar smile on his face.

“Do I look like I am in your eyes as well?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes.”

“Do I look happy?”

“Yes.”

“You saw it right,” admitted Lloyd. “I am happy.” And he added, “I think my plan will go very well.”

“Your plan?” asked Javier.

“A man and woman with different backgrounds, paths, and purposes in life will finally become one.”

“Could it be,” Javier mused, “that you are referring to Master Julian and Miss Sheherazade?”

“Hm? How did you know that?” Lloyd raised his brows.

“It would be impossible for me not to know,” Javier said matter-of-factly. “There was something special in their gaze toward each other. It is a look I have seen countless times.”

“You? You’ve seen this look countless times?” said Lloyd with a frown.

“Yes.”

“How?”

“It was very similar to the gaze I receive from women before they hand me a love letter,” Javier said expressionlessly.

“…”

“To give the exact number of times I have received this gaze-”

“Don’t count.”

“860-”

“I said don’t count.”

“…”

Lloyd felt a distinct wave of envy surging in his heart each time he met someone handsome. He was jealous about how they enjoyed so many perks just because they were good-looking. But Lloyd, with a bit of struggle, decided to pull himself together.

“Tsk. Anyway, you were right. It looks like the two will confirm their feelings for each other soon,” huffed Lloyd.

“How?”

“What do you mean ‘how’? I pushed them.”

“You, Master Lloyd?” asked Javier in disbelief.

“Uh-huh.”

Lloyd nodded his head. And he recalled what he had done the previous night.

“I went up to Julian,” narrated Lloyd. “I probed him a little to test the water. And just like I predicted, Julian was completely oblivious to his feelings.”

“Is that why you told him?”

“Yeah, and I did the same to Sheherazade,” divulged Lloyd with a smile.

Yes, he did. Right after encouraging Julian, Lloyd visited Sheherazade, who, at first, raised her guard when the former tried to strike up a conversation. Her attitude was nothing surprising. After all, she had been tricked by Lloyd multiple times already ever since they were Ahinsya. And she was unexpectedly coerced with an employment contract after traveling all the way across the desert to come here.

“She seemed determined to not believe anything I say,” Lloyd recalled.

“But how did you-”

“How did I make her come around?” stated Lloyd.

“Yes.”

Putting a big smile, Lloyd replied, “Well, nothing much. I told her that Julian would leave for the palace soon.”

That was true. All Lloyd needed to do was make them feel more anxious to stay together. And if he had solid evidence to back his claim, his words would become much more impactful. That was the very method Lloyd used against Sheherazade.

“I told her the plain truth,” added Lloyd. “I told her that Julian graduated second in his class, and he’ll soon be assigned a position in the palace. And he’s waiting here at home before he gets his assignment.”

Every word that came out of his mouth was true. Julian was set to return to the capital soon, and he’d become a resident of the capital as a palace official. So obviously, there was no reason for him to stay here, which meant he would grow distant from Sheherazade.

“I threw out a crucial fact that would make her turn toward Julian again. You know, people are always like that. They don’t take any action until they realize that there’s a time limit. That’s when they get all nervous and obsessive.”

It really was true. Even the things people took for granted evolved into something valuable when there was a time limit. For example, this could be seen during a 24-hour sale. A pack of ramen that someone normally didn’t eat would become a necessary purchase if it had a 50% discount for the day. That was the case even with products people didn’t plan to buy. The fear of regret made them eventually justify that they’d want the ramen sometime later and that purchasing it on sale was a smart move.

The same goes for cars. In particular, foreign cars. Sometimes, dealers will spread a rumor during their end-of-year sales that a specific model will be out of production starting next year. And a whole pack of people who’ve always wanted to buy the model but hesitated to flood to the dealer because they don’t want to miss their chance to own one. But come next year, the rumor turns out to be nonsense. They sell the same car all year long without a halt.

This sales method, “Now or Never,” stimulated people’s minds, and what Lloyd did to Sheherazade was similar.

“I told her Julian would stay here for a limited time. Now, she’ll start to think that she doesn’t want to lose him. Then this will naturally lead her to wonder why she’s having that thought.”

“Do you mean that she will realize her feelings in the process?” asked Javier.

“Yeah, I like that you’re a fast thinker,” Lloyd uttered, raising one of his brows.

“And I do not like this side of you, Master Lloyd” Javier’s face straightened. “I wish to ask your reason behind pairing them together. Your intentions can’t be pure, can it?”

“Mhm, of course not.”

Lloyd’s lips curved up, but his smile wasn’t innocent of course.

“I am doing this because I don’t want the queen to walk all over me.”

“Excuse me?”

Javier cocked his head to the side, unable to understand what he was talking about. What does pairing together Master Julian and Miss Sheherazade have anything to do with the queen? Javier felt a flood of curiosity surging in him.

“When the two get married,” Lloyd explained with a wry smile, “the queen won’t be able to treat me like a slave. No, even if it doesn’t go the way I want to, at least the grounds for my ultimate goal will be set.”

“May I ask how?”

“Sure thing. Simply put, when Julian and Sheherazade get married, my family will closely be allied with both the Magentano Royalty and the sultan’s kingdom.”

“Is that not treason?” Javier asked seriously.

“Not at all,” noted Lloyd. “Julian isn’t the eldest in the family. He’s the second son, so he won’t inherit the family estate. Thus, there’s nothing wrong with him marrying the princess of a sultan. Actually, it’s something to welcome at this moment in time.”

“What is that?”

“We just signed the peace treaty with the sultan.” There was confidence in Lloyd’s voice. “The sultan unexpectedly accepted his responsibility for the Monster Domino phenomenon. He paid a hefty compensation and even signed a safeguard agreement. As a result, these two arch-nemeses entered a period of peace.”

“Then,” Javier contributed, “if the second son of the Frontera county, which served as the hub of reconstruction after the Monster Domino tragedy, marries the princess of the sultan, who accepted responsibility for the incident, amid this peaceful era…”

“Their marriage will bring a promise of peace for everyone,” Lloyd finished. “For the royals, the public, and everyone else.”

In other words, their marriage would become an icon of peace. Furthermore, the Frontera fiefdom would become the symbol of peace and restoration. Once that happened, Lloyd would be able to two-time the two kingdoms without being accused of treason and under the blessing of everyone.

Plus, I have a useful title with me.

Lloyd recalled the very first title he had earned. Magentano Carry. The effect of the title enabled him to be absolutely free from receiving doubts of treason during the queen’s reign. In other words, as long as he didn’t act like a complete idiot, he wouldn’t be accused of treason.

“So, Queen Magentano won’t express any complaints though she may feel somewhat upset about the marriage. No, she might even give us more favors in fear of us getting closer to the sultan.”

“And that is how… the queen will not be able to use you like a slave…” Javier postulated.

“Yeah,” said Lloyd brightly. “It’ll look like I’d rush right into the arms of the sultan if she tried to do that.”

“…”

“The sultan will welcome me with open arms. It was me, not Julian, the sultan wanted to be his son-in-law, you know,” reminded Lloyd.

“…”

“And that’s how I will gain the upper hand with these two kingdoms. They will be forced to be more careful with us. Obviously, this will also guarantee a peaceful retirement for myself.” Lloyd smiled victoriously.

“Whew. Master Lloyd… You are really…”

“Really what?”

“Sly. Nasty. I have never met a more cunning swindler than you, Master Lloyd.”

“Sure, thank you.”

Lloyd smiled after revealing the idea inside his head. This was the polished plan he had come up with. The marriage between Julian and Sheherazade would be used to prompt the queen and sultan to win his favor. As long as that happened, he could spend the rest of his life in leisure without needing to grovel at anyone or get called by the queen.

But of course, it’ll take a lot of effort for me to maintain that upper hand.

The final goal? To acquire the right to have autonomy over his labor from the queen. That was his ultimate goal.

I can do it.

Inspecting his plan, Lloyd looked at the manor. The window to Julian’s room was tightly shut. Sheherazade’s window was also shut but with a layer of a curtain drawn. Looking from one to another, Lloyd smiled.

Yes, you two. Think about your feelings. Ponder on them. React to the arrow of realization I pierced in each of you.

The arrows had already been released, hitting their target spot on. So, Lloyd was confident. Julian and Sheerazade would react just the way he hoped they would. And indeed, his expectations were not wrong. Something really did happen deep into that night.
