# His Aspiration to Be a Slacker (2)

---

It’s painful and unfair. Just a little more. If only I had a few more days. Then all of my plans would have actualized as planned.

“Alicia… Termina Magentino… You despicable…”

Warlock Targa recalled her despicable face. Her eyes glinted coldly. The knife that pierced his neck was equally fatal and cold. That icy sensation was his final memory before he faced death. It only took one slash to disembody his neck. His head flew up in the air, and left a foul feeling as it rolled around after hitting the ground. In the last few seconds of his remaining life, he had to push aside his emotions and turn to his last resort.

He still remembered it like it was yesterday. Reluctant but absolutely left with no choice, he had to give up his dying flesh and transfer his soul into a giant skeleton. He was enraged, and his whole body still trembled in anger. And so, he resolved to be patient for just a little longer. In a few more months, his revenge would actualize just as he desired.

Once my soul is settled…

Stashed inside the flashy repository, his giant skull minutely clacked as he solidified his dark resolution.

“I recall you have never led a garden construction before, right?”

“Yes, for one reason or another,” replied Lloyd.

Here we go again, Lloyd thought to himself with an embarrassed smile. Viscount Nort flashed a proud smile. “It is all right,” Viscount Nort comforted him. “there is no need to be embarrassed. Your career is just starting now, so this will serve as a good opportunity for you, won’t it?”

“You could not be any more correct,” Lloyd said, “I am counting on you, viscount.”

“Hahaha, is that so? My, I feel like you are using flattery to pawn off your responsibilities on me.”

“Oops, was I too obvious?” Lloyd asked heartily.

“I can see through you, my boy,” cheerfully replied Viscount Nort.

“But I have no other choice. How can I dare compete with you, Viscount Nort, in the field of garden construction?”

“Gosh… Your words are nonsense…”

Viscount Nort flashed a toothy smile, and even though he waved his hands to stop Lloyd, it looked like the viscount was absolutely pleased. This made Lloyd more eager in buttering him up.

“No, no. I mean it. So, I want to make the most out of this opportunity and learn everything I can by observing you execute your design. As you have said, this will be a good experience for me,” stressed Lloyd.

“Ahaha. My boy, I see you are bent on stealing my design technique, aren’t you?” Viscount Nort crossed his arms.

“Isn’t that how everyone improves?” quipped Lloyd.

“Ha, it is truly fascinating. I don’t hate you even when you say those things.”

“No,” Lloyd said as he swayed his hands. “It is all because you are being kind toward me with your endless generosity.”

“Is that so?”

“Yes,”

“Hahaha!”

Viscount Nort continued to cheerfully laugh, and he soon spread wide a bundle of paper on the work table.

“Then will you look at this? I spent some time on the outline of the garden last night.”

“Could it be that you already started with the basic design?” Lloyd asked, incredulous.

“For there is no reason to wait.” Viscount Nort shrugged. “That land, that space where the garden would be put up, seems to plead to me for a beautiful transformation. It wants to reveal its true self to the world.”

“…”

“That is why I enjoy this process although you may disagree. I get overwhelmingly happy through the process of earthing the beauty, balance, and harmony hidden in a neglected piece of land.”

Viscount Nort spoke carefully as he looked at the blueprint. Gazing at his side, Lloyd thought for the first time that this viscount was a charming man.

This person truly loves to design gardens.

The viscount beside Lloyd was different from him, who materialistically accepted and completed jobs for money or another generous form of compensation.

Wow, this means that I can pawn off more work on him now.

I’ve got to toss some more work and praise to him. Then his happiness will probably soar through the roof. And at the same time, I’ll get to slack off. He’s scratching my back, and I’m scratching his. If this isn’t a win-win situation I don’t know what is!

From then onward, Lloyd fawned over Viscount Nort even more shamelessly. And the older man took up the surveying and design with sheer happiness written all over his face. But leaving every bit of the process of the construction to Viscount Nort proved to be impossible about 15 days after the construction took off.

“Whew, this is quite troublesome,” said Viscount Nort in a worrying tone.

“Eh?” Lloyd asked. “Is there something wrong?”

Could it be that Lloyd had mooched off too openly? Had Viscount Nort finally reached the limit of his patience? But when Lloyd carefully asked what it was, to his relief, Viscount Nort was troubled by something else.

“It is because of the waterway construction demanded by Her Majesty.”

“What about it?”

“You see, you remember it too? The Magena River penetrates the capital. Her Majesty instructed us to pull the water from the river and create a waterway in the garden,” Viscount Nort reminded him.

“Yes, she did.”

“And she stressed several times that the waterway must be wide enough for a 50-pax ship to traverse freely and enjoy a view of her sculpture and the dragon skeleton.”

“Yes, I remember it, too,” confirmed Lloyd.

“That is where my trouble comes from. The thing is, hmm, paving the waterway is fine, but…”

Sensing a problem, Lloyd asked, “Is there a problem in the waterway design?”

“The water level difference between the two points, that is, the entry where the river water flows into the garden and the exit where it flows out, are significant.” Viscount Nort scratched his head.

“You must mean that the water current is faster than expected.”

“Exactly. That is what I mean.” Viscount Nort stroked his eyes heavy with fatigue. “The velocity of the moving water is faster than I expected it to be. Of course, we can float a boat on it, but we’ll have to give up on providing a view of the garden at this rate.”

“Hmm,” mused Lloyd, “you are certainly right.”

Lloyd nodded as he looked at the blueprint the viscount was pointing at. Indeed, the water level of the two ends was unexpectedly different.

They almost differ by 82 feet!

So. floating a boat in the waterway would be, to exaggerate a little, like turning a cruise ship into a flume coaster. A beautiful view of the garden? A magnificent sight of the sculpture and dragon skeletons? They wouldn’t even be able to dream of it.

Everyone’s going to be busy getting seasick or distracted from the thrill of a ride equal to kayaking in a rapid stream of the Grand Canyon.

After organizing his thoughts, Lloyd asked, “Then how about extending the length of the waterway?”

That would be the simplest solution. It would be similar to constructing a spiral path along a steep mountain to prevent any accidents from happening. They would gain more control over the velocity of the waterway by expanding its length.

Count Nort shook his head. “It won’t do. I have considered that solution as well.”

“Is there a problem with that method?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes. It is not beautiful.”

“Excuse me?” Lloyd retorted right away.

“The waterway is going to uncontrollably widen. A huge garden like this has a theme for each of its sections. Expanding the waterway would ruin all the themes. So, we cannot use that method.”

“Then how about using a double-leaf miter gate?”

“What…?”

Count Nort paused, unable to process the words he just heard. Double-leaf? Miter gate?

“Actually,” Lloyd explained as though it was nothing. “It is not a complex concept. May I use your pen?” “Okay, here you go.”

“Thank you,” said Lloyd as he took the pen. “To illustrate the basic principle… look here and here. We will shape the waterway to resemble a staircase.”

“Hold on. But what about the ship? How is it going to pass the waterway when it is shaped like a stair?” “That is why we will need a floodgate.”

With a grin on his face, Lloyd drew a rough outline of a floodgate.

“The middle part of the waterway, that is, the end of the stair, is where the floodgate will be installed. It will close off the entrance the ship comes through, just like an aquarium.”

“And next?”

“We can drain the water in the aquarium.”

Lloyd sketched another drawing, this time in the area with a lower water level.

“Once the water drains out of the aquarium, the ship is going to come down along with the water level.” “Yes, indeed.”

“So, when the water level is sufficiently lowered, all we have to do is open the opposing floodgate as well as the one we just opened.”

“Oh…” Viscount Nort’s eyes glimmered. “So the opposing floodgate will become the ‘next flight of stairs’ in the waterway, and lower the water level. Am I right?”

“Yes, that is a precise summary.” Lloyd nodded.

It was actually a simple idea. Build a floodgate, a partition, that would shut the water flow at a particular point in the waterway. Let the ship enter, and close the floodgate, which would make the inside of the floodgate a square aquarium. Then add or drain water to control the water level.

If the ship traveled from a point where the water level was high and heading downward, the water could simply be drained. What about when it was going upward? They could just let the water flow in and balance the water level of both sides of the waterway like so. This way, the floodgate would act as an elevator for the ship, much like the floodgate in Canal Saint Martin in France.

“Of course, we can’t just thoughtlessly build a huge floodgate as it has to be able to bear water pressure. Based on some quick math, I think each floodgate can control about ten feet of water.”

“Then should we install floodgates all throughout the waterway?” asked Viscount Nort.

“Yes,” agreed Lloyd. “That will allow us to maintain the velocity of the current quite well, and let a ship pass through with ease.”

“I agree. Moreover, the passengers admiring the garden on the ship will likely become entranced by the moving floodgate. So, we will simply have to decorate the inside of the floodgate to captivate people’s attention once it opens,” mused the viscount.

“Oh…” gaped Lloyd with admiration. “That is something I haven’t thought about.”

“Really?”

“No, I never did,” Lloyd said. “I really never thought about that. Truly, you are a skilled expert, Viscount Nort." 

"Haha, how humble you are. Hahaha.”

Viscount Nort chuckled aloud, and at the same time, he was amazed. He gazed at Lloyd meaningfully. 

This boy. I knew he was being considerate of me.

Actually, Viscount Nort noticed Lloyd’s careful behavior toward him from the first day they met. He was sure the young fellow was hiding his potential and refraining from brusquely acting as though he knew everything. He seemed to be wary of receiving praise from others and constantly lowered himself. It was the same case just now.

A floodgate, huh? I never thought to use that in the garden. But he carefully picked his words to make sure I didn’t feel embarrassed about it.

What was more, he even credited the idea of decorating the floodgate to Viscount Nort, acting as though he didn’t think so far ahead. Viscount Nort easily noticed that Lloyd was being considerate.

There is no way a man who thought of building a floodgate here didn’t have that idea. I can tell that he is obviously trying to give credit for the idea and this project overall to me.

Why? There was no way of knowing. But Viscount Nort didn’t hate Lloyd for it. Instead, he was proud of this young man and appreciative for refraining from pompously showing off his skills, which were superior to his own, thereby saving his face. At the same time, Viscount Nort felt a rush of competitiveness against Lloyd. 

I should work harder.

Viscount Nort wasn’t an unsightly man who relied on the favors of his juniors in the field. His skills were acquired through years of experience on the job, and his enthusiasm shot through the roof once more.

From that day onward, backed by Viscount Nort’s active guidance and Lloyd’s minimal participation, the construction of the largest garden in the history of the Magentano kingdom took off in full force. The House of Magentano generously supported the garden project and over 5,000 workers were hired. Even the royal magicians rolled up their sleeves, their powerful magic mixing, flipping, and rearranging the soil. Thousands of workers then used their shovels and tools in a fit of sweat. On top of them, Lloyd’s fantastical creatures joined in on the effort.

“Okay, you all. Dig, flip, and remove!”

“Ppo! Bang! Ha! Ggo!”

Ppodong dug the waterway. Bangul ate the dirt pile. Hamang filled the pond after drawing water elsewhere. Ggoming busily moved thousands of trees. And Lloyd even contacted the Frontera fiefdom to bring Bibeong. “Bibeong!”

Throughout the whole process, Bibeong strove hard to show off his tremendous strength and great perseverance to Bangul by participating in the construction with all his might. As the fantastical creature, whose body size reached over 328 feet, joined the project, the construction speed increased to an incredible level.

Seasons changed. The maple trees reddened. The leaves fell. The branches thinned out. White snowflakes covered them. The year ended with its first snowfall.

With the new year, Javier teased Lloyd for getting older, and Lloyd forced a smile as he tried to look cool. Flowers started to bloom, and one day when the early spring came along with the full blossom of magnolia, “Termina Grand Garden,” which the House of Magentano completed to show off its power to the outside world, was revealed to the world in its completed form for the first time.

A festival was held in the capital that same spring day. It was a grand completion ceremony held in “The Magentano Sanctuary” and “The Queen’s Advance Road.” The latter sat in the center of the garden and the former was at the end of it. The sculpture of the queen stood tall, and a skeleton of a dragon, stretching over 200 yards, humbly bowed down at the sculpture. Everyone at the ceremony let out words of admiration at the amazing magnificence they gave off. And that was the reason why no one sensed the subtle change in the dragon skeleton through the ceremony.

Clack… Softly and minutely, and yet certainly, the skull of the dragon started to move.
