# The Defendant’s Method of Blackmail (2)

---

Rattle. Rattle. The carriage shook, but not like the carts drawn around in the markets. Regardless, it still had a rattle as it wasn’t a car with a modern suspension device. However, Lloyd didn’t pay attention to it. His trial had ended successfully. The ruling he received was exactly what he expected.

My blackmailing worked like a charm because they had too much to lose. The head and the other members of the House of Peers.

Lloyd smiled faintly. The event back at the court flashed across his mind. He felt good. Refreshed. Duke Toscano acted exactly the way Lloyd wanted to and accepted all of his demands despite the man’s intractable character. Thanks to that, Lloyd was now on cloud nine while heading back to his chamber, enjoying the expensive, spacious, and velvety leather seat.

“Don’t you think so, Javier?”

When he heard his name, Javier, who was looking out the window, turned and stared at Lloyd. “What about? By the way, what did you do?”

“What? What did I do?” Lloyd asked defensively.

“At today’s trial,” Javier went on, “The more I think about it, it is strange. Duke Toscano had rolled up his sleeves to condemn and charge you for what happened at the construction site.”

“Yeah, he did.”

“So it is beyond my comprehension why he suddenly changed his attitude and advocated for you.”

“Is that why you’re doubting me? Do you think I used some sort of trickery?”

“Yes, Young Master.”

“Come on, trickery? How can you look so relaxed after saying those brazen words,” wailed Lloyd

“Mind you,” Javier raised his voice a little. “You were the one who used such a description.”

Lloyd pretended he didn’t hear him. “Anyway, you’re suggesting that I’ve done something shady under the table that changed the nobles’ attitude. Right?”

“Yes, you are right. Did you really do something shady?”

“Uh-huh.” Lloyd flashed a grin at him. No need to deny it. “I blackmailed him.”

“Blackmailed?” Javier lifted his brow.

“Yeah, I did it right after the day after the House of Peers submitted the bill of indictment.”

“How could you have possibly blackmailed him? You never stepped out of your room.”

“I sent a letter, via Ggoming, to Marquess Bergamo.”

“A letter?”

“Yes.”

Lloyd nodded. Just then, the events of the previous days came to his mind, from the day the Heart of Winter went berserk to the day of cleaning up the mess. He didn’t laze around as the others had thought he did. He was pretending to relax around in bed but did all the work that needed to be done.

I analyzed the accident site. Thankfully, I unconsciously had my design skills on when I arrived at the accident site, allowing me to scan the whole site.

The blown-up refrigerant room. The shattered control chest and passed-out royal magicians and knights. The formation of cracks and scatters, as well as the position and location of the victims. All of the information he had about these events was scanned and saved as a piece of data. This enabled Lloyd to summon the image of the accident in his head in the comfort of his bed and conduct a thorough, slow, and meticulous analysis of the whole situation.

“I found the smoking gun. The fifth son of Marquess Bergamo. He was the only one who died in the accident. I found conclusive evidence that it was his doing.”

“What was it…?” A line of frown appeared in between Javier’s brows.

Lloyd shrugged. “I found the skin of someone’s palm ripped on the cover of the destroyed control chest.”

“The skin of someone’s palm, Young Master?” asked Javier.

“Yeah.”

Lloyd was telling the truth. Indeed, the skin of someone’s entire palm was glued to the chest facade. It was undeniable evidence that pointed to the fifth son of Marquess Bergamo.

“Hey,” went on Lloyd, “Do you know what happens if you stick your tongue against a cold piece of metal in the freezing winter?”

“I know. It sticks to the metal.”

“Right? And what happens if you try to pull it off by force?”

“A terrible idea. Your frozen skin would rip off.”

“That’s exactly right. That’s what seemed to have happened with the piece of skin on the chest.”

“Do you mean… that it belonged to the fifth son of Marquess Bergamo?”

“Yes,” said Lloyd confidently, nodding for emphasis. “I saw it when I arrived at the scene. That royal magician’s hand was bleeding. The blood had frozen because of the cold, but it definitely looked like it had been ripped off.”

This, however, was a lie. Lloyd hadn’t observed anything like that when he got to the scene because he was too out of his mind to catch that sort of small detail. But he nonetheless knew and was 100% sure about it. After all, he was able to look back at the scene in his head for observation and analysis purposes.

“So, what else could it mean other than the magician had put his hand on the control chest, prompted the Heart of Winter to go on a rampage, and then wreak havoc on the place by emitting cold air? That was when his hand got stuck. You know what happened afterward. Boom. The control chest exploded.”

“So,” contributed Javier, “The explosion must have shoved him and forcefully tore his hand away. Hence, the skin of his palm was left stuck on the control chest.”

“Correct.” Lloyd grinned. Javier’s deduction was right. “This evidence was too conclusive. I had circumstantial evidence that he was our guy, not to mention I had the testimonies of the royal knights that he stabbed his colleague, but I didn’t have any direct witnesses.”

“Certainly,” affirmed Javier gravely, “For all we know, the stabbed magician might have framed himself as a victim when he was the one who was at fault.”

“That’s exactly right. It’s not entirely impossible since they’re magicians, although the chances are small. The man could’ve stabbed his own back using magic and blamed it on his colleague.”

“But the ripped piece of skin ruled out the possibility of that.”

“Yeah. I’m telling you. It’s a smoking gun.”

Javier furrowed his brows. “Well then, um, I see. You then sent a threatening letter to Marquess Bergamo that the dirty secret of his son would be put out in the open unless the House of Peers changed its stance. Is my guess correct?”

“Yeah, I say, you’re quite good.” Lloyd flashed a mischievous smile. Javier was right. “What do you think would happen if I disclosed everything, including the smoking gun? The investigative body in the palace will have to conduct another investigation, only this time more focused on the new evidence. The result? The Bergamo family will take every fault. Sure, I won’t be entirely exonerated. I was in charge of the site when the accident happened. But I’ll receive lighter punishment compared to the Bergamo family.”

“I certainly think so. More so now when you have become a hero to the people.”

Javier smiled, remembering that Lloyd had become the hero of the capital in the past few days. Everyone adored him for a simple reason. The eldest son of the count and meritorious retainer of the Magentano imperial family had rolled up his sleeves to save the plebian laborers under him. This heartwarming news spread like wildfire across the capital through the mouths of the laborers and their families as well as other relatives.

“The public opinion has tipped over to your favor, and once it was proven that you are not the direct cause of the accident, your punishment would have been as light as a feather.”

“Yeah,” agreed Lloyd heartily, “Plus, I had the status and privileges of a meritorious retainer of the imperial family. I’d have gotten a small scolding from Her Majesty. A verbal warning. But the marquess? He would’ve caused an uproar.”

“It would have really been so if you revealed the evidence in court.” That was when Javier understood the whole situation. And he thought to himself further that the fake Lloyd was a sly man. A cunning, manipulative, and small-hearted man. But those qualities were what made him great.

Right. If this piece of evidence was revealed, hmm, the Bergamo family wouldn’t have been able to avoid a grave punishment as they directly created the cause for the loss of the royal house’s divine item. Their sin would be graver than treason. In the worst case, their family could have fallen.

That would not be the only consequence. The sword of reckoning could reach all the noble houses related to the marquess by blood. It was possible that their crime would be considered worse than treason.

The thing is, Marquess Bergamo and Duke Toscano are in-laws. Duke Toscano is the former’s father-in-law.

In other words, Duke Toscano was the maternal grandfather of the culprit. The central nobles who had influence over the House of Peers were tightly connected to each other by blood, location, and education. And so, any punishment that was similar to exterminating the family would have affected every noble related to the marquess in varying degrees.

What a dirty method.

Javier couldn’t help but smile wryly.

“That is why the head of the House of Peers changed his stance. He looked like he was ready to risk his life for you.”

“Well, you could say that.”

The same smile hung on Lloyd’s face now. His meticulously designed blackmail worked, which was about how they’d be guillotined if he received a guilty sentence. Since the powerful nobles had a lot to lose and were trapped by their social network of connections from education, blood, and region, Lloyd simply had to softly suggest that they would be rewarded in the future so long they did what he demanded. Eventually, thanks to his blackmail and bribery, the trial went how Lloyd wanted it to.

As for the queen, she just had to go along the flow as she was already advocating for Lloyd. So, at the very moment the Duke Toscano unexpectedly rooted for Lloyd, she simply echoed his words in joy.

When Duke Toscano said that Lloyd was a true patriot, Queen Magentano replied that he was right. When he argued that punishing Lloyd would be more damaging than more beneficial, she repeated that he was right.

As for Lloyd himself, he had to only throw some groveling remarks here and there, things like “I will follow the great will of Your Majesty.” And the final verdict: “The loss of the Heart of Winter is painful, but Lloyd holds no substantial responsibility over this incident as the item went into a frenzy due to magical instability, something beyond human control. As such, placing all the responsibility on Lloyd, the imperial family’s meritorious retainer, for this is extremely harsh. Only a small fine will be levied on him for neglecting his work site.” That was the verdict.

“Thanks to that, a slap on the wrist is all I’ve got. And everything went well. Now, time to get to work!”

Indeed. It didn’t mean that Lloyd could rest now just because the trial ended. He still had jobs to do at the site, and it was something that he berated.

I seriously want to grab the author of The Knight of Blood and Iron by the neck and give him a terrible shake for planning out this merciless world.

Lloyd berated his fate of having to live in this kind of world setting instead of an easy, happy-go-lucky one. Fighting back the urge to rant about his life, he continued his work to extract the tauranga in the structure. Since the construction was already in its final stage of nearing the isolated piers of the foundation, it was not done before long. After five days, Lloyd reached his desired point of progression.

Found it!

At last, the isolated piers of the foundation revealed itself. A rectangular piece of tauranga that was about sixteen feet in width and nineteen feet in width was stuck in the middle. By this time, Lloyd started to carefully start his work. Ppodong joined the effort, and so did Javier. The stone was harvested and replaced with another piece of material, and cement was applied for adhesion. This process was repeated twelve times. At last, the moment the twelfth piece of tauranga was harvested safely…

Ding dong.

[You have attained twelve pieces of the tauranga stone, a key material for the Jewel of Truth.]

[You have succeeded in excavating the key material of Aotearoa that was destroyed during the mythical age.]

[This is truly an impressive excavation that will be remembered in history.]

[When the Jewel of Truth is restored, your name will be remembered in history as the excavator who found this key material.]

[For that achievement, you have been awarded a large amount of bonus RP.]

[You have acquired 2,000 RP.]

[Current RP: 4,655]

Eh?

Lloyd received an unexpected achievement notification and a large amount of RP. This was a monumental moment for him as he now had enough RP to use the Time Freeze skill.
