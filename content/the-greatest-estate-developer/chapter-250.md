# I’ve Become the Dragon King’s Alarm (2)

---

Frontera fiefdom. The province that sat at the far east of the Magentano Kingdom. Originally, it was a shabby, good-for-nothing boondocks, but recently, it had been reborn as the hottest hub for development in the whole kingdom. Even today, its coal mine produced plumes of dark smoke, and the ondol laid out on house floors warmed up the carpets. There was also the arable land that evolved from the Maritz Wetland, the terraced fields, the water system that ran 24/7 to produce clean water, the generous apartment complex, and the water sewage system for human wastes, no other fiefdom in the entire kingdom with such advanced technologies.

Furthermore, the Frontera fiefdom received a generous amount of funding from the royal palace. There were plenty of supplies for reconstruction arriving on a monthly basis. It was similar to having thousands of tons worth of food delivered for free every month. There was more. The water bill from the Lacona viscounty. The slope stabilization maintenance fee from the Namaran fiefdom. All of it was an excellent source of financial capital.

And moreover… haa. We received more Elensia sap from the elf village this month too. It’s amazing how we can obtain one whole bottle of such precious sap in exchange for just 30 bags of coal. Lloyd told me to store it in the warehouse as soon as it arrived, right?

Count Arcos Frontera recalled what Lloyd had said and busied himself.

Psh! Fwoosh! Thwack! The trowel moved busily at the hard-working hands of the count, and each time, the weeds that tried to take residence in the manor’s garden disappeared. Count Arcos’s heart grew lighter with it.

“Whew.”

The fiefdom had everything it could possibly need. Its future was solid, and on top of it, the count’s two sons were dutifully moving forward to build their own future. As the owner of the fiefdom and head of the family, Count Frontera couldn’t be any more satisfied.

“Isn’t that true, my love?” Count Arcos asked.

“Come again?” Countess Marbella cocked her head at the question, stopping her job with the trowel. “What do you mean by that, honey?”

“Just. I am happy with how light and warm my heart feels. The weather is nice. Speaking of, what do you say we go horseback riding in the afternoon?”

“You are not suggesting we should race, are you?” asked Countess Marbella.

“Of course not. Let’s ride together with one of us in the front and back. Pressed close to each other,” suggested Count Arcos with a smile.

“My dear, how you make me blush.” Countess Marbella turned away.

“Hahaha, do you feel shy?”

“No, it is not that.”

“Then?”

“Only that I cannot believe you brought up such a pleasant suggestion just now,” she looked down as she said.

“Hahaha!”

The two then erupted into a hearty burst of laughter. Though they were squatting down with their hands muddied from working on the garden, the sensation of the dirt gave them joy. And even the sun looked particularly bright and beautiful today. Just like that, under the cozy sunlight, both looked around before they brought their lips close. Just then, all of a sudden, a magic circle appeared in the air beside the flower garden. Bzzt! Swaa!

“Eek…?”

“My!”

The two jumped back right before their lips touched. However, they were still holding each other’s hands as they stared at the magic circle, which disappeared a second later until it was replaced with three people and a giant lump.

“Whoa…”

Lloyd staggered as he stepped on the ground after the teleport magic circle vanished.

Ugh. I feel sick.

Up until a moment ago, Lloyd was at the center of the dragon cave when Verkis flicked his finger. One snap and everything drastically changed. A mana wave enveloped the whole surrounding, floating his body. All sorts of lights shimmered around him. But they only lasted for a second. By the time his feet touched the ground, the entire scenery around him had been altered. Instead of the inside of the vast cave, the warm and familiar garden of his manor came into Lloyd’s view. And then he also saw two welcoming faces of people who fell to the ground by surprise at his appearance.

“L-Lloyd?”

The faces he was welcomed to see belonged to Count and Countess Frontera, and both murmured his name in unison.

Thankfully, Lloyd was able to grasp the situation right away.

So, we teleported from the cave to the Frontera manor in one flicking motion.

Truly, the magic of the dragon was impressive. Lloyd bowed toward the count and countess and said, “I am here. I must have surprised you quite a bit.”

“Um… Y-yes, but… Who is that? And the lion?” Count Arcos pointed at Dragon King Verkis and the manticore.

Lloyd’s wry smile deepened. “Very important guests of ours. I will give you an explanation later. It is more important for me to hurry and escort them inside first. Excuse me for a moment, please.”

Lloyd sensed that this wasn’t the time to dawdle explaining the whole situation to them. And he was right. The look on Dragon King Verkis’s face was slowly changing as he stood beside Lloyd.

He’s signaling that he wants to go to sleep right now!

Half-shut eyes. Unfocused pupils. Drooped shoulders. Hunched back. Slow hands and legs. It looked like Verkis could sleep for a whole day right here if Lloyd put down a blanket on the dirt ground.

No. I brought the dragon king here. I have to provide him with a good place to sleep. That’s how I’ll be able to have more bargaining power in the future.

Lloyd grew impatient as he feared that Verkis might collapse to the ground right this moment. After quibbling a few words to the count and countess, Lloyd said as he nearly pushed Verkis, “Excuse me, my king?”

“Mhm…” replied Verkis dully.

“Thank you for the magic. Please follow me.”

“Must I have to follow you…” mumbled Verkis.

“Argh! Eek, no, you can’t fall asleep here!”

“I’m sleepy…”

“It’s not very far!”

Lloyd helped Verkis hurriedly as if he was helping out an injured comrade on a battlefield, almost carrying the king with his body. And then, he took Verkis to the annex building for guests and threw him onto the bed.

“Okay, then I’ll wake you up in the morning! Please sleep well!” yelled Lloyd.

Snore…

Another crisis was overcome by that. Lloyd was able to breathe in relief at last. And then he went back to the count and countess, who were still recovering from surprise at the whole event, and explained everything from the beginning.

“So…” Count Arcos gulped before continuing. “The guest a while ago… is the king of all dragons?”

“Yes, that is a precise summary,” conceded Lloyd.

“And we will be serving such a great figure? For a whole year?”

“Yes. That’s what has been decided somehow,” replied Lloyd.

“Is there any request he particularly asked of us?”

“Um, there is.”

“What is it?” Count Frontera gulped.

“We need to wake him up once a day in the morning,” answered Lloyd.

“Wake him up?”

“Yes, he told me that he needs to contact his wife, the dragon queen, every morning. As I told you a while ago, that’s how I came to bring him here.”

“Hahaha…” Count Arcos burst into laughter. Even though he tried to suppress it, he couldn’t.

How did my son grow to be so…?

Count Arcos wondered how his son had grown to be so capable. He looked at Lloyd with a gaze filled with awe. It had become commonplace for Lloyd to be summoned by the queen for an assignment, and as if that wasn’t enough, he just arrived at the manor with none other than the dragon king. Now, Lloyd held connections not only with the queen of a kingdom but also with the king of all dragons.

Perhaps, I will become a father to a son that will be remembered by history.

Such a thought flashed Count Frontera’s mind. But there was one thing the count was oblivious of. Lloyd had another king in his network on top of the human queen and dragon king. It was the King of Hell. But Lloyd didn’t bother to tell the count and countess about him.

I mean, it’s Hell. It’s quite literally the underworld. He’s going to get worried if I tell him that I’ve been there.

On one hand, it was pretty uncomfortable to share that story. After all, Lloyd had met the real Lloyd in Hell and helped the latter stop wandering and reincarnate. Sure, he could leave out that part of the story, but doing so made him uncomfortable as if his head was being wretched with his voice of conscience. So, he decided the story about the real Lloyd should be revealed in the future. When an appropriate opportunity arose, he would bring it up. Even if it invited rebuke and resentment from the count and countess, Lloyd was determined to come clean about the whole truth.

But now’s not the time for that.

First, he had a lot of work to take care of. And so, Lloyd excluded the part about his trip to Hell while he narrated the whole story to Count and Countess Frontera. And naturally, the story flowed to another subject, which was first brought up by Count Frontera, who said, “By the way, that lion… is that a new friend you have summoned like Sir Ppodong?”

Count Frontera was intrigued by the manticore that was sniffing the flowers in the garden. Lloyd smiled and said, “Oh, no, it’s not that. It’s a manticore, one of Dragon King Verkis loyal servants. And that is why it came along with us.”

“Hmm, I see,” said both Count and Countess Frontera as they nodded without showing any other reaction. The guards, servants, maids, and villagers reacted similarly to them when they saw the manticore.

“Eh? There’s a giant lion I’ve never seen before.”

“It’s extremely big and fat!”

“It’s as big as Sir Ppodong!”

“Mom! I want to touch its belly!”

In short, the people in this region weren’t terrified by the sheer size and stateliness of the manticore. The rationale was simple. They already had plenty of experience with Ppodong, Bangul, Hamang, and Bibeong. Moreover, they had endless encounters with all sorts of evil monsters during the Monster Domino incident. Bluntly put, the villagers had all been through hell and back, so in their eyes, the legendary manticore was nothing but a big, chubby lion with a pink belly or a fat cat. Regardless, the Manticore happily enjoyed the welcoming reactions of people toward it.

“Dad! I want to ride that!”

“Cororong!”

“It says you can!”

In the next few days, the manticore immediately became a fluffy ride and an insect-collecting friend for the kids of the Frontera fiefdom. While all of this was happening, Lloyd became fairly busy himself as there was a sizable pile of work for him to take care of.

There’s the water pipe that repeatedly froze and thawed throughout the winter. And I should check on the water system in the Maritz Wetland and the retaining walls in the terrace fields. We’ve accumulated a fair bit of ondol maintenance requests. Plus, the safety check for the coal mine and maintenance of the water pipes in the main sewage needs to get done. Wow. This is nuts.

He had been away for too long. Count Arcos had tried hard in his own way, as did Sir Bayern, the Engineering Corps, and the Skeleton Corps, but there was an inevitable difference in the inspection directly led by Lloyd in terms of quality and meticulousness.

Well, it’s all right since I won’t be going away like before anymore.

He could say goodbye to leaving the fiefdom and working for any outsiders. His days of going out of the fiefdom and earning money to save his family, protect Frontera fiefdom, and make money for retirement were over. There wasn’t any need for it anymore. All of his obstacles had been overcome. Right now, what awaited Lloyd was basking in the goodness of life and lazing around for his remaining days.

And so this will be my last time putting this much effort into repair and inspection.

Down the road, maintenance would only take a little effort since he’d stay near the fiefdom. He would just need to maintain and protect his golden goose, the fiefdom. When Lloyd had this thought, the fatigue in his body disappeared, pushing him to work harder. Meanwhile, he also didn’t forget to wake Verkis up every morning. It turned out that acting as an alarm for the dragon king was not an easy job.

“Um, my great king,” called Lloyd.

“…….”

“It’s time to wake up,” said Lloyd again.

Snore…

“Um, don’t you need to call the dragon queen?”

“Eh… Later…” mumbled the groggy dragon king.

“Later? Do you want me to wake you up later?” replied Lloyd.

“Mhm…”

“When do you want me to wake you up?”

“Tomorrow…”

“…”

Lloyd was dumbstruck and didn’t like what the dragon king was saying. It turned out Verkis was even less of a morning fellow than Lloyd thought. So, left without any choice, he made a last-ditch effort.

Please forgive me, Dragon King Verkis. You have brought this onto yourself. Ehem! Lloyd cleared his throat and started to sing in a clear voice.

“Dear, dear Dragon King! Sleepy, sleepy Dragon King! Chicken, chicken, don’t cry! You might wake up the dragon king!”

The song was destructive. It sounded like a wake-up call from Hell. It deafened the eardrum of its listener and successfully opened the gates of Hell. It was so painful and sharp it made the listeners feel like they had acid smeared in their eardrums. As such, Dragon King Verkis became completely awake.

“Do you… want me to punch you right in the stomach and make you return to mother nature?” growled Verkis, visibly annoyed.

“Ehem! Ehem! Please forgive me,” apologized Lloyd.

“Or do you want to become a speck of dioxin in this wretched world?”

“Please, f-forgive me. But still!”

“Still?”

“I thought I should still make sure you keep your promise to call the dragon queen.”

“Tsk. Is that why you sang that awful song on purpose?”

“No,” denied Lloyd.

“Then?”

“I sang to the best of my ability.”

“That’s your best?” said Verkis in shock.

“Yes, my lord.”

“I’m sorry…”

“Ehem…”

Anyway, Lloyd managed to act as an alarm for the dragon king every morning. Soon, he was able to attain certainty.

Finally, I really did it.

He really did everything. His goal was to spend the rest of his life in a peaceful province without any hard work or complicated affairs. This was the reason why Lloyd worked so hard and overcame countless obstacles. At times, he felt hopeless but always jumped back on his feet again. He breathlessly raced forward without giving up, and finally, he achieved the goal.

The fiefdom was absolutely safe and held heaps of capital. He had no more future concerns. And the truth about the real Lloyd would be revealed to the count and countess soon. After clearing the emotional debt he owed to them, there would be no more storms to break through. Lloyd was dwelling on these thoughts in this bedroom. And now, he took a deep breath. It was late at night, with no one to call for him. He composed his heart amid the tranquility.

This will be the last time I check.

He had achieved everything he wanted in life. There was nothing to worry about anymore. Would he really be able to die in peace after living a life of relaxation? Or would there be more unexpected events and incidents? Lloyd opened the skill window to check his future and activated a skill he hadn’t used in a while.

Clack.

[Skill Name: Ending Spoiler]

[Skill Feature: Absolute Authority]

[Required CP: 80 (Third)]

[You can take a peek at the portion of the ending scene that will unfold in this story by spending some of CP. It may have a happy or sad ending.]

The familiar skill description spanned. Lloyd took a deep breath.

Let’s take a look.

Lloyd selected the activate button at the bottom of the skill window.

[Skill Name: Would you like to use the Ending Spoiler?]

[Required CP: 80 (Third)]

[Current CP: 1,192]

[YES / NO]

Of course, it’s a YES.

Ding dong.

[Skill Name: Ending Spoiler has been activated.]

[You have used 80 CP.]

[Current CP: 1,112]

The skill activation message cascaded in front of him. From then on…

Crackle…! Time around him terribly slowed until it finally stopped. The window curtain swaying against the spring wind, the flower petals blowing in the air all stopped as though they were pegged in a wall called time. And just like it always did, the space ahead of Lloyd swayed until it opened a round blue passage.

Let’s go.

Lloyd walked into the passage, and everything in his vision was enveloped by light.

Fwoosh! Lloyd cast his glance at the new scenery spanning in front of him. And then he was appalled by what he saw.

What… is this?
