# Smack the Back of the Head and Run (1)

---

“Bibeong!”

8,200 feet off the sky of the Magentano palace, Bibeong distantly shouted aloud. Then he grabbed the Bone Dragon’s spine and rib bone. Clench!

The Bone Dragon had nothing but bones left in its body, so Bibeong had plenty of areas to grip. Though his hands couldn’t grip as well as a human’s, the Bone dragon’s entire body was essentially a handle for him.

Eh?

The soul of warlock Targa in the Bone Dragon was flabbergasted.

What is this?

Targa was flustered. And dumbstruck. What just happened? A guy on a parrotbill flitted his way out of the blue and hurled a beaver, which slammed into him after growing 328 feet tall. Anyone would snort at him if he told them this story. Heck, if someone else told him such an outrageous story, Targa would have yelled at them to shut up and cursed the person with black magic. That was how ridiculous the whole thing was to him. But unfortunately, that ludicrous event was happening now. Thus, he was tightly trapped in Bibeong’s grip.

“Bibeobeong!”

Bibeong struggled, tightening his embrace around the Bone Dragon. He leaned against the back of the dragon as he tensed his chubby butt and belly, pressing his wing joint. That severely interrupted its fluttering.

A-Argh! Let me go! Surprised, Targa gave a delayed response. He twisted and turned to wrench Bibeong off of himself. But he was stronger than Targa thought. That was thanks to Bibeong’s weight.

This punk is a lot heavier than I thought!

Targa believed that he would be heavier. The Bone Dragon’s body was 656 feet long, which was almost twice the length of Bibeong. So, Targa naturally thought that his weight must be heavier than this beaver. But what he missed was their interior anatomy which was another different story.

Is this beaver heavier than me?

That was when it dawned on Targa. The Bone Dragon had nothing in its body but bones, so it was super scrawny compared to its gigantic size. Moreover, its tail made up a good portion of the length of its body.

Whereas Bibeong, was fat. Chubby buttocks. Flabby belly. Ample cheeks. Every part of him was fat and flabby. On top of that, the muscles that bore the sheer weight of his fats were solid and thick. In short, there was no way for the Bone Dragon, despite its dragon-thick bones, to outweigh Bibeong’s limitless fats. Bibeong cuddled the Bone Dragon and pressed its wing joint with his monumental weight and belly fat. That brought a stop to its fluttering. And thus, it began to crash down.

“Bibeong!”

Ackkk!

8,200 feet off the ground. The Bone Dragon turned its long head and attempted to bite Bibeong, who was on its back. Bibeong ducked down and used his giant front teeth to fiercely bite down on its neck bone.

“Bibebeong!”

Gargh!

4,200 feet off the ground. Bone Dragon wielded its whip-like tail and smacked Bibeong’s back. Bibeong fought back by whipping with his flat tail, which was wide and flat like a giant roe, the perfect shape to protect his whole body. The Bone Dragon’s tail bounced off Bibeong’s shield-like tail.

“Bibeong!”

Arggh!

1,640 feet off the ground. Sensing the crash, the Bone Dragon attempted one final attack to turn things around. It tensed its whole body to flip its body upside down. But it didn’t work. There was no other reason for its failure. Bibeong was too fat to be turned over. So the Bone Dragon was pressed toward the ground, slammed in the air by 3,000-ton weight, and plummeted. No brakes or anything.

“Bibebeong!”

Daaammit!!!

“…!”

They hit the ground without a single shout. Everyone who gazed at the whole scene thought for a second that the ground had flipped over. The impending roar, earth tremor, and storm of sand arrived in the next moment. Kaboom! As 4,000 tons of total weight rammed into the land with massive kinetic energy, the whole ground shook, and shockwaves burst all around, toppling the trees nearby. The royal guards covered their ears to protect their eardrums.

“Get down!”

“Argh…!”

The dusty storm faded sometime later, and the guards staggered to their feet in a coughing fit. Among them was Queen Magentano, whose eyes shone brightly.

“…”

She wasn’t staring at the crash location like the rest of the guards. Instead, her deathly gaze flickered over to the large parrotbill in the air, whose vague outline could be seen through the thick dust.

Lloyd Frontera.

She remembered how he had run for his life when she called. The sight utterly disappointed her. But now, a part of her wondered if he had intentionally run away to do this.

Tsk. What a mysterious fellow he is.

Queen Magentano chuckled. It was a relief nonetheless. She had been utterly clueless about how she should deal with the Bone Dragon. A sense of hopelessness gripped her, but thankfully, Lloyd showed up just in time and did something extraordinary with his fantastical creature.

That large fantastical creature crushed the Bone Dragon all the way in the air.

The queen’s gaze moved toward the crash point. She believed that the Bone Dragon couldn’t have remained intact after falling down at such a height. No, she even suspected the dragon might have been completely subdued just now. She was starting to become hopeful when…

Ruuumble…!

Something giant rose amid the thick sandy dust. A gray outline of a jagged skeleton appeared. It was the Bone Dragon.

“…!”

Whoosh! A giant tail swept the surface.

“Argh!”

Queen Magentano jumped just in time and closely avoided the attack. But the next one that followed seemed unavoidable. The dragon’s gigantic, open wing swung across the whole area. Whoosh!

She clenched her teeth. There was no way to avoid or block the attack.

Then I only have one choice!

Goosebumps rose all over her body as she gripped the sword hilt. She decided to confront the incoming blow head-on. Even if this was her last moment, she resolved to combat valiantly until she died. Thus, she wielded her sword with such determination. But before she could actually do so, someone grabbed the back of her neck and tugged hard.

“Your Majesty!”

Grab!

She felt someone grab the back of her neck and heard a voice. Frightened, she withdrew her sword and turned around. Then she saw who it was.

“Lloyd Frontera?”

Lloyd had gripped her collar with one hand while using the other to hold a riding rein. She also spotted the large parrotbill that Lloyd was riding frantically fluttering his wings. Just like that, she was saved in midair by Lloyd and Ggoming.

“Y-Your Majesty! Stop flailing!” Lloyd bellowed, almost threatening to let go of his grip if she continued to struggle.

The queen obeyed his words and relaxed. Her gaze then darted to the gigantic menacing wings that chased toward them. Fwoosh!

My…

Could this be what a fly saw while looking at an incoming fly swatter? The dragon’s huge wing which was the size of a cliff hurtled toward her way, sweeping across her entire range of vision like a broom. The scene itself made her heart drop. But to her relief, she was at the mercy of a mobile creature that could avoid such an attack.

“Ggoming!”

“A little more! Ggoming! Let’s goooo!” yelled Lloyd.

“Ggomiiing!!!”

Ggoming fluttered his wings at a maddening speed, and the explosive acceleration allowed them to distance themselves from the incoming wing. Just then, the parrotbill closed his wings and dove down in a flash, almost touching the ground. Swooosh!

The expansive wing bone passed over her head with a proximity of less than 3 feet. Taking a deep breath as though she was shaking off her shivers, Queen Alicia got back on the parrotbill.

“Your Majesty, are you hurt?” asked Lloyd.

“No. Thank you. I owe you my life.”

“That is a relief,” said Lloyd.

Lloyd really felt that way. He genuinely was relieved that she didn’t die.

It would be troublesome if the queen died.

Queen Magentano was his boss that always had more work to give him. But at the same time, she was his most reliable supporter and contact. She didn’t accuse him of treason for most things and showered his fiefdom with all sorts of assistance.

It’s really not easy to meet and serve a boss like her.

She surely was the ideal ruler to serve, disregarding her tendency to swamp him with work. So, losing her would be a loss for Lloyd. There was no guarantee that the new monarch after her would be favorable to him.

The queen can’t die here. This applies to Javier as well.

Lloyd surveyed the ground as he led Ggoming outside the sphere of the attack. The Bone Dragon was going berserk, whereas Bibeong was staggering, probably due to the body-press attack. Lloyd passed by and pulled on Javier in a flash. But it was too early to be relieved.

Here comes the important part. Speed is critical.

He would run away with the queen and Javier. His first priority was safely escaping. That was the reason Lloyd came here.

“Then let’s go!” yelled Lloyd proudly.

“Where?” the queen asked right away.

Wetting his lips, Lloyd shouted, “To a better battlefield!”

“A new battlefield?” asked the queen.

“Yes, Your Majesty. This area is not suitable for a battle since this garden is new, and the palace, city district, and people are nearby!”

When Lloyd gave the remark he had prepared, the queen reacted positively to his relief.

“Lloyd Frontera. Are you worried about the well-being of innocent citizens who could be affected by this affliction?”

“Yes, Your Majesty!”

“Good. You are right. Then do you plan on drawing the monster out of the capital?”

“You are right, Your Majesty!”

“Good. I approve of your plan.”

“I am overwhelmed-”

“There is no time to be overwhelmed. Hurry up,” interrupted the queen.

“Yes, Your Majesty!”

Lloyd was relieved that the queen gave her approval. Deciding to make his plan a success, Lloyd grabbed his

reins.

“Ggoming!” Lloyd called.

“Ggoming?”

“Let’s gooo!”

“Ggomiming!”

His flight speed increased, and Lloyd threw a glance at the Bone Dragon.

Okay. Everything is going according to plan.

The queen had given her trust. And now, it was time to knuckle down and execute his plan.

That is, for us to run without looking back.

Far away. Far away outside the capital. Farther out. Somewhere very far where a battle could take place. If possible, he intended to fly away for about three days straight.

Fight? Why should we fight that thing? We should just keep running away.

In actuality, Lloyd didn’t plan on fighting against the Bone Dragon head-on. He planned on playing tag with it just like now.

And if possible, I’ll fly outside the border to a place far, far away from the capital. That way the people in the capital and the queen will be safe.

That was his plan. Moreover, if he was lucky, he might be able to take care of the Bone Dragon too. How?

To Gapel. That’s where we should go. I heard that something more formidable than the Bone Dragon lives there.

Just then, the entity that was briefly mentioned in the novel came to his mind. The Gapel region. A seemingly ordinary and small neutral county. However, an extraordinary creature was said to be inhabiting there.

His name was Verkis. He was the Dragon King, if I recall correctly?

Dragon King Verkis. The history book of the Lorasia Continent made a mention of him as the most powerful dragon to ever exist. The novel touched on it as a mere rumor just once, and that was it. That was why Lloyd had forgotten about this matter until now. It was a miracle that he happened to recall this just today.

But that is enough.

Lloyd decided to put his trust in the story in the novel. Dragon King. He was the dragon king, for Pete’s sake! Being the king, this mythical dragon would be interested in the affairs of dragons, and he would be a faithful and hardworking figure.

So, taking the Bone Dragon that’s running amuck would be the same as taking a zombie or skeleton soldier to a human king. With that, the situation will naturally resolve itself. I mean, he’s the Dragon King. He won’t sit back and just watch the Bone Dragon go berserk. I presume he’d look sideways and order for the Bone Dragon to be taken care of. Or, maybe, he’d take care of it himself.

Lloyd had a feeling that this king would straighten the situation out.

And I don’t care if my plan fails either.

The rumor could be false. It was possible that the Dragon King was not there. But Lloyd concluded that it didn’t matter.

Then I can opt for plan B, which is leading the Bone Dragon to an unpopulated area.

That would complete their escape. Of course, the Bone Dragon would return in the future. But they would be better prepared than now as the chase between the dragon and Lloyd toward Gapel would buy them some time to respond.

It takes about three days to fly to Gapel. While we were flying, the news would spread to the capital and throughout the whole kingdom. The sultan kingdom and other nearby nations would hear about it too.

Then everyone would be able to brace themselves and assemble strong forces to fight against the Bone Dragon’s invasion. That would be a much more organized response than the queen’s solo confrontation with her sword.

That’s how I can buy more time. It would also be more advantageous for us by any standard. So, why risk ourselves and fight here? We should obviously leave!

His judgment couldn’t be any more reasonable and wiser. Satisfied with his plan, he prompted Ggoming to flutter harder before throwing a glance at the Bone Dragon’s side. Lloyd decided that he had to attract its attention so that it would be engulfed in rage and hot on his heels. He needed to make the dragon obstinately pursue him for multiple days on end.

“Javier, your sword, please.”

Lloyd borrowed a long sword from Javier and stretched his sword at the Bone Dragon as he flew around it on the ground.

Boom! A triple mana blast came out of the sword, which flew across for several yards and hit the back of the Bone Dragon head.

Clung! The Bone Dragon’s skull staggered at the sudden blow.
