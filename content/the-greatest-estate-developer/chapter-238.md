# Noise Pollution Complaint (1)

---

“Grrr! Brrr!”

A vast lava lake sat about 1.8 miles off the construction site. It was a peaceful, sticky, and commonplace lake in Hell. But today, something was slightly different. It was boiling with intensity and fierceness that weren’t there before. The reason was simple.

“Grrr!” The Lava Giant slumbering in the bottom of the lava lake tossed and turned, clamping its ears with its hands in irritation. How could it not? It was asleep for the last 20,000 years, and it wanted to sleep for at least 100,000 years more because it would then be able to recharge a bit. That seemed like a satisfactory amount of good sleep. But the reality was cruel for the Lava Giant.

Boom! Thuuump! For the past few days, its senses had been assaulted by blaring noises and annoying vibrations, and the Lava Giant could not figure out where the sounds were coming from. It was too lazy to even try making sense of them and get up to check them out. So, the giant just let it be, thinking the distractions were just a passing ruckus and would soon disappear. It would be best to ignore the disturbances and fall back asleep. But as time progressed, it started to feel its initial thoughts had been wrong.

Boom! Boom! The vibrations lasted for 24 hours, terrorizing the giant every moment of the day. Something kept drumming the lava lake and pierced the giant’s ears as it slept at the bottom.

“Grrr!”

Its eyelashes trembled in annoyance, and its gigantic hands that covered its ears tensed up.

At every thumping, the giant knitted its brows. At every noise that got under its skin, the face of the giant darkened. And at last, it had enough. It ran out of patience.

“Grr! Grrrr!”

The moment its last strand of patience snapped, the Lava Giant opened its eyes and gradually rose, bringing a change in the lake. The stagnant pool of bubbling lava started to absorb into its entire body, becoming a part of the giant. Its already ginormous body started to swell and at last…

“Grrrrrr! Gargh!!”

Once the Lava Giant sucked every drop of the lava in the lake, it rose and stood, its whole body stretching 656 feet in length, and it shot an enraged glare at the plain full of volcanic ash toward the origin of the pestering din and vibration.

“Grrr!”

Since the giant was 656 feet tall and there weren’t any mountains or hills near the area, the giant had a clear vision of the volcanic ash mound stretching horizontally in a straight line, about 1.6 miles away. Thousands of demons were busily moving while grouped together around the mound. The sight set off the giant.

“Grrr!”

It was crystal clear that these demons had been the ones creating all the recent commotions while they built that tall mound for whatever unknown reason. The solution? It was simple.

“I’ll kill them all, grr!”

That was the solution. Kill every demon. Then they wouldn’t make a fuss anymore. The whole place would quiet down again, and it could happily fall asleep again. Resolved, the giant took a step forward.

Boooom! The whole ground shook as it took a step forward with its 104-foot-long blazing foot. Since it had been a while since the giant last walked, it moved slowly until it became more comfortable with its steps. Soon, the giant’s gait slowly became more aggressive until it reflected all the unhidden rage inside of it.

Thwaaack! The giant ran. Sprinted. Hurtled. Like a storm. Like an earthquake. It reached the 1.6-mile distance in great strides, approaching the construction site with a violent roar and destructive ground tremor. And without a hint of hesitation or concession, it jumped high and trampled on the railroad with all of its weight.

Kaboom! The rails and ties the demons toiled hard for smashed to pieces, and the bed splintered to little bits. One leaping and trampling. About 260 feet of the bed was destroyed without any trace. The same went for the demons.

“Wh-what the?”

“Aaack!!”

“Get down!”

Dozens of demons flew across the air in various directions like bullets, collapsing to the ground and bleeding blue. They screamed as they grasped their wounds. However, those able to voice their pain were the lucky ones compared to the roughly 20 demons who were turned to dust after being trampled without warning.

“I-It’s the Lava Giant!!”

One of the demons that made sense of things screeched aloud. The demons flailing amid the abrupt destruction and demolition stopped dead in their tracks, all of them blanching at the same time in realization. 

“The Lava Giant?”

Chills ran down their spines. The Lava Giant was a terrorizing creature that was incomparably strong to the lava centipedes that they had mastered over. There was no one who could stop the Lava Giant except the King of Hell.

What’s the Lava Giant doing here?!

According to stories their parents had told when they were young, the Lava Giant came out of slumber only once every hundreds of thousands of years. The last time it went berserk was said to be 20,000 years ago. And so, there was supposed to be an obscene amount of time before it went rampant again.

“S-Somebody!” muttered a demon in a panic-stricken voice. “Somebody go report to the King of Hell!”

“Acck! I want to go too!”

“Me too! But the Hell Castle! It’s too far!”

“Then hide! Run!”

Disheartened, the demons threw their tools away and scurried for their lives, hiding or running far away. But there was one thing they didn’t know. It was that the Lava Giant could capture all their movements with its two eyes from a height of 656 feet. Soon, it unleashed a flood of unspeakable violence.

Wham! The Lava Giant softly stomped the ground. Ten demons that chose to hide in the rocks became crushed between them and turned into powder. Those who chose to run away weren’t safe either.

Whoosh! Booom! This time, the gigantic foot of the Lava Giant moved sideways like a broom, sweeping the ground with a swish. That simple gesture covered hundreds of feet. Dozens of demons were killed by the blow like a colony of ants.

“Please! Ack! Save me!”

The demons dashed and ran for their lives, all yelping and running in every direction. But the Lava Giant was merciless. It believed that it could only peacefully sleep when it killed every single demon who had caused a commotion and awakened it. Thinking so, it jumped into action. It trampled, thrashed, hurtled, and blew out air that was thousands of degrees hot onto everything nearby. The demons were killed in groups like swarms of fruit flies being exterminated with pesticides. The whole land was mixed with violent noises and hurried screams, which were then combined with savage roars and painful screeching. However, unfortunately, they failed to run away because, in the first place, the construction had taken off in a vast open plain. There was no hiding place nor escape route.

“Grr! Grr!”

The Lava Giant grew more fierce, and now, it looked like the giant was having fun massacring the demons. But had it not been for Javier, who stood in its way, the giant would not have stopped the slaughter until thousands more were killed.

“Grr?”

The giant was enjoying its killing spree when it suddenly paused attacking at the sight of a mysterious creature. 

“Grrr…?”

A stranger with an ant-like silhouette appeared out of nowhere. It seemed slightly smaller than even the smallest demons. But this creature’s face was amazingly intriguing. Its skin wasn’t red. And instead of a horn or its head, there was silvery fur. The creature dared to look up back at him with its blue eyes. And so, the Lava Giant thought…

“Grrr!”

What a strange-looking demon! What would it feel like to smash that with my palm? The giant didn’t hesitate. Fwoosh! The giant acted and struck Javier with its giant palm, and just then, his sword shone.

Fwish! Without any other noise or fancy rays of light, the long sword briefly flashed in the air and stirred the space with unbelievably mediocre skill. But next, the giant saw the horrible scene of its palm getting sliced into dozens of pieces. Thwack!

“Grr…!”

Dozens of aura strands bloomed along the path his sword swung in. It then engulfed the space like a brilliant net and slayed the giant’s hand into dozens of pieces. The arm, leg, and upper torso weren’t an exception.

“G-grr!”

Thwack! Pow! Thwamp! Its upper torso was sliced into hundreds of pieces, lava gushing out of the wound like blood. And then… everything coagulated back again.

“Grr!”

Whoosh! The chopped upper torso was restored in a snap. The giant rose as if it wasn’t damaged at all. And with a nonchalant gaze, it bent down toward Javier. Javier’s brows quaked. His sword moved once again. Thwaaack! His sword once again sliced its upper torso, slaying and cutting. But each time, the giant came back to its original shape. Unharmed, it roared.

“Grrragh!”

Lloyd, who was watching the sight from afar, swallowed.

Shoot. What is that?

He was 984 feet off the ground on Ggoming’s back, and he clenched his fists, recalling the sight a while ago. He and Javier were cleaning up the site just a few miles from the place of the incident. He was in a super mood because the job had gone well for the day, and the tracks were functioning very smoothly. That was when a sinister din came, the kind one you hear when a volcano erupts. He turned, saw the whole scene, and wanted to deny what he was seeing.

That giant was raising hell.

Since the construction site was mostly done on a flatland, it granted Lloyd the whole view of the violent giant even a few miles away. From the moment he learned what was happening, he led Ggoming to fly at the utmost speed and saw Javier jumping off to the ground first and stopping the giant with his stormy aura. And he also saw the monster casually restoring its shape.

I think this situation is a bit hopeless.

Lloyd looked below as he nibbled his lips. The Lava Giant and Javier were in a fierce battle, spewing lava. 

Slash! Swoosh!

Dozens of aura strands flashed each time Javier moved, and the giant’s body was sectioned into hundreds of pieces. But only to be pieced back together.

It’s like slicing water with a knife.

Except that the giant wasn’t water but more like a boiling soup or curry. Truly, the body of the Lava Giant was like soup or curry. And so, Javier’s powerful and perfect aura didn’t do any damage. No matter how many times he sliced and cut, its mushy body just went back together. It was like slicing a hot soup or curry. No damage could be done.

This isn’t a matter of strength.

Lloyd frowned, his forehead wrinkling.

Javier’s strong. He’s a grandmaster. And the Lava Giant is getting beaten so badly that it can’t even lay its hands on Javier. Still, things are bad even with those conditions. So, this is a matter of quality.

Repeatedly trying to slice into soup that couldn’t be sliced. Vigorously wielding a kitchen knife even if it wasn’t possible. That summed up the whole fight. In short, Javier was at a laughably bad disadvantage. He even thought that this was the fight that Javier could never win in the first place.

Ha…

Lloyd’s shoulders trembled and racked his brain after heaving a heavy sigh. He analyzed the situation, predicted results, and calculated the best outcome.

It can’t go on like this. We’re stuck. Javier can’t win. Never. The same goes for me. My mana blast and Supercharge won’t work. I don’t know how and where the Lava Giant came from, but if it keeps going berserk like this, the whole construction will go down the drain.

Lloyd accepted this. He didn’t overestimate his abilities. When he objectively judged the situation, he found it to be awfully despairing. But Lloyd didn’t give up. No, he didn’t want to give up.

What if I move the construction site? It sucks to just throw away everything we’ve been building here… But what if I build a new railroad on a different route?

The construction can resume, he thought. Though all the efforts spent in this one would be wasted, though he seriously teared up at the thought of the time and effort poured into surveying, designing, and constructing Lloyd wondered if a new start was the answer. A vague ray of hope appeared amid the blinding darkness. Just then, the Lava Giant roared aloud as it was getting beaten to a pulp.

“Garrrr! I will kill you all! With my rancor for not being able to fall asleep at your commotion! I will kill you all! That strange mound! I will follow you all even to the end of Hell and break everything!”

“…”

The moment Lloyd heard its roar, he thought he was screwed.

The monster was running amok because the noise from the construction site pissed it off? And so what? That strange mound? It’s going to follow us until the end and destroy everything?

Lloyd’s legs gave way. What else could it mean? Now that the construction and commotion had enraged the giant, it was bent on interfering and breaking everything, even if it required following to the end of Hell.

Ha. What even is my life?

His eyes were wet as he started to acutely feel his three months of work going down the drain. But he couldn’t think of another way to stop the giant.

I’m seriously stuck.

The solution to stop the Lava Giant didn’t come to him at all. Team up with Javier? Nothing seemed to change, even if he did. Mobilizing the fantastical creatures looked the same. So, this was a hopeless affair, and it was useless and moot to hold on to it. So the conclusion was…

Cutting it off is the fastest way to minimize my loss.

Lloyd sighed at length. He pulled himself together, telling himself that he should accept defeat this time. He led Ggoming to fly lower.

“Hey!” shouted Lloyd at Javier. “Dude! Let’s run away!”

Lloyd didn’t even wait for his response. This construction was a flop and no doubt about that. He could try out and build a new construction railroad somewhere else, but the Lava Giant would eventually arrive and turn things upside down like now. Constructing anything would be an impossibility. Holding on to it would only waste time without any fruit. And so, Lloyd, who decided to bear the loss, lifted his left hand and concentrated his mind. His mind went to the magic mark in his left hand, which Anticus had explained about the safety measure before he got here.

Was this how I can open the exit from Hell? I think.

Then he activated the Asrahan Core Technique and concentrated the mana on his left hand. It started to tickle, and a magic circle, which was marked in the left hand like a transparent tattoo, flashed to shoot out a strand of mana in the air.

Ziiing! Clash! Slash! The strand of mana created a circle in the air, and with a crackle, the circle widened to 16 feet in diameter. This was the Hell gate Anticus had provided Lloyd to escape out of Hell. It was the only passage that connected the realms between Hell and the human world where the Lorasia continent was. As he opened it up, Lloyd thought to himself…

I think the dragon said that the area in my world that connects to Hell gate is random. I pray that it’s near the Frontera fiefdom.

Lloyd was exhausted now. The construction in Hell was on the brink of failure, and the contract with the Hell of King was over. And he won’t be able to release the soul of the ancient dragon, and it’d be impossible to attain membership in the Dragon King club. He was drained. Empty. Now, Lloyd just wanted to rest.

Right. Like I’d be able to enjoy the assistance from the Dragon King and two lives. I should just take good care of my one life and live well in comfort.

Lloyd decided that he should be content with what he has now. Stop now, and rest up. And so, now that he was escaping out of Hell, he wished that he’d arrive at a place near his fiefdom to get home and take a break even a tiny second faster. Wishing to enjoy a carefree, peaceful, and comfortable life a tiny bit earlier, Lloyd stared at the Hell gate. At the same time, the gate was completed.

Spark! Whoosh! A spark crackled. Wind from the human world blew through the gate. The wind felt like 14 Fahrenheit (-10 ºC).

Whoosh!

Eek…?

The gust of freezing wind blew so suddenly that Lloyd hugged his clothes tighter.

What the heck?

Feeling dismayed, Lloyd tossed his glance to the other end of the gate. He then saw the scene inside the Hell gate.

That’s…

There was a sea. But not just any kind of sea. The whole water was frosty with a sheet of slushy ice on top. And there were glaciers floating around. So, that was…

A polar region?

Lloyd had opened the Hell gate in his desperate attempt to run away. When Lloyd made sense of the scene…

“Grrr! Achew!” For the first time, the Lava Giant, which was going berserk, sneezed helplessly as its whole body gave a terrible shiver.
