# The Aotearoa’s Blueprint (1)

---

Crash! Lloyd’s world quaked as the impact immediately threw him into a dizzy stagger. He couldn’t tell if he was underwater, on top of land, or up in the sky. Everything was a blur. Lloyd curled up amid the devastating impact, chaos, and confusion. And he behaved just like the first time he let himself get blackout drunk after a night of binge-drinking at his college orientation.

Blergh! He dry-heaved with his empty stomach. It wasn’t a surprising response from him.

Ughh… I almost passed out.

Lloyd looked up after just barely being able to suppress his mild concussion and dizzying nausea. He saw a giant silhouette right in front of him. No, it was a mountain, only that it was covered with a thick outer skin instead of rocks, stones, and soil. Its body diameter extended over several miles long and was as vast as Mount Everest. It was the Kraken, the giant octopus, convulsing before Lloyd’s eyes and retching like Lloyd was a moment ago.

“Krung! Blergh!”

When he saw the Kraken, a thought crossed his mind.

I’ve gone mad. Completely mad.

No matter how critical the situation was earlier, he still couldn’t believe he rammed into this massive creature. Lloyd was almost certain that he was a madman.

But still, I had my reasons for trying. For one, its skin isn’t hard.

Lloyd recalled the trail of events that took place earlier. The operation ended in success after the merpeople pricked all eight of the octopus’s giant legs in almost one try and caused dark blood to gush out of them. In response, the Kraken gave a deep and resounding burp.

But the burp wasn’t enough. It was about two percent short of what it needed to be.

That was the thing with a sudden, acute upset stomach. It was tenacious. You could take digestive medicine, prick your finger, and then have your back smacked by your mom after she finds out your test scores. Or, you could do a handstand and bow down to your boss after sending a wrong text to them. But it never, ever went away.

The Kraken was in that exact state.

It wasn’t enough to prick the Kraken’s legs and make it burp. The effect of this plan was only temporary. Lloyd, thus, took action the moment he realized what was happening. Since he had no time to waste, he used the power of two consecutive volcanic explosions from Bangul and his triple manablast, catapulting the attacks between the Kraken’s brows. In comparison, this was the same area as the upper abdomen for humans. In other words, what Lloyd did was equivalent to throwing an almighty punch to someone’s upper abdomen. And the punch now left the Kraken squirming and emptying everything in its stomach.

Great! See? That’s what I’m talking about! Vomiting is the best treatment for an acute case of an upset stomach if pricking your finger doesn’t work.

Even though forced vomiting could cause reflux esophagitis, it was a last-ditch effort to treat an upset stomach when nothing else worked. It had to be done even when stomach acid could damage a patient’s esophagus as well as teeth and take a serious toll on their stomach.

That was the reason why Lloyd had gone on with it. But prior to doing so, he first scanned the Kraken’s upper abdomen to guarantee his own safety and examine how sturdy and firm the area was. He even activated his simulation skill afterward. In other words, Lloyd boldly threw himself into action only after his simulation assured him no serious damage would be done to him. But, of course, Lloyd was not left entirely unharmed.

Argh. I feel nauseous.

Was it a mild concussion? His dizziness and nausea continued to assault him. However, soon, a reward that would easily make up for his pain emerged in front of his eyes.

Ding Dong!

[You have provided excellent treatment for the Kraken’s upset stomach with your sharp situational awareness, sound judgment and courageous actions.]

[For this, the treated Kraken and merpeople praise you, and they are extremely impressed by you.]

[You have earned a new title.]

Ohh?!

It was a message Lloyd would never get tired of seeing. Lloyd determined that this issue had been successfully resolved before focusing on his new title and relishing his reward.

[A new title <Upper abdomen! Upper abdomen! Upper abdomen!> has been created.]

[Upper abdomen! Upper abdomen! Upper abdomen!!]

[Title Level: Maritime legend]

[Mother, my stomach feels sick.

Is that so? Give me your stomach, my dear.

But Mother, why?

Your stomach will feel better after a hit.

I feel that it will not just make me comfortable but put me in a permanent state of sleep.

It’s all right, baby. Do not be afraid.

But still, I’m scared it will hurt. I’m scared.

Tsk, tsk. Learn from the Kraken.

What about the Kraken?

It was hit in the stomach and is still well alive.

But still, it doesn’t mean that getting hit won’t hurt.

Then do you want to keep having an upset stomach?

Um… No.

So, bring over your stomach to me.

But, mother! I am scared!

It’s all right, baby. How hard could I possibly even hit, my dear?

Waa… It’s the punch of… Love!

Wham.]

[Compliment Effect: You have resolved the Kraken’s indigestion in one try. The Kraken and thousands of members of the Mermaid Corps clearly witnessed this. For this, your heroic feat will spread throughout all the seas in the world, and getting hit in the upper abdomen will become the go-to method among marine creatures to treat indigestion. Moreover, you have acquired <Underwater Breathing> and <Upper Abdomen Jab>. The former skill allows you to freely breathe underwater, and the latter skill enables you to empty anyone’s stomach in the water with just one jab.]

[Title Region: Any terrain that has a water level of above 20 inches, which includes seas, rivers, lakes, and bathtubs.]

[Active Period of Title: 3,000 Years.]

[A title’s powers can be activated for 24 hours in the region for which the compliment was given. Moreover, the time and region can expand or shrink depending on your actions.]

[Monthly CP Earned from the Title: 5]

[Current CP: 1,312]

Nice. Super nice.

Lloyd clenched his fists. The title was awesome, just like the others. And it was a confirmation that the Kraken was no longer suffering from an upset stomach. The optional skills he was rewarded were extremely useful too.

Underwater breathing? This means I can freely breathe three-foot below the water without this necklace the merpeople gave me. Of course, the water pressure and temperature will be a different story. But I won’t drown, at least. And as for the upper abdomen jab… Um, I suppose it’s a useful skill to empty the stomach of someone I don’t like.

In any case, Lloyd was able to acquire two ever-so-useful powers.

Okay, I should back off now.

A thought crossed Lloyd’s mind that he might face a terrible ending if he hovered around any longer. It was because of the Kraken.

I gave a pretty hard punch to this monster. I mean, it even vomited.

Only the heavens knew what kind of payback the Kraken would unleash against Lloyd in its anger. If it came charging at Lloyd with its body that was the size of Mount Everest, he wasn’t confident he could handle that honestly. He wondered if even Javier, who was a grandmaster, could block the octopus. Just then…

Ding Dong!

Lloyd was sneakily watching the Kraken’s movement for any chance to escape when a clear ringing noise rang again.

Нmm?

At first, he wasn’t sure what he was hearing, so he reflexively moved his gaze to find a set of unexpected messages floating above.

[The Kraken has opened its heart to you.]

[It took you as the true friend for treating its indigestion.]

[The Kraken now considers you as a friend on equal footing.]

[You can now increase your likeability through the Kraken and acquire RP.]

[50 RP has been rewarded for forming a new connection with another creature.]

[Current RP: 2,532]

[Your likeability with the Kraken has increased by 11 points.]

[Current RP with the Kraken: +11]

[You have earned 33 RP by improving your relationship with a character.]

[Current RP: 2,565]

Damn.

His likeability with the Kraken? Lloyd never thought this would be possible. Just then…

“Krung! Krurung!”

The Kraken roared in almighty ferocity under Lloyd’s nose, rolling its eyes over to his side. Its eyeballs, Lloyd thought, were scarily huge.

“…”

Have you ever received the gaze of football-field-sized eyeballs? For a second, Lloyd was glad that he could now give a confident “yes” to the question, should it appear in a job interview. If the interviewer further asked what it felt like, then he’d say, “I wet myself within a second.” That was how overwhelming this situation was. But could it be that the Kraken recognized Lloyd’s feelings? It extended, very carefully and slowly, a leg in his direction.

“…”

Lloyd almost screamed, “I’m sorry! I won’t eat octopus legs anymore!” as a giant shadow cast over his head. The second before he gave way to pressure and burst into an inconsequential gibberish, an unexpected object stuck to one of the Kraken’s suckers caught his eyes.

A sculpture?

It was an approximately ten-foot-long seahorse sculpture made of glimmering emerald. The sculpture was vibrating terribly, much like a phone buzzing from a misplaced call on a lazy Sunday morning.

That vibration feels familiar. Why is that?

Lloyd thought to himself as he looked at the sculpture.

The foundation site vibrated exactly like that when Mr. Rotorua touched the starfish symbol. And the surrounding seawater resonated with the vibration.

The sculpture appeared to be vibrating similarly to the resonation a while ago. No, its intensity and pattern were almost the same. A theory popped into Lloyd’s head.

Wait a minute!

Lloyd immediately asked the Kraken, “Did you vomit this out?”

“Krung!” The Kraken roared.

“Is your stomach all right now that you spat this out?” Lloyd followed up with another question.

“Krurung!” The Kraken nodded, having understood Lloyd’s words. Then it added, “Krurung! Krung! Krurung! Krung!”

The Kraken was saying that it had accidentally engulfed the sculpture about 300 years ago. It was an accidental mistake that occurred when it curiously discovered something glinting in the deep sea. And it had forgotten that this happened as nothing particular took place afterward. Of course, Lloyd didn’t understand any of the things the Kraken was saying. But he was able to make out the meaning behind the octopus’s words through its actions.

So, it felt fine after spitting out the sculpture… That’s it. The sculpture was vibrating inside the Kraken, which upset its stomach. That was the cause behind its sudden upset stomach.

The blow sent to its upper abdomen forced the sculpture out, which relieved its stomach pain.

Plus, the vibration in the sculpture is the same as the one from the foundation site. The pattern and the intensity. That could only mean one thing…

The object had much to do with the Jewel of Truth. Convinced, Lloyd asked, “Um, are you showing me this… because you’re giving it to me?”

“Krung!” When the answer came right away, Lloyd immediately snatched the sculpture before the Kraken changed its mind.

Pop! Bzzzt! The sculpture left the sticky sucker and landed in his embrace.

Whew…

Lloyd wondered if this was what it felt like to carry a 10-foot mobile phone put on the highest vibration setting. He struggled to fight the dizziness and nausea surging from his stomach and hurriedly swam his way back to the foundation site.

If my guess is correct, this sculpture should be part of the site of the jewel.

So, it should be placed in its rightful place. Then a secret of some sort had to be revealed. From then on, Lloyd tirelessly swam and left the merpeople gathering up to check on the Kraken. Javier and Rotora soon joined Lloyd, and all of them went back to the jewel’s site. As they approached, the vibration in the sculpture amplified. Bzzzzzz!!!

Ack!!

The concussion was already making him nauseous and dizzy, and the sculpture’s vibration was making it all feel worse for him. Lloyd swallowed the bile that kept rising above his throat.

From his side, Javier worryingly asked, “Are you all right, Young Master?”

“Do I look okay in your eyes?” Lloyd’s voice dripped with annoyance and exhaustion.

Javier ignored Lloyd’s unwelcoming tone as he replied. “Um, you seem fine and not fine at the same time.”

“What is that supposed to mean?” snapped Lloyd, frowning.

“It means that I am worried and amused at the same time.”

“…”

“Give me your back, please,” Javier, who was gloating with his eyes, reached out and placed his hand on Lloyd’s back. Is he using the Asrahan Core Technique? Lloyd thought to himself as he felt a warm influx of mana, which traveled through his skin, muscle, spine, nerves, and blood vessels, slowly circling around his whole body until it flowed out to Javier’s palm through his back. The mana felt like a warm breath of air on a painful injury. Soon, his dizziness and nausea completely disappeared.

“You should feel better now, Master Lloyd. Your complexion has improved.” Javier grinned. And he added, “Your face is mediocre to begin with, so you should at least have a healthy complexion.”

“Sure… I’m tearing up at your kindness,” replied Lloyd.

“Not at all.”

“Haah. You treated my body, but you’re killing my spirit. It feels great for you, right?”

“Yes, indeed.”

“The heck. You aren’t even trying to deny it.” Lloyd shot a flustered look at Javier.

Javier’s smile grew bigger. “It is enjoyable to watch your flustered expression that appears in moments like this.”

“…”

“You do not have to compensate me for treating you. You amused me well enough.”

“Ha. Hahaha.” Lloyd burst out in laughter. Still, he figured that this was Javier’s way of showing concern.

Anyhow, time to get back to business.

Lloyd then focused on the sculpture in his embrace.

Bzzzzz!! The vibration was much stronger now, and he focused his attention on its intensity.

On my way here, the vibration was consistent in its intensity, but it changed a little now.

The vibration intensity was changing depending on his direction around the jewel lot. This indicated only one thing.

The vibration is getting stronger as we get closer to the spot it should be placed at.

From that moment, Lloyd directed his steps following the vibration of the sculpture, approaching confidently and wading through the current until he found the right location.

This is the spot.

About 20 yards south of the jewel’s foundation site, there was a hole that was about 27 inches in diameter. The sculpture’s stylobate was shaped exactly like the hole.

Could this be the entrance to the jewel?

It appeared so. Thinking so, Lloyd put down the sculpture.

Swoosh! The bottom smoothly went into the hole, instantly fading the vibration of the jewel and foundation to a stop. From that moment onward…

Crackle…! The sculpture was covered with a glistening light that dizzyingly reflected inside its emerald interior. The light streamed out from the eyes and sketched a three-dimensional painting of light in the water. Elegant lines and surfaces. An elaborate dance of light. Finally, the blueprint of the Jewel of Truth emerged, and Lloyd murmured to himself when he realized it looked surprisingly familiar.

What is that? A pyramid? The Tomb of the General?
