# Third Ending Spoiler (2)

---

Internet, the gigantic network that covered the entire world. It was a convenience that everyone in the 21st century enjoyed, much like breathing. Even at this very moment, there were probably countless people casually opening their laptops or using their smartphones. Some would be doing so to work, to study, or even to just kill time and have fun. The internet was as vast as the Pacific Ocean, storing millions of pieces of information. At any time, it had the power to draw in countless people while unknowingly making them develop turtleneck syndrome. That was what the Internet was. And now, there was one man in the Frontera study who wretchedly missed this convenience of modern civilization on Earth.

“Argh…” Lloyd groaned at length as he rested his chin in his hands, at the same time mechanically opening another book. He lost count of how many books he had read but was sure it was over a hundred.

This is nuts, really.

Lloyd shook his head hard in an effort to shake off his drowsiness. And then he glanced at the pile of books next to him and let out a sigh.

I’ve stayed up all night for the past four days, and there’s still so much left to read.

He seemed to have around 50 more books to read through. Most of them were philosophical books on fate and magic. Some even dealt with astrology, astronomy, spirits, myths, and regional folklore. What was he doing now? Lloyd was devouring every book that could give him a clue to the future he had glimpsed through the Ending Spoiler.

But it’s not like I can skim them.

Indeed he couldn’t. In all honesty, he wanted to roughly skim through all the pages, which was impossible. These books were different from modern South Korean books, which were neatly designed and arranged in clear paragraphs and pages. Back home, writings with poor readability were rejected by customers and didn’t make any sales, whether it was an essay, self-help book, or magazine. And so, every book was designed to provide a hassle-free reading experience.

But the books of this world were very inconsiderate and far from being readable. Since there was no advanced printing technology, the books were transcribed by hand. Some of them were even written in such a tiny font that Lloyd had to use magnifying glasses.

These books are totally inconsiderate of their readers!

What was more, all the books dealt with new topics for him, so he feared that he’d miss a vital piece of information if he skimmed through them. He was practically forced to carefully study all the materials. It was an exhausting job.

Internet. I need the Internet.

Lloyd missed this modern convenience he once enjoyed in South Korea. What would it have been like if the Lorasian Continent had the internet? He would have been able to search up the destiny he had changed and the series of accidents that followed afterward. Or, at least, he would have been able to ask questions on an online forum.

That would have been a thousand times better than burying myself in books for four days straight. I’d have gotten more information or clues about what I wanted to know.

Thump. Lloyd closed the book. He lifted his head, feeling numb.

I know I like to punish myself, but this won’t do.

Ransacking books to find a clue for the explanation behind everyone’s impending misfortunes, which he foresaw through the Ending Spoiler, appeared unwise. He became surer of it when he thought about the internet search system on Earth.

Working my fingers to the bone isn’t going to cut it. It’d just be a waste of time.

That was his conclusion after spending four stressful days of research. The reason was simple.

One. There are too many books, and I don’t know what information they will hold. So I have to manually read through all of them one by one. But that has proved to be a slow process and takes an absurd amount of time.

Lloyd had spent four whole days stuck in the library except for the times he went outside to wake Verkis. He even tried his surveying skill to see if it worked here, but it didn’t. The skill could only find the thickness and width of the paper pages, not the content of the books. As such, he got to work doing nothing but reading for four days straight. However, he was only able to get through about 100 books despite his efforts.

And if I continue what I’m doing now… How much more time will I need?

And what if he continued spending his days reading? It would take 30 days to go through 1,000 books and 300 days to read 10,000 books.

By then, I’d have browsed through all of the relevant materials available in the Central Library in the palace.

But Lloyd was skeptical about investing 300 days in this task.

Will I be able to find key information by spending 300 days reading every single book in the Central Library?

He wasn’t sure. What if he found nothing? Then he’d be wasting 300 precious days.

Even if I find any relevant resources or information, there’s another problem.

There was more he needed to do on top of looking for relevant resources and information. He had to collect and analyze the gathered materials and cross-check whether the conclusion drawn from them was right. It was only then that he would be able to come up with a solution.

That’s the problem. I don’t have enough time.

Lloyd recalled the journal he saw through the Ending Spoiler. The count’s horseback riding accident was the very first mishap. And that’s only three years from now.

I would need to find relevant materials, analyze them, and draw a conclusion. Three years will pass by in a flash while I go through all these steps. That won’t do. By the time I have a solution, the accident would have already happened. It’d be too late.

Lloyd shook his head. When he did the math, the answer became clear to him. He would waste far too much time searching for information in books.

I need another method that doesn’t require me to go through everything by hand. Oh, how I wish I had access to the internet–the place where I can upload questions for knowledgeable people to answer. It’s so convenient.

Wait a second. There is a god-like being who has the answer to everything here too!

Lloyd stopped as he sighed, his eyes flying open. That was right. Come to think of it, there was one. Moreover, they were very close by!

The Dragon King!

Lloyd jumped to his feet.

I was an idiot! I hadn’t considered that this entire time. The Dragon King may know something about those incidents.

A possibility appeared, and come to think of it, there was someone else other than Verkis who could possibly answer his questions.

And if I can’t get any satisfactory answer from the Dragon King… Then I should ask the King of Hell.

He knew his way to Hell. The Hell gate was still open in the northern region, and he could enter and fly right toward the Hell Castle.

Though, of course, I’ll have to pay a fair price to hear a response from the King of Hell.

It was possible the king would demand a fairly high price. So Lloyd decided to ask Verkis first since the latter wouldn’t demand payment. Furthermore, it was the perfect time to do so as the sun was about to rise soon.

Afterward, Lloyd took a short nap, resting his head against a book he was reading. Soon, when the sun rose, he jumped up and hurried to the annex, where he woke Verkis as usual. “Um, my king, it’s time to wake up.”

“Ah…” mumbled Verkis sleepily.

“I will start singing if you don’t wake up.”

“Hey, I’m awake.”

“Congratulations,” said Lloyd, “You woke up right away.”

Yawn. Verkis half-opened his groggy eyes, still heavy with sleep. And he drew a magic circle in the air and tried to connect to his wife. But the result was the same as the previous day.

Bzzt!

“Ha..”

The same today. She rejected his call. It looked like she was still upset. Thinking she would eventually come around as long as he kept trying, he lay back on the bed.

In any case, I’m done for the day!

The thought excited him. He covered himself with the blanket with an excited heart, but something strange happened.

“Hey… Why are you still here?” Verkis muttered

Strangely, unlike usual, Lloyd was still in his room. Normally, the man would quietly leave after saying he would return again the next day while Verkis prepared to fall back asleep. But today, he wasn’t doing that. Instead, he was looking at him with a strange gaze.

“Hey, what are you doing?” blurted Verkis again. “Why are you staring at me with a pleading face?”

“Um, may I ask you something?” said Lloyd at last.

“No, you can’t.”

“…”

“I’m off to sleep.” Snore!

“…”

Verkis fell back asleep right after he finished talking. Dumbstruck, Lloyd tried to wake the king but failed. He even audaciously poked the king’s shoulder, danced, and sang like a nutcase. Verkis was unperturbed. However, Lloyd wasn’t willing to give up so easily.

I can’t give up! If I can’t get an answer here, I have to travel all the way down to Hell.

He was hesitant about traveling down to Hell, particularly as Hellkaros wasn’t the type to do anything for free. Lloyd could be forced to pay a huge sum of money to obtain a single piece of information.

If possible, it’d be best if I can get help from the dragon king.

From that morning on, a match began between Lloyd and Verkis.

“Dragon King Verkis!” yelled Lloyd. “I have a question for you!”

“Sure, see you tomorrow.” Snore!

“…”

Verkis happily went back to sleep. And each morning, every time he woke the dragon king, Lloyd persistently asked him a question, never giving up.

“Dragon King Verkis!” yelled Lloyd. “Does altering the original path of fate influence the present time?”

“I have no clue. See you tomorrow.” Snore!

“…”

His question was ignored once again. Regardless, a new day began the next morning.

“Dragon King Verkis!” yelled Lloyd. “I’ve come from another dimension and somehow ended up changing the fate of this world!”

“Oh, did you?” whispered Verkis.

“Yes!”

“All right. See you tomorrow.” Snore!

“…”

A new day arrived once again.

“Dragon King Verkis!” yelled Lloyd. “I’ve learned that tragedy will strike, and all the people who were supposed to be dead in the original novel but survived because of me will be killed!”

“I see. You are very impressive.”

“Thank you so much!”

“Okay. See you tomorrow then.” Snore!

“…”

As expected, the next morning came soon enough.

“Dragon King Verkis!” yelled Lloyd. “Do you remember the questions I’ve been asking you?”

“No, I don’t. See you tomorrow.” Snore!

“…”

And the sun rose each day without fail.

“Dragon King Verkis!” begged Lloyd. “Please give me an answer now!”

“I don’t want to. I’m sleeping. See you tomorrow.” Snore!

“…”

Nature brought yet another morning again.

“Dragon King Verkis!” bawled Lloyd. “If you don’t give me an answer today, then I’ll wake you up with a kiss!”

“You have finally lost it,” Verkis replied as his eyes grew wide.

“Yes, I have!”

“…”

“So could you answer my question please?” begged Lloyd.

“Hm, no. I don’t plan on giving you an answer. Go away now.”

“But you’ve just answered a question from me!”

Lloyd glued himself to the side of the bed and pitifully stared at Verkis in desperation. Verkis was shocked.

Should I kill him?

Verkis considered it for a second and wondered if Lloyd had truly lost it. But he decided not to kill him and sighed at length.

I shouldn’t kill him. I’m supposed to receive help from him. I said I’d stay in this fiefdom in return for him waking me up every morning. It was a promise I made.

He couldn’t break it. What would happen if he went back on his word? He would risk losing his power as a dragon.

I can’t kill him. Should I just shut his mouth with magic? No. Then he won’t be able to wake me up properly. Tsk. This is bothersome.

Verkis was extremely bothered by Lloyd, who had been throwing weird questions at him for the past few days. He couldn’t get any peskier. But at the same time, Verkis couldn’t ignore him indefinitely as it was obvious that Lloyd was hellbent on badgering him without giving up.

I’ll just tell him whatever comes to my mind.

Verkis finally figured that Lloyd would eventually call it quits to his incessant questions then. That seemed to be a better scenario. Having reached such a conclusion, Verkis asked, “So you’re from another dimension?”

“Yes, I am.”

“And this world was a novel in your dimension?”

“Yes, I am telling the truth.”

“Okay. You don’t need to convince me. In the first place, the dimension you’re from is nothing but a piece of hard drive saved in a storage of another dimension.”

“Excuse me…?”

“What are you getting surprised for? That’s the way it is with dimensions. Anyway, to sum up your questions, you came from another dimension, and you’ve altered the original destiny of this world. Am I right?”

“Yes, my lord.”

“However, the people you saved in that process will all lose their lives at some point in the future. And you want to prevent that from happening. Right?”

“Yes. That is exactly what I mean.”

“Then it’s simple,” Verkis smirked. “Have you ever heard of the phenomenon called ‘The Restoration of Destiny’?”

Even the Dragon King surrendered to Lloyd’s persistence, which had at last earned Llyod a reward equal to getting a question answered on an online forum.
