# Third Ending Spoiler (1)

---

What… is this?

Lloyd tightened the muscles around his eyes to open them wide but failed. It was obvious why he couldn’t do it. He was watching the ending spoiler for himself, and only his consciousness entered his body nearing its death. As such, he couldn’t freely move his future body. He was merely a direct observer, watching the future unfold before his eyes.

His gaze landed on an old note, the cover of which was unfamiliar, titled “The Records of Lloyd Frontera.” It was a journal.

I’m keeping a journal?

Lloyd curiously tilted his head in wonder as he had never had a habit of journaling. The only times he jotted down in a diary were when his elementary school teacher gave it as an assignment and when he was forced to keep one in the army.

That hasn’t changed. I don’t journal.

Could it be that he picked up the habit in the future? How accustomed could he have become that he would continue to do it in the face of death?

Still, I don’t think this is a bad thing.

Lloyd chuckled. He was surprised at first at the unexpected scene. But on second thought, this wasn’t a bad sight to see. He actually quite liked what he saw.

The fact I’d keep a journal just goes to show how relaxed and sweet my life must have become.

He must have been stuck in a groove. Bored. That must be how peaceful and problem-free his life was. Otherwise, there was no chance he’d cultivate a journaling habit. Lloyd felt relieved at that thought. But that relief was short-lived, cracked and interrupted by a cough from his future self.

Cough! Cough! Spurt! Lloyd erupted into a terrible coughing fit, drops of dark blood splattering onto his diary. He raised his quivering hand and wiped them off the paper with his ragged and muddied sleeve.

Eh? Hold on.

Lloyd’s heart sank.

What’s wrong with my sleeve?

His gaze hurried toward his sleeve, wiping the blood off his journal. It was originally a brightly colored shirt, but now, it was covered in gray and yellow stains, making him wonder when it was last washed. Plus, the fabric was completely frayed and the button on it had long fallen off.

What the heck? Why do my clothes look like this when I should be living a comfortable life?

Something was wrong. His heart started to pound. He didn’t want to believe it, but a sense of nervousness started to grip him, and his gaze hurried once more.

Hold on. What about my hand?

Just then, he recalled the second time he used the Ending Spoiler option. Since he was only an observer who peeked through the future with the eyes of his future self, Lloyd couldn’t get a good look at his appearance unless he was standing in front of a mirror. As such, the most convenient method for him to check his age was by looking at his hands.

A-and my hands?

Lloyd looked down at his hands inside the sleeves. And his heart sank once again.

I have no… wrinkles.

His hands were smooth. They were slightly rough but had no wrinkles. He couldn’t spot any dark spots that came with old age either.

So, I’m not dying of old age. What is going on here?

Nervous, Lloyd decided to keep watching and observe the situation. Meanwhile, his future self picked up a pen once he stopped coughing and opened his journal with trembling hands. This moment granted Lloyd the opportunity to peek at the writing inside.

Flip.

[This is the first time I have chosen to write in a journal. I am sad. The count got into an accident. He was simply enjoying horseback riding like usual. He was riding through a path he often enjoyed taking. Who would’ve imagined that a weasel would appear and surprise the horse into suddenly jumping? Who would’ve imagined the count would fall off and hit a tree branch on the neck? The physician says we should wait and see, but… I’m not so sure. I’m just sad.]

Flip.

[The count woke up after a day. He says he can’t feel anything below his neck. But he forced a smile and tried to calm everyone down. He said that he was fine. That it doesn’t hurt because he can’t feel anything. That he’s comfortable since he’s on his back all day. He flashed a bright smile. And those were his last words. Right now, the countess is quietly weeping in secret. To be honest, I want to cry too. However, I should go out and comfort her first.]

Lloyd glanced at the journal, feeling utterly incredulous. The pages of his journal turned one after another.

[The countess doesn’t talk anymore. She doesn’t say a word. She no longer smiles and tends to her garden. I picked up the trowel for her lest the overlooked flowers wilt. I hope the freesias bloom beautifully this year and lessen her grief.]

Flip.

[The freesias have bloomed pretty. But the countess can’t see them. In the morning, I knew that something was wrong when I heard Emily scream. No one would have thought that the kitchen once filled with everyone’s laughter would be where she passed. No one knew until last night. Could it be that she missed the count, who passed away before her? I must call Julian. He should be in the capital. Now, our manor, once warm, feels cold and desolate.]

Flip.

[Julian isn’t responding to me. I can’t arrange the funeral for the countess until he arrives. I suppose he’s hurrying back home now instead of spending time sending me a response like he did when the count fell ill. I sent Ggoming, so he should be here soon. That’s what I hope at least.]

Flip.

[There’s a response. But it was bad news. Not from Julian. But by Sheherazade. She wailed that she shouldn’t have left the house. That she shouldn’t have left Julian alone. She said she never knew robbers would break in. No, had she gone home earlier, she’d have found him and taken him for treatment. Her letter breaking the bad news was filled with guilt and resentment. I feel numb. I can’t believe it. Julian was stabbed by a robber when he was alone in his house. This feels like a lie. I hope it’s all a lie.]

Flip.

[I don’t even know what I’ve been doing for the last month. In all honesty, I still feel numb. But I’m forcing myself to pick up a pen to leave a record of what is happening. After I held a funeral for the countess, I went to the capital and held a funeral for Julian as well. I wonder if I’ve become desensitized after going through a series of sad and tragic events. I don’t know. Now, this whole thing feels strange. The death of the count and countess, followed by Julian’s death. Is it weird that I think these situations are very similar to the novel? Something feels wrong. I’m nervous.]

What the heck? What is this?

Lloyd gritted his teeth, his gaze moving faster. He struggled to catch every word in the pages that slowly flipped one after another.

[It looks like the ominous feeling I had wasn’t baseless. I received a call from the royal palace. So I went and had an audience with the queen, but something felt wrong. Something was unnatural about the way she moved her left arm. After sending everyone away, I asked her carefully if there was something wrong with her left arm. She smiled bitterly, which confirmed my suspicions. Then I was hit with something at that very moment. The Knight of Blood and Iron. The attempt to assassinate the queen. The arm that had been sliced off was her left arm.]

Flip.

[I’m now in the fiefdom. It’s been a while. I tirelessly comforted Sheherazade while I was in the capital. I had a feeling that something would happen to her as well if my hunch was right. I pray that nothing will happen to her. I sincerely hope that my hunch is wrong.]

Flip.

[My heart is heavy. Shiloh and Meatloaf. The loan sharks that burdened my family. I received news that they’ve been murdered. A small scuffle went wrong, and they got stabbed in the neck. My heart sank when I heard the news. The count and countess. Julian. The two loan sharks. All of them were the people that died in the early part of the novel.]

Flip.

[The last few months have been a nervous ride. Thankfully, there weren’t any other major incidents or mishaps. But I feel desolate and nervous. I keep thinking about drinking these days. I shouldn’t act like this.]

Flip.

[A war broke out in the south. It’s nothing big – just a regional war. Well, that probably isn’t something I need to worry about. I should send a letter to Sheherazade and suggest that she move here.]

Flip.

[I haven’t received a response from her. Something’s off. I’m nervous.]

Flip.

[I received news about her. A notice stating that Sheherazade was killed in action. That was only when I was able to learn what happened. It turns out she volunteered for the war in the south the moment it broke out. Was it to vent off her steam after losing Julian? It’s said that Sheherazade recklessly charged ahead at the very frontline. At one point, she was desperately fighting after becoming isolated in enemy territory. That was said to be the last time she was seen. Now… I don’t know. The destinies I’ve changed with my hard work while living in this world. And the people who were able to avoid death because of my efforts. Each and every one of them is leaving my side one by one.]

Dammit.

Lloyd shivered in shock, but the pages kept flipping with more news of death. The sultan’s death after accidentally falling off from a height at a festival. Orc Arosh’s whole body became paralyzed from getting injured during a hunt. Even Lady Namaran, the leader of the Namaran fiefdom, got into an accident. She slipped on a busy city road with a steep slope and got crushed under a carriage. Her death was instantaneous.

What is this? Is fate pulling a prank on us?

Lloyd was dumbstruck. Did all of these things really happen? They were unbelievable, dumbfounding, and also disturbing at the same time. This was as he could see there was a commonality between all the incidents.

All of them die or get into an accident in the novel, but their destinies changed when I stepped into this world and saved them.

But every single one of them died similarly to the story in the novel.

What about me?

Just then, he remembered how Lloyd died in the novel. He became terribly sick from his heavy drinking and died in the corner of a pub, coughing out blood.

It can’t be.

Now, the journal was on its last page. Lloyd’s heart tensed as he looked at it. Meanwhile, at last, the future Lloyd shakily moved his hand to painstakingly fill up the last page with crooked letters.

[Now, I don’t know. I don’t know how this happened. Should I have stayed away from alcohol? No, I failed to protect anyone. The pain was unbearable. Now, I just want to… rest. I don’t know about a life of luxury anymore. My extra life? That turned out to be real. I came back alive a few days ago, but look at me now. Everything’s a struggle. I’m fatigued and worn out. It seems that fate isn’t something you can escape after all. What was it all for? I am really…]

At that moment…

“Cough…! Argh! Gargh! Arggh!”

His whole body convulsed vehemently as he wrote. He coughed like he was about to spit out his soul. But instead of his soul, something else shot out. Spurt!

‘…!’

Blood. A flood of dark blood came out of his mouth. It covered the entire page of the journal he was writing in.

Crash! Then he fell off the chair, and it was only then that the present Lloyd was able to see the room. It was a familiar place. A shabby room on the second floor of a bar in the fiefdom.

“Master Lloyd!”

Someone rushed into the room, and his silver hair was visible in between Lloyd’s fading vision.

“Javi…er.”

Javier’s panicked gestures toward him. That was the end.

Fwoosh! Everything darkened, and Lloyd felt his body float in the air for a moment. And he opened his eyes.

“…”

Swish. The curtain that had been frozen in place resumed swaying against the night air. That sight gave Lloyd a slight shiver. It was only when he turned and saw the window that he realized what happened.

I’m back.

The Ending Spoiler was over, and he was in his bedroom at the present time. But his heart continued to pound, refusing to calm down.

What did I just see?

He couldn’t believe it. But at the same time, he had to believe it.

The Ending Spoiler had never lied to him before. It was all thanks to this skill option that he could block the locust swarm’s attack during the Monster Domino incident and prevent a cholera outbreak when the fiefdom saw an influx of refugees coming in. So what he saw had to be true this time as well. Lloyd began falling into despair.

Those incidents weren’t merely coincidental.

Seated on the edge of the bed, Lloyd struggled to calm his breath while organizing his thoughts on the future events he read about in the journal.

I only saw a glimpse of it, but the count gets injured while horse-riding three years from now.

That marked the beginning of incidents. At first, Lloyd thought of it as an unfortunate accident, but that proved to be false as he continued reading the journal.

It can’t be a coincidence. What I read was far too sinister to just be mere accidents. Only those who were supposed to die in the novel but got saved by me started to die. As if someone cherry-picked them.

Worse, their manner of death shown in the Ending Spoiler was almost identical to the way they died in the original novel. It was the same case with Queen Magentano, whose left arm became paralyzed for an inexplicable reason.

While she loses an arm in the novel, her arm gets paralyzed here.

Given that the queen was a sword master, a paralyzed arm was the same as not having one. And what about him?

Those series of events sent me into a state of depression and made me look for alcohol. Then I got sick until I died wretchedly in the corner of a bar…

He died while vomiting a flood of blood from his mouth. Ultimately, he was set to meet the same ending as the character Lloyd Frontera.

Shoot. This is unbelievable.

Lloyd couldn’t help but curse. Could it be that fate was playing a joke on him? Or did this mean that fate wasn’t something he could change in the end? If so, was this an issue that couldn’t be solved at all?

No, I don’t think that’s the case.

Lloyd shook his head. Whether this was a cruel joke from fate or an inescapable reality, he figured that he had to try something first. It would be much too regrettable for him to give up so early.

Duh! I’ve worked hard to get where I am now!

It was only now that he had finally obtained everything he needed for a life of abundance and was ready to happily live in peace with the resources he collected one by one. But to think he would go through those cursed events. That wasn’t something he wanted. If this was how the story was meant to end, Lloyd wanted to grab the author by the collar and pummel him to the ground. And so…

There must be a cause that brings about this scandalous catastrophe. Whether it be destiny or anything else. There has to be a way I can alter or prevent it.

Lloyd pondered. The simplest solution would be to prevent the count from falling off his horse, which was the start of everything. But he shook his head.

No. No matter how I look at it, this isn’t just an unfortunate incident. So, that alone will not be enough. Something else will happen to the count even if I stop this one incident. In the end, a series of similar accidents will keep happening one after another.

What if these accidents were the price he had to pay for going against fate? What if fate was interfering now to lead all the events of this world to the original storyline? In other words, Lloyd thought that simply preventing the next incident from occurring wouldn’t be enough.

Of course, it could be a simple accident. But what if it isn’t? What if something horrible happens due to my lack of preparation? Then it’s over. I have to do something. First, I should expect the worst and find the root cause of the situation.

Lloyd reached that conclusion. The answer was simple. He had to find the root cause of all the incidents.

I don’t have much time.

Lloyd jumped out of bed and walked into the corridor in the middle of the night. At the same time, a thought started to form in his head. If all the events that took place until now were to overcome the threats toward the Frontera family, from now on, he was embarking on a journey to change his own destiny. Even so, however, he wasn’t discouraged.

What else can I do? I just have to go through with it.

Just like a mountain of assignments given by a merciless professor, this couldn’t be avoided. It was much like his days in the past when he had to work to make a living while completing all his school assignments. If there was something he had to face and get done, there was one move he made before anything else.

Let’s cut down on my sleep first.

Lloyd opted for the simplest and most standard decision he made during the roughest periods of life. If he had homework to finish or if he had to work part-time but lacked time, sacrificing his sleep was the only option. He could gain more time by pulling an all-nighter. From then on, much like the desperate days of his past, Lloyd started to burn the midnight oil in his study. Of course, he grumbled and complained about his life the entire time.

Crap! I wish there was Internet here!

To change the cursed future he got a glimpse of through the Ending Spoiler, Lloyd embarked on a laborious endeavor to search for a solution to change his tragic destiny.
