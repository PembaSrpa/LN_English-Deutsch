# The One Who Left and the One Who Stayed (1)

---

“This ticket you are planning to give me… Can a wandering ghost reincarnate if it uses this?”

“What is that?”

Lloyd’s question was unexpected. The King of Hell scowled before saying, “Indeed, it can. It can reincarnate. The pass was made to grant that privilege in the first place. But why do you ask?”

“Actually…” mumbled Lloyd before making the confession. “There is a ghost I would like to help.”

“A ghost you would like to help?” replied the King of Hell. “I wonder if it’s the one I am thinking of.”

“Do you have a guess for who it is?”

“Of course,” the King of Hell chuckled and laughed. “Suho Kim. You are about to help the ghost of Lloyd Frontera, who started to wander around Hell after you have taken your body. Is my assumption wrong?”

“No, you are right.”

Lloyd conceded without a fight. This matter required him to gain the king’s permission anyway. It wasn’t something for him to hide in the first place. He continued to speak.

“Your assumption is right. I wish to give this train ticket to the ghost.”

“Why?” asked the King of Hell with curiosity in his voice.

“Excuse me?”

“You must have a reason. You see, the ticket I am bestowing you is an extremely valuable gift. It offers the tremendous benefit of being unconditionally exempt from punishment in Hell. Even if you have committed hundreds of unforgivable crimes, you can avoid being punished in Hell and attain a new life. I am sure you know the value of it.”

“Yes, I am aware.”

“And yet, you plan on giving such an incredible gift to the ghost? Why?” demanded the king.

“Because…”

Lloyd sucked in his breath and reflected on himself. Honestly, he couldn’t pinpoint the reason himself. He simply followed his heart to make a decision, albeit a bit impulsively. It was a mystery to him even after he thought over his decision and reflected on himself.

“I don’t know,” said Lloyd at last.

“What? You do not know?” The king lifted his brow.

“Yes.”

Laughter escaped Lloyd as he answered. He really didn’t know the reason. But his heart was relieved when the decision was made. He wondered if he had lost his mind and just gave a chuckle instead.

“I really don’t know. I just made the decision to do so. It is that simple. Ah, of course, I do think it is a waste. As you see, there are tears in my eyes.” Lloyd approached closer.

“Hmm… Well…” the King of Hell observed. “You aren’t lying.”

“Yes, I do think I’m wasting this opportunity, and I ask myself why I am doing this. But even more strangely, I have no thoughts of changing my decision.”

“Suho Kim. You are more foolish than you look.”

“Yes,” answered Lloyd with a sigh. “I think I am. But now, my heart is at ease due to my stupidity at the moment.”

“Is that so?”

“Yes.”

It was truly a strange thing. He was giving away a tremendous gift without anything in return. Yet, his heart was light and filled with relief. And even though he said it was a wasted opportunity or whatever, truthfully speaking, he didn’t feel that way.

Tsk. It’s not because that hooligan Frontera made me weak, or because I feel guilty. I’m doing this because I’m tired of its whining. It is beyond annoying to hear Frontera whining, insisting, and begging for me to take responsibility and help out.

Lloyd thought this was an opportunity for him to get rid of one less burden of his. It was the cost of getting rid of this pesky burden. This was an investment. So, he concluded that he wasn’t being a fool. He was doing this for Arcos and Marbella Frontera. Lloyd reiterated these thoughts in his head and pulled himself together.

A bitter smile appeared on the King of Hell’s face. But his eyes grew soft.

Suho Kim.

The king concluded that Lloyd was a sly but not an evil person. His sly character was merely a result of trying to survive in extreme poverty.

I want him.

Lloyd was normally a stout-hearted man. However, in critical moments, he didn’t lose the humanity in him while also making sure to receive his proper dues. And so, the more the king saw of Lloyd, the more he wanted him.

This human. If he dies and ends up in Hell, I might as well just hire him and put him to work.

It looked like he wouldn’t need to share much of his authority with Lloyd. It seemed giving him discretionary power to act freely on his own would be enough. Delegating him that kind of power alone would…

He’d work the Knights and demons to death, just like he did while constructing the railroad.

The King of Hell was impressed with how Lloyd put 30,000 demons to work and wondered if this was what it felt like to be a grandfather who had a clever grandchild.

I will make you work under me once you die.

The King of Hell resolved his plan to scout Lloyd in the future and bent over before he spoke.

“Suho Kim. Are you really going to give up your Hell Express ticket for reincarnation to Ghost Frontera?”

“Yes, I will, my lord,” said Lloyd without any second of hesitation.

“Good. I think highly of your decision.”

“Thank you.”

“Do not be.”

The King of Hell smiled with a glow of satisfaction. Lloyd didn’t need the reincarnation ticket anyway since he would be mindlessly working next to him later on. Hiding his true motive, the king held out a piece of paper to Lloyd, the man in charge of the future in Hell.

“This pass bears my signature. Present it, and no one in Hell will ever stop you at the Door of Reincarnation,” instructed the King of Hell.

“Thank you.”

“Then, can the train be operated now?” asked the King of Hell.

“Oh, I just have to run a final test drive.”

“A test drive? Get it done quickly. I wish to get off work for the first time in 670,000 years.”

“Yes, my king.”

After paying his respect to the king, Lloyd removed himself from the office and headed to the Hell Express station at the Hell Castle. A prototype train, which was created for the test run, was there.

Woah. They built this better than I expected.

Lloyd had separately ordered the construction of the train while he focused on the construction of the railroad. Its structure was very primitive and simple. The massive carts that the demons used were connected in one line and their wheels were modified to fit the rails. Then bones and tarps were laid on top to protect everything from volcanic ash and flame sparks.

The train even utilized a manual engine. It moved by having eight demons at the very front car industriously pressing its lever and generating power through the pulley. And when the demons grew tired, another eight on standby would take their place.

So, the train adopted a bionic and eco-friendly power generation method, utilizing a total of 24 demons that were grouped by eight. That was the stateliness of the Hell Express. In other words, the Hell Express existed for the sole purpose of transporting souls to the edge of Hell.

“Don’t expect a comfortable ride from this.”

“…”

Inside the modified cabin that used to be a giant cart, Lloyd sank down on the floor and grinned. He was staring at the ghost of Frontera sitting across from him. It was back to its original size. But the latter wasn’t speaking.

“Seriously? Are you still sulking?” Lloyd lifted his brow.

“…”

“What? Why? What? Don’t look at me like that. Speak.”

“…”

Ghost Frontera didn’t speak. But its gaze at Lloyd was distinctly different from a while ago. There wasn’t anger, resentment, frustration, or any of the negative emotions that played across its eyes as it blew off steam at Lloyd.

The corners of Lloyd’s lips rose slightly. “Hey, you’re grateful but embarrassed for getting angry at me earlier, right?”

“…”

“I can see you flinching in guilt.”

“Oh, tsk… Come on.”

“If you’re thankful, just say it. Don’t curse me out.”

“Who says I’m grateful?” snapped Ghost Frontera.

“Really?”

“Of course. Grateful for what? You surprised me.”

“Ha,” Lloyd heaved lightly. “I guess I really took you by surprise.”

“How can I not, you bastard? You just did something unthinkable and-”

“Touched your heart?” put in Lloyd with a smirk.

“Touched, my ass.”

“More like your personality is ass.”

“So what? You got a problem with that?”

“No, I’m relieved. I won’t have to see you anymore.”

“The feeling is mutual, scumbag.”

“So, are you happy?”

“Of course.”

Ghost Frontera chuckled. Lloyd put on the same smile. Meanwhile, the train started to run for the first time, a test run that would be remembered throughout Hell’s history.

Thump! Clunk! Clank! Thump! The cabin shook terribly. There was a blaring din and vibration that hurt his buttocks. But Lloyd didn’t feel too uncomfortable since the feeling was familiar to him.

Well, this is similar to the army truck I used to ride in the military.

The rattling that felt like it was about to break his pelvis. The chaotic ringing noise. This train ride was as comfortable as the truck used in the military, which made the men in uniform feel like loads of cartons. Putting on a relaxed smile, Lloyd stared at Ghost Frontera.

"Hey, tell me if you feel motion sick.”

“Why?”

“I’ll remove the tarp. That way, you can stick your head out and vomit.”

“I might as well just kick you out of the tarp,” growled Ghost Frontera.

“Did you forget already how I beat you up after you tried to do that?”

“No, I didn’t, bastard,” huffed Ghost Frontera.

“Then let’s just go reincarnate like a good boy. Anyway, how do you feel? What does it feel like to be the passenger of this monumental train?”

“Well, nothing much.” Ghost Frontera roughly scratched its jaw. “I want to get a drink. It’s been a while.”

“A drink?” repeated Lloyd.

“Yeah.”

“Have you not been drinking?”

“Of course. How do you expect me to drink when I’m like this?”

“Well, you have a point.”

A chuckle escaped because it looked like he had just asked a silly question. Ghost Frontera did the same and chuckled.

“Then let me ask you a question as well.”

“What is it?”

“You came from another world, right?”

“Uh-huh. You knew?”

“Of course. I stuck to your side for over a year to get my body back. How could I not?”

“Well, it does make sense now that you mention it.”

“Do you have any unfinished business in your world?”

“Nothing really.”

“You’re a heartless bastard. Don’t you miss your family back there?”

“No. My parents all passed away.”

Ghost Frontera paused for a second before saying, “They say your family is always with you as long as they’re in your heart. Cheer up, scumbag.”

“What are you saying?” Lloyd frowned with a smile.

“Forget it. What about my parents? Are they well?”

“Of course. They are as healthy as a horse.”

“Really?”

“Yeah.” Lloyd stared at Ghost Frontera. Then he continued in honesty, “Come to think of it, I’m relaying this news late. The Frontera family is no longer a barony.”

“What? What do you mean?”

“It’s a county now.”

“Whaaat?” Ghost Frontera’s eyes widened.

“Surprising, right? I’m surprised, too. Anyway, the count has resumed his hobby recently too.”

“Wood carving?”

“Uh-huh. There are a bunch of wooden sculptures in the study already.”

“Then what about Mother’s garden?”

“It’s still there. Oh, it has doubled in size. But I’m slightly worried about the count’s back and knees.”

“What’s up with that old man’s back and knees?”

“He’s helping out with the gardening. He insists on crouching and giving a hand when he’s not used to the work.”

“Tsk. I guess the man is still senseless as always.”

“Aren’t you going to ask about Julian?”

“Don’t have to. He’s a smart kid, so I’m sure he’s doing well on his own.”

“Correct. He recently got married.”

Cough!

“I’m serious,” Lloyd stressed when he saw doubt flash across Ghost Frontera’s face.

“Really?”

“Yeah.”

“With whom?”

“The daughter of a sultan in the east. She’s a high-level sword expert, so she’s really good at fighting. It would be quite a sight if they got into a fight.”

“Julian will be beaten to death.”

“Probably?”

“…”

Lloyd and Ghost Frontera. The two paid tribute to Julian for a second as they imagined Julian having an imaginary quarrel with his wife. And then they both burst into a chuckle at the same time.

“So, what do you want to be reincarnated as?”

“Me?”

“Yeah.”

“I don’t know. This is all too sudden. Well, if I have to choose…”

“What?”

“A whale.”

“A whale?”

“Yeah. A whale.” Ghost Frontera nodded. “I’ve spent far too much time in Hell, and it’s flipping hot here. So, I want to be plunged in cold water.”

“Then you don’t have to be a whale. You could be a sardine. Or an anchovy,” pointed Lloyd.

“Those are easy to hunt and kill, you little jerk.”

“Oh, is that so?”

“Obviously. Anyway, I just wish to live freely. So, I’d hate to be born as a human again. I think it’d be stifling.”

“I guess you won’t be able to have your beloved drinks if you’re born as a whale.”

“Oh, tsk. Come on. You better be ready if I am reincarnated as a dragon.”

“Even if that happens, I’ll be dead from old age by the time you are fully grown.”

“…”

“Heh.”

“Haah. This bastard, seriously.”

“Seriously what?”

“Thank you for keeping your promise, bastard.”

“Aren’t your words a bit late,” Lloyd threw a snide remark.

“Who cares?”

They laughed again. In a terribly rattling train, against the backdrop of a slowly passing Hell scenery, Lloyd and Ghost Frontera both laughed. And Lloyd felt a part of his heart growing lighter.

A burdensome weight he didn’t know he was carrying started to grow light. The weight was guilt. The crime of pushing away Frontera from his own body, no matter how unintentional it was. The guilt of hiding the truth and parading around as the real Lloyd. He had even felt like a cuckoo bird inside a nest it didn’t belong to until then.

“So, I will take good care of the count and countess. I’m sorry.”

“Forget it, you bastard.”

“And I plan on revealing the truth in the future. That is, about you and me.”

“I said I get it, bastard.”

“Whew, sorry. I just felt weird all of a sudden.”

“Ha.” Lloyd smiled bitterly while Ghost Frontera smiled and blinked back the tears. The latter thought Lloyd might as well not make it cry if he was apologetic. The train slowly rattled its way, and beside the adjoining car was Javier, the knight of the Frontera family, quietly listening to Lloyd and Frontera’s conversation.
