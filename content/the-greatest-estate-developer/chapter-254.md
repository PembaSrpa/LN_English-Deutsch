# The Restoration Force of Destiny (2)

---

Swoosh! Splash! Below the spring sun, cargo ships arrived as they cut through the splashing waves, and people busily moved on and off the ships. After all this time, Port Cremo was still a bustling scene. It was also more vibrant than the last time Lloyd had come here. He felt a sense of inexplicable emotion at the sight.

“Whew.”

The vibrant scene of the port was all possible due to his success in getting rid of the Gigatitan. As the giant monster vanished, the looming threat over the port had forever disappeared, inviting in ships from all across the world and making the port as energetic as it was in the present moment. Lloyd tightened his fists as he had this thought. Did he do so because he felt a sense of accomplishment? No.

Ugh…! I should have asked for more pay!

He belatedly regretted his decision then. He felt a wave of resentment as bitter as a strong cup of espresso when he recalled his work here a few years ago.

It was a given that the port would be developed once I captured the Gigatitan… But I didn’t know it would be to this extent. I was foolish. Idiotic. Too relaxed. I should have been more forward and asked for a bigger compensation. Or I should have at least insisted on receiving a small portion of the customs tax for this port.

Had he acted thick-skinned and shamelessly asked for more, he would have been rolling in money earlier. What would have happened if he received one percent of the customs tax when the amount of trade occurring grew exponentially? He wouldn’t have had to whine to the queen to fund his fiefdom or worked so hard to make money in Namaran.

My life could have been so much easier! Ugh!

Lloyd wiped the tears that welled up in his eyes like humidity in a basement studio during the rainy season. The scenery of Port Cremo in all of its bustle and hustle under the bright sun. This beautiful sight made him green with envy. Naturally, his steps became more hurried.

“Tsk… Let’s get right into business,” blurted Lloyd.

“Do you plan on requesting an audience with Count Cremo?”

“Yeah, of course,” replied Lloyd after turning around and grinning at Javier following behind him. “Isn’t it a given to first greet the most powerful individual in a particular place wherever you go?”

“Truly, that sounds like the course of action for a sycophant who leeches off those in power,” retorted Javier.

“Ehem. Can’t you call it a daily course of action for ordinary people instead?” suggested Lloyd.

“Well, I have never lived like that before.”

“Wow. That must be nice. I’m jealous,” replied Lloyd sardonically.

“Yes, it is. All of this is thanks to the kindness of the lord. He allowed me to only focus on swordsmanship growing up.”

But what about you? What kind of life did you live in your world? Javier gazed at Lloyd in a meaningful way, and he recalled the things he accidentally heard many mornings before they left the fiefdom. He didn’t mean to listen, but it just happened.

Dragon King Verkis. Master Lloyd said something strange after he woke him up every morning.

At first, when he heard about the possibility of the altered state of destiny returning back to its original course, how this world was a fictional world, and how the people he had saved, including the count and countess and Julian, would pass away after a series of accidents, Javier thought it was all a lie. He believed that Lloyd was saying those strange things to pester Verkis. But he started to think differently as he listened to him every day.

His story was too consistent to be dismissed as gibberish.

Moreover, this Lloyd wasn’t real. This man was Kim Suho from another world. And when Javier remembered that, the things he first considered silly started to sound different, particularly about the calamities that would fall on Count and Countess Frontera. It was shocking. It was his first time learning that this world was a fictional story in another dimension. Moreover, in that story, the Frontera barony devastatingly crumbled, and fake Lloyd had been the one to prevent this tragedy from happening.

But then Master Lloyd said that a similar tragedy will strike again. And he has to put a stop to it… That was why he begged the dragon king for a solution.

Javier cast a meaningful gaze toward Lloyd, who was walking ahead of him.

Maybe my choice was not wrong then.

Javier had decided to watch over him even after he learned that the man in front of him was a fraud. There was a time when he agonized about his judgments in this matter. But now, his decision and resolution to observe and protect fake Lloyd appeared to have been the right choice.

That is why I did it. It’s why I followed you to Cremo without any fight. It’s why I decided to protect you.

If the tragedy in the Frontera family can be stopped, Javier was ready to protect and obey the fake Lloyd and risk all dangers. Just like he has always done up to this point. Much like the thinking when he left the Frontera fiefdom two days ago. Much like the resolution he chewed over throughout the travel by Ggoming. And before he knew it, he was already at the manor of Count Cremo.

“Woah. It’s been a while.”

Lloyd looked up at the manor with a different emotion than his last memories here. Back then, when he first set foot here, he was struggling as he had yet to pay off his family’s debt.

Though it’s not like my family is too great now, either.

No, in some sense, he was faced with a greater threat than a mountain of debt. At this rate, three years later, starting with Count Frontera, everyone around him was expected to meet a tragic death one by one. It was imperative for him to find a clue for a solution to stop these incidents from occurring. Resolved, he entered the manor and told the guard who he was and his purpose for a visit. Thankfully, he didn’t need to wait long afterward as Count Cremo rushed to leave everything behind and escort Lloyd before anything else.

“My, my, how long has it been!” exclaimed Count Cremo in sheer joy. “The hero of Cremo! The pride of the Cremona region. For what reason have you traveled all the way here for, Lloyd Frontera?”

“Ahaha…”

“Hmm? Why are you chuckling like so?”

“Nothing. It’s just that you seemed to have become much more lively than when I last saw you a while ago.”

“This is all thanks to you.” Count Cremo smiled brightly. “As soon as the Gigatitan was gone, trading ships came to my port in batches! And look who is living the life! Hahaha!”

Thwack! Thwack! Count Cremo tapped Lloyd’s back with his steel-like or bear-like hand.

He’s treating me entirely differently from before.

Lloyd suppressed his smile. On his first visit here, he had to come up with all sorts of gimmicks to win the construction contract.

But now, it was different. The success he had with catching the Gigatitan alone elevated his status as the hero of Cremo.

But first, let’s focus on the main reason I came here.

Lloyd focused his mind. He wasn’t here to be served as an honored guest and show off. As soon as they sat down at a table, Lloyd shared the purpose of his visit to Count Cremo, who was sitting across from him.

“Um, I came here to ask you a favor, Your Excellency.”

“Favor? What is it?” replied Count Cremo, intrigued.

“Could you find the most capable whaler in this port for me?” asked Lloyd.

“A whaler? Do you mean those at the whaling ship hunting the emperor whales?”

“Yes, that is what I mean,” affirmed Lloyd with a nod. Experienced whalers who sailed in the Arctic Ocean. Only they would be able to give him a clue about the Mermaid Kingdom. Those were the expectations Lloyd held, and he wasn’t disappointed.

“I know a few whalers. And some must be lodging at the port just in time. Do you have business with them?” asked Count Cremo.

“Yes, I do, and I would like to meet at their earliest convenience. I have some questions.”

“I see. Then I shall set up the meeting. But…” Count Cremo stopped after nodding pleasantly. And he threw a meaningful gaze at Lloyd and said, “My dear hero of Cremo. The pride of the Cremona region. Could you remind me of your age?”

“Excuse me…? My age?” asked Lloyd, dazed.

“You seem to be well past the age to start a family. Tell me, are you not in a hurry?”

“No, I am not,” said Lloyd flatly.

“Really?”

“Yes, truly.”

“Not at all?” insisted Count Cremo once again.

“No. Not at all.”

“Tsk. You do not have to be so adamant.”

“Please forgive me.” Lloyd smiled humbly, realizing that Count Cremo still hadn’t given up on Lloyd. He figured that was the case with many nobles in the kingdom. But he had no plans on accepting their marriage offers.

I’m sorry. I just want to date normally and get married just like everyone else.

Lloyd despised the thought of marrying someone he never met and didn’t love just for the sake of business. And so, he wanted to marry someone from a humble background instead of a noble lady.

Anyway, that’s not important right now.

Having succeeded in evading the pressure from the count, Lloyd left the manor right away. The following day, the message from Count Cremo came that the meeting with the seasoned whaler was set up.

“Allow me to introduce you to him,” motioned Count Cremo. “This is Felipe. He is the most experienced seaman I know and has been the captain of the fastest whaling ship in the Arctic Ocean, Feliconia, for the past 23 years.”

“Nice to meet you. I am Felipe.” As soon as Count Cremo finished his introduction, Captain Felipe offered a handshake to Lloyd.

“Nice to meet you. My name is Lloyd Frontera.”

Lloyd felt that this man’s hands were thick, full of callous and rough edges. With one manly handshake, Lloyd instantly knew how many storms Captain Felipe had overcome all these years.

Count Cremo brought an extremely experienced sailor.

Captain Felipe must have squinted a lot to gaze beyond the horizon, Lloyd thought, because tiny wrinkles naturally sat around his eyes. But even his eyes seemed to reveal his abundant experience.

Feeling that this man could be trusted, Lloyd brought out the topic at hand. “Thank you, Your Excellency, for making this meeting possible. I was in need of someone like you, Captain Felipe, as I had a question to ask.”

“To me?” Felipe replied. “What could you be so curious about?”

“Tell me, are you well aware of the mermaids?” asked Lloyd.

“Mermaids?”

“Yes,” replied Lloyd with a nod. “Certainly, I myself know as much as everybody does. Mermaids are the strongest creatures in the sea. They whiz and swim around the seas with their massive strength and swimming capability, and even the Gigatitan are terrified of their corps.”

“Hmm,” mused Felipe, “I can tell you know as much as what is possible for a regular person. What more do you want to know?”

“I wish to know the location of the Mermaid Kingdom.”

“The Mermaid Kingdom?”

“Yes.”

“Why do you want to know?”

“For personal reasons.”

“So, you have a business that requires you to go find the Mermaid Kingdom?”

“Yes, do you know where it is?”

“No, I don’t. But I somewhat know a way to find it,” replied Felipe, his eye wrinkles deepening.

Lloyd’s eyes glimmered. “Can you tell me more?”

“Well, that isn’t too difficult,” said Felipe. “Go to the Arctic Ocean. The sea with the coldest winds and waves whose giant floating icebergs intimidate all nearby ships. The deepest area under these waters is where you can find their kingdom.”

“Yikes.” Lloyd found himself clenching his fists. It was done. At last, he came to know it. It should be accurate, given that it’s coming from the old hand in sailing. And so, the only thing left for Lloyd was to call Yongyong and get ready to take a dive into the Arctic Ocean. Just then, Felipe spoke and shattered Lloyd’s plan altogether.

“But even if you know the location, you can’t go there.”

“Pardon? What are you talking about?” replied Lloyd, bemused.

“It’s just as I said. Even if you know the location, you can’t just go there.”

“Is it because it’s too deep?”

If the kingdom sat in the deepest part of the sea, that would be slightly problematic. Yongyong should be okay, but Lloyd wouldn’t be able to withstand the water pressure. In any case, the Asrahan Core Technique and manaheart had their limitations.

Um, does that mean I should pay a huge sum to get some magic done on me?

Lloyd’s thought was soon interrupted by Felipe, who chuckled and shook his head slowly.

“Too deep, you say… No, the problem is not that simple. This is a matter of gaining permission.”

“Permission?”

“That’s what I heard. That is, from the old-timers when I was still just a newbie. They told me that if I ever bump into a mermaid in the sea, I must ask permission to pass through the sea since the sea is their home. The same goes when you have any business to visit their kingdom, I’ve been told.”

The captain paused for a while until he began again soon.

“Of course, what I’m telling you right now may sound strange. But I can tell you with certainty that you need their permission and recognition before you can go to their kingdom.”

“Um, excuse me, but do you have proof to back you up?” asked Lloyd politely.

“Of course I do. I’ve been there.”

“Wait, to the Mermaid Kingdom?” Lloyd’s voice cracked in surprise.

“Yes. Just once. It was for just a brief moment. It was before I even turned 20, so about 31 years ago. I was cracking and removing the ice stuck in the sides of the ship that day. Then I slipped and fell into the sea. I could feel the freezing cold in an instant.”

“So, what happened?”

“I screamed. But the wind and snowstorm were too strong, so nobody on the ship heard me. Eventually, the ship sailed on without me, and I thought I’d freeze to death. I was scared and despaired. That was the moment when a creature I’ve never seen before appeared in front of me.”

“A mermaid.”

“Yes. They were big. And they stared right at me. They seemed confused about whether I was an intruder or not. Scared, I frantically thought that I must show that I was no intruder. So, I acted out until I succeeded, and under their permission, I was allowed a short visit down to the kingdom with them, where I received treatment and safely made it back to the land.”

“Wow. Are you serious?”

“How else am I alive now and be with you now?” chuckled Captain Felipe.

“Wow.”

Lloyd was amazed. Truly, this man was a veteran in sailing who experienced all sorts of storms.

I’ve done it.

Lloyd clenched his fists. The Jewel of Truth that had been mentioned by Dragon King Verkis. It was a legendary artifact among mermaids that could stop the restoration of destiny. At least, it would be able to stop those awful incidents from happening. And that was why he traveled all the way to be here, that is, to find out more about the Mermaid Kingdom.

Awesome.

Lloyd was able to locate a person who had been to the Mermaid Kingdom with one quick search. He felt like he made acquaintance with a tour guide of the Mermaid Kingdom instead of merely obtaining a clue about the place.

“Then…” Lloyd spoke, his voice growing with expectation. “When you met the mermaid in the sea, what did you do to get its recognition?”

“Do you mean the secret behind earning their acceptance?”

“Yes.”

Lloyd nodded. At last, he would finally hear the answer he had been searching for. He could go to the Mermaid Kingdom! At that very moment, a strong, fiery ember of hope ignited in his heart. Captain Felipe grinned and gave an answer.

“I sang the mermaid a beautiful melody.”

What the hell did you just say?
