# The Aotearoa’s Blueprint (2)

---

Pyramid. It was a structure that everyone thought of when hearing about ancient Egypt. It was also the first structure everyone thought of when asked to name an ancient relic. But the pyramid wasn’t exclusive to Egypt. There were similar structures across the world, albeit in slightly different time periods.

This is because of the technological limitations at the time. Construction technology hadn’t advanced far, and there was an absence of lightweight, strong materials like steel. As a result, buildings were made using rocks, which made them exponentially heavier the larger they were. But since there wasn’t any effective technology to stabilize their weight, most buildings in ancient times naturally took the shape of a pyramid.

The pyramid-shaped buildings boasted a wide bottom and pointed top that was relatively narrow and light. These tapered structures had a wide and heavy bottom while their upper portion was narrow and light. This allowed their center of gravity to be extremely low and stable because of the balanced distribution of their substantial weight. As such, pyramids, despite their humongous size, managed to stand tall even after all these years.

Thanks to it, most pyramid structures have been fairly well preserved in several parts of the world. To name a few, there’s the Chichén Itzá of Mexico which was built during the Maya civilization, the Aztec pyramid with the stairs, the Ziggurat of Ur, and the Pyramids of Gümar in the Canary Islands of Spain. The tombs of ancient Chinese emperors are also another example. We even have one in South Korea as well. The Tomb of the General.

The Tomb of the General was the most developed stone mound tomb of the Goguryeo Kingdom in ancient times. It was possible that some would accuse him of being a jingoist for calling this structure a pyramid. But clearly and evidently, without an inkling of doubt, the Tomb of the General was some sort of pyramid.

Though, of course, there’s a great difference in size between the Egyptian pyramid and the Tomb of the General.

But why did he think of the Tomb of the General now out of all times? Why did his brain associate it with the sight in front of him, which was the blueprint for the Jewel of Truth?

How could I not? The two look very similar.

Lloyd lifted his gaze, taking in the jazzy hologram video of the emerald sculpture he had gone on a great length to place into its rightful place. The video was the blueprint of the Jewel of Truth, but it felt familiar. From any perspective he looked, it was an appropriate mixture of the Tomb of the General and the Pyramid of Egypt.

Kudos to me for joining the traditional architecture club back in college.

Thanks to his club, Lloyd could specifically identify these sorts of historical architectural styles.

Strong rocks are laid out as the foundation. As for the stylobate, is that granite? The top is composed of seven flights of stairs. I also think I see some holding stones. There are even a few raised areas to prevent the bottom of the structure from getting crushed by the weight coming down. Plus, the inner stone chamber is roofed with a ceiling where the stones are set in parallel to each other. It’s the typical stone chamber tomb. I guess the chamber isn’t meant to hold a corpse but is where the magic circle is activated.

Lloyd’s sharp eyes ran a thorough analysis of the blueprint, and the more he looked at it, the more it looked like the Tomb of the General from the Goguryeo time period. Just then, the hologram changed its shape. Crackle…!

Нmm?

Countless dots appeared around the hologram blueprint, most of them as small as a speck of dust or a fruit fly. Upon a closer look, Lloyd noticed that they weren’t dots.

Humans? Merpeople? Orcs?

A countless number of humans and merpeople were fighting, their forces clashing. The orc warriors and elf archers tussled with each other, and the giants ran amok as they swept the surrounding area.

It’s a war.

But this one was different from any ordinary war. It resembled Hell, where hundreds and thousands of forces of different races charged forward to kill each other. Lloyd recognized which war this was as soon as he saw it.

Mr. Rotorua said before that Jewel of Truth used to be the most powerful treasure and weapon during ancient times. Everyone sought to get their hands on it, and a war started. This is that war.

The hologram was playing before Lloyd’s very eyes, and every race was battling against each other in the war that ended the mythical age. Warriors were thrown into a bloody frenzy until a critical blow destroyed the Jewel of Truth. No one could know who the culprit was. Not a single one had the time or sense to notice what happened amidst the constant chaos. Eventually, the Jewel of Truth collapsed, toppling down the stairs and crumbling to pieces. The entire structure, aside from the foundation, was utterly destroyed and reduced to a war trophy.

Everyone is… taking the rubbles of the jewel.

Human knights, orc warriors, elf archers, and giant magicians… They were all competing with each other to collect the rubbles of the jewel as their trophy. That erupted into another conflict. Everyone was filled with tenacity and determination to get their hands on one more piece. The scene reminded Lloyd of a colony of ants assembling around a giant carcass. And with that, the video ended.

Crackle…! The dots, lines, and sides that beautifully hung in the air vanished. Now, there was only dark, murky seawater. Just then…

Ding dong.

A notification alarm dinged in his ears.

Hmm?

Lloyd wondered what the notification was about. Cocking his head, he read the messages starting to appear in front of his eyes.

[You have activated the “Guideline Sculpture to the Jewel of Truth.”]

[You have witnessed the destruction of the Jewel of the Truth from it.]

[In the process, you were able to closely observe the structure of the Jewel of Truth. This experience had a strong effect on your design skill.]

[The blueprint of the Jewel of Truth, the Aotearoa, has been registered in your design skill data.]

What?

Lloyd was taken aback. The Jewel of Truth, the Aotearoa, was registered as part of his design skill? This was something he never expected and was experiencing for the first time.

Until now, it was only possible to upload a blueprint for my design skill if I surveyed or designed one myself.

That was the way it was up to this point. Whether it was an empty piece of land or a building, Lloyd had to personally survey it in detail first before he could utilize its blueprint in his design space. He could only insert a blueprint in his virtual design space after going through the strenuous process of surveying the data of a building while straining his eyes the entire time. Another way to do so was to use all his focus and pull several all-nighters for the drudgery of designing a blueprint that was absolutely error-free. But right now…

Wow!

All he did was view a hologram video one time. And the message appeared to tell him that he could show the jewel blueprint in his design space.

Let’s first check it out. The design.

Lloyd activated the skill at once, and the virtual design space visible and recognizable only to him appeared.

Bring out data. The Aotearoa’s blueprint.

Will this really work? Lloyd gave it a try. The result came immediately.

[Fetching the “Aotearoa’s Blueprint.”]

Fwoosh! For a second, the design space brightened until it was marked with a complex array of dots, lines, and surfaces. The sturdy and wide foundation. The tapered top. The blueprint was the same as the Jewel of Truth he saw through the hologram video a moment ago.

This is real. Amazing.

Lloyd found himself clenching his fist. What appeared in his design space was more than a simple illustration.

Zoomed in like this, I can see that everything is included here. The measurements, materials, standard requirements, and compound method. It’s all there.

Not just that. The stress distribution, weight, compressive force, axial force, bending moment, strain rate, temperature expansion coefficient, etc. Nearly all information required for the construction was marked there. In other words, it was so impeccable that he could start the reconstruction right away.

I’ve got enough laborers. The merpeople using the jjimjilbang can work at the reconstruction site. As for the materials and funds, I should ask for the queen. This so-called jewel is an amazing treasure from the mythical age that belongs to the merpeople. Since that’s what I’m restoring, I’m sure the queen will provide me with a sufficient amount of aid.

Lloyd swelled with confidence as he thought about it. How he’d prepare and start the reconstruction. The challenges and the solutions. It was all assessed in his head in no time. Moreover, Lloyd was familiar with the structure of the jewel.

It’s the Tomb of the General in a pyramid shape. I researched these structures back when I was still active in the club. I’ll just need to combine the information from the documents I studied then with the blueprint. This is more than doable.

Thankfully, the pyramid structure wasn’t very complex. Sure, precisely allocating the proper distribution of weight would be tricky, but this structure wasn’t a highly difficult design to construct for a building.

It’s a relief that the Tomb of the General was a part of my home country’s history. And that it was built like a pyramid.

The Tomb of the General. Otherwise dubbed as the Pyramid of the East. Certainly, this one was poles apart from the Egyptian counterpart in terms of size. It was as different as a mammoth and a dog. But still, structure-wise, the difference wasn’t noticeable. If the Egyptian pyramid was built in the middle of the ancient Korean peninsula…

People might have called it the Brobdingnagian, the giant Tomb of the General. Oh, since Gulliver wasn’t written back then, maybe they’d just call it the giant.

Anyway, Lloyd felt that the construction would be smooth sailing, given his encounter with the Tomb of the General, when he noticed a tiny red message subtly floating in the bottom of the blueprint.

Hmm? What’s this?

He read the message.

[The Aotearoa’s Blueprint]

[Status: Construction not possible. You are missing key materials.]

[In the event you continue the construction without its key materials, the magic circle function, which is the crux of the Jewel of Truth, will be inoperable.]

“Ha… Right. Nothing is ever easy for me.” Lloyd felt growingly bitter after reading the message.

I’m missing key construction materials? The Jewel of Truth will only operate once I obtain them.

What if he built the jewel without its key materials for construction? It would result in a fake imitation that only looked like the Jewel of Truth.

That won’t do.

Lloyd shook his head. He wasn’t trying to restore a cultural artifact here. His goal was to activate the Jewel of Truth and prevent the restoration of destiny from occurring.

Hmm… So, where can I get my hands on these key materials?

Lloyd grumbled to himself as he observed the blueprint, thinking that the answer would be there. And he was right.

What are these areas that have been marked red?

Upon a closer look, some parts of the design were slightly off. Only a few select areas were marked in red.

Let’s see.

Lloyd poked one of the red areas as if he was clicking it. But nothing happened. No information whatsoever popped up.

Is it not meant to be clicked?

Dismayed, Lloyd decided to give every red spot a poke. He continued to poke when the one at the bottom of the design reacted with a click. It was the part about the holding stones of the Tomb of the General.

Ding dong.

[You have selected the first key material for construction, “tauranga.”]

[Do you wish to access information about tauranga?]

[YES / NO]

Of course, it’s a yes!

Lloyd nodded earnestly. Just then…

Fwoosh! A new video emerged in front of him as if it was trying to intrude his vision or he was dreaming with his eyes open.

A video recording, maybe?

It looked like one. Lloyd focused on the video.

Boom! Baaang! A large human, no, a giant was landing onto a beach, and it was carrying twelve pieces of rocks in its embrace. Lloyd instantly recognized the rocks.

Those look like the key materials I just saw for the stylobate.

Lloyd recalled how the materials became a form of trophies once the jewel was destroyed. Tauranga, one of the key materials for construction, seemed to be one of them.

A giant, huh.

Lloyd squinted his eyes as he watched the video. In the video, the giant strode off while carrying the tauranga and built a city by the river, decorating its own palace with the same material. For the longest time, the giant ruled over humans as slaves until they waged war for independence. A long period of war, conflict, and struggle ensued. Eventually, the giant was killed.

Then came the world of humans. The leaders destroyed the giant’s palace, and the Tauranga that decorated the palace was abandoned at the corner of the city. The city run by humans flourished for a long period of time until an enraged dragon took over the palace, causing the downfall of the imperial family and bringing in a new ruling house. Another palace was then constructed under the new regime with hard work and collaboration. At this point, twelve pieces of taurangas were brought back and used for the palace’s foundation. There was no particular reason for this decision except that the aforementioned material was sleek, well-manicured, large, and sturdy. That was all.

In other words, the people under the new imperial regime were completely in the dark about the backstory of the twelve pieces of tauranga. That hardly was surprising. The war was something of the far distant past. It had become a mere legend from the mythical age. And just like that, the twelve pieces of tauranga were utilized to build a new palace. The video ended with the palace standing firm and tall.

“…”

Lloyd remained silent even well after the video ended. Instead of speaking out loud, he merely sighed. Why? Simple. The palace he just saw in the video? The palace that used the twelve taurangas was very familiar to him.

“Ha..” Lloyd sighed at length again. He couldn’t help it. Then he grumbled to himself, “Out of all the places… Really? The place holding the tauranga had to be the palace of Queen Magentano. Ha!”
