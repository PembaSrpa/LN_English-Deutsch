But to think that I became a noble from an old book I picked up again to take a breather.

I probably won’t be getting into any kind of mishaps, like a rebellion or something.

That was always the problem in any historical drama. It didn’t matter if you were a well-off aristocrat. Once you got tangled in a rebellion, it was over. There was no room for excuses and you were beheaded in one fell swoop.

I’d much rather be a baron of a remote area. It’s like its own niche market.

No big conspiracy plots, just the countryside and a sweet life of affluence. He could live a life of ease if he held on to his comfortable, stable status.

Although, that can only happen after the baron’s debt is settled.
