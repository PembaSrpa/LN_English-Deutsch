# I’ve Become the Dragon King’s Alarm (1)

---

“My, dear Dragon King!” The man’s voice dripped with shamelessness. “Pray, do tell me, is there something bothering you? Or a problem on your mind?”

“…”

A voice suddenly tickled his ears. Dragon King Verkis turned and found a human standing there, rubbing his hands and tightly smiling in a friendly manner. Verkis cocked his head.

“What are you?” blurted Verkis.

“Excuse me?”

“Who are you to stand here right now in front of me-”

“Oh, I am Lloyd Frontera,” broke in Lloyd. “It is nice to meet you. I was brought here by a manticore as I have somehow become eligible to receive your sponsorship by some unexpected turn of events. Do you not remember? I even put on a blanket over you and fanned you a few days ago. Oh, and this.”

“…”

“I brought water just in case you were thirsty after waking up. Try drinking this refreshing cup of cold water first.”

“…”

Verkis stared at Lloyd with a strange gaze, trying to make sense of him. And soon, he started to recall what had happened.

Right. This fellow crawled into my dungeon a few days ago.

Verkis remembered that this man, who attained the right to receive his sponsorship, got here with the help of the manticore. It had been 150 years since he last saw a visitor, but he couldn’t bother entertaining him. The mere act of talking to a human being was bothersome, and so was shifting his eyes to get a look at him. Thus, Verkis fell back to sleep, much like he did to the few heroes who had set foot here. If he slept and ignored him, this human would leave on his own like the others. Thinking that he would eventually give up on receiving his sponsorship, Verkis fell into a sound slumber.

But it turned out that giving up must have been the last thing on this human’s mind. And it didn’t seem like he would beg like the other heroes, who had pleaded for their rewards and for the king to keep his promise. Lloyd didn’t straightforwardly demand anything from him either. Instead of begging and clinging to him, this groveling human offered to help.

Verkis swallowed his smile and took the glass of water. After drinking, he said, “You? Me? Help out?”

“Yes, my lord,” replied Lloyd.

“How? Do you plan to lend an ear to me or something like that?”

“My, if I could help you out by listening, then I’m ready to lend you an ear a hundred times over.”

“Really?”

“Yes.”

Lloyd nodded earnestly, sensing that this was a golden opportunity.

I was getting a shade jittery about when the dragon king would wake up, but thankfully, the opportunity came earlier than I imagined.

Even when Lloyd was enjoying his vacation in the cave for the past few days, he didn’t feel completely at peace. In fact, he had kept his eyes and ears open for the dragon king at all times. Even when he was goofing around on the king’s bed, looking around the area set up with traps in the cave, and roasting sweet potatoes to ease his hunger, Lloyd was on full alert in order to avoid missing any movements from the dragon king. And so, a part of him had started to grow worried.

It seemed like the dragon king was going to sleep longer than I expected.

At first, Lloyd believed that the king wouldn’t sleep for more than a few days. But as time progressed, he realized that wasn’t the case. The king looked like he was going to sleep a few months straight. So, Lloyd was on the fence about whether to wait some more or figure out another way.

Thankfully, the dragon king woke up by himself at a great time. My, how thankful I am.

What was more, Verkis didn’t just wake up. It looked like something was terribly bothering his mind. This was a golden opportunity that would be difficult to come across again. Thinking so, Lloyd hurriedly decided to take action.

“I am ready to listen to whatever it is that is bothering you, my king. Am I troubled by this? Not at all. Why would I feel troubled? This world is all about helping each other! We should all treat one another with kindness!That is the beautiful future we should work toward.”

“…”

“Please forgive me,” apologized Lloyd when he noticed Verkis was being silent. “I got carried away.”

“Let’s move on. So, you are willing to hear out my concern, is that right?” Verkis asked.

“Yes.”

“Then are you married?”

“Excuse me?” Lloyd’s brow lifted.

“I said, are you married?” reiterated Verkis.

“No, I am not.”

“Then are you in a relationship?”

“No,” replied Lloyd.

“Have you ever been in one up to this point?”

“No…”

“What kind of life have you been living?” huffed Verkis.

“…”

Come on, Dragon King. There’s no need to hurt me with your words, said Lloyd to himself, trying to hold back the tears starting to well in his eyes. But Verkis was cruel and merciless.

“You’re not married, and you never had a girlfriend. I doubt that you will be a good listener. You wouldn’t know the struggles of a married man,” dismissed Verkis.

“Oh! I know a little,” exclaimed Lloyd.

“How?”

“I used to lend an ear to my dad all the time. By that, I mean whenever my mother was upset.”

“Really?”

“Yes.”

“Hmm, I suppose you can help me a little to solve this emergency crisis of my sulking sweetheart,” mused Verkis.

“What do you mean by sweetheart?”

“My wife.”

“Your wife… Oh, the dragon queen?”

“Yes.”

“Please speak. I am all ears.”

Lloyd then got down on his knees, bringing his body slightly toward Verkis. And he even wiggled his ears by moving the muscles behind his head to persuade the dragon king of his desire to hear about his problem. This attitude of his touched the latter’s heart.

“Tsk… It’s simple. My sweetheart is upset.”

“She is?”

“Yes.”

“What happened…?”

“She went on a trip with my mother and daughter.”

“Your mother and daughter?”

“Yeah,” Verkis went on to explain. “You wouldn’t know this, but she is extremely diligent. Beyond what you can imagine. For hundreds of years of her life here, she has never missed a day of cooking or cleaning.”

“Oh,” said Lloyd, understanding the situation. “Could that be the reason why your mother took your wife on a trip?”

“Oh? Correct. You’re quite clever.”

“I made a rough guess.”

“Anyway, you’re right. My mother felt sorry seeing my wife do nothing but housework and thought she was living too diligently to enjoy life. So, she suggested getting some fresh air together. And just like that, Mother took off, telling me that they’d be traveling for just a year.”

“Your mother has taken your wife?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes.” Verkis nodded his head. “So, I was left here all alone. You know, this is an opportunity.”

“That is true. It is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity. You can do whatever you want to do all by yourself. And laze around,” seconded Lloyd.

“That’s exactly right. I see you aren’t completely clueless.”

“Ahaha.”

“Anyway, so I planned on sleeping to my heart’s content, but it turned out my wife knew what was going on in my head already. So, she gave me some homework.”

“Homework? What was it?” Lloyd was intrigued.

“She wanted me to call her every morning.”

“…”

“In verbatim, she said, ‘My dear husband, for the first time, I will be going around the world away from your side. As such, I am truly worried about you, who will be left alone all those days. So, I wish for you to call me every morning and tell me how you are.”

“Ahahaha… How sweet,” mumbled Lloyd uncertainly.

“But scary,” Verkis added.

“Yes, it is.”

Lloyd seconded without any hesitation. When he thought about it, there was something scary about those gentle and sweet-sounding words.

Really, it’s the gentle suggestions that are truly scary.

Lloyd’s mind flew back to the days when he lived in South Korea, particularly the times when his father got grilled by his mother. And Lloyd noticed that Dragon King Verkis had an expression that was similar to his father’s during those past occasions.

“I will never understand.” Verkis shook his head. “Call her every morning? What kind of lifestyle in this world does one, no, a dragon need to wake up every morning on their own? Is that even possible?”

“Oh, that’s a challenge to all the humans of this world as well,” agreed Lloyd gravely.

“Is that true? I’m not the strange one here, right?”

“You’re not.” Lloyd playfully agreed with Verkis again, pointing out the crux of the latter’s concern. “Wait, have you not called her at all in the meantime?”

“No.”

“How long has it been?”

“I’m not exactly sure, but I believe it’s been about a month.”

“Did you try today…?”

“Of course I did. I tried through communication magic but failed.”

“It can’t be.” Lloyd gaped.

“You catch on quickly.”

“She denied your call. Am I right?”

“Yes. So, help me out here. How can I overcome this emergency crisis?”

“…”

Lloyd clamped his mouth shut, wondering how he should help out and what benefit he might be able to get out of it. He analyzed the situation, calculating it all piece-by-piece. He did the math for what his potential gains and losses were. And at last, a conclusion was reached.

“Um, I would like to ask one thing… Visiting the dragon queen is something you are not considering, right?” asked Lloyd carefully.

“Of course not. She’ll lash out at me if I go there now.”

“Right.”

Verkis couldn’t be any more right. Recklessly approaching a sulking wife was the same as asking for death. So, the dragon king had to appease her before he could go see her, and that was his main point of concern. How would he be able to soothe her anger? Thankfully, Lloyd had an answer.

With confidence, Lloyd said, “Then come to my fiefdom.”

“What?” Verkis tilted his head. “Me? You want me to come to your fiefdom?”

“Yes.”

“That sounds cumbersome. But why?”

“That way I can give you a wake-up call every morning,” explained Lloyd.

“A wake-up call?”

“Yes.”

Now came the crucial part. Lloyd licked his lips before putting on a sweeter and gentler smile on his face. And he spoke in a voice that said, “You can trust me.”

“Think about it, my king,” explained Lloyd, “You said it earlier. What kind of lifestyle does a dragon need to wake up on his own?”

“Yes, I did.”

“I agree with you. There is nothing more difficult and agonizing than waking up on your own every morning. But what if you have someone to wake you up?”

“Someone to wake me up? Are you suggesting you would wake me up?”

“Yes.” Lloyd nodded confidently, and in an honest, trustworthy, and credible voice, he said, “I will wake you up every morning at the same hour, so you can call your wife.”

“Hmm, but what if she doesn’t answer?”

“You should keep trying nonetheless.”

“Will it work?”

“It will,” replied Lloyd with equal confidence. “The dragon queen is enraged because your promise to call her every day was broken. The events of the past cannot be changed, and your fault will not be erased. But as everything is with life, your realization that it’s too late is the earliest time to take corrective action. So, you should dutifully contact her every morning from now on.”

“Are you sure?”

“Yes, and of course, she will ignore your call a few times. But what do you think will happen if you keep calling her? Eventually, she will come around. Why? You will be showing her how remorseful you feel, even if you can’t reach her.”

“Hmm. That sounds logical,” replied Verkis, accepting Lloyd’s suggestion.

“Yes, and I am here to help you out.”

“So, you will wake me up every morning?”

“Yes, all you need to do is laze, no, sleep all day until I wake you up to contact your wife.”

“After that will I be able to sleep for the rest of the day until you wake me up the next morning?”

“Exactly, what is more…” Lloyd paused before speaking. “Think how pleased she will be when she learns that you stepped out of this cave.”

“And that’s why you’re insisting we go to your fiefdom,” postulated Verkis, nodding.

“Yes, exactly.”

Lloyd smiled, swelling with confidence. A satisfied smile appeared on Verkis’s mouth as well.

Good.

Verkis and Lloyd. The dragon king and the sly human. Both of them had the same thought in their heads.

This fellow is quite useful!

Verkis’s eyes blossomed with hope as he looked at Lloyd. His suggestion was good even when the king thought about it again. There wasn’t much he needed to do other than just sleep like usual until the boy woke him up when it was time to call his wife. The human had seemingly solved his biggest problem after being left alone in the dragon cave.

That manticore of mine is reluctant to wake me up from sleep. It’s still afraid of me.

But that thorny issue was about to be solved very soon. And it didn’t look like he was losing anything from the deal. Content, Verkis shot a glance toward Lloyd.

“So, you must be expecting something big in return,” stated Verkis.

“Yes.”

“You won’t even pretend to be modest?” Verkis was slightly dumbstruck.

“What would be the point of that considering you’ve already seen through me?” Lloyd dared to say. “That would only bother you into saying a few more words.”

“Wow. I like your way of thinking.”

“Thank you, my lord.”

“Fine. I’ll give it to you.”

“Come again?” Lloyd paused, and just then, Verkis flicked his fingers. Snap!

“Done. An additional life and access to this cave. I gave it all to you.”

“What…?”

“Those were the benefits that have been promised to come with my sponsorship. I just gave them to you. All of it,” Verkis said dully.

“All of it?”

Lloyd was dazed. With just a flick of his finger, he gave him all of the promised rewards? Lloyd couldn’t believe it.

“What? Did you expect to see something sparkle or flash? Hm? I can’t be bothered to add those unnecessary showy effects,” said Verkis as he wrinkled his brow.

“I-is that so?”

“Of course. From now on, you have free access to this cave. You won’t trigger any of the traps. So, you can take whatever trinket you want from here. Plus, as for the additional life…” Verkis stroked his jaw.

“Yes,” replied Lloyd with a gulp.

“There isn’t a way for me to immediately verify this all to you,” finally said Verkis. “Hmm, or you could die right now. Want to try?”

“No…”

“All right, good thinking.” Verkis nodded. “Moreover, I’m sure you’ve also factored in the rewards you’d be getting by serving me in your fiefdom, right?”

“Yes.”

“At least pretend to be embarrassed if you’ve been caught.”

“Ahaha.”

Lloyd smiled in embarrassment. What Verkis just pointed out was true. Lloyd would be serving the former at least for a year, and this time would grant him the opportunity to curry even more favor from the king. He would dance to his tune and earn a little more brownie points from him. Lloyd figured that his connection with Verkis would come to be helpful for the rest of his life.

I mean, he’s the dragon king. The richest bum in the whole world!

Once again, Lloyd told himself that he had to keep this man by his side. He would serve Verkis as his great role model and ally even if it required Lloyd to pander to him. His life would be greatly enriched by building a constructive and close relationship with him.

Plus, there isn’t any hard work involved for me to help the king like this.

He just needed to wake the king up every morning. That was easier than taking a dog out for a walk.

And in return, I’m building a connection with the dragon king. I should jump to take this chance come hell or high water.

With only one gram of effort, he earned an equivalent of 1,000 tons in gains. Lloyd wasn’t the type to miss out on such a reward.

“So, can I escort you now, my king?” suggested Lloyd.

“Are you going to carry me on your back?”

“That way, you will be free from the tedious job of walking.”

“I like you. You’re my type of guy. But what you just suggested is creepy, so leave it. Let’s just teleport.”

“Yes, duly noted.”

Lloyd agreed right away, and Verkis prepared to teleport. The sly human and lazy dragon king. The two’s interests were perfectly aligned at this very moment. And Javier watched everything that was happening by their side. He thought to himself…

That fraud.

Javier wondered if this man, Kim Suho, used to be a scammer in his world and asked himself if he was wasting his energy to protect such a con man. Amid the growing sense of resentment, he started packing nonetheless to get ready to return to his fiefdom. And just like that, the two humans and the dragon king were set to depart for the Frontera fiefdom.
