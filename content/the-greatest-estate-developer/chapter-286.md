# The Defendant’s Method of Blackmail (1)

---

Rattle. Rattle. The carriage shook, but not like the carts drawn around in the markets. Regardless, it still had a rattle as it wasn’t a car with a modern suspension device. He could feel even the smallest bump from his buttocks.

Well, it’s better than being crammed into a bus during rush hour and getting attacked by the sweaty underarms of the person behind me.

Lloyd smiled faintly as he recalled one of his memories from South Korea for a moment. This was much better than those days.

He had a large and soft luxury leather seat all to himself right now. He could comfortably cross his legs and chat with Javier who was sitting across from him.

“Don’t you think so, Javier?”

Lloyd’s words brought Javier to lift his head. Tearing his gaze away from the papers, Javier stared hard at Lloyd. “What do you mean what I think? By the way, are you not worried?”

“Worried?” Lloyd shot him a quizzical look.

“About today’s trial.” The bottom of the papers was tapped into an organized bundle. Javier held it out to Lloyd. “You will arrive at the court any minute now, and the trial will commence right after. Will you be all right not reading this in advance?”

“Yes,” said Lloyd with confidence. “I am fine.”

“Even when this paper details the points that will be mentioned in the courtroom?”

“Uh-huh,” affirmed Lloyd again as he nodded. He was sure. He didn’t have to read it. “I already have a good idea of what’s inside without reading it.”

“Hmm.” Javier tapped his fingers. “Are you sure?”

“Well, try testing me if you want,” Lloyd said as he sat up straight.

Javier’s eyes narrowed. “Fine. What is the biggest charge the House of Peers has against you?”

“The loss of the Heart of Winter, which is a divine item of the imperial family. They’re demanding that I take responsibility for it since I was in charge of the site where the accident took place.”

“You got that right. One point for you.”

“What was that?” snapped Lloyd. “Are you counting points now?”

“I will continue.”

“Hold on,” stopped Lloyd, holding up his hand.

“Yes?”

“What’s the best possible score?” asked Lloyd.

“100.”

“But that question was worth only one point.”

“It was the easiest and most basic question I have. To be frank, if you don’t even know what the court is charging you with, you might as well jump out of the carriage right this minute.”

“But this carriage is passing a suspension bridge,” Lloyd pointed out in confusion.

“Yes,” sighed Javier with glee, “What a splendid morning to check out the water temperature.” Ignoring the dazed look on Lloyd’s face, Javier continued his test. “Next question. What punishment did the House of Peers demand for you while making their accusation?”

“Strip me of my title as a meritorious retainer.”

“You got that right. Ten points.”

“Oooh!”

“Do not get ahead of yourself. What do they want by stripping you of your status?”

“Obviously,” Lloyd huffed, “They want to take away the privileges I have and taint my reputation. If I’m stripped of my title as a meritorious retainer right after I earn it, it’d leave a huge damage to my reputation. I might as well not have received it in the first place instead. History will remember my name with dishonor.”

“Correct. 30 points. Plus, once your title is gone, they will truly start attacking you.”

“I think so, too. Since I’ll lose the privilege of no-arrest and legal immunity.”

“That is right,” agreed Javier, “Once they arrest you, you will be put in front of the court presided by the House of Peers.”

“Tsk. I guess this country doesn’t have the principle of ne bis in idem.” Lloyd looked out the window.

“What was that?”

Lloyd turned and said, “Nothing. Anyway, it’d be like taking away my shield before stabbing my heart.”

Lloyd could see it already. Despite that, he was not scared in the slightest. But he was worried that the situation could get bothersome for him. Meanwhile, Javier continued with his questions.

“If so, do you have a guess on why they’re so insistent on attacking you?”

“Yeah.” Lloyd nodded. It was so obvious that he didn’t have to read the document to know the answer. “Because I am dashingly handsome.”

“Wrong,” Javier made a repulsive face. “Minus 100 points.”

“…”

“Please answer seriously. We are arriving at the courtroom soon,” pleaded Javier in a serious voice.

“Tsk. Since I’m just a nobody from the countryside. A parvenu in the eyes of the capital nobles. And I’ve got a history of revealing the corruption in the royal academy, which propelled the queen to wield her wrathful power against quite a number of noble children and professors. I imagine a considerable number of families were waiting for a chance to pounce on me.”

“I am relieved that you are aware of it. Then, next question.”

“Hold on. My answer is correct. How many points do I get?”

“None.”

“Why not?” Lloyd’s voice broke.

“Since you gave a completely ludicrous answer before you gave the right one.”

“Hey, if you look closely from various angles, I can pass as handsome-”

“Next question,” Javier immediately put in. “What should you do to effectively refute the accusations the House of Peers will make against you at court?”

“How about kneeling down and begging them to let it go this once?”

“…”

“I’m serious. I’m not lying.”

“Master Lloyd.” Javier stared hard at Lloyd.

“Yeah. What?”

“Are you confident that you can win at court?”

“Of course I do. It’ll be a clean finish. I will trounce them.”

Lloyd had no doubt about it. But before he could spell his plan out to Javier, the carriage came to a stop.

“Looks like we’re here. Let’s go.”

Lloyd immediately opened the carriage door before Javier could scold him, and the royal courtroom appeared in his view. That was the place for today’s trial. Lloyd went in. The trial was ready to start soon. After touching up his clothes, Lloyd received a walk-through of today’s trial from the courtroom administrator. The etiquette and order of speakers in court. And later, the trial commenced.

Woah. What an impressive structure this building has.

Lloyd’s gaze traveled the width of the courtroom as he stood at the defendant’s seat. It was dominating. The place was designed like a miniature baseball stadium, with the defendant’s seat as the batter’s box. That was where Lloyd would sit. To his right was the place of the first baseman. That was the seating for the head of the House of Peers, who will question Lloyd today. On his left, the third baseman, was the place for the legislative officials offering the queen advice regarding the sentence. And the center, the place for the pitcher, was reserved for the queen.

And the rest of the audience will watch the trial from afar, from the spot of the outfielders. Tsk. What’s worse is how the seats are dramatically tiered. Mine is the lowest one.

The defendant’s seat was tiered in the lowest. About 10 feet deep. The room was structured with a steep, much like a college lecture hall, so everyone, minus the defendant, looked down at the defendant.

Aha. It’s designed to psychologically pressure the defendant. Here, all their crimes and wrongdoings will be spelled out. Their punishments will be discussed while everyone stares down at the defendant. By doing that, the defendant will naturally feel small. With this kind of terrible pressure, anyone’s shoulders will droop by reflex.

Lloyd fought hard not to smile. Who cared if the seats were designed this way?

Victory will be mine anyway.

Lloyd decided to get this trial over with, resume his construction project, and harvest the pieces of tauranga as soon as possible. Meanwhile, the queen’s declaration of open session was made.

“I hereby declare.”

Queen Magentano rose from her seat, and everyone followed suit. Grave silence ruled over the courtroom before being broken by the queen’s declaration.

“I openly declare that today’s trial, which takes place on the ninth of this month, will discuss his responsibility for the leakage of frigid air of the Heart of Winter at the palatial repair construction site, the loss of the Heart of Winter, and various other matters. But before anything else, I want to express some words of comfort to the royal magicians, guard knights, and laborers who suffered from the accident.”

Her gaze raked through the courtroom. And then it stopped on Lloyd.

“First, the defendant.”

“Yes, Your Majesty.” Lloyd immediately lowered his head.

She asked, “I shall check your identity first. Are you the successor and eldest son of the Frontera family, Lloyd Frontera?”

“Yes, I am, Your Majesty.”

“Good. Let it be known that you have the right to deny making statements based on your subjective judgment and to refuse to answer questions throughout the trial. However, there will be no one but you who will be held responsible for how you exercise your rights. Does the defendant understand the meaning of my words?”

“I do, Your Majesty.” Lloyd lowered his head.

“Good. Then. Duke Toscano.” Her gaze moved to Duke Toscano. A short, chubby man who looked like a bulldog gave his respects to the queen. Duke Toscano was the head of the House of Peers.

He’s the one who accused me. Though it wasn’t his sole decision.

Lloyd smiled. The man was a heavyweight who represented the central nobles of the kingdom. He was also related to the imperial family by blood. The queen continued giving her declaration and notice speech.

“Duke Toscano, you shall conduct yourself in the courtroom solely guided by the aim to realize the grave law and justice of the kingdom. Moreover, you shall not involve your personal feelings in the trial, and in the process of proving the charges outlined in the bill of indictment, you are forbidden to ask leading questions that will prompt the defendant to give the answers of your desire and must respect and pursue the process of fair trial. Do you understand the meaning of my words?”

“I do, Your Majesty.”

Duke Toscano bowed politely. The queen’s face froze a little, and a faint glint of concern appeared in her eyes.

Will he be able to exonerate himself from today’s trial?

The more she thought about it, the more worried she became. A sense of doubt filled her heart, mainly because of Duke Toscano. This was the man who submitted the bill of indictment against Lloyd as the head of the House of Peers. And he was set to interrogate Lloyd today.

Duke Toscano is a stubborn fundamentalist.

Much like his bulldog-like appearance, he was an extremely stubborn man. A stickler for rules. He rarely deviated from the routines he had set for himself. And yet, that man was here to charge Lloyd for his crimes. That was the part the queen was worried about the most.

He will not easily let go of Frontera. He will be like a hellbent bulldog who will let go of his bite only if Lloyd’s crimes are paid for.

Moreover, he wasn’t the only problem. There was something else, too.

The entire House of Peers supports him.

The mere thought of that made her sigh. The thing was, even if she was presiding over the trial today and giving the final verdict, she wasn’t in the position to dismiss every charge against Lloyd and side with him.

That is how serious the matter is. One of the divine items of the royal house was lost.

The Heart of Winter was, no doubt, a crucial object. It was the artifact given by Ice Dragon Tyrannus to the founding king, Mikael Magentano. A strategic weapon that held the power of a winter. That was what was lost. Its loss was national, so priceless that it couldn’t be translated into value. As such, even if she was the queen and Lloyd was the meritorious retainer, she couldn’t cover for him without a clear ground. What was more, the process of how the Heart of Winter came to be used rose as another contending issue.

It can be used only if the kingdom is on the brink of collapse or if the queen requests to use it. It can only be used with unanimous agreement from the nobles in the House of Peers.

As the protocol dictated, Queen Magentano filed a request to use the Heart of Winter. At first, the nobles expressed dismay. The palatial repair didn’t constitute a national emergency. That was the initial response of the House of Peers, extremely obvious and reasonable. And so, she met up with each noble to persuade them, sometimes sweet-talking or bribing them, until she reached a consensus. But now, despite the political risk she allowed herself, the item was gone. So, technically speaking, she was slightly responsible for the loss.

Therefore, I cannot stand by Lloyd’s side so openly. That will make the House of Peers turn its back on me.

A grimace appeared on her face, the lines of her forehead wrinkling. Even though she was a queen with powerful political power, she couldn’t entirely ignore the House of Peers. The national affair went smoothly only if the monarch’s leadership harmonized with the cooperation of the House of Peers. That was how her legitimacy could be maintained and also why she was worried.

She was worried that Duke Toscano was dead serious about charging Lloyd for his sins. If so, how should she protect Lloyd without showing her favor? While she was wrapped up in her concern, Duke Toscano rose from his seat to speak.

“My deepest respects to the sacred courtroom and to Her Majesty the Great. I shall start my statement. First, defendant Lloyd Frontera holds the impressive accomplishment of preventing an assassination attempt against the queen, and he is truly a role model for taking in tens and thousands of refugees after protecting his fiefdom from the disaster.”

What?!

Queen Magentano couldn’t believe her ears. Why was he complimenting Lloyd now? The series of compliments deepened her frown as she tried to figure out what the man was up to. Meanwhile, he continued.

“It does not end there. Lloyd Frontera inspired others by confronting the catastrophe that fell on Namaran without hesitation. He also came before anyone else to help Her Majesty when the Bone Dragon attacked. For his accomplishments, he was appointed as a meritorious retainer of the royal house. But that is not all. Even with the unfortunate accident of the Heart of Winter…”

“…”

Queen Magentano cocked her head to the side. Something was definitely odd here. Even the jury exchanged looks as they whispered among themselves. Unperturbed, Duke Toscano went on to praise Lloyd.

“Even when the divine item went on a rampage… He didn’t cower despite the terrifying situation… Rolled up his sleeves to save the laborers, the precious people of the royal house… He did his best…“

“…”

Could it be that he had lost it? Queen Magentano started to become seriously concerned about him. This whole event was unfolding very differently from what she had in mind.

I doubt he’s gone mad.

Originally, the duke was supposed to accuse, rebuke, and question Lloyd Frontera. That was why he had submitted the bill of indictment and came to this place. So, it was a wonder as he was busy overlooking and complimenting Lloyd from the start.

I cannot wrap my head around this at all.

Could it be that she was dreaming? She seriously considered the thought. That was how dumbfounding and dismaying this situation was for her.

But she wasn’t alone. The jury was as befuddled as her. They all exchanged glances, saying among themselves that the duke was strange today and acting like Lloyd’s lawyer. They whispered and murmured among themselves in confusion.

While the duke went on, a satisfied smile hung on Lloyd’s mouth. And he thought: It was the same for anywhere in the world. Blood, education, and regional connections. Plus, blackmailing and bribing. With all those combined, anything was possible in this world.
