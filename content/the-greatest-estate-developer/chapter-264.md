# Mermaids Love Jjimjilbangs (3)

---

And thus, the seabed jjimjilbang construction began.

“So,” said Lloyd, “From now on, bring the giant tube worms’ secretions.”

“The worms’ secretions?” Javier looked at Lloyd with disgust all over his face.

“Yeah.”

“How much should I bring?”

“As much as you can. Keep it coming until I tell you to stop,” chuckled Lloyd as he held out a conch shell that was incomparably larger than average. It was large enough to fit a human into it.

Taking the conch from Lloyd, Javier sighed, “I see.” He wasn’t entirely happy about it. But he was left with no choice. After all, every single thing the fake Lloyd did ended up being the correct choice, and Javier knew that would probably be the case this time too.

He always does something strange. I cannot wrap my head around his ridiculous orders or bizarre actions. But sometime later, his orders and actions are always revealed to be backed by clear reason and purpose.

That was how Lloyd had been saving the Frontera family up until today. Javier presumed it’d be the same this time as well.

It is beyond me how building a jjimjilbang facility deep down in this water is relevant to stopping the restoration of destiny, but I should just follow his orders. I’ll trust him.

Recalling his previous observations of fake Lloyd until this moment, Javier obediently moved forward and approached the boiling hydrothermal vents.

Shhhh! When he approached the hundreds of worms, they reacted by secreting sticky liquid. Javier immediately held up his conch and filled it up just in time.

This liquid is sticky. I suppose they usually use it to build their houses. It is like glue.

At last, Javier understood, though vaguely, why Lloyd instructed him to collect the giant tube worms’ secretions. It was to be used in place of cement. From then onward, Javier filled the conch up and moved it to the construction site, where Lloyd mixed it up with mud. He then slathered the mixture onto the floor, walls, and ceiling of the cave.

Tralalala. Lalala.

Lloyd worked on his giant tube worm cement as he hummed, closely resembling a construction plasterer as he did. And much like one, Lloyd adeptly covered almost the entire cave with cement. Even though it was an immense amount of work for one person, Lloyd’s stamina was well beyond that of an average person.

Huff! Hufff! Huffff! His manaheart was already at the level of a mid-level sword expert, and on top of that, he was equipped with a triple circle. As a result, he was a tireless ball of energy that was able to work nonstop. All the while, he didn’t forget to secure and lay the pipes that would draw up the hot water from the hydrothermal vents.

“Hey,” called Lloyd, “This is enough worm secretion. It’s time for something else.”

“Should I bring the worms’ shells now?” asked Javier.

“Oh! I don’t even need to ask anymore.”

“I hold a bounty of experiences with you, Master Lloyd.” Javier sighed, almost in a grumbling manner. “Survey. Design. Basic construction. Clear the ground, set up the structure, and pour cement. Finish that up. If pipes are necessary, lay them down when it’s around our current time. I roughly know the order of how construction sites are operated now.”

“Is that so?”

“Yes.”

“Wow, good for you,” Lloyd said without bothering to hide his sarcasm. “I’m jealous. You must be happy about it. Congratulations, sincerely.”

Javier gritted his teeth, trying to make sense of how he had wound up here in the seabed for a construction project. An intense wave of dismay and self-hatred crept up from the bottom of his soul. Despite that, however, he didn’t idle and slack off. He dutifully transferred the shells he collected in advance and tidied them up with Lloyd. He cut and refined them into their necessary size, glued them together with cement, and connected them to five hydrothermal vent chimneys.

“Hey! Push!”

Crack! Lloyd and Javier moved in sync, pushing the pipes while maintaining distance from the vent to avoid getting burned. The pipes fit perfectly and covered the hydrothermal vents.

Brrr! Brrr! The 752°F, highly acidic hydrogen sulfide, lead, and iron-enriched seawater traveled into the pipes, and Lloyd activated his survey skill right away.

[Scanning will begin.]

Bzzt! His eyes meticulously surveyed the pipes to ensure they were absolutely free from any leakage of hot water or toxic substance. His eyes shined like they did when he was picking out a cut of Australian sirloin steak on his payday. A moment later, Lloyd sighed in relief.

Great. There aren’t any leaks.

There were no leaks from the hydrothermal vent, the inner area of the jjimjilbang, and even all the way to the cave exit. There wasn’t a single leak. The construction was a complete success. From then on, Lloyd cracked down and devoted himself to the construction with greater zeal, starting with laying down the pipes. Most of the pipes received an additional layer of cement to avoid unintentionally having any heat escape from somewhere. Lloyd made sure that the heat would only flow to the rooms inside the facility. With this, a clear change had been made.

Oh! It’s gotten hotter.

The temperature inside spiked dramatically. Once that was taken care of, Lloyd moved on to refine and touch the inside of the cave, first building a door out of gigantic clam and crab shells. And then he divided the whole place into rooms with varying themes: a traditional red clay room, a seaweed-filled seawater room, an emerald room filled with glowing plankton glass marvels, a tropical beach room covered in the white sand, a Himalayan glacier room, a spin-dryer room, and an extreme bungee-jump room. Lloyd took extra care to ensure that the merpeople would not get bored. And lastly, he constructed a resting area that was as big as an auditorium, which was set to be the main facility of the jjimjilbang.

So, this would be the place for merpeople to laze around and read comics as they eat boiled eggs.

After 21 days, the construction was finally complete. It was hardly an exaggeration to say it had been done at the speed of lightning.

Haah. What a relief. I was able to finish quickly because there was a cave nearby. I got lucky.

And the inside of the cave was equally impressive. It almost seemed like a gift from mother nature to build a jjimjilbang down here. Lloyd was happy to skip the foundation work that usually took up the most amount of time and energy during construction. As a result, he was able to completely focus on installing and laying down the hydrothermal vents as well as the interior construction work.

Now, it’s time to welcome customers.

Now that the jjimjilbang was completed, it was time to bring the merpeople to the place. This job was entrusted to Rotorua.

“What now? When are the merpeople who promised to be here coming?” asked Lloyd.

“Oh, the thing is…” The night the jjimjilbang construction was finished, Rotorua came to Lloyd with a troubled look on his face. “Um… The thing is, I did receive a promise from some of my acquaintances a few days ago that they will be here as soon as this place opens. They promised, really…”

“But what?” blurted Lloyd, his voice dripping with alarm. “Wait…”

Rotorua looked down.

“They said they will come next time.” Lloyd instantly realized what was going on.

“I am sorry. I have nothing to say for myself.” Rotorua’s shoulders drooped.

Lloyd clicked his tongue.

Tsk, tsk. I had a bad feeling about this, and I was right.

Without a doubt, Rotorua was an outsider who didn’t have many friends. So, Lloyd didn’t hold high expectations of him. Even when Rotorua beamed proudly at Lloyd and boasted to the latter that his acquaintances were coming, Lloyd told himself, “Let’s see.” And sure enough, he was right.

Them saying that they’ll come the next time they can mean they will not come at all. It’s the same as the empty promises people make in Korea to grab a meal with someone soon.

However, Lloyd wasn’t disappointed. He instead tapped Rotorua’s shoulders.

“Don’t worry,” Lloyd comforted. “Do not be too discouraged. I have another plan in mind.”

“Um,” replied Rotora as he lifted his head and looked at Lloyd. “Did you prepare for this to happen in advance?”

“I sure did.” Lloyd’s voice seemed to indicate that it was only obvious. “The merpeople are not familiar with the concept of a jjimjilbang. This should be their first encounter with one. And not only that, it was constructed by Javier and me, people from the surface. So-”

“You expected that the merpeople would not regard it so positively,” Rotorua broke in.

“Yes,” said Lloyd, “I have been thinking that the only way to gain traction would be to stimulate their curiosity and excitement.”

“Hmm… So, what plan do you have in mind?”

“It’s this one. Hold on.” Lloyd lifted his finger and headed to the counter, where he took out two giant lumps. Rotorua had to look closely to make out what they were.

“Is that… the shell of the gigantic lobster and crab?” observed Rotorua.

“Yes, that is right.” Lloyd grinned. “Javier and I will be promoting the jjimjilbang wearing this.”

Javier, who had been listening to the conversation without saying anything, flinched. He then looked at Lloyd as if he was a madman. Lloyd smiled more shamelessly.

“What? What? What is it?”

“…”

“Don’t insult me with your gaze. Speak,” challenged Lloyd.

“Can I insult you with my mouth?” Javier finally asked.

“What’s wrong?” Lloyd picked up the giant, human-sized crab shell. “Is it because I’m going to make you wear this?”

A look of anger appeared on Javier’s face. “I am not a clown, Master Lloyd.”

“Yeah,” acknowledged Lloyd, “I know. You’re a knight.”

“I am. I am a knight as you said. I am glad that you are aware of this fact. So why do you suggest that I, a knight, wear such an unsightly thing?” asked Javier indignantly.

“For the sake of the family you are loyal to?”

Javier frowned in confusion.

“Wear this, and everything will be solved,” promised Lloyd. “The Frontera family will further prosper and even be able to avoid danger. You know, it’s not like we’re doing this for me. I don’t want to do this either.”

Javier merely stared at Lloyd, trying to comprehend where he was getting at.

“But do you know what will happen if you refuse? Oh! That was the moment.” Lloyd suddenly started to talk theatrically, even making hand gestures. “No one knew that this moment marked the beginning of the Frontera family’s downfall. But the circle of tragedy had been turning long before then… You will be hearing a history teacher saying these words 200 years from today.”

“That is just outrageous-”

“All because Sir Asrahan did not cooperate,” Lloyd chimed in, this time with more effect. “He failed to attract the merpeople. The jjimjilbang went under. The jewel couldn’t be found. The family fell. Society collapsed. The villagers plunged into misery.”

“…”

“One boy in the miserable fiefdom was forced to starve all day. His puppy starved together with him. The two tightly embraced each other, only relying on each other’s body heat to get through the cold night. But not a single person came to their aid. And just like that, the boy and the dog slowly but surely drew in their final breaths of air. And right before he died, a drop of tear welled up under his eyes. He whispered to himself, his last whisper sad. Oh, oh… Only if Sir Asrahan had put on the gigantic crab shell…”

“…”

“Hearing those last words, the dog whimpered and licked the boy’s cheeks. But the boy didn’t move. That is when the dog realized that the time had come for him to follow the boy. So, the dog-”

“I will wear it,” Javier huffed, shaking his head.

“All right, good thinking. My little puppy.”

Javier’s face darkened.

“Oops, I made a mistake because you interrupted me in the middle of the story. Ehem. Good thinking, Javier.”

Javier clamped his mouth shut, and Lloyd happily handed over the shell as if he had been waiting for this moment. Javier could not hate the latter enough. But at the same time, he had to admit it.

Ha. It is beyond me what’s going on, but…

It was undeniably true that the fake Lloyd was doing all of this to save the family. As such, actively helping this eccentric madman was the right thing to do, even if his ways were unconventional and absurd. If not, the lord was set to pass away in three years’ time.

“…”

At last, Javier threw away his pride and decided to wear the thing. He put on the shell and inserted his hands into its pincers, which covered half of his torso except for his legs. Meanwhile, Lloyd also threw on a giant lobster shell and went further by wearing a sign he had prepared in advance. The sign had “Grand Opening Event! Free Jjimjilbang Entrance!” written in a large font.

“All right, let’s go,” hurried Lloyd at Javier with his pincers.

“…”

“What are you doing? Come, hurry up and follow me.”

“…”

Lloyd glanced behind his shoulder as he waddled in his lobster costume. Javier, who was behind him, had to desperately fight against his desire to resign on the spot.

Haah. Patience. This is all for the lord and his family.

With that resolution in mind, he waddled over to Lloyd and entered the merpeople’s city. And when they settled in the most crowded area in the egg, Javier heard Lloyd’s enthusiastic shouting to bring in customers.

“Everyone!” bawled Lloyd. “This is a rare opportunity! To celebrate the first-ever jjimjilbang in the Mermaid Kingdom! It’s a completely free event! Don’t you want to safely relax your muscles without having the scent of sulfur assaulting your nose? Don’t your aching joints bother you?! Come over and enjoy yourselves, my dearest customers!” Lloyd clapped and bawled in excitement.

“…”

Javier suddenly felt a shaft of reverence toward Lloyd for being able to scream those words without shame. At the same time, he thought to himself..

I don’t think I’ll be as effective.

Indeed, Javier was right. The ridiculous costume. Lloyd’s exaggerated shouts. Sure, they were able to make the merpeople pause their swimming. There was a crowd gathering around them in curiosity. But that was all there was to it.

They are laughing at us.

They were laughing at Lloyd because they were amused by his clownish manners. They were simply giving momentary attention to them as if they were looking at a circus attraction. Not a single merperson in the crowd seemed interested in the jjimjilbang.

What on earth was he thinking…?

No matter how Javier looked at it, this was a hopeless affair. A desperate, unsightly effort. Javier licked his lips regretfully and wondered if he should stop fake Lloyd, so he could at least be saved from the blatant mockery. The moment he had that thought, Lloyd came to Javier’s side.

“Plus, if you all come to my jjimjilbang!” Lloyd dramatically shouted and held out his hands and put them on Javier’s shoulders. Then he gave a hard tug downward. “You can admire this face as you keep enjoying your treatments!”

Crack! As soon as Lloyd pressed down on Javier’s costume, Javier’s head broke out of the shell like a pimple popping out of someone’s skin. And with that, his face was revealed.

“…!”

And his face had a phenomenal effect on the crowd.
