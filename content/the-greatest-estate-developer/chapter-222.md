# An All-out War (1)

---

Argh!!

Targa was baffled. It was an abrupt turn of events. The volcanic ash burst from the ground out of nowhere, and he was simply barreling through the plume of smoke when his 19th tailbone was struck twice and shattered to pieces.

What the…!

Targa was incredulous. He possessed the body of the Bone Dragon, which couldn’t receive damage from the aura swords of sword masters. More accurately, that was what he used to believe.

Dammit!

Targa fluttered his wings in desperation, but it was too late. He lost his entire tail as his 19th tailbone was crushed off. As a result, he completely lost his balance.

Argh…!

In spite of himself, his body lurched forward, and he was getting closer to crashing into the ground with every passing second.

…!

Just like that, he rammed into the ground at the same sonic speed he was flying with. The crash created a 2624-foot furrow, shattering the trees, rocks, and everything surrounding it. He collapsed amidst the storm of dust from his fall. But a little later, Targa, the Bone Dragon, moved.

Boom! Propping up its foreleg on the ground, the Bone Dragon hoisted its body.

Wham! But it collapsed to the ground midway up. The reason was simple. The abrupt loss of its tail ruined the dragon’s balance.

We did it!

Queen Magentano exclaimed inwardly and clenched her fists as she saw the dragon from a distance. It worked. Lloyd’s plan really worked.

That was close.

The queen recalled her moment of anxiety just a second ago. She and Javier had laid a series of attacks in the smoke Bangul produced with her volcanic explosion. In the last moment, she flung her body to strike the 19th tailbone of the dragon with her aura sword. She almost lost grip of her sword from the repulsive power that fired back at her as she struck the dragon’s tailbone.

It must have been because of the mana that sustains the body of the Bone Dragon. Its sturdiness was incomparable to slicing through granite.

It wasn’t just an ordinary piece of granite. She felt like she was striking down on a metal slab that was 6.5 feet thick. What would have happened if she hadn’t undergone stringent training after her assassination attempt?

I’d have failed.

She would have let her sword go after failing to resist the repulsive power during the most critical moment. And the dragon’s tailbone wouldn’t have been fully destroyed. However, she did it. The draconian training she went through until this point wasn’t all for nothing.

“Whew…”

Queen Magentano fixed her grip as she took a breath, and her gaze flicked over to the thick cloud of dust. Javier was there.

“Then should we go finish the dragon once and for all,” asked the queen heavily.

“Yes, Your Majesty,” Javier replied.

This was their chance. It was likely their only chance. The Bone Dragon couldn’t even stand up after losing its tail. But there was no guarantee that it wouldn’t regain its balance soon.

It’ll adapt if we give it time.

There was no way that the Bone Dragon would forever be unable to stand for thousands of years just because it had no tail. In one hour or even less than that, the dragon would adjust just fine. As such, it had to be finished before then.

“Javier Asrahan,” called the queen. “You and Lloyd Frontera can read the flow of mana, I presume?”

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“I knew it.”

The queen marveled. Raising the corner of her lips, she asked, “Then I shall ask you one thing. What part of the dragon has the most active mana flow?”

“Its skull, Your Majesty.”

“That is what we must destroy.”

That spot would be its greatest weakness. With luck, they would be able to completely bring it down to its knees. The queen and Javier sped their pace toward the crash location of the dragon.

“With all your strength. In one stroke. As soon as possible.”

“I shall keep that in mind, Your Majesty.”

The large shadow of the Bone Dragon, repeatedly rising and falling, came into view through a thick fog of dust. They gradually ventured closer. Without reservation, they sprinted and leaped toward it.

Jump! The queen jumped first, followed by Javier, and landed on its back. The two sword masters sprinted side by side along the Bone Dragon’s backbone toward its skull.

Like a while ago.

The queen threw a glance at Javier. He nodded and increased his speed. Then he jumped off the dragon’s back, which was violently flailing like a giant ocean wave. He activated the circles around his pulsating heart, clashed them, and let out a blast after transferring the energy from the fierce clash into his sword. Boom!

The three strands of consecutive mana blasts flew toward the back of the dragon’s skull. To be more exact, they charged toward the part that connects the skull and neck. Thwack!

The Bone Dragon flinched along with the explosion. But it was not weak enough to receive any damage. So, Javier prepared more mana blasts. Many, many more. Thwack! Thwack! Thwaaack!

…!

Javier fired. He fired once more. Then again in the same spot. He aimed from different angles but was only after one spot.

Bam! Thud! Thump! Boom! Crack! Since one wasn’t enough, Javier fired twice. Thrice. Ten times. 20 times. 30 times. All in the same spot. Of course, the Bone Dragon didn’t just sit back and accept the attacks.

Ack!

It writhed its body and moved its long neck. It struggled to avoid the mana blasts hitting the same spot, and in the process, it managed to attempt a counterattack by moving its head a few times. But all of its attempts failed. Javier was too small compared to the Bone Dragon. He was nimble and delicate in his movement. No matter how much it struggled by jerking its neck violently, Javier succeeded in avoiding its blows and firing a mana blast in the same place over and over again. But the Bone Dragon was still unable to stand up on its two feet, so an effective counterattack was nearly impossible. Anyhow, Javier launched a series of unilateral attacks on the dragon. In other words, it was being tortured.

Even Lloyd, who watched the scene in the air, clicked his tongue.

Wow. This is nuts. This is absolutely nuts.

Lloyd admired the sight with his whole heart. He had watched the whole scene from the moment the Bone Dragon crashed into the ground. He meant to wait and see if he should fly away, happy to have wounded the dragon’s tail. Or ride the momentum and continue his attacks. But the queen and Javier began to charge first. From then on, Lloyd watched the two sword masters stage a fierce rally against the dragon with slight anxiety. But now seeing the current situation, Lloyd was at ease.

Actually, this makes me pity the Bone Dragon.

That was how tenacious and merciless Javier’s attack was. Javier thoroughly practiced the Three Rules of Merciless Beating: Strike the most painful spot, strike the same spot, and strike to no end.

Looks like he’ll really defeat the Bone Dragon.

A faint hope appeared in him. The feeling only solidified when he saw the queen bracing for their final blow near Javier who was mercilessly unleashing his attacks.

Is the queen planning on using the giant aura sword she used a while ago?

Its width looked to be over dozens of inches wide and its length well over 13 feet. It was quite literally a giant sword made from a swordmaster’s aura.

It was different from an ordinary aura sword.

Normally, an aura sword only had a thin layer of aura covering it like a lightsaber. At best, it would only be about three feet long from the tip of the blade. But the queen’s sword was different.

She might be close to becoming a grandmaster.

That thought just popped into his head. Though hers was weaker than the awe-instilling aura Javier created after he became the grandmaster in the novel, it was clear that the queen was getting near that stage.

She must be getting tremendously stronger.

At this rate, Sir Kyle, the previous royal guard commander, wouldn’t be able to put up any kind of fight against her. She might even be able to take down two sword masters on her own.

Lloyd then noticed the queen lift her sword. With that, her sword manifested a uniquely large aura sword. Its brute strength contrasted Javier’s meticulousness. The aura sword was powerful and massive. It stood over 15 feet tall. It shone menacingly like a weapon made for a siege. On the other hand, the skull of the Bone Dragon was weakened due to Javier’s series of mana blasts.

Maybe. Just maybe, she might be able to destroy it.

A faint hope of victory appeared. That was when Lloyd figured he should lend a hand. So, he encouraged Ggoming to fly low and prepared to launch a triple mana blast, determined to fire away should the queen miss her aim. He let Ggoming glide through the air while making his resolve.

The fight beneath him neared in a breath, and Lloyd noticed the queen hurtling toward the dragon, her aura word stretched out. He noticed her zooming off to strike the back of its skull. And then he noticed another scene. He noticed the Bone Dragon strike Javier with its giant tail. Thwack!

“…!” Lloyd saw clearly. It was the tail that had been broken off. The tail that was attached below the crushed 19th tailbone snapped off from its body during the crash. It was left on the ground. But it moved. Without any warning. Suddenly and quickly.

It whipped around like a giant chain. In a flash, it slammed into Javier.

“Ah…!”

That was the only sound that came out of Lloyd’s mouth. The scene was too unexpected. Watch out! Look out! Move away! He wasn’t able to give them any warnings. All Lloyd could do was gawk at the unbelievable scene of Javier catapulting hundreds of feet away with a booming sound. Soon afterward, Javier fell. Somewhere outside of Lloyd’s field of vision.

“Oh…”

Lloyd couldn’t believe it. What did he just witness? Was that for real? Javier was fighting tooth and nail just a second ago. He was cornering the Bone Dragon by tenaciously shooting out mana blasts. A little more. Just some more. And Lloyd thought they’d be able to defeat the dragon. He was about to help them.

What happened?

Unbelievable. He refused to believe it.

“Javier!”

His shout came a second late. No, he didn’t remember how he came to be shouting. The moment he shouted, he was already encouraging Ggoming to rush and see if Javier was all right. Hurry up, Ggoming, seriously, hurry up. Ggoming hurried at his words. But he couldn’t fly in Javier’s direction. The Bone Dragon was flying now, fluttering its wings and glowering at Lloyd. With its lost tail restored it was blocking Ggoming’s way.

“…”

No way. Does it know my intentions?

Lloyd could feel it with certainty. The dragon was aware that Javier was a threat, and that was why it was blocking his way, lest Javier get treated and well. That would only be trouble for the creature. But still…

"You see, I have to get to him.”

And in a hurry. Javier was slammed in the face with the dragon’s giant tail. It was different from when he was hit by the Gigatitan in Cremo. Javier was able to skillfully lessen the impact of the blow then. This time, however, he was directly hit and flew hundreds of feet away from the impact. Even if he was strong, that would leave him seriously injured at least. So, he could be in danger of losing his life now. Lloyd had to get to him as soon as possible.

After all, I have the Asrahan Core Technique!

Lloyd could save him as long as he is breathing. He just needed to reverberate Javier’s mana circle using the Asrahan Core Technique. That would circulate his mana by force and amplify the natural healing power in his body. This method could bring someone on the brink of death to a less critical state. And bring someone in a moderately critical state to be an averagely ill patient. So, he had to hurry. Now.

“Move!”

Lloyd’s bawling came out involuntarily. Was it because Lloyd thought it a great loss to lose Javier? Did he not like the idea of losing his most useful worker and knight? No. Lloyd’s heartbeat pounded. To be honest, he was scared.

Javier, you bastard!

Lloyd was scared. He felt a sense of emptiness in a corner of his heart. The scary nervousness ambushed his entire soul. Come to think of it, from the moment he opened his eyes and set foot in this world and met Javier for the first time, he was never away from Javier. What was more, the thought of being forever separated from Javier never crossed his mind. Never. Not even once.

Lloyd never held such a thought. He thought he’d always be with Javier for the rest of his life. Just like now, he thought they’d spend all their time together moving forward. And like they always did, they’d be by each other’s side during hard times. Happy moments. Relaxed days. They’d share all these moments as they exchanged insults and corny jokes, growing old as a lazy lord of a fiefdom and his knight. Lloyd always thought so. The thought just came naturally, without much effort from him, much like breathing. Lloyd believed that Javier would always remain by his side as that was how it had been until now.

But I took him for granted.

Lloyd realized that he had been keeping Javier by his side, enjoying his company and treating him as an equal. And now, he was finally able to recognize why.

Javier.

Lloyd finally saw what Javier was. At last. A guard knight? Useful construction equipment? Strong sword master? No, more than anything else, he was…

“Move, you bastard. I have to check on my friend.”

Lloyd’s gaze turned heavy and grim as he stared at the menacing Bone Dragon that blocked his way.
