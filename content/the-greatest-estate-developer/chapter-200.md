# A Cunning Man’s Survival Tactic (3)

---

The following morning rolled around.

Whew. Goodbye to this place. Finally.

Count Ventura tearfully looked up at the sultan’s palace that had grown on him.

I never expected to stay this long.

It had been almost five months since he arrived here at the order of the queen. At the time, he thought he would negotiate with the sultan in no time. But he realized that wasn’t the case when he set foot on the land. Their reaction was poles apart from what he expected. He was intentionally ignored and deprived of proper treatment. What would have happened if Lloyd, the eldest son of the Frontera family, didn’t take the matter into his own hands?

We would still be far from getting into a negotiation.

His delegation would still be bearing the ongoing ignorance and mistreatment. The deal with the sultan would still be unimaginable. Otherwise, they might have had to return home empty-handed unlike now.

I am relieved. I am truly relieved.

The negotiation was a huge success. If they went back home now, they would be commended by the queen.

And it’s all thanks to Lloyd, that young fellow.

Was Lloyd able to slip out of Ahinsya unharmed? Had he succeeded in safely running away from the sultan last night? The count was anxious for a second. But a chuckle soon escaped him. Seeing that he hadn’t heard anything about the matter, it was safe to assume that Lloyd didn’t get captured, at least. Moreover, judging from the atmosphere of the palace, something bigger must’ve happened than a member of a foreign delegation going missing.

“I heard something happened last night. Is that true?” asked the count expressionlessly.

“Yes, Your Excellency. It appears it is.” A senior envoy nodded. “There was a stir in the palace all morning. So, I quietly asked someone I was acquainted with.”

“What did your acquaintance say?” said the count, interested.

“One of the princesses has gone missing.”

“A daughter of the sultan has gone missing?” emitted the count, his eyes widening.

“Yes, Your Excellency.”

“Was she kidnapped?”

“I do not think so. It seems…”

“That what?” hurried the count, disliking the man’s hesitation.

“She left the palace on her own,” finally finished the man.

“Ha…? Then she ran away from home?”

“That is right, Your Excellency.”

“Huh! Hahaha.”

Count Ventura burst into laughter. A princess who ran away from the sultan’s relentlessly secure palace?

That explains the unsettling mood here.

The count nodded. Truly, the sultan’s palace was different from usual. There was something unsettling about it even when the Magentano delegation was leaving the palace. The sultan didn’t even escort them out, let alone give them a grand farewell.

But it is fine since I did not expect either in the first place.

Plus, the disappearance of the sultan’s daughter had absolutely nothing to do with him and his delegation.

“Let us get going right away.”

Count Ventura led the delegation, leaving the palace at the modest farewell of officials of the palace. Soon, they were out of Ahinsya and took the path back home. Their journey was smooth and safe. They passed the same course of direction and city they used to travel to Ahinsya. Taking only the safest route in the desert, they finally crossed the border. And that night, they joined Lloyd and Javier, who were waiting for them near the border.

“Have you been waiting long?” queried the count, who was glad to see Lloyd.

“Ah, to be honest, yes, quite a bit.”

Lloyd, who was camping beneath the tent set up between the boulders in the wasteland some distance away from the border, shrugged and glanced around in happiness at Count Ventura and his delegation.

“You all safely made it out with no one dying at the hands of the sultan. I am very relieved,” stated Lloyd.

“Hahaha. Yes, that is a relief. You see, unlike someone else, we are not coveted by the sultan.”

“Hmm?” mused Lloyd as he narrowed his eyes. “Your words seem to carry a sting, Your Excellency.”

“How can it not?” said Count Ventura with a smile. “I am being nice now, considering how much you made me sweat…”

“Is that so, Your Excellency?”

“Yes, of course,” assured the count. “Even now, when I think about the sultan’s expression and gaze after he heard that you contracted a disease and returned home… Whew.”

“You must have had a hard time.”

“It was nothing compared to how you rushed off in the middle of the night with barely any preparations,” consoled Count Ventura. “How are you? I hope the trip wasn’t too difficult.”

“Thankfully, it wasn’t bad,” replied Lloyd with a shrug. Then he pointed at Javier with his chin. “It’s thanks to that fellow. The presence of a swordmaster is undeniably useful.”

It was true. The night he decided to leave Ahinsya, the best tool he had packed was Javier. The sword master. If anybody pursued and tried to capture him, Javier was capable of breaking through the siege and escaping by force. And what if he faced a monster in the desert or wilderness?

“That is a fearsome monster in the desert. However, it has become my lunch now.” Lloyd was happy to be able to mumble those words. What was more, the title “Top Gun from the West” he had earned recently was extremely effective.

I can’t experience dehydration under any circumstances. This is true within all desert surfaces and areas with an average annual temperature of 104 °F and above.

Lloyd was skeptical at first and wondered if it would work. However, as he traversed the desert, he soon realized that this was one of the best skills he would have gained, at the very least, for this place.

I wasn’t dehydrated even when I tap-danced under the scorching sun all day.

Javier, who was crossing the desert with Lloyd, didn’t hide his shock from him. Even a sword master like him was gradually getting tired. So, how was Master Lloyd perfectly fine? As Javier shot such a question, Lloyd smugly retorted back with a piece of advice.

“Dude, it’s all about willpower. Your willpower.”

Anyway, thanks to the title, rushing off in the middle of the night turned out to be unexpectedly easy. There wasn’t any problem while they were waiting for the delegation to arrive either.

“Anyway,” Lloyd went on, “I am truly glad to see you all safe and unharmed.”

“The feeling is mutual. Oh, and…”

Count Ventura smiled in satisfaction after pausing.

“I haven’t shown my appreciation for you until now. This comes late, but let me say it at this time. I truly thank you,” confessed the count.

“Oh, thank you, Your Excellency.”

“No, the gratitude is ours. I was wrong to regard you as materialistic at first.”

“Haha. But I am materialistic,” corrected Lloyd without any sense of shame in his voice.

At Lloyd’s banter, the count recoiled and shook his head. “No. You are not. You are our savior. Without you, the negotiation with the sultan would have been impossible. No one among us thought of splurging the Padashar to grab the sultan’s attention.

Moreover, the construction you completed after signing a contract with the sultan, the…”

“You mean the Qanat?”

“Yes, the Qanat. I know that was what opened the negotiation table. If your construction wasn’t a success, we could have never even dreamed of negotiating with the sultan.”

The count spoke from his heart. It was true. While Lloyd was gone to the Kandahar region for the Qanat construction, the count initially believed the talk would come through after some months of waiting. But as days passed, he found that wasn’t the case. One month. Two months. Four months. Through his stay in the palace, he was able to understand their intentions and realized that the palatial official’s mocking words to wait a minimum six months couldn’t be taken literally.

Six months? That would’ve been far from enough. I doubt there would’ve been an agreement even if we waited for six years.

The count was confident of it. The sultan had no intention of ever meeting them in the first place. He had despaired when he made this realization. He had cursed himself for his incompetence.

“I saw the prospect of us unable to fulfill the queen’s mission and the negotiation reaching a stalemate. I saw how we would return home without any good news, which would eventually lead to war and… countless young men perishing on the battlefield. My heart was torn in despair. But then you took action.”

Count Ventura gazed at Lloyd before continuing.

“All credit goes to you for the successful negotiation, preventing a war, and saving our young men’s lives.”

“Uh, um, I did it because I didn’t want my fiefdom to be forced into war-”

“That is the same thing,” concluded the count.

“Oh? Okay…”

Count Ventura spoke, emphasizing his words. Lloyd could sense from this that this gentleman had very little room for another person’s opinion. So, Lloyd was flabbergasted by what the count said next.

“I will report your achievement to Her Majesty in great detail when I return to the palace,” announced Count Ventura.

“Please… can you hold back on that…?” Lloyd begged.

“No, this is the only way I can return the favor I received from you.”

“…”

Lloyd sighed at length. I suppose I have another accomplishment under my belt. I hope I don’t get summoned before the queen for this. Lloyd wished direly to himself. Just like that, he joined the delegation and crossed the wilderness and eastern mountain range until he arrived at his home sweet home, the Frontera county. The count and countess welcomed Lloyd with open arms, and they showered him with words of concern.

“My… Are you all right?” queried the countess as she eyed Lloyd up and down.

“Oh, yes, I am.”

“How skinny you have become!” she gasped.

“Um, actually, I put on some weight because of how well I ate,” defended Lloyd. “Just a little.”

“No, no.” Countess Marbella shook her head. “Your cheeks have grown so hollow. What a disaster. Well, come on in. Let us eat first.”

“Um, I already ate while coming down from the range-”

“What do you want to eat?” she asked.

“Oh, wait for a second, are the defectors here? Led by Turtle Neck? His name is Termes-”

“Yes, he is here. Does duck sound good to you?” Countess Marbella wasn’t going to let Lloyd change the subject.

“Um… Then what about the pottery and silk? Have they arrived? I actually shipped some items to come here passing through Cremo. They should arrive at any time now, and-”

“I know nothing about pottery and silk, my dear. Beef sounds good for tonight,” she put in again.

“…”

And just like that, even before Lloyd could rest up from traveling, he had to dine by force. In between the feverish eating, he heard a piece of good news. Julian, his younger brother, graduated from his academy.

“Do not be surprised,” announced the count. “But Julian is second in his graduating class.”

“Really…?”

Lloyd’s eyes widened in genuine surprise at the dinner table when he heard the news the count shared with a beaming smile.

Wow!

Second place at the academy? That was definitely something.

Of course. What’s harder than getting into that academy is graduating there.

Lloyd’s mind flew back to the academy system briefly mentioned in The Knight of Blood and Iron. The academy was notorious for being difficult to graduate from. For example, only about 20 students managed to graduate in a class of 100 students. Moreover, graduation didn’t promise a bright future for them.

Among the graduates, only about the top ten percent are hired as officials in the royal palace. The other graduates need to take the employment exam and compete with ordinary people. But of course, the students receive more incentives than the others.

And here was Julian, graduating second in his class. This guaranteed direct employment in the palace. The South Korean equivalent would be placing second in the level five Public Officer Examination.

This fellow. It looks like he’ll be working his head off for the queen.

Lloyd inwardly mourned for his brother because the queen wasn’t the type to leave a talent alone. She was a frugal extortionist when it came to talents, and any capable individual was sucked dry for the kingdom. So Julian, who proved his intelligence by graduating second in his academy’s class, would be forced to put his nose to the grindstone to the point that he’d suffer from chronic fatigue. But he would be happy to be exploited. After all, that was the reason why he earnestly studied until now.

Lloyd, who was thinking such, asked, “Then is that brat coming back home now?”

“Yes,” replied the count. “He says he has time to spend time at home before he gets assigned. He wrote you a letter before he left the capital. Here, read it for yourself,” the count said as he handed the letter.

Lloyd read it.

To Lloyd, I graduated, and I got fairly good grades. It’s all thanks to you. I was able to give all of myself to my studies. I was able to give my all. And I realized a lot of things the last time I met you after a long while. It was all possible because of you. Thank you. See you soon. Julian, your young brother who wants to be your pride.

“…”

Lloyd carefully studied the letter. He was proud of every black word written on the white paper. It was only a few lines, but he swelled with happiness nonetheless. A part of him even felt an immense sense of contentment. Before he knew it, he had read over the letter five times already.

Graduation, huh.

Lloyd, too, wanted to graduate. He wanted to graduate, pass the exam, and land a job. But he was forced into this world before he saw the fruit of his efforts. Though he couldn’t deny that the knowledge he had acquired in his world helped him a lot, a corner of his heart was occupied with a pang of sorrow for being unable to finish his degree.

Well, I know it’s not like a diploma will feed me, but still.

South Korea was a tough society, and a piece of paper proving one’s completion of studies didn’t vouch for a life of success. It was the opposite. It was the ticket to enter the deathly, cut-throat competition. In some ways, it was more ruthless, aggressive, and brutal than this world with its spears and swords. That was what South Korea was like.

But still, I wanted to get a diploma.

Lloyd still felt regretful that he couldn’t get one when he had put in great effort to get one. He occasionally pondered on the matter. And now, here came the news of Julian performing beyond any expectations of him and giving the credit to Lloyd. The boy even added a pleasing remark that he wished to become a proud young brother to Lloyd. Lloyd felt grateful and content as he read those words. His heart swelled with pride as though he was the one who had graduated.

That rascal.

I’m going to give him a noogie on his chestnut-like head. Lloyd carefully folded the letter thinking to make sure that it wouldn’t get crumpled. Then he carefully put it in his inner pocket.

Later, even when he continued to have his meal and enjoyed reuniting with the count and countess, he had his hand placed on the middle of his chest. And as though the letter was responding to him inside, it rustled. Lloyd swelled with gratitude and joy once again every time it happened. The entire time he couldn’t erase the smile off his face.

The count and countess were overwhelmed with happiness to see their son Lloyd being so joyful. They basked at the moment, realizing that their eldest son, who used to be a troublemaker, had grown up to become a reliable pillar to their family. And their second diffident son grew up to work for the palace. But more than their achievements, they were happy that their kids grew up as faithful and healthy individuals. They grinned as though the whole world, no, the whole universe was theirs. So, the two were absolutely ignorant to the fact that an uninvited visitor, who had business with their eldest son, had arrived at the outskirts of the fiefdom.

“Whew.”

At last, the tediously long journey has come to an end. And now, I simply have to meet Lloyd Frontera and record his amazing lullaby in my magic orb and go back home. Then I’ll be able to fall asleep again.

The dignified princess of the sultan kingdom. A high-level sword expert plagued by lullaby withdrawal symptoms. Sheherazade’s dark pearly eyes shone as they looked down at the Frontera county sitting at the foot of the eastern mountain range.
