# The Sluggard’s Inner Thoughts (1)

---

A sluggard. In this world, sluggards come in various types. Case in point: there was Yeongjin, who only ate delivery food because he couldn’t be bothered to cook. And there was Daho, who only grilled one side of meat because he couldn’t be bothered to flip it with tongs. And there was the sluggish Sarin, who stayed all day in the house because removing sunblock before going to bed would just be too much work. And manager Park, the man who only parked outside buildings but never under a basement parking lot because the few steps to the elevator were just too far. As such, Lloyd was assured that he had more than his share of experience with sluggards back in Korea.

But now, his pride was falling apart. Why? Because the very first thought that crossed his mind upon seeing Dragon King Verkis at the center of the cave was…

What the heck is this wretched-looking homebody?

The thought just came to him. This sluggard was on a level of laziness that Lloyd had never seen before. He couldn’t help thinking otherwise. The king was lying flat on the sofa as if he was a part of it. Despite the fact that a human visitor came to his home and that this human was supposed to receive his support, the king didn’t rise. No, he didn’t even turn his head.

Swish. Instead, the king flickered his idle eye by point two centimeters over. And after a second of gazing…

Close.

Why is he going back to sleep?!

Lloyd exclaimed inwardly, completely flustered at what was happening. It never occurred to him that the dragon king would be a sluggard. Who would have imagined that the king would drift back to sleep without even saying a greeting? Lloyd expected the king to look stately and dominating as he was the dragon king, the ruler reigning over every dragon in the world.

What should I do? Should I wake him up? Introduce myself? Maybe he will wake up to the sound.

Such a thought crossed his mind for a second, but he shook his head in resignation.

No, I think I’m rushing into things by waking him up.

His instinct told him so. Would the dragon king get on his feet and greet Lloyd just because the latter introduced himself and woke up the former? Would the king finally welcome Lloyd and remark on bestowing the promised awards in all of his stateliness?

No, I don’t think that would be the case.

Lloyd shook his head again. He received the feeling that the dragon king was far from normal. If the king was the type to welcome Lloyd after being woken up, he wouldn’t have fallen back asleep at the sight of the visitor in the first place.

Tsk. What should I do then?

Lloyd licked his lips in dismay. His job in Hell was done, and the only thing left was to receive his rewards. That was why he had come all the way here. And Dragon King Verkis, who was supposed to reward him, was irresponsibly asleep.

“Javier,” called Lloyd.

“Yes, Master Lloyd.”

“I think we should wait first.”

Lloyd made a decision. He would act in discretion and refrain from waking the king up. If he let himself become blinded by the reward, he could ruin everything by behaving recklessly. It would be like dropping a plate of fresh food on the ground.

Trying to keep his voice to a bare minimum, Lloyd whispered, “So, you should stay far away from here.”

“What about you?” asked Javier.

“Me? Well. I’ll have to dance to his tune.”

“By that, what do you mean?”

“I have a plan,” assured Lloyd. “So just keep quiet first.”

Lloyd motioned with his hands and sent Javier far away. Then he tiptoed toward the sleeping king as quietly and discreetly as possible and studied the latter. A sense of conviction and envy surged in Lloyd’s heart.

He’s really living like an unemployed hermit, spending all of his days lazing around. And yet, he’s the richest fellow in the world. Ha… Crap. I’m jealous.

A landlord. An individual whose properties were beyond enough to sustain their life. An individual who could idle around in the corner of a room scratching their feet while still making money. An individual who could live comfortably without any grand goals or passions in life. The life of an unemployed landlord was Lloyd’s dream life. Lloyd sensed that his role model was right in front of him.

I’m jealous. I’m dying of jealousy.

His stomach churned as he looked at the sleeping Dragon King Verkis. His heart thumped, and his lower abdomen ached. Was it all because of jealousy? Envy? No.

My gosh. He must be the brother I never had that I can look up to for the rest of my life.

Lloyd believed that life was all about making good friends, that is, those who liked to show off, splurge their money, and buy you food. Lloyd believed he should keep these kinds of friends by his side and treat them kindly to ensure they wouldn’t go anywhere else. Flattery and praise were tools made for this purpose.

Service. Consideration. Respect. All of these precious and heartwarming universal values existed in the universe for moments like these. So, Lloyd collected his thoughts and picked up the high-end silk blanket lying around him, softly and gently putting it over Verkis.

My, my good friend. You might catch a cold if you sleep without anything to cover you.

Lloyd flashed a wide smile as though he was staring at a sleeping gold nugget or lucky charm. His acts of kindness didn’t end there. Like a cockroach roaming around a kitchen sink at 2 AM, he slowly wandered around with caution until he found an item of his desire, a glass made of gold, which he used to draw spring water flowing around the area. And he also found a large peacock-feather fan with which he started fanning. When a soft breeze pleasingly tickled Verkis, his face started to appear more relaxed. At the same time, a content smile bloomed on Lloyd’s face.

Perfect. It feels amazing to run a fan on its weakest setting while sleeping on a couch with a blanket over you. I guess it’s the same for humans and dragons.

Lloyd recalled his experience. A long time ago, that is, before his family suffered from financial hardship, Lloyd lived in an ordinary apartment complex that had a leather sofa meant to fit three people at once. It was fluffy and comfortable. Nothing was more comfortable than lying there and watching TV. It wasn’t entirely without any downside, however.

Since it was made out of leather, I started to sweat when I lay down for a long time.

That was the case even after placing down cushions. As he lay down, it grew hotter and hotter as the leather touching his body absorbed heat. When that happened, he, without fail, brought out an electric fan and wrapped himself with a thin blanket while he let a light breeze cool him. That experience couldn’t be any more heavenly.

That will be the same for both humans and dragons.

As luck had it, the dragon king was idly lazing around on a leather sofa. This sight made Lloyd remember his time in South Korea, and he presented the king with a secret trick he frequently used back then. Unsurprisingly, the result was very successful.

Snore…

Could it be that the dragon king was feeling more relaxed? He started to snore aloud, which brought a smile to Lloyd’s face. But Lloyd was unaware of something. It was that Verkis, despite his snoring, was observing him.

What is that?

Dragon King Verkis thought to himself while half-asleep. While he snored at ease and regarded this human being pesky, he glanced at the human fanning at him.

This is my first time encountering a fellow like him.

This human had earned the right to his support. But he wasn’t the first one to win it. Several had come to him in the last hundreds of years, but they all gave up and returned empty-handed and without any support. The reason was simple. Verkis simply ignored them.

Why? I couldn’t be bothered.

Dealing with humans was a tedious job. It involved rising up from a sofa just to entertain a menial human being, which required him to move his back, body joints, and muscles. His arms also needed to touch the sofa, and his shoulder needed to be tensed. Even more, he had to lift his head and open his eyes to focus them on the human. As though that wasn’t enough, he had to chat. That involved unnecessary moving of his mouth.

He despised all of it. It was all a chore. He couldn’t stand the thought of wasting several seconds of his life as a dragon to needlessly move. Instead, he could use the time to let his muscles and red blood cells rest. It would make his heart ache to waste his life that way. It would hurt him. And so, Verkis ignored all his visitors. He simply kept sleeping without opening his eyes. Surprisingly, however, this pesky problem of his resolved on its own when he did.

All the visitors became flustered at first. But then they would start to beg and throw a fit before eventually turning back.

The candidates set to obtain his support had behaved all the same. First, they were caught off-guard by his absence of a reaction. One even got fidgety and tried to wake him up. But Verkis shut his senses completely to them. Then most started to beg and get clingy. Dear dragon king, please open your eyes. Do not turn your eyes against me. Wake up, soldier. And yet, Verkis kept sleeping, persistently ignoring their pleas. Afterward, their subsequent reaction was anger.

They burst with anger when they realized they were being ignored. Why? Most of the humans had a great sense of pride.

Normally, earning the right to receive support from Dragon King Verkis involved accomplishing something massively grand. Something that was beyond human capacity, like saving a dragon, for example. In the first place, the achievement wasn’t something any ordinary human could accomplish, so humans with such feats were far from ordinary. They were the heroes of the world. Swordsman beyond human limits. The most powerful magician. The cleric once considered the saint of the world. Or even a conqueror the world will remember. Each and every single one of them who came here to attain the right to receive support from the king was worthy of making into human epic tales. But they all failed to achieve what they came here for.

Their pride was just too strong.

A faint smile spread across Verkis’s face. These heroes swelled with pride and ego due to their great achievements that would certainly be remembered in history. With that in mind, it wasn’t hard to imagine their reaction to being blatantly and persistently disregard for the first time in their lives inside the very dragon cave that they waltzed into with anticipation of amazing rewards. It crushed them.

They couldn’t handle it.

At first, they begged in tears. Later on, they whined that even a dragon king shouldn’t behave this way and treat them with such disregard. They angrily cried out that it was a violation of their promise. Eventually, they went back after a few days, months, or even years of Verkis continuing to ignore them in his sleep.

Of course, he was upbraided by his wife for it. She gave him an earful that he shouldn’t be doing this again. The visitors had traveled far and long and didn’t deserve such harsh treatment. Why are you not fulfilling the Dragon King support system your mother and sister painstakingly strove to create? It wasn’t right for him to laze around like this. What happened to the promise he made before getting married to become hard-working? She berated him for giving her an empty, short-lasting promise. His wife’s scolding lasted for months.

Well, it’s still okay.

Verkis grinned inwardly. It was amusing to hear his wife scold him and made him feel happy. The sound of her chattering couldn’t please him enough. And so, ignoring the visitors was a good thing. He was saved from entertaining them against his will, and it invited nice scolding from his wife. Thus, killing two birds with one stone. So, Verkis believed it would be the same for this human visitor, who just arrived with a foolish smile on his face. Lloyd must have come in excitement to receive his support. That Verkis’s first thought when he saw the human. The man was no different from the other human heroes he had seen. As such, all he had to do was treat him the same way as the rest. He was quite convinced up until a second ago.

Who is this man?

Verkis was starting to become speechless.

He’s helping me fall asleep instead?

Yes. This human who just entered the cave. This man wasn’t throwing a tantrum or begging or making formal and polite remarks to wake him up from his slumber. Instead of those, this one put a blanket over him and fanned him instead of saying anything. There was more.

He even got me a glass of water. Is that for me to drink when I wake up?

It appeared so. The way Verkis looked at Lloyd changed slightly.

Ha. This fellow. I like him.

Lloyd was different from all the other human visitors he encountered. Verkis liked Lloyd because it looked like he wouldn’t bother him too much. So he was able to fall asleep more comfortably.

Okay. I like him. This is your reward. I’ll unintentionally ignore you instead of doing it on purpose.

Verkis made up his mind to fall asleep for real. This was something he hardly did to anybody. And so, he thought that Lloyd should consider this an honor of a lifetime. Verkis shut his half-closed eyes all the way with a sense of immense satisfaction and fell asleep, determined to sleep for a few years.

Snore… Phew…

His entire body relaxed, and he plunged into dreamland. The king thought that the human fanning him would be gone by the time he woke up. If so, he would let out a big yawn after opening his eyes and kiss his wife after receiving another scolding from her.

Verkis set up a solid sleep plan. Of course, even when he drifted into a deep sleep, he didn’t worry a single bit. He wasn’t worried at all about a rancorous human attacking him while he was asleep. In the first place, no attack could hurt him. Even if a supernova explosion occurred right in front of his face, he would only get a small scrape that stung his nose and nothing more.

No creature in this world was capable of hurting him. Verkis went back to sleep with complete peace of mind. And so, he was entirely clueless. He was completely unaware of what outrageous plan Lloyd, who had been politely serving him, was about to put in place.
