# The Unexpected Encounter (2)

---

“Excuse me, will that be okay for you?”

Sheherazade swiveled at the worried voice. She looked at Julian’s eyes, which were one spot lower than her own.

“Stay back.”

A weakling who did nothing but play with pens in front of a desk. A uselessly adorable rascal. That was her impression of Julian from afar. Her feelings stayed the same when she first spoke to him later.

I plainly moved out from the bush, but he took notice of me much later and got frightened.

How pathetic, she mused. Had she held any ill intent, he would have been killed without having a chance to fight back. He behaved no differently from then on.

He let me walk all over him.

Panicked, he asked who she was, and she gave a genuine answer. My name is Sheherazade, and I’m a visitor from the sultan’s kingdom residing at the Frontera manor. I bumped into you while I was strolling here and overheard your conversation with your men. So, I’m planning to help you. She quickly voiced her objective without giving Julian any time to respond. Then pinning her gaze onto his eyes, she asked where the boulder was and said she would clear it out.

His reaction after that was more dumbfounding. He pointed to the boulder, his face looking absolutely blank.

How carelessly he behaved! Even when his men stood around in alarm, he didn’t care. Julian simply pointed to the obstructed path, his eyes wide like a surprised bunny.

Is he seriously related to Lloyd Frontera, that annoying manipulator? I find that suspicious.

A shiver ran down her shoulders. Lloyd Frontera. The mere thought of him made her feel frustrated. That was why she had expected Julian to be as annoying as Lloyd. But when they started to talk, she found Julian to be surprisingly boring and ignorant of the real world.

He’s a young noble who’s good at nothing but studying.

She looked straight into his eyes, thinking so. And she blurted in a monotone, “Farther.”

“Oh, yes.”

Julian scrambled a few steps back. The men around her shot her a wary look. They were covered up in mud from trying to move the boulder away. They were the ones who escorted Julian here and also knights who ranked as mid-level sword experts.

“…”

But she didn’t think much of them. She only cared about moving the boulder for Julian and helping him. That would make acquiring the lullaby from Lloyd much easier. Thinking so, she turned and looked at the boulder.

“…”

It was oval in shape and huge. The shortest side seemed to be over 6.5 feet tall. The longer part seemed to reach over 13 feet tall. It completely blocked the mountain path, which was already narrow in the first place after having been constructed along the flank of a steep slope. The boulder seemed to have rolled off from the top of the hill and landed in the middle of this narrow path.

Whew.

Sheherazade took a deep breath in front of the boulder. With a scimitar in her hand, she sharpened her senses in a relaxed yet elaborate manner. She glared at the target, searched for its weaknesses, and revealed her fangs.

Slash! Her scimitar emitted a deathly glow and slashed one side of the large boulder.

Crash! A fist-sized chunk was sliced off the tip of the boulder.

“…!”

Julian and the knights shot open their eyes in shock. But that was just the beginning.

Clang! Clang! Clang! Sharp, rhythmic sounds endlessly echoed in the air. The outline of Sheherazade’s body grew blurry amidst the glaring streaks of light coming from her sword. At the same time, the boulder started to grow thin near the top. More precisely, it was being cut down into fist-sized chunks.

Thwack! Crash! Crack! The process was similar to carving a sculpture piece by piece or slicing vegetables for fried rice.

“She looks… like she’s chopping a potato…”

One of the knights blankly murmured to himself, and everyone nodded in agreement. This spectacle naturally reminded them of cutting potatoes. Sheherazade’s swordsmanship and strength came as a sheer shock to everyone.

How is this possible?

Everyone was extremely curious. Only highly skilled swordsmen and women could slice through rocks with their swords. It was something only the best among high-level sword experts could succeed in. But slicing into a boulder several times in a row was impossible for even those sword experts. Why? Their swords were not durable enough to allow that.

It’s not surprising at all. In the end, swords are nothing but sharpened metal sticks.

So, striking down on a rock would obviously damage the weapon. It was unrealistic to wish for a sword to remain unharmed after smiting a rock when cutting a piece of leather or a chunk of meat was enough to damage its blade. That was the reason why a sword master’s “aura” was considered the most powerful ability they possessed. The skill allowed them to slice through any object without harming their sword. But as for that woman…

She’s not using an aura and isn’t a sword master. How… how could her sword remain fine even when it hit the boulder dozens of times?

Everyone gawked at Sheherazade’s back in shock and astonishment. Of course, they were ignorant of the fact that she was only one step away from becoming a sword master.

Hmph!

Her eyes grew calmer the more she wielded her scimitar. At the same time, her movements became more precise and focused. Every strike of her sword served a purpose. She used the minimum amount of mana needed to maintain her momentum while wielding the sword.

The moment her scimitar’s blade met the surface of the boulder, she concentrated her mana in a flash and ramped up the sword’s cutting force. This process was repeated throughout her stormy wielding, her focus never breaking at any point. Previously, it would have been impossible for her to possess this boundless ability to focus. However, it was made possible for her after going through a hellish training with Javier. But she wasn’t aware of what she had achieved yet. Right now, she was only focused on the goal of getting rid of the boulder in front of her.

Smph! Spph!

Now, the boulder had been hit with over 200 sword strikes. At last, she gave her final blow when there were only 20 inches of the rock remaining.

Bam! The remaining boulder was neatly cut in half. Sheherazade took her scimitar away, blowing the edge of the blade that had grown hot from friction. Then she put her sword back in its sheath as if nothing had happened and turned to everyone else.

“Will this do?”

She pointed back with her thumb where the boulder once stood. Now, it was only a pile of fist-sized, square rocks. No one, including Julian, could give an easy response. A smile came over Sheherazade’s face.

“Clean it up.”

With that, she coldly turned back. It was only then that the knights came to their senses and started to clean up the debris.

The night wore on, and it was like any other day. But not for Sheherazade. She was invited to dinner by Count Frontera for lending Julian a hand earlier in the afternoon.

“Without you, Miss Sheherazade, my youngest son would have been in a lot of trouble. Thank you.”

“Please do not mention it.”

The size of the table was modest for a count. Similarly, the food spread across the table was simple yet well-made. Count Frontera conveyed his gratitude to Sheherazade, and she shook her head and spoke.

“I simply came across a troubled man during my stroll by accident,” she said humbly.

“If someone else was there, they may not have made the same choice as you. Your kind heart and goodwill are what brought this fortunate meeting,” reasoned Count Frontera in a gentle voice.

“…”

His compliment made Sheherazade uncomfortable. Grabbing her fork, she thought to herself how she only helped Julian to get Lloyd’s lullaby. So, why was everyone doing this to her?

They’re making me uncomfortable.

Actually, she wasn’t accustomed to compliments. Her subjects often praised her, sure, but it was rare for her to receive genuine words of compliment and gratitude from people of higher or equal status as her. And so, she felt uncomfortable at this dinner table as if she wore something that didn’t fit her body. Furthermore, she couldn’t get used to the unexpectedly sweet and heartwarming air around this family.

“My dear son, wasn’t the journey too rough on you?” asked Marbella Frontera to Julian.

“Not at all,” replied Julian with a smile. “I was treated very well by the knights who escorted me.”

“Is that so? They sound like wonderful people,” she commented with a genuine smile.

“Yes, Mother. Not only that, like I told you, Miss Sheherazade was of great assistance to us.”

“Indeed. Thank you, Miss Sheherazade. Our family has received a great favor from you today.” Countess Marbella’s eyes were filled with gratitude as they fixed on Sheherazade.

“Oh, is that so…”

Marbella Frontera beamed at Sheherazade, who awkwardly bowed in response. Forking the salad without any words, she focused on the conversation at the table.

“Julian,” Arcos Frontera chimed in.

“Yes, Father.”

“It must have been hard for you to study alone in a foreign place.”

“Not at all,” replied Julian in a confident tone. “I had everything I needed during my stay there. It is all thanks to Father, Mother, and Lloyd.”

Count Frontera shook his head knowingly, however. “You may say that, but I understand how you really feel as your father. How can I not? I know how upsetting it is to live in a place full of strangers…”

Could it be that he was overwhelmed by his emotions? He suddenly chugged down three glasses of champagne.

"Father…? Are you all right?” Julian spoke with a worried look on his face.

“Haha. I am perfectly fine,” Count Frontera assured him. “I am just very happy. This day couldn’t get any better. How can I not be joyful?”

“Ahaha, y-you are right.”

“That is why.”

The alcohol suddenly made his face flush red, and his eyes became wet as they looked from Lloyd to Julian.

“I am happy,” went on Count Frontera, “that I am your father.”

“Father…” Julian’s voice trembled.

“In all honesty, I do not know how to express my feelings right now. I am proud of you both. There is nothing in this world I am more proud of than you two. Do you think it is because of your success? Since my oldest son is a reputable engineer and my youngest son graduated from the royal academy with honors? That is not it.”

The count tearfully continued to talk.

“I am not happy because you two are successful. I am merely thankful that you two grew up to be healthy and kind adults. I am grateful to be your father even if I don’t deserve the spot. I want to thank fate for giving both of you as my sons.”

“Um, Father, I think you are quite drunk-”

“No, I’m not. Julian and Lloyd… I am ready to give you the world even right now. If fate cruelly told me that I needed to roll around for the rest of my life to protect you both, I could roll back and forth thousands, tens of thousands, and billions of times with a smile on my face. If only that would make you two happy. Do you understand?”

“Yes… Father…” A shaft of embarrassment terrorized Julian as he answered.

The same went for Lloyd sitting at his side, and every cell in his body cringed at the count’s remarks. Countess Frontera, who was sitting across from the two, covered her flushed face with her hands, and her gesture seemed to say, “Why did you drink this much when you are a lightweight and when we have a guest?”

However, Sheherazade was inwardly shocked. Was she shocked because she, too, couldn’t take the heartfelt, toe-curling, and emotional speech from the count? No. It was the opposite. She was flooded with envy.

Is this… what family is supposed to be like?

She thought this kind of family only appeared in lofty and innocent fairy tales. She never expected to see one in real life.

This is… so different from mine.

Sheherazade clenched her fists under the table. Just then, the sultan came to her mind.

He was her father, but he was closer to being a merciless ruler. The life she had led so far to get acknowledged by the sultan! How desperately she had to push herself to get the slightest amount of acceptance, acknowledgment, and attention as she grew up among the dozens of princesses and princes!

Not a day went by when she didn’t use her sword. Even when the blisters in her hands burst over and over again and her father never bothered to look in her direction, she never let go of her sword. She lived with a firm conviction that her father would proudly accept and treat her as his daughter only if she became a skilled swordswoman and a sword master.

The same was true right now. The only reason she desperately wanted to acquire the lullaby from Lloyd and become a sword master was to get accepted by her father.

“…”

But this. There was something unfair about this. It turned out that getting accepted by your parents didn’t require you to put in back-breaking effort and soak your pillow in tears every night while comforting yourself that everything was fine.

Do other people not need to put in work to get accepted?

They didn’t. That was normal. That was what an ordinary family was like. Parents liked their children for no reason. That was how family and parent-child relationships worked. However, she never grew up in such a family and only had that realization just now. A pang of resentment and emptiness struck her heart. She felt like her years of struggle were all for nothing.

“…”

What am I? She clenched her fists under the table, shivering. She suddenly felt small. Her shoulders drooped, and her head hung low in meaninglessness.

From then onward, everything around her grew bleak. The food going into her mouth and the conversation she had with the Frontera family became a blur. She finished the meal in a daze and long forgot about striking a new deal with Lloyd, which was her original plan. Once she came back to her room, she sat blankly on the edge of her bed for a long time.

The night wore further on, and the moon and stars shimmered, ignorant of her feelings. As the white moonlight scratched the windowsill, Sheherazade rose as though she was allured by something and started to pack her things.

I’m going back.

She didn’t know why. She felt empty. The effort she put in until now. The acceptance she yearned for from her father. The sword master status she direly wished for. Everything felt meaningless and empty. Now, she didn’t want to think anymore. She wanted to rest for a bit.

I need to leave.

Creak. She slipped out of her room and entered the hallway, which was void of any light. The bleak darkness ahead strangely made her sigh.

“Whew..…”

She couldn’t understand why she was sighing. Everything felt too empty and unbearable. She moved forward. With one, two steps, she trudged off to the end of the hallway. Tears started to run down her cheeks onto the floor. Right when she was about to wipe them off with her hand like an idiot…

“Miss Sheherazade…?”

Out of nowhere, Julian’s voice carefully caught her.
