# Hell Express 666 (1)

---

“Tell me, don’t you wish to get off work?”

The moment Lloyd uttered those words, the King of Hell put a sudden halt to his pen that had been moving at lightning speed.

“What?” The King of Hell shot a cynical glare toward Lloyd. He wondered if this was what it would look like if ice could glare.

But Lloyd wasn’t intimidated. After all, he saw this response coming.

Of course. He’s been working overtime for 670,000 years. How else would he answer whether he wants to go home?

His response was obvious.

“It goes without saying that I do,” huffed the king. “Why are you asking such an obvious question?” Hellkaros’s voice was rough and on edge, and he had a face that said, “You kidding me?” Lloyd saw it as a good sign. This was better than a while ago when he simply buried his face in paperwork without giving a single glance to Lloyd. At least right now, he had the chance to strike a deal.

“I think I can help you with that,” Lloyd responded.

“Help me?” Hellkaros frowned. “You?”

“Yes.”

“How?”

“I will tell you once you give me the promise to release the soul of dragon Anticus who is under your confinement right now.”

And with that, Lloyd clamped his mouth shut, staring only at the king with a meaningful gaze. Their eyes locked, and a moment later…

“Ha.” King Hellkaros scoffed before saying, “Are you striking a deal with me?”

“Yes, I am.”

“How dare you. What are you thinking?”

“I heard that you are after extreme efficiency and fairness in all things, My Lord.”

“You did?” asked the king.

“Yes.”

Lloyd nodded his head. It was true. He did hear about the king. Where? In the novel.

There was a brief mention of him. The book wrote that the King of Hell uses the rule of law to punish the dead. But he started to chase after efficiency as his work became overwhelmingly hectic. And so…

The king demanded a fair cost for everything. Nothing was free for him. In order to receive a favor from him, you had to pay the proper price.

Just perfect. I like how he thinks. How can I not? Life is all about give and take in the first place.

There were no such things like free lunch or charity. King Hellkaros was similar to Lloyd in that sense. And so, throughout his travel to meet the king, Lloyd placed himself in the king’s shoes. How would the king react when Lloyd suggested releasing the soul of the dragon? Thinking from the king’s perspective, the answer came immediately.

I’d scoff, saying that the dragon can’t be freed without a cost. This would prompt the suggesting party to plead and beg me to name a price for the dragon’s release. I can’t do that. If I did, then the king would have the upper hand right from the beginning.

If possible, it was best to begin all negotiations by making the other party plead. That was a good way to get a strong start in the war of negotiation. Lloyd thought of it as a fight for dominance. And so, he was able to devise a strategy customized just for the King of Hell, who was chronically overworked. Lloyd first caught the king’s attention by throwing a bait; in other words, he mentioned there was a way for him to get off work. And taking advantage of the moment, he subtly brought up his request by saying he would tell him how he could get off work if he released dragon Anticus.

“How does that sound?” asked Lloyd. “If you give me your promise, I will be able to tell you my secret method.”

“…”

Lloyd flashed a grin, and the king frowned, his brows forming a wrinkle in the forehead. Lloyd Frontera, no, Kim Suho truly was a nasty person with his cunning and sly ways. Hellkaros had felt that way the last time the latter eliminated Sir Giolexius, that is, Hell’s Knight. But at the same time, Lloyd became more desirable to the king the longer he watched him.

Should I just make him take my place?

At first, the plan was to make him sing and become the speaker for Hell’s broadcast since his terrible singing seemed suitable to punish the dead in purgatory. That was why he made an effort and used his precious time to appear in Lloyd’s dream. But now, Hellkaros’s had a slight change of heart. If only he could, he wanted to throw every bit of his responsibility onto Lloyd’s plate. He appeared more than competent enough in his job to allow Hellkaros to rest for tens of thousands of years.

But he soon shook his head. It was realistically impossible. In order for his dream to materialize, he would have to renounce his position to this human. Or, grant him enough power to serve as his substitute. Neither of those two options was very appealing.

Well, the dragon’s soul has the same worth as a rare goldfish ornament to me.

As such, he would just feel slightly regretful about letting the dragon go. There wouldn’t be any significant loss for him. And then what would he get in return? He would be able to get off work and get back his life in the evening.

Gulp. The King of Hell swallowed involuntarily. He would be liberated from his mountain of paperwork. He could throw away his stifling necktie. He could leisurely enjoy a glass of highly acidic juice as he bathed in a volcanic hot spring. After he refreshed himself, he could relax in bed and catch up on the novels, legends, and other tales he missed in the last 670,000 years. At this point in his train of thought, he had found his answer.

Though a part of him was slighted because this Suho seemed to know his needs very well, the suggestion was a very appealing one. And so, at last, the king made a decision.

“All right. I promise.”

“Truly, My Lord?” Lloyd beamed.

“Indeed. I promise to release the soul of Anticus. So, tell me. Tell me the secret to getting off work and away from all my responsibilities.”

“Your Majesty has to trust me with one construction project. That’s all,” Lloyd answered. “Construction?”

“Yes.”

The king’s grimace deepened. A smile appeared on Lloyd’s mouth.

My plan worked.

Lloyd secretly clenched his fists. Just as he expected, the King of Hell showed a positive response.

I knew this would work. In the end, this exchange is a win for both of us.

The King of Hell could work less and enjoy his evenings when he was off duty. As for Lloyd, he would eventually obtain the funding of Dragon King Verkis, endless treasures, and an extra life by freeing Anticus’s soul from Hell.

All of this can happen if I successfully complete one construction project.

Lloyd licked his lips. The sweet life he had dutifully built in his fiefdom would be free from ruin with endless treasures. And he’d be able to enjoy himself more as he’d have one more shot in life. His dream of becoming a happy, carefree bum would come true.

With such ambition in his head, Lloyd spoke, “Let me ask you one question, My King. Is there anyone in Hell who can replace your position as a judge?”

“No, there isn’t.”

“Then are you unable to get off work because you have too much on your plate?” asked Lloyd.

“No, it is not that.” The King of Hell shook his head. “I am the King of Hell and the judge of the deceased. Since I hold the power to bestow judgments on fallen souls, it does not make sense that I cannot get off work because there is too much. I can go home regardless of if I have dozens, hundreds, or even trillions of souls left to judge for the day.”

Truly, it was almost impossible to count the number of judgments the King of Hell made in one day. The number of souls from deceased humans only represented a portion of the total number. Along with humans, all the animals and microorganisms on earth also had souls in their own special way. And the souls of these unicellular organisms and bacteria reached astronomical amounts. An astronomical number. Why? 40% of bacteria in the bottom of the ocean were killed by bacteriophage in the sea every day.

“Judging them is easy,” the king continued to speak. “Their simple souls don’t possess the same flaws as humans. So unlike the complicated human souls that are either sent to Heaven or Hell, these creatures don’t require much deliberation. I just need to lump together all their souls for the day and shove them into the Door of Resurrection, which sits at the edge of Hell. To do that, I just need to confer one judgment. However…”

The King of Hell, who lifted his pen, smiled bitterly.

“The problem is transferring the souls from this castle to the very edge of Hell. Wait, did you hear about this on your way here?” asked Hellkaros.

“Yes, I did, to be honest.”

Lloyd nodded. It was true. He heard from Pagenti, the demon Lloyd had thrashed to get to this castle.

“I heard that there’s a huge number of souls that need to be transported, but the road isn’t paved properly yet. I heard that the road cannot be paved because of the lava boiling across the land.”

“Yes, you are right. That is the reason why I am tied to this desk. To be more correct, it is because I supervise it,” corrected the king.

“You are busy because you are setting the transportation route, which changes every day depending on the road conditions.”

The King of Hell heaved a sigh. Since roads melted in lava every day, the transportation route had to be changed accordingly. He had to oversee it in real time and inspect it. His back broke, setting a new transportation route every day, every hour. Nothing changed even when he entrusted the job to the demons under him.

“I get reports almost every minute from the demons in the field pulling the carriage of the souls. Reports that the transportation route is blocked. That it melted in the lava. That the carriage can’t be passed. That a new path must be recommended. But when I do that, a similar report comes up a few minutes later. And then I have to open a new route again. Do you know how many demons are working to drive the carriage? 800,000.” Lloyd couldn’t say anything. So he kept listening.“It’s a terribly onerous job,” the king went on. “800,00 demons clamor and make reports and demand almost every minute. Plus, the only one who can exert strong control over the demons in Hell is me. No one else. And do you know what happens if I fall behind on my work and the carriage gets delayed? Catastrophe. No questions about that.”

“By that, you mean-”

“If I fail to put the souls of the bacteria in the Door of Resurrection, most of them won’t be able to resurrect when they support the entire ecosystem on this planet. Cell division will stop. More will die, while those coming to life will be few. The microbial food chain will completely break down. Can you guess what the aftermath will be?”

“Well, the entire planet will face massive extinction, I suppose,” postulated Lloyd. “That’s exactly right. And that is why I’ve been working nonstop for 670,000 years.”

“Um,” mused Lloyd, “then how were things 670,000 years ago?”

“How else? The same as now.”

“The same?”

“My predecessor worked just like I did.”

“Do you mean there was a king before you?” asked Lloyd, slightly befuddled.

“Yes. The previous King of Hell. He eventually committed suicide after struggling with depression from being unable to go home for 38 billion years.”

“…”

Lloyd had no words to say. The smile on the King’s face grew much more bitter.

“That’s what is at stake when you declare that you can solve this problem. So, what project is this? What construction project can solve this awful transportation issue?”

The king’s voice seemed to be laden with bitterness and resentment. Lloyd shuddered at the question.

Woah. I heard that there was an issue transporting souls from Pagenti, but this is…

As the problem was brought to the table, it turned out to be much more serious and risky than Lloyd expected. It was about a delivery system that carried the fate of an entire planet. Well, that didn’t matter to Lloyd. He had tried to find a solution the moment he speculated what the issue was. On his journey to this castle, he searched for and found a solution.

“To dare to tell you the answer, Your Majesty…” Lloyd paused. He then looked up, looking straight into the king. “Why don’t you put up a railroad?”

“A railroad?”

“Yes.” Lloyd nodded his head.

The king narrowed his eyes. “Railroad? An object that exists in the world of Kim Suho.”

“Oh, yes…” murmured Lloyd, shuddering. The constant mention of his real name agitated him because he kept minding Javier beside him. Lloyd feared that Javier would grow suspicious. But he shook off his feelings of agitation as his attention was needed on more important matters. He was in the process of negotiating with the King of Hell over a construction project. Furthermore, it was almost time for a decision to be made from their negotiation.

Focus.

Lloyd collected his breath. Now was the crucial moment. He had to effectively inform the King of Hell in detail about the ups of putting up a railroad in Hell and how it will solve the soul transportation issue. No, he had to brainwash the king. Really well.

Whew, this is a presentation. Yes. A presentation before a professor.

Every presentation began by hooking the attention of the audience. That required a strong, creative name to gain traction. Finally, having determined his strategy, Lloyd lifted his head. He looked straight into the king’s eyes with a confident gaze.

“Allow me to introduce the ‘Hell Express 666’ project.”
