# A Secret Plan to Stop Forced Labor (2)

---

A long, long time ago, about 600 years ago, in the land of Joseon ruled by the Great King Sejong, there lived a minister named Hwang Hee. As a praised premier, he was very good at his job and King Sejong cherished him. Along with his favor, the king entrusted many responsibilities to Hwang, who broke his back every time he received more work. His broken back made him want to run away.

So one day, Minister Hwang pleaded to the king. Your Majesty, I wish to spend the rest of my life back home as my body has grown fragile. The merciful King Sejong thus said, “I shall send you my carriage, so come to the palace every morning riding that.” Sometime much later, Minister Hwang entreated the king. Please let me rest as my mother passed away. Please let me use my overdue vacation. Please put me in retirement. Please dismiss me from the palace. Please fire me.

But each time, King Sejong yelled, “No!” Because of that, the praised premier could retire only four months before the passing of King Sejong. What happened after that? He died before he was able to enjoy his life to the fullest. Ah, ah, what a happy, hellish ending!

This story may sound funny to some, but if I really think it through, it isn’t an unrealistic one… at least for me.

Lloyd replayed the story in his head and clenched his fists. Minister Hwang Hee desperately begged to retire. But King Sejong exploited and used every bit of his talent until he passed away. Someone could regard this as a slightly amusing story in the history of Korea. Lloyd thought the same before.

But now, the story wasn’t just an old tale to him. King Sejong and Minister Hwang Hee. Queen Magentano and himself. Unsettlingly, Lloyd felt that his life was dangerously flowing in the same direction as Hwang’s.

I’m not just being emotional.

Lloyd nibbled his lips. Actually, this was a feeling he had since a while ago. A swirl of nervousness sometimes rushed through him, waking him up in his sleep on occasion. He suffered from a nightmare where the queen summoned him to the kingdom and gave him endless work. When in fact, he planned to spend the rest of his life in peace now that he overcame all his challenges and obstacles.

But that nightmare could become reality if I let my guard down. That’s the sense I get from the current state of things.

Lloyd licked his lips, his mouth feeling bitter. Ever since he joined the delegation to Ahinsya at the queen’s command, a part of his mind started to whisper to him, “This isn’t right.” The same thing happened when he had gone to meet the queen with Termes to get the latter approved for asylum. That was when he felt confident about it. He’d certainly become the queen’s slave or SVC if he did nothing like now.

So, I need a plan.

Lloyd had revealed too much of his capabilities. He had shown skills beyond what was necessary to overcome the financial, physical, and biological crises in his fiefdom, leading him to gain the queen’s approval. But it turned out that the queen was the type to exploit talent and believed that the sufferings of competent individuals brought more comfort for the people and more prosperity to the kingdom. So, without a plan in hand, Lloyd would forever be doomed to work. He would have to say goodbye to the life of leisure he desired so badly.

That won’t do.

Chills ran down his spine at the thought. He had already worked hard enough in South Korea. Even in this world, he had gone through all sorts of hardship. To him, the fate of working for the rest of his life was equal to capital punishment. That was exactly the reason why Lloyd felt the need for a plan that would save him from the queen’s future exploitations. He had spent the past few days racking his brain for a way out.

And it’s not like I can protest against her.

Queen Magentano was a ruler with absolute power. Her political power was unrivaled across the kingdom’s history, and she was a high-level sword master. It was thanks to her that the kingdom enjoyed immense stability. Lloyd shook his head when his train of thought escalated to revolting against her because he wanted to laze around.

I’m not that desperate.

The chances of failure were high. Even if he succeeded, it would be the same as opening the gate of Hell, bringing more work to him. If he got unlucky, he could become the new king and be stuck handling government affairs for the rest of his life. Lloyd wanted to stay as far away as he could from that kind of situation.

Plus, Queen Magentano has complete trust in me. If I use this well, she could be my biggest supporter until death. So, it’s better that she stays on the throne for as long as possible.

Queen Magentano’s place on the throne had to remain secure. His goal was to relax and enjoy life under her stable rule. So, there was a need to solidify his footing in a new way, which would grant him a lot of rights without many responsibilities. A political position that would advantage him more than anything. That was what Lloyd had on his mind recently.

But the problem was that I couldn’t come up with any ideas. Not until now, that is. Julian, it’s all thanks to you.

The corners of Lloyd’s lips curved upward. His gaze traveled to the pathway beneath the hill, where Julian and Sheherazade were walking together side by side. Sometimes one went ahead of the other to avoid the searing summer heat. Regardless, the air of awkwardness was still strong between them. The wheels in Lloyd’s head started to turn at the sight, and finally, he found a plan to stop the queen from exploiting him.

Okay, it’s doable. I think I can do it.

Lloyd saw a glimpse of hope. A chance for success. It was worth a try. Once he succeeded, the queen would have greater difficulty attempting to burden him with more work. Heck, the atmosphere would even turn awkward, at least if she did try. At this point, a mischievous smile appeared on his face.

So, I think I should owe you something this time.

Now that he had a plan, it was better for Lloyd to get started right away instead of procrastinating. That night, Lloyd started to execute his plan to actualize his grand scheme. His first target was Julian.

“Whew…”

A helpless sigh came out of him. But Julian didn’t know why. He couldn’t understand why he kept sighing during dinner and why he didn’t enjoy eating at home after he finally returned.

I really don’t know.

Julian softly shook his head. His mind grew restless for some reason. Even during dinner while talking with his parents. Even when he was having grilled duck, his favorite. Something kept pestering his heart. His heart thumped without any reason, only to feel stifled. Then he would start smiling without meaning to. As a result, he couldn’t even tell what he was eating right now. To put it simply, he was…

“You feel like you’re going nuts, right?”

Flinch! It was right after he slipped out to the hallway after the meal, leaving his half-full plate and putting on an awkward smile at his worried parents. Julian shuddered at the voice that came from the side. He was surprised as he was just wondering whether he had lost it. It was like his thoughts were read aloud. Taken aback, Julian turned to the voice.

“Hyung…?”

Lloyd was standing there, stroking his chin and glancing at him with a strange gaze.

“Wh-what are you saying? I’ve lost it?”

“I didn’t say you were,” denied Lloyd brazenly.

“What?”

“I was just asking whether you were having that thought about yourself,” clarified Lloyd in a matter-of-fact voice.

“Um…”

Lloyd’s nonchalant response put Julian at a loss for words.

“No, it’s not that,” hurried Julian, who suddenly felt exposed. “It’s really nothing. I’m fine. My stomach feels a bit unwell. That’s all.”

“Really?”

“Yeah,” swallowed Julian.

“Really?” Lloyd sent another meaningful gaze.

“Uh-huh.”

“Is it indigestion? Should I prick your finger?”

“Eh?”

Prick my finger? How?

Julian lifted his head and found an ever-so-suggestive smile on his brother’s face.

“It’s simple,” explained Lloyd, motioning his hands. “Tie your thumb with a thread and poke the area near the root of the nail in one stab! When dark blood starts to gush out, loosen the thread and rub your back. Works like a charm. Your stomach will feel much better.”

“…”

“Wanna try it out?” Lloyd waggled his brows.

“N-no, thanks.”

Stab his finger with a needle? Draw out dark blood? Julian instinctively shook his head, and he turned toward his room.

“I am fine,” refused Julian. “I don’t want that.”

“Really?”

“Yep,” said Julian, and he started to walk off.

But Lloyd continued to follow Julian. Why is Lloyd following me? I feel restless as it is and want to be alone, Julian told himself. But his brother continued to follow him, disregarding his feelings. What was more, Lloyd started to ramble again.

“Julian, I’m your brother. I know,” Lloyd remarked.

“Wh-what?”

“I know what you are thinking.”

“What… am I thinking?” muttered Julian.

“You feel like you’re in a strange mood, right?”

“Not really.”

Julian hurried his steps, but to his dismay, Lloyd walked faster to match his pace. To his greater dismay, Lloyd was able to directly pinpoint his inner thoughts.

“And yet, you don’t know why you’re feeling this way,” stated Lloyd.

“…”

It was true. He had been restless all evening. He couldn’t figure out why no matter how hard he thought. Helpless, he started to even feel worse. And here was Lloyd, speaking as though he could see through him. At last, Julian stopped and glanced up at Lloyd with a serious gaze.

“Hyung,” he muttered hesitantly. “Actually, something has been bugging my mind.”

“Yeah, tell me,” Lloyd said.

“You’re right. I feel really strange.”

“Great,” Lloyd complimented. “Are you admitting your state of mind now? This is a great start. Should we walk more calmly?”

“Oh, yeah,” Julian replied to his older brother.

Lloyd had become so reliable unlike when he was a delinquent. His sweet and gentle voice. His face says, “I get you.” Julian completely lowered his guard, and he started to share the feelings in his heart as he strolled along the pathway in front of the manor with Lloyd.

“To be honest,” Julian began. “I’ve been feeling slightly off since this evening. But I can’t figure out why I’m like this.”

“You can’t?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes.”

“Then let’s slow down for a second. Think back to late afternoon today. Tell me exactly when you started to feel weird.”

“When?”

“Yeah.”

“Hmm…” Julian started to think back. “Um… You see… I strolled with Miss Sheherazade in the afternoon.”

“Okay.”

“We talked a lot.”

“About what?”

“How she grew up. The difficulties she has with sword training. The reason behind the desert’s beauty. Stuff like that. It was nice listening to her. And…” Julian trailed off.

“And what?”

“…”

Julian paused. An epiphany struck him. He knew now.

“I think I’ve started to feel this way ever since I came back alone from the stroll with Miss Sheherazade.”

“Is that so?”

“Yeah.” Julian nodded, giving Lloyd more confirmation. “That’s right. I really think it is. Ever since then, I’ve been feeling restless, weird, and-”

“Empty,” Lloyd broke in. “Am I right?”

“Yeah. How did you know?”

Julian was amazed. He was amazed at how Lloyd was able to point out his feelings with perfect accuracy. As though he was begging for an answer, Julian gazed at Lloyd, who grinned. And Lloyd spoke as if the answer to his younger brother’s question was obvious.

“You’re experiencing your first love.”

“What…?”

“First love,” said Lloyd again. “Don’t you know what that is?”

“Um, I know what it is,” replied Julian curtly.

“Then good. That’s what you’re going through.”

“Me?” gaped Julian.

“Yeah.”

“First love,” said Julian again.

“Uh-huh.”

“With Miss Sheherazade?”

“Probably?”

“…”

Julian shut his mouth. The sheer shock of realizing his feelings made him freeze. He fell into a daze, looking back at himself to see if Lloyd was really right. He even stopped breathing and dryly swallowed. Lloyd chuckled as he looked at Julian.

I knew I was right.

Lloyd was right about what he saw. Julian and Sheherazade were strolling earlier this afternoon. He could see right through them. The two looked like a typical pair of lovebirds in the early stages of dating.

How could I not tell? I’ve seen this sight more times than I can count.

Lloyd was once a university student in South Korea. Though he was a civil engineering major where girls were thin on the ground, he saw many couples while walking around campus in the spring. Tons of them. His colleagues and friends often dated each other as well.

There were especially a lot of them during my freshman year.

Through the new student get-together, orientation, general assembly, and even blind dates, there were a number of couples that Lloyd saw come together in different ways. Sometimes, there were times he had to drink with a heartbroken friend whose situationship with a girl ended. And, of course, there was the time he himself had a thing with a girl.

Dammit. Had it not been for the coin karaoke.

Lloyd blamed the karaoke in front of the university gate. And he pettily hoped his friend would get athlete’s foot for urging them to sing there.

Tsk.

Lloyd immediately suppressed his old memory, and he looked at Julian standing right in front of him.

Yeah, he’s in a situationship for sure.

His radar, which was sharpened at his school campus, was right. Julian and Sheherazade had something going on, but it looked like the two were oblivious to their relationship. It made sense.

Of course. They’re probably totally novices in this area.

Julian grew up doing nothing but studying. It wasn’t much different for Sheherazade.

She likely dedicated her whole life to sword training until now.

Dating was a foreign concept to them as they engrossed themselves in the field of their expertise, that is until they experienced love for the first time. And now, they were set to have a rough path full of mistakes with little emotional intelligence.

But I can’t leave them to break up. That would be a waste.

It was a waste for them both and for Lloyd himself. So, he decided to become a Cupid for them. Do a good deed that brings these two lovebirds together. And reap the best benefit of it. With such resolve in his mind, Lloyd grinned with satisfaction. He then put on a confident face, gaze, and voice. And he started to make the comments he had come prepared.

“You’ll lose Miss Sheherazade if you keep fumbling like this.”

“What…?”

Lose her. Those words prompted Julian’s eyes to widen in surprise. Anxiety filled his eyes so evidently. That made Lloyd smile in a glow of satisfaction. Why? Lloyd was confident that his plan to make their marriage happen would elevate his status where he would be desirable and influential to both the queen and sultan and be able to masterfully two-time them. From then on, Lloyd licked his lips, ready to jump into action.
