# The Sluggard’s Inner Thoughts (2)

---

Snore… Phew…

The dragon cave. This was the home of Dragon King Verkis, and it was a divine place worshiped by all dragons. Its vast space in the dungeon could accommodate a full-court basketball game played by ten gigantic dragons. And through that very space rang the snoring noise of Dragon King Verkis. He was extremely comfortable in his current position fused together to the couch with the soft blanket the human had put on him. Moreover, there was a fresh breeze from the human’s fanning. It couldn’t get any more restful than this. Verkis’s eyes shut on their own, leading him into dreamland. And just like that, Verkis fell into a deep slumber. This made the corners of Lloyd’s lips pull up.

Whew, it’s done.

Lloyd put on a rewarding smile on his face, having succeeded in putting the sluggard to sleep. He then cast a quick glance to Verkis who was in a comatose state. And to make sure that the latter was completely knocked out, Lloyd waved his hands before the king’s face. As expected, he received no reaction, and instead…

Phewww! Snore…

His breathing grew more relaxed. It had happened. Verkis was fully asleep. With that conviction, Lloyd tiptoed away from him.

Javier, who had been observing Lloyd since a while ago, asked, “Master Lloyd, what did you do?”

“What do you think?” Lloyd grinned at Javier. “I helped the dragon king fall asleep.”

“I saw that myself, so I know that,” replied Javier, whose face grew suspicious. “I am asking what you are up to by putting him to sleep.”

“Two things,” replied Lloyd with a shrug.

“Two things?”

“Yeah,” said Lloyd. “One is not upsetting the king. I could feel it the moment I saw him. Did you see it, too? How he fell right back to sleep without giving a single greeting even when he saw us.”

“Yes, I have seen it.”

“Right?”

“Yes, he reminded me of your previous self.”

“What?” Lloyd raised his brow, surprised by what he just heard.

Javier’s face straightened. “I mean how it used to be impossible to wake up after a night of drinking.”

Having never expected Javier to say this, Lloyd was left speechless.

“I am not sure if you remember,” continued Javier, “but that is exactly what you were like. You refused to get out of bed. The more I tried to wake you up, the more upset you’d get and hurl insults as well as physical objects at me.”

“…”

“Now that I think about those days, I can understand your intentions at the moment. I agree with you. It would be very upsetting to wake someone up by force when they wish to get some more sleep.”

“…”

Lloyd wasn’t sure whether what he just heard was a compliment or another unrestrained insult from Javier. Whatever it was, he felt a slight sense of resentment and a bit wronged.

Dammit. The Lloyd you saw then wasn’t me, you bastard.

But no one in this world, including Javier, would be aware of this. The truth was only known to him and only for him to know. These false accusations were something he had to accept for the rest of his life. When he thought so, he was able to plaster a shameless face and give a snort.

“Tsk. You never pull your punches while insulting someone. Anyway, as you say, I intend to not upset the dragon king before anything else,” admitted Lloyd, his voice sounding slightly bitter.

“Then what is the second one?”

“Having fun.”

“Excuse me…?” Javier’s eyes widened a shade.

“Didn’t you hear me? I’m going to have fun.”

“Wait, here?”

“Yeah.” Lloyd earnestly nodded as if there was no other way around it. He then continued, “Think about it. The dragon king called for us, even sent us the manticore, and said nothing when he saw us in here. What could that mean? It means we’re officially an invited guest here, don’t you think?”

“You are right, but…” Javier protested feebly.

“We should have fun as invited guests here,” reasoned Lloyd. “The dragon king is out like a light, and we can’t force him to wake up. And it’s not like he’s going to be asleep for years. So, we should enjoy ourselves while we wait.”

Javier caught the excitement in Lloyd’s voice. He then asked, “Then what are you going to do to have fun?”

“Laze around.”

The corners of Lloyd’s lips pulled up. To laze around like an unemployed bum. It was something that Lloyd wanted to try all his life. And watching the dragon king slumbering on his couch like that only reinforced Lloyd’s desire. That was how exhausted he was.

“To be honest,” Lloyd said, “I want to get some rest. Think about it. How long have I been working?”

“Um, almost five months from Hell, and…”

“And before I headed down to Hell, I worked on the construction of the Termina Garden for about six months for the queen, right?”

“Yes.”

“Right? So, know that you’re looking at a person who worked for almost a year straight without a single break,” said Lloyd.

“How diligent of you.”

“I don’t want that sort of compliment.”

“How restless you have been.”

“Yes, you’re right. That is why I’m doing this.” Lloyd pointed to the inside of the spacious empty cave. “This is more like a long-awaited vacation for us. Who can reach me when I’m in here, right?”

It was true. In a way, this was the safest place in the whole world since this was the place resided by the dragon king. So no one would dare venture their way here or come to call Lloyd. This place offered absolute freedom and rest, free from anyone’s interruption. So from then onward, Lloyd had the run of the whole cave and went all out in enjoying his break. It was a vacation, a kind of hocance. What was more, there was a resting area that was on par with a five-star hotel. Believe it or not, that area was the royal castle. And there were two of them.

“It looks like… These castles have been plucked out from somewhere,” observed Lloyd.

Two royal castles sat in the corner of the cave, and he was flabbergasted at first when he spotted them during his adventure around the cave. No matter how he looked at it, from the left and right, the structure in front of him was the royal castle. But something was off about its lower half.

There are signs that it has been ripped, no, plucked off.

Lloyd was sure. The supporting structure of the entire castle, along with a portion of its lower foundation, had been ripped apart to be transferred here. Just then, Lloyd recalled an old memory.

Right. The novel mentioned this. I think it was around the time the royal capital Magenta had a different name. There was a foolish king who lost two royal castles to a dragon. And I had a conversation about this with the messenger the first time I went to the capital.

He remembered now. The story was something he definitely heard about before. And now, it turned out…

The dragon that plucked the castle is Dragon King Verkis!

The situation became clear to Lloyd at last. Dragon King Verkis had plucked out the royal castle like a radish on a field and brought it all the way here.

Then he used it as decoration for his house.

Lloyd felt dazed for some reason, but he immediately shook it off. Since they were already here, Lloyd figured that he should take a good look inside. And soon, he noticed something.

The inside is a lot more well-kept than I imagined!

It was a surprise. The whole castle shone with gloss as though somebody had cleaned it this morning. Not a single speck of dust could be found throughout the palace. And what was more, even the bed in every room was dry and fluffy!

Hahaha. This is amazing.

Lloyd made a decision. If not now, he would never get a chance to sleep on a king’s bed. So he hurried and settled in the king’s bedroom, ready to enjoy his vacation to the fullest.

The cave, moreover, held plenty of food. Around the center of the vast empty cave, there extended spiderweb-like passages that grew sweet potatoes underneath their dirt. The sweet potatoes were tremendously large, the same size as a basketball.

“Hey, let’s grill it,” suggested Lloyd to Javier.

“…”

“What are you doing?” hurried Lloyd when he saw Javier staying wordless. “Start a fire.”

“…”

Lloyd had just walked over carrying five giant sweet potatoes in his arms, and Javier stared at him, completely dazed and speechless.

Javier asked, “They look like valuable produce from the dragon cave. Are we allowed to grill them without permission?”

“Yeah, probably,” replied Lloyd nonchalantly.

“How do you know?”

“I asked the manticore,” replied Lloyd, maintaining the same tone of nonchalance.

Indeed, he did. Fearing that getting sweet potatoes could invite the wrath of Verkis, Lloyd went up to the manticore just to make sure. And he asked matter-of-factly if he could eat some of the sweet potatoes lying around the area.

The manticore gave a very chill “YES.”

“Corong! That was how it answered,” Lloyd explained.

“Are you sure that was an affirmation?” asked Javier dubiously.

“Yup. The manticore nodded as it coronged.”

“And that counts as receiving permission?”

“Of course,” replied Lloyd as he placed down the sweet potatoes. “And I even introduced it to Ppodong to show my gratitude.”

“Sir Ppodong?”

“Yeah. The manticore cocked its head at first. But when it saw Ppodong grow large after eating the red sunflower seed, it was very welcoming.”

Javier mused, “I see. Indeed, the two are fairly similar in size.”

“It appears so. Thankfully, Ppodong got along with the manticore pretty quickly.”

“So, are they playing right now?”

“Yeah,” said Lloyd, “Probably playing tag. Anyway, when are you going to start the fire?”

“…”

“Hurry up now. I’m starving.”

“…”

And just like that, the hero, who saved the world in the original novel, was degraded into becoming a sweet potato griller with a face full of soot. Like so, in the next few days, Lloyd idly lazed around, making up for all the days he spent being overworked and exhausted. He was relaxed, happy, and content. On the other hand, Javier observed Lloyd with a strange gaze.

“…”

Ten days had passed since they came to this cave, and Javier started to have a realization. The Lloyd in front of him wasn’t real. He was a man from another world.

That was why he suddenly seemed to behave like a different person.

Javier recalled one morning a few years back. Lloyd had thrown a nasty drunken fit the night before. But when the following morning rolled around, he acted like a completely different person. It was strange, but Javier let it slide because the change was rather welcoming.

Now I know. The meaning behind the name he was called by the King of Hell, Kim Suho.

Kim Suho. Javier figured that it must be the name of fake Lloyd back in his world. So, the unexpected actions and mysterious knowledge of construction displayed by this man until now must have been a product of his own world. At last, he was able to get some clarification. Javier finally understood what happened. On the one hand, he also became curious.

That fake Master Lloyd… How would he react if he knows that I know? Would he act shamelessly like he always does? Or would he get scared? How should I react when such a day comes?

There wasn’t any way of knowing. However, one thing was clear.

As long as you don’t change from now, I will protect you by your side until the end.

Faint warmth filled Javier’s gaze as it fixed on Lloyd. But when the latter noticed Javier’s gaze, he cocked his head to the side and asked, “Hey, what’s on your mind right now?”

“Excuse me?” Javier replied, taken aback.

“You just gave me a really cheesy look.”

“…”

“Tell me honestly,” Lloyd went on, “You’re hiding that you’ve found something tastier than sweet potatoes, right?”

“Not at all,” replied Javier, feeling relieved.

“Really?”

“Yes,” assured Javier again and chuckled, thinking to himself that there actually was something he was currently hiding but planned to keep hiding his secret for the time being.

Lloyd chuckled too and said, “Then hurry up and grill the sweet potatoes. I’m hungry.”

“Understood.”

With that, Javier started to busy himself with roasting the sweet potatoes, and another day passed by as the two enjoyed their long-awaited vacation. At the same hour, in the corner of the cave, Verkis, who was happily sleeping, felt a sense of crisis and flew open his eyes.

♣️

“Eek…?!”

Dragon King Verkis opened his eyes, but was it because his sleeping position was uncomfortable? Or because Lloyd stopped fanning a cool breeze over him? No. The sofa was comfortable even without a cool breeze. The reason why he abruptly woke up after feeling a sense of crisis was simple.

This is bad. I’ve forgotten the most important thing.

Verkis recalled the promise he had been forgetting all this time, the promise he had made with his lovely wife.

I promised to contact her every day…!

But it slipped his mind completely, and he never made any calls. Since when?

I don’t know. How long did I doze off?

Verkis searched his memory for the last time he wasn’t half-asleep. That very day, his wife had gone out to the world with his mother dragon for a trip.

She told me that they’d be traveling for about a year and reiterated that I should call her every morning.

It was homework, in a way. But it had slipped his mind altogether. Verkis felt his heart tighten in nervousness. So, he immediately raised his hand and created a magic circle in the air to activate advanced communication magic that allowed him to contact her wherever she was.

Swaa…! The magic circle rotated in the air and tracked her coordinates before attempting connection. Then it failed.

Crash! The communication magic circle then vanished in thin air. The connection was lost. Verkis knew why this happened.

“She… rejected my call.”

This was bad, really bad. His wife was sulking now.

What should I do?

Verkis racked his brain. His wife was an angel, so she never became upset over anything. But given that his call was denied, it appeared that she was really angry this time. Verkis wondered how he should fix this situation.

Should I run to her right now and beg for forgiveness? No. That’s too much work.

He didn’t want to fly such a long journey. That demanded a lot of muscle movement. And he would even have to use space teleportation which would require him to activate a large amount of mana. That was too much work.

What should I do then?

Verkis deliberated how he could appease his wife with minimal effort.

“Ugh… Think. Think…”

Verkis wrapped his head with his hands, racking his brain fiercely. Just then, while Verkis was lost in his deliberation, someone approached him from behind and spoke.

“My, dear Dragon King!” The man’s voice dripped with shamelessness. “Pray, do tell me, is there something bothering you?”

The voice belonged to Lloyd, who ran all the way here the moment he realized that the king had woken up. He then politely rubbed his hands together to make sure that he was making a good first impression before audaciously offering several words of flattery to him. And he plastered the smile of a fraudulent insurance salesman on his face as he gazed at Verkis.
