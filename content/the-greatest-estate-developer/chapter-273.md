# A Shameless Demand (1)

---

The City of Magenta. It was the political and economic hub of the Magentano Kingdom and boasted a long, rich history. Right after the great war of the mythical age, the Giant King Oculus erected a palace on this land and enslaved humans until they ousted the giant king by waging a war for independence. And the humans, having succeeded in attaining freedom, built their own kingdom for the first time in human history.

Their city was named “Taliah,” taken from their proud leader. Fast forward many years, the city enjoyed unhindered prosperity until one day, it went crashing down. It was all because of a greedy king who let a dragon take over the palace twice. After its miserable downfall, a new royal family took over the city, and naturally renamed the city “Magenta.” It was around that time the capital’s current landmark, the half-buried egg-shaped palace, was constructed. And today, inside the office of this egg-shaped palace, Queen Alicia Termina Magentano was sighing to herself as she slurped down a raw egg. Crack. Slurp!

“Whew…”

Queen Magentano wiped her wet hair with a towel, drinking the raw egg that her servant brought for her. Yet, the wrinkles on her face didn’t leave. It was because she was frustrated.

Truly, this is a mess.

Grimacing, she gazed at her office desk. There were messy piles of documents strewn all over it. They were the reports on the reconstruction of the Termina Garden wrecked by the Bone Dragon.

Thankfully, the reconstruction went well. It was completed without many issues. The final costs were quite high but not enough to put any burden on the kingdom’s finances. But the problem is…

They hadn’t redeemed all the damage that came from the Bone Dragon’s uproar. In particular, the intangible assets that couldn’t be seen by the eye were what took the biggest hits.

The imperial family has suffered considerable damage to its reputation.

The grand garden had been constructed to flaunt the kingdom’s national prowess to the outside world, so the completion event similarly served the same purpose of exhibiting its grand results. In other words, it was a promotional showcase meant to announce to the world the authority of the House of Magentano. However, the event was a failure. A complete blunder. All because the bones of the dragon installed at the center of the garden moved against everyone’s expectations and ran amok. Everyone in attendance had to run for their lives, and the royal guards were left floundering around. The situation was so terrible that the queen herself had to grab her sword. That, however, wasn’t the worst part.

The worst part is… that we failed to capture the Bone Dragon.

Queen Magentano’s furrow deepened. That day, she failed to suppress the Bone Dragon. No, she did. To be more exact, she failed to announce that to the others, which tanked the reputation of the imperial family and made it an international laughingstock.

We were branded as the imperial family that adorned its capital with a grave threat. The imperial family that failed to properly take down a dangerous enemy. The imperial family that shakily tried to defend itself…

A glint of rage flashed in her eyes. Right after the Bone Dragon incident and when Lloyd and Javier left for Hell, she concentrated all her energy and attention on cleaning up the mess. She commemorated the victims, praised them as heroes and heroines, and accordingly compensated them. She poured an exorbitant amount of money into restoring the demolished garden. Meanwhile, she strove to revive the diminished authority of the imperial family, shedding light on the event by announcing that Lloyd Frontera and Javier Frontera bravely slayed the monster under her leadership. She even strove to clear her name by incorporating the accounts of the royal guard knights that witnessed the event. But the international community was…

They did not believe any of my words.

A bitter smile appeared on her face as she thought so. The world’s doubtful reaction wasn’t very surprising, however. After all, even if the Bone Dragon was indeed subdued, which was mainly possible due to Javier’s contribution, there wasn’t any evidence to back up her claim.

Why? Because Lloyd took the Bone Dragon as his subordinate.

If there was a way to exhibit the demolished pieces of the Bone Dragon to the world, things would have been better. But to her misfortune, no evidence was left. And so, the explanations the House of Magentano put out were received with global mockery. Everyone cast doubts on the authority of the House of Magentano, throwing various theories that the Bone Dragon could have simply fled because it got bored after having its share of fun destroying the place. That there should be evidence if the dragon was dead. But there wasn’t any evidence. Moreover, the shadow of doubt didn’t linger only among foreign countries.

The nobles of our kingdom started gossiping too.

Of course, she hardly worried much about them since her authority over the kingdom was strong and secure. Her legitimacy wasn’t going to get impacted by this. Nonetheless, it wasn’t exactly a pleasant development.

I must restore the damaged reputation of the imperial family.

Losing authority in an international setting meant several things. One was a reduction in diplomatic influence. From now on, she would experience inconveniences in international rows, clashes of interests, disputes, and negotiations, which all happened quite often. It was as if they were an injured lion getting looked down by a pack of hyenas.

But…

There didn’t seem to be a striking solution to restore their international authority. This was the reason why she kept grimacing all day.

“Tsk.” She snatched a towel with annoyance and roughly shook the water off her hair. Despite doing so, her concern wasn’t shaken off.

“…”

She used to feel refreshed after snacking on a raw egg once she finished her sword training session and bath. But these days, this wasn’t the case anymore. Like a puzzle that couldn’t be solved, her unanswerable concern never left her head.

What about restoring the garden and holding another event? That wouldn’t be enough. A reconstruction celebration would change nothing. It would be better if a small-scale military conflict broke out on the border.

That would grant her the opportunity to shift the domestic and international attention elsewhere, and it could restore its diminished authority by showcasing its massive military power.

But that is not right.

The queen despised the idea of sending her innocent soldiers onto a battlefield just to regain the kingdom’s authority. The very purpose of getting it back in the first place was so she could better protect and care for these people of hers. As possible solutions got ticked off in her head one after another, her woes only grew.

After a long time, around the time her wet hair was half-dried, someone interrupted her thoughts. “Your Majesty, there is an individual who has requested a special audience with you.” It was the Lord Chamberlain who spoke from outside of her office.

“Audience with me?” Queen Magentano inquired. “Who is it?”

“It is Lloyd Frontera, the eldest son of the Frontera county, Your Majesty.”

The moment she heard his response, she squeezed her fist without realizing it. But why? Because the name was uncalled for? Because she felt a flash of inexplicable expectation? But before she could find the answer for herself, she blurted out a response. “Let him in.”

“Your words are my command.” The Lord of Chamberlain’s footsteps grew distant. He must be bringing Lloyd to the office, the queen thought.

Queen Magentano propped her chin against her hands as she waited for the unexpected guest to emerge. “Haah.” She didn’t know why she was sighing. Was it from concern? Or surprise? Whatever it was, one thing was certain. She was glad to hear of Lloyd’s visit.

Lloyd Frontera.

A man who was extremely good at reading people. A subject of hers who came to possess greater power than her. A subject of hers, despite his capabilities, that she could strangely be at ease around.

It presumably should be because he and my political interests are aligned.

She was glad that Lloyd wasn’t an ambitious man. That he opted to live a normal life in the province. Apart from the gratitude in her heart, there was also wonder.

What is it that made him come here?

Queen Magentano smacked her lips, thinking to herself that Lloyd was not someone who would act without a clear purpose. And he was even more so not someone who would travel all the way here for nothing.

He only reaches out when there’s something he needs from me. Wait, is this his first time coming to visit me out of his own will?

The queen thought about it, and indeed, she was the one who always called for him first. He usually dragged himself to her with a terrible grimace and fidgeted the entire time, exuding his evident reluctance and annoyance to be summoned as if he was being forced there.

Tsk.

As such, his visiting of his own volition meant he wanted something from her. Otherwise, he’d have never willingly come to her.

What could it be that he wants?

What grand request does he have that prompted him to come here? She became very curious and expectant.

He is a sly man. If he wants something from me, he knows that it won’t be free.

He likely had something valuable to negotiate with. Something very helpful to her, she thought.

He is a smart fellow.

In her current state, that is, where she spent every day worrying. It could only mean that Lloyd was ready to negotiate if he was coming here to make a request.

He must have identified what was worrying me and found a way to solve my worries. I’m sure he plans to capitalize on that to ask for help or assistance in return for his help.

It was a simple matter, come to think of it. And like that, Queen Magentano saw through the hidden meaning and intention behind Lloyd’s unexpected visit. Around the time her hair was completely dried, she heard from the Lord Chamberlain again.

“Your Majesty, Lloyd Frontera has arrived,” the Lord Chamberlain announced.

“Let him in.”

The door opened. She saw Lloyd behind it.

“Hail to Her Majesty. I, Lloyd Frontera, the eldest son of the Frontera County, am honored to be in the audience of the rightful owner of this land.”

Lloyd had his head down, revealing the back of his head. For a second, a sudden impulse to smack him instead of greeting him came over her. His head was chestnut-shaped, round, and circular, the kind that evoked the impulse to give it a whack.

“…”

Queen Magentano smothered the senseless impulse in her and focused on looking at Lloyd with a cold gaze. “It has been truly a long time, Lloyd Frontera.”

“Yes, Your Majesty. I hope you have been in good health,” remarked Lloyd, still bowing.

“Not so much. Unfortunately, since you did not heed my reminder.” Her voice was cold, emotionless.

“Your Majesty…?” Lloyd looked up, dazed and befuddled.

Queen Magentano scoffed as she looked straight into his eyes. “You said it yourself before crossing the gate of Hell into the underworld. Do you not remember?”

Lloyd searched his memory. “Before going to Hell, then…”

“How you said that you could only come back to my side if you went to Hell through the method of your choice,” reminded the queen.

“Oh…” gaped Lloyd.

“I told you then,” went on Queen Magentano, her voice now sounding judgmental. “If you really care for me, make sure to come back to my side unharmed. Do you remember how you responded?”

“That… I will try my best, Your Majesty.”

“How confident you sounded.”

Lloyd’s face reddened as the memories came back to him. He knew where this was going.

“And yet, why did I have to hear about your pursuits from someone else after you came back from Hell?”

“…”

“I was suddenly given news about you one day. They said you, who disappeared during the Bone Dragon incident, suddenly appeared in your fiefdom.”

“…”

“I believe they said you visited Count Cremo afterward, is that right?”

“…”

“Indeed. I understand. Count Cremo is a big fan of yours.”

“…”

“Answer,” she ordered as the silence was starting to irritate her.

“Please kill me, Your Majesty.” Words broke out of him as he pleaded.

“Are you sure? Do you mean it?”

“N-no,” Lloyd stuttered with evident hesitation in his voice. “Your Majesty, it is not that.”

“Then?”

“I beg your forgiveness for saying these things, but due to several urgent and complicated situations, I could not simply abandon everything and come back to your side at once, Your Majesty.”

“Is that so?” She looked at Lloyd as she raised her brow.

“Yes, Your Majesty,” replied Lloyd, stressing his voice. “All the while I was away from Your Majesty, my allegiance toward you never left for even a second.”

“Hmm..” mused Queen Magentano, “I have no way to tell if that is true.”

“That is-”

“However,” she interrupted, “It has been made very clear to me that you have unfortunately made your way first to Count Cremo, not me.”

“T-that’s…” Lloyd’s face turned crimson red.

“How did he welcome you? Did he open a grand ball? If not, that would have been truly problematic. After all, you first visited the count instead of me. Count Cremo must have welcomed you with wide open arms, knowing your devout feelings for him. Am I wrong?”

“Please save me, Your Majesty.” Lloyd dropped to the floor and bowed

With that, the bitter smile on the queen changed into a satisfied one. And now her eyes subtly danced with amusement and contentment. “This is why I like you.”

“Excuse me…?” Lloyd slightly lifted his head to read her face.

“Never mind,” she said immediately and changed the subject. “Did everything go well in Hell?”

“Certainly, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd.

“Everything is well with you? Are you injured anywhere?”

“As you can see, Your Majesty, I am completely fine, and-”

“And I’m sure that is due to my generosity and support?” said the queen.

“…”

“All right. This is enough small talk. Let us get to business.” Her eyes then grew icy. And she pulled no punches when she went on. “I ask you, Lloyd Frontera, did you request for an audience with me to solve the recent worries on my mind and request a reward in return?”

“Yes, Your Majesty,” affirmed Lloyd.

Lloyd didn’t deny her assumptions. Instead, he found this kind of conversation with the queen convenient. There was no beating around the bush. It was straight to the point. And then he recalled the events that happened over a month ago. The foundation of the jewel at the Mermaid Kingdom. The jewel’s blueprint and key materials. The things he must do to attain them.

With those in mind, he replied, “Your Majesty’s estimation is all precise.”

“Is that so?”

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“Then do you have an innovative solution to restore the authority of the imperial family that was diminished after the Bone Dragon wreaked havoc on the grand garden celebration?”

“Without a doubt, Your Majesty,” answered Lloyd as he nodded. He really did. He had somewhat guessed what would be worrying her and came up with a solution for her concern.

I knew it.

She was right about Lloyd and why he had come here. After all, he was a man who always calculated the gain. If he wasn’t convinced about the results, he wouldn’t have made his way here.

Thanks to his character, I can trust him to do the job.

A sense of relief and confidence flooded her heart. No one was as clever and reliable as Lloyd Frontera. The mere sight of him brimming with confidence made her feel that her problem was solved already. And so…

“Good. If your plan is that certain, I am sure you have already thought of the reward you want from me.” Her voice was now softer.

“That is also without a doubt, Your Majesty,” replied Lloyd.

“Tell me in advance what you want.” Queen Magentano resolved to give him whatever he wanted, even if it was out of the line.

Hear what he wants in advance since he was a reliable subject set to relieve her quandary. She wanted to cherish him and continue to have him under her wing. Feelings of generosity filled her heart.

And so, Lloyd began to speak rather shamelessly. “My trivial request is very simple, Your Majesty. I would like to request your permission… to pull out your palace from the ground.”
