# The Grand Completion Ceremony (2)

---

“Arghhh!”

“Wh-what… What is that?!”

“Run! No, move now! Get out of my way!”

The people scrambled, trying to outrun one another. Every single one of them was thrown into a maddening frenzy. The men had their shirt collars fly up as they sprinted, and the women threw off their heels to escape for their lives. Everyone looked to evacuate as quickly as possible, prompting a mass exodus. Bibeong roared at the fleeing group of people.

“Bibeong!”

The loud shout easily made their pace about ten percent faster.

What in the world is that?!

Fear and dismay gripped everyone. They were just at the bouquet simply enjoying the excellent food and aromatic wine, admiring the beautiful grand garden, feeling proud to be invited to the royal celebration, and networking within their social circle. That was all they were doing. Nothing more.

“Bibebeong!”

“…!”

And so, none of them understood or wanted to understand why they had to face this colossal monster in front of them and process what it was doing here.

“You over there! Run faster!”

“Here, take my hand!”

The people ran for their lives, flooding out of the banquet hall in a rush. Everyone ran and grew farther away, and soon enough, silence filled the previously bustling banquet hall. This made Lloyd smile.

Wow, I didn’t expect this to work.

Lloyd wasn’t sure, to be honest. Even when he brought Bibeong out, he wasn’t sure if this would work as many people in the hall knew him.

It’s unsurprising since Bibeong joined the garden construction and became a big help. Thanks to it, word spread about him amongst the people in the capital.

It was only a slight exaggeration to say that almost everyone in the capital was aware of Bibeong. The few who weren’t would be newborn babies. That was why Lloyd was a bit unsure whether this method would work. He wanted to evacuate people outside the hall, but a part of him had doubts about them running away at the sight of Bibeong. Nonetheless, Lloyd decided to give it a try and hoped that using shock would work.

Honestly, not many people would have come across Bibeong in real life outside of rumors. Moreover, this closely.

It was a lot different to see a giant monster in front of your eyes compared to hearing about one. The difference was even more stark than seeing a skyscraper in a picture and staring at it from the ground level. Why? Bibeong could move and roar, whereas the building couldn’t.

The length of his body alone is 328 feet. That’s the size of a building. And imagine that kind of giant creature standing up on two legs, roaring and tap-dancing as his belly swings. It’s only natural to be frightened and run for your life.

That was the effect Lloyd wished to bring about, and he expected people to run away from a skyscraper-sized, hula-dancing beaver. Even the ones who knew that Bibeong was meek and gentle would do the same.

How can they not? Imagine that there’s a domesticated polar bear and it starts loudly roaring in your face. I’d be surprised if I didn’t wet my pants. It would scare the wits out of me even if the bear held a coke bottle in their paw.

Anyway, Lloyd’s plan worked just as he expected. Most of the guests were thrown into panic and ran for their lives. Soon, the whole hall was emptied, minus some dutiful servants and acrobats. Now, the royal guards started to fill up the space as they shot stern gazes at Lloyd.

“Lloyd Frontera, the eldest son of Count Arcos Frontera, and his knight better kneel down at once and follow our instructions.”

With an authoritative tone and a gaze more commanding than his voice, the royal guard commander spoke tonelessly, and Lloyd suppressed the wry smile that was forming on his face.

He must think I caused a scene.

Lloyd suddenly summoned a fantastical creature in the middle of the ceremony and threw people into chaos and panic by creating a terrorizing scene. Lloyd figured he would face serious punishment for killing the party. Lloyd called Bibeong as he carefully eyed the royal guard commander. He fed him a blue sunflower seed and put him inside his inner pocket. Then Lloyd obeyed the commander’s instructions and got down on his knee. 

“Looks like she’ll come down on us this time,” said Lloyd, “that is if nothing happens from now on.”

“I did not expect you would bring out Sir Bibeong…” grumbled Javier as he got down on his knee beside Lloyd. 

Turning to his side, Lloyd chuckled and said, “I couldn’t think of any other way to evacuate everyone." 

"Was calmly alerting the queen of the situation not an option for you,” reminded Javier.

“But then I would need to receive permission from the queen. And provide an explanation for what we’re dealing with right now and persuade her. It’s too slow. We don’t have time to go through all those steps.” Right around the time Javier and Lloyd were bickering, someone broke in their conversation.

“Now is the time for you to explain and persuade me.”

It was Queen Magentano. She was standing three steps away from them and looking down at them with an arrogant gaze. Thankfully, there was no resentment or anger in her eyes.

“Lloyd Frontera. I am flustered. The Lloyd I know would not cause this sort of turmoil without a good reason.” “Forgive me, Your Majesty.”

“No, explain yourself first before asking for my forgiveness.” Lloyd realized from her firm voice that she didn’t want any formalities. A faint smile appeared in his mouth.

I knew she’d be wise.

Once again, Lloyd felt that the queen was extraordinary. She didn’t jump to conclusions, even amid this kind of situation. She didn’t readily lose her calm or temper either. Instead, she stared at Lloyd with a calm gaze.

“Yes, Your Majesty,” Lloyd replied quickly with calmness. “I will cut to the chase. I felt an explosive increase of mana flow in the dragon skeleton.”

“You felt a flow of mana?” asked the queen.

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“You did when even I couldn’t feel anything?”

“To be exact, it was Sir Asrahan who felt it.”

“…”

Her gaze toward Javier stiffened for a second. Lloyd wondered if it was because she realized that she was still behind Javier. But Lloyd brushed the thought aside as he knew there was something more important right now. The flow of mana inside the dragon skeleton was growing more fierce with every passing second.

“Therefore, I dared to do something unbelievable.”

“Was it to evacuate people as quickly as possible?” asked the queen.

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“Moreover, I presume you determined that your ruckus would bring the troops to this place. Am I right?” “Forgive me, Your Majesty.”

“It’s too early to forgive you,” said the queen. “I must confirm your words.”

At her order, a senior palatial magician hurried and walked over to the dragon skeleton and pointed his magic wand to it. After a prolonged session of focusing, he detected a flow of mana. Meanwhile, Lloyd was still down on his knee, nibbling his bottom lip.

This feels like I’m waiting to get my test grade back.

But Lloyd was certain that his test would get a perfect score. Unlike the queen, who didn’t own the Asrahan Core Technique, he could detect the flow of mana in the dragon skeleton in real time from a distance.

The flow is slowly getting more fierce.

It was almost like the mana of a living creature who had a pumping heart and pulse, breathed in and out, opened its eyes, and finally moved.

“…”

What if it really started moving? What was he supposed to do? Suddenly, Lloyd was gripped with an uneasy feeling, and he thought for a brief second. If he was right about his hunch, then there was only one course of action for him to take.

I obviously have to run away.

Just then, the setting in the novel hit his mind. Among the undead monsters who came back to life, the novel stated there were two that stood above the rest.

Hell’s Knight and Bone Dragon.

The skeleton of the Bone Dragon itself was a mass of mana, and its bones moved using the mana as its source. The novel event mentioned that it was nothing short of a walking disaster.

Javier was the one who said it in the novel when he was still a sword master. That running away is the only way to survive when faced with such a creature. Theoretically, only the grandmaster could fight against the Bone Dragon.

The Knight of Blood and Iron. For some reason, the Bone Dragon was more powerful than any other dragon in this novel. To analogize, if most dragons were considered ordinary humans, the Bone Dragon was a skeleton. Just like how ordinary people could be beaten by the skeletons, most dragons couldn’t be left unscathed after a fight with the Bone Dragon. And so, only the strong and powerful warriors among the ordinary dragons could defeat the Bone Dragon. What if this dragon skeleton came back to life and started to move? What if it was Bone Dragon?

I’ll run without looking back.

Lloyd resolved to do so. Moreover, he resolved to take Javier, who was kneeling right beside him.

Of course, I should take him. There’s no reason for either of us to risk our lives here.

Lloyd knew that he was no hero. He wasn’t like the superheroes that appeared in films. He was just an ordinary person who wanted to retire and enjoy his life. Fighting in combat? He didn’t have it in him. Sure, one thing had led to another, and he came to own the Asrahan Core Technique and manaheart in the process of overcoming some crises. However, he never expected to use them and show off his strength and power to other people.

I can do some self defense. That’s about it.

Moreover, if the enemy he was up against was the Bone Dragon, which only grandmasters could put up a fight against, it was only wise for him to run away for his life. And he wouldn’t feel the slightest amount of guilt about it.

The troops are here. And the queen’s here too.

They existed to protect the kingdom. And so, Lloyd didn’t plan to risk his life when there were people like them.

I pray that I am wrong. Come on.

With his heart in his mouth, Lloyd stared at the palatial magician and the dragon skeleton. Meanwhile, he sharpened the flow of mana in the skeleton.

Please.

The flow grew fiercer.

Haa… Please.

The mana flow became stronger. Lloyd gulped in spite of himself.

Seriously. Stop.

Lloyd begged inwardly that this ruckus would conclude as a false alarm. He didn’t care about being reprimanded by the queen. Lloyd pleaded and begged. Could it be that his prayer worked?

Eh…?

The mana flow suddenly stopped its amplification, and it gradually started to weaken like a Vitcoin graph after its peak or like the housing prices that would plummet the next day

“…”

Lloyd turned to Javier, who was looking at him. Javier nodded. He, too, felt it.

I’m sure of it. The mana is weakening.

Lloyd smiled involuntarily. He could feel it. The mana flow inside the dragon skeleton was sharply calming down as though it was never any different. The palatial magician seemed to have felt it too.

“Your Majesty,” announced the magician, “It is true that there was mana flowing inside the skeleton much like what the eldest son of the Frontera family reported. But it is not strong enough for us to worry about it.”

“It is nothing to worry about?” asked the queen.

“No, Your Majesty.”

Pulling his wand back, the magician replied courteously.

“I have read records of similar instances as this one. To begin with, a dragon bone naturally holds a considerable amount of mana. Therefore, the records said that the mana flows on its own from time to time.”

“So, are you saying that the mana flow that you detected does not posit any special danger or abnormality?” “Yes, Your Majesty, I-”

Wham! Right before he could finish, the foreleg of the dragon crushed down on the magician.

“…!”

A fierce vibration spread across the land, bringing a thick, explosive cloud of dust. The dragon skeleton raised its head amid the dust and let out a roar.

“…!”

It was a silent roar. Without any vocal organs, it could only use mana to let out a roar, but that was exactly why it was so menacing.

Fwoosh! A shockwave of unadulterated mana blasted in all directions, tossing the banquet tables and smashing the podium in a flash. Trees were rooted out, and dishes and wine glasses shattered into thousands of shards. Everything within about 300 yards of distance was swept away by the force.

“Argh!!!”

One of the servants hurtled away with a shout. The guards tried to persist as they bled from the piercing shards of glass. Amid the stormy mana, Queen Magentano flew open her eyes in fright.

This… What… What?!

Her eyes darted toward the Bone Dragon. She couldn’t believe it. She thought it was merely a spoil, a tool that would memorialize her great achievement. But…

It can’t be.

This was not an accident or a coincidence. A shaft of doubt and suspicion arose in her mind. She pieced together all her memories related to the skeleton until now. She had brought the dragon skeleton after suppressing the warlocks and put it up at the center of the capital. Then there was a celebration ceremony. And now, the skeleton was moving and stirring up this entire area. And at last, Bone Dragon was looking down from a towering height. The moment their gazes met, she understood at last.

I was deceived.

She recalled the group of warlocks. An easy win, she thought. A great success. But that was a mistake. A grave mistake. She was arrogant to regard so. As soon as she realized, she leaped high and moved to correct her mistake, her sword already shining bright with a brilliant aura.

Thwack! She charged toward the Bone Dragon’s foreleg that was touching the ground. She wielded her sword, her aura turbulently glinting. But it bounced off.

Clang! “…!”

She couldn’t scratch let alone split its foreleg in half. Terror filled her eyes. But she didn’t allow panic to settle in her heart.

Instead, she calmly shouted, “Lloyd Frontera!”

Lloyd was the first person who foresaw this catastrophe. So, he was the most reliable individual at the moment. He might be able to enlighten her on how to break through this situation. She looked sideways and called out to Lloyd. That was when she realized something.

“…”

She realized that Lloyd was already far away and running faster than anyone else.
