# The Membership for the Dragon King’s Support (2)

---

The dragon king?

Lloyd’s eyes widened when he saw the figure. He was sure. There was no one around up until a moment ago. Javier had verified that himself. But now, someone was here. No, not someone. It was an animal. To be more exact, it was a lion larger than an elephant. And its claws, which were revealed between its front paws, looked menacing. Its magnificent mane gave off a majestic atmosphere, and it had bat wings and scorpion tails. But contrary to all, its belly was so chubby that Lloyd wanted to give it a touch to its fluffy and comfy texture. Anyway, based on its physical features, this creature could only be…

The manticore?

As soon as Lloyd uttered the name of the monster that emerged in front of him, the manticore opened its mouth and said, “Corong?”

“…”

“Cororong! Corong!”

“…”

Lloyd wondered what this monster was saying. Wait, could it be that it was growling? Or was it trying to start a conversation? Nonetheless, Lloyd didn’t show any outward reaction to the manticore’s roaring or greeting. He neither flinched in fear nor took a step back. His face didn’t show any hint of surprise or dismay. However, deep inside, he was flustered. But he repressed his emotions, determined to reveal nothing, controlled his facial expression, and tensed his muscles. The rationale behind his forced nonchalance was simple.

You never know with life. What if this manticore turns out to be Dragon King Verkis?

Such a thought crossed his mind, and he thought it was very likely.

Dragons can perform powerful magic. It’s said that they can even morph into other races.

What this meant was that dragons were capable of shapeshifting into monsters as well. That was why Lloyd couldn’t rashly show any reaction.

Duh! What if Dragon King Verkis came here with that appearance because of some weird kink of his? Shouting my dismay out loud or reacting with shock will kill my chance to leave a good impression on him!

That scandalous event was the last thing he wanted to see. He had taken a trip to Hell to earn the support of the dragon king, constructing his butt off for months in sweltering heat. And here he was, at last, in the presence of the king. Lloyd did not want a momentary mistake to ruin his first impression.

That can never happen. The dragon king must become my number one supporter.

An extra life. Endless treasure. Other additional rewards on top of everything. The dragon king was the very benefactor that would add one final, luxurious touch to Lloyd’s opulent retirement. And so, he detested the thought of leaving a bad impression on him.

“Hmm! Ehem!” Lloyd cleared his throat. “Javier, I want you to politely wait.”

Lloyd stopped Javier first, fearing that the latter might pull out his sword like a clueless fool. After that, he politely folded his hands and looked up at the manticore in the most respectful manner, his eyes glimmering and shining. Could it be that his tactic to be courteous worked? The Manticore’s face started to soften as it gazed at Lloyd, and it stood up on its hind legs, lifted its chubby body, and motioned with its front paws.

“Cororog? Corong! Cororong!”

“…”

“Corong! Cororong!”

“Ahaha…” mumbled Lloyd. “Okay, yes…”

“Cororog? Corong! Corong!”

“Hmm, is that so? Ahaha…”

An awkward smile spread across Lloyd’s face. Javier inched closer to Lloyd and whispered, “Master Lloyd.”

“What, why?” replied Lloyd with slight annoyance.

“Do you understand what the manticore is saying?”

“No.”

“Then-”

“Shut your mouth first,” growled Lloyd under his breath. “You’re not the only one going bonkers over this.”

Lloyd wiped the cold sweat off him. This manticore was different from other fantastical creatures such as Ppodong. Lloyd couldn’t make the slightest sense of what the chubby lion wanted from him with its growling or motioning.

Goodness. What is this old, hairless creature saying?!

Just then, Lloyd wanted to burst into tears. If only he could, he wanted to give a hard pinch to its pinkish belly, which was splotchy with hair loss, until something hit him.

Wait, hold on.

Come to think of it, there were a few creatures that were briefly mentioned in the novel. Dragon King Verkis. And the five evil creatures that served him.

I remember. Verkis was said to have them at his side.

Lloyd couldn’t recall everything about the five creatures. But he did manage to remember that a 26-feet long manticore was the leader among them.

Is this the creature mentioned in the novel?

Lloyd looked up at the manticore, his eyes now filled with wonder. His suspicion that it could be the creature he read about soon turned into conviction. If that was the case, he needed to confirm the truth before anything else.

“Excuse me,” Lloyd asked in a careful voice. “Are you here because Dragon King Verkis sent you?”

“Corong!”

The manticore nodded with a big smile on its face, and it started to gesture with more earnestness as though Lloyd could finally understand its words.

“Cororong! Corong? Corong!”

“Oh, wait, please gesture a bit more slowly…”

“Corong? Cororong! Corong!”

“Okay, so, you’re here to run an errand for Dragon King Verkis?” asked Lloyd.

“Cororong! Corong!”

“Oh, so you’re here to take me.”

“Corong!”

The manticore nodded again. And then, it gave its chubby butt to Lloyd and flopped to the ground before glancing at Lloyd and motioning toward its back with its jaw.

“Cororong!”

“You want me to ride on your back…?” Lloyd questioned with hesitation in his voice.

“Corong!”

“…”

It appeared so. Lloyd and Javier exchanged glances.

Javier nodded. “I will protect you if something happens.”

The manticore was evidently a powerful creature. A single angry manticore possessed the strength to wipe out one entire corps of elite human soldiers. Though it was as big as Ppodong, it easily transcended the latter in terms of combat power. Plus, a manticore as old as this one had to be a seasoned fighter on the battlefield. If Lloyd didn’t have Javier by his side, he’d have been left to pray that he wouldn’t be eaten up.

I’m glad that this fellow is on my side.

A sense of relief swelled over Lloyd and, trusting Javier, he hopped on the back of the manticore.

“Eh, um, please fly safely.”

“Corong!”

The manticore gave a snort as if there was nothing to worry about. And thus, a frenzied race began. Kaboom!

“Eek!!”

They grew far from the ground in a split second, and the view of their surroundings passed by them with overwhelming speed. Lloyd was able to avoid being thrown off to the ground by reflexively grabbing onto the creature’s mane. It was only then that he was able to make out what was happening.

What kind of speed is this?!

The manticore was unbelievably fast. Lloyd was used to riding Ppodong and flying on Ggoming’s back. However, the manticore was much faster than those two. It was so fast that the headwind blowing against his face made it difficult to breathe.

Argh!!

Fwoosh! The violent flurry of headwind blew against him, and his facial muscles and skin crumpled from the force. Even the air traveling into his nostrils felt like an invading enemy, making him feel they were being expanded three or four times wider. Lloyd had to cast his head down to breathe out. And as he could finally breathe, Lloyd started to grow excited.

What about Javier?

A thought popped into Lloyd’s head. This was a golden opportunity. The powerful gust of headwind blowing against his facial muscle and skin had to be making Javier put on an ugly face too. Lloyd felt his heart race at the thought.

Oh, I wish I had a smartphone right now!

That way, he would be able to take a picture of Javier’s funny face and keep it for eternity. He was regretful he couldn’t but nonetheless excited about what he’d witness soon. Then Lloyd threw a glance toward Javier, only to find the knight looking perfectly fine and normal.

“Why are you looking at me like that, Young Master?” asked Javier calmly.

“Eh…?”

“For some reason, your eyes are much more sparkly than usual.”

“…”

Lloyd was left utterly speechless. Javier’s face was perfectly fine! It was completely normal despite the strong headwinds. He didn’t even have a single eyelash fluttering out of place.

This is not fair!

Lloyd inwardly screamed in disbelief. But could it be that Javier figured out what was happening? He scoffed at Lloyd.

“It looks like you are curious why my face is fine, unlike yours,” said Javier.

“…”

“Well, it is because I am a grandmaster,” added Javier matter-of-factly.

“…”

“I am currently using mana. Just in time, actually, as I was recently experimenting with how I can increase my body’s level of resistance against foreign forces. According to my calculation, it seems I can increase my skin’s strength, elasticity, and sturdiness to that of metal. Like now.”

“It makes no sense,” Lloyd uttered, incredulous.

“It makes perfect sense.”

“How?!”

“Like this.”

Whoosh... Javier raised his hand. He straightened his index finger and stirred the air. Then a strand of brilliant aura appeared from the tip of his finger, which then drew an image in the air. But the image looked strange. It was a human face. A terribly ugly one. It looked like someone had stuffed their head inside a stocking and was propelled dozens of feet in the air. Lloyd scowled.

“What is this wrinkly half-formed face?”

“This is what you look like now, Master Lloyd.”

“…”

“I am serious,” Javier went on, “You look extremely memorable. Avant-garde, even, I must say. Your face is usually plain, nothing much to enjoy. But now, with all the terrible crumpling, scrunching, and wrinkling from the wind, you are presenting a forever unforgettable sight to every beholder of your face, even with just a quick glance.”

A pang of shock left Lloyd speechless.

“How to describe this image…” Javier continued, his voice filled with delight. “It is a reinterpretation of the human face. Extremely powerful and dynamic, yet its asymmetrical uniqueness and singularity are clearly visible. This disharmony creates a multidimensional appearance that is completely outside the standard human face. It is remarkable. I feel your face is the new exemplar of asymmetry.”

“…”

Please, stop praising my face as if you’re viewing modern art. Despite the desire to say that thought out loud, Lloyd couldn’t do it. The manticore changed its direction, and the wind threw another hit to his face right before he could speak, causing a devastating tremble on his lips. He couldn’t fight back even though he wanted to. So, he was left to hold back his tears and promise himself to return this humiliation someday. Meanwhile, Javier continued to speak with a serious look on his face.

“Anyway,” Javier said, “what really is outrageous is the speed of this manticore.”

“Eh… Umph.. Yes, I agree with you,” Lloyd replied, ducking his head to avoid the wind.

“It looks like the Dragon King Verkis has cast a special magic on this creature,” Javier said.

“Right?”

“Yes.”

It probably was the case. Sure, the manticore could be a powerful creature. But this speed simply just transcended the limits of biology.

Seriously, the dragon king is definitely something.

What would it feel like to be born like this? What good did this king do in his past life to be born with a silver spoon in his mouth and such outrageous powers?

Would it be enough to have saved a kingdom around a hundred times in your past life?

Lloyd was green with envy. As his thoughts dwelled on such, the snapshots of his surroundings changed in seconds, the mound suddenly becoming a river, which soon became a field and wilderness. Past several villages and cities and passing over the lake and valley, the manticore finally climbed up the foot of a mountain until a giant cave came into view.

A dragon’s cave, I suppose?

Gaffel is a neutral and autonomous city. Lloyd recalled how a dragon’s mountain sat there, in which Dragon King Verkis quietly resided. What stood right ahead of Lloyd seemed to be the very dragon’s cave in that mountain.

“Corong!”

The manticore continued its race toward the cave and passed through the well-polished and complicated passages. Lloyd’s eyes grew serious as he studied them.

I knew it.

It was the dragon cave. Even though he couldn’t see thoroughly because everything was passing by in seconds, he noticed that the architecture was remarkable.

This entire place was built by carving and digging out the inside of a mountain. The rock foundation that makes up the mountain was used as the exterior of this dungeon.

Lloyd wondered how much capital and labor he would need if he had to deal with a project of this size. Chills ran down his spine just at the thought of it. Even with the surveying and design skills and the assistance of the fantastical creatures, a project with such an enormous demand, that is, to utilize this gigantic mountain itself as construction material, would require at least ten years.

No, I won’t be able to do it in even ten years. This is seriously no joke.

At the sight of the dungeon, Lloyd felt in his bones the sheer power of this dragon king. It wasn’t to be compared to the dungeon of Solitas, who matured only recently. That was how overwhelming this place was, and that was why Lloyd’s heart raced.

And the creator of that is going to become my ally.

In life, there are a few elements that must be cherished. A heartwarming family. Enough wealth to live by. Connections you could always rely on. And right now, Lloyd was about to acquire the last element, the best of the best connection. He had the heartwarming Frontera family. And he held enough wealth in the Frontera fiefdom. On top of those two, the support from Dragon King Verkis. Lloyd was just seconds away from setting up his life in luxurious retirement.

Lalalala! Wahahaha!

The humming came out naturally. It didn’t stop even when the manticore stopped and when he got off its back. He even did a joyful tap dance when his feet touched the ground. And he was led into the deepest part of the cave.

“Cororong! Corong!”

When he turned to the last corner, an empty cave welcomed his arrival. The length and width appeared to be several miles. The ceiling, too, was inconceivably high. There was more than enough space for several 656-foot dragons to do a full-court basketball game. Moreover, all kinds of treasure carpeted the whole area.

But despite the vast space and the overwhelming number of treasures that filled up the space, Lloyd didn’t bat an eye.

He couldn’t help it due to the sofa sitting randomly in the center of the cave with someone on it. The sight entranced Lloyd so ferociously that he couldn’t see anything else around him. It was the dragon king. Did the former grab Lloyd’s attention with a kingly charisma? Or with an oppressive dignity? Not at all.

What is this homebody doing here…?

Much like a father lazing around on a sofa over a weekend baseball game, the dragon king was lying on the sofa, as if he was a part of it. Lloyd couldn’t tell what was the sofa or the dragon king. That was Lloyd’s first impression of Dragon King Verkis.
