# How to Freeze Time (3)

---

“Damn… This is amazing.”

Lloyd murmured involuntarily. It just slipped out. How can it not? Time was fair to everyone. It dutifully flowed onward. But now, Lloyd came to somewhat possess control over it. If the description in front of his eyes was true, he could now take part in the flow of time.

So, I’m using the power of the Heart of Winter in my manaheart. I can freeze time in this world.

This was too much, Lloyd thought to himself. This skill was transcendental. Godly.

But I shouldn’t get too pumped up.

Lloyd got a grip on himself and didn’t allow himself to cheer or get carried away in joy. Instead, he meticulously studied the description, picking at its words, thinking, and looking at it with suspicion.

It’s too good of an optional skill. It’s so amazing it leaves me speechless.

Like everything, if a deal or award sounded too good to be true, it was a trap. There was no such thing as a free lunch in this world. There was no such thing as a giving tree. Lloyd believed that the first thing he should do when someone shoves a much too sweet deal up to his face was to be a skeptic. And that was how he managed to survive all these years. The same thing was happening now.

There wasn’t a time the system message deceived me. But this one’s too good to be true. So, I should be careful, investigate, and confirm it myself.

He shouldn’t be overjoyed. First, he needed to organize his thoughts.

To sum up everything, I can freeze time in this world, but I can designate certain areas to be excluded. If so, time will flow in those areas normally, but no one will age.

The more he chewed it over, the better the option was. But, of course, it came with some restrictions.

Once the skill is activated, time can freeze only up to a year. And it’s impossible to change the no-freeze zone until I deactivate the skill. Also, it gets automatically deactivated when I leave the no-freeze area. Plus, using the skill comes with a price. One usage costs me a whopping 3,000 RP.

Lloyd checked how much RP he had.

Hmm… 2,665. It’s not quite enough.

He wanted to use the skill and confirm its capabilities right away. But he couldn’t. He didn’t have enough RP.

Looks like it’s time for some RP hunt.

Better yet, Lloyd wondered if there was any opportunity he could explosively up his likeability. He figured he ought to collect more than 3,000 RP, even if it took some toil on his part.

Since it’s the most perfect and best thing that ever happened to me.

The mere thought of it made his heart thump in excitement. He couldn’t help it even when he tried to calm down. But how could he not? He could come up with millions of ways to use the skill to his advantage.

I can use it for the most urgent matter right now – finding the materials for the Aotearoa and conducting the construction. I didn’t have much time left, and it was starting to irk me.

Just then, the Ending Spoiler came to his mind. And the restoration of destiny. Lloyd now had two years and a half until that effing event started to roll. But he had too many tasks to accomplish before then. That was the problem. That was what was gnawing at him.

I’ve got to collect all the key materials for the jewel’s construction in two and a half years. Only the heavens know how long that will take. But will it be over after I find them? No. I’ve got to build the jewel. What about after? I’ve got to ask and find a way to stop the restoration of destiny.

He also knew that there was no chance his job would end after getting an answer.

Once I have the solution, I’ll get busy again trying to put it all into action.

He had no way of knowing what the solution would look like. But he figured it wouldn’t be simple. It was also a mystery how much time it’d take. And yet, despite everything, he only had two and a half years, and the thought had been making him toss and turn in the middle of the night.

But now. I have this spectacular option. From finding the materials to building the jewel… Each time I build something, I should simply freeze time after I designate the work site as a no-freeze area. That way, I’ll be able to work while the world’s time has been stopped. A few months. A year. It doesn’t matter how long the construction will take. Time will be frozen anyway.

It appeared that he’d be able to save an obscene amount of time.

And if all my efforts, all this drudgery ends up futile, and if I can’t stop destiny, then I’ll just freeze time after designating Frontera County as a no-freeze area. It sounds kind of drastic, but well. I’ll be earning one year’s worth of time. Meanwhile, I’ll have to rack my brain and figure something out. All the while, the world’s clock is put on pause.

Yes, it was a drastic method. But it was a good band-aid solution for if he became stuck.

Anyway, having such an option alone is extremely useful.

Lloyd resolved to himself: He would work harder to collect RP. Just then, an unexpected voice pierced his ears.

“Are you fine?”

“Hmm..?” Lloyd was jostled out of his trance, and when he looked up, he saw an outline of a figure looking down at him with the sun behind her back. Wavy short hair. A wry smile on her face.

“Your Majesty.” When Lloyd saw her take three steps further and smile mysteriously, he immediately got down to his knee and said, “Hail to Her Majesty. I, Lloyd Frontera, the eldest son of the Frontera County, am honored to be in the audience of the rightful owner of this-”

“There is no need for formalities in this circumstance. I asked how you are,” she said gravely.

“Your Majesty?” asked Lloyd.

“I am asking if you are hurt.”

“As you can see, Your Majesty, I am completely fine.”

“Is that so?”

“Yes, Your Majesty.” Lloyd nodded promptly and felt flustered.

Crap. This is bad. I haven’t thought up an excuse yet.

One thing had led to another, and Lloyd inadvertently absorbed the Heart of Winter, which he had borrowed from the queen. It wasn’t any royal treasure. It was a divine item of the imperial family. He still hadn’t thought of a believable excuse as he was busy reading through his new optional skill and thinking of ways to utilize it. He had become too excited, despite his efforts not to, and thus, failed to prepare any excuses yet. He had never expected to face the queen so quickly.

Tsk. What should I tell her?

Lloyd racked his brain. The events that occurred at this scene. The myriad of different circumstances he saw during the accident. His actions and their results. The expected response from the queen and the public. Lloyd recalled, compounded, and predicted every one of these things, running a plethora of scenarios and adjusting his reactions to them. Not too long after, he was ready to make a case for himself in a sly voice and was about to speak when the queen questioned him first.

“Looking at the situation, I assume that the Heart of Winter has been lost. Am I right?”

“Oh, Your Majesty. If I could explain-”

“There’s no need,” the queen interrupted in a gentle voice. “I came here after realizing what had happened.”

“…”

Lloyd stared at the queen. For some reason, he couldn’t get a clear read of her expression or eyes. There was something strange about her tone and expression. Could it be that she was angry?

I’d be angry if someone broke a precious item I’d lent them.

Lloyd then thought that apologizing and begging for her forgiveness seemed to be a better choice to improve his relationship with her. Just then, the queen spoke something that took Lloyd by surprise.

“I have heard,” she said, “From the one of the guard knights you saved. When he opened the door to the refrigeration room, something already happened, and two royal magicians were covered in blood. He said it looked like one stabbed the other.”

Lloyd didn’t say anything.

“I presume that whatever happened between the two magicians accounts for this incident. Although there will be a series of additional investigations, judging from the witnesses of the guard, I can assume that you are not directly liable for it.”

Lloyd’s eyes softened.

“I also heard that,” she went on. “You risked your life saving the magicians and guards. And how you willingly jumped to save the laborers trapped inside the cave. Thus, how could I lash out at you for this?”

“Um, Your Majesty, please forgive me, but…”

“Tell me.”

“Why is it that you favor me to this extent?” There was genuine curiosity in Lloyd’s eyes.

“Are you curious?”

“Yes, Your Majesty.” Lloyd spoke frankly. He was surprised by her response and attitude. Her willingness to exonerate him from this whole mess. The queen chuckled once again.

“I am not favoring you or confirming what you have done is right. I am simply informing you.”

“What is it for, Your Majesty?”

“Consider this an example of the statement you will make when you are under the inquiry of the House of Peers.” Her smile softened. “Since an accident broke out nonetheless on your construction site and resulted in the loss of the royal house’s divine item, this is extremely grave and significant, whatever my personal feelings lay,” said Queen Magentano.

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“That is why I am doing this. The House of Peers will not let this slide as it had given its consensus to the usage of the Heart of Winter. It is likely that they will soon submit a bill of indictment and petition to the courtroom.”

“Will they, Your Majesty?”

“Yes,” the queen said as she nodded. “Since you are my meritorious retainer and have legal immunity that limits any judgment on you to me, they will file a request to have you stand in front of court.”

“Oh.”

Lloyd saw this coming. He nodded. The queen continued to speak.

“Moreover, I will have to grant the request from the House of Peers. There is no reason for me not to. After all, I should be the one flying into a rage and sending you into the courtroom.”

“But why is it that Your Majesty is not-”

“Are you asking why I am siding with you?”

“Yes, Your Majesty,” affirmed Lloyd.

“I like you.”

“Your Majesty?” Lloyd raised his head, taken aback by her response. She was smiling. He even caught some playfulness in her eyes.

“To me, I find you are much more useful than a divine item I can only use twice or thrice in a span of a few centuries. Whereas you will be of use to me for several decades. Don’t you agree?”

“Um, that is a very direct way to say you’re going to make me work…”

“This is my investment in you as a talent. Don’t you think so?”

“Not at all, Your Majesty,” denied Lloyd.

“Tsk. You do not have to refuse me so cold-heartedly.”

“Consider it a tantrum borne out of guilt for causing trouble, Your Majesty.”

Lloyd cast his head down, genuinely feeling apologetic and grateful at the same time.

She is openly telling me that she’ll cover for me in the future. We’re talking about the loss of a divine item here. Yet, she’s going to overlook it.

Surely, the queen was no ordinary monarch. She was a big-hearted queen. And her outspoken intent to put him to useful work? Lloyd partly understood her greed.

Well. She has been generous to me. She didn’t lash out when I sucked her mana with my Supercharge during the assassination attempt against her. Moreover, she’s been showering my fiefdom with large amounts of supply aid since the Monster Domino incident. There are also several other instances when I received special treatment from her.

Come to think of it, the queen was his biggest source of help in overcoming the obstacles he had faced until now. But, of course, it didn’t mean that he intended to become her servant.

It’s not like I’ve done nothing for her.

He had given and done as much for her as he had received from her. That alone was enough. So, Lloyd decided to happily accept her assistance. He would just return the favor at another time.

“Words fall short of my gratitude to you, Your Majesty,” Lloyd expressed sincerely. And he was also grateful that everyone was safe. Now, it was time to clean up the mess.

♣️

A few days passed. The construction site was cleaned up in no time. To Lloyd’s relief, the cave didn’t suffer much damage. The frozen ground wasn’t affected, either. It looked like the remaining portion of the construction could resume normal operation without delay. However, something else stopped the construction from resuming. The House of Peers filed a petition against Lloyd, submitting a bill of indictment to hold him responsible for losing the Heart of Winter.

The queen was right.

Things took place as she had expected. She accepted the bill from the House of Peers and announced the date for the trial.

Meanwhile, Lloyd enjoyed his rare break as days passed. At last, the day of the trial came.

“Are you ready, Master Lloyd? We should leave now if you do not want to be late.”

“Yes, hold on.” Lloyd stood in front of the mirror. It was the first day of the trial. When Javier insisted they must leave now, he replied, “Hold on a second. Let me just finish my warm-up exercise.”

“What warm-up exercise?” asked Javier.

“Stretching my tongue.” Lloyd knew. In a fierce court battle, his tongue would be his weapon. Smiling mischievously at the mirror, Lloyd licked his lips.
