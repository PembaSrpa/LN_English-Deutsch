# My Responsibility (1)

---

It’s cold, Lloyd thought. A gust of icy wind nipped his skin. He opened his eyes wide and saw a message through the swirling ice storm around him.

[<Frozen Hands, Frozen Feet> has been activated.]

“…!“

[You are now in a region where the annual average temperature is under 32 °F. As such, "Frozen Hands, Frozen Feet” has been activated. You will no longer suffer from hypothermia or freeze to death in cold climates.]

“Emph..!” Lloyd choked as he wrapped his hands around his body and thought fast. He thought about the current situation and the message’s meaning. Then he realized a potential danger.

The effect is useful… but it’s only protecting me from hypothermia. There’s no mention of protecting my skin from frostbite.

The moment he realized it…

Ziiing! Lloyd activated the Asrahan Core Technique and started desperately absorbing heat around him, amplifying it into three-strand circles. After loading it into his manaheart, he received a feeling of warmth throughout his skin, lungs, and eyes. It was only then that he was finally able to stand straight.

Woah. That was dangerous.

Hypothermia and frostbite were two entirely different conditions that could result from the freezing cold. The former described nothing short of freezing to death. The latter meant tissue necrosis in the skin.

So, if I blindly trusted my title and didn’t do anything, I’d have been able to survive but with frostbite in my ears and fingers. The sudden exposure to cold air would have dramatically decreased my blood flow, and as a result, my skin tissue would’ve died because of the lack of oxygen.

This kind of injury from cold, especially on the earflaps, was common among South Korean men while standing guard at the country’s border in the military during the middle of winter.

That was close… I’m now saved from getting frostbite and freezing to death. I had no idea the effects could have loopholes. I should be more careful from now on.

Lloyd learned from this experience that he shouldn’t blindly trust his title effects. Thinking that in his head, he pulled himself together and looked straight ahead into the rough, swirling storm. At first, his vision was blurry. Everything was vague and hazy. The storm was relentless, and the white snow hellishly blew around him. But when he trudged a few steps forward, his view became clearer.

This is a mess.

The refrigerant room in which the Heart of Winter was stored, protected by thick metal doors and six guard knights, was a mess. The metal doors were crumpled like a soda can and stuck into a corridor wall, along with several sharp shards of ice.

Where’s the Heart of Winter?

Lloyd wondered whether the treasure he’d begged to obtain from the queen was safe and whether the people in here were okay. He stepped across the threshold, jittery and anxious, and peered into the room. And he saw…

“Dammit.”

Everyone was on the ground and immobile. Two royal magicians and six guard knights were sprawled like mannequins on the floor. But the Heart of Winter was there, fiercely pounding. It was emitting frigid air through the cracks of the broken control chest. In short, it was on a rampage.

I knew that was the problem.

Lloyd’s heart pounded. But something was strange. Each time his heart beat, the Heart of Winter squelched and released more cold air.

What the heck? Is it reacting to my Half-Human Half-Dragon skill option?

It appeared so. Lloyd was initially skeptical, but when he took a closer look, that was the case. Plus, it looked like the burst of air it released grew rougher as he neared. It was like a wild beast leaping around in joy to meet its master.

What’s going on?

Lloyd bit his bottom lip. Now wasn’t the time to investigate. It didn’t matter if this object was matching the beat of his heart or reacting to his optional skill. Right now, there was a crisis to solve.

That’s a national treasure. How should I compensate for it if it gets destroyed? And what about the laborers inside? Let’s think. Think…

Come hell or high water. The situation had to be controlled. He had to do something. Anything. But Lloyd had no clue about how he could silence the out-of-control Heart of Winter. He felt stuck.

I wish Javier was with me.

Lloyd wondered how different things would be if Javier were here. After all, he was a grandmaster who possessed a monstrous manaheart and matching mana capabilities. He was the main protagonist of the original storyline in the novel. But Lloyd knew his wish was futile. Javier wasn’t here. He was currently at the palace training with the queen.

I can only take this matter into my own hands.

A thought crossed his mind: this was a battle of time. The longer he let this incident progress, the harder the situation would be to resolve. Just then…

“Argh… Ah…”

Lloyd picked up an ever so faint, agonized groan. It was coming from one of the royal guard knights on the floor.

Eh? Is someone alive?

Lloyd studied the groaning guard in surprise. To his astonishment, the man was breathing, though very faintly. His skin and lips were blue, and his pulse was faint from his severe hypothermia. But he was alive and holding on.

What about the others?

Lloyd jumped to his feet and checked the others. And he uttered, “Almost everyone is alive.”

Except for one person, who was the closest magician to the Heart of Winter, carrying a bloody dagger. The rest of them were breathing but on the brink of death.

It’ll be difficult for all of them to survive if this goes on for much longer.

The realization compelled him to immediately grab the six knights and one magician out to the corridor one by one. He first hoisted up the magician, who appeared to be in the most critical state. The man was bleeding in the back from an injury that looked to be from a dagger. However, the bleeding strangely wasn’t severe, and Lloyd figured it was probably because the wound had frozen during the explosion of frigid air in the room.

It looks like the dead magician stabbed him.

Lloyd frowned, his forehead crinkling, thinking what event could have possibly taken place in here. But he knew now wasn’t the time to dwell on such curiosity.

I’ve got to hurry.

There were people who needed to be saved. Lloyd focused all his efforts on rescuing them without missing a beat, first by carrying the magician on his back and transferring heat into the man’s major organs through the Asrahan Core Technique. And he climbed the vertical shaft toward the cave exit.

“Here! Call a doctor. Quickly!”

Lloyd shouted as he made it out on the ground. Just in time, there was a group of laborers near the exit. They were nervously chatting among themselves about the explosion and the strange gusts of cold air coming from inside the cave.

“Did something happen inside?”

“Yeah. An accident. There is cold air leaking from the Heart of Winter. Call for a doctor immediately.” Lloyd then looked at the leader of the group, who just spoke. “There are more people down there. I’ll get them out. Call a doctor and inform the queen. Get some blankets to warm all the bodies. As many as you can. And this guy over here has a stab wound. Press down against it firmly until a doctor gets here.”

“Yes.” There was nervousness in his eyes.

The rest of the laborers in the group became busy with their tasks. Meanwhile, Lloyd sprinted back to the cave and got down the ladder. He then carried one of the knights in the corridor on his back, dashed away, climbed the ladder, and laid him under the warm sun. He then left the others to care for him and went back in. Lloyd repeated this trip six times. Eventually, Lloyd managed to save everyone. Thankfully, everyone lived, likely due to how the knights were well-trained and strong.

“Ha… Whew…”

Lloyd’s body gave way to exhaustion as soon as he rescued everyone to safety. Calming his breath under the warm early summer sun, he dropped to the soiled ground and threw his head down in between his knees. People were running around in hurry and alarm, and yet, despite the chaos around him, the position offered him a dazing warmth. The sunlight stroking his back couldn’t feel any sweeter. He felt warm, cozy, and relieved. And then he flinched.

What the heck am I doing here? Why am I feeling relieved already? There are twenty guys down there… in the deepest part of the cave.

Gulp. Absentmindedly, Lloyd swallowed dryly. How could they have slipped from his mind? Or was he pretending to have forgotten them?

I told them to be on standby while I go and see what happened. I told them to stay low since it’s dangerous. I left them there like that.

Lloyd wondered if the impending arrival of the rescue team and the idea of transferring his responsibility to someone else were the source of his irrational relief as he sat on the warm ground under the sunlight.

Should I be the one to go and save them?

A thought came to him. It’d be too late by the time the rescue team arrived. The frigid air would spread all the way to the end of the cave, and they’d be in danger unless he did something then and there.

They won’t stay alive until the rescue team can come.

He had to get down there pronto and get them out. That was the right thing to do. He knew that. But at the same time, he was hesitant. Frankly, he was nervous.

Although I won’t die as I have the title… It doesn’t mean the icy air won’t hurt me. Or that it isn’t dangerous.

He just carried seven guys out. That added up to seven roundtrips. Each trip made him feel like his whole body was frozen, no, like he was thrown into an icy field naked in the middle of the winter. His skin, from head to toe, was in intense pain like he was being ripped apart. It was even the case when he had the Asrahan Core Technique activated to its full capacity. Plus…

I’ll have to go much further down to get to where the laborers are.

The longer travel time meant getting more exposure to the frigid air, giving him an equal amount of pain. He could end up suffering even with the Asrahan Core Technique. The constant exposure to the air could freeze his ears, face, fingers, and toes until they turned black. His fingers and toes could have to be amputated, like those of the frostbitten hikers of Mount Everest. Even if that didn’t happen, his lungs could get injured too. That meant his ability to breathe would be permanently impaired for the rest of his life. All the possibilities he could think of brought him uncertainty and anxiety.

I’m not confident about my safety.

There was a greater chance of his worries coming true. And honestly, Lloyd didn’t want that to happen in the slightest.

I’ll be disabled for the rest of my life. The rescue team should be coming soon, anyway. Is there really a need for me to risk myself?

It was selfish to think so. Yes, he knew that. But deep down and for just a second, he was hesitant. Nervous. He wasn’t a hero in an epic tale nor someone with a strong spirit that couldn’t be broken by anything. He was just an ordinary man and wanted to keep it that way for the rest of his life. He was scared.

But at the same time, he was being bugged by a memory from his period of destitution. The days when he was living in a room rental in South Korea and worked tirelessly every day on construction sites. The most wretched, infuriating memory of his from then pestered his mind.

There was a middle-aged man at the construction site he worked at. He and Lloyd bumped into each other a few times at the employment agency and worked as a team from time to time. But Lloyd didn’t know the man’s name. They weren’t very close and merely had some chats over a few meals. Lloyd thought of him as nothing more than just a slightly pitiful older man.

He said he was a goose father.

The man’s story went like this: He was a manager in a well-to-do corporation. Financially sound, he sent his daughter to the United States to study along with his wife as he was nervous about sending the former alone. He lived as a goose father for a few years.

But one day, he got laid off. Unable to break the news to his family because he feared worrying them and affecting the child’s studies, he endured the pain of his abrupt retirement alone. That was when he switched gears to construction sites. He didn’t tell his family about it. Since suddenly starting a business came with the risk of losing his severance pay and he had family members to feed in the US, he started to work in construction sites, drenching himself in sweat, while his severance pay was squirreled away.

How the man laughed while saying that doing physical labor suited him better than being at a desk all day.

He was a good-tempered man despite his pitiful circumstances. His humor involved a lot of dad jokes, to Lloyd’s discomfort. However, he was a good man with a bright smile. That was Lloyd’s entire impression of him. After all, he was as pitiful as this one. So Lloyd had some compassion for him but thought no more beyond that.

But that same afternoon, the man fell. An accident happened which took away his ability to walk for the rest of his life. Yet how did people react at the construction site? Business continued as usual. As if nothing happened. As if the man was just a broken component being replaced. The site returned to operation as though nothing happened. Lloyd was dumbstruck. He couldn’t wrap his head around it.

What really struck me even more dumb was the supervisors at the scene.

In the first place, the construction site had been poorly managed and lacked safety protocols. The accident could have been prevented if proper safety net regulations were put in place. But in spite of that, none of the supervisors were willing to take responsibility for what happened. Instead, the blame was put on the man for being careless. They made pathetic excuses for themselves, giving out all kinds of excuses for their wrongdoings, and merely provided a little money for his medical expenses and compensation pay. No one offered the man a proper apology or reimbursement for the accident. What did Lloyd think after seeing the whole incident unfold?

Dirty. Filthy.

The same emotions he felt that day arose in him again. How enraged and indignant he had felt when the supervisors refused to take responsibility. How wretched and cheated he had felt, even when he wasn’t the victim. And now?

I’m just like them.

His last train of thought immediately propelled him to leap to his feet. A pang of repulsion came over him as he recalled how he was mere minutes ago, comfortably perched on the ground under the warm sun while making a preposterous excuse that the rescue team would come and save the workers.

If that’s how it is, then I’ll be no different than the supervisors I had berated at the time.

He didn’t like that. This was his construction site. A job he obtained from the queen. He was responsible for it as the supervisor. And now that an accident took place at his site, even though it wasn’t his fault that it happened, it happened. And who was supposed to take responsibility for it?

Me.

Lloyd was the man in charge. At least for this work site, he was. And no one could take his place. No one could take on his responsibility for him.

This is my duty.

Before he knew it, he was running. He rushed into the break room and took all the thin blankets and large-sized overalls inside before running back to the cave entrance. He was about to jump right down when he was stopped by the group leader.

“Master Lloyd? You are not… going down there, are you?”

“I am.” It was obvious that he should. There was no time to dawdle around here. He spat, “Winter blankets. Coats. Get as many as you can. Hurry up. And give an accurate report of the situation to the rescue team once they arrive.”

“I understand your instructions,” the leader reasoned hurriedly, “But it’s going to be dangerous down there. The rescue team will be here soon anyway. What do you plan to achieve by personally going down there alone?”

“What else?”

The decision was already made. Lloyd knew what he had to do. Thinking so, he stared at the group leader and replied in the most nonchalant manner, “I have to save them. Everyone.”

There was nothing left to say and no more time to waste. Just like that, Lloyd jumped into the vertical shaft.
