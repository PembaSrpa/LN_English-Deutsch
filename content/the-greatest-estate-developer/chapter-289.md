# The Second Key Construction Material (2)

---

Ruuumble…!

Dozens of horses foamed at the mouths, their muscular necks, chests, and bodies sweating in buckets. The shouts and orders to pull harder rang aloud the area, mixed with the knights’ commands, laborers’ shouts, and stifled neighing. As a dust storm emerged, there was a giant staircase made of stone being carried forward. It wasn’t a simple and sleek staircase. The inside of the stairs where the steps of stone met had something resembling a speed bump. The bump had a unique shape. It was designed to connect to the stone beside it. Dozens of horses dragged the staircase along with countless other spoils.

Knights marched on with victorious smiles on their faces after having survived the destructive battlefield and war that marked the end of the mythical age. The procession was making its way toward their home, the land of the humans, when something happened.

Fwish! Stab! An arrow flew and stabbed a knight’s forehead. It was followed by a salvo of arrows, all aimed at the horses, knights, and laborers. Chaos descended the land. Screams were heard. Amid the chaos and painful shrieks, the knights prepared for a counterattack in rage. This rage was directed toward themselves for allowing this ambush to happen by lowering their guards and to their enemies for their sneak attack. They flew into a rage and raised their spears and swords, confident that they were unrivaled in the entire continent. There wasn’t a single creature who could ever defeat them while they rode their horses. Their confidence was absolute and passionate.

But their beliefs weren’t entirely groundless. It was the truth that no creature ever managed to strike them down in a mobile warfare on a plain. There were countless armies and monsters that had been helplessly defeated and died at their hands. And thus, they earned the saying, “one with the horse.” Brimming with assurance that victory would come like so many other times, they charged forward. Yet, they faced defeat.

The issue was that they were only described to be one with their horses, whereas their enemies were actually part animal. Their lower torsos were that of a strong, wild horse, and their upper torsos were that of a human body. This perfect bodily combination assured a level of speed and maneuver no cavalry could ever come close to. Rapid acceleration. Rapid stops. Rapid turns. Unrestrained speed control. The half-horse and half-human creatures wreaked havoc, using their spears to stab, kicking with their hind legs, and shooting arrows when they had a chance. And with that, the continent’s greatest knights met its doom.

Terrified, the laborers ran for their lives while their enemies celebrated their easy win by stealing the stone staircase. They tied the ropes around the stones with sweat dripping down their body as they galloped toward the vast plain of the northwest.

A long time had passed since then. The world had changed. A new chapter of history had begun, and the story had become a legend. The existence of the staircase had faded away with the passage of time. With that, the video had ended.

Ding dong.

[You have finished accessing the information on the Taupo staircase, the second key material of the Jewel of Truth used to build its staircase.]

[If you wish to watch it again, please select “Replay.”]

“Whew.” Lloyd exhaled the breath he’d been holding for a while. This video reminded him of the VR experience he had once back in Korea. And so, he was amused and also felt nostalgic thinking about his past.

Ah, I wish I could watch a movie, a drama, or even a MeTube video.

Just then, a desire to watch a film came over him. He wanted to sprawl around on his sofa or floor, binge-watching movies, dramas, and variety shows for several hours as he flipped through different channels. He missed the mere act of mindlessly watching something entertaining. Lloyd became wretchedly nostalgic of it since it was an enjoyment unavailable in this world.

Tsk. The records of the mythical age didn’t have to be pretentious by showing me a video. It’s just making me want to watch TV.

Lloyd grumbled to himself but didn’t forget to mentally go through the contents he had just seen.

The Jewel of Truth. The second key material of the Aotearoa was the stone staircase.

The video featured a giant staircase with distinctive bumps at its end. It was something Lloyd was familiar with.

Suppose tauranga was the material used in building the holding stones for the Tomb of the General. The Taupo would be the first flight of stairs at the far bottom of the Tomb of the General. Its structure and basic principles are very similar.

The more he thought about it, the more it was the case. Since the Tomb of the General was made of blocks of stones, it was gigantic and massive. As such, how to safely distribute the weight was the biggest task he had when it came to building it.

Thus, the holding stones and the first flight of stairs played a big part in managing its weight. The holding stones were placed on all four sides of the Tomb of the General as if they were spalled on. They prevented lateral pressure from centering to the bottom of the building. As for the first flight of stairs…

They, too, had a purpose. Its bump structure is interconnected with the stones next to it. Like putting arms around each other’s shoulders. Should I say that it was meant to withhold the weight pressing down from the top and distributing it around? That was how the Tomb of the General managed without its lower structure crumbling under its weight.

The shape of the Taupo in the video held a striking resemblance with the first flight of stairs in the Tomb of the General.

And as for the race that ambushed the knights…

Lloyd, who was still 10,000 feet above the ground on Yong Yong’s back, thought about the ambushers.

Yeah, they’re definitely centaurs.

Centaurs were half-human and half-horse creatures. They were equestrian by nature, and the plains were their running ground. They were born to gallop, and at times, gave their lives away for it. They were the creatures that made you wonder how they scratched their backs or stomachs.

Anyway, the centaurs took the second key construction material.

Lloyd sighed at length after reaching the conclusion. The Great War was said to have seen participation from all sorts of races, and now, it was the centaurs. Really?

The setting for this novel sucks.

Lloyd was condemning the author of the novel when he heard Javier’s voice.

“Did you see something this time again?”

When Lloyd turned, he found Javier shooting a cold, cynical gaze typical of him.

“Yeah.” Lloyd nodded. “I saw a video record.”

Lloyd didn’t bother to lie about the video. There was no need to. This pertained to the legendary artifact of the mythical age. Getting clues from a magical vision wasn’t anything suspicious.

Plus, he won’t doubt me if I’m honest with him about these small events.

Lloyd thought that he shouldn’t hide it from A to Z and make hardly believable excuses for everything. He believed he ought to be frank about the minor details since he hid his identity from Javier.

That way, Javier won’t be skeptical about my important matters. He’ll trust me.

And besides, Javier wasn’t a slow guy. Lloyd didn’t want Javier to find out about him first before he bore his heart out to Count and Countess Frontera. Part of it was because of fear. Lloyd was scared that Javier might point his sword at him for faking his identity and abusing him for all these years once he found out.

And so, Lloyd decided to be shameless. “I’ve seen the events after the Great War. The knights were ambushed when they were taking the stone staircase, the second key material for this construction, as a spoil of war.”

“Ambush? What do you mean?”

“The centaurs attacked the knights and took the staircase from them.”

Javier stroked his chin. “Then I suppose our next destination is the Great Plain in the northwest.”

“Right,” affirmed Lloyd. “That’s where we are heading.”

Lloyd was glad that the centaurs’ habitat was widely known, like the merpeople. They lived in the great plains of the highlands located in the northwest. The climate here was similar to the highlands in Mongolia.

It’s cold because it’s located inland and sits quite high in altitude. The area is unimaginably cold and dry, making it barely habitable. Farming is difficult there too. Thanks to this, no kingdom covets the land.

It was just a cold and dry neighborhood with barely any arable land whose inhabitants were powerful centaurs. The place was hard to take over, and even if it was taken over, it was barely useful. Thanks to these factors, no one lusted after the land. It was absolutely free from trespassing humans and invading kingdoms. That was how the land managed to solely be used by the centaurs to this day.

“So, let’s go,” urged Lloyd, “Yong Yong.”

Clack!

Yong Yong precisely understood Lloyd’s message. It changed its flight direction toward the south and removed itself from the Arctic Sea. Past the northern end of the continent, the scenery beneath Lloyd’s feet started to change. The forests in the tundra and taiga flashed past, the treetops covered with snow. And the giant mountain range stood tall, its height shooting up from the bottom enough to touch Lloyd’s feet. And once they passed several peaks, at last, the highlands as high as 8,200 feet spanned in front of his eyes. It was the Great Plain, the largest highland in the northwest area. Lloyd urged Yong Yong to land at the entrance.

“Yong Yong. Play here for a while.”

Creak?

“I’m sorry,” apologized Lloyd. “I fear that the folks in this area will panic when they see you. They might think we’re invading. But don’t cock your head like that. You’re making me feel guilty.”

Claaack!

“No, no. People don’t hate you because you’re ugly. They don’t hate you. Um, so… I don’t know. Just make sure you’re hiding in the woods and hang around here. Got it?”

Clack!

Yong Yong nodded but not without casting a discouraged look at Lloyd. He smiled apologetically. He felt bad, but there wasn’t anything he could do.

Yong Yong looks too menacing on the outside. It’ll easily ruin the first encounter we have with the nearby centaurs. I feel bad, but there’s nothing I can do. I should play ball with it next time.

Resolved to appease Yong Yong in the future, Lloyd went into the vast plain with Javier, both riding on Ppodong’s back. After about three or four hours of galloping and enjoying the endless stretch of the plain…

“Something is coming this way.”

Javier, who was seated at the back, pointed to one area. Lloyd turned and found several dots dancing on the horizon, coming close toward him. No, it was charging at him. The dots grew in size. Sandstorms raged behind their trails. At last, they came close and surrounded Lloyd and Javier.

“Stop! The stranger should stop at once right now!”

Gallop! Thwack! They circled around Lloyd, and the noise of the galloping footsteps menacingly drummed in his ears. These centaurs were clad in dark-brown armor over their muscular human torso and armed with spears and arrows. Their tail manes on their horse body swayed sideways, and their gazes glimmered icily. These centaurs belonged to the patrol team.

Woah. Look at their muscles beneath their armors.

Lloyd studied the centaurs surrounding him. He counted about twenty in total. Every one of them exuded power and strength. Although they’d be an easy opponent against Javier, Lloyd got the impression that they were stubborn as a mule apart from their actual strength.

Looks like folks in this neighborhood are hostile to humans too.

It looked like they were seconds away from hurling sharp arrows or spears into his heart if he slighted them or ignored their warning.

Well. That should be how they managed to protect their land for hundreds and thousands of years from human invasion.

Lloyd understood them very well and simultaneously found a way to break their stubbornness.

“What will you do?” Javier softly spoke beside him. He was standing in front of Lloyd as if he was preparing for a possible confrontation. Javier’s voice was calm.

Lloyd grinned at him. “What do you mean? There’s no need for confrontation.”

Really. There was no need for unnecessary confrontation with the patrol team. He had no reason for it. There was a softer, healthier, and more effective way to sort this out.

“So, excuse me for a second, Javier.” Lloyd slipped in front of Javier.

Swish! Lloyd’s movement immediately brought the twenty spears to glint and aim at him. But Lloyd didn’t even bat an eye. Instead, he casually spoke up, recalling the story he read in the novel, and suggested a method that would win their hearts and access to their land in one go.

“Hello, my name is Lloyd Frontera. I heard that you are the fastest runners on land. I want to see if that’s actually true. Is there anyone here who is faster than me?”

“…..!”

This suggestion took them by surprise. It was an explicit provocation and challenge. Everyone’s eyes immediately held a glint of instinct competitiveness. They were offended by the man’s daring challenge. They boasted that they were the reckless runners of the vast plain, the only ones who could so rapidly take off, halt, turn, and freely increase and decrease their speed and change sides. How dare a human challenge us, they thought.

“Fine. I will race you.” The patrol commander came out with ease. Pointing at a small rock and laughing at Lloyd, he said, “Start at the same time. The one who gets to the rock first wins.”

“Awesome.” Lloyd got on Ppodong’s back and stood at the start line with the patrol leader. At last, the signal came.

“Go!”

Gallop! Sprint! Sprint! The centaur and Ppodong raced as they created a dust storm all over the area. And about fifteen seconds later…

Slap! A red speeding ticket, which allowed access to the vast plain, was stuck on Lloyd’s back.
