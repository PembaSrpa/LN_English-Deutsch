# Hell Express Ticket (1)

---

[A new title <Frozen Hands, Frozen Feet> has been created.]

Eek?!

Lloyd swallowed as he looked at the message floating in front of his eyes. His glance flicked over to the Lava Giant that was being chopped into hundreds of pieces.

“Grrrr!”

The giant let out a shriek before it died. Its cry grew faint until it was heard no more. Instead, the thudding sound of rocks hitting the ground reverberated in the air. Boom! Crash! Thump!

The searing hot rocks were half-solidified. It looked like they could no longer be fused back together.

They’ve cooled down too much for that.

Lloyd clenched his fists. Javier’s sword deeply pierced into the giant’s body when it was sufficiently cooled. With that, the giant had finally been defeated. The message that appeared in front of Lloyd’s eyes proved it.

Let’s see.

Collecting his breath, Lloyd read the message.

[Frozen Hands, Frozen Feet]

[Title Level: Scary Story]

The Lava Giant is scary. The Lava Giant is hot. The Lava Giant is violent. So shh. You can never anger the giant. That is what we believed in. The King of Hell is busy. Hell’s Knights hate doing rough work. We never dared to call them, so we just got beaten up. We thought staying low was the answer in life. But it wasn’t. There was always a way. Lloyd Frontera, that human. I never expected that human to start this. Wonder what it is? Let’s close the door first if you want to hear it. The cold wind is blowing… A-achoo!

[Compliment Effect: You have played a critical role in destroying the most dangerous and violent creature in Hell, the Lava Giant. Thousands of demons clearly saw your feat, and this heroic tale will spread across Hell. Thanks to that, you will be feared and respected by the demons equal to Hell’s Knight. Moreover, you will not suffer from hypothermia in cold climates and will not freeze to death.]

[Title Region: All Hell regions. All areas whose annual average climate is 32 °F and below.]

[Active Period of Title: 120 Years (Human world)/ 100,000 Years (Hell)]

[A title’s powers can be activated for 24 hours in the region for which the compliment was given. Moreover, time and region can expand or shrink depending on your actions.]

[Monthly CP Earned from the Title: 6]

[Current CP: 1,142]

Wow.

A smile hung from one ear to the other, and Lloyd couldn’t suppress it.

This title has an amazing effect too.

Lloyd could sense the power of this new title the moment he saw it.

First, about the part where I’ll be feared and respected like Hell’s Knights by the demons of Hell. This is going to help me out considerably in the remaining construction moving forward.

The railroad construction had been a challenge because it was taking place in the special environment of Hell, relying on special laborers, the demons. Ensuring that the construction went smoothly was a hellish job.

How could it not? The lava makes the ground unstable all the time, and the demons are an infamously unruly bunch. Ha… Just thinking about this is making me go bald from stress.

It was a burden he had been carrying alone for the past three months. Among the existing difficulties, the issue that gave Lloyd the hardest time was controlling the demons. They were extremely disobedient.

Without Javier’s constant surveillance, 20,000 of the 30,000 demons would have run away already.

Demons, in the first place, were impossible to correct. So, he could only use force to put them to work. The problem was that doing so put a burden on him.

As soon as I pay less attention to the construction site, I will lose my authority over them right away.

Then the construction field would quickly descend into chaos. At worst, it would become a complete disaster. There were similar instances in South Korea very rarely where construction went down in flames. Lloyd had seen it himself and heard about it too.

But now, that concern is off my shoulders.

In the hierarchy of Hell, the Hell’s Knights were right beneath the King of Hell. Thanks to the title, Lloyd now held equal authority as the knights. And he looked forward to having a proportionately easier time controlling the demons.

Plus, there’s the immunity to hypothermia and freezing to death. This is another awesome effect.

This effect wasn’t useful in Hell, where the whole place boiled with heat. But if he ever traveled to a numbingly cold region? The effect would be extremely useful.

I learned this from direct experience when I came down to Hell.

The title he had obtained by building the Qanat back in the sultan’s kingdom came to his mind. “The Top Gun from the West.” This new title had a similar effect to that one.

The effect that prevents me from getting dehydrated in desert regions with an annual climate of 104 °F and above.

Lloyd was able to survive in Hell all this time because of that effect. He could stay here in comfort even when Javier was sweating bullets from the suffocating heat. The effect he had acquired proved to be extremely useful in this place, at least. And what about the effect he had acquired from this new title?

You never know. I might visit a cold region sometime in my life.

Therefore, the more effects like these he had, the better off he was. Similar to accumulating antibodies from a vaccine, hoarding titles always helped in the long run. Lloyd then closed the message with immense happiness. But earning a new title wasn’t the only thing that filled his heart with satisfaction.

I can resume the construction now.

It had been a laborious three months to get to this point. The roadbed had just been completed, and he was in the middle of working on the ties. In other words, the construction was soon to be over in a month or two. Now that the threat of his efforts going down in flames was solved, he could continue working in peace. His pounding heart calmed down a little. Relieved, Lloyd rushed to Javier first.

“Hey,” Lloyd called, “are you all right?”

“Yes, thankfully.” Javier was catching his raspy breath, with the debris of the giant all scattered around him. His whole body was soaked with sweat. It made sense.

Even though there was cold air coming out from the Hell gate, he ran around and fought against the Lava Giant in this place filled with fire and smoke. Thankfully, he doesn’t look like he’s hurt anywhere.

Lloyd studied Javier with concern filling his gaze. However, he spoke contrary to those feelings of his.

“Are you hot?” blurted Lloyd.

“Yes, a little…” gasped Javier.

“Tsk. You’re a weakling.” Lloyd shook his head.

“…”

“Who cares if you’re a grandmaster and can shoot auras? This much heat is enough to make you get soaked in sweat. Am I wrong?” Lloyd smirked and lashed out at Javier.

Javier removed a sweaty strand of hair stuck on his forehead. “I may be sweating a lot, but come to think of it, I think I am in a better state than you.”

“Excuse me?” shrilled Lloyd. “You are in a better state than me?”

“Yes.”

“How?”

“Even when I am soaked in sweat and covered with ash, I am better looking than you at your cleanest, Master Lloyd. ”

“..”

"I feel thrilled whenever I realize this fact,” gloated Javier with a smug gaze. “It never gets old, nothing is better than being handsome.”

Lloyd inwardly wished for all disgustingly beautiful people to get hemorrhoids. He wondered if this was what it felt like to fire a tiny bullet with a slingshot while giggling, only to be shot back with a 14-ton GBU-57 MOP. Blinking back bitter tears, Lloyd cried, “Hey, you think this is the time to prattle?”

Javier said nothing, dumbstruck,

“Can’t you see that the construction is a mess? We have to hurry and take care of it. You don’t have time to just sit back and catch your breath. Come on.”

Still, Javier stared at Lloyd without saying anything.

“Come on! Move it, Hurry,” urged Lloyd.

“..”

Lloyd forced Javier to stand up while he was catching his breath. He heartlessly pushed the knight’s back and started cleaning up the site. Even then, the faint smile hanging on his mouth didn’t disappear. To Lloyd’s relief, the unexpected crisis had been overcome, and Javier was safe. That alone was enough for him. Now, it was time to clean up the site.

The construction field was cleaned up in no time. Though more than a thousand demons had died, which Lloyd was apologetic and hurting for, and about 300 yards of the roadbed were destroyed by the Lava Giant’s stomping, most of the demons had survived. So, the bed was able to be quickly restored. Moreover, the team gained a giant crane, no, an additional member. It was the Bone Dragon.

Clack! Creak! The Bone Dragon, who joined the team late, worked harder than anyone else. It unwaveringly abided by the rule for the undead to not receive any wages or breaks. If there was one thing the dragon aspired to gain from working, it was praise from Lloyd.

“Hey?”

Clack!

“Can you scoop out the lava pooled over there?” Lloyd pointed to an area.

Clack!

“Good. Good job. Now, let’s fill the hole you left behind with volcanic ash. Make sure to thoroughly flatten it out with your feet.”

Clack! Creak!

“My, good boy! As a reward, I’ll give you a name. How about Yong Yong?”

Clack! Creak!

The Bone Dragon was more motivated to work now that it was named as a reward. Thanks to its zealous attitude, the other fantastical creatures were motivated as well.

“Ppo, Bang, Ha, Ggo!”

Just a few months ago, all of them fought tooth and nail against the Bone Dragon. But what about now? They still did, but not for the same reason. They weren’t very happy about the Bone Dragon, the new recruit in their group, getting all the spotlight in the construction field and even receiving a new name. A fierce sense of competition burned inside them. They resolved to show off their true skills and worked with inextinguishable zeal to reclaim their glory.

“Ppodong! Ppododong!”

Ppodong engrossed himself in cleaning up the leftover soil from where the roadbed was constructed. He blew on the hot volcanic ash and put them inside his cheeks, moving as quickly as he could. At the same time, Bangul knuckled down to produce rebars.

“Bangul! Bba-bangul!”

Even if this place was hot, there were still areas that required the usage of rebars. One instance of this was in attaching the abutment between the rails made of centipede shells. Similar to melted silicone from a silicone gun, the rebars held different materials together. As for Hamang, he played his part to the best of his ability as well.

“Humung! Humu-mung!”

Splash! Hamang guzzled the hot spring water in various places of Hell to his heart’s content, and he soon became a 229-foot tall giant roller. Rolling around the construction site, he flattened the areas that needed his contribution. Ggoming also worked hard every day, the same as the rest of the others.

“Hey, Ggoming! Let’s gooo!” said Lloyd.

“Ggoming!” exclaimed Ggoming.

Ggoming flew around the construction site carrying Lloyd until his wings developed a heat rash. The demons joined the momentum and labored in the construction site with greater earnestness. Lloyd now held the same amount of authority as Hell’s Knight, and the demons listened to all of his commands regardless of how menial a chore was. And so, thanks to everyone’s zealous contribution, the construction field was restored in a blink of an eye. The tie construction picked up speed as well, progressing at a scary pace.

The gravel ballast was laid out tightly, and a transverse sleeper was chosen as the method to lay the tie to distribute the weight of the train and maintain the gauge of a track. Next, the standard-length rail, 82 feet in length, was processed from the centipede shells and tucked in the ground. They were then bonded as if they were welded like melted rebars. The last part of the construction was the platform. That, too, wasn’t particularly complicated or difficult to complete.

In the first place, I only need to create two stations. One at the Hell Castle and the other at the edge of Hell.

The starting point and destination. He could only fashion two stations at both ends. Lloyd decided to simplify the structure of the stations to guarantee swift construction and easy maintenance.

It doesn’t have to be complicated. I just need to lay rails on two sides and place a concrete platform in the middle of them. It’s not like the train will hold any fussy passengers. It just needs to conveniently transport a large number of souls.

Lloyd thus focused his efforts on maximizing the space in the platform. The space needed to be vast, so the demons wouldn’t get caught in traffic as they loaded and unloaded innumerable souls. A massive amount of rebars and volcanic ash concrete were utilized. Accordingly, the demons poured their blood, sweat, and tears into the project without ever leaving the work site.

It took one whole month to finish the platform. And finally, 984-foot long and 328-foot wide platforms were built at both ends, one in the Hell Castle and another in the edge of Hell. Lloyd had proposed the project “Hell Express 666” to the King of Hell five months ago. This moment marked the completion of the very first railroad in Hell, which stretched 86 miles long.

“Finally… It’s done!”

“Waaah!”

“We’re free!”

“We can go home now!”

“I can slack off and worry my parents!’

"Hurray!”

The day the railroad was constructed, there was no one happier than the demons working out on the field. Did they feel proud for completing a construction that would be remembered throughout Hell’s history? Not at all.

They simply teared up in joy, moved by the sole fact that they were liberated from enslavement. They could now go back to their life as good-for-nothing bums. Of course, they didn’t forget to request a reward for their efforts, and they selected the one-horned demon to represent them to strike a negotiation with Lloyd.

“Master Lloyd! I have something to tell you,” the one-horned demon said.

“What is it?”

“Don’t you think we worked back-breakingly hard up to this point?”

“Oh, did you?” Lloyd picked his ear.

“Yes! So, we ask to be compensated! Please give us our proper wages!”

“Not happening. Scram.” Lloyd flicked his finger.

“Thank you so much!”

Just like that, the negotiation was over. The other demons who received the news nodded in sheer admiration. They resolved in their hearts that they would be like Lloyd and become an evil employer when they had the chance to exploit other demons. Then each of them went back to their hometown. It was only then that Lloyd isolated himself and built something.

Clang! Twang! Clang!

Right after all the demons went back home, in the corner of a desolated platform on the edge of Hell, Lloyd held up the hammer and a chisel. He carved out a volcanic rock he had gotten from someplace, hammering and chiseling. The shape was simple and rough without any delicate calligraphy. He simply pounded and carved out the words he had been keeping to himself.

<This memorial stone is for all the demons who sacrificed themselves to build the Hell Express.>

It was a memorial stone that paid tribute to the demons who died during the Lava Giant’s ambush.

I think this will suffice.

Lloyd stared at the completed stone with a wistful look. He erected this only now because he was afraid to receive comments and gazes of disappointment from the demons who would judge his kind character.

Still, this puts me at ease.

Sure, they were demons. But they had died tragically while they were working for him. So, he wanted to mourn and pay tribute, at least in this manner, even if this was only to satisfy his guilt. Lloyd thought this was the least he could do for them. After he finished building the secret memorial stone, he made his way toward the Hell Castle. It was time to receive his payment for the construction from the King of Hell.
