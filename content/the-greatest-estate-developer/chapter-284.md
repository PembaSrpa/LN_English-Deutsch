# How to Freeze Time (2)

---

“It’s cold. I’ve always hated the cold. But not that there’s any particular reason for it. It’s been like that since I was young. By the way, what was your name? Heart of Winter? I can’t believe you’ve assimilated into me. Why did this have to happen? What for? Aren’t these obvious questions for me to have?”

“It’s hot. I’ve always hated the heat. But not that there’s any grand reason for it. I’ve been like that since I was created. By the way, what was your name? Lloyd Frontera? Now that I’m assimilated, this is going to be harder than I thought. Why do human bodies have to be so hot? Don’t you agree?”

“You make me speechless. I’ve never asked you to assimilate into me. And who gave you permission to whisper inside my unconsciousness? You barged into me, and now you’re yapping? Where are your manners, really? Don’t you see where I’m coming from?”

“You make me speechless, too. Who was it that absorbed everything? I was just a little interested in you. That was all. And yet, you sucked me into your system, and you’re complaining, really? Don’t you see where I’m coming from?”

“You. You’re copying my words. Are you doing it on purpose?”

“You. You’re grumpy. Are you doing it on purpose?”

“Yes, I’m doing this on purpose. Might as well come bearing a gift if you meant to come into me without permission.”

“But I already did.”

“You did?”

“Yeah.”

“Where?”

“Here. Right here. Right now. In front of you.”

“…”

Lloyd opened his eyes. Did he take a nap? Or had he lost track of time for a while? Or…

Did I pass out?

Lloyd blinked. He narrowed his eyes until the focus in his eyes came back, and everything started to be clear. The surroundings around him appeared in his vision. The first thing he saw was the blue sky. It was bright and blinding. Sunlight flooded right into his eyes as if it wanted to grill them.

“Ah, it’s blinding me,” grumbled Lloyd as he frowned and squinted. Then, someone popped into his vision in the corner of the blue sky.

“Master Lloyd, are you awake?” A head. But Lloyd could only see the outlines because of the sunlight. And yet, the man’s head shape alone told of his handsomeness.

“Javier?”

“Yes, Master Lloyd. It is me.” Javier grinned softly. The wind blew to sway his silvery hair, and only then did Lloyd realize he was lying on the ground. And he also noticed the concerned gazes of many people around him. They belonged to the laborers.

“Everyone, you are…” Lloyd hurriedly sat up, which ushered in a terrible pang of dizziness. Javier quickly held out his hand and assisted Lloyd’s back.

“We are outside the cave. You are safe now. So, please rest at ease.”

“…”

Lloyd looked around. It was true. One, two, three, four, and everyone else. Everyone at the site was there. They were gathered under the sun with a thick blanket as they relaxed and drank hot water. Ppodong and Bangul were in his embrace. That was when he realized.

Everyone made it out safely. I’m safe as well.

Lloyd searched for his memory, and the previous events started to come back to him piece by piece.

I was supervising the upward vertical shaft when I heard a strange explosion and vibration. The Heart of Winter, and the ice storm…

And he started to save all the people, carrying them out one at a time. This process was repeated twenty times. He was exhausted and worn out. At the last moment, he made a decision.

Supercharge.

He took the card out. Everything after that was lost in his head. It was not so surprising. That was what the Supercharge did. You fall into a state of stupor. No situations or changes can be felt.

What happened after that?

Lloyd lifted his head and looked back at Javier, who was supporting his back.

“Hey,” called Lloyd.

“Yes, Master Lloyd.”

“Did you save me?” Lloyd looked at Javier.

“Yes, probably.” Javier smiled mysteriously.

“Then what about Ppodong, Bangul, and the group leader at the site? They were the last to leave.”

“Hmm, I am not sure.” Javier’s smile deepened.

What could that answer mean? Lloyd cocked his head to the side. “You’re not so sure? What do you mean by that?”

“It was me who sensed the accident and came to help you before everyone else, but I am not so sure I can say I saved those you just mentioned.” Javier’s smile turned wry. He knew that his answer was ambiguous. But that was the only answer he could give. Why? Simple.

How should I explain the situation? I do not know.

Javier recalled what had happened. Right after he helped Lloyd’s Supercharge and the Heart of Winter melted into Lloyd’s manaheart, Lloyd lost consciousness. But only his consciousness. He was moving, albeit like a sleepwalker or an inebriated man. Lloyd staggered up to his feet and walked into the cave like a zombie.

Unable to believe his eyes, Javier hurriedly followed Lloyd and stopped the latter, saying that they must go up to safety. Lloyd swiveled at Javier, and he spoke, staring right into his eyes.

“But I am the one in charge.”

Javier was flustered. Lloyd continued mumbling to himself like a drunk.

“Someone’s inside. The group leader is there. He came to work at the construction site because he trusted me. So it’s only right that I save him. Don’t you think?”

“But Master Lloyd,” argued Javier, “Then I will go in myself.”

“It has to be me. He’s my responsibility. I am the one in charge. I am the one in charge, Javier.”

“…”

Lloyd couldn’t be talked out of it. He was stubborn. Incorrigible. From then on, Javier silently followed Lloyd into the cave, which was still cold, worrying that the fake Lloyd might fall. On the way, he was granted an audience to fake Lloyd’s soliloquy.

“Everyone is rotten. The site supervisor. The inspecting company. Construction company. Developer. How is that possible? A man fell off. He’d have been saved if the safety net was in place like in the regulation. A man’s spine was smashed, for Pete’s sake. He’s stuck in a wheelchair for life. And no one’s at fault? It’s his fault that he climbed all the way there? To hell with them.”

Javier didn’t say anything. He simply listened.

“Pass the buck, all right. Pass the buck to the innocent. Until the situation gets so convoluted and involves tons of people that no one’s there to hear him out. Drain his mind, and that was when you slipped an envelope into his hand. Oh, and you say what? It’s not my fault, but this is out of courtesy? Wrap it up, this is enough? Wrap it up?! Shoot. Those nut-jobs make me go mad. Even when I’m not the one they’re bothering.”

“…”

“Why are you living like that? That’s not how you should live. But you know what? Life is a stage for the bad guys. That’s not the way it should be. But it is. How much I cursed… the supervisor. He wears an angelic mask. But behind that, he takes bribes. The same goes for the others. I promised myself. I won’t be like them. If I get my license and become in charge, no such thing will happen at my construction site. That’s why I’m doing this.”

“…”

“If I don’t do this, if I avoid this because I’m scared, I’ll be no different than the bastards that I looked down on. And that’s not what I want.”

Master Lloyd… Fake Lloyd. What kind of life did you live? What was your life like in the world I cannot imagine? Javier shook his head. There was no way of knowing or guessing. But one thing was clear. Fake Lloyd’s grumbling and ranting were genuine, and so was his intention to stagger far into the cave.

For some reason, Javier couldn’t get himself to stop Lloyd. Soon, Lloyd arrived at the end of the cave, where he carried the unconscious group leader even when he couldn’t walk properly. Afterward, he downsized Ppodong and Bangul and placed them in his embrace. He then walked out of the cave, climbed up the high ladder, put the man down under the sun, and passed out. That was what Javier saw happen.

“…”

Javier had a sudden thought. This could have been the most genuine side of Lloyd he ever got a glimpse of.

Moreover… Master Lloyd absorbed the Heart of Winter. All of it. Not a single piece was amiss.

Javier sensed it too as he had helped Lloyd while the Supercharge skill was activated. He was able to feel it from the connected manaheart and mana circle. At the end of the Supercharge, the moment his mana absorption went beyond its limit, the Heart of Winter suddenly grew silent. It had absorbed into Lloyd’s manaheart.

Does fake Lloyd know about it?

Half-worried and half-expectant, Javier looked at Lloyd. He wanted to closely study Lloyd as, after all, it was a divine item that he had absorbed. Would he know what repercussions it’d have? Would it be harmful to him? Or greatly benefit him? Javier decided to keep a close eye on Lloyd. But to Javier’s ignorance, Lloyd was well aware of what happened.

Ha… Crap. This is bad.

He ate the Heart of Winter. The thought of it made him gulp.

All I wanted to achieve by using the Supercharge skill was to absorb some energy and fill up on a bit of mana to escape from the place.

It never occurred to Lloyd that he’d devour the Heart of Winter by the whole. He never knew that was even possible.

How could I ever think that was possible? It’s the Heart of Winter. One of the divine items in the House of Magentano. It was an impressive artifact. But what about his Supercharge? Sure, what I have is a great skill too. After all, I defeated the undead Mastodons and Hell’s Knight with it. But it has its clear limits.

Though it was very good at absorbing mana, it had its limits. It couldn’t absorb beyond a certain capacity. A case in point was when he used the skill against the Bone Dragon.

At that time, the total volume Draggy carried was just another level… Supercharge? It was like trying to drain a lake with a bucket.

And so, he barely did any damage to Draggy. He thought it’d be the same case this time. He thought it wouldn’t be enough to absorb the Heart of Winter since it was a divine item belonging to the royal house. All he expected was to absorb a tiny portion of the cold air being emitted out. That was enough for him. Fill up the empty manaheart, recharge, and save Ppodong, Bangul, and the group leader. That was all he wanted.

But this? Woah. Could it be that the Heart of Winter doesn’t have as large of a reserve as I thought?

He couldn’t help but think so. It was that outrageous of a situation. Sure, Lloyd was glad. But given the item’s unimaginable value, Lloyd was forced to worry about the repercussions.

Tsk. Should I just make excuses? The Heart of Winter ran amok, and it broke. Yeah. That should be more believable to the others. Who would believe me if I told them I absorbed it?

Lloyd then stole a glance at Javier on the side. Could it be that he felt the gaze? Javier turned and faced Lloyd. And to Lloyd’s surprise, he whispered, “Master Lloyd, just tell them that it broke.”

“Wait… Did you know about the absorption?” Lloyd’s voice broke.

“Yes. Thanks to the Asrahan Core Technique, I have been able to feel it. And that is why…” Javier paused and stared right into Lloyd’s eyes. Lloyd held his gaze without saying anything. “Since the others do not know that there is such a skill, no one will know as long as we keep our mouths shut.”

Lloyd’s mouth opened slightly in surprise. “But a divine item of the imperial is the one that would be broken. I’m surprised that you would suggest this.”

“Nothing to be surprised, Master Lloyd. My loyalty is to the lord, not the queen.”

“You mean you want to prevent putting the family in a hard place because I absorbed the Heart of Winter, right?”

“I heard nothing. I do not have a clue what you are saying, and I am ignorant of the whole thing.” With that, Javier clamped his mouth shut. Lloyd licked his lips as he stared at Javier.

You know he can be quite playful and cheeky. Anyway, I should just tell the queen that it broke after it went savage. Ugh, but it won’t save me from the chaos that will ensue.

He might have to pay for the damage. If not, at least be liable for the way the construction site was managed. But Lloyd didn’t worry about it too much. The queen was on his side, and everyone was rescued and alive.

Oh, whatever. I saved people. That’s enough. I should be content with that.

That thinking relieved some burden off his heart. And a few seconds later, he received good news. The stabbed royal magician Lloyd had saved before anyone else lived after receiving emergency treatment.

It must be because of the wound that froze.

That must be what stopped the bleeding and saved him. Lloyd was glad. And soon, every part of his body relaxed at ease. That was when something in the corner of his vision caught his attention. It was the skill message notification that had been floating.

What’s this?

His gaze moved.

[A new manaheart skill option is available.]

[Manaheart Skill Option ⑤: Time Freeze]

Time freeze?

Lloyd was confused. The new option was freezing time?

Am I getting this because I absorbed the Heart of Winter?

It appeared so. He opened the description as a sense of excitement crept into him.

Ding dong.

[Skill Option ⑤: Time Freeze - Allows you to freeze the world’s passage of time by borrowing the power of the Heart of Winter in your manaheart. The user can designate an area of exception, a no-freeze zone, and the area’s range is determined by the user’s total volume of mana. Inside the no-freeze zones, time will progress normally.]

[More Information (1): The range of no-freeze zones cannot be changed until the effect is deactivated. You can stop time for up to a period of one year.]

[More Information (2): The effect is deactivated in the event the user goes out of the no-freeze zone.]

[More Information (3): All living creatures (including the user) can freely go around within the no-freeze zone. However, due to the frigid air of the Heart of Winter, they will not age.]

[More Information (4): It costs 3,000 RP every time to activate the option.]

“Damn. This is amazing.” Lloyd murmured to himself after reading the detailed information.
