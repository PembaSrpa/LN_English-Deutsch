# Hell Express Ticket (2)

---

“Master Lloyd, may I ask you one question?” It was Javier.

“Sure. Go ahead,” said Lloyd, giving him the green light.

“Why are you going alone?”

“Do you mean to the Hell Castle?”

“Yes.” Javier nodded as he darted his gaze at Lloyd, who was on Ggoming’s back. “I wonder if there is anything for me to do here all alone.”

“You bet there is. Why shouldn’t there be?” A smile spread across Lloyd’s face. “You have to protect this place while I’m away to report to the King of Hell. You never know with life. Another Lava Giant might come to raise hell like the last time. And the railroad we busted our guts to build could get destroyed. Am I wrong?”

“As I thought, is that your reason?” Javier’s eyes relaxed.

“What do you mean?”

“I was afraid you were abandoning me here.”

“The heck? Are you serious?”

“Yes,” Javier said firmly. “You are fully capable of doing so at any point in time.”

“Who do you see me as?” A frown appeared on Lloyd’s forehead.

“A sly, cheap, and petty person.”

“I see you even have… a list,” stated Lloyd.

“It’s convenient that way. Anyway…” Javier shrugged and gave a grin, which was a rare thing for him. “I will then hold down the fort here and protect this place as you ordered. Have a safe trip.”

“Ah, yes. And if I happen to abandon you, make sure to find your way home.”

“Find my way home? How-”

“You have that.”

Lloyd pointed at the sky with a smirk. The Hell gate was still in the sky. Lloyd continued to speak.

“That thing is not going to close anyway. The cold wind blowing out of it will lower the temperature around this area, which is good for us. It means that the lava will harden faster, stabilizing the railroad and ground with it. So, I intend to keep the gate open.”

“Wait then-”

“Yes, you’re right,” Lloyd interrupted. “Why would I abandon you? If something happens, you could always cross over the Hell gate. Then you’d be able to easily escape from here. Sure, it’s going to be chilly because it’s the polar region on the other side, but a grandmaster can’t possibly die while shivering from the mere cold, right?”

“…“

"So, you can swim from there to the Frontera fiefdom. Wow, give me a round of applause.”

“…”

“Ehem,” Lloyd cleared his throat as he saw Javier say nothing.

“…”

“Sorry, I was just kidding.”

“I clearly felt your sincerity,” Javier said at last.

“Fine. You got me.”

“I also realized that your apology was a joke.”

“Tsk. You’re too needlessly sharp.”

“Thank you. Now, hurry back from your trip.”

“Okay.”

Lloyd made Ggoming take off, and he quietly flew in silence for a while. It was only when they grew fairly distant from the edge of Hell that he let out a breath of relief.

“Haah. I did it.”

Lloyd meant that he succeeded in leaving Javier and taking a lone trip to the Hell Castle.

I was worried that he’d be stubborn about going with me for safety reasons.

Surprisingly, the man didn’t insist on doing that. It was a relief, and the reason was simple. Lloyd didn’t feel like going to the Hell Castle with him.

I have to meet the King of Hell. But the king keeps calling me Suho Kim.

That made Lloyd uncomfortable and nervous. Javier wasn’t a dull person, so he’d naturally get curious about the strange nickname the king had for him.

I want to avoid that kind of situation at all costs.

At worst, his true identity could be blown. How would Javier react if that happened? How would he treat him? Lloyd wondered but couldn’t imagine it in his head. That made him even more nervous and reluctant.

Anyway, I’m glad to go see the King of Hell alone without him.

The thought lightened his heart a little. Just then, something wiggled in his chest, and there came a grumbling voice.

“Hey… No one’s around you, right? Can’t you at least let me out during these kinds of moments?”

It was Frontera who had been hiding in Lloyd’s inner pocket. Ghost Frontera was grumbling. Lloyd shook his head.

“No. You can’t.”

“Why not?!”

“I can’t be bothered.”

“What?”

“If I get you out, you’re going to get all whiny and clingy. Am I wrong?”

“Hey, t-that’s-”

“I’m right. So, I’m not letting you out. You’re noisy and pesky,” firmly said Lloyd.

“Ha. So what, you’re going to keep me locked in here, you bastard?” Its voice was high now.

“It sounds a bit wrong to say that I’m locking you. Let’s say that I’m protecting you.”

“That’s the same thing! Eek! Argh!”

Lloyd suddenly felt something tumble and scuffle inside his inner pocket, and at last, his button popped open with Ghost Frontera sticking its head out.

“Whew! Ugh. I could barely breathe inside, seriously!” said Ghost Frontera in an accusing voice.

“…”

“Bastard. There’s no need to straighten your face like that. I’m not getting out. I’ll just stick my head out like this. Is this not allowed, either? Huh?”

“Haah…” sighed Lloyd

“I’m the one who wants to sigh, punk. Think about it. It’s been already a month since I’ve been imprisoned in your inner pocket, crushed under Ppodong or those other things.”

“Must have been warm, comfy, and fluffy.”

“Nice, my ass.”

“…”

“All I want is to get some fresh air for the first time in a month, and you won’t even let me do that? Can you still call yourself a human being?”

“Yeah. I am a human. And you’re a ghost.”

“…“

"You were about to curse, weren’t you?” asked Lloyd, already knowing what Frontera would say.

“Of course.”

“Did you stop your reflexes because you were about to insult your parents by accident?”

“Dammit. I despise you.”

“Thank you. Thank you for hating me.”

“…”

Ghost Frontera clamped its mouth shut. Lloyd, who caused it to shut its mouth, enjoyed the satisfying silence. After a long silence, Frontera opened its mouth first.

“Hey, you fake,” called Frontera.

“…”

“Hey?” It called again.

“…“

"Oh, come on. Answer me now.”

“I can’t unless you call my name,” Lloyd replied in a relaxed voice.

“Hey, Lloyd. Happy now?” blurted Frontera with a frown.

“Sure. What do you want?”

“It’s about how you’ll help me.”

Ghost Frontera had been watching the outside scenery with its arms propped on the opening of Lloyd’s inner pocket. When Lloyd looked down, it had a surprisingly serious and grim gaze. He realized why it decided to stick its head out by force after silently remaining in his pocket for a month. It was to discuss this topic with him.

Does Frontera think that it’s my turn to help it since my construction is over? Or is it nervous because I might leave Hell like this?

Probably both. That must be the reason why Ghost Frontera insisted on having a conversation.

“What happened with that? You said you’d look around.”

The question wasn’t one he welcomed. Actually, it was a hard question for him. So, Lloyd replied with feigned nonchalance, “I haven’t found anything yet.”

“What? Still?”

“Yeah.”

“Why?”

Lloyd noticed its brows slightly knitting together. He crinkled its nose as well.

Tsk. This is why I didn’t feel comfortable talking with this dude.

“…”

Lloyd felt strangely indebted to Ghost Frontera, and that was the reason why he couldn’t dismiss its whining. Lloyd was annoyed and didn’t want to deal with it. But at the same time, he was apologetic toward Frontera. And so, he didn’t know how to react.

“I just couldn’t find a way to help you. Really.” he replied with the least amount of emotion possible in his voice.

“You couldn’t find a way?”

“Yeah.”

“Really?”

“Yeah.”

“Isn’t it more like you didn’t try to find one?”

“…”

Its questions started to feel like a nail. And its interrogating questions continued to come.

“Hey, be frank with me. You don’t have any intent to help me out, right?”

“Hey, come on. It’s not like that-”

“But it’s the truth.”

“…”

“Did you even try?” accused Frontera.

Lloyd seemed slightly offended. “I’m trying to think of ways to help!”

“Liar.”

“What?” Lloyd’s frown deepened.

“You think I don’t know?” Ghost’s voice dripped with rancor. “Thinking, my ass. You aren’t even bothering to think about this. You’ve been too preoccupied with thinking about how to construct the railroad or whatever it was the King of Hell told you to build. Am I wrong?”

“Hey.”

“Do you think I’ve been goofing around in your pocket for the past month? I was listening and brainstorming. Can you bastard honestly say that you ever thought about helping me for even a second?”

“Hey, watch your words.” Lloyd gritted his teeth. “You think it’s easy to help you out? In all seriousness, you don’t know how I can either. Still, I’m willing to find a way for me to. Do you really think that’s going to happen in a month? Really?”

“Ha. You and your silver tongue. I hear nothing but excuses.”

“What? Excuses?”

“Yeah. Excuses.” Ghost Frontera smirked in derision. “You think I don’t know what your plan is?”

“My plan?”

“To stall for time by saying you’re taking care of urgent matters first. And later, apologize, saying that you just can’t find any way to help me out. Then abandon me. That’s your plan.”

“…”

“First, you steal a happy man’s life, as well as his family and everything. And now, you’re playing with the ghost. Ha. You scumbag. You’re a despicable thief.”

“Hey.”

“What? Speak up if you have something to say.”

“Don’t talk that way. What does it change?”

“Nothing. That’s why I’m doing this,” said Frontera.

“What?” Lloyd’s frown deepened.

Ghost Frontera’s smirk deepened. “You won’t help me. I know, you bastard.”

“Ha… Seriously. Are you going to keep treating me like a criminal?”

“Of course. Can you even make any excuses for yourself?”

“I really wasn’t going to say this, but…” Lloyd, unable to hold any longer, spoke the very thing he’d been keeping to himself. “You were destined to die a year and a half later even if you didn’t lose your body. Did you know that?”

“What?” A scowl appeared on Ghost Frontera’s face.

“The pub where you made a scene every day. You were fated to vomit blood in the corner and die there.”

“Bastard! What are you talking about?!”

“Want me to go on?”

“…”

Ghost Frontera shut its mouth while Lloyd opened his, and the latter continued to speak the words he dared not tell anyone. The downfall of the Frontera barony in the novel. The tragic end of Lloyd, his parents, and his brother Julian. Lloyd narrated the story without any emotion, and Ghost Frontera intently listened without saying anything all the while. When Lloyd finished sharing the story, Ghost Frontera burst out in bitter laughter.

“Ha! So what? You want me to believe that?”

“…”

“Fine,” Ghost Frontera went on, “I’ll be generous, and let’s say that I do. But so what? You scumbag. You want me to bow down because you changed my family’s fate? Should I praise you? Is that what you want? Huh?”

“Praise, my foot. I don’t have any high expectations for you,” denied Lloyd.

“Then what are you trying to say?”

“I’m just stating the truth. You’re directly involved, so you should know about it.”

Lloyd’s voice was sharp as a knife. He was getting tired of arguing with the ghost.

“Fine,” Lloyd went on, “you might be right. I am a thief. You can curse me out all you want. Even now, to be honest, I don’t know how to help you. I’m at a complete loss. But is that so wrong of me?”

“…”

“Sure, if you say that it’s wrong, then I am wrong. I’ll be the bad guy here if I don’t help you out. But do you know what’s going to happen in that case? I’ll be the bad guy but still manage to live a good life without consequences. Then what, do you want me to die then? That isn’t it, either. I can’t think of anything else for me to do.”

“…”

“I really don’t know,” Lloyd’s voice was now tired. “I tried in my own way. Look what I’ve achieved ever since I got in your body. And yet, if you keep blaming me, I really don’t know what to do anymore. Do you understand?”

“Sure, since I was going to die anyway, I should be thankful that you let my family live in exchange for taking my body, right?”

“Hey, that’s not what I mean.”

“Bullshit. To me, it’s the same thing.”

“What are you…”

Lloyd was about to refute but stopped short when he cast a glance at the ghost. Ghost Frontera was crying.

“Yeah. I was set to die anyway. But you know what? If I did die, like you said, a year and a half later, bleeding in the corner of the pub, then I’d have been able to get judged here in Hell.”

“…”

“Yeah. If I did die, then I’d have been punished in Hell. I would have been able to hope for reincarnation after enduring my time here. But look at me now. What am I? Why am I like this? I no longer belong in the outside world or the underworld. I’m just a ghost, drifting around forever without any hope of reincarnation.”

“Hey…”

“Forget it. Sorry for pathetically whining at you bastard. Just do your job and scram.”

With that, Ghost Frontera carefully made its way back into his pocket. It never stuck out its head or pleaded to be let out. Lloyd tried to talk, but it didn’t answer.

“…“

He had no clue what to say in his current situation. Lloyd simply heaved a sigh with mixed emotions. He tried to focus on flying as he silently stroked Ggoming’s feathers with a nonchalant face. And soon, the uncomfortable flight came to an end as they arrived at the Hell Castle. Past the familiar entrance, he went into the king’s office.

"Lloyd Frontera is honored to be in the audience of the rightful King of Hell.”

Lloyd paid his respect to the King of Hell. His office hadn’t changed since the last time he visited. It had a large desk chair and an even bigger work desk with a mountain of documents on top. And even the King’s quick glance at him as he took care of a breathtaking amount of tasks was the same.

“Are you here? State your request before anything else.”

His weary attitude from being overworked was also no different. Holding back a wry smile, Lloyd replied, “Yes, I have completed the railroad construction you assigned to me previously.”

“Is that so?”

“Yes, my lord.”

“As far as I know, you also took down the Lava Giant.”

“Yes, I did.”

“It must have been tough.”

“Yes,” Lloyd said firmly. Thank you for your acknowledgement.“

"Ha. I see you aren’t even trying to feign modesty?”

“For there is no need for it.”

“Is that so? Anyway, I see. Thanks to you, I was able to learn quite a lot about railroad construction,” said Hellkaros.

“Wait, have you been watching the whole construction?” Lloyd lifted his brow.

“Of course.”

The King of Hell put his pen and paper down. His gaze glimmered through the rimmed glasses.

“I’ve understood the basic principles. Thanks to that, I think I have gotten an idea of how we should maintain the tracks.”

“Will you be all right without separate instructions?”

“Well, I would appreciate it if you offered a more detailed explanation. Your award for it should be sufficient as well,” said Hellkaros.

“Reward?” He didn’t expect to hear that.

“You give, and you take.”

The king’s answer was so nonchalant. Lloyd secretly clenched his fists.

As expected of the King of Hell.

The king hated free charity and preferred to live by the “give and take” principle. The king continued to speak.

“Suho Kim. If you tell me how I can manage the railroad, then I will give you one pass for the Hell Express.”

“The pass to use the Hell Express, my king?” Lloyd was slightly dazed.

“Yes.” The king nodded. “If you use this pass, if you die in the future or are sentenced to Purgatory because of your sins, you will be able to use Hell Express once no matter what.”

“You mean…”

“It means you can enter the Door of Reincarnation at the edge of Hell riding the train.”

“Woah.”

Lloyd unconsciously swallowed. How could he not? He realized the true meaning of the reward, this one pass to use the train.

It’s essentially a free ticket out of Hell, isn’t it?

This meant that any evil person or a soul bound to be in pain locked up in the Purgatory of Hell could go to the edge of Hell via the train and enter the Door of Reincarnation. In other words, one-time unconditional immunity to punishment and reincarnation.

It’s… awesome.

Lloyd’s heart thumped very fast when he thought how he’d secured a good time not only when he was alive but also when he was dead. The unexpectedly better reward made his fists clench, and Lloyd was glad that he’d decided to come down here. Just then, he flinched. Something came to his mind.

“…”

Lloyd’s attitude changed. His clenched fists relaxed, and the thumping heart grew calm. And his face looked deliberative. The change in his attitude was so evident that even the King of Hell noticed and became curious.

“Suho Kim, what is wrong? Do you have an issue with my reward?”

The King of Hell cocked his head to the side, curious whether Lloyd didn’t like the Hell Express ticket. Lloyd slowly lifted his gaze, and with a contemplative look on his face, he replied, “Oh, it is not that. I have no issue with regard to your reward. However…”

“However, what?”

“I have a question.”

“What is it?”

“This thing…” Lloyd slowly took his breath, organizing his thoughts in his head. Getting rid of greed in his heart, he resolved and asked, “This ticket you are planning to give me… Can a wandering ghost reincarnate with it?”
