# Difference Between an Order and Request (2)

---

“Ehem. Hmm! Today, as we continue further into this peaceful season of fall and all our crops ripen, I sincerely express my congratulations to the groom, Julian Frontera, and the bride, Sheherazade. Moreover, my congratulations go to the parents of both the groom and bride as today would be impossible without their love and care.”

Count Ventura solemnly gave a speech. He was the officiant of the wedding ceremony and part of the diplomatic delegation dispatched by Queen Magentano. His voice was calm and authoritative, much like a professional diplomat. Silence gradually fell over the garden as he read aloud the declaration of marriage. Everyone’s gaze focused on the bride and groom. Julian and Sheherazade. The two were holding hands as they stood upright facing the officiant. A smile of satisfaction appeared on Lloyd as he gazed at the two.

Woah. I can’t believe this is really happening.

His mind went back to what happened about two months ago. It must have been the day after he acted as Cupid between Julian and Sheherazade. A ruckus erupted out of the manor in the middle of the night. Sheherazade had gone to the count and countess’s bedroom past midnight and made a shocking request as she got down on her knees.

“Your Excellency, please give me your second son.”

Her remark at that time still amazed Lloyd to this day. It was so incredible that he couldn’t help but chuckle. She and Julian had just started becoming more than friends the day before, and the former didn’t even realize her feelings until Lloyd personally went to advise her.

Who would have thought that she would cause this chaos after only one day?

That was something Lloyd failed to foresee. That was how outrageous and unbelievable her actions were. It was something he only thought happened in soap operas. And the aftermath of the incident was equally explosive. The whole manor was turned upside down. Count Frontera, still in pajamas, sank onto the corridor floor, shell- shocked. Counter Frontera clapped her hand over her mouth and froze in place. The startled guards, who found them during their night duty, shouted to see if something happened. It was only afterward that they discovered Sheherazade on the floor.

According to the guards, she was said to have her gaze fixed on the count as she remained on her knees.

She was said to be ready to turn into a statue if Count Frontera didn’t permit their marriage. Anyway, the whole fuss woke every servant and maid in the manor. It was the same case for Lloyd, who was summoned by Count and Countess Frontera to join a family meeting before he could even rub the sleep from his eyes. Next, when he got to the count’s office, he heard another shocking remark, this time from Julian.

“I feel the same way about her.”

One from Sheherazade. Another from Julian. The two relentlessly hurled shocking and stubborn remarks one after another. Despite that, Count Frontera’s reaction was nothing like Lloyd had expected. He was not flustered anymore. He was not at a loss for what to do. Seemingly reaching a decision after some careful thinking, he put on a serious gaze and separately asked both Julian and Sheherazade whether they were being sincere.

The two nodded, of course. And that was it.

Count Frontera simply acknowledged them, and he didn’t ask any further questions. Instead of asking an unnecessary question, he immediately picked up his pen and carefully formatted two letters directed to two different destinations. One was for the Magentano palace, and the other one was for Ahinsya. Count Frontera informed the two monarchs of the marriage now that he confirmed the feelings of the two people. Without raising any doubts, Count Frontera chose to support and believe in the couple’s sincerity. He stepped forward and volunteered to take on the heavy role of asking for permission from the two kingdoms and adjudicating the two parties. From then on, everything moved forward without a hitch.

Of course. Queen Magentano and the sultan have no reason to oppose this marriage.

Their reactions were just as Lloyd expected. Since a peace treaty had been signed, both the royalties welcomed the union and even went further to float it as a symbol of harmony and reconciliation between the two kingdoms. It was because Julian was the second son of the Frontera fiefdom, which had been damaged by the Monster Domino, and Sheherazade was the daughter of the sultan, the culprit behind the incident.

Plus, the sultan was obsessed with having me as his son-in-law. Though he would have preferred me, I’m sure he determined that Julian is a great alternative. From the sultan’s perspective, he’s the next best option. 

Thanks to their union, the Frontera family had become in-laws with the sultan. The sultan would likely try in the future to use this as an excuse to make Lloyd his son-in-law. But of course, Lloyd didn’t have any intentions to dance to his tune.

Anyway.

Jostled out of his thoughts, Lloyd looked up. Under the bright fall sun, the wedding ceremony was quickly nearing its end in contrast with the weeks of hard preparation that was put into it.

“Therefore… The two have vowed in front of everyone gathered here today to be together for life. Now, I officially declare them as married before all of you who are watching this blessed ceremony.”

Pop! Crackle! Right after the declaration was read aloud, firecrackers went off in several places, petals and tiny papers of various colors flying about. The new bride, Sheherazade, tossed the bouquet, which blasted into the air from the strength of a high-level sword expert. The guests regretfully swallowed as no one could even attempt to catch it. The bouquet flew out of the garden and for several hundreds of yards until it lost its strength and fell on a studio.

Thud. “Eh…?”

Solitas, who was busily processing gravel in a corner of his studio, raised his eyebrows. He was engrossed in his work when a bouquet of flowers flew out of nowhere and hit his work table. It was as if someone had sent him a gift.

“What is this?”

Could it be something from the wedding that was said to be happening today? Was this the famous “bouquet” that he had only heard about? Solitas raised his brows higher.

I’ve heard of this before. I heard that whoever catches a bouquet tossed by a bride will be able to get married. Tsk. Humans, really. They stupidly obsess over groundless superstitions. What, did they think I’d be happy to receive this?

If so, they were correct. Solitas quietly stashed the bouquet under the table, and a mysterious smile hung on his mouth.

Meanwhile, the wedding ended in a success. Julian and Sheherazade hopped into a carriage, their faces blushing red. The sight brought tears of joy to Count and Countess Frontera.

The sultan, who couldn’t come to the wedding in person, joined his daughter using a magic mirror that the delegation held up for him. He experienced a surge of emotions. Sheherazade was his favorite daughter, though he kept that to himself as he had to act cool on the outside. Any thoughtless show of favor on his part could make her the target of envy and hate from her dozens of brothers and sisters. There was a fair chance for her to wind up in a power struggle that results in a feud. And so, he hid his feelings as her father, waiting for Sheherazade to become a sword master who’s strong enough to overcome the envy and slander of her brothers and sisters. Once that day came, when he wouldn’t have to worry about her safety as she could protect herself, he decided to keep her on the side and reveal his true heart. After all, he was a father before a ruler like any other person.

“Hahaha…” The sultan inwardly smiled in contentment.

The guests sent a round of applause as they filled the manor garden. Just like that, the newlyweds went off on their honeymoon with everyone’s blessing.

“Whew.”

Once the wedding ceremony was over, Lloyd heaved a big sigh he had been holding for a long time.

I did it.

At last, Julian and Sheherazade were officially married, and they were congratulated and acknowledged by both the queen and the sultan. This was exactly what Lloyd had hoped for.

That rascal.

The carriage, which Julian would be sitting inside as a husband, grew farther away. A satisfied smile appeared on Lloyd’s face. But was it because Julian had become a useful pawn for him? Not entirely.

I… just feel happy.

Originally, Julian should have died already. That was what originally happened in The Knight of Blood and Iron.

The Frontera family goes under, and having no more money to pay his tuition, he gets kicked out of the royal academy.

That day, Julian was killed by a robber while wandering around a back alley in the capital to find a place to sleep. That was the original story in the novel. But now, it’s changed. Julian wasn’t dead. He graduated from the academy with honors and became a husband to a woman he loves. Now, he was happily going on his honeymoon. It was a stark contrast with the miserable, meaningless death he faced in the novel.

And that’s all thanks to me, your big brother, you punk.

The same went for Sheherazade. If it wasn’t for Lloyd, who built the Qanat in the Kandahar region, she would have been swept away in the hands of the rebels and died before her talent could be fully developed. But the story had changed once again. She was alive, needless to say, and also a happy bride on the way to her honeymoon. What was more, she even obtained a recording of Lloyd’s lullaby last night!

Well, she’s now my sister-in-law. Haha.

So, Lloyd concluded that it was only right for Julian and Sheherazade to return the favor they received. From now on, they were to be excellent pawns in his game. Fully basking in overwhelming satisfaction, Lloyd watched the newlyweds’ carriage disappear.

A few seconds later, Lloyd pushed his feelings away. Now wasn’t the time to get emotional. It was time to check how well his plan had been set. Thinking so, Lloyd swiveled away, walking through the crowds of people leaving the garden. Then he greeted Count Ventura who officiated the ceremony.

“It must have not been easy back there, Count Ventura,” Lloyd said as he stood in front of the count.

“Ahh,” gasped Count Ventura in happy surprise, “Why, if it isn’t Lloyd Frontera.”

“Yes, Your Excellency,” greeted Lloyd, “I was too busy to greet you before the ceremony. I hope everything is well with you.”

“Things are going well, of course. And you?”

“I am doing well. It is all thanks to you,” Lloyd replied.

“Haha, your flattery is the same as always.” Count Ventura beamed at Lloyd like he was his lucky charm.

How could he not? The count was able to pull off the negotiation with the sultan thanks to Lloyd and received a huge reward from the queen. But today, they had a more important matter at hand.

“But Count Ventura, do you not have a message for me?” asked Lloyd with a soft smile.

“Excuse me?”

“I know you have one.”

“Haha…” Count Ventura let out a snort and looked at Lloyd in amazement. “Have you caught on already?” “Well, I have a rough idea.” Lloyd shrugged his shoulders.

It was true. He had seen it coming. Count Ventura, Lloyd figured, would relay Queen Magentano’s true feelings regarding this marriage.

With tension building around his eyes, Lloyd requested, “Then should we go elsewhere? There are too many people here.”

“Yes. Let us do that.”

Lloyd slipped out of the ceremony with the count and entered the manor as they pretended to have a casual conversation. And they sat opposite each other in the reception room where no one would come near.

“Since you seem to have a rough idea of the situation, I won’t beat around the bush,” started Count Ventura. “Your guess is right. There is a message Her Majesty ordered me to specially convey to you.”

After that, he paused before he continued again.

“First, Her Majesty has made no official comment about this marriage. Her Majesty only mumbled something to herself with a scoff.”

“What did she mumble…?”

“She said, "Tsk, I have a job for that mole cricket so bring him here.”“

"Her Majesty has a job for me…?” asked Lloyd.

“Indeed.” The count nodded. “Do you remember Cannavaro, the warlock that you captured back in Namaran?” “Yes, Your Excellency.”

“Actually, we have already finished interrogating him, and Her Majesty launched a punitive operation based on his confession. This happened while you were building the Qanat.”

“Punitive operation?” Lloyd said, the words sounding unreal in his ears.

“Yes, and it ended successfully.”

“…”

Lloyd was secretly impressed. The queen lived up to her name. She not only finished interrogating the warlock, but also completely took care of all the other remnants. That was much like her, Lloyd thought to himself. 

“But what does the suppression of the remaining followers of the warlock have anything to do with Her Majesty calling for me… Do you know the reason, Your Excellency?” asked Lloyd, unsure.

“I do not know the answer to that question.”

“Then could it be an edict, this calling me to the palace?”

After asking, Lloyd swallowed. Now came the important part. Why the queen wanted him hardly mattered. Her attitude as she called for him was more important. Lloyd had paired Julian and Sheherazade because he didn’t want to be worked to death and wanted to two-time both the queen and sultan. All of this was his attempt to put the idea in her head that Lloyd might get upset and side with the sultan if she treated him poorly. By creating alarm, she would treat him more favorably after feeling a sense of crisis.

This is my chance. And depending on his answer, I can get a sense of how she plans on treating me.

Would he still be treated like a slave? Or, if not, with more courtesy than before? He could make a judgment from her attitude toward him. Thinking so, Lloyd looked at the count. Just then, the latter nodded.

“Edict?” Count Ventura repeated. “No, it is not.”

Now, with a graver and more solemn expression on his face, he added, “Her Majesty has specially advised me to tell you that this message is neither an edict nor a unilateral demand for you to come to the capital.”

“Then, what is it, Your Excellency?” Lloyd’s voice dripped with anticipation.

“She emphasized that it was a courteous and polite ‘request’ from her as your ruling queen.

The moment Lloyd heard him speak, he secretly clenched his fists.

It was a success.

The queen’s unilateral order had now changed into a polite request. Lloyd felt the beginning of a seemingly menial but critical change.
