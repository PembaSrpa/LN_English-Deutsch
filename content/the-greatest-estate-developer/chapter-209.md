# Difference Between an Order and Request (1)

---

Wahaha. Who knew that things would go this well?

It was a hot summer night. But being a young dragon himself, Solitas didn’t mind the heat at all as he sauntered around. Instead, he simply clenched his fists for what he was able to achieve.

I did it. I did it. I finally mastered the wood block.

Solitas recalled what happened earlier in the day. He was wrestling with a wood block like any other day. He sliced, carved, and refined the wood to a size smaller than his nail. He was keeping his mind on the job when he heard a voice beside him.

“That will be enough. Let’s move to the next step.” It was Corgidus, his teacher.

Solitas wondered when his teacher had approached him and started to watch him work. He had been so focused that he didn’t notice Corgidus’s presence. But what surprised him more was the unexpected comment that came out of his teacher’s mouth. Corgidus let out a snort at him.

“At this point, you can move on from the wood blocks,” Corgidus said. “I am more proud of you because I can see that you have put a lot of effort in your own way.”

“Th-then…” muttered Solitas, feeling expectant.

“Tomorrow, we will start carving stones.”

Corgidus then tapped him on the shoulder twice as though he was complimenting him for a job well done and was proud of his work. Solitas inwardly jumped in sheer joy as he watched his teacher leave after dropping the good news.

I thought I’d never move past working on the wood blocks.

It was true. He himself could see how terrible he was with his hands. Even with the master dwarf as his teacher, he couldn’t absorb even one percent of what he was being taught and Corgidus was becoming frustrated with him as well. But Solitas didn’t give up.

I can’t give up. I need to get married!

He had a grand wish. Getting married. Starting a happy family. He swore early on that he would succeed in this no matter what it took.

So, I didn’t give up. It was really tough. Whew…

Solitas wiped the tears off his eyes with his sleeves as he walked along the forest trail. He became overwhelmed with emotions as he looked back on his hard work after coming to this fiefdom as Corgidus’s apprentice.

The thing is, these jewel craftsmanship lessons aren’t free.

Solitas had to take care of the waste sludge of the main canal once every month. The dung of humans, orcs, and the smelliest of them all, the elves. How self-deprecating it was for him to let out a fiery breath at the enormous pile of dung every month!

But of course, this will continue for the foreseeable future. I will have to take on more endeavors similar to this one. But it’s fine. My craftsmanship skills are improving. I just need to work a little harder.

Solitas clenched his fists. He was now set to process stones. Next time, he would be working with metal, and one day, he would employ actual jewels.

Then I’ll be able to store a mountain of priceless and beautifully refined jewels just like all the other dragons. I can do it.

If he worked just ten more years, he’d be able to confidently compete in the market for marriage. Solitas allowed his fantasy to develop and thought about the outfit he’d wear to meet his future wife’s parents, where their honeymoon would be, the babies they’d have, and how they’d grow old together. Then his reverie naturally evolved to select the candidates for his bride.

Whew. If I really get married, who am I going to marry?

Solitas was curious, so he thought some more.

How many unmarried female dragons around my age are there… Let’s see… From what I heard before… about 17 of them in total?

Solitas counted with his fingers, listing the female dragons he could potentially marry. Just then, he recalled one more.

Oh, one more. The daughter of Verkis, the dragon king.

The daughter that resulted from a union between the dragon king and a human woman. She was said to be half dragon and half human. As such, her marriageable age was different from ordinary dragons, and this meant she was also one of his potential partners for marriage. But when his train of thought reached that far, Solitas shook his head.

Ah, forget it. What am I thinking? The daughter of the dragon king? She’s way out of my league.

He was entitled to let his imagination take flight, but there was a limit. How could he dare imagine marrying the daughter of the dragon king? There was no chance they would ever meet each other, let alone fall in love. From the start, she was someone he couldn’t even lay his eyes on.

So, I’ll leave her out of my list. Whew. I hope my candidates stay healthy and safe until I stockpile a hoard of jewels ten years from now. Whoever you are, don’t catch a cold, take good care of your skin, and don’t get esophagitis from breathing out too much fire.

Just like that, Solitas wished for the well-being of his bridal candidates, one of whom could become his life companion. Resolving to work harder moving forward and get married someday, Solitas took a stroll in the middle of the summer night, satisfied and content. Just then, a suspicious noise interrupted him.

Hmm?

Solitas paused his walk for the first time, and he pricked up his ears and focused on the sound coming ahead of him.

Someone is coming.

Whom could it be at this ungodly hour past midnight? He noticed two people walking toward him. Solitas sharpened his senses a tad, and he scanned the features of the intruder with a vision that transcended human capacity. Then he saw them.

Humans. Two of them. One is a man. The other one is a woman. At this hour? Walking side-by-side? And they are blushing?

Solitas swallowed in spite of himself, and he was able to recognize what they were the moment his eyes laid on them. A couple. Despicable beings. This shameless couple was rubbing salt on the wounded heart of an innocent dragon as they strolled together side by side. However… Solitas couldn’t think of any words to criticize them.

C-crap!

Solitas gritted his teeth. Here he was, breathing out fire and incinerating piles of dung just to get married. He was carving out wood blocks in a dusty studio all day in exchange for his precious labor. Yet, that despicable couple over there was heartily laughing and smiling at each other. Solitas was green with envy. It was unfair. But there wasn’t anything he could say to them.

Shoot! Dammit! I’m going to try harder once I start working on stones tomorrow!

Solitas held back his tears, feeling a shaft of wretchedness, and rushed to the opposite side lest that despicable couple saw him cry. The woman cocked her head to the side.

Hmm? I definitely felt something from a distance. But it disappeared in a flash. What is it?

There was no way of knowing. But it didn’t feel like a human. No one could hide their presence that quickly unless they were a sword master.

A bunny, maybe?

Sheherazade let out a sigh. Relatedly, her own bunny was beside her at the moment.

“Um… Is something… wrong?” Julian asked carefully, studying her.

“No, nothing.”

Sheherazade quickly shook her head when she heard Julian speak in a concerned tone. She was thankful that the moon wasn’t bright tonight. Julian wouldn’t be able to notice the blush on her face this way.

Whew… I’m going nuts.

Sheherazade secretly nibbled on her lips. To be honest, she never intended to go on a midnight stroll together. Walking with him wasn’t part of her original plan. She never expected it to happen.

I felt trapped inside, so I stepped out to get some fresh air for a bit.

She had confined herself all day in her room, pondering on what Lloyd had told her the night before about how Julian would soon depart for the capital. She spent the whole day stressing over the matter.

I don’t understand my feelings.

For some reason, her heart had sunk when she heard Lloyd’s words. And his words stayed stuck in her head from then on. She didn’t know why, however. At the same time, she was nervous about bumping into Julian with such a disorganized heart. What face should she make in front of him? How should she greet him? It was beyond her. So, she couldn’t take a step outside of her room for fear that she would bump into him. The thought of it seemed scary and awkward. She worried that she would do something idiotic in front of him. In the end, she spent the whole day hovering around her small room.

So, that was why I came out to get some fresh air for just a moment.

Since it was past midnight and no one would be around, she stepped out of her room feeling slightly more relaxed. Hence, she never expected to bump right into Julian in the middle of this trail.

“Whew…”

Sheherazade wasn’t alone. Julian, too, was taken by surprise. He never imagined her to be out for a stroll at the same time and place as him.

What am I supposed to say? I mean, should I keep walking with her like this?

Julian walked as he thought in his head. Should he come up with an excuse and take another path? The thought crossed his mind for a second, but he soon shook his head.

If I do that, Miss Sheherazade will look at me more strangely.

She might also get upset. Thus, this meant that he should continue walking alongside her.

But what am I supposed to say?

He had to come up with a topic to discuss. But he couldn’t get himself to speak. It was because of what Lloyd had told him the other night. He was anxious that the feelings he realized he had for her were one-sided. He couldn’t dare recklessly open his mouth in front of her. As they hesitated, they arrived at the end of the trail.

“Oh…” Julian gaped.

In no time, they found themselves standing in front of the manor. Realizing that, Julian hurriedly turned to Sheherazade.

“Miss,” called Julian, his voice diffident.

“Yes?” Sheherazade turned.

“Oh… umm.” There was a heavy silence in between his words.

“Good night,” Sheherazade said first.

“Oh… Okay…”

Julian wanted to tell her good night first. But she had already wheeled around and was walking into the manor. Julian nibbled his lips as he watched her disappear.

The night wore on.

It’s strange. I can’t sleep.

He blinked hard, his eyes wide open. Julian, now back in his room, blankly lay on the bed. He wondered why he couldn’t fall asleep. Was it because he couldn’t stop thinking about Sheherazade from their stroll earlier? Was he regretful about not telling her good night first? It was a mystery. No, to be honest, he knew why. It was because of what his brother told him last night. ‘You’ll lose Miss Sheherazade if you keep going like this?’

“…”

What if I really lost her? What if he continued hesitating and acting like a fumbling idiot whenever they met? Would she slip out of his grasp then?

I don’t want that.

No matter how many times he racked his brain, there was only one answer. Julian nibbled his lips. It was fine if he didn’t know what to say to her. It didn’t matter if they didn’t exchange a single word. Or, just strolled together like before and occasionally met each other’s eyes. Even if he couldn’t express these feelings he held, it was all the same.

I miss her.

He suddenly bolted upright in his bed and walked into the hallway full of darkness.

It’s strange. I don’t want to sleep.

Her eyes were wide open, and Sheherazade lay down in bed after she got back to her room. She wondered why she wouldn’t fall asleep. Could it be because she kept thinking about Julian from their stroll earlier? Was she bothered by how she had rushed into the manor after giving a hurried farewell, feeling shy and awkward? There was no way of knowing. No, she actually knew. It was because of what Lloyd said to her last night. His words kept pestering her heart. ‘Julian will leave for the capital soon.’

“…”

Julian, the bunny-like man. She wondered when he would leave. She realized she should have asked that question earlier. What if he suddenly left straight away tomorrow morning? What if their stroll from just before was her last chance?

I don’t want that.

No matter how many times she thought about it, there was only one answer. Sheherazade clenched her fists. She didn’t want to send him off to the capital like this. She didn’t want to hopelessly part with him like that. It was fine if they didn’t exchange a single word. It didn’t matter if they just silently strolled together like earlier. She wanted to spend every day with him from now on. And so…

I’m going to do it.

She jumped out of her bed and walked into the dark hallway.

Julian walked carefully and slowly yet confidently. He moved past the dark corridor that led to the annex. Taking down the stairs, he made way for the visitor’s room with a resolution to knock on her door.

Sheherazade walked, too, her steps relentless and confident. She collected her breath, passing the dark corridor that led to the main manor. She went upstairs and stretched her hand out toward a closed door.

Knock knock. The noise from the door echoed aloud the corridor. Julian flinched at the sound he made. He was worried that he might be rudely imposing. He corrected himself. Knocking on a lady’s room so late at night? This was obviously a mistake.

But…

He missed her. He wanted to see her even if he was imposing. With such thought in mind, he stared at the closed door. Then he knocked again.

Creak. The door hinge noisily rang aloud the corridor, and Sheherazade became nervous at the sound. She was worried that she might be rudely imposing. She corrected herself. Knocking on someone’s room so late at night? This was obviously a mistake.

But…

She had to do it. That was how much she wanted to be with Julian. With such thought in her head, she stared at the opening door.

“Who is it-”

Could it be that she woke him up? Count Frontera appeared sleepy through the door. As soon as she saw him, Sheherazade dropped down to her knees and blurted the comment she had practiced hundreds of times to herself.

“Your Excellency, please give me your second son. I promise I will make him happy for the rest of his life.”
