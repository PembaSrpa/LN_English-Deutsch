# An Even More Shameless Demand (1)

---

Lloyd was in the Magenta Palace, which strangely appeared like a giant, half-buried egg. It was structured this way to prevent dragons from lifting it up from the ground. Lloyd was there, carefully inspecting all its nooks and crannies.

Let’s see… Survey.

[Scanning will begin.]

Bzzt! Lloyd activated his surveying skill, and various types of information appeared everywhere he could see. There was everything there could be, pieces of information about the structure’s design, weight, strength, compound method, etc… He could even see the price of the land because he activated the first optional skill of the surveying skill.

Ding dong.

[Skill Option 1: Land Price Appraisal has been activated.]

[You can appraise the land you measure. (Error rate: +/- 5%)]

Frankly, Lloyd wanted to find out the worth of the palace as it sat at the center of the kingdom. He figured he might as well satisfy his curiosity since he had to run a scan on the palace anyway. The result was jaw-droppingly shocking. He felt a rush of self-hate spread throughout his entire body.

Wooow. I somewhat expected this, but it’s obscenely expensive.

His mouth hung open when he saw the price. It was unimaginably, devilishly expensive. The district of Gangnam in central Seoul was nothing compared to this. The value of one corner of the palace was enough to purchase the entire Frontera County and still leave enough money to buy about 1,000 meals.

It’s quite literally priceless.

Come to think of it, such a price made sense. After all, the House of Magentano was currently enjoying its golden days in history. The queen was a mighty sword master, backed by great lords in the province who gave her unwavering allegiance. On top of it all, she now had the Bone Dragon, which just meant another formidable weapon was added to her already stable and strong political and military arsenal.

Though technically, the Bone Dragon is mine.

But people didn’t know that. They thought that the dragon was loyal to the queen. In their knowledge, the Bone Dragon was dispatched to Frontera County because she ordered it to go.

All of these factors contributed to the dramatic hike in the palace’s value. Now, it’s at an overwhelmingly high and unreachable level that even its long-standing enemy, the sultan’s kingdom, will have to submit.

Literally speaking, the Magentano Kingdom was a global hegemony. So, it went without saying that the price of its land had skyrocketed.

Ha… Crap. I’m jealous.

As he continued his thoughts, a pang of envy crept up in him. But he soon shook his head, telling himself that now wasn’t the time to be concerned about trivial matters like the price of land. He urgently needed to find some tauranga, and the Jewel of Truth had to be constructed so that the status quo would continue without the restoration of destiny.

I don’t have much time.

Just then, Lloyd remembered that the count was set to fall off the horse in less than three years. To be more exact, he had two years and seven or eight months, give or take. Someone might say that Lloyd had enough time. It was longer than the required period of mandatory military service in South Korea, after all. But to Lloyd, it didn’t feel long at all.

I doubt I’ll be able to retrieve the key materials for construction in time. The construction of the jewel is still left for me to do, and I need to find a way to stop the restoration of destiny after building it. And it’ll take more time to execute the solution I find. Really, time isn’t on my side.

When Lloyd thought about all the work ahead of him, he could only think about how much time he lacked.

I don’t have time to dawdle around here.

So he affirmed his resolution once again. It was to make the most of his time and minimize the amount of trials and errors he went through to save time. And that was the very reason why he was conducting a survey at the moment.

“Are you certain, Young Master, that there’s tauranga in this palace?”

“Of course.” Lloyd looked at Javier, who was walking by his side as he asked. “So, we have to find it.”

“Do you plan on harvesting it once you do?”

“Yeah. Just that. Nothing else.” Clean and neat.“ Lloyd shrugged. "Look, I told the queen I’d lift up the entire palace, but realistically, that would be too wasteful.”

“By wasteful, what do you mean?” There was genuine curiosity in Javier’s voice.

“The situation will get too complicated. Think about it,” said Lloyd, “What do you think will happen if I lift this palace up like I’m pulling radishes? You think it will remain intact?”

“Even I can see that will not be the case,” replied Javier calmly.

“Right? It’s going to take someone with extraordinary strength to lift up the palace without causing any damage. Even if the Bone Dragon and Bibeong worked together for the job, it wouldn’t be enough. Unless Dragon King Verkis comes out to help us.”

It was true. Bibeong and the Bone Dragon would only cause serious damage to the palace by doing so. To avoid any damage to the palace, it had to be plucked out in one almighty and clean pull that ensured an equally clean return. The only person Lloyd knew who could do it was Dragon King Verkis.

That sleepy dragon has actually done it twice before. Moreover, I even saw there were two palaces standing tall in his cave as if they were decorations. Those palaces hardly had any damage on them.

But bringing the dragon king here was impossible. As far as Lloyd was concerned, Verkis was the laziest creature in existence, so there was a chance that Lloyd would only invite wrath from the king if he tried to make him move.

“Anyway,” went on Lloyd, “If I lift up the palace and cause damage, I’ll have to make repairs. I already have a mountain of work to do, so I don’t have the time to do repairs. So-”

“Your plan is to locate the tauranga and just take out the area with the pieces of stone,” finished Javier in understanding.

“That’s exactly right. Like popping a pimple.”

That would minimize the damage to the structure. In addition, it would cut down the time needed for construction.

“So, I need to hurry and find the tauranga here. Better if it’s in a spot conducive for us to harvest.”

“But,” pointed out Javier, “It appears that you have already surveyed all the convenient areas to harvest the stone.”

“Yeah, so I’m starting to get sad.”

Lloyd sighed at length. It was just what Javier had said. Lloyd had wished for the tauranga to be found in an easy, harvestable spot. Reasoning that at least one or two pieces of it had to be an easy find, Lloyd checked the roofs first, followed by the ceilings, pillars, and floors. But the stone was nowhere to be found in any of those places. Lloyd was starting to get restless.

Tsk. It’s going to be troublesome if I find all of the stone underground or around the palace’s foundation.

Lloyd direly wished that wouldn’t be the case. Begging and pleading, he tensed his eyes and continued his survey as he scanned every palatial corner for over three days. He even was granted access to secret places and vaults with the generous cooperation of the queen. But even still, tauranga was nowhere to be found. By then, Lloyd’s anxiety grew worse, along with his dry, bloodshot eyes. And finally, two more days later, he discovered pieces of tauranga.

“And… it had to be here out of everywhere… Really?” Lloyd heaved a sigh. Once again, he realized that his life never went easily. Why? It was because he had found the tauranga at the far bottom portion of the palace’s foundation.

I wouldn’t have found it without my optional skill.

Lloyd gazed down below. The chunks of tauranga were in the stone chamber, the lowest underground floor. The humidity was so thick down there that various types of molds fought each other to find more space. To be more exact, Lloyd was studying a giant rock beneath several layers of stones. That was the tauranga that Lloyd had been direly searching for. But its location was extremely, utterly, and seriously problematic.

I’m screwed.

They were inserted in the foundation that supported the weight of the palace. To be more exact, they made up the backbone of the palatial foundation’s isolated piers.

Okay. I can’t just take them out. If I were to compare… Hmm, yeah. Yes, it’d be like taking out and replacing the stakes that hold the entire weight of a skyscraper without toppling it over.

Or it was the same as flicking the bottom of a Jenga tower without toppling it over. In other words, the tauranga here was used as the backbone for the isolated piers in order to support the palace’s foundation. The worst thing was that all twelve of them were used separately. What would happen if Lloyd haphazardly pulled them out?

The palace will sink.

Just like a Jenga Tower that topples by wrongfully removing the bottom blocks, the foundation of the palace was set to sink and bring about a disaster. One way to prevent this from happening was to prepare stones that were the same in size and strength as the tauranga used for the pier and throw them in as Lloyd pulled everything out. It would be a quick exchange like replacing a part of a machine. But the process of getting there, as well as the clean-up afterward, appeared to be quite challenging.

Tsk. Should I approach this by digging from the top until the foundation appears? No. That won’t do. For starters, each piece of the tauranga is way too big. The smallest piece of the stone looks to be nineteen feet in length. I’ll have to turn over the floor here to salvage it out and make a tunnel to move the stone outside. Nah. The job will become too complicated if I do that.

Countless layers of floors, walls, and other structures stood between where he was positioned and outside the palace. Even if Lloyd managed to pull this piece of tauranga out safely, he would have to procure a pathway that was at least nineteen feet long.

Making the pathway isn’t the hard part. The job after that is the challenge.

If he were to fix and repair the areas he dug out, it would only add another mountain of tasks to his list and also take a considerable amount of time. Lloyd didn’t like that. If he could, he wanted to finish his job in the most efficient and orderly way possible.

I should think it over.

Lloyd got a feeling that the answer could be found in his wide breadth of knowledge. Once he sensed that, he went straight back to his VIP chamber and confined himself for two days in a row. He even skipped singing the lullaby for Javier during this time. He shut himself in his room, looking over various calculations and formulas based on the data he retrieved from surveying. He considered various factors, including the data points he retrieved of the foundation from surveying, the weight each pier held, and the expected changes that would occur to the ground if the tauranga was replaced with something else. Lloyd considered, predicted, prepared, experimented, calculated, and decided.

“Good.”

As soon as a decision was made, he left his room, entered the palace’s main building, and requested the audience with the queen to the Lord of the Chamberlain. He was then led to the queen’s office without much waiting, and he fell on his knees as soon as he entered and instantly got to business.

“Hail to Her Majesty. I, Lloyd Frontera, the eldest son of Frontera County, am honored to be in the audience of the rightful owner of this land. I requested a meeting as I have an urgent request to make.”

“You have a request? And it is urgent?” asked Queen Magentano.

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

Queen Magentano frowned a little. It felt like Lloyd had been paying her visits with ease recently, and she got the feeling that his requests had become bolder and more open.

Soon, his arrogance will soar through the roof. Even though he’ll be careful not to cross any boundaries.

Given his cunning temperament, he would never do so. He’ll be insolent, but not to the point he’d cross the line. Doing so will only plunge him into the life he doesn’t want to live.

He will be a king after driving me out or declaring independence.

But that wasn’t the life Lloyd wanted. Laughter escaped her when she thought so.

His goal is to laze and idle around despite his abilities and powers. And yet, he works harder than anyone else in the world. No one will be more amusing and ironic than this man.

Lloyd amazed her. At the same time, she felt sorry for him. Her voice softened slightly more. “I see. You have the right to make a request. What is it?” What kind of outrageous request could he have come with? She felt curious and sorry at the same time.

“Yes, Your Majesty. If I dare may…” It was like Lloyd’s tongue was equipped with a twin-turbo engine. It smashed the gas pedal without hesitation. “I would like to request your assistance in performing what is called the freezing method. This is one of the methods to improve the foundation, and it was first used in the 19th century in the United Kingdom when they excavated a vertical shaft in its mines. It operates by inserting the freezing pipe and injecting -22 Fahrenheit icy liquid into the ground, freezing the moisture in the soil around the pipes like ice cream. After some time, the frozen pillars thicken until they touch each other, forming a giant ice wall. Since icy walls are extremely harder than the soils-”

“Pause there,” Queen Magentano raised her hand.

“Yes, Your Majesty.” Lloyd stopped and shamelessly stared at the queen, who held his gaze with a mildly sickening look. And a part of her felt that it’d be better to grant him whatever he wanted instead of hearing him out and bleeding her ears.

At last, she sighed and asked, “So, what is it that you want specifically?”

“Here it is, Your Majesty.”

Swoosh! Lloyd brought a note from his pocket and held it to the queen. And he spoke in a brazen and shameless manner as if he was demanding something back from her.

“I would like Your Majesty to permit me to freely use ‘The Heart of Winter,’ one of the three divine items of the House of Magentano.”
