# A Special Host (1)

---

“You have to do well tonight. Got it?”

Clack! Yongyong earnestly nodded at his master’s words. Lloyd. The most awesome master in the world. The incomparable, fantabulous commander of the Skeleton Corps. Yongyong thought to itself that its master was the best. Oh, how dearly it missed him!

Clack! Creak! Yongyong recalled how it had been spending its days as it rubbed the giant bow tie around its neck. The loneliness. The boredom. Yongyong was on cloud nine when it met Lloyd back in Hell and extremely excited when it came back to the capital through the Hell gate with him. But its joy and elation last only for a short while. The emotions were banished from its heart when Lloyd requested something.

“Yongyong. You’ll cause an uproar if someone sees you, so go hide for now. Head over there. That’s the east direction.”

And then he went on to say…

“Keep flying, and you’ll see a giant mountain range. That’s the eastern mountain range. Hang around there for a while. Be friendly with the orcs if you bump into them. You’re a good boy, Yongyong. If you behave, I’ll introduce you to your brothers in the Skeleton Corps. Okay, off you go.”

So, Yongyong did exactly that. There was no reason to disobey Lloyd. It flew toward the eastern range and even took a nice bath in a deep lake there. That was when Yongyong bumped into the orc tribe. Upon seeing Yongyong, the ores roared and greeted Yongyong, and their welcome reminded the dragon of Lloyd’s advice. Determined to be faithful to its master’s order to act friendly around the orcs, Yongyong returned with a welcoming roar. This got the ores to convulse in joy. Their reaction was wild. They crawled on top of its body and pet Yongyong with their various types of axes, spears, and hammers. Sometimes, they even tied Yongyong’s whole body with ropes and twisted and turned its bones. And since the orcs were strong, their massages were far more refreshing and relaxing than the typical massages or chiropractic treatments from pseudo-experts.

From that moment onward, hanging out at Lake Kapua with the orc warriors became a part of Yongyong’s daily routine. They were joyful days. But still, the emptiness in the corner of its breastbone lingered, primarily because it missed Lloyd.

Clack! Creak! Despite the great fun it had with the orcs and their refreshing massages, an unquenchable emptiness lingered in its heart. Yongyong yearned to spend time with its master. It wanted to play, work, and get complimented. But such an opportunity didn’t come because Lloyd didn’t call for Yongyong, even when he was back at the fiefdom!

Creak! Though Yongyong was wistful, it endured because it knew very well why Lloyd wasn’t calling or introducing it to its skeleton brothers.

Clack… Yongyong believed it was all because it was ugly. Particularly because of its ugly skull. Yongyong was convinced that Lloyd’s negligence had much to do with his embarrassment of showing it to the world. Whenever such thoughts crossed its mind occasionally, Yongyong was terribly discouraged and became envious of handsome creatures whose existence alone wasn’t fair. Sad and resentful, Yongyong grew tired of playing with the orc warriors. Making up its mind to go astray, it sank to the bottom of the lake and stayed there all day until it, out of the blue, received a signal from Lloyd.

Creak! It immediately rocketed out of the lake as if it was never sulking and fluttered its wings to the location provided by Lloyd’s optional skill, Zombie Domination. And it arrived at the capital in a flash and basked in the joy of reuniting with Lloyd. And today, it was given a grand mission.

“Remember! Who’s the main character of today’s event?” asked Lloyd.

Clack!

“Right!” exclaimed Lloyd. “It’s you. So, the success or failure of this event will depend on your performance. That makes you the main character of today. So, tell me, what should you do?”

Creak!

“Okay. You remember it. Follow the script I recited to you a while ago. On my cue, appear at the designated spot doing the pose I told you. Be powerful, dashing, and forceful. And then?”

Clack!

“Good job,” complimented Lloyd, “You do remember. Match the queen’s pace. Got it?”

Creak!

“Awesome. My. You’re a smart boy. Do well here, and you’ll be able to confidently appear in front of other people. You won’t need to hide anymore. So, do your best!”

Clack!

After walking through the whole plan with Yongyong one last time, Lloyd headed back to the banquet. Yongyong gave an earnest nod as it gazed at the back of Lloyd’s head. If it did well today, it could always be with Lloyd. Resolved, Yongyong fixed its clothes, making sure that its bow tie wasn’t crooked and tux was wrinkle-free. Yongyong checked over and over again. That was how anxious it was about this job.

Clack…

Yongyong was worried that people might get shocked by its looks. Even though Lloyd assured it that it was all fine, Yongyong was still worried. A few moments later, Lloyd’s signal came.

Now!

Kaboom! At the signal, Yongyong flew up and soared into the night sky, its tuxedo tail fluttering against the wind. It then checked the direction and angle of the full moon before flying to the spot that masked the moon and filled the sky with its silhouette. After finding this spot, Yongyong filled the night sky with its body and let out a roar.

…!

The roar was controlled and silent. But its shockwave shook the sky, making everything tremble at the banquet that was several miles away. The musicians’ instruments vibrated. The wine splashed inside their glasses. One of the foreign diplomats disparaging tonight’s banquet and the House of Magentano quivered in shock.

“I-It’s the Bone Dragon!”

His loud screech and shivering finger pointing to the sky immediately sent a ripple of fear across the banquet. The guests berated their luck. The Bone Dragon had to appear today, the very night Queen Magentano was celebrating the recovery from the previous disaster, and they happened to be here. Everyone felt a twinge of bitterness as well as fear and couldn’t stop thinking about how much of a nightmare this was. Furthermore, since the queen failed to subdue the Bone Dragon and was left helpless previously, everyone guessed in despair that they’d end up dead tonight. The Bone Dragon touched down to the ground.

Boooom!

“Ackkk!”

“Run for your lives!”

Yongyong’s landing brought a terrible tremor to the ground, and as it completely covered the sky by just standing, everyone’s heart sank. Terror drained the color from their faces. Dead, I’m dead, they thought. They were frozen in place from shock and couldn’t even think of escaping. No one could muster up the courage to run away. Just then, the moonlight shed light on its appearance, and everyone rubbed their eyes in shock.

“Eh…?”

Why is the Bone Dragon dressed in a tux? And what was that bow tie around its neck? Was it a trend among dragons now to eat people while wearing a tux? All sorts of outrageous thoughts dominated their brains when something entirely uncalled for took place.

“My Bone Dragon, the ultimate weapon of destruction and the guardian of the House of Magentano. My faithful servant. Don’t shock everyone here.”

It was Queen Magentano, and she was striding toward the Bone Dragon with only a sword around her. What was more, the sword was still in its sheath, and her gait was so relaxed and confident, as though she was walking toward her servant.

Everyone thought among themselves that this was it for her. This was the end for Queen Magentano, an unwed and childless monarch who had no one to pass down the throne to.

But something strange took place: nothing happened. The Bone Dragon didn’t stomp and kill her, which was something everyone expected to happen. Instead, the dragon lowered its head at her in the utmost polite and careful manner, like a loyal dog bringing its head forward to its master. The dragon lowered its body, propping its jaw against the ground and giving its forehead to the queen, who drew her sword. She then touched the dragon’s temples for a second with it as if she was softly tapping them or like she would to a knight’s shoulder at their ordination.

“I am aware of your feelings,” Queen Magentano said solemnly. “I am aware how you struggled with a sense of guilt and accountability for your commotion last time. I only ask that you pay for your wrongdoing by protecting us instead of writhing in guilt.”

Clack! The Bone Dragon nodded its giant skull. Her mouth formed a faint smile.

“Then, as a proud subject of the House of Magentano and special host of tonight’s banquet, can you provide assurance to the guests?”

Creak! Yongyong nodded once again, and its gaze flickered over to the guests, who all flinched when their eyes met. They couldn’t wrap their heads around what was happening. They wondered if they were having a weird, eccentric dream and gulped dryly, feeling delusional and bewitched at the same time.

Then Yongyong stood up and brought something out from its tux. Giant signs.

[I won’t hurt you.]

Absolute silence fell over the crowd. Yongyong brought out the next sign.

[I won’t bite you.]

Flip!

[I won’t hit you.]

Everyone was relieved to see that. Even so, they feared that a simple sneeze from the dragon would make them die from pain. As they thought so, Yongyong flipped to the final sign.

[The Bone Dragon, Yongyong, is your friend. ♡ ]

As silence heavily fell over the place, the Bone Dragon closed the signs and made a heart sign with its forelegs. Then it sat back down like a dog that was ordered to sit, its buttocks against the floor and looking up. It was only then that the people started to make sense of the situation.

“Haha, ha! Hahaha…”

“That is… Ha… This is… Hahaha.”

The heavy silence started to wear off as they started to realize what happened. The Bone Dragon wasn’t here to invade the banquet. It was just an attendee, and what was more, it came at the order of Queen Magentano. So, in other words…

Queen Magentano really did… suppress the Bone Dragon! Is that how it became a follower of hers?

Everyone had jeered at the queen for her incompetent response to the previous commotion and the Bone Dragon. They gloated in joy and even refused to believe her when she refuted that the Bone Dragon had been taken care of after a fierce battle. But no one trusted her and dismissed it as nothing but a pathetic bluff and an excuse to save face. Everyone had regarded her claim as a lie…

But she was telling the truth. She… really suppressed the dragon and took it under her command.

Fright filled their eyes at the realization.

This is nuts! She brought in the Bone Dragon as one of her followers!

What this meant was clear. The Bone Dragon, which was stronger than normal dragons, had been tamed and joined her forces.

There is no country now in the Lorasian Continent that can fight alone against the Magentano Kingdom.

Yes, this moment marked the birth of the Magentano Kingdom as the most powerful kingdom on the continent. They were untouchable.

Gulp. The diplomats swallowed dryly, and a host of thoughts filled their heads, and their eyes moved busily. The expression of mockery was completely gone from their faces. Instead of mockery, there was only a sense of alarm and urgency. They nervously thought about sending a message to their home countries as soon as possible to prevent getting into any conflicts with the House of Magentano at all costs. Seeing this, Lloyd, in the corner of the banquet, grinned as he watched.

Whew. I can almost hear how quickly their eyes are widening.

A burst of laughter escaped Lloyd as his gaze traveled across the dismayed diplomats that used to mock the Royal House of Magentano and the queen. This confirmed something for him. Today’s plan was a victory. Now, Queen Magentano only had to fulfill her promise and make a declaration. Then all plans would be finalized.

Sometime later, Queen Magentano’s gaze swept over the guests at the banquet. She was now the queen of the most powerful kingdom in the Lorasian Continent. She was the Iron Fist Queen, Alicia Termina Magentano.

And she declared, “I hereby announce to everyone gathered at this place.” She then pointed at Lloyd, which immediately attracted attention and caused everyone to focus on him.
