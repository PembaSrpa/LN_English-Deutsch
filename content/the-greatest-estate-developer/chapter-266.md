# Mermaids Love Jjimjilbangs (5)

---

Addiction. A state of being so intensely into something that you couldn’t stop doing whatever that was. It made quitting extremely difficult. A case in point was smoking, which most people started by taking a drag of a cigarette out of curiosity until it developed into a full-blown addiction that they couldn’t stop. In an attempt to take care of their health and quit, they would try the nicotine replacement items, such as gums and inhalers, until those became yet another addiction, this time only more expensive. There were also cryptocurrencies and stocks, wherein people just couldn’t wrench their gaze away from their phones, even during meals, because market prices might shoot up. Lastly, there were video and PC games. After three days of not playing, an addict would start seeing the summoner’s rift in their dreams. All these examples highlighted what addiction did to people. And the Mermaid Kingdom was no exception.

“Golly, my entire body is sore,” grumbled one granny mermaid as she tapped her back with her ripped forearm. Each tap produced a loud thudding noise, much like hitting a drum. A look of dissatisfaction appeared on her face. Her back was in a great deal of pain.

Whew… I did too many dolphin kicks back when I was young! Tsk, tsk.

A wave of regret washed over the old mermaid. There was a time when her skin was tight, her muscles were firm, and she could ram into emperor whales unharmed. She mistakenly thought that her muscles would last forever and her firm, robust body would always remain strong for tens and thousands of years. But over the years, she learned that wasn’t the case. Her entire body ached as soon as she hit 60 due to the strain that her gigantic muscles placed on her joints. And now, she was faced with a hellish case of arthritis.

“That sauna was very relaxing,” she remarked with a tinge of nostalgia in her voice. “Don’t you agree, honey?”

“Hohoho,” her husband laughed boisterously. “Well, I was happy to not hear your nagging while you were at the jjimjilbang.”

“Dear?” she growled and shot a look at him.

“Hmm?” replied her husband innocently.

“Are you tired of living with me?” Her eyes held a glint of anger.

“Hmm! Hmm! Ehem!” The grandpa merman hurriedly looked away, sensing danger. This deepened the wrinkles on the grandma mermaid’s face.

Whew. I wonder if there is any way the both of us could use a sauna one way or another.

She was wistful. She was happy when she was relaxing at the jjimjilbang. Her sickening joint aches could be forgotten there. But not anymore, to her utmost displeasure.

This is all because of that cheap, cold-hearted land human!

The human that constructed and ran the jjimjilbang. What was his name? Lloyd Frontera? The more her thoughts dwelled on him, the more critical she became of him.

Of course, the jjimjilbang can’t be free. He needs to receive a fee. Of course. But why does it have to be the labor of all things?! Tsk, tsk!

She’d have understood if he wanted pearls or precious clam shells. But no. The payment had to come in the form of labor hours for that human Lloyd. Otherwise, the jjimjilbang was off-limits. That was the reason why she had ripped the seaweed paper containing the terms and conditions of membership and stomped out of the jjimjilbang, foaming at her mouth. But now? Just within a few days, she began to regret her decision.

I thought I could live without it.

She was confident she’d forget the jjimjilbang after a few days and easily move on with life. But now, it turned out that wasn’t the case. Instead of forgetting it, it kept coming back to her. Its vacancy left a hole in her heart that she couldn’t live with.

This won’t do.

The granny mermaid jumped to her feet.

“Honey,” she called, “This won’t do. Stay here for a second.”

“Eh? Where are you going?”

“To the jjimjilbang.”

She herself was unsure what she’d do by going there. However, it didn’t feel right just to stay stuck at home while massaging her aching joints.

But, of course, I am not going to go use the facility and be pulled into his game.

She would just take a quick look around. That was what she’d do. There might be other merpeople like her in the same agony. Those who might be swimming around the jjimjilbang in remorse and regret like her.

I’ll just take a small peek. That’s all.

The granny mermaid was convinced that there would be no one at the jjimjilbang. There was no reason for anyone to be hovering nearby. No one in the kingdom would be as foolish as her. That was what she thought until…

What is this…

Suddenly, she felt a shaft of numbness. It turned out the jjimjilbang area was far from quiet. It was bustling with merpeople! So many merpeople were there! What was more…

“Oh my! Is that you, dear? You’re finally here!” Another granny mermaid, who was passing by with many others, beamed when she saw the old mermaid that had just arrived. They waved their fins, approached her, and rapped her shoulders. They were her friends, who were only a few years younger and would hang out with her often.

“What on earth are you all doing here?” inquired the wistful granny, her voice sharp and edgy.

“Why else?” One of them remarked. “We are here to enjoy the sauna.”

The muscles on her face tensed. “Since when have you been coming here?”

“Us? We’ve been coming here since yesterday.”

The wistful granny mermaid froze, and she didn’t respond.

“Dear?”

“You ungrateful and disloyal bunch of mermaids,” she snarled at last before wheeling around to leave.

“There, there, my dear,” one of them grabbed her. “No need to get upset. Come over here. I will massage your back. No need to pout. It will only leave wrinkles on your pretty lips. Okay?”

With that, the rest of the younger mermaids cackled and chortled as they pushed the angry granny mermaid’s back. The latter feigned her reluctance to enter by grumbling to the group, but her lips were faintly rolled upward. And with that, she processed her membership at lightning speed. Lloyd, who was manning the counter, beamed, his rewarding smile as clear and bright as a jam-packed portion of salted pollack roe.

Mwahaha. Everything is going according to plan.

In no exaggeration, it was a complete success. Lloyd cast his satisfying gaze at the table, which was piled up with a thick pile of sign-up scrolls. And his gaze then moved to the inside of the facility. It was packed with merpeople. Not a single room was vacant. The red clay room. The seawater room and sandy beach room. The extreme bungee-jump room. The merpeople were even competing to get their place. A similar sight to what he saw during the opening event for the jjimjilbang. No. There was one small thing that changed.

“Yo, Javier,” called Lloyd as he tapped the pile of scrolls.

“Yes,” said Javier, who was standing by Lloyd’s side beside the counter.

“Why don’t you go around and check the jjimjilbang rooms?”

“Check what, Master Lloyd?”

“The luminescent coral.” Lloyd’s eyes shone acutely. “There should be a few merpeople who’ve stayed here for six hours now. The luminescence on the coral around their wrist should be faded by now. Ask them if they’re going to charge it or go back home for today.”

“I see. But…” There was a heaviness in his hesitant voice.

“What’s up?”

“Do you plan on using me as a clerk now?”

“Yes.” It took him less than a second to respond.

“…”

“Look at you,” said Lloyd as he amusingly pointed his finger at Javier. “There, you’re doing it again. You’re glaring at me. Come on,” said Lloyd in an almost entreating tone. “It’s just for now. Do you see this? I’m writing a job posting. Free jjimjilbang entry while on call. Merpeople will pounce at this opportunity. Sure, it’ll take time for me to do interviews and look for the most diligent merperson out of the pool. However, I’ll pick someone soon. You got nothing to worry about.”

“Whew… I see, Master Lloyd.”

Javier sighed and started to move around, which caused an eruption of cheers from the merpeople watching him. Everyone had a wristband that contained a lump of glowing, deep-sea coral. It was designed to glow for exactly six hours, and the merpeople were given two choices when the light went out. They could extend their stay for six more hours and fill up the daily 12-hour limit or leave the facility. Those who chose the latter were asked to record their time of stay in their membership log as they left the facility to calculate the number of hours they would need to work.

“Thank you very much for visiting the deep-sea cave jjimjilbang!” shouted Lloyd.

“Not at all.” A mermaid stopped when she heard him and approached Lloyd. “Um, does this mean that I have to do my assigned work within 48 hours, just as the terms and conditions state?”

“Indeed,” Lloyd replied. “That’s exactly right.” She was a young mermaid who had just completed her first-ever sauna visit. Lloyd flashed a big smile at her. “The work site will be announced tomorrow morning, and it will be posted on the bulletin board at the jjimjilbang entrance. You can complete your labor hours according to what is written there.”

“Is there anything I should prepare in advance?” she inquired, her voice sounding anxious.

“All you need to bring is your strong body.”

Lloyd’s answer must have taken her by surprise. “My strong… body?” Her voice trembled.

“Yes. Is there anything else you need?”

“No… I understand now.”

Lloyd smiled benevolently, but the young mermaid felt a clawing sense of nervousness. The following day, the excavation began.

“Ehem…!” Lloyd cleared his throat to have attention drawn to himself. “Thank you very much for gathering here to pay for the jjimjilbang. As I have introduced myself before, I am Lloyd Frontera, the owner of the jjimjilbang. But today, I will be your supervisor who oversees the excavation site.”

Among about 120 merpeople gathered at the site in the early morning, one raised their hand.

“Excuse me? Excavation? What do you mean?”

“Exactly as it sounds,” Lloyd explained, “From now on, we will be shoveling to find something in the bottom of the sea.”

“Shoveling?!” the merperson repeated in shock.

“Yes, shoveling.” Lloyd nonchalantly nodded before holding up one of the clam-shell shovels he had prepared by piles in advance. They weren’t the typical shovels used at construction sites on land. They looked like those used for cleaning snow.

“This is the shovel you will be using today,” continued Lloyd, “To dig up the mud in the area I designate for all of you.”

“How will we know where to dig?” One of the gatherers inquired.

“No worries. Look over there.” Lloyd then pointed at one area.

Bzzt! A glowing straight blue line started to appear, following Lloyd’s fingers until each met and connected with one another. About 0.6 miles long and wide, it soon formed a gigantic square. This was the work of the Construction Guideline, one of the optional skills Lloyd had. The merpeople watched in sheer amazement, their eyes growing wide, and wondered if this human was a magician. They murmured among themselves and looked at each other until Lloyd’s voice interrupted them.

“Now, you can see the line I just drew for you at the bottom. Shovel the mud within the square area and get it clean. Oh, but please be careful not to break the shovel. And you must log the hours you worked. Don’t forget. If you forget to log your hours, all your hard work will be good for nothing.”

Gulp. Those last words made the merpeople tense up. Everyone resolved to log their hours of work on the card. They had to log their hours no matter what. And with that, the excavation to find the site of the jewel began.

“Okay, let’s get started!” announced Lloyd with a clap.

“Wooo!!!”

120 merpeople took action with a clam shovel in their hands. Their monstrous physique gave them a tremendous aptitude for shoveling. They swam at a speed that even dolphins couldn’t keep up with. And just like that, they pierced through the mud.

Crack! The clam shovels dug deep into the seabed, and it went in deep, much like how a bull would graze the land.

“Gwoahhh!”

Boooom! When five merpeople scratched the bottom first, a giant hole, 20 inches deep and 9.8 feet wide, appeared. Then the next five followed right away.

“Gwoahhh!”

Boooom! They dug into the same hole one more time. It went in deeper this time. Then another five came to work on the same hole. This process was repeated, the mud turning over at a marvelous speed. Lloyd found himself clenching his fists at the exhilarating sight.

This is nuts! This is totally nuts!

Their shoveling speed went beyond his imagination. Lloyd was always aware of their strength and stamina, but seeing them himself, it turned out they were better. It was like having 120 bulldozers turn over and work on the earth. Of course, Lloyd didn’t just sit back and watch while everything was unfolding.

Surveying!

[Scanning will begin.]

Bzzt!

Lloyd activated his surveying skill toward the seabed the merpeople dug, and it allowed him to look 16 feet underground.

Great. I can see deeper than before.

Since the merpeople were ridding of the thick mud carpet, Lloyd was able to take a look beneath the mud. But he couldn’t find the jewel site right away.

“Okay!” shouted Lloyd. “Let’s work harder! You may be sweating from work today, but you will be sweating at the jjimjilbang tomorrow!”

“Wooo!!!” The merpeople worked more energetically at Lloyd’s encouragement. They dug, dug, and dug until their muscles began to feel fatigued. Naturally, their worn-out muscles started to fill their head with one thing.

Whew, this is tiring. Should I stop by at the jjimjilbang before heading home and relax for just a little bit?

Like that, most merpeople headed back to the jjimjilbang after work instead of heading home. And then they found themselves being welcomed by Lloyd, who was just back at the construction site to supervise the labor. But this time, he was before them as the owner of the jjimjilbang.

“My! Welcome, my dear customers!” beamed Lloyd with a delightful smile on his face.

The merpeople sensed something was wrong but soon pushed away the thought and decided to hit the bath and sauna pronto, relieving their pent-up fatigue after a day of labor. The next day, however, they had to sweat and work again to pay for enjoying the facility the previous day, only for the drudgery at the site to helplessly remind them of the relaxing bath and sauna. And so, they unwound at the jjimjilbang again, which only spelled more gruesome work. Work. Unwind. Work. Unwind. It was an endless cycle. The merpeople finally came to a realization.

What should I do? I can’t… free myself from this vicious cycle!

They relaxed in the jjimjilbang. They toiled until fatigue ushered them back into the facility again. And another toil was due. Yes. This was a vicious cycle, a drug that was impossible to stop! And just like that, while the merpeople were stuck in a cycle of working and going to the jjimjilbang, the construction site ran very smoothly under Lloyd’s command. The number of merpeople he pulled in through his plan increased, and their excavation range grew larger with each passing day. The range for Lloyd’s surveying also expanded just as much. And two months later…

Oh…!

At last, something caught his eye.
