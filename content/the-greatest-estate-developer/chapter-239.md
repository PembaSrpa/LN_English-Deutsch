# Noise Pollution Complaint (2)

---

“Grrr! Achoo!”

The Lava Giant gave a hard tremble, and it belched a terrible sneeze as it bent over. Its lava snot spewed in all directions and burned the land. Seeing that, Lloyd thought to himself…

Eh? Come on… It is so rude to sneeze without a mask… Wait, that’s not what’s important. Is the Lava Giant vulnerable to cold?

Lloyd received a feeling as if a bell tolled in his head with a clear ding. The Hell gate had been opened to provide an escape for Lloyd, but it had to be connected to the polar region out of all the places in the world of the living. However, this thankfully brought a gust of biting wind through the gate and caused the Lava Giant to pitifully shiver in front of it. It hugged around its arms in pity, constantly sneezing and slouching its neck.

“Grr! Achoo! Grr! Ichoo! Grr! Watchoo!”

At the sight, Lloyd became curious. But curiosity soon turned into suspicion, which evolved into a possibility. Then that started to shine in his head as a vague hope.

It could work. Let’s first check it out!

Lloyd held up the shovel in Ggoming’s saddle and activated his two circles lightly. Causing them to collide, he stretched out a shovel at the same time. Kaboom!

The mana blast jetted out dozens of feet from the shovel and hit one shoulder of the shivering Lava Giant. Thwack!

“Grr!”

The Lava Giant shot a glare at him. Lloyd immediately raised Ggoming’s reins in fear and soared high in the air to dodge the giant’s attack. Then he observed the giant’s shoulder that was hit with his mana blast. 

Please… I hope my guess is right.

Desire and hope filled Lloyd’s eyes. If he was right, he really believed that he could defeat the Lava Giant, and the railroad construction didn’t have to be abandoned. All of his efforts poured into this place didn’t have to go down the drain.

So, please!

Lloyd’s desire-filled gaze pricked the shoulders of the giant. Meanwhile, its shoulder slowly started to regenerate.

“Grr!”

Reminiscent of boiling curry, its shoulder was made of bubbling lava. The hole, which was about 25 inches wide and 9.8 feet deep, was easily restored despite the hit from the mana blast. But the restoration speed was…

It has gotten slower!

Lloyd’s eyes glimmered. He was certain. The speed had slowed down. Though it was just a small hole, way smaller than the damage taken by Javier’s blow, the restoration took place slower than the first one. It was clear. Lloyd thought he knew why.

The wind from the polar region has cooled its body down.

That was the reason. It was actually a simple principle. It was easy to think of it as a boiling pot of curry. Hot curry was impossible to bore a hole or slice into when it was boiling. The holes would be easily filled again regardless of how many times you sliced or stabbed it.

But what would happen if the curry was cold?

It thickened. Solidified. Anybody who refrigerated curry in a plastic container before would easily understand what was happening.

Hardened curry can be sliced with a knife or bore a hole!

The lava appeared to be the same. Lloyd figured that once the temperature dropped and the lava solidified, the giant wouldn’t be able to quickly restore itself like now.

I can do it. This is doable. How lucky am I!

Gliding through the gust of biting wind that started to swirl through Hell’s sky, Lloyd stared at the Hell gate with eyes full of tears.

Ah, my good-luck charm!

At this moment, the Hell gate looked like the most precious piece of treasure in his eyes. He had just opened it in order to escape. Thus, it never occurred to him that it would connect to the polar region and grant him hope for victory.

“Okay, time to knuckle down! Ggoming!” called Lloyd.

“Ggoming!” exclaimed Ggoming.

“Let’s get down first!”

“Ggomiming!”

Ggoming flew lower against the wind, and just when he thought he was in Javier’s earshot, Lloyd shouted, “Javier!”

Javier responded to the shout by throwing a glance at Lloyd about 200 yards away.

“There! Above you! Do you see the Hell gate in the air?”

Javier nodded. Infusing his voice with more mana, Lloyd boomed, “There! The cold air is coming out now! The giant is growing cold because of that! I think! We can defeat it!”

Javier nodded again. He, too, had been feeling the same way.

It’s all because of the Hell gate Master Lloyd has opened up.

Javier saw the Lava Giant shivering against the cold wind, and it was clear that the freezing wind was the cause as Lloyd shouted to him just then. In that case, the giant wouldn’t be able to heal as fast as it did a while ago.

I must play my part here without any mistakes.

Javier remembered how Lloyd had come to Hell because of his trust in him. So, he could not let Master Lloyd down. He had to get the job done, even if it was dangerous. Javier believed that was his primary duty while escorting his young master through Hell, to begin with.

Grab! Veins bulged on the back of his hand that grabbed the sword. From that moment, Javier’s offense started to become fiercer.

Slash! Without any mercy or hesitation, with nothing but conviction, Javier slashed and slayed on repeat. With only one target in mind, his sword cut, slashed, and sliced into every part of the Lava Giant’s body.

“Grrr! Achoo! Grrr! Grr!”

The Lava Giant had been flabbergasted by the sudden freezing coldness. And now, the giant was at a loss in front of Javier’s attacks which were growing more brutal by the second. Its whole body was sliced and slashed by the aura, but it regenerated the wounds nonetheless. Its regeneration speed, however, gradually slowed down as the cold winds cooled its temperature. Eventually, the giant realized that it could die if the current situation continued. It didn’t think it would remain intact if the silver-haired knight struck down on its cold and hardened body. As such, it arrived at one conclusion.

“See you next time! Achoo! Grrrr!”

The giant wheeled around as it received dozens of aura slashes, openly exposing its back to Javier. And it started to run as it limped.

“Eh? Could it be?!” Lloyd mumbled as he watched the scene from the sky. Just then, he was gripped with an ominous feeling. The feeling brewed in him, strong and thick like a broth that had been cooking for ten years And soon, his premonition turned into reality.“

Stomp! Stomp! Stomp!

"That bastard! It’s getting away!” Lloyd shrieked. And it really was. The Lava Giant was bolting away as the situation was becoming unfavorable for it.

What kind of monster does that?

Lloyd almost grabbed the back of his neck in stress. How could he not? That monster appeared out of the blue and ransacked the whole construction scene and ignored Lloyd’s effort to stop it. Yet, now it was running away right when it was put in a slightly disadvantageous position?

“Don’t lose it!” Lloyd’s shout grew more hurried, but it wasn’t simply because he was nettled by the giant’s opportunistic behavior.

If we lose the giant now, the construction is really over!

Lloyd was certain of it. If he lost the Lava Giant now and failed to stop it once and for all, he may never get a chance to catch it again in the future.

I don’t know about the others, but I’m sure of that. Without a doubt. I’ll never get another opportunity to kill it, if not now!

The reason was simple. The Hell gate that he had just opened up could only bring in a limited amount of wind. Those sharp winds couldn’t go very far out.

Of course. The wind may be freezing cold, but it’s coming through a hole only a few dozen feet wide. But Hell is so vast compared to that!

To analogize, this was the same as installing an air conditioner only good for a small room in a giant mansion. In that case, it’s only cool at the very front of the air conditioner. It quickly gets hot once you move away from it by even a little.

Just then, Lloyd recalled his life in South Korea, particularly the searing days of summer. It went without saying that his room didn’t have an air conditioner. It’d be a luxury for him, given that his room was the oldest and cheapest one in the market. And so, on days when the heat was deadly and unbearable, he used to head to nearby banks and residence centers to get some cool air for free.

But it wasn’t a heavenly place. The people there used the lowest setting on the air conditioner to reduce their electricity bill. How cheap!

The bank was large, but the air conditioner was quite weak. And so, Lloyd had to stay right in front of the air conditioner to get the cool air. Thanks to this valuable experience, he was able to see through the Lava Giant right away.

It knows that the cold air will disappear as soon as it moves away from this area!

The giant knew that it had to get away from this place, far from the Hell gate until the cold winds became warm again. Soon afterward, its body would run hot again and wouldn’t receive any damage from being attacked. Its invincible body would be back.

I’m sure that is the giant’s line of thought. But what if things do develop according to its plan? I’ll neither be able to kill it nor put a stop to it. The construction project extends outside this area after all. The railroad stretches 86 miles long!

In other words, if it slipped away today and decided in the future to continue disrupting construction, there was a chance for the giant to wreak havoc at any time when it was outside the reach of the winds coming from the Hell gate. This meant that he and Javier’s efforts to stop it would be meaningless.

I can open the Hell gate only once. Nowhere else. I can keep this gate opened like this, but I can’t open additional gates.

And so, the Lava Giant must be caught here. The first step in achieving that goal was stopping it from running. “Get it!” Lloyd shouted with a pleading heart. “Attack its legs first!”

Lloyd picked up the shovel and urged Ggoming to fly. From that moment, Lloyd launched a series of attacks on the Lava Giant in the sky while Javier did so from the ground. Lloyd ceaselessly used the triple mana blast, striking the giant’s knees and shins. Javier joined his efforts and slashed and swung down on its lower torso over and over again. Each time it was attacked, blood-like lava dripped and burst out from its two legs. And soon, it started to limp from losing heat, which slowed down its regeneration. But…

“Grr! Grrr!”

Lava Giant couldn’t be stopped. Even when its limping grew much more evident and dragging more explicit, the Lava Giant moved its two feet without ever stopping, each big step taking itself far away from the Hell gate. At the same time, Lloyd’s face darkened.

Shoot!

The giant couldn’t be resisted. It couldn’t be pinned to the ground. Even with the bluster of freezing wind from the polar region, it was impossible to freeze the inside of that huge lava lump. Only the outside was being cooled, so countless thrashings and piercings and slayings had no effect. Just some delay, but the regeneration didn’t stop. Realizing that the corners of Lloyd’s eyes teared up a little.

The giant is too big. I need a giant, a big one, to stop it with force in moments like these. That would be just perfect. Oh, I wish I had Bibeong with me!

A bitter pang of regret filled Lloyd’s heart as he thought that things would be different if Bibeong was here.

 Bibeong is 328 feet tall. He’s also chubby and heavy

Though the Lava Giant was twice bigger than him, it seemed plausible for Bibeong to hang on its legs and drag it down when it had such a bad limp. But that was a meaningless fantasy, and he knew that.

I shouldn’t have lent Bibeong to the queen for free. You idiot!

If he could, Lloyd wanted to go back to four months ago, clutch his collar, and shout at himself to either keep Bibeong or ask for a high rental fee. In particular, to not give anything for free even family members didn’t lend each other things for free. Lloyd wanted to yell into the ears of his past self at the top of his lungs.

Dammit. Dammit!

The construction seemed to end with a debacle because he had thoughtlessly lent Bibeong like a fool. Now, the strand of hope that had appeared in front of his eyes was ripped apart. His three months’ worth of efforts were about to go down the drain. He was about to fail in rescuing the dragon’s soul and obtaining the Dragon King’s support.

Why did you have to lend him for free, Lloyd?!

His eyes were wet now because he regretted his decision too sordidly. Meanwhile, the Lava Giant continued to make a run, still limping. Even though Javier was still throwing auras with greater ferocity, the Lava Giant didn’t cease its run.

“Ha…”

Lloyd had seen a faint ray of hope when the Hell gate opened with a flurry of cold wind. But now, there really was no other method. He had to admit that. He let out a sigh.

Lloyd stared at the back of the Lava Giant, feeling helpless and empty like when someone else snatched the last Buy 1 Get 1 Free noodle pack left in the supermarket. And so, it was unbeknownst to Lloyd that he held another solution, a hopeful one. In the Arctic Sea not so far off from the Hell gate, the Bone Dragon, the youngest recruit in the Skeleton Corps, was there. It was there, dutifully following Lloyd’s order to stay low in the Arctic Sea when it sensed Lloyd’s presence. Clack!

It’s Lloyd! This is the smell of Master Lloyd!

Splash! Like a puppy that smelled the owner after a long time, the Bone Dragon was tickled pink. And it threw its head out of the water with a splash and discovered the Hell gate overhead.
