# A Lucky Accident (2)

---

Cesare Di Bergamo, a low-ranking royal magician, was born with a silver spoon in his mouth. Everything used to go well for him. He was born into the family of Marquess Bergamo, who had raised top royal magicians for several generations. He was the fifth son of the family and grew up with host privileges around him. As the rightful son of a noble family set to lead the kingdom and manage many under him, Cesare received an elite education, and his life lacked nothing. As a result, when he became ten, he almost mastered all the basic theories of magic, which earned him the title of a genius.

That was when it hit Cesare. He was a special being. A dignified and important person. He deserved special treatment from others. From then on, he didn’t view anyone below him as a human being. They were merely a bunch of subservient vermin, bugs that would lick his shoes at his command

With that mindset, he enrolled in the royal academy. He continued to receive special treatment there. After all, he was the fifth son of a family that raised top royal magicians. Moreover, they were distantly related to the imperial family. His sixth grandmother had been a royal princess, which meant that divine royal blood flowed in him. Cesare was untouchable in school. The students, housemasters, and professors didn’t dare to treat him badly, and even the president of the academy studied the same year with his father. With power on his shoulder that gave him all sorts of privileges, he ruled the roost in the academy. It only took one word from him to get something done. His grade, the way he was treated. Everything. This confirmed something in him again. He was special. Even in a place where noble sons and daughters came to study, he was special. Anyone below him deserved to be treated like vermin.

As such, Cesare pestered the sons of low-ranking nobles. He had no grand reasons for doing so. It was simply amusing to watch them beg and plead before his nose. And Julian Frontera amused him the most. The petite fellow from a remote eastern province provided him with the greatest entertainment. Unlike the others, who never met him in the eyes after one bullying, Julian’s eyes remained alive and spirited. Whatever harassment the vermin received, the next day, he shot back with the same clarity in his eyes. Furthermore, he received good grades at every chance he was given as if he wasn’t bullied. To Cesare, Julian was an amusing toy worth destroying to pieces, and so he decided to dial up his bullying by ordering the other lowly students to harass Julian, saying they have to do so until he dies. A hick from a province shouldn’t be getting good grades. A man of low birth was better suited to lick his shoes in subservience. The bullying was endless. However, then something happened.

Lloyd Frontera…

The name was stuck in Cesare’s head. Lloyd Frontera. The older brother of Julian, his toy. Lloyd ruined everything. After taking Julian out of the academy and buying the trust of the queen by building that weird suspension bridge, the man reported everything to her at the completion event.

He told the queen about Julian’s bullies. How those bullies defiled the fair and honorable spirit of the academy. With that came Cesare’s downfall. He was the leader of the group of students Lloyd had reported to the queen. The result of this event was merciless. Enraged, the queen expelled Cesare from the academy, and he was forced to keep a low profile in his manor for two years. But those two years felt more like a barless prison.

But what really hurt him was the loss of reputation from high society. He became the black sheep. Even though his status blocked him from more serious punishment, torture, or execution, his honor and future were taken away from him. He was out of the queen’s favor. He was stripped of the chance to become a public official and ended up as a laughingstock among the nobles. Even his family looked down on him; his parents never spoke a word to him since the incident. And his brothers and sisters tossed repulsive gazes at him as though he was the embarrassment of the family. Even the servants and maids gossiped behind his back.

How dare you! You’re nothing but vermin!

Cesare felt indignant. The treatment he was receiving was unfair. Even when his father pleaded to the queen on his knees for forgiveness, which secured him a low-ranking job as a royal magician, Cesare was terribly embittered about the whole thing. He felt cheated. He was supposed to be special.

Of course! I’m more capable than other magicians! More noble in blood! Yet what? A low-ranking magician?

It was complete bogus. With his status and power, it was only right for him to be placed as the second most powerful magician from the start. But what happened to Lloyd, who destroyed his life by telling on him to the queen? He was having a streak of success, unbefitting his status!

A meritorious retainer?!

Lloyd was even appointed as a meritorious retainer for suppressing the Bone Dragon. No words came out when Cesare heard the news. No. Even now, he was left speechless.

“Everything all good today?”

It was Lloyd who spoke. Cesare shut his mouth and simply glared at Lloyd with a straight face. Lloyd shrugged back with a faint grin.

“Having a bad morning, I guess? Well, loosen up, Have a good day today,” greeted Lloyd nonchalantly.

Cesare couldn’t tell if the latter was being brazen or friendly as he greeted him. Lloyd was about to leave the operation room for the Heart of Winter when Cesare spoke as he looked at Lloyd’s back.

“Wait, hold on,” Quite surprised that he called Lloyd, he impulsively asked, “Do you not know who I am?”

Lloyd turned and stared at Cesare. “I know who you are.”

“You do?” Cesare asked in a rude tone.

“Yes.” Lloyd nodded, and in a matter-of-fact voice, he added, “You’re a magician from the palace who manages the Heart of Winter. Hahaha.”

“…”

“Thanks to you, the construction is going smoothly. The Heart of Winter is stably providing cold air. Once this construction is finished, why don’t we have dinner together with everyone else? Have a good day today, then.”

Lloyd innocently greeted him farewell and left the room. Now, Cesare was left alone in the room. There was a flush of uncontrollable rage and shame on his face.

How dare he… not recognize me?

Cesare was utterly dumbfounded. He thought Lloyd would remember and berate him for bullying Julian. But this wasn’t the case.

He doesn’t remember me. It’s all because my life has been ruined, and I’m just a low-ranking magician. On the other hand, things are going superbly for him as a meritorious retainer of the imperial family. How dare he… a mere vermin!

Lloyd did not remember. Even after destroying Cesare’s life, he didn’t recognize him. And as though that was not enough, Lloyd was his superior.

He attained the trust of the queen and became the supervisor for the repair construction of the palace… And I’m here rotting, doing what he tells me to do. Why? Why is that?

Cesare couldn’t wrap his head around it. He was a special person. Someone far too dignified to be rotting down here. He couldn’t erase the thought that something was going wrong with this world. He couldn’t stop thinking that his life was over.

One day, two days, and soon, five days later. His rancor and disappointment increased sizably. And finally, on the day the upward vertical cave construction started, which was the same morning he received another scornful glance from his brothers, Cesare made a decision as he came to work.

I should die.

But not alone. He’d take Lloyd Frontera with him to Hell and ruin his project, even tainting his reputation in the process. He came to work with such a dark resolution in mind and sought for an opportunity all morning and afternoon. And finally, he made his move.

“Eup…?”

Cesare brought out his dagger, plunged it into the back of his colleague in the room, and covered his mouth. His colleague fell to the floor.

I don’t have time.

Six royal knights were outside. Even though they were guarding outside and couldn’t hear the noise of his colleague falling because the doors were thick, it would be only a matter of time for them to notice that something was going on inside.

Right now.

Cesare chanted to himself his malicious goal of killing everyone, Lloyd Frontera in particular, and stood before the chest that contained the Heart of Winter. The chest required two royal magicians’ magic power to control it, but this wasn’t an impossible task for him. Although he was only one person without his colleague, Cesare was a talented magician and could somewhat control it on his own.

Argh…!

Cesare upped the capacity of the chest as much as he could. Beyond its limit. To the point of damaging the chest. There was an immediate reaction.

Whoosh…! The chest made an eerie noise as frost swept over the chest at lightning speed. And Cesare, who was standing in front of it, received the blow without exception.

“…!”

Frigid air, now at its lowest possible temperature, swept his whole body, but he tried to endure it. He couldn’t even let out a scream. A few seconds. That was all he could endure in the face of the Heart of Winter that released its maximally frigid air. And finally, a crack appeared in the chest. Cesare smiled. He realized his time had come.

“What is going on here?!”

The knights shouted as they kicked the door open and entered upon sensing suspicious activity inside. At that moment, the chest went off, causing a chilling explosion in the area.

♣️

Baaang!

“Eh…?”

Lloyd abruptly raised his head when he sensed a strange vibration. He had been excavating the vertical cave with an intense focus on his work as it was happening from the bottom up unlike before.

What was that?

It had come from somewhere nearby. Cocking his head to the side, Lloyd judged from the strength and echo of the vibration that it came from the cave’s exit.

It was quite strong. But this shouldn’t be happening near the exit area. I installed enough support for it, and the concrete is well-plastered. I inspected everything. Could it be the Rock Burst phenomenon? No. Judging by the state of the ground, there’s no way.

Lloyd shook his head. The Rock Burst phenomenon referred to when rocks burst out from the sides of a tunnel due to the pressure inside it.

It’s similar to how a pimple pops when you squeeze it.

This was a phenomenon that actually took place in Europe during the construction of tunnels in the Alps. Here, rocks with a diameter of up to seven feet were said to have burst out from the tunnel’s walls.

But it’s not possible here… It can’t happen down here. Then what was that noise?

The noise gnawed at Lloyd, more so because he was in a tunnel, which could crumble even at a tiny crack or sink. Everyone would be in danger if that happened.

Something like that happened when I built the mine for bituminous coal. I was locked inside with Javier when I blasted the methane gas to kill the ants.

But today, he wasn’t with Javier. The latter was at the palace after being summoned by the queen, just like any other time. He was currently teaching his sword fighting skills as a grandmaster to the queen.

So, it’ll be a big problem if something happens when he isn’t around.

Lloyd raised his hand, thinking he ought to check it out, and was about to send a stop signal to the team when…

Whoosh… Cold air traveled from the exit through the tunnel.

Hmm?

Lloyd flinched. Something was off.

What? Why is there cold air blowing down here?

The wind was cold, like the one that blows in the early winter morning. The problem with it was that the wind was blowing into the underground tunnel. It was impossible for this to happen. It was supposed to be hotter down in the tunnel since it was underground.

“Everyone, stop! Stand by here!” The moment Lloyd realized how abnormal the cold wind was, he yelled at the laborers before hurrying toward the exit.

It’s strange. Really. I should check it out.

An ominous feeling crept in him. The strange tremor. The cold wind. As the bad feeling grew worse, his steps became more hurried. And the air grew colder as he sped more, turning into a biting wintry wind blowing in the Arctic area. By this time, Lloyd was already sprinting.

It looks like something happened with the Heart of Winter or the refrigerant supply pipes.

Even though Lloyd didn’t want to admit it, the frost on the tunnel walls and the biting wind scratching his skin confirmed his bad feeling. But he didn’t stop. Even when his body was covered with frost and when he could see his cold breath, Lloyd dashed toward the source of the freezing air. Something definitely happened to the Winter of Heart. Do nothing, and everyone down in the tunnel will freeze to death. Something must be done. But first, he wanted to check what was going on.

If something really happened to the Heart of Winter, and if that is what’s releasing the icy air… What should I do? Get Bangul to use her explosion through the tunnel? Would the heat storm dissipate the cold air? No. If I do that, the explosion pressure could kill them since it’s a confined space here.

Lloyd ran as he racked his brain for a solution. At last, he arrived at the room that stored the Heart of Winter, the site of the horrible explosion. It was just then.

Boom! Even before he had the time to see, the cold storm of the Ice Age swept him. Simultaneously, his title effect was activated.

Ding Dong!
