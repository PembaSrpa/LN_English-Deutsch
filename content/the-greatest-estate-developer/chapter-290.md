# Reckless Driving is Bad (1)

---

The Grassland of Wind and Stone was the central area of the Great Plain as it was the hometown of all the centaurs. There, residents witnessed a spectacular sight.

“I-It’s a red speeding ticket!” One of the young centaurs shouted. Everyone’s gaze immediately focused on one place. Then they saw.

“It really is. It’s really a red speeding ticket.”

“My goodness…”

“I never imagined I’d see one in my lifetime.”

“But it’s not on one of us but a human.”

“Maybe it’s fake?”

“No, that is not possible. Only the patrol commander protecting the outskirts of the Great Plain can issue that ticket.”

“Hold on,” one gasped. “Does that mean… that human galloped faster than the patrol commander?”

“What else could it mean?” A senile centaur replied.

Everyone gulped at it. And they took another glance at the human with a red ticket on his back. He wasn’t ordinary outside. He was a human outsider. And now, he was confidently striding across the village center, flashing a red speeding ticket. The goggled-eyed centaurs gaped as they beheld the scene. Lloyd, who was receiving the awe-filled attention, smiled.

This feels like I’ve become a celebrity.

It really was the case. At first, the centaurs were astonished by the appearance of an outsider. Then their astonishment grew when they saw the red speeding ticket on his back. After some chaotic, confusing, and doubtful moments, they realized that the ticket was real and gazed at Lloyd with admiration. Their manner was similar to the fangirls of VTS when they bumped into their idol.

Of course, it wasn’t easy to get this ticket.

Two days ago, Lloyd arrived at the entrance of the plane and was confronted by a patrol group. At that moment, he challenged them to a race by arguing that he was faster than them. The bold suggestion immediately brought the patrol leader to come forth. A drag race was held.

Seven seconds.

Seven seconds after they left the start line, Ppodong came in half a foot earlier than the patrol commander. Ppodong was equivalent to a vehicle with a V-8 5,000 ce natural engine, and the patrol commander burst with admiration toward Ppodong.

It looked like he was going to burst into tears right after he crossed the finish line.

Was it because he was indignant about his defeat? No. It was tears of overwhelming admiration, the kind only a speed-obsessive nut-job would show in the presence of a faster being. And thankfully, Lloyd was able to get a hold of a red speeding ticket.

“There is no insignia more glorious than this red speeding ticket. It was an honor to have galloped with you. May you have a safe and enjoyable stay here…”

And just like that, Lloyd acquired permission from the patrol commander. The speeding ticket was a pass that granted access to anywhere in the Centaurian territory. And the red ticket was the highest honor he could attain. It was quite literally a symbol of glory. This had much to do with the character of the centaurs.

They are wild horses racing through the plains.

And that was why they regarded galloping as the most crucial skill to have, and everyone yearned to be the fastest. Just like how South Korean society idolized one’s money-making capacity, the Centaurian society valued the ability to run fast above anything else. The fastest individual was regarded as the most capable, and the slowest individual received pitiful tongue clicks from others. These horse-humans were obsessed with speed.

So, the ability to rapidly start running and stopping is just a basic skill they learn. And their quick turns and boundless control of speed are something they learn when they’re young.

What would happen if the centaurs were born in South Korea? They’d overspeed nonstop and violently drive around, changing lanes without a signal until they eventually became an avid collector for speeding and traffic-light-violation tickets. Anyway, due to their propensity, Lloyd received a wide welcome from the five villages he passed through until he arrived at the territory of the Stone and Wind tribe.

I even met a tribal chief who pleaded with me to marry his daughter.

Lloyd had been shocked by the incident, only to be gobsmacked once again when the centaur lady wailed after seeing that Lloyd had two legs instead of four.

The heck with lookism!

Crack! Lloyd pushed his memories of the past two days away from his head. And he walked over to the centaurs who were gazing at him with respect and admiration.

“Um, I just have a question to ask,” said Lloyd in a careful voice.

“Yes! Ask anything!”

“Me! Ask me!”

“Mister! I can answer you better!”

At Lloyd’s question, three centaurs, who were each young, female, and middle-aged, battled to answer him first. Lloyd suppressed his smile. “Oh, yes, thank you. But I’m not asking for just one answer. Anyone who can give me an answer is welcome to.”

“Understood! What do you want to know?”

“Me! I’ll answer it for you!”

“Ehem! I said I can answer you properly!”

“Ahaha…” laughed Lloyd awkwardly. “Yes, thank you. The thing is, I’m looking for this stone.”

Flap! Lloyd brought out a piece of paper from his pocket. It was a detailed illustration of the Taupo he had drawn on his way over here.

“The size is about 26, 19, and 3.9 feet, respectively, in width, length, and height. And there’s a unique bump as you can see here at the end. Have you seen a stone like this one around here?”

“Eek! No, I didn’t! I’m sorry!”

“Waa! I want to see it too! I want to say my answer, too!”

“Shucks! I’ll go find it right away! Just wait here!”

Despite everyone’s enthusiastic response, nothing satisfied Lloyd.

Tsk. I suppose it’s been too long since the Taupo was brought here.

Lloyd smacked his lips in bitterness. It was like this at the Mermaid Kingdom as well. No one barely remembered the whereabouts of the Jewel of Truth because too much time had passed.

And it’s not like I can survey the whole area.

Lloyd looked around. There was a seemingly endless stretch of land everywhere he looked. Just like the name of the plain, this land was too vast and expansive. And so, he felt even more stuck.

No. It’s too early to tell. I should ask around some more.

Lloyd made the decision. He would continue to ask people around. He asked every centaur he met about the Taupo. And at last, thanks to his efforts, he was able to finally get the answer that interested him.

“Oh! I know what that is! Someone said that the tribal chief head knows all about the old stories!” a kid centaur hurriedly answered. And he pointed his foal horseshoe to the center of the tribe.

“The head of the tribal chief lives there! I will take you there!”

“Oh, sure. That’s kind of you.”

Lloyd then followed the child centaur. The house of the tribal chief head wasn’t anything spectacular. It was like the rest of the centaurs. A typical tent stable. But his appearance was far from typical.

“Welcome, a human with a red speeding ticket. I have been following the stirs among the tribe members.” His voice was grave and solemn. But his appearance was a complete contrast to the gravity and seriousness in his tone.

Damn.

Lloyd sucked in his breath as soon as he saw the head chief standing in front of him. Despite his senility, he was extremely muscular, his fur shaved up in a flashy manner. It made his skin look like a tattoo. No, to be more exact…

That’s it. The car stickers that car aficionados stick all over their four wheels. Those kinds of decorations. There are even patterns with fire, lightning bolts, and eagles, tigers, or skeletons and hearts! The whole collection, I say.

Without any exaggeration, the head chief’s body was a sketchbook of tattoos. From his lower horse torso to his upper human torso, tattoos covered his whole body, excluding his face and nape. And all the piercings in his ears, nose, and lips! The long hair that flowed down him like a hippie’s made him give off a free-spirited vibe.

He even got his horseshoe modified. It’s sharp and pointy. One kick from that, and I’m going to be diced like a pork cutlet.

In many ways, his appearance was extraordinary, befitting his status as the head chief of the speedy tribes. Thanks to that, Lloyd had to quickly manage his facial expression as he was greeted by the chief.

Hmm… Looks like he lived a wild life in his youth.

But it didn’t matter if he was one of the gang members racing around back in his days. Lloyd had a business to take care of. Clearing his throat, Lloyd politely said, “I am honored to be in the presence of the owner of the tribe of the great plain.”

“Haha. Not only do you possess such an incredible ticket, but you’re also humble on top of it. It’s nice to meet you. My name is Cherokan, the head chief that is leading the centaurs.”

“Oh, yes.”

Head Chief Cherokan welcomed him, and his manners were so open and hospitable without any hint of suspicion or trickery. Encouraged by the chief, Lloyd openly revealed the purpose of his visit.

“Chief,” said Lloyd, “Thank you for welcoming me. The thing is, I am here to search for an item called the Taupo.”

“Taupo?”

“Yes, it’s an object your ancestors brought during the mythical age.”

“Um, the mythical age? Are you looking for what was taken from the Jewel of Truth?”

“Oh, yes. That’s exactly it.” Feeling that things are going somewhere fast, Lloyd hopefully said, “Do you happen to know, chief, where the Taupo is at?”

“I do.” Chief Cherokan made a knowing smile.

“Then can you tell me where-”

“Nope.”

“Excuse me?”

“I know, but I cannot tell you.” The rejection was as straightforward as his big-hearted attitude.

Flailing his nostrils, Lloyd uttered, “Why… can’t you tell me?”

“Because I don’t want to.”

For a second, a sudden impulse to grab him by the neck or make him listen by force surged in Lloyd. He had to suppress his desire, however.

Is he playing with me or what? Should I force it out of him? No. Mobilizing Javier or Yong Yong will do the job, but I might regret it in the future.

Lloyd shook his head and looked back on his experience.

I have to find the key material for the Jewel of Truth. Knowing where it is might not be enough. What if I force things around here and antagonize the entire tribe? What if I can’t get cooperation from them when I need it? I might be in big trouble.

When he set out to find the Jewel of Truth and look for tauranga, the merpeople, and the queen were a massive source of help. Lloyd received a feeling that it might be the same this time.

So, let’s act with discretion. Life is longer than you think, and you never know what will happen. That’s why it’s important to have connections.

Just then, Lloyd recalled his days in room rental. He had no close family or other relatives around him. All of his parent’s acquaintances and distant relatives cut him off the moment they learned about their drastic decision. No one offered to help. He had lost all his connections.

And because of their drastic absence, I was able to feel how important social connections are to humans. It’s the same now. Once I build a good relationship with the head chief and centaur tribes, it’s going to benefit me in some sort of way. Throwing away such a good opportunity isn’t a smart choice.

And so, he decided to be on good terms with the chief. Approach things with utmost extra care. Resolved, Lloyd said, “Then, Chief Cherokan…”

“Speak.”

“Is there anything you want from me?”

“Finally,” blurted the chief, “You are talking. I surely got one. And if you give me what I want, then I should be able to resolve your curiosity.”

As I thought, he was saying nothing is free.

It was the same in both the world of humans and centaurs. Meanwhile, Cherokan spoke. “I am hoping you could solve a worry of mine.”

“What do you mean?”

“The youth of my race. The children. Their hot-blooded and reckless behavior is giving me a headache.”

“Hot-blooded and reckless behavior?”

“Yes,” Cherokan sighed softly. “You must be well aware of it. How much my race loves speed. How we worship speed. And so, we race even for marriage proposals. Only the fastest, male or female, can marry the desired partner.”

“Um, so?”

“It’s a problem. The over competitiveness is causing too many accidents.”

"Um, is it because they are speeding?”

“That’s exactly right. The violent race, things like overspeeding, changing lanes, hostile driving… All these put the lives of themselves and others in a risky situation. That’s why a large number of our youth are getting hurt and becoming disabled.”

“Then…”

“Why not stop them from violently racing each other, you ask?” interrupted Cherokan preemptively. “I can’t. It’s not something you can stop. Running is a part of our instinct. It’s not something to be stopped. That is why I am racking my head here, figuring out a way for the young centaurs to run freely and let their instincts run wild more safely than now.”

“Um, that’s what you were worried about?”

“Yes.” Cherokan nodded.

Lloyd nodded after hearing him. “That’s a simple concern.”

“What? Simple?”

“Yes.”

It really was a simple problem. Lloyd chuckled. The moment he heard his concern, Lloyd immediately came up with the answer he had in mind. “How about putting up a circuit-shaped coliseum for them to race?”
