# Hell Express 666 (2)

---

The history of the railroad was long. Surprisingly long. Similar to the endless connection of cars in a long train, the history of the railroad was much longer than what people would expect.

It really is. If I date back to the earliest history of railroads, it starts all the way from 600 BC.

Around 600 BC, there was a carriage road on the coast of Corinth, Greece. There was something different about this road compared to most others. The ground on the road had two furrows along it, and the distance between the furrows was identical to the size of carriage wheels. Thus, this road possessed the distinguishing feature of railroads with wheels that traveled along a line of designated tracks.

That’s not all. There are other similar cases.

There was another similar case around 400 AD in the Eastern Roman Empire. What was more, a track with buried furrows was even discovered far east of the Eurasia Continent in Ancient China.

It was the Warring State period. The tracks were used to mobilize large crates of supply during these endless days of war. It was also found that each country’s tracks had different sizes of wheels to prevent any enemies from using them when they invaded their land.

Just like that, humanity discovered the basic principle behind railroads in their early years of history. When these rudimentary railroad designs met the ripples of industrialization, the modern railroad came about to alter the history of human civilization altogether.

It’s the advance guard of industrialization. And the impetus that enabled development along with the large- scale manufacturing industry. It also served as the foundation for war. Railroads can be found anywhere throughout history.

It was true. Humanity enjoyed all sorts of transportation methods, such as cars, ships, airplanes, and many others, but nothing came close to being as efficient as the trains. Cars? Try driving around Seoul, and go figure. For long travel across large towns or cities, most people would prefer taking a subway over a bus. In fact, one would be able to fully experience the comfort of a train by taking a train ride from Seoul to Busan. What about ships?

It can transport large numbers of crates at once. That’s a clear advantage. However, a ship is limited by how it can’t move without water.

And so, ships couldn’t be used as the main method of transportation within a country’s border.

The same goes for planes.

First off, it was too expensive. And due to the weight-sensitive nature of airplanes’ landing and take-off, it was impossible to carry a lot of cargo at once. Plus, it was vulnerable to storms and other inclement weather conditions. Thus, each transportation method had its ups and downs. However, trains were…

In terms of efficiency, it’s almost perfect.

That was what Lloyd believed. Trains could carry large amounts of cargo at once similar to ships. Traffic was not a concern for trains, unlike cars. Trains also didn’t stop just because the weather was bad like planes. To top it all off, it was affordable, precise, and massive. These were all large advantages for trains.

“However, the initial costs of building a railroad and its infrastructure are costly, so it’s a matter of whether you can afford the price,” explained Lloyd.

“Do you mean the price to construct what you called a railroad?” asked the King of Hell.

“Yes.” Lloyd nodded and plastered a confident smile. “But I’m sure the Hell Castle won’t have any problem shouldering the cost.”

“You are right, but…” The king knitted his brows slightly. “Railroad. Efficient transportation. I understand everything. But how do you plan on setting it up here?”

“Do you ask,” Lloyd mused, “because of the flowing lava?”

“Indeed.” The frown on the king’s face deepened. “You said it. The railroad involves processing steel and laying them down on the ground. But the tracks will likely melt before long on the grounds of hell filled with pockets of lava everywhere.”

“Yes, that is certain.” Lloyd’s voice didn’t shake a bit.

“Then you have a plan?”

“Of course.”

“Tell me.”

“It’s a business secret.”

“Not because you lack confidence?”

“If you can’t believe me, then you don’t have to entrust this job with me.”

“…”

The King of Hell, Hellkaros, stared down at Lloyd, trying to make sense of the human in front of him. 

Kim Suho.

Hellkaros could see through the core of someone’s soul, so he was somewhat aware of Lloyd’s real identity. At least he knew that Kim Suho belonged to another world and ended up here for some inexplicable reason. So, Hellkaros regarded Suho as a laughable and absurd figure. But at the same time, he was intrigued. Why? While it was laughable that Lloyd was running wild when he was just a soul from another world buttressing the affairs of this world and making such bold conditions for him, it was interesting to hear about this railroad. His interest was piqued.

What he explained… can solve the problem here in Hell.

The King of Hell thought about it. The advantages and disadvantages of the railroad. The issues those advantages could solve.

It’s possible. That is if what he said is true.

A train would be formed by lining up hundreds of carts. And to generate power for the train, he simply needed to connect pulleys and levers to the wheels, assigning dozens of demons to push and pull the lever by taking shifts. It’d be like rowing a boat, only with levers. The train would move using the demons’ strength.

That would allow me to reduce the number of demons I need while transporting souls. Even with just 8000 demons, I would be able to transport a similar number of souls as now using 80,000 demons. 

Hellkaros earnestly did the math. Transporting souls would require much fewer demons. And the leftover demons could maintain the railroad. Even so, there’d still be those who had no work to do.

Then they can assist with my work.

Hell Castle had a plethora of menial jobs inside it. Assisting with the king’s judgments. Organizing records of the good and bad deeds of souls. Distributing queue numbers to the souls waiting to be judged. Dividing souls bound for Hell, Heaven, and Purgatory after their verdict.

I’ll give those chores to them all. My own workload will also get substantially lighter. I won’t have to continually find a new transportation route for those idiotic demons with a reliable railroad. If that happens…

He would be liberated from every single one of his miscellaneous chores. All that was left for him to do was sentence dead souls, a basic task for him. And that meant…

I can get off work.

Thump! Hellkaros’s heartbeat vigorously sped up. He could go home. After 670,000 years. Away from his sickening work. He could finally activate the door lock to his house, which still had a protective film on. He could finally open the door to his house, remove his dress shoes, dip himself in a warm lava bath, and binge- watch TV shows with his mana-powered marble. HellTube. Hellflix. Hellcha. Hellver Series.

I’ve never spent a day of my life like that before.

Gulp. The mere thought of it made his heart thump. It was exhilarating and thrilling to just think about it. And there was one more benefit to this. Liberation from anxiety. A sense of crippling anxiety gripped Hellkaros that he might meet the same fate as his predecessor, who ended his own life from depression after overworking for 3.8 billion years. And there was the despair that such fate was unavoidable. All these years, such feelings haunted him. But now, the dark cloud of anxiety and despair that shrouded his heart for the longest time started to thin out. That was probably why Hellkaros stared at Lloyd differently and why he put on a content smile, unlike a while ago.

“I like that.”

“Yes, so that means-”

“Kim Suho, I will trust you and leave the railroad construction to you,” announced Hellkaros.

“Thank you!” Lloyd hung his head down. He always welcomed new clients. And now, it was time to…

“Time to sign the construction contract?” Hellkaros asked first.

“My, that would make me the happiest man in the world,” said Lloyd as he smiled and rubbed his hands, inwardly praising the king. He indeed was the icon of fair judgment and negotiation. Lloyd admired how the king first suggested writing a contract even when he didn’t say anything.

This kind of client is so convenient to work with.

Satisfied, Lloyd said, “Oh, there is one thing I must ask of you.”

“Ask of me?”

“Yes, and it’s something essential I need from you for this project.”

“What is it?” asked Hellkaros.

“It’s simple. Please spread the news throughout the land that Lloyd Frontera will be singing at the edge of Hell,” revealed Lloyd.

“Hmm, that won’t be difficult to do. You say this is essential for the construction?”

“Yes, it’s essential to make the construction run smoothly. In the first place, I doubt you have any plans to set aside a group of demon workers for me.”

“That is obvious. The obedient ones are already transporting souls. The others are as good as trash, slacking off and threatening to end their lives should they be put to work. Could you possibly be thinking of trying to use them?”

“Yes, that is what I intend to do.”

“Then I see. I shall send a public announcement throughout Hell,” Hellkaros promised.

“Thank you.”

Everything after that took place smoothly. Lloyd drew up the construction contract like usual. The job was laying the railroad in the areas Hellkaros requested. Then Hellkaros was to release the soul of Anticus after the construction. Those were the terms written in the contract. Since the terms were simple, there weren’t any adjustments to make. Once the two checked the content of the contract, each signed and took one copy. That was it.

“Good,” said Hellkaros with a smile. “I am glad that we made a final contract, at least. I wasted at least 20 minutes listening to your explanation on the railroad and signing this contract.”

“By waste, you mean-”

“That is how long the carriage has been delayed,” explained Hellkaros nonchalantly. “And how long the propagation of the microorganisms on earth has been delayed.”

“…”

“Let us say the chance for grand extinction on earth has increased by one percent due to the broken microbial ecosystem.”

“Eeek,” gasped Lloyd.

“It’s nothing to be surprised about. Okay, so the contract is made, so I will send you to the construction site right away.”

“To the site?”

“Yes. I will get you there right away as you will be wasting time on your travels otherwise.”

The King of Hell gestured, and a red ripple appeared. It soon spread and wrapped Lloyd and Javier’s bodies.

“You will find yourself at the edge of Hell,” explained Hellkaros. “Construct the railroad from there until you get here. Well then, I shall see you again when the railroad is finished.”

And with that…

Zap!

“…!”

The red ripple that engulfed them beamed and flashed like an explosion. Everything around them became dark. Lloyd felt like his whole body was floating in the air. After his stomach churned for a moment, he felt his feet touch the strange ground. Thwack!

“Woah.”

Lloyd blinked hard. His whole surrounding had changed after some quick thrashing. The Hell Castle had disappeared from his view along with the King of Hell. To be exact…

“I suppose this is the edge of Hell the king spoke of,” said Javier.

“Yes, I agree.”

Lloyd felt a small rush of relief when he heard Javier beside him.

The king must have transported us here. After all, Hell is his territory.

Lloyd surveyed straight ahead. The ash-covered plain was devoid of even a strand of grass. There was sulfur- scented smoke rising from scattered spots. Lloyd also spotted a lava pond still soft and unhardened. The scenery was Hell itself, desolate and barren. But the opposing side was slightly different.

“I guess this is the edge of Hell,” Lloyd said.

“It appears so, indeed,” Javier seconded.

Lloyd watched the landscape of the edge of Hell. It was an open cliff whose depth couldn’t be measured. There was nothing but a dark void ahead.

I can’t even imagine what would happen if I fell down there.

Lloyd shivered, taking 30 steps back from the cliff, as he resolved to never fall from it. Just then, he heard Javier speak.

“Master Lloyd, I have a question.”

“A question?” Lloyd looked at Javier.

“Yes.”

“What is it?”

“Who is Kim Suho?”

“Cough…! Ergh!” Lloyd spluttered, choking on his own saliva.

“Are you okay, Young Master?” Javier asked with a frown.

“Ah, yeah. My throat got dry. It’s the air here. Terrible.”

“…”

Lloyd feigned and half-joked. But Javier didn’t smile.

“I kept thinking about it, and yet, it is still strange. The king a while ago. The king kept addressing you with a strange name, ‘Kim Suho.”“ Javier looked at Lloyd with a curious face.

"Oh, that?” said Lloyd, stressing his voice to sound calm.

“Yes.”

“I know, right? That was just strange. Don’t you think so?” Lloyd said with equal, feigned curiosity in his voice.

“Yes, you clearly have a name. Lloyd Frontera. Then I suppose you are unaware of what that address means to you?”

“Of course, I don’t. How would I know? Well, it’s probably a nickname used in Hell or something,” speculated Lloyd. And he shrugged.

“Is that so?” Javier asked dully.

“Yeah, like I said.”

“I see.”

“Probably?”

Lloyd then plastered a grin, making sure that the corners of his lips didn’t shiver awkwardly and that his thumping heart wouldn’t give him away. He pretended to be ignorant and made an effort to look calm. At the same time, he also didn’t forget to bash the king inwardly.

Shoot. That was close. That King of Hell is so evil. Why did he have to call me by my real name? Goodness. 

Lloyd decided that he should meet the king alone next time. Thankfully, Javier had another subject to discuss.

“By the way, I have one more question.”

“What again?”

“How on earth do you plan on constructing a railroad here?”

Javier pointed at the vast land covered with thick volcanic ash and stagnated with lava. The whole place boiled at a massively hot temperature.

“I, too, heard about the railroad when you explained it to the king. So, I have some understanding now. The crux of it was to put up a line of tracks that fit the size of a train’s wheels.”

“You’re right,” agreed Lloyd.

“But now that I see this place, I wonder if the railroad will be safe.”

“Oh, you are right about that, too. It’s truly reasonable that you ask it. Go on.”

“Yes, if we install the railroads made of steel, I think the road will melt because of the heat here. It will become malleable as it melts, and that means the trains will not be able to travel properly,” theorized Javier seriously.

“You are right. Plus, the ground for laying out the railroads will be pretty unstable because there’s lava everywhere,” added Lloyd on top of Javier’s explanation.

Javier frowned. “Then how on earth do you plan on going about the construction?”

“I have a plan.” Lloyd’s assurance, however, meant nothing to Javier.

“Wait, do you plan on scamming against the King of Hell?”

“Whaaat? Scamming the King of Hell?” gasped Lloyd.

“Yes.”

“How little do you think of me?” Lloyd asked with a smile.

Javier, on the other hand, looked grim. He reeled off, “A sly cheat. You are also cheap and rude. Sometimes, I wonder if a pathetic crook who does nothing but scheme like yourself can even be called a human being. Personally speaking.”

“…”

“Am I wrong?” Javier’s face looked innocent.

“You might as well just break one of my bones, you know.” Lloyd shook his head.

“I cannot let that happen,” Javier said defiantly. “Breaking your leg is an act of mutiny and violence.”

“Just so you know, there is this thing called 'verbal violence.”“

"That seems to be the field of your expertise,” pointed out Javier.

“My, my. What an absurd remark against my young and innocent soul.”

“I do not know anything about being innocent or fresh, but I do know that you are so lucky with women.”

“Lucky with women? Me?” Lloyd looked at Javier with curiosity.

“Yes.”

“What are you saying?”

“No, sorry, Master Lloyd. I mean out of luck.”

“…”

“Anyway, I see you have a plan for how to build the railroad here.”

“Hey, don’t change the subject after beating me up with your words." 

"But I want to.” Javier shrugged.

“Woah,”

“Can I not change the subject?”

“Fine. Let’s change it. Sure. Haah.”

Lloyd broke into laughter. It was clear to Lloyd that Javier was enjoying this moment, and not enough with that, it was clear that he had not only gotten comfortable serving Lloyd but learned how to enjoy himself doing it.

Dammit. And it’s not like I can strike him.

Lloyd decided inwardly to revenge him hundreds of times more severely someday. He then shifted his focus to the matter at hand.

“So, I guess you want to know how the railroad will be built here?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes, I am.”

“Well, it’s simple.” Lloyd grinned. “First, we need to rally the tens of thousands of unemployed demons living all across Hell,” Lloyd then added, revealing the first step of the plan to build the Hell Express 666.
