# An Even More Shameless Demand (2)

---

The Heart of Winter was a divine marble that held the frigidity of the harshest season of the year. Despite its diameter of one inch, it was a malicious weapon that carried the freezing nature of winter and was one of the three divine items in the House of Magentano. Its power was beyond imaginable. If the frigid energy in the marble was ever released, the entire land around it would be swept with a chilling weather that was worse than the Ice Age. In just a single day, it could freeze over an entire fiefdom. Within ten days, the average temperature of a region as big as the Gyeonggi Province would plummet by twenty degrees. And the harvest for that year, as well as humans and livestock, would be ruined.

That’s what was said in the novel. Because of its destructive power, it was used only twice by the House of Magentano throughout history.

Lloyd recalled the mid-part of the story in the novel, where the Heart of Winter was used for the third time.

Queen Magentano utilizes it after she becomes a tyrant. She uses it to end the riot in the West and set an example of her terrorizing regime. Ultimately, this destroyed five fiefdoms and caused famine and calamity across the western region.

It was nothing short of a disaster. Too many people to count perished in the cold, and eventually, there was a great famine where countless people died of hunger the following year. That was how powerful and destructive the Heart of Winter was.

That should be why the queen is now giving me that look.

A wry smile escaped Lloyd. The queen’s gaze wasn’t that of fright or anger. It was the opposite. She was giving him a very pitiful look.

“My…” she sighed, her voice dripping with pity. “I should have stopped you when you busted your gut. I am responsible for this.”

Lloyd wasn’t sure where she was getting at.

“Humans are not livestock. We ought to rest and enjoy ourselves when we can. But you could not. You were always on the go to overcome the disaster in the east and reconstruct it after. That explains it. That is why. Tsk, tsk.”

“…”

“But it is all fine. You are my meritorious retainer. You may sometimes speak like a fool, a madman, or as if you are unwell. Even then, I can understand you. How could I not? How could I not when you ended up in this state after living such a diligent life?”

“Um… Your Majesty…”

“Yes,” the queen put on a benevolent smile, and her voice sounded like she was talking to a child. “Speak up. Speak up, Frontera.”

“Um, first of all,” pointed out Lloyd, “I am not a fool and did not go mad.”

“Of course, that’s what you say. I never heard of a madman who admitted his state of mind out loud.”

“The thing is, Your Majesty, I am completely fine,” Lloyd pressed further.

“Hmm. We will see,” said the queen.

“How can I prove that I am mentally sound to you, Your Majesty?”

“Will you marry me, Lloyd Frontera?”

“Please forgive me, Your Majesty.”

“Tsk, so you are sane.”

“Forgive me, Your Majesty.” His wry smile deepened. Moving past the scary question, Lloyd wiped his cold sweat and went on, “I did not lose it, Your Majesty. I am not speaking gibberish, either. This request is being made after a series of precise predictions, calculations, and conclusions.”

“So,” mused the queen, “You wish to borrow the Heart of Winter for the foreseeable future?”

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“You should be aware of what kind of item that is.”

“Certainly, Your Majesty, and that is why I requested an audience with you.” Lloyd hung his head down. It was true. The Heart of Winter was a divine item owned by the royal family, and it wasn’t something anyone, including the man with unreserved trust from the queen, could easily get his hands on. Lending it would be the same as increasing the queen’s level of support by 100 folds.

Of course. Even the queen can’t unilaterally use the three divine items. It can be used only if the kingdom is on the brink of collapse or if the queen requests to use it. There needs to be a unanimous agreement from the nobles in the House of Peers for anyone to use it.

Of course, there were exceptions. A case in point was when Queen Magentano became a tyrant in the novel.

But that was only possible because she was an evil ruler and did everything she wanted to. She beheaded anyone, be it a noble or a subject, who disagreed with her on the spot. The kingdom was heading toward downfall. But now, the circumstances are different.

The queen was extremely rational, and the kingdom was very stable. The power of the queen and the custom of the nobles were in harmony as they led the national affairs. And so, Queen Magentano wouldn’t even think about unilaterally permitting Lloyd to use the Heart of Winter. So, if Lloyd wanted to use it, he’d have to ask the queen to file a request for its usage to the House of Peers and obtain its agreement.

“Whew. Then you should also know that this puts a political burden on my part,” said Queen Magentano.

“Certainly, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd.

“And yet, you are making this request?”

“Yes, Your Majesty. For there is an imperative need for it.”

“An imperative need, you say. Explain.”

“Yes. If I may, Your Majesty, the Heart of Winter is essential to performing the freezing method I explained to you a couple of minutes ago.”

“Freezing method?” She frowned a little. “Your explanation was too esoteric for me to understand it clearly.”

“To put it simply,” said Lloyd, “the method involves freezing the moisture in the soil by inserting the pipes underground and injecting cold air.”

“Freeze the water in the ground?”

“Yes, Your Majesty. That will allow the ground to harden more than usual.”

“I believe it will. Much like how the soil gets as hard as a rock on wintry mornings. But what do you plan to achieve by freezing the ground?”

Lloyd did not hesitate to answer. “I plan to dig a large cave down the castle, Your Majesty.”

“A cave?” Queen Magentano frowned a little. But Lloyd was relieved. Her frown meant he managed to pique her interest.

Reading the positive atmosphere in the room, he quickly replied, “Yes, Your Majesty. It is to harvest the tauranga I mentioned to you the last time.”

“Right. You said you were looking for the material called tauranga. Are you saying that it is beneath this palace?”

“Yes, Your Majesty. To be more specific, they were used to make the isolated pillars of the foundation that were placed down like tree roots in the ground.”

“Will it not affect the palace if you pull those out?”

“That is where the freezing method comes into the picture.”

“Be more specific.”

“Yes, Your Majesty,” Lloyd replied, sensing things were going somewhere. And then he began to rattle off, “Once the ground freezes and becomes more firm than usual, it will allow the vertical shafts I dig under the palace to hold still without falling apart. And it will also give me a bit of time until I replace the pieces of tauranga with a replacement stone.”

What he said was true. The freezing method was first developed in the nineteenth century in the United Kingdom, and it allowed excavations of vertical shafts to be done safely and free from collapse. In modern days, it was a great way to revamp any land by using excavation while protecting the vital structures on them.

So, it’s a perfect method to pull out the pieces of tauranga in the foundation.

The project involved digging vertical and horizontal shafts underneath. But that would inevitably sink the palatial foundation. The freezing method could prevent such from happening. Moreover, it could lower the weight being added to the independent pillars when the Taurangas are replaced. That was what Lloyd was after, and it was the product of countless theories, calculations, simulations, and experiments.

So, please! Queen! Your Majesty! Come on, my dear friend! Just this time!

Lloyd desperately hoped that the queen would happily agree and allow him to use a more convenient method for construction. He looked up at the queen with puppy dog eyes.

“Ha.” A scoff escaped Queen Magentano, and she finally shook her head in defeat. “You are truly incorrigible.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty,” replied Lloyd.

“Tsk. I have not granted your request yet.”

“Forgive my impudence, Your Majesty.”

“Sure. You should be sorry. You are shamelessly making such a burdensome and outrageous request. Yet, you also make it impossible for me to refuse your request.”

“Words fall short of my gratitude to you, Your Highness.”

“I have not permitted you yet, I said.”

“But Your Majesty, your eyes tell me that you will.”

“You dare to claim to know my intentions?”

“Forgive me, Your Majesty.” Lloyd looked away.

“Tsk. If only I could hit you right now.”

“Your Majesty?” Lloyd immediately looked up.

“Not at all. I whispered my wish out loud, so forget about it. However, I have a question I would like to ask you.”

“I am at your service.” Lloyd hung his head down again.

Queen Magentano asked, “Can you promise me that your project that involves the usage of the Heart of Winter and taking out the tauranga will not harm the imperial family or me?”

“Certainly, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd.

“Good. Then you have my permission.”

“Excuse me…?” Surprised by her immediate approval, Lloyd raised his head. He found her smiling confidently.

“You made a promise, and I shall trust it. Do you need anything more?”

“Certainly not, Your Majesty. However…” said Lloyd, stopping midway.

“However, what?”

“Your immediate approval makes me feel like a bad man…”

“A bad man? That doesn’t sound bad.”

“Excuse me?”

“Never mind. Anyway, you would not have opted to use such a tedious method if your intent was to harm me and the imperial family. I am simply trusting that you have good intentions and won’t go in that direction. So, do leave now. I will persuade the House of Peers.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

At last, he obtained permission from the queen! Lloyd quickly paid his respects, hiding his joy, and left at lightning speed. He was worried that she might change her mind. After he left, Queen Magentano held back her bitter smile.

“Ha.”

Up until a while ago, her office felt like a busy market. But now, it felt desolate and aloof without Lloyd.

“Truly, he is a sly and cunning man.”

She wanted to keep him by her side and put him to the toil for the rest of his life, so she could lay the foundation for the kingdom to last over 1,000 years. But Lloyd didn’t allow himself to be on her palm. And now, it was as good as half losing him as he came to possess powers too great for her to control. Tables have turned now, and she was faced with the danger of being dragged around by him. It was happening at the very moment. She already had a pile of work to take care of, which only grew taller with the addition of Lloyd’s request. It looked like she had a lot to prepare to get a positive consensus from the nobles.

“How dare he put me to work. And yet, he only seeks me when he needs something. He truly is a bad man,” grumbled Queen Magentano.

Ten days passed. It had been a busy time for Lloyd. He had to get ready to freeze the ground. He hired laborers with the help of the queen and put together the materials for the construction. Afterward, he closely measured the spot for the vertical shafts. To get a deeper look, Lloyd mana blasted the ground, selected the areas for the freeze pipes, and designed the plan.

For the queen, the last ten days had been equally busy. She filed a request to use the Heart of Winter for the House of Peers. At first, they expressed dismay because it was for some repairs in the palace. They wondered if it was indeed necessary, even if the repair was urgent and had a direct correlation with the authority of the royal house. They doubted her at first. But as time progressed, the atmosphere of the house changed as each member slowly came around with either persuasion or bribery. The queen’s coaxing, flattery, and gifts worked. Eventually, all of them signed the agreement, and a consensus was reached.

At last, the Heart of Winter was transferred to the construction site under the strict management and guard of the royal magicians and knights. Although the marble was a strategic weapon carrying horrifying powers, none of it mattered to Lloyd. He didn’t give a rat’s ass about its power and danger. It was nothing but a handy tool to freeze the ground. Just like that, the construction to attain the Tauranga began. But Lloyd, Javier, and the other laborers were clueless about something.

Thud! Thud! The Heart of Winter, resting inside a specially designed control chest, was now resonating with Lloyd’s heartbeat.
