# Noise Pollution Complaint (3)

---

Northern sea. Three days ago, the Bone Dragon had arrived at the bitter cold polar region where there was only ice, biting wind, and waves. It was there to fulfill the order of its master, Lloyd.

Lay low in the northern sea of the capital for a while.

The assignment had become the Bone Dragon’s sole point of focus. Lloyd was the first master it served after being dominated by the Zombie Domination skill, and so, the Bone Dragon loved its master, Lloyd. It yearned to be praised, and that required complying with his orders. So, it flew north day and night until a sea appeared. It was satisfied for a split second until it realized it shouldn’t be content. The sea stretched north without an end in sight.

Clack! North. North and only north. To the most northern part. Lloyd had told the Bone Dragon to hide in the northern sea. It believed that it would be lavished with praise once it traveled far north of this world.

With that thought, a sense of excitement rushed through the Bone Dragon. It started to put more force into its fluttering wings. Toward the north, it flew on and on in almost religious zeal until the dragon finally arrived at the sea of the polar region, which sat far north of the planet.

Clack! The Bone Dragon shook its large skull as soon as it arrived at the destination and studied the planet’s magnetic field with the magnetite laden in its horn. Thanks to that, it confirmed in satisfaction that it was in the far north. This meant that Lloyd would shower the dragon with praise as long as it quietly stayed hidden in there. Filled with satisfaction and conviction, the Bone Dragon dove into the sea, breaking several feet of ice and enjoying a cold bath. But its original plan turned out to be quite a challenge as the neighbors were very cold to the newcomer.

Gaargh!

The first resident of the sea that took action was the Gigatitan. It boasted a body length of 295 feet and a weight of 2,500 tons. And it also possessed a shock-absorbing shell. The Gigatitan was a sea monster with a formidable physique. However, in the presence of the Bone Dragon, it was nothing but a pestering lobster.

Clack! Flick! The Bone Dragon sent a flick at the Gigatitan, who scurried away in fear for its life with just one blow. And the dragon felt a wave of boundless trust for Lloyd, realizing the greatness of its master once again from the stately battle.

“If monsters like the Gigatitan try to harass you, just flick them with your foreleg. You’re stronger.”

Master Lloyd had assured it so, and his words turned out to be true. This excited the Bone Dragon even more, which marked the beginning of its ruthless attacks against the northern sea monsters that tried to pick a fight with the newcomer trespassing on their territory. The emperor whale that was as big as the gigatitan yet faster, the ginormous shark Teralodon that had a charming set of straight teeth, and the strongest predator of the sea, Kraken. They were all beaten with a light flick from the dragon. And sooner or later, the most powerful predators at the top of the underwater ecosystem appeared. The Mermaid Corps.

“We can no longer sit and watch the monster guided by evil magic defile the sea! My dear sisters!”

“Streamline with flexibility and force!”

“Dolphin kick without any hesitation or restraint!”

“Sisters, advance!”

The mermaids were indeed powerful. It wasn’t possible for them to not be strong. This was especially true looking at the swimmers on the earth. Michael Phelps, the best swimmer in the world who held eight Olympic gold medals, was told to undergo six hours of hectic training each day. And such hellish training was done for six days every week.

But what about the mermaids? They swam for 24 hours! Seven days a week! Swimming was a way of life for them, a part of their identity. And so, their bodies were a weapon in every sense of the word.

They owned a physique that made a sturdy Olympian look like a child in comparison. Their shoulders were as wide as the Pacific Ocean, their pecs were thick enough to bounce off meteors, their abs were so defined that one could literally use them as a washboard, and their veins were so vivid that they looked like worms. They were swimmers who spent their entire lives going through extreme and hardcore training under the ocean. Furthermore, there were 1,000 of those terrifying mermaids.

They had enough strength to easily dismember most Gigatitans in three parts – their head, chest, and stomach – in under 30 seconds. But how did the Bone Dragon react when it was faced with the charging Mermaid Corps? It was in high spirits.

Clack! Clack!

The mermaids were too fast for the dragon to flick them. When it was lucky and managed to strike, they simply endured the pain with the thick shield of their muscles and got back on their feet. There was considerable force behind their strikes. However, their attack only made for a relaxing and rejuvenating massage for the dragon’s tense bones and joints after its long flight here. The mermaids also had great endurance. Their impressive lung capacity was what allowed them to prolong the battle for three days straight without losing momentum.

Thanks to them, the Bone Dragon wasn’t ever bored during those three days after it arrived at the polar region. It was happy and felt fulfilled. The dragon wished it could share this joy with its Master.

However, it knew that was impossible since Lloyd was in Hell. The Bone Dragon thought that was a pity. That could be why the dragon felt elated when it suddenly sensed Lloyd’s mana nearby.

Clack! At first, the Bone Dragon thought it was dreaming. But no, it undoubtedly sensed Lloyd’s mana. The mutual location tracking feature of the Zombie Domination skill had been deactivated ever since Lloyd went to Hell, but it had started to work again. So, the Bone Dragon could tell that its master was near!

Clack! Clack! Splash! The Bone Dragon snapped its head up, tossing its glance toward the sky above the sea where Lloyd’s mana was coming from. Then it saw the 16-foot tall Hell gate hanging in the air about 328 feet from the sea and felt the heat of Hell flowing out from it. In addition, it sensed the region’s time zone starting to adjust to that of Hell. The moment the Bone Dragon perceived the adjustment, it was convinced that Lloyd was on the other end of the Hell gate.

Kaboom! In sheer onrush of joy, the Bone Dragon flung itself high from the sea, wagging its pelvis and shaking off the mermaids glued to it. Then it made its way toward the Hell gate. However, it failed to enter inside.

Thump! The Bone Dragon was too big to pass through the Hell gate, which was only 16 feet in diameter. But it wasn’t flustered as there was another way to get to the other end.

Claaack! The Bone Dragon took apart all its joints, from its large skull to its pelvis. And after that, it lined the bones up in the air, leading each one to pass through the Hell gate like a railroad car. Afterward, it flew into Hell without hesitation and pieced back its body again.

Claaack! Clack! Clack! Once it was done with the assembly, it surveyed the area. The landscape of this place was poles apart from the polar region it was a while ago. In a flash, it located Lloyd.

Clack! The Bone Dragon spotted Lloyd flying low on the parrotbill, so it zoomed off at an explosive speed toward him and landed nearby. With a banging, crushing sound, the Bone Dragon wagged its tail, even flipping over and revealing its belly to show its happiness. It waited earnestly for Lloyd to praise it.

But instead, Lloyd cried out, “Hey! Let’s skip the pleasantries since we’re short on time! If you’re here, do what you came here for! Catch that thing!”

Creak? The Bone Dragon cocked its head to the side in confusion and tried to make sense of Lloyd’s lukewarm response to its happy greeting. No, Master Lloyd had shouted at it as if he was in a panic. The Bone Dragon wanted to know why he was acting like that, so it turned to the direction where he was pointing at. And there, the Bone Dragon spotted the Lava Giant stomping away. Just then, the Bone Dragon’s eyes were filled with rage.

Clack! It’s because of that giant. That giant stirred Lloyd into a panic. That giant prevented it from getting a warm welcome from Lloyd. Therefore, that giant must be destroyed!

Claaack! Enraged, the Bone Dragon leaped from the ground, flying across hundreds of feet in one leap and landing right in front of the Lava Giant. And it grabbed its enemy.

Boooom! The Bone Dragon and the Lava Giant. The two monsters with similar physiques collided. For the first time, the unstoppable escapade of the Lava Giant came to a pause.

“Grrrrr? Grr?”

The Lava Giant’s eyes widened when the Bone Dragon suddenly appeared and grabbed it. Confusion rushed through its mind, and it wanted to wrench off the other’s grip. However, the giant couldn’t.

“Grrragh! Grr-!”

So, the Lava Giant breathed out lava fire at the head of the Bone Dragon and clutched the ribs of its intruder with a boiling-hot lava grip. The Lava Giant expected its opponent to panic at the heat, but that was incorrect. The dragon didn’t react to the heat at all, and the reason was very simple. Quite literally, the dragon was nothing but bones. As a result, the hot lava felt more like a heated blanket to the creature.

Claaack! The Bone Dragon showed greater strength, causing the Lava Giant to slowly step back in alarm.

Watching the situation, Lloyd yelled, “Good! Keep pushing it!”

Lloyd inwardly exclaimed in joy. The Bone Dragon showed up just in time and stopped the Lava Giant from getting away. Lloyd’s eyes were full of pride and affection as they gazed at the youngest member of the Skeleton Corps.

My precious little boy!

Truth be told, Lloyd never expected the Bone Dragon to show up. Why would he? About four months ago, right before he crossed over to Hell, he had ordered the Bone Dragon to hide in the northern sea of the capital. It never occurred to Lloyd that the dragon would take his order so seriously and hide all the way in the polar region.

But then I could feel that lovable dragon of mine approaching me.

He felt so from the mutual location tracking feature available in the Zombie Domination skill, which he had used to recruit the Bone Dragon. He sensed it was nearby, moving closer to him. Lloyd was overjoyed. And so now, it was time to finish off the Lava Giant that ruined his construction site.

“Javier! Let’s go!”

It was time for an all-out attack. While the Bone Dragon was holding on to the giant and causing its body to cool near the Hell gate, Lloyd had to finish off the Lava Giant.

If his plan succeeded, he could complete the construction and reap the massive rewards of his months of hard work. With that mindset, Lloyd grabbed the shovel and urged Ggoming to fly. He coordinated with Javier on the ground, hurtling toward the Lava Giant right then and laying a series of attacks without holding back on anything.

Kaboom! Starting with the mana blast and the triple mana blast, Lloyd aimed at the giant’s knees, ankles, back, and other joints. He even aimed at its most private area, although it was a bit of a low blow. The Lava Giant was shaken and flinched. Of course, its wounds regenerated soon enough as the lava hadn’t completely cooled yet. However, Lloyd didn’t care.

If hitting isn’t enough, then I just have to keep going until it is!

Lloyd decided to tie the giant to the ground until the lava grew completely cold. To that end, he had to give everything to hitting the monster even though it seemed hopeless. He needed to slash, pierce, and damage the giant as much as possible. That was the only way to ensure the arctic wind blowing through the Hell gate would cool its body.

Duh! When you’re drinking hot broth, you don’t just stare at it until it cools down! You whisk it with a spoon and blow at it!

The same principle applied here. He would create holes into its body with the mana blast, and Javier’s aura would add more of them. Jab, penetrate, slash. And as its whole body became damaged, the hot lava inside it would naturally be exposed to the cold wind. The heat would then escape faster. But Lloyd didn’t stop there. He instead used every means possible to steal the heat from the giant’s body. One method he used involved Hamang.

“Hamang!” Lloyd called earnestly. “It’s always hot here, right?”

“Hamang?”

“How would you like to drink ice water?”

“Hamang!”

“Then go and spray it here!”

Lloyd threw Hamang to the other end of the Hell gate. More specifically, he was thrown across the air into the polar region from Hell. Hamang plunged into the Arctic Sea and started to drink salty water to his heart’s content.

“Hamang! Hamamamang!” Sluuurp! Swoosh! His body grew 229 feet tall in a wink. Next, he spewed the water downward.

“Humu-mung!”

Thwack! The violent water stream sent Hamang 328 feet off the ground, and he instantly changed the direction of the water jet toward the Lava Giant visible through the Hell gate.

Swaaa! The gush of icy water passed the Hell gate in a straight line, like tap water jetting out of a hose. In return, this forced the giant, who was exposed to the arctic winds while being held in place by the Bone Dragon, to take a freezing shower on top of everything.

“Grrrr! Achoo!”

The giant’s sneezing grew worse. And its temperature started to drop at a faster speed amid the thick volume of vapor.

"Don’t stop! Keep going!” Lloyd encouraged more earnestly,

Javier’s attack grew fiercer. Hamang continued to shoot water on repeat. The Bone Dragon took hold of the giant with more tenacity. The body of the Lava Giant cooled at a faster speed. This slowed down the damage regeneration speed.

And its movement started to grow duller. As though it was freezing up by the merciless coldness, the giant’s boiling red skin started to thicken and turn black. The inside shown between the cracks started to blacken a little by little as well. And just when Lloyd judged that it was hardened enough, Lloyd bawled.

“Now!”

At Lloyd’s shouting, the Bone Dragon pushed the giant, which caused it to stagger back. When the distance between the two increased for the first time, Javier rushed in between.

Swoosh! With his feet on the land made of volcanic ash, cutting through the heat, he coldly passed an eye over the Lava Giant.

…!

Silently, hundreds of aura strands swept over the Lava Giant without giving it any time to think. And next, welcoming messages started to pour down in front of Lloyd.

Ding Dong!

[You have appropriately made use of your wisdom, unexpected luck, and trustworthy allies. Thanks to that, you have succeeded in destroying the living disaster of Hell, the Lava Giant Dunkleos.]

[For this miraculous battle, you are awarded a legendary title.]

[You have earned a new title.]

[A new title <Frozen Hands, Frozen Feet > has been created.]
