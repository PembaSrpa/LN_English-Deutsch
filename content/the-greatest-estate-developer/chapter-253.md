# The Restoration Force of Destiny (1)

---

Altering the path of fate was not an easy task. No, it was almost impossible. Like an overly tight bowstring, no matter how much you attempted to yank and stretch it to change its shape, it returned to its original form as soon as you let your hands go.

“That is the phenomenon called the restoration of destiny,” explained Verkis dully. “That is how tenacious destiny is. And its restoration power is formidable.”

“Is that what it is?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes.” Verkis nodded before elaborating, “For some inexplicable reason, there are rare instances where the path of destiny goes through a drastic change due to external interference like yourself. And destiny exerts great force to restore its original flow of events.”

“So it strives to go back to the way things were supposed to happen.” Lloyd’s face darkened.

“That’s right,” affirmed Verkis. “Did you say in the novel you read, the count, no, the baron and baroness hang themselves due to their crippling debt?”

“Yes…” Lloyd nodded heavily. The baron and the baroness’s death were appallingly similar to the manner in which his parents passed away, the very tragic incident Lloyd never wanted to see but witnessed with his own eyes.

Verkis crinkled his brows slightly. “And you were able to save them with immense effort, only for the count to fall off a horse and break his neck. Furthermore, this also causes the countess to end her own life in grief. That’s the phenomenon of the restoration of destiny. You won’t be able to easily stop these incidents from happening. No, it will be almost impossible to make any changes.”

“Even if I prevent the count from falling off the horse?” Lloyd’s voice sounded more desperate.

“Mhm. You may stop this one incident, but the baron will keep finding himself in absurd situations. For example, he might slip from the tub after that day and break his neck there.”

“…”

“That’s the way things are. You could go out of your way and do everything possible to prevent these incidents, but eventually, everyone will face their original destiny.”

“Then what am I supposed to do to stop destiny?” Lloyd felt dizzy as he heard these words.

“I don’t know,” blurted Verkis nonchalantly.

“Excuse me?” Lloyd’s brow lifted.

“Didn’t you hear me? I don’t know.”

“Wow.” Lloyd gaped.

“How am I supposed to know that? Do I look like a god?”

“But-”

“Are you trying to complain about why I bothered to rattle on about destiny and its restoration when I didn’t have an answer for your question?” accused Verkis with a stinging gaze.

“Yes.”

“Tsk, you don’t even deny it.”

“I am being honest, my lord.”

“Well, but I might have an idea that could work.”

“My lord,” called Lloyd, suddenly changing his attitude. “I’ve truly admired you for a very long time now.”

“…”

“I am being sincere.”

“Ha… Anyway, I don’t know if it’s going to be the solution to the restoration of destiny, but it might offer you a clue. Have you ever heard of the Jewel of Truth from the Mermaid Kingdom?”

“No, I haven’t.” Lloyd shook his head. The Jewel of Truth? He had never heard of it. The novel didn’t mention it, either.

“I heard this,” continued Verkis, “from a mermaid I knew a very long time ago. I was told about something called the Jewel of Truth in the Mermaid Kingdom.”

“Is it possibly something that reveals the truths about this world?”

“Mhm. Something like that. More specifically, it only answers one question.”

“Only one question?”

“Yes. Just one.” Verkis lifted his index finger. “It gives the truth to any question you ask. But here’s the catch. Your question needs to be extremely specific to get an answer.”

“Then…” Lloyd rolled his eyes, pausing and thinking for a second. “If I ask it to tell me how I can stop the restoration of destiny from occurring around me, would that be a specific question?”

“Probably? You will find it out once you get to the Mermaid Kingdom.”

“All right. Then how do I get there?” Lloyd’s voice slightly shook. It was done. He finally found a clue. At one point, he had even fantasized about using the internet in all of his desperation! But now, after poking around, he finally got a useful answer from the Dragon King!

The Jewel of Truth in the Mermaid Kingdom. He’s saying I have to go to the Mermaid Kingdom to find that, right?

Hope was rising above the horizon. A vision of hope appeared in his head. He thought that he might be able to resolve this matter much more easily than he expected. That was until he heard Verkis’s next words, which shattered his hope to pieces.

“I don’t know.”

“Excuse me?” Lloyd’s brow lifted in dismay.

“I don’t know where the Mermaid Kingdom is.”

“But you just said you have a mermaid friend-”

“Oh, she died of old age.”

“…”

“What was her name? Houhora? That woman used to be the queen of the kingdom. But she died of old age.”

“Wait, but you must have kept in touch with her until she passed away.”

“We did. Though I mostly just used her to do my work.”

“…”

“But that was until she became the queen. She changed the kingdom’s location and never told me what it was.”

“…”

My Dragon King Verkis! What kind of interpersonal relationship did you have, you sleepyhead? Lloyd wanted to heave a sigh. Lloyd sensed that this was all that he could get from Verkis. The latter didn’t know, so what could Lloyd do?

I should back off at this point.

It would be crossing the line to cling to Verkis right now. And sure enough, his eyes were now heavy with sleep.

Okay. I’ve gotten enough information. It’ll only hurt me if I excessively bother him.

One had to know when to step back after they obtained what they wanted. Now was the time, Lloyd sensed. He plastered a smile of a service worker on his face.

“I see. Anyway, thank you. My Dragon King, no, my hero, I sincerely thank you.”

“Your hero?” Verkis repeated.

“Oh my, my true thoughts accidentally slipped out-”

“Leaving aside if you’re being sincere or not, does this mean that you’ll be off to search for the Mermaid Kingdom?”

“It looks like I will be,” replied Lloyd.

“Then what about my wake-up call?” Verkis shot back.

“I will turn over that job to one of my subordinates.”

“Really? Are any of your subordinates capable of waking me up as well as you?” asked Verkis incredulously.

“Yes, though they can’t speak.” Lloyd smiled as he thought of the members of the Skeleton Corps.

Turtle Neck, Square Jaw, and Frozen Shoulder. You’re going to wake up even if you don’t want to when they come here, tap, hula dance, and every morning.

Their bones would crack and creak, and the noise of their breathless dancing would force Verkis up, even if he was the dragon king. Thanks to these folks, Lloyd was able to leave Verkis rest assured.

“You will be properly woken up every morning without a doubt. You have nothing to worry about.”

“Well, if you say so.” Verkis then sprawled on the bed. A satisfied smile hung on his face.

Great. He finally got off my back. What a pesky little fellow.

The past few days came to his mind. How cumbersome this Lloyd Frontera, no, Kim Suho, from another dimension was! As a result, Verkis had to waste a few more minutes being awake even after he was done with his morning duty. He endured the pain of not being able to sleep as soon as he could for several days. That was the reason why he told Lloyd the story of the Jewel of Truth in the Mermaid Kingdom.

Well, if he’s lucky, he’ll find it. He won’t bother me until then, at least.

Just then, an event from hundreds of years ago came to his mind. If he remembered correctly, it was the mermaid Houhorah who told him about the legend in the Mermaid Kingdom.

It was the story about the Jewel of Truth. She said she wasn’t sure whether it really existed because it emerged only once in the far distant past. It made her wonder if it was just a legend or folktale.

So the item was only mentioned briefly as if it was a silly joke. In other words, it was unidentifiable even among the mermaids in the kingdom. Its Korean equivalent would be the Dangun legend of a bear eating garlic and mugwort until it became a human woman.

Lloyd will have a hellish time trying to find the jewel. Even if he can find it, it’s going to take him a long, long time.

The corners of Verkis’s lips rolled up maliciously. He wouldn’t be bothered by Lloyd as long as the latter was off to locate the jewel. Verkis figured that he would eventually stop needing to call his wife every morning at some point while Lloyd was away.

If so, I’ll return back to my cave. There will be no reason to see that human or listen to his whining anymore. This is perfect.

The thought satisfied Verkis to the point that his toes under his blanket wiggled on their own in happiness. Verkis was happy at these thoughts of his, while Lloyd was happy that he had obtained a clue. Both smiled.

Haah, what an annoying fellow.

Haah, what a sleepyhead.

After that, Lloyd left the room, leaving behind the snoring Dragon King, and went to his study to find the location of the Mermaid Kingdom.

I read about it a few days ago.

Lloyd picked up the book of myths and legends once he got back. It was one of the books he had studied a few days earlier as he pulled the all-nighters.

I think I saw something about the kingdom somewhere here. Mermaid… The Mermaid Kingdom… Okay. Here it is.

Thankfully, Lloyd was able to find it quickly.

[Mermaids can dive into even the deepest and roughest part of the sea, and they hide their kingdom with their power in a nearly unreachable place. As such, generally speaking, no one knows where their kingdom sits, and only clues about its whereabouts can be obtained through the words of a few seasoned sailors who voyage through the most violent seas…]

Hmm…

Lloyd rested his chin in his hand, thinking.

The most violent seas?

Lloyd searched his memory. There were countless places mentioned in the novel. Some of them being in oceans and other similar locations. He pieced together, analyzed, and compared this information in his head. Thankfully, he was able to make a conclusion quite easily.

It must be in the north.

Lloyd was certain that it was. For starters, it definitely wasn’t in the south. Though quite a large sea sat in the south, it was occupied by a huge continent much like the one on Earth.

The Arctic Ocean is said to have the most violent waters in the Lorasian Continent.

It was the most dangerous and violent ocean on the continent, having waves that sometimes reached over 300 feet in height. He heard that icebergs floated along the terrifying body of water, and this particular ocean was a true nightmare for sailors.

But I was also told there are a few masochists who sail through these violent waters on purpose.

Those individuals in question were whalers hunting for emperor whales. Anyone who caught even one whale would be able to boast about it for the rest of their life. And so, it was rumored that the whalers were hoping to become rich overnight through this method.

And I’m familiar with the home port of the whaling ships.

Their home port was Port Cremo, the place where he had defeated the Gigatitan. From then on, he hurried to leave for the port. The travel preparation was simple. He just had to pack three days’ worth of food for his flight on Ggoming and some equipment for sleep, such as a tent and sleeping bag. Of course, he still had the task of convincing the count and countess aside from everything else.

“What? You will be visiting Port Cremo?”

“Yes.”

Clack. Lloyd shared his plan at the dinner table, joined by the count and countess. But, of course, he wasn’t entirely frank about the whole thing. He simply cooked up an excuse that he had to visit Port Cremo for research as he had a new business idea in mind.

Count Frontera looked slightly worried as he heard Lloyd. “But are you sure that will be all right?”

“Come again?”

“You have just come back from the capital. Did you not say then that you were going to take a long break for a while at home?”

“Yes, I did,” Lloyd replied.

“That’s the reason I am worried, Lloyd,” said Count Arcos. “I understand you are a hard-working man, but what is this talk about a new business all of a sudden? Did something happen that you can’t talk to me, your father, about?”

“Oh, no, that’s not it.”

“Really?”

“Yes.”

Lloyd smiled as he ate a forkful of salad. He could faintly feel the concern Count and Countess Frontera had for him. He could feel that he failed to lift the weight of concern from their shoulders.

Well. I wouldn’t believe myself, either. I must look like I’m rushing things for a reason that hasn’t been mentioned.

But Lloyd had no choice right now. Time was too precious for him to spend it lazing around. He had three years before the count got into an accident. Before that happened, he had to find a way to stop destiny from becoming restored to its original course.

So, please trust me, even though you may be worried right now.

Eventually, both of them pretended to trust Lloyd’s cursory response. But they still gazed at Lloyd with concern in their eyes. Lloyd looked at them with warmth in his eyes. He was grateful for them. He wanted to protect them no matter what. It wasn’t because he was afraid of being left alone in this world or because he needed them to live a comfortable life. Sure, it could’ve been like that a long time ago. But now, it wasn’t the case, more so after he had helped the real Lloyd reincarnate.

After all, there is something I must reveal to you.

Lloyd vowed that he would come clean in the future someday and repent for his sin. He didn’t care if they couldn’t forgive him. To him, it was more important to help them live a comfortable life after all the things they had done for him. And he was going to try his best to make that happen.

I wish to never relive that tragedy ever again.

Just then, his life in South Korea came to mind. There was something he direly wanted to erase but couldn’t, a memory so painful that it was unforgettable. The day he had to bid farewell to his parents, who chose to end their own lives.

I don’t want to experience that here.

At that time, in South Korea, he had been too naive and ignorant of the world and had nothing. He had failed to protect them. But now, he was different.

I can do it.

Lloyd was certain that he could protect them. He wanted to protect them. He never wanted to feel the same sorrow and regret he felt on that particular day ever again.

“I will do my best.”

Lloyd then grinned at them as if everything was all right, and his grin reflected his determination.
