# Along with Money: Hell (1)

---

“Let me take him with me.”

Whoosh. The wind blew. Lloyd’s fingertip pointed to Javier. The corners of Javier’s ears flinched. To Hell? Together?

I meant to obey his command if Master Lloyd made such a decision.

The image of the dragon that had suddenly appeared. Lloyd who had negotiated with the image. As he listened in to the conversation, Javier believed that he would be with his young master should the latter choose to help the soul of that dragon and descend to Hell. Javier had inwardly resolved so to himself. But now…

I resent him.

Javier’s eyes narrowed as they moved toward Lloyd. However, Lloyd didn’t care. Lloyd simply gave a big smile to Javier, and his brazen smile seemed to say, “So, you’re not going? You’re going to send me alone? Wow. Javier. I thought better of you.”

“…”

Javier decided to drop the matter. It felt like the roots of his soul were thrown into chaos. In a half-defeated way, Javier nodded. A wide grin hung on Lloyd’s mouth.

Whew.

“Yes, it looks like this friend agreed. I and this friend will go to Hell to help you. But!” Lloyd paused before continuing for effect. “I want you to vouch for the rewards you promised in return for our assistance.”

“Vouch?” Anticus cocked its head.

“Yes, vouch.” A meaningful smile hung on both sides of his lips. “There’s a vouch between parties even when they’re loaning small amounts of money. We’re helping you out in Hell out of all places by throwing ourselves in harm’s way. Don’t you think we need some kind of guarantee of a reward?”

“But I promised.”

“A verbal promise hardly means much.”

“Are you saying we should draw up a contract then?”

“No, I don’t think it’s going to have any effect on this.”

Lloyd shook his head. It was true. There wasn’t any effectiveness in signing a contract with the video letter the dragon left before it fell to Hell. The soul of that dragon lost nothing by violating the terms in it. Therefore, it had to offer something, a giant loss, if it didn’t fulfill its promises. That was the only promise and guarantee Lloyd could trust.

Thinking such, Lloyd said, “Swear on your soul.”

“What…?“

“You’re making a promise anyway, so I want a guarantee of it with the vow of the soul,” suggested Lloyd.

“What are you saying…?”

A look of dismay appeared in the image of Anticus, and the corners of Lloyd’s lips rolled up as he looked at it.

I knew it.

The vow of the soul. He commended himself for bringing it up.

That vow has more weight than a verbal promise.

It was a vow that could only be made once in a dragon’s lifetime on the utmost important matter. And humans didn’t know anything about it. It was exclusive to dragons.

And if one party breaks it, its soul is destroyed. Forever. There’s no way to bring it back. Its existence disappears from this world for eternity.

To be more explicit, it’d be like erasing a character in an RPG game.

“Human. How do you know about the vow of the soul?”

Its eyes were now serious. But Lloyd decided to act brazen. How else? I read the novel, Lloyd said to himself.

“But that’s not what’s important now, is it?” reminded Lloyd.

“That’s-”

“Vow or no vow? Answer me.”

“…”

"Without that much certainty, I won’t budge,” Lloyd said, setting his foot down.

Lloyd was being genuine here. There was a lot to gain by making a slave out of the Bone Dragon. The endless treasures and an extra life. But despite such tremendous benefits of being a member of the Dragon King club, it entailed traveling down to Hell. Sure, there was a safety device, an exit from Hell, but it didn’t offset all risks. So, he adamantly told himself that he’d not move an inch unless he was given a vouch that was proportional to the risks.

“Whew,” sighed Anticus. “Do you really want that vow?”

“Of course.”

“Goodness. I never imagined making this vow after my death and to a human at that…”

“Then you can leave it,” huffed Lloyd.

“H-hold on!”

“Anything you want to say?” Lloyd said as he lifted his chin.

“Of course I do. I did not say that I would not make that vow.”

“Are you scolding me for being impatient?” frowned Lloyd.

“That is not what I am doing.”

“Then, what is it?”

Lloyd stared at Anticus with the attitude that said, “Take it or leave it since I’ve got nothing to lose.” Eventually, Anticus raised the white flag.

“I will do it.”

“Do you really mean it?” asked Lloyd.

“Since I said that I would do it, the vow has been executed.”

“Awesome.”

Lloyd was happy. It was just like the novel. The vow of the soul took place the moment one uttered the intention to do it. There was no strange chanting, flashing a magic circle, or any other dramatic procedures involved. A vow was literally a vow. A promise was literally a promise. It happened the moment one said it would be done. That was it. And it was actually the scary part as well. The simple words, “I’ll do it,” held the weight of one’s soul as failure to fulfill the promise meant the eradication of their soul. Nonetheless, thanks to that, Lloyd was able to place his full trust in the vow Anticus had just made. Now, the latter’s gaze held warmth toward Lloyd.

“Thank you… for helping me.”

“Please do not mention it. Just make sure to fulfill your vow.”

“Of course. Once my soul is saved with your help, Dragon King Verkis will be your supporter. Now, then, I will open the path of Hell.”

Bzzt! As soon as Anticus was done, sparks buzzed in the air, and a circle portal that was about six and a half feet in diameter appeared in the air. Lloyd saw a glimpse of the scary scene inside with plumes of smoke bursting out of volcanoes. His skin stung from the hot and stuffy air blowing onto him.

“Here, the gate to Hell is open. The other side is Hell.”

“…”

“It is quite hot in there, so be careful. Make sure to be mindful of Satans that roam around Hell. They take joy in deceiving good souls and perverting them.”

“Then,” asked Lloyd, “where is your soul in this place?”

“I do not know.”

“…“

"But I suppose the King of Hell has his hands on my soul.”

“Whew.”

After hearing that much, Lloyd breathed in. Was this really happening? Who would have ever thought that he’d be meeting the King of Hell? Even though the gate to Hell opened right in front of him, Lloyd was still in a daze. But the decision was already made, and Anticus made a vow. And so, success in this job meant…

I will obtain the support of Dragon King Verkis.

The Bone Dragon. Everlasting treasure. Two lives. Add to that…

“Lloyd Frontera. In the end, do you plan on leaving my side like this?”

Lloyd turned and looked at the queen. She had been listening to the conversation with Anticus by his side. And so, she had become somewhat aware of his intentions to accept the dragon’s wish. The queen let out a chuckle, and Lloyd bowed courteously to pay his respect.

“Certainly not, Your Majesty.”

“Then?”

“Even having the support of the Dragon King, I will never leave your side,” assured Lloyd.

“Do you mean you will remain as my subject until the end?”

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“And yet, you are wary of doing the things I ask.” There was a slight aghast in her voice.

“That is-”

“I am already aware.” The queen’s bitter smile deepened. “I have experienced and seen today. And yet, why do you presume that I am ignorant?”

“…”

“Sir Asrahan at your side has completely ascended above the levels of a sword master at last. Although I have no way to ascertain that the feat he performed a moment ago was the level of a grandmaster, it is plain that no troops of mine can easily defeat him,” stated Queen Magentano.

“Your Majesty.”

“And now, you have the Bone Dragon at your command, correct?”

“That is true, Your Majesty.”

“Your knight who rose above the level of a sword master. Your obedient subject, the Bone Dragon. These two alone already signify that the powers of your house have surpassed the throne,” stated the queen.

“Forgive me, Your Majesty.”

“It is time that you do not have to ask for forgiveness.”

“Certainly not, Your Majesty. I wish to ask for your forgiveness on and on.” Lloyd insisted before hanging his head down and revealing, “I have told you this before, Your Majesty. I only wish to lead an ordinary and peaceful life in my fiefdom.”

“I am aware.”

“Yes, Your Majesty. As such…” Lloyd paused before speaking. “Dominions and powers do not interest me. All I wish is to relax in peace, so I still need Your Majesty’s support.”

Those words came from his heart. And sure, the queen was right that Javier, the grandmaster, and the Bone Dragon could overpower the House of Magentano. Seriously, he could wage war against any country on the continent. But Lloyd didn’t want to do that.

Why would I do that?

He could live happily without doing so. A happy, laughter-filled life awaited him at the fiefdom, where he was spoiled in many ways. He was free from worrying about being short on cash, rent or transportation fees, or unpaid wages. He was eternally liberated from such a life of agony.

And what? Dominion war? King? There’s no reason for any of that.

Lloyd didn’t want it, either. If he tried, he was certain he’d find himself under a terrorizing amount of work. It guaranteed he’d never be able to live lazing around like a rich landlord.

That won’t do.

So, Lloyd decided to keep things modest. He had to remain a favorable subject of the queen. He would enjoy life under her reliable rule.

To do that, I need her cooperation.

After organizing his thoughts quickly, he looked down. With precise calculations and an honest heart, Lloyd spoke to the queen.

“Therefore, Your Majesty, I shall not reveal Sir Asrahan’s new level and the existence of the Bone Dragon to the outside world. I only ask that Your Majesty treat me as an indolent subject.”

“An indolent subject?” asked the queen for clarification.

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“So nominally,” she presumed, “you will be my subject. But you shall refuse all the work I give you.”

“Certainly not, Your Majesty.”

“Then?”

“There is the support and assistance I received from Your Majesty until now. I am a man who not only remembers my enemies but also my saviors.”

“Really?”

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“Really?” she stressed once again.

“Yes, Your Majesty,” replied Lloyd with equal emphasis in his voice.

“Hmm. We will see,” said the queen.

“…”

“Anyway, you mean that you have room to interpret my assignment as a favor, that is, depending on what the job is. Your favor. Did I understand it correctly?”

“Truly yes, Your Majesty,” answered Lloyd.

“Tsk. You are a smart and impertinent man, but the problem is that I do not hate you.”

Queen Magentano clicked her tongue. She couldn’t help it as she could clearly see through Lloyd’s intentions. His desire was to be a slacker and live a relaxing life despite the massive power he possessed. He was asking for her cooperation to make that a reality.

Well. I should be relieved about it.

She pictured a fictional scenario for a second. What if, really if, Lloyd and his family decided to raise a coup? Would she and the royal family be able to suppress them?

No.

She swiftly drew a conclusion. It was impossible. The Bone Dragon alone required the mobilization of all the forces in the palace. Add to the Bone Dragon, there was Javier, the grandmaster. Her leadership would be destroyed. Her control system would fall apart. The number of remaining troops would mean nothing without any leadership, and they’d eventually scatter away. Thus, that would lead to the fall of the royal troops and the Magentano house.

“…”

Though she only imagined it for a moment, the mere thought made her hair rise. And so, the queen smiled at Lloyd Frontera, who was paying his respect to her. She appreciated him for making the suggestion to remain as her subject.

“All right. I shall grant your request.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

The queen nodded. Lloyd bowed to the ground. A content smile appeared on his lips.

The labor exemption. I finally got it.

His shoulders shivered as he was overcome with emotion. He’d have hula-danced and shouted in joy if no one was here.

As expected of the queen. I had faith in her.

A monarch with a generous and bold heart. A just and principled figure. Lloyd had trusted those qualities of hers. And it was from that faith that he was able to make such a request, and he was spot on.

It’s a relief. If the queen was a small-hearted person, then none of this would have worked.

A typical monarch wouldn’t be able to sleep at night so well in the knowledge of the subject outpowering the royalty. They might chuckle that everything’s fine, but behind their backs, they’d rack their brains to assassinate the subject or find ways to neutralize them. But Queen Magentano wasn’t like most monarchs.

She isn’t. She’ll instead use this opportunity to strengthen her power.

Of course, she would concern herself to develop a deterrence that would prevent Lloyd from getting too ahead. But the deal here was simple. Both Lloyd and the queen had to continue their life without clashing against each other. They would accept each other and co-exist. And to press on this choice to the queen, Lloyd intentionally accepted and joined the Dragon King club in front of her, declaring that he’d be joining hands with a power greater than herself. And now, against that backdrop, he suggested that she never bother him as he wouldn’t go berserk but instead respect the queen and live in peace in return. The queen saw his intentions right away and chose to support him. Satisfied with the deal, Lloyd rose and looked at the gate of Hell.

“Whew.”

The volcanic ash and lava ground appeared in vague shapes through the thick smoke inside the gate. The sight slightly made him feel more aware of his situation. It was time to go to Hell to become a member of the Dragon King club and be awarded endless treasures, the Bone Dragon’s unlimited labor, a live-once-more ticket, and enjoy other benefits. At the same time, all the demons in Hell detected the gaze Lloyd was throwing into the gate. They lifted their heads and grabbed their thumping chests. And they recalled the very title Lloyd was known for all at once in unison.

What?! Sir Frontera, the devilish singer, is making his way here?!
