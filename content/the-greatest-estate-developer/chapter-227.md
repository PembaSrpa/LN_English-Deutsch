# What Goes On Inside the Dragon (2)

---

“Please… help me…”

Нmm?

Lloyd blinked hard as he heard something strange. It sounded like a vague scream coming from hundreds of yards away. So, he wondered if he had heard it wrong. But that wasn’t the case.

The message appeared just a while ago, I think? It wrote that the option has been activated.

The option pertained to the optional skill, Half-human Half-dragon, added to his manaheart skill, which Lloyd had acquired after using the Supercharge skill inside the dragon head. The option had a simple effect. It would cause dragons to become curious about Lloyd.

It’s not a bad option.

Most dragons were hostile to humans. Like how people grapple their slippers or newspapers at the sight of a cockroach, dragons first thought about ending humans when they saw one. But with this option, Lloyd was spared getting killed without initially being inquired by the dragons. It made him an object of curiosity at first glance. Now, a message appeared stating that this skill had been activated. Lloyd focused on his hearing. Soon, the voice from a moment ago began to sound clearer.

“There… Can you hear me…? I am Anticus… An ancient dragon… I ask for help from the one listening to my voice…”

It’s real.

Lloyd did hear something. The voice continued to speak.

“I am Anticus… An ancient dragon… A member of the great tribe that once conquered the world… I ask help from the one who laid his hands on my bone…”

It was clear. Lloyd could postulate to whom this voice belongs.

Could it be that this was the Bone Dragon’s voice when it was alive?

It looked like so. Lloyd thought for a second.

Oh, sh… I kind of want to pretend I didn’t hear it.

Should I just ignore it and pretend I didn’t hear it? Such desire surged from his heart. It looked like helping the dragon out would result in a lot of bothersome work for him.

I don’t know what it wants, but I don’t want to get involved. I already have a lot on my plate now.

Lloyd gazed around. The Termina Garden. The very garden that used to be splendorous and marvelous. But now, it was likened to a room that housed 30 young children on Christmas night. In short, it was a mess.

I have a feeling that the restoration construction will be a fairly lucrative job.

And the money from the undertaking could be amassed in the storage back in the Frontera fiefdom to be used as his retirement fund. The mere thought of it overwhelmed his heart with a sense of reward. And so, Lloyd made up his mind to ignore the voice and was about to force-shut the Half-Human Half-Dragon option when…

"Oh, you who are listening to my voice… I sincerely request this for your sake… If you do not lend me your helping hand… My noble bones will break down into powder in three years…”

“I am here. I believe you need my help?“

Lloyd reacted at the speed of light, his eyes glimmering. The queen and Javier shot a strange glance at Lloyd, who was talking to himself from their perspectives. But Lloyd couldn’t care less about them.

Obviously. The bone is going to turn into powder in three years? The Bone Dragon will be gone? I can’t let that happen!

Lloyd hated doing favors when there wasn’t any benefit for him. But when the expiration date for the Bone Dragon was going to be three years after all that he had to go through, he had to help out and prevent that from happening. And so, Lloyd replied to the dragon voice, whose voice dripped with joy when it came back.

"Ha… A kind… human, I suppose. Are you not one of the warlocks that abused my bone…?”

“Not at all,” assured Lloyd.

“Is that so?”

“Yes. I’m not. On the contrary, I defeated them. Do you know a warlock named Targa?” asked Lloyd.

“I… am aware of the name. He excavated my body… And abused it.”

Lloyd sensed violence in its voice. And so, he jumped in and replied, “That villain. I took care of him.”

“You did?”

“Yes,” assured Lloyd. “Just a short while ago. Wasn’t he living in your parietal bone?”

“Seeing as you know that… I believe you are not lying…”

“Yes, that is what I mean.”

Since it was the age of self-PR, Lloyd earnestly appealed his accomplishments to the dragon voice. Thankfully, the voice warmed up a little. And evidence of the change soon appeared in front of him.

Fwoosh! The finger heart gesture of the Bone Dragon began to glow, and light gradually floated from the fingertip. The light then created a video in the air.

A film letter?

Lloyd sensed that it was, and he was right. A dragon skinned in green appeared in the video. The horn had the perfectly same shape, and judging from the state of the skin and countless scars, Lloyd postulated that the Bone Dragon was old. The image seemed to show the dragon when it was alive.

“What now…”

Queen Magentano’s eyes widened at the emergence of a dragon video floating high in the air. Hardly minding her, Lloyd focused on the video. The dragon in it opened its mouth and spoke, its voice loud enough for everyone to hear.

“This is the record left by Anticus, the ancient dragon, a fragmented letter kept in a glass tossed in the sea. Moreover, it is a gift and favors for the human who will help me.”

The dragon in the video, Anticus, rolled its ember eyes. And it lowered its body with dignity like a living dragon.

“First, I wish to know the name of one who responded to my voice.”

“I am Lloyd Frontera.”

Lloyd replied fast lest someone spoke for him. Anticus smiled with satisfaction.

“Good. Human Lloyd Frontera. Thank you first for responding to my voice. Are you ready to help me?”

“Um, I first wish to hear your conditions,” said Lloyd.

“Condition?”

“Yes.” Lloyd nodded before asking back to indicate his confusion as to why it was asking the obvious. “I cannot lend you my helping hand without listening to what it is about, right?”

It was truly what was right and the most obvious. That was what Lloyd thought.

That really is the case. The most troubling question in the world is this: Hey, can you help me? But the person never tells you what their request is about. They just ask for your help and demand an answer before anything else. What the heck, seriously?

Lloyd believed that the proper thing to do when you ask someone for help was to tell them what help you need. Lloyd hated getting tricked into a scheme and suffering from any losses or liabilities.

Even if the Bone Dragon expires in three years, I’d feel this way still.

Lloyd seriously wanted the Bone Dragon to be part of his Skeleton Corps. His heart thumped in immense anticipation at the thought of putting the monster to work. But what if maintaining the dragon’s form required a significant amount of effort from him? What if it came with an unexpectedly great cost? If so, he’d abandon the dragon without hesitation. Lloyd thought it was an obvious choice.

I mean, what other choice is there? How’s that different from buying a high-end car with a loan when you don’t have the money to maintain it?

So, first thing first. Lloyd had to have a clear understanding of what that dragon wanted. Lloyd stared up at Anticus with such desire in his eyes. It appeared that Anticus took the hint.

“Ha.”

A scoff played across its face.

“Yes. You’re right. Technically speaking, I’m the one begging for help. Fine. I shall do what you say. I will elaborate on my request.”

“Yes, I am all yours,” answered Lloyd, his voice happy.

“To cut short, my soul has fallen into the depths of hell.”

“Hell?” uttered Lloyd in mild surprise.

“Indeed.”

“Then how do you explain yourself in this video?”

“This video is really me, but it is not my soul. It is just a letter I drafted right before I was dragged to Hell. A magical letter I threw in the sea that is the world, using my bone as a glass bottle.”

“…”

Hmm. So, the video was just a video letter but with a considerable level of magical artificial intelligence. That was why it was able to mutually interact with another intellectual entity. That was what it seemed, concluded Lloyd.

“Well, anyway, what kind of help do you need when you’re in Hell? It can’t be something like dragging you out of it, right?”

Lloyd surmised.

“That is it.”

“What…?” Lloyd said in shock.

“Your guess is right. The warlock and his men that you defeated a while ago used my body for all sorts of evil purposes. My soul was resting when it was cursed and got dragged into Hell. So, I ask you. Please, go to Hell and save my soul.”

“Okay, goodbye.”

Lloyd bowed as soon as he heard Anticus. And he was about to instruct the Bone Dragon to shut this video. He would have done it had Anticus not yelled.

“Wait! You cannot turn down my request, can you?”

“I am.” Lloyd shrugged and turned Anticus’s way. “I’m not foolish enough to go to Hell!”

“But-”

“Absolute bogus,” defied Lloyd. “You see, I have a bright future ahead of me. I have no reason to set my foot in Hell yet, so why should I ride the Hell-bound express train to save you, a complete stranger?”

Lloyd was being frank. Going to Hell and saving a soul? Lloyd wondered if this dragon had lost it the moment he heard its words.

“I mean, it’s not like I’m a hero in a legend or myth or a son of a god. How dare you try to send a perfectly ordinary person to Hell? What are you, a siren? Aren’t you trying to drag me down with you to not suffer alone?” Lloyd huffed in almighty annoyance.

“That is not it!”

“Then I’m sorry, but why don’t you consider it your fate and stay there? I don’t want to go to a place like Hell. What’s more, I don’t know the way there either.”

“I can open up the gate to Hell right now.”

“No, please, I said there’s no need.”

Lloyd was flabbergasted. He had just shot a quick response because he heard that the Bone Dragon would become powder in three years if he didn’t assist it. It never occurred to Lloyd that this Anticus would have such an absurd request. He felt the same as when he opened his door for mail, only to be met by religious people trying to share their beliefs.

Hell-bound?

It was nonsense. He would refuse even if he was given ten dragon skeletons.

If that’s the condition, then I’m not going to keep the Bone Dragon. It kind of sucks, but I’ll live perfectly fine even without it.

Losing the Bone Dragon would be much more beneficial than being thrown into Hell. Safety came first, more than anything else. That was what Lloyd thought. Just then, Anticus rushed forward and started to speak.

“Wait! If you save my soul, I will provide massive, unimaginable rewards!”

“Excuse me?”

Unimaginable compensation? Massive rewards? Lloyd’s ears jerked instinctively the moment he listened.

Shoot. I shouldn’t listen to it.

Lloyd deliberated for a second. He then decided to hear it out first. So, he looked up without saying anything. Anticus spluttered in nervousness.

“Did I not say it a while ago? This video is the greatest gift and favor bestowed upon the human who will help me.”

“So, what is this great reward?”

“Official support from Dragon King Verkis.”

“Excuse me?”

Verkis? The official support from the Dragon King? While Lloyd was thinking about it, Anticus continued to speak.

“If you help me and lead me out toward the path of rest by saving my soul from the pit of Hell, I, Anticus, shall report your good deed to the Dragon King and the bravery filled with goodwill.”

“So, you’re going to set me up so this Dragon King can fund me?” asked Lloyd.

“Indeed.”

“What good is there?” asked Lloyd once again. Here came the important part. Assistance was good, but it had to be specific. He needed to check thoroughly what benefits he’d be getting.

It’s obvious. We go over the benefits of our plans when we sign a contract with a carrier for our phones. Even in supermarkets, people negotiate the accrual rate of their shopping points when they sign up for a membership card. This time, it’s over the Dragon King’s support. So, I should do some math and nitpick what gains I’ll be making in specific terms.

It couldn’t be any more obvious. Lloyd stared at Anticus with a gaze of a picky customer.

“What good is there for you?” Anticus replied in dismay.

“Yes.”

“Hmm, first of all, you will never lack money for your entire life. The dragon cave the Dragon King lives in is filled with more treasures than this world has.”

“Oh, oh,” exclaimed Lloyd in joy. “I like it. And?”

“You will have two lives.”

“Wait, I will be able to resurrect?”

“That is it. The magic of the Dragon King is said to be profound that even the dead can be brought back to life once.”

“And?”

“Those are the main benefits.”

“That’s it?” Lloyd asked to see if there were anything more.

“Who knows? Additional benefits may come depending on how much the Dragon King likes you. But I can promise you one thing.”

“What is it?”

“I can promise you the surest way to safely exit Hell when you help me out and go there.”

“The surest exit?”

“Yes. Just once. I can mark your hand with one disposable magic that can open up the exit gate of Hell anytime, anywhere you want.”

Anticus then stopped talking after listing the benefits of receiving the support of the Dragon King and the way to get out of Hell. Lloyd nibbled his underlip.

Нmm…

He was on the fence.

The Dragon King’s offerings are more appealing than I expected.

It was beyond his expectation. It wasn’t just about gaining a powerful supporter. A bottomless pool of treasure. The ability to resurrect. And if lucky, there were set to be more. Moreover, the Bone Dragon wouldn’t have an expiration date for its usefulness.

Goodness, this is bad. I’m intrigued.

The money and the Bone Dragon weren’t exactly what appealed to him. It was the chance for resurrection. This world was just too harsh for him to live a carefree life. And so, having a chance to resurrect once would bring more stability to his relaxed, happy, and carefree life.

But the condition is going to Hell. Whew.

That part bugged him. But at the same time, there was the minimal safety device, a chance to leave Hell at any time once. The benefits the dragon suggested were too appealing.

Hmm… With these benefits, I don’t think it’s entirely undoable.

Lloyd then threw a glance to the side. Javier was watching the conversation between him and the dragon without saying anything. Javier met the gaze. And he looked at Lloyd suspiciously as if he understood the meaning of Lloyd’s glance. Lloyd grinned without a word.

Yes, let’s just say that you’ve become a grandmaster for this purpose.

When Lloyd looked at Javier, he started to be more hopeful. The rough journey to Hell looked somewhat bearable, at least a little, with the grandmaster by his side. They could likely overcome most of the small obstacles that came their way. Lloyd decided to drag Javier down on this, and, at last, he reached a decision.

“Good,” Lloyd said, “With a generous heart, I shall help you, Dragon Anticus. But…”

Lloyd’s hand moved to point at Javier, who was looking at them with curiosity. And he shamelessly added, “That friend over there must go with me!”
