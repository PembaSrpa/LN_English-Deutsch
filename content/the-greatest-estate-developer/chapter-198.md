# A Cunning Man’s Survival Tactic (1)

---

“How come you are still not my son-in-law…?”

“Your Majesty…?”

The sultan’s question sounded like a blaze of fire as it rang beneath his throne. Shocked, Lloyd almost jerked his head up and committed the mistake of looking at the sultan. Lloyd swallowed.

What the heck? This guy’s more uptight than I thought.

He couldn’t help but form such ideas about the sultan because, after all, he came to report the good news that the construction was over and that the Kandahar region was free from drought. But here came the sultan, spewing this nonsense as soon as he laid his eyes on Lloyd.

How should I answer this?

Lloyd was flustered and wondered how he should respond to the sultan’s overly straightforward question. What reaction would save him from this distressing situation? Around the time Lloyd was thinking deeper about the

matter…

“What are you so surprised about?”

Lloyd heard the sultan chuckling.

“Lloyd Frontera, the son of Arcos Frontera, what ruler in this world would discover a talent like you and not become greedy after finding out you are unmarried?” said the sultan.

“Oh…”

“I was expressing my wish. That is it. I presume you did not like my daughter very much.”

“Did you notice that I figured it out already, Your Majesty?”

“She told me the details.”

“…”

Lloyd clamped his mouth shut. It looked like Sheherazade had already met with the sultan and reported everything as soon as she was back.

I hope she didn’t twist the story in her favor.

Lloyd remained silent, feeling antsy.

“An affair between a man and a woman,” the sultan went on, “cannot be forced if they do not see eye to eye regardless of what I think. Therefore, I simply spoke my wish out loud, so do not be too bothered.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

“You can be grateful to Your Majesty in your kingdom, not me.”

“…”

Lloyd suddenly felt he was getting scolded. However, to his relief, the sultan didn’t stay on the subject for long. “Now,” the sultan said, “let’s discuss more important matters. Have you completed the Qanat?”

“Yes, oh, great and omnipotent sultan.”

Lloyd, who direly wanted the subject to change, answered swiftly without missing a beat.

“After four months of construction, I was able to draw water from the foot of the mountain. It was all thanks to my efforts as I strove hard to fulfill the contract with the great and omnipotent sultan. Moreover, thanks to it, Kandara and most villages in the Kandahar region are supplied with water. Again, it was all thanks to my efforts as I strove hard to fulfill the contract with the great and omnipotent sultan.”

“So, the credit is due to your effort…?” asked the sultan, his voice slightly dumbstruck.

“I simply strove hard to fulfill the contract with the great and omnipotent sultan.”

“It sounds like you are warning me to fulfill our contract terms,” the sultan accused.

“How could a mere peasant as I dare do that, Your Majesty?”

“I think you are.”

“Not at all, Your Majesty,” denied Lloyd persistently. “As the great and omnipotent sultan is great and omnipotent, I have no inkling of doubt that Your Majesty will easily fulfill the contract terms Your Majesty made with a worthless man like me. Given that, there is no need for me to voice such a ludicrous statement." 

"I see… You are telling me to sit at the negotiation table with the Magentano envoys.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

“Tsk.”

Sultan Samarkhan clucked his tongue. What a competent and shameless fellow Lloyd was! And yet, he couldn’t hate Lloyd. It was the opposite. He wanted Lloyd even more.

How wonderful it would be if he were my son-in-law.

If Lloyd became his son-in-law, he thought about making the former his right-hand man and using all his talent to change the future of the sultan kingdom.

Alicia Termina Magentano. I wonder why that foolish woman isn’t actively using such a talented man. 

Samarkhan wished that she would hand over Lloyd to him if she was going to waste his talent. He couldn’t suppress the thought of it. That was how direly the sultan wanted Lloyd, who solved the drought in the Kandahar region. Not only that, there was the detailed report of Sheherazade and the delegation that went there with Lloyd.

I heard he received cooperation from the rebels. And after bringing the project to success, he even sent the rebels to an area my powers can’t reach.

The sultan almost clapped in amazement when he heard the report. That was how perfectly Lloyd did his job. Lloyd, the rebels, the people of Kandahar, and the sultan himself all benefited from his actions. It was that aspect of Lloyd that the sultan liked.

He’s not just good at his job or intelligent. He’s different from those that learned about the world through books.

His plan would’ve been impossible for mere bookworms to execute. It could only be done by someone who suffered through the tribulations of life. The sultan’s eyes held a glint of desire as they looked at Lloyd. But it soon disappeared to be replaced with a heavy gaze befitting his status as the desert’s ruler.

“I see,” said the sultan, his voice now sounding like a ruler of the deserts. “The exchange between us was a clear promise and contract. Since you fulfilled your part, it is now my turn. I declare that I will meet the Magentano envoys tomorrow.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

“I told you. You should be grateful to Your Majesty in your kingdom, not here.”

The sultan then chuckled and told Lloyd to leave now. After Lloyd was gone, the sultan glanced at the place where Lloyd was a moment ago.

“…”

Samarkhan felt bad that Lloyd was leaving. He was regretful seeing Lloyd slip out of his hands like this. So…

Let’s give it one more try.

The sultan nodded to himself slowly, and his eyes gradually reflected determination.

The sultan fulfilled his promise the following day. The negotiation took off, and Count Ventura entered the palace with a grim look on his face. The palace was unusually calm and quiet that day. There was something different about its atmosphere, something different from the usual air of somberness. Everyone waited for the results with bated breaths. Only the sultan and the envoy’s head entered the room. The others waited in anxiety about what would happen once the room doors opened up. The delegation. The sultan’s officials. The people of Ahinsya. Everyone felt the same except for one person.

“Master Lloyd, I suppose you are not too concerned.”

“Uh-huh.”

Lloyd nodded his head earnestly as if any other reaction was strange. Then he sucked the straw in his mouth hard. Slurp!

The juice of the coconut, which only grew in the nearby regions of Ahinsya, flowed through the straw to enter his mouth. It was sweet and sour. Salty and nutty. Cool and refreshing.

“This is what life’s about!”

The taste was too good not to let out an exclamation. The fruit cost one gold ring to just buy one, and the taste reflected its price. It made sense why even the royal emirs sparingly enjoyed this high-end and exquisite fruity flavor.

If I were to compare, I’d say this tastes similar to fresh, fizzy Coke.

Lloyd couldn’t believe that he could get the taste of such a beverage in this world. He eyed the coconut as his heart overflowed with emotion. Then he licked his lips in regret that he couldn’t have this back in his fiefdom.

“Why should I care about the negotiation?” blurted Lloyd, “I’d rather drink this stuff to my heart’s content before I go back home. Don’t you think so?”

“No, I do not think so.” Javier frowned pathetically at Lloyd. “This negotiation can alter the history of the kingdom. If it goes wrong, a war could break out. So, I say it is only right that you care for it.”

“But what good will it do?” asked Lloyd innocently.

“Excuse me?”

“It’s not like I can butt in this negotiation. And the result is obvious, so what’s the point?”

“How is it obvious?” said Javier as he cocked his head.

Lloyd grinned. “It will go well. Inevitably so.”

“How can you be so certain?”

“The sultan no longer has the reason to wage war.”

It was true. The sultan had been anxious to the bone about his people turning their backs against him due to the drought that plagued the Kandahar region. He had intentionally decided to start a conflict with the Magentano kingdom to shift the public’s attention elsewhere. But now, the situation had changed.

“I found the solution to their drought,” drawled Lloyd, “and though it’s really up to the sultan how things will go down the road, right now, there’s little worry about people turning their back against him. From the sultan’s perspective, there’s no reason to go as far as to wage a diplomatic conflict and war.”

“Then…”

“It’s most likely that he will acknowledge his responsibility for the Monster Domino incident,” said Lloyd. 

A brow lifted on Javier’s face. “Then can I ask you one more thing?”

“Yes, what is it?”

“If the conclusion was so obvious to them, there seems to be no reason for the talk to draw out this long. Why do you think it is taking half a day?” asked Javier.

“That, too, is simple.”

The corners of Lloyd’s lips pulled further up. “They are probably bargaining. I mean over things like how much liability the sultan will accept, the exact amount of compensation, and whatnot. Moreover, it reflects better on them to do this rather than cut the negotiation short.”

“Reflects better?” asked Javier.

“The sultan’s reputation. If the talk ends too soon, it will leave an impression to the people that the sultan GGed… No, that he easily surrendered,” explained Lloyd, correcting his words. “I wouldn’t be shocked if they’re sipping tea inside after they have already finished adjusting their agreement. That way, as the talk drags out, the sultan’s reputation will improve all the more to the public.”

“Is that so?” asked Javier.

“Yeah. So, as for us, we should just drink more of this.”

Lloyd spoke as he held up the expensive coconut.

“This is super expensive and all on the great and omnipotent sultan. One more thing, we can’t drink this even if we want to once we’re back home. You can ship it there, but I assure you, it’s not going to taste the same.”

“…”

“What are you doing? Come on now. Drink up!” Lloyd urged.

“…”

Taking the coconut Lloyd rashly held out, Javier let out a sigh. He sighed at length again when he saw Lloyd gulping on the coconut juice like a beggar who had gone hungry for months. This young master he served… Sure, the man was smart. But honestly, Javier was ashamed of him when he acted like this, being all materialistic.

Disliking Lloyd’s behaviors, Javier asked, “Can we not have an ordinary meal instead of something like this?" 

"Eh?” asked Lloyd as he removed the straw from his mouth.

“It is time for a meal.”

“That’s why I ordered this.”

“…”

“They’re not giving us food today,” informed Lloyd. “I canceled everything and told them to only bring a bunch of this fruit.”

“Why did you do that?” asked Javier in annoyance.

“I told you. You can only drink this here. It’s super expensive.”

“Haah… Seriously…”

Javier could not help but sigh. Eventually, he spoke, giving up.

“Then I shall have only one since I cannot skip my meal.”

He was hungry, and it was time to eat. Though he didn’t particularly want it, Javier grabbed one coconut with the intent to put something in his stomach and have a taste for politeness’ sake. Then he put his mouth to the straw and slurped. One sip. Two sips. Three sips. He slurped and slurped again. Before he knew it, the coconut was empty.

“Hey, you finished it in one chug.”

“…”

Lloyd giggled at him. It was only when Javier saw Lloyd’s face what he had done. But it was too late.

“You want one more?”

“…”

“No?” said Lloyd as he looked at the fruit. “Then I’ll have-”

“Give it to me, Master Lloyd.”

Thwack! Javier Asrahan, the historical hero of The Knight of Blood and Iron. He, too, helplessly was a young man in his 20s who couldn’t resist the taste of coke he experienced for the first time in the coconut juice in front of him.

The negotiation with the sultan and the envoy ended in the evening.

“It is done. We have succeeded at last.”

Count Ventura entered the reception hall, his face flushed red. The rest of the delegation jumped to their feet. “How did it go, Your Excellency?” one of them asked.

“You are smiling. Does that mean…” another asked simultaneously.

“Yes, it is right.”

Count Ventura beamed as he turned around and faced everyone.

“The sultan accepted our demands. Every one of them.”

“All of them?”

“Is that true?!”

“Hahaha! Do you not trust me? As I said, it was a complete success. The sultan acknowledged that he is responsible for the Monster Domino incident. Not only that, he has agreed to all our terms for compensation. On top of that, he even offered to cooperate on one more term.”

“What do you mean by that, Your Excellency?”

Everyone’s ears jerked up. It was the same with Lloyd, who was tapping his belly filled with coconuts in the corner of the reception hall.

An additional term? The sultan agreed to it?

That was strange. He could have just accepted his responsibility for the incident and provided compensation. It was strange that he went out of his way to make an offer that the envoys didn’t even ask for.

Why would he do that?

Lloyd was curious, so he inched closer to hear the conversation better. Count Ventura continued to speak.

“Ahaha. Now, do not doubt a single thing I say here. The sultan has even signed an agreement that promises the prevention of incidents like the Monster Domino from reoccurring. Do you see what I mean? The sultan has willingly put aside his authority during the diplomatic negotiation and offered his genuine apology.”

“Oh, oh!” one of them cried out.

“Is that true?!”

The confident announcement of Count Ventura excited the entire members of the delegation. And it probably was the reason why Lloyd, who was observing the scene from a corner, suddenly felt his stomach flip over and the coconuts coming back up.

A prevention agreement? That man did what? He was generous enough to sign that? Hold on. Shoot. Could he be…

A bad feeling gripped Lloyd, sending chills down his spine. At that moment, Lloyd jumped to his feet and rushed off to his room so fast no one could stop him, and at lightning speed, started to pack to flee that very night.
