# A Prison Called Lullaby (2)

---

“Master Lloyd.”

“Yeah.”

“Master Lloyd, you… You are truly hardworking.”

“Hmm?”

Lloyd’s eyebrows twitched. Javier’s icy stare grew colder.

“But you are not just hardworking. You are also truly dutiful.”

“Hey?”

Lloyd cocked his head to the side, wondering what was up with Javier. He had given Javier permission to insult him. Javier wasn’t the type of guy to let go of this chance. Could it be that he was sick? In between Lloyd’s thinking, Javier continued to shower him with praise.

“Moreover, you are detailed. Always meticulous and dutiful in your preparation for the future. Not only that, but you also stick to your plan once it is decided.”

“Uh-huh, really?”

“Yes. I feel a sense of devotion watching you. Contrasting other haughty nobles, there is something comforting and familiar about you, especially when you’re covered in dust during construction,” smirked Javier.

“Comforting? Familiar?”

“Yes.”

“Like what?”

“Like a mole cricket,” said Javier with a big smirk on his face.

“…”

“The way you dig dirt so industriously, drill holes so faithfully and flatten the earth so meticulously reminds me of that insect. In particular, when I lay my eyes on your ugly and wiggly bottom as you struggle to shovel up the ground is the spitting image of a mole cricket. Not only that, your bottom smells just the same.”

“Hey, I don’t smell,” defied Lloyd to protect his honor.

“I suppose you do not know this,” continued Javier, not interested in Lloyd’s honor, “but mole crickets eject brown liquid from their bottom, a terribly stenchy one. Have you ever smelled it? The scent is reminiscent of a cross between dog poop and rotten grass, so it is extremely similar to how you like to hold a grudge against people even when things are well over.”

“Hey…” muttered Lloyd.

“What is wrong? Did you not permit me to insult you?”

“Yes, I did.”

“So, I did it. I poured my heart out.”

“You should’ve just broken one of my bones instead,” said Lloyd with a bitter smile on his face.

With a fiercer glare, Javier added, “This insult is kind considering what kind of evil you’re capable of.”

“Well, are you going to hate me more if I tell you that I had no choice?”

Javier didn’t say anything. He merely stared at Lloyd with a piercing gaze.

“Tsk. No need to glare at me so hard.”

“Should I come up with a worse insult?”

“No, spare me, please.”

Lloyd forced out a pained laugh. It appeared that Javier somewhat understood Lloyd didn’t hold any bad intentions. Unlike his sharp remarks, his likeability wasn’t dropping by even a single point.

Well, actually, it’s not like I did a horrible thing to Sheherazade.

It was true. Sure, he did intentionally put a brake on her talent development with his lullaby. He could also admit that it was an unfair method on his part. But from another perspective, Lloyd had saved her life.

She lived because I constructed the Qanat. That has to count for something.

Lloyd shrugged. The favor he bestowed on her was beyond unbelievable for Javier, Sheherazade herself, and so many others in this world.

After all, I changed her future. I changed her destiny of dying after being swept into a rebellion.

However, no one would know the future had changed. Lloyd didn’t particularly mind that.

I should be satisfied with saving someone’s life.

He didn’t need any gratitude. He didn’t even want it. He shackled her with his lullaby, so they were even. That thought granted him peace of mind.

“Anyway,” Lloyd said, “I am looking forward to how she’ll react.”

“React?”

“Her life was greatly affected by my lullaby. So, I expect one of two reactions.”

“One of the two, you say. What are they?”

“She will come to find me here or remain in her kingdom.”

“Wait, do you mean that she will visit here because she cannot forget your lullaby?”

“Yeah,” said Lloyd in regret, licking his lips. “But given that she is nowhere to be seen, it looks like she has chosen the latter.”

“She will overcome the situation by herself, I suppose.”

“Yeah.”

“So,” asked Javier, “are you sorry?”

“No, not really.”

Lloyd grinned. He wouldn’t lose anything because Sheherazade didn’t come here. It didn’t change the fact that she was going to have a massive uphill battle to overcome her sword master syndrome after discovering the comfort of his lullaby. That was going to postpone her from becoming a sword master by about ten years and thereby delay the sultan from gaining more power.

“So nothing to be sorry about,” said Lloyd, “But, you know, no one knows. She might unexpectedly appear right in front of us.”

“Master Lloyd, no way,” Javier remarked with a small gasp.

“Hey, did you already get what I’m trying to say? You’re smart. Your guess is right. The moment she sets foot here, she’s going to work as a piece of high-end construction equipment, I mean, personnel in the Civil Engineering Corps.”

“…”

“I already wrote the employment contract just in case. Very meticulously.”

“…”

Dear heavens, save this poor woman. Javier was speechless. He direly wanted to tell her to run away if she was near the Frontera fiefdom or would be met with the same fate as him. Javier frowned, feeling uncomfortable and uneasy, and saw Lloyd grin at him. Then the two walked side by side as the night wore on. The following morning, Javier’s fears turned into reality.

“Bibeong!”

Morning arrived in the Frontera fiefdom. The sun pushed out the fog that carpeted the mountain at night. Count Frontera stretched his shoulder amid the moist air.

“Whew.”

He was tired. It was unexpectedly harder to do something he wasn’t used to. But he couldn’t stop now when he had already started the job. Thinking so, he gripped the trowel and got moving again.

Stab. The earth cracked, and a chunk of weed was pulled out. Beads of sweat dripped along his forehead.

“Whew…”

His legs ached. He wasn’t used to squatting this way, and it was his first time tending to flowers. His knees were sore, and his shoulders were stiff. But he was able to power through upon recalling the promise he made to his wife.

“My, you are feverish. Are you catching a cold? Please don’t overwork yourself tomorrow.”

It was around when they went to bed the night before. The countess didn’t look well. At first, Arcos Frontera thought something bad had happened to her, so he put his hand on her forehead. It was hotter than usual. A mild fever. So, he told her to rest and relax lest her cold worsens. A look of agitation appeared on her face when she heard him.

“No, I can’t,” said the countess feebly.

“Why?”

“Too many weeds have grown in the flower garden. I need to pick them out as soon as I can.”

“But your health matters more than the garden certainly.”

“But…”

“Leave it to me then.”

“What?” Her voice grew a pitch higher.

“I can manage a few days. I will take care of your flower garden for now. Would that give you peace of mind?”

“Dear…”

One of his wife’s only hobbies was taking care of their flower garden at home. It was so precious to her that she didn’t leave the job to maids or servants in the house. There was so much gratitude in her eyes when she heard him talk. Her hands had warmly tightened as they held his hands.

Yes, I made a promise. So, I can’t get careless just because I’m tired.

It was a promise he made to none other than his wife. The world could end, but he had to keep it. He had to exterminate all of these weeds despite his sore knees and shoulders. With that determination, he then gripped the trowel. Along with his awkward trawling, the weeds started to disappear little by little. His mind cleared as he focused. Everything around him began to fade, including his sore knees and shoulders. Instead, his heart swelled with a sense of pride.

Come to think of it, I am truly a happy man.

He really was when he thought about it. The financial crisis that burdened the fiefdom was resolved. The countless number of refugees had settled down. His days were free from worry. And what of his sons?

Lloyd.

A smile of satisfaction appeared on his face as he thought about Lloyd, the ex-troublemaker. He couldn’t forget the time when Lloyd claimed he’d build something called an “ondol” and make money. With a serious air around him, he had requested to borrow some soldiers to use as workers.

That kid, he’s still keeping his promise from then.

In return for his assistance, Lloyd said he’d give up drinking. The count didn’t believe him at first. The boy used to practically jump at the mention of alcohol, even when he was asleep. But, even now, he kept his promise. Not just that. Count Frontera had wondered if he was brought into this world to watch over Lloyd every time he saw how reliable and earnest his oldest son had become.

And Julian.

The same went for his second son. This one never troubled him very much. Always a faithful and wise son. But he had a streak of weakness. So, the count was somewhat concerned about how Julian would overcome this cruel world and how he would make a living when he wasn’t the family heir. There were times the count sighed to himself as the worry dominated his mind. But not anymore. He was ranked second in his academy when he graduated and was on a straight, clear path to becoming a high-level official.

I am… so happy, my sons.

In a blink of an eye, his two sons had grown to become healthy adults. They were ready to spread their wings and live their lives without needing his protection. That made him extremely proud, satisfied, and happy. His shoveling now reflected the strong emotions he felt. He smiled under the early summer sun. Just then, he heard an unexpected sound.

“Stop right there.”

“What business do you have here?”

The sound was coming from the main gate, and the voices belonged to the guards. Whom could it be so early in the morning? The main gate sat near where the count was, so all he had to do was stretch his neck up without having to stand. But the roses that bloomed along the fence blocked his view. He could only see the backs of the guards. The visitor behind the closed gate was covered by roses.

Judging by how the guards are reacting, it does not appear they are talking with a local.

The fiefdom wasn’t big. He knew all the people who lived just a couple blocks away. So; the guards’ cold attitude only indicated that the visitor was either a refugee or a foreigner.

Oh, well, the guards will take care of it.

Count Frontera scoffed. He couldn’t help it. It wasn’t his job to entertain every random visitor that came by. Furthermore, he was doing something important right now. He was decorating his wife’s precious flower garden. Not only that, he had gotten comfortable being squatted, and getting up or changing his position would make his legs hurt more.

And so, he kept digging with his trowel, remaining mindful of the conversation between the mysterious visitor and the guards.

“Excuse me, is this the Frontera estate?”

Count Frontera cocked his head when he heard the voice of the visitor. It looked like the visitor was a woman, and there was something different about her pronunciation and accent. Not from here. As the count thought so, the guards continued to entertain her.

“Yes, this is the Frontera estate. Please identify your place of origin, status, and purpose of your visit.”

“Me?”

“Yes.”

“I’m from the sultan kingdom in the east.”

“From the sultan kingdom…?”

“Yes.”

“Th-then what is the purpose of your visit?”

“I come to meet the eldest son, Lloyd Frontera.”

“Young Master Lloyd?”

“Yes.”

Her voice was clear and confident. The count stopped his shoveling.

She’s here for Lloyd? From the sultan’s kingdom?

The count cocked his head. A woman from a neighboring kingdom. Here. To meet him. It was unusual for sure. He couldn’t just sit and watch as a spike of curiosity arose in him.

What business does she have with my son to travel all the way here for him?

At last, the count let go of the trowel and got up, supporting his soring knee with his hands. His view elevated as he moved. Over the rose bushes, he finally caught a glimpse of the visitor. Quite tall. Traditional clothes of the sultan’s kingdom. Light brown skin. Sharp nose and confident face. Dark pearly gaze.

“My name is Sheherazade from the sultan’s kingdom that sits in the east,” she divulged confidently. “To tell you about my purpose, I came to meet Lloyd Frontera, the eldest son of this estate.”

“May I ask that you reveal your reason for meeting him?”

“Yes.”

The woman nodded. She drew breath to reveal the purpose of her visit. Count Frontera concentrated. At last, she went on in a confident voice.

“I can no longer go to sleep without him.”
