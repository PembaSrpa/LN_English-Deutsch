# New Level (1)

---

Ziiing! When the Supercharge optional skill was activated at full speed, the three strands of the mana circle rotated quickly, gradually picking up speed and swerving around. The flow of mana rippled like water in a tub, gurgling and swirling down as it was drained. The skill began to absorb all the mana in the air. No, it started to eat it.

Fwoosh! The surrounding air roared with noise, and the dust particles in it thinned out. Even the microorganisms stuck to the dust weren’t an exception. The bacteria that swayed around the cilia and the virus that sought the chance to leech in them lost the mana loaded in their molecules and wilted as a result. Of course, the larger targets around Lloyd, like the head of the Bone Dragon, weren’t exempted from the blackhole either.

Cruuush! Lloyd was inside the ventricle of the Bone Dragon, and an ominous din rang across the whole space. The dragon’s joints clacked as its bones twisted and turned when its mana was usurped away.

Crack! Crush! Crack! The colors of the ventricle changed, and it started to shake as though there was an earthquake. And as if the dragon was writhing in sudden destruction, the whole space that fell within the range of the Supercharge was distorted and abused. This was happening while Lloyd was in a trance, so he didn’t notice any change happening. With his eyes closed, he simply plunged everything around him into destruction as he remained still in silence like he was in a cocoon. How did the Bone Dragon react? It simply shook its head as if there was nothing wrong.

What is this?

Warlock Targa murmured in dismay. Lloyd Frontera had been inside his head since earlier, and Targa could sense that the pest was onto something. The nearby mana was being sucked with an intimidating force. That frightened him at first, but just for a second. After that, Targa was unperturbed.

Well, I don’t know what he’s doing, but he’s absorbing mana violently. And quite amazingly.

The Bone Dragon carried far too much mana for him to be scared of what was happening. There was a scary and even stupid amount of mana in its body. So, Targa didn’t feel nervous at all, even when Lloyd was absorbing his mana at a fast speed. Unbeknownst to Lloyd, what he was doing right now was the same as bucketing out water in a city-sized lake. That was how much mana the Bone Dragon held.

In short, he’s trying to empty a lake with a bucket.

Of course, Lloyd could empty it. But that would require an obscene amount of time. A few years. Or maybe even decades. That was what Lloyd’s effort was. A laughable deed from Targa’s perspective.

Should I try hitting my head again?

Targa wondered if he should use the same attack he used on Lloyd earlier. But he decided to wait out. With the mana being sucked out of him now, he suspected that his skull was slightly more brittle than usual. It would cause a huge problem if he needlessly launched an attack and created a crack in his skull.

I should wait a little longer.

And just like that, Targa decided to wait.

He won’t be able to absorb my mana forever.

That was obvious. Even the most powerful skill was inevitably limited by the threshold of mana the user could take in. Since Targa was once a warlock, he knew this very well. So eventually, the absorption would come to an end soon. He just needed to wait for a bit. That would be enough. Targa’s prediction was proven right. Soon, the Supercharge reached its limit.

Ding dong.

[The Asrahan Core Technique Skill Option 5: Your Supercharge time is up.]

Ziiing… Inside the Bone Dragon’s skull, where Lloyd was sitting now, a message appeared in front of his eyes. The mana circle had calmed now, and the violent rotation slowed, putting an end to the fierce and indiscriminate mana absorption. Lloyd slowly opened his eyes.

“Whew…”

This special sensation that he could feel after using the Supercharge, which he had gotten familiar with, ran through his body as he opened his eyes. He skimmed through the messages that rushed out in front of him.

[The manaheart skill has been upgraded.]

[Skill Name: Manaheart.]

[Stage: Mid-Level Sword Expert Lv. 10]

[Physical Ability Improvement Rate: 1,200%]

[Currently Owned Optional Skills: 1. Shock Neutralizer / 2. Complete Resistance to Poison / 3. Frenzy]

[Required RP to level up: 1,700]

[Your manaheart has experienced exponential growth in a short time by absorbing a large volume of mana of the Bone Dragon. In the process, a small portion of the dragon mana has settled in your manaheart. For this special experience, your skill is awarded new optional skills.]

[Skill Option 4: Half-Human Half-Dragon - By coincidence, a part of your manaheart has come to possess a nature unique to dragonheart. For this, every dragon that meets you will show curiosity instead of hostility. Of course, it is up to you to develop that curiosity into favor.]

Woah. Lloyd was shocked after he finished reading. His manaheart leveled up to own a part of the nature of the dragonheart and make the dragons curious about him as a result. But there was something else that caught his attention.

I’ll become a high-level sword expert if I level up my manaheart skill one more time.

Lloyd felt a shaft of panic run all over his body along with anxiety. He was scared that he’d suffer from sword master syndrome once he mistakenly became a high-level sword expert. He’d be an insomniac. But Lloyd brushed the thought away. There was a bigger issue at hand.

What about the Bone Dragon?

What could have happened? Lloyd wondered, thinking that the Supercharge would have knocked out this dragon very well. Lloyd believed that the inside skull would have taken the full blow since the skill ranged over 22 feet.

Then let’s head out quickly.

Lloyd quickly jumped to his feet. He knew that the Supercharge wouldn’t be enough to end the Bone Dragon in one go no matter how powerful the skill was. But he expected that it would have left a strong blow. A severe migraine. Or a short faint. Anyway, Lloyd figured that he should slip out at this chance.

I didn’t expect to defeat the Bone Dragon anyway.

The purpose of this whole thing rested on buying some time for Ggoming to locate Javier and take his badly shaped body to safety. Lloyd’s plan served solely that goal.

This is enough.

Lloyd didn’t expect anything more. And he didn’t want to pick a fight with this monster anymore. So he decided to head out and run away without so much as a glance behind. Resolving such, he attempted to slip out of the ventricle through a hole when a destructive impact swept over his whole body.

Boooom!

“…!”

Could this be what a beetle inside a soft drink felt like while being thrown against a concrete wall?

“Argh!”

With a crushing noise, Lloyd was thrown to the other end of the wall, his whole body rammed into it. For a moment, he felt his bones dislocated, and he even wondered if his inside had spilled out of him. His vision grew dark for a split second. He couldn’t tell which was up, down, left, or right. He lost all senses.

Gargh..! What the!

Lloyd shook his head in panic, and amid his slowly recovering vision, he continued to think.

Is the Bone Dragon perfectly fine… even after being directly hit with the Supercharge?

Chills ran all over his body. Lloyd wanted to deny it wasn’t the case, but he couldn’t. The very same thing that took place a while ago happened just now. The Bone Dragon had inflicted damage on Lloyd by striking its own head. Nothing had changed from then. So…

The Supercharge skill didn’t do anything to harm it.

Everything became dark around him. It was the blow that blinded him a while ago. But now, it was the overwhelming sense of abyss that clouded his vision. Lloyd, however, gritted his teeth in spite of the looming despair. He started to crawl.

Like I would let myself die in here!

He couldn’t die here. He couldn’t die for someone else, that is, buying time to save Javier. It was too unfair.

I didn’t get to laze around to my heart’s content yet.

His life had been full of toil and hardship. He had lived a miserable, lowly life as the bottom inhabitant of the social pyramid in South Korea, living in an impoverished room rental. It was pretty much the same in this world. A pile of crippling debt pressured him from the start, and he had to work his butt off to clear it. He had overcome several life-or-death crises one too many times.

That’s how I turned my fiefdom into a livable place! And all that awaited me was rest and happiness!

Dying in the hands of the Bone Dragon now? That was too unfair, the strong resentful feeling inside him only strengthened his desire to survive.

No. I’m going to live. I’m going to live no matter what! I’ll live!

With a shovel in one hand and raising his nails, Lloyd crawled with utter desperation. He removed himself from the ventricle, passing through the passages of optic nerves, and finally slipped out of the eye socket. The fresh night air rushed into his chest as he breathed.

“I did it…”

Lloyd beamed. Just then, the Bone Dragon smacked its head once more. Bam! Bam!

“…!”

Lloyd snapped open his eyes, and the sheer force helplessly sent him to fly off. If he were a beetle locked inside a thrown can a while ago, now, he felt like an unbuckled driver hurled out of his car as the car collided. Amid the hazy consciousness, Lloyd gawked at the woods speedily passing 500 feet below him as he was flown away into the night sky. A scoff escaped him.

I think I was too… thrifty.

A pang of regret ran through him. RP. CP. He had saved them like a miser.

I should have splurged my RP and become a high-level sword expert.

Had he done so, he might have been able to hold up a little longer than now. He could have avoided this miserable situation where he was thrown to the ground, all helpless and impuissant.

Or CP. I should have used my CP.

Lloyd wished he had used the Ending Spoiler, at least. It’d have then allowed him to brace for this ridiculous final moment of his. He could have predicted today and planned ahead.

I was a fool. I had been too careful.

He felt like a fool for deciding to save the CP because each usage of the Ending Spoiler amplified the points the next time. He had decided to spend it only on occasions he was 100% sure about his bad feelings. Save, and save again. Otherwise, he wouldn’t have enough when needed.

I didn’t know things would end like this.

His half-hazy gaze moved, and as the gust of cold wind wrapped him, he saw the ground coming close to him at a scary speed. The rocks protruded from the ground here and there, and Lloyd figured that he’d be jellied once he landed there. That, Lloyd told himself, was the spot he’d die. Shivers ran down his spine. He opened his eyes wide, trying hard to be cool about the incoming death. But right before he was nailed to the ground, someone grabbed his belt.

Clack! “Lloyd Frontera! Wake up!“ It was the booming voice of the queen. Lloyd felt a massive grip force along with the shout. He answered his savior’s calling violently.

"Argh!!!”

Lloyd dry-heaved as the momentum of the fall was broken by the grip, which gave a sudden tightening to his stomach. He felt like he was going to be split in half.

“Wergh! Achew! Arrrgh!”

Lloyd felt a severe pang of pain even though he was only half-conscious. The pain was so great that he couldn’t faint. But thankfully, it allowed him some sense to look around.

“Get on my back, now!”

The Queen?

In no time, he realized that the queen was carrying him on her back and starting to sprint off toward the garden. She jumped over the shrubs and broke the sculptures that stood in her way with her sword. She broke through, breaking everything. A violent race and scattered breathing. Heat transferred to Lloyd from her back, and he even felt her desperation. She was running away with him on her back, away from the Bone Dragon pursuing them. A snort escaped Lloyd just then.

“Your… Majesty.” Each word spilled heavily out of Lloyd’s mouth. “Why… are you saving me?”

“Why, my foot!” The queen bawled. “I am saving you because you will die if I leave you alone! Is it your wish for me to abandon you and run away?”

“Please do not do that…” Lloyd answered with sincerity.

He couldn’t utter words like, “Your safety comes first, Your Majesty. Leave me and run away.” He wouldn’t dare say those. He was afraid that the queen might agree with his meaningless words and leave him saying, “Yes, it hurts me, but you are right.”

I don’t want to die.

Lloyd struggled and lifted his head. Carried on the back of the racing queen, he looked behind. The moon was nowhere to be found. Instead, he saw a giant figure ready to sweep his way. It was the Bone Dragon hot on their heels.

“…”

The queen was slow, Lloyd noticed. At this rate, they would get caught.

“Your Majesty,” called Lloyd.

“What?!” She snapped.

“I entreat you to run faster.”

“I am doing that already!”

“Take my mana, Your Majesty.”

“…!”

Her back flinched for a second when Lloyd placed his hand on her shoulder and squeezed the last bit of mana in him to transfer it to her.

Ding dong.

[You are now exhausted. Therefore, you are under the influence of the Asrahan Core Technique Skill Option 3: Power Saving Mode. Your mana amplification rate is fixed at 25%, and the whole amount of mana you can transfer to someone is limited to 2.5%.]

Ziiing!

It was a mere 2.5%. But that was enough. Even a bit of strength was better than nothing. That would still slightly increase their chances of living.

“By now, the royal forces must be ready to attack,” Lloyd pointed out.

“I know!” retorted Queen Magentano.

“To that way… You should run toward the forces.”

“I am doing that!”

Lloyd encouraged the queen, and she started to race more hurriedly. He wondered why the reality was so harsh in moments like this. Why was that Bone Dragon flying to catch up on them without ever getting tired?

Baaang!

“…!“

A blaring noise roared, and the dust storm blew. Lloyd sensed that the queen stopped in her tracks fast.

"Oh…”

He then heard her collect her raspy breath and mumble. Lloyd lifted up his head too, and there was the Bone Dragon standing in their way like a wall of despair. His gaze met the dragon bending over.

“Ha, haha…”

Laughter escaped him. It was over for real now. He had done everything in his power. Nothing more came to his mind, even when he beat his brain. At that realization, Lloyd burst into fits of laughter. And as he felt her shoulder tensing in fear, as he stared at the giant silhouette of the Bone Dragon coming in his way, Lloyd sensed his last moment. And he forced the corners of his lips to rise amid the terrifying shivers. And he thought to himself…

Javier. I’m going to die here. But you should live, you rascal.

Lloyd inwardly begged Javier to survive with the time he had bought for the latter. Take good care of Ppodong, Bangul, Hamang, Bibeong, and Ggoming. And the good Count, Countess, and Julian. Feeling a little bit unfair about this whole thing, Lloyd added that Javier should have a fat belly, balding hair, and athlete’s foot because it would be too unfair if this boy gets to live so well without him.

So…

Lloyd looked up. The Bone Dragon had its mouth wide open now. It was rushing in his way, closing as it neared to eat them up. Its pillar-like fang was fulgurated. Just then, a sword ray shining brighter than the fang appeared in a flash and blocked it.

…!

He didn’t make any sounds. There wasn’t even time to feel anything. Right when something seemed to have glimmered, Lloyd opened his eyes wide.

Javier?

Out of nowhere, Lloyd saw the back of the silver-haired knight. Javier was standing in front of Lloyd, blocking the latter’s way as though Javier was protecting him and fighting against the enemy. And when his long sword let out a force that split into thousands of iridescent auras, something popped into Lloyd’s head. The novel. The image in the story. The final level Javier reached. That was…

“Grandmaster?”

The moment Lloyd mumbled, the Bone Dragon was sliced into thousands of pieces.
