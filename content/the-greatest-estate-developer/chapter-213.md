# His Aspiration to Be a Slacker (1)

---

Viscount Nort. Soon to be 56 years old, he was a landscape architect with an impressive career. He was only a young apprentice when he started to show his talent in gardening. His hands transformed countless wild and ruined gardens into beautiful works of art. He soon rose to become a world-class gardener.

But his career didn’t stop there. His focus shifted to civil construction. From simple landscaping, he ventured into designing the actual sites and divisions of land in gardens. Soon enough, he rose to become an unrivaled figure in garden construction and management. His talent alone elevated his status as a peasant to a viscount. As such, it went without saying that the man held great pride in his skills. In particular, he thought he was significantly above a young engineer who had just started to make his name known in the past few years.

Hmm, I suppose this is Lloyd Frontera.

This man was much younger than what was suggested by the rumors. He was practically a child. The queen had shared a few things about him, about how he is the rising star and how he’d join a large, upcoming garden construction. About how he constructed that stunning Lloy-Javi suspension bridge. But really, it never occurred to Viscount Nort that Lloyd would be this young.

He’s young enough to be my son.

A flicker of vague dissatisfaction appeared in Viscount Nort’s eyes as they watched Lloyd. A youngster that hadn’t even turned thirty yet. What was he doing at the same age?

I was making my name as a gardener until I was exposed to design work for the first time. Then I had to work and study for ten more years. But this kid. He what? He’s gaining a reputation as a developer at such a young age?

Truth be told, Viscount Nort was a little dumbstruck. He, who was initially a commoner, figured that Lloyd was different from him. Having been born into a noble family, this kid was able to happily study in peace. The viscount felt a surge of distaste, and his voice reflected his displeasure as he introduced himself to Lloyd. 

“So, you are the eldest son of the Frontera family?” blurted Viscount Nort.

“Yes, nice to meet you, Viscount Nort. I have heard a lot about you,” replied Lloyd.

“The queen has spoken about me?” asked Viscount Nort, a shade surprised.

“Yes,” Lloyd said, “she said that you are an excellent developer that I should look up to as a role model.”

“Ahem. Hmm!” Viscount Nort cleared his throat. “You flatter me. I, too, heard a bit about you.”

“Is that so?” Lloyd looked at Viscount Nort.

“Yes, I heard you’re talented and quite accomplished.”

“Haha, you are too kind.”

Lloyd plastered a shy smile, and he thought to himself…

This man doesn’t really like me.

For some reason, the viscount was staring at Lloyd with fairly obvious distaste. Just then, Lloyd recalled how the queen had explicitly shared her sentiments with him a moment ago.

“What?” asked Queen Magentano. “The dragon bone?”

The queen’s question came out in a snap, and Lloyd clearly caught the flicker of dismay that crossed her eyes, which was replaced with a hearty chuckle.

“What? Ha! My goodness. Lloyd Frontera.”

“Yes, Your Majesty.” Lloyd pretended to not hear her reaction.

“The dragon bone must be what you wanted in the first place, am I correct?”

“Forgive me, Your Majesty.”

Lloyd quickly bowed. It was true. He was after the dragon bone in the first place. That was why he shook her up with the mention of a ten-year exemption of labor. If she didn’t take it, oh, well. If she did, awesome! Lloyd suggested his initial deal with that thought in mind. The smile on her face became slightly bitter.

“What you want is the dragon bone, but you used the labor exemption to attract my attention,” the queen pointed out in annoyance. “Why do you subtly keep bringing up the latter?”

“I wish to rest, Your Majesty.”

“Ha. And now, you say it out loud.”

“It is my genuine wish, whether it is granted or not, Your Majesty.”

“However,” said the queen in a reminding tone, “I’m sure you’re aware it is an impossible wish for me to grant.” “Please allow a man to dream, Your Majesty.”

“You are free to dream, yes. Anyway…” The queen went on, her gaze meaningful. “A chunk of dragon bone? Are you aware of how valuable this is when you dare ask for it?”

“Certainly, Your Majesty,” affirmed Lloyd.

Lloyd didn’t even bother to avoid her gaze. He shamelessly responded to her in a heartbeat. “I believe it truly matches the value of the earnest work I do for Your Majesty.”

“Really?” snapped the queen.

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“Ha. You are brazen.”

“Please forgive my rudeness, Your Majesty.”

“Fine… Just a piece of the tailbone.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

“But it cannot be a lot. I will give you a small end piece that won’t be too noticeable.”

“I am overwhelmed-”

“More like underwhelmed, aren’t you?”

Could it be that the queen thought she snubbed him? A mischievous smile appeared on the corners of her mouth. It was a rare sight.

“Even so,” she carried on, “it will be a sizeable chunk that’s as big as a single carriage. You can’t possibly be dissatisfied with that, right?”

“Certainly, Your Majesty,” affirmed Lloyd.

“Good.”

Just like that, the negotiation with the queen ended. A construction contract was signed, and Lloyd was told that he’d be working with Viscount Nort for the project.

“Follow this servant, and you will get to meet him,” the queen instructed. “Since Viscount Nort is a talented man in the field of garden design and management, he will be a helpful partner for you in this project. Oh, and…”

Lloyd was about to give a polite farewell and leave when the queen stopped him.

“Viscount Nort has tremendous pride when it comes to his work and accomplishments,” the queen said as an afterthought. “If possible, please be understanding of that pride of his.”

So, this is what she meant.

Lloyd suppressed a wry smile from his face. He was confused about what the queen meant when she said those words. But he understood her now that he was introduced to Viscount Nort.

This man is jealous of me.

Lloyd could feel it. Viscount Nort was visibly displeased with him. His stare seemed to say, “You’re nothing but a spoiled brat who got lucky.”

His snobby attitude didn’t change even afterward.

“Well, anyway, now that I have the chance to work with such a reputable young developer, let me ask you a question. Have you ever designed and constructed a garden all by yourself before?”

“No, I have not,” stated Lloyd expressionlessly.

“Tsk. Is that so?” Viscount Nort frowned.

“Indeed.”

“That will be an issue. I cannot wrap my head around how you earned your great reputation when you have never built a garden before,” grumbled the Viscount.

“It was all luck,” drawled Lloyd.

“That was it? Hmm… Tell me, how many years of experience do you have in construction?”

“Hmm, just a few years.”

“It can’t possibly be less than ten, right?”

“It is.”

“Tsk, tsk, tsk. That is also an issue.” Count Nort clucked his tongue in pity.

“How exactly is it an issue?” asked Lloyd, sounding absolutely unfazed.

“You see, my boy, many years of quality experience is what makes someone stand out on a work site. Am I wrong?” Count Nort spoke as though he was talking to a child.

“You speak the truth,” seconded Lloyd.

“Do you agree with me as well?”

“I do.”

Then as though he finally saw the chance, he snapped, “Then I suppose you have no qualms when I take over the command of this construction project?”

“My, by all means,” replied Lloyd with glee.

“Really?” asked Count Nort in surprise.

“Yes.”

“But, if so, I will be the one who is remembered for constructing this garden.”

“I am aware of that,” Lloyd tonelessly said as he nodded.

“And yet it is all right?”

“My, of course.” Lloyd nodded hard to sound genuine.

Lloyd nodded as if he truly didn’t mind. With a bright, earnest smile, he happily welcomed and accepted Count Nort’s suggestion.

“How can a novice like myself work next to you side by side, Viscount Nort?” asked Lloyd, shaking his head. “It’s nonsense. Don’t you think so?”

“Yes… It is,” replied Count Nort, slightly dazed by Lloyd’s willingness.

“Yes. Viscount Nort, you are wise. What can a novice like myself know? I’m only known for a couple of noteworthy construction projects. My reputation is a bubble soon to burst. The achievements you obtained with persistence and hard work are much more legitimate.”

“Ahem. Hmm!” coughed Count Nort, suppressing his grin. “You definitely… know a thing or two, eh?”

“Oh, not at all,” Lloyd said, waving his hands. “I merely follow along by watching brilliant senior developers like yourself work at construction sites. Do you disagree?”

“Hahahaha!” Count Nort finally erupted in joy. “Is that so?”

“Yes. Honestly, I had planned on bringing this up first and asking you to lead this construction project.”

“Did you now?”

“Yes. If anything, I am sorry that I senselessly made you speak on this matter before I did.”

“Ahem! You do not have to apologize for that…”

“No, no. I am truly sorry. I want to express my sincerity.”

Lloyd then offered his hand. Viscount Nort heartily grabbed it with both of his hands and shook it politely. And Lloyd met the viscount’s eyes, tensing his own to appear genuine. And to top it all off, he groveled, “I am honored to support an excellent man like you, Viscount Nort, for this construction. I kindly ask for your guidance.”

“Hahahaha! Well, well, the pleasure is mine. Ahem. Hmm!”

The viscount’s face, which was as rough as a piece of sandpaper, melted away like snow in the spring. His chin lifted in pride and satisfaction as Lloyd buttered him up. An irrepressible smile hung on the corners of his wrinkled mouth, and he was slightly embarrassed by his attitude a while ago.

Oh my. I made a mistake. I simply thought he earned his reputation out of pure luck.

Count Nort had regarded Lloyd to be an insolent and impertinent brat. But now, he didn’t think that was true. He realized from their conversation that Lloyd was an excellent and humble fellow with good character.

He surprised me.

Count Nort’s gaze relaxed. He was a young man who knew how to respect an experienced senior and stay humble in spite of his fame. Count Nort began to see Lloyd in a new light. He had thought of the latter as an immature child just a moment ago, but now, he seemed quite smart. Seeing his thoughtful words and handshake, Count Nort could not believe how polite he was.

“I wish I could always work with someone like you. Hahahaha.”

Viscount smiled with a glow of satisfaction. The change in his attitude rendered the same feeling to Lloyd.

Good. Excellent. Operation Moderation was a success.

From the start, Lloyd never planned on letting the queen exploit him. He also didn’t want to take on this job in the first place. And here came the viscount eagerly volunteering to supervise and lead the whole project! Lloyd could only welcome him with wide open arms.

There is a saying in the military. Work in moderation. Don’t stand out too much.

If you needlessly showed off, you would only be given more responsibilities and work. More often than not, it created hassles that could’ve been avoided. This was true based on Lloyd’s past experiences as well. He remembered the first time he was given a placement. He was anxiously sitting upright at a corporate administration office when a sergeant walked up to him, asking whether Lloyd was really a civil engineering major and any good at shoveling.

Then I foolishly rushed to say that I was.

There wasn’t any grand reason for his answer. He simply wanted to earn some brownie points by making a good impression. But unbeknownst to him, he had opened the hellish gates of endless labor.

Dammit. I should have stayed put at that time.

His life in the army wouldn’t have been a disastrous wreck full of manual labor had he told a small lie or quietly said that he wasn’t good at it. Wretchedly shoving aside his foolish past, Lloyd looked up.

“Yes, please take care of me from now on.”

Please work hard on my behalf. In the meantime, I can relax and play around. Lloyd plastered on a sincere smile, and Count Nort, completely unaware of Lloyd’s intentions, smiled back. And just like that, Lloyd acquired a hard-working construction robot. The construction robot’s abilities were immediately uncovered the following day.

“Hmm,” mused Count Nort. “Tell me, are you proficient in surveying?”

“Surveying, Count Nort? I just look over the area with my eyes…” Lloyd broke off.

“Hahaha! You eyeball the survey data?”

“Yes.”

Lloyd nodded his head. A look of dismay appeared on the viscount’s face.

“An excellent and reputable developer like you conducts surveys with nothing but your bare eyes? That is a surprise. Then have you never used this survey machine before?”

“Oh, never. For one reason or another…”

Lloyd smiled in embarrassment. He had truthfully never used the survey machine after coming to this world. 

Why? I have my surveying skills.

His skill could acquire data much more conveniently and accurately than the machine. Moreover, it could be used in conjunction with his design skill. But there was no way for the viscount to know this.

“Tsk, tsk. That is too bad. I don’t tell just anyone about this, but…” Count Nort motioned his hands to Lloyd. “Come over here for a second. I will specially teach you how to use the survey machine.”

Count Nort held up the surveying machine, which was made of a 4-foot-long supporting pole with a cross- shaped bar at the top. The four corners of the cross-shaped bar had threads and pendulums dangling from them.

“Now, watch how I do it. Erect the supporting pole, and arrange the crossbar. Like this and this. Do you see me?”

“Oh, yes…” murmured Lloyd expressionlessly.

“Then place and balance the pendulums on the four corners… Like this.”

“Okay,” Lloyd said with feigned interest.

“Check whether the pendulums are vertical to the ground. Like this.”

“Yes.”

“Now, take a look here. That way, you can accurately see whether the machine’s line of sight is at a desirable angle. What do you think? Is this not amazing?” asked Count Nort with evident pride in his voice.

“Yes, it is amazing,” seconded Lloyd, feigning his admiration. “I would like to observe you for a little longer.”

“Haha. I suppose I amazed you with my skills.”

“Yes, a little…”

“Then keep on watching and feel from the side how accurate and precise my surveying is.”

“Understood,” Lloyd played along.

Count Nort was as enthusiastic as he was prideful. What did this mean for Lloyd?

If I throw a few cheers and compliments at him, this man jumps for joy and does all the work for me!

It couldn’t get any easier than this. Thanks to him, Lloyd was able to sit back and relax. He was happier now than during any of his past construction projects. At the same time, Lloyd hoped that nothing would change in the meantime and Count Nort would complete the entire construction for him.

Meanwhile, I can enjoy a leisurely lifestyle here in the capital. Then once the construction ends, I’ll take the dragon’s tailbone and return to my fiefdom. Perfect. Just perfect.

It was a fact that everything in this world was better free. Lloyd beamed as though he had just won a coupon for a free year-long vacation at a hotel.

“How dare you… What are you going to do with my tailbone?”

Clack. In the deepest part of the national repository that stored the most precious treasures in the Magentano kingdom, something jerked in the vast and opulent space.

Clack… Clack… The movement was not significant. It was subtle enough to be easily missed by the naked eye. But the very entity that clacked was not insignificant. It was the head of a dragon.

“Once I am… done adapting to this new body… Once my soul is settled here… Then…

The dragon recalled the queen that dared to suppress and slay him and the young engineer that groveled at her.

 "All of them…”

Clack. The dragon’s head intermittently clacked and pictured a dark, sinister plan.
