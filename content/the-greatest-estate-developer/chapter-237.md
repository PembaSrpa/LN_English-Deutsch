# The Pleading Hooligan (2)

---

“Master Lloyd? Why are you sighing so heavily?”

The sound of Javier’s voice came much too abruptly, catching Lloyd by sheer surprise.

Was this what people meant when they said their hearts jumped out of their chests? Lloyd flinched but tried hard not to show it. He bit his lips involuntarily, held his breath for a moment while darting his eyes, and racked his brain for something to say. Then he turned around, thinking about how relieving it was that Javier didn’t see his expression as he approached him from behind. Lloyd chuckled.

“How can I not?” Lloyd grumbled as he pointed to the roadbed construction site. “Do you see that? The roadbed there? It’s driving me up the wall, really.”

“Is there something wrong?”

“Yes, there is. The upper part of the bed. It’s a big mess. Seriously, I warned them so many times not to build it like that. Those demons never listen,” sighed Lloyd, shaking his head.

Javier observed the area. “Hmm, it looks fine to me.”

“I see a lot of problems even if it looks fine to you. If the bed and rail are left in this state, everything’s going to completely warp later on.”

“Is that so?”

“Yeah, I should call the demons and fix it up again. For Pete’s sake. I can’t trust them with anything like this.”

“You must miss Sir Bayern,” Javier said.

“Mhm, I do, along with the Engineering and Skeleton Corps as well.”

Lloyd’s smile turned rueful. Even though he brought up the topic to change the conversation, he did miss Sir Bayern and the Engineering and Skeleton Corps in his fiefdom. After all, he had undergone countless construction projects with them. They were seasoned, experienced workers whom Lloyd could trust to do quality work.

“But what are you doing here?” finally asked Lloyd.

“I think it is time for you to eat.”

“Please tell me that we’re not having grilled lava centipede belly for dinner again.”

“Unfortunately, that is on our menu for tonight as well.”

“Is that really our best option?” asked Lloyd accusingly.

“Indeed. It is the only meal we can cook with what is available here.”

“But,” reasoned Lloyd, “what about steaming it? Or frying it even?”

“There isn’t enough water to cook with steam, and there is no flour to coat the meat.”

“Then what about eating it raw?”

“Eating it raw?” Javier frowned.

“Yeah, like soy sauce marinated raw crabs… Anyway, I mean just raw.”

“Do you truly want to eat it raw?”

“No…”

Lloyd let out a sigh. Three months had passed since the construction took off. And all this time, he had eaten a sickening amount of centipede meat. It was virtually the only food they had in this place. The meat was quite juicy at first, but the mere smell of it made his stomach turn now.

“Still, nothing can be done about it. Let’s go.”

Lloyd started to walk while letting out another big sigh. He moved one step ahead of Javier. A faint smile appeared on his face unbeknownst to the knight, and his heart felt lighter.

Whew. That went well.

It was a challenge, but he managed to change the subject of their conversation. By the looks of it, it didn’t seem like Javier noticed anything.

It’d have been a serious headache if Javier caught me.

Lloyd stroked his chest, feeling the bulge of his inner pocket. Hooligan Frontera’s ghost was there. Lloyd’s heart had jumped out of his chest, fearing that Javier had heard their scuffle. But as he changed their conversation topic and quietly observed, Javier seemed to not have seen anything. This realization, at last, calmed his heart which was pounding in panic. His steps grew slightly lighter, whereas Javier’s gaze grew heavier.

There’s something strange about Master Lloyd.

Javier knew his young master wasn’t the type to grumble over food. At least, he wasn’t after giving up his hooligan ways. He never refused food, even when the given offer was plain or unpleasant. The man wasn’t picky when it came to food, nor did he get sick of anything. Even if he was served the same dish for ten consecutive days, or even a month straight, he still ate without a word of complaint. Perhaps he had some complaints, but his young master never voiced them out loud. The man ate like he had been starved for years and seemed to fully understand the preciousness of food. But just now…

He blatantly said that he was tired of eating centipede meat.

The man was without complaint in the morning but suddenly threw a fit over their repetitive meals during dinner. It was only natural that Javier regarded his behavior as strange.

Moreover, the way he was talking to himself earlier was definitely strange.

Javier shot a meaningful gaze toward Lloyd, who was walking ahead of him. Though he didn’t say anything, he had heard Lloyd talk to himself right before he approached him.

It was like he was murmuring to himself. No, it seemed as though there were two Master Lloyds conversing with each other.

It was hard to believe, but he did feel that way. The din that came from the nearby volcano deafened his hearing, so he couldn’t catch the details. But Javier caught on to the tone and atmosphere of the conversation.

It felt like Master Lloyd was talking with another Master Lloyd. What is stranger is that… I heard two different tones of voice in the conversation.

Lloyd’s self-talk sounded more like two people conversing or even quarreling with each other in the same voice. 

Not only that, one of the two sounded… much like the old Master Lloyd.

It really did. One was using the very tone his young master used as a hooligan before he started leading a proper life. In other words, the chatter Javier accidentally overheard sounded like a quarrel between Master Lloyd of the present and the past.

“…”

Javier considered if Master Lloyd was bipolar.

No. It doesn’t seem that way.

Javier softly shook his head. There weren’t any signs in Lloyd that indicated such. This made Javier feel more confused.

Furthermore, the King of Hell addressed him with a strange name. Suho Kim. What could that mean?

Lloyd had retorted that he knew nothing about it. But Javier begged to differ.

Master Lloyd seems to know something.

But whatever that was, it looked like his young master wasn’t revealing it. The longer he stayed by the latter’s side, the feeling just grew stronger.

Right. From long ago, there was always more than one strange thing about him.

To be exact, it was a few years ago when he changed, that is, around the time he suddenly undertook the construction of ondol. That truly marked the change in him. A completely different person. At that time, Javier regarded the change to be mysterious but not so bad. After all, the hopeless hooligan had become dutiful and hardworking in one day. Everyone, including Javier, welcomed the change in Lloyd. But what about now? Javier felt a mild pang of nervousness over the possibility that he’d go back to his old hooligan ways. The man had a record of a complete change, so there wasn’t any guarantee that he wouldn’t change again. Javier was more nervous because of that.

Master Lloyd, I wish to protect the current hardworking version of you.

Though Javier nitpicked Lloyd for being a petty and sneaky person, the former thought that the present Lloyd was incomparably better than the hooligan Lloyd from before. Deep down in his heart, Javier knew that he could sincerely trust and follow his young master. That made him all the more anxious.

What should I do… if Master Lloyd goes back to his old ways?

Javier had no idea. He could only pray that such a thing would never happen and bring misfortune to everyone. With that thought, he simply made a resolve.

I do not know how I can help out, but I will do everything to protect your current self.

Javier’s eyes held a glint of firm determination as they gazed at Lloyd, who was walking ahead of him.

The railroad construction continued, so there wasn’t any immediate way to seek ways to help out Hooligan Frontera. And so, Lloyd focused on the construction at hand, the 68-mile railroad bed from the edge of Hell to the Hell Castle entering the last phase. During the construction, Lloyd abused and exploited 30,000 demons in a more efficient manner. The secret was simple. He needed to do his best to act as an evil developer.

“Everyone already expected this, right? Get ready to pull an all-nighter!”

A bonus stipend for working overnight? There was obviously none. What about welfare benefits for working in a poor environment? That was non-existent as well. Lloyd maliciously exploited the others to no end, never letting them get off work. And when it seemed like their productivity went down, Lloyd summoned everyone and lashed out at them, grilling them to do better.

“Okay,” shouted Lloyd, “Repeat after me! I am tired and slow because I lack passion and tenacity!”

“Passion! Tenacity!”

“Speak louder!” boomed Lloyd.

“PASSIOOON! TENACITYYY!”

“Okay! Work harder with that energy!” proclaimed Lloyd with a glow of satisfaction

“Eek…!” one of the demons cried out. “But it’s too hard and tiring!”

“Shut up! Demons are supposed to be in pain and tired!”

Of course, all of this went without saying that it stood entirely opposite to Lloyd’s usual philosophy. Hard work was to be met with fair compensation. Dangerous tasks were to be guided with safety measures and hazard pay. And everything was to be included in the labor contract. This was all that Lloyd normally believed because he hoped that none of his awful experiences on construction sites in the past would happen in his own field. He didn’t want there to be any sorrow, mortification, or extortion of wages for his workers. But right now, the situation was completely different.

I’m in Hell!

Hell turned out to be a fanciful place. Had he treated the human workers in the same fashion he treated the demons, the entire construction site would have been flipped upside down, and a massive ruckus would have occurred. But this place was poles apart. The harsher he treated the demons, the more they enjoyed it! The greater the burden on their shoulders, the happier they became! The more Lloyd disregarded their welfare and whatnot, the closer they floated to cloud nine!

They seem like perverted nutcases. It’s almost disturbing at this point.

The demons reacted in such a way that the thought just came naturally. So, in short, demons in this world thought lowly of the others who treated them in a meek and reasonable manner.

This goes really against my site rules, but I have no choice. I’m trying to adapt by operating the construction site in accordance with the customs here.

Silencing the bothersome voices of his conscience and telling himself countless times that it was fine, Lloyd continued exploiting the demons. Thankfully, however, that sped things up, and the construction of the railroad bed was finished swiftly. And once he was done, he advanced to constructing tracks right away.

It doesn’t have to be grand or complicated. Just good enough for the train transporting the souls to travel north and south at a smooth speed.

There wasn’t any need to construct a complicated or flashy train like those in South Korea. He just needed one that possessed basic functions.

First, we should work on the ballast that will support the tracks on the bed.

Lloyd opted to go for the most traditional option, which was a carpet of gravel to build the ballast.

The gravel on the ground creates friction and absorbs some of the shock and vibration. It’s easy to construct, and the structure itself is simple.

The downsides were that the train created dust as it traveled and was high-maintenance. But he didn’t care. 

Dust is not a problem since I’m in Hell, and there are plenty of demons to help with maintenance anyway. So, all’s good.

Currently, over 800,000 demons were mobilized to transport dead souls through carts. And so, once the train was ready to go, many demons were set to be unemployed. The maintenance of the train tracks would solve this massive unemployment issue.

Haah. Let’s get to work! Work!

Laying the gravel was a walk in the park. Hell was full of rocks. All sorts of rocks, which came about as the pyroclastic materials cooled down, scattered all about the area. Lloyd scraped and saved them and laid everything flat on the 86-mile railroad bed. And then, he constructed the ties and rails. His method of construction wasn’t necessarily modern. Sure, he could obviously install the Alternative System, an anti-dust prevention device widely used in developed countries like Germany, the United States, South Korea, and Switzerland.

But I don’t necessarily have to.

It went without saying that modern techniques were useful. The train created less vibration or noise when it traveled. But here, this sort of technique unnecessarily complicated the construction and delayed its completion.

And besides, the train will be carrying demons and souls, so who cares about their travel experience?

It wasn’t Lloyd’s problem, and so he opted to use the comparatively old and simple “STEDEF” method. 

It’s the oldest and simplest method I’ve studied.

The STEDEF track was invented in France in 1964, and it was widely used in the early days of train-building in South Korea. For instance, it was utilized for subway lines one through four in Seoul city.

“Lay the tie! Then put the rails! Then secure the locks! Follow the Construction Guideline!”

The ties were laid horizontally on the ballast to fasten the rails and distribute the weight. To make those, Lloyd used the centipede legs he had obtained by a large volume by disintegrating the lava centipede and tying them by fours. That resulted in a firm, solid tie. And the rails were installed on the centipede-leg-ties.

And centipede shells were used to create the rails, which the train would directly touch as it moved. Who made it? Javier. How? With the blinding aura he obtained after rising to become a grandmaster.

Slash! One wielding of his sword rendered dozens of aura rays. These rays pounced on the dead centipedes and quickly dismantled their shells in a flash. All the corpses were cut up and perfectly processed with such precision that it made Lloyd’s hair stand. Then he carpeted the rails on the ties.

Next, the centipede antennas were used as the lock. Much like tying a cable tie, the ballast was looped through a hole under the rails. Then the ties and rails were fastened to the ballast.

“Ppodong! Step on it!”

“Ppodong!”

Ppodong, who had gotten giant after eating the red sunflower seed, jumped in excitement, his chubby belly and buttock dancing up and down. He stepped on the ties and rails with all of his weight, the booming and blaring sound of thumping and thwacking ringing across the whole place all day. But Lloyd gave no hoot to the din. The reason was simple. This place was Hell.

Why should I care if it’s noisy when no human lives here?

He didn’t have to worry about causing trouble for people nearby with noise or dust during construction. And so, the construction was advancing smoothly. But there was something Lloyd was unaware of. It was about the very noise he didn’t pay any attention to since he was in Hell and it was unpopulated with humans. He was ignorant that the noise was starting to put the construction site in danger.

“Grr! Noisy!” Under the lava lake near the construction site, there was a creature quietly getting some sleep. But now, it was boiling in anger because of the unexpected construction din, much like the ill-mannered neighbor throwing a party at night.

“Grr!” It deliberated whether it should come up and kill everyone. It was the most dangerous and violent creature in the wilderness of Hell. The Lava Giant opened its eyes, feeling a burning anger to destroy everything in sight.
