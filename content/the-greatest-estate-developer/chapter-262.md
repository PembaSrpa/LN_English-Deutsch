# Mermaids Love Jjimjilbangs (1)

---

[Scanning will begin.]

Lloyd looked at his surroundings along with the message in front of his eyes. Until this moment, Javier’s aura was the only source of light in the originally pitch-dark abyss. And it was the only thing that allowed Lloyd to discern what was around him. But when he activated his surveying skill, everything changed.

Crackle…! As though he had put on a pair of infrared night vision goggles, Lloyd’s sight turned black and white, granting him a clear view of everything. The hydrothermal vent boiled near him, but apart from that, everything was quiet and calm. That was the view he had of the deep sea floor through his surveying skill. But Lloyd couldn’t care less about the majestic scenery of the deep sea.

Like I can bother to admire the scenery of the deep sea when I have the Jewel of Truth to look for!

Lloyd’s gaze traveled down to the bottom and got a glimpse of the ground 16 feet below him through his subterranean scanning skill, and he smiled confidently.

Why would I put in the effort to shovel when I have the subterranean scanning skill?

This optional skill allowed him to scan the ground that was 16 feet below him. Lloyd had found the skill to be beneficial for him in several instances throughout the past. With just a brief glance, he could see deep into the ground beneath him. It didn’t require any effort from him to analyze the underground structure. Lloyd easily regarded it as the most useful skill in his possession and was confident that would be true for today as well.

I’ll be seeing everything, including the seabed. And the Jewel of Truth buried in it will be the same.

Lloyd figured that the skill would cover every corner of the seabed. Sure, it could take one or two months to scan the whole area because of its vast size. But that was thousands of times better than going through the tedious process of hand shoveling. Lloyd was absolutely confident that this was the case. However, exactly a minute later, his conviction started to shake and tremble like the gaze of a person whose last train home left right in front of their eyes.

What’s going on? What is this? Why’s the mud… so thick?

His eyes darted around in alarm, carefully scanning the ground beneath him. But all he saw was a thick carpet of mud. A large amount of excessively thick mud and dirt. Otherwise, there were only some deep-sea eels wiggling in the mud. As soon as Lloyd saw the dancing eels through his surveying skill, he learned something right away.

Oh, crap. I’m screwed.

The mud was unexpectedly too thick. It was due to the static environment of the deep sea and the build-up of various organic matters and sediments that sprinkled down like snow from the surface.

The mud is thicker than the range of the Subterranean Scanning. Not just here but everywhere. It’s all the same.

Lloyd clicked his tongue.

This is no use.

Lloyd had regarded Rotorua as a pathetic man for manually shoveling and grubbing around the sea for the Jewel of Truth. Lloyd regarded himself differently. After all, he had his surveying skill. The subterranean scanning skill would come into use and prove its worth. That was the source of his almighty confidence. But when he arrived and started surveying, he realized his mistake.

Agh… What should I do?

Lloyd was in a fix, indeed. Only the heavens knew where the Jewel of Truth was amid this boundless sea, and the situation was going downhill because the unexpectedly thick mud rendered his skill useless. And now, Rotorua was pressuring him with a series of questions by his side.

“Now that you are here, how do you like it?” asked Rotorua expectantly. “Do you see the chance of finding the lot for the jewel?”

“No.” Lloyd frankly shook his head, suppressing his embarrassment. He wasn’t here to save his face or ego but to prevent the restoration of destiny from destroying everyone. He was here to find the Jewel of Truth.

Plus, I don’t have a lot of time on my hands. The Ending Spoiler told me I have, what, three years? Three years may seem long, but it’s actually short.

Truly, time was flying. It had been well over three years since he entered this world in The Knight of Blood and Iron. The restoration of destiny would begin before he even realized it. As he had that thought, he admitted his mistake without a fight.

“Now that I see it myself, this place is a lot larger than I expected. Plus, the mud is too thick,” pointed Lloyd with remorse.

“I knew it. I knew you would say that.”

“Yes,” agreed Lloyd, “But have you been digging through this thick mud to search for the jewel all by yourself until now?”

“There was no other way.” Rotorua smiled regretfully. “It’s not that my ancestors and I did not consider other methods. The most simple and efficient solution would be getting help from other merpeople. But that’s impossible for us to do regardless of what we know.”

“I see none of the other merpeople agreed to cooperate.” Lloyd understood right away.

“That is what happened.” There was more remorse on Rotora’s face now. “You should be familiar with the atmosphere of this place after asking around about the jewel. The merpeople here? None of them have any interest in it. They think of it as a distant legend of the past and impossible to find. What’s more, the majority thinks it’s a waste of time to go out and look for it.”

“Is it because they don’t feel any inconvenience without it currently?” asked Lloyd.

“Probably. After all, everyone believes swimming and working out is better than wasting their time searching for the Jewel of Truth.”

“I see.” Lloyd seconded Rotorua. Indisputably, the merpeople had no interest in the jewel.

Persuading them to join their search wasn’t going to be plausible.

Tsk. But it’s such a waste not to try. The merpeople are muscular nutjobs.

As long as they can be mobilized, they’ll be able to shovel all day with their endless endurance.

Lloyd clicked his tongue in remorse. The more he let his imagination fly, the more regretful he became. It would be child’s play to dig through and turn over a large portion of the sea floor if Lloyd could mobilize hundreds of merpeople with amazing physiques. With that, the chances of finding the jewel site would jump dramatically.

The chances of that happening are the same as winning a lottery.

It was a simple matter. Having one worker dig through the vast sea compared to having hundreds dig through it. Buying one lottery ticket compared to buying hundreds of them. The latter would promise a bigger chance of winning. Even though there wasn’t any guarantee of winning, the chances would shoot up. On top of that, if he convinced Javier to join the effort? The jewel was sure to be found.

I really wish I could get these merpeople to help. Or should I just summon Yongyong down here? No. Then they’ll find out that I was behind the Yongyong incident.

Lloyd shook his head. He couldn’t call Yongyong. Also, leaving all the digging to Javier was inefficient.

He could use his manablast on this whole place and remove the mud, but it wouldn’t be as powerful as it is on land. The water pressure is terrible down here.

The water pressure at this depth of water was unbelievable. It was beyond unbelievable. To give a dramatic comparison, imagine what would happen if a nuclear bomb were dropped in the Mariana Trench, the deepest part of the earth, and if the bomb was the RDS-200 Tsar Bomba, the most powerful nuclear bomb in history.

I saw it in a documentary before. Tsar Bomba? That was nothing to joke about. The blast wave from it circled the earth three times with the power of 50 megatons of TNT. Just its mushroom cloud reached 34 miles high into the air.

It wasn’t just that. The shockwave alone swept up and destroyed everything within 1,000 square kilometers. Its discharged energy was destructive enough to even dry up an entire lake. Without exaggeration, the diameter of the fireball’s crater reached over six miles long, and its height touched the boundaries of the troposphere and stratosphere.

But what would happen if I dropped it here? That would yield a very different outcome.

The fireball that was six miles long on land would only be six-tenth of a mile long down here due to the immense water pressure and turbulence that would crush it together. And eventually, the compressed fireball would diminish into a warm radioactive vapor that bubbled up to the surface.

It’d be like a big radioactive fart in the water. That’s just about it. The terrifying Tsar Bomba. It would be crushed into nothing under the water pressure.

That was how immense the water pressure was in the deep sea. As such, Javier’s thousands of aura strands wouldn’t have its usual capability to sweep across hundreds of yards at once.

Of course, he’ll still be able to cover about a few dozen feet. Although that’s better than just shoveling by hand, it’s not quite enough. It’s kind of a waste to completely rely on Javier when there’s a whole pool of high-quality workers for this environment like the merpeople!

The merpeople were acclimated to the immense water pressure, and they could easily use their muscles here. That was the type of laborer Lloyd wanted. The chances of finding the jewel’s site location would then increase significantly. But persuading them was one big hurdle.

Tsk. But still, there’s got to be a way.

The merpeople were too valuable for him to give up on them so easily. From that moment on…

Work! Cerebrum! Think! Frontal lobe!

Lloyd desperately racked his brain. He painstakingly searched for a plan and agonized over finding a solution. Before he knew it, he began pacing back and forth, swimming along the water current close to him. After an unknown amount of time, he suddenly noticed a marginal change in his surroundings.

Hmm?

An unfamiliar scene unfolded in front of his eyes. Pillars shot up from the ground like a chimney, and a jet of water, hot and black, squirted out of them. Deep-sea organisms gathered around the dark water. Soon, Lloyd smelled a whiff of pungent sulfur. This was the place he glanced at from a distance with his skill.

Is that a black smoker hydrothermal vent?

Hydrothermal vents were sort of like a deep-sea hot spring, wherein the water that leaked underground shot back up after being heated with magma.

Tsk. I should avoid them.

It seemed that Lloyd mindlessly swam here while he was lost in thought. Frightened by the smoky smell and heat of the hydrogen sulfide, he hurriedly turned away. Just then…

Hold on. Wait a minute.

Ding! A brilliant idea hit Lloyd, so he stopped and quickly wheeled around to take a good look again at the vents.

“Um, Mister Rotorua?” asked Lloyd. “I have a question.” Lloyd then looked at Rotorua, who had followed the former with concern on his face. “Do joint diseases worsen with old age for merpeople like you?”

“Excuse me?”

“Joint problems,” Lloyd pointed, “Like arthralgia, where your joints remain sore year-round.”

“Well, somewhat… They do,” Rotorua mumbled and nodded. “As you have seen so far, we merpeople have excellent muscles. But because they are so huge, our joints are strained oftentimes. In particular, as this kind of stress accumulates over the years, quite a number of us suffer from joint pain.”

“Is that so? I knew it.”

The corners of Lloyd’s lips rose a little. His mind flew back to the time he went around asking merpeople about the Jewel of Truth. Particularly, he recalled the granny mermaid who was exceedingly bothered by Lloyd as if he were a cult member.

That granny mermaid told me that I shouldn’t be pestering her when she’s already going nuts from her sore joints.

Lloyd remembered the way she constantly tapped her shoulders and waist like someone with severe joint pain. When Lloyd thought about it more, the granny wasn’t the only one.

The senile and mid-aged mermen were like that too. Out of the countless merpeople that I’ve stopped, those past puberty all looked like they were sore and in pain somewhere.

Much like sedentary, white-collared office workers and taxi or bus drivers at the wheel. No, more like a retired athlete who strained their body from overtraining, they all habitually massaged and rubbed their aching joints.

Lloyd could speculate and assume that the merpeople’s excessive volume of muscle was constantly putting stress on their joints, which was naturally becoming a source of pain for them with age.

My speculation was right. In that case, I have a chance.

Lloyd sensed it. And he followed his intuition to put together everything he knew to form a broader perspective - the merpeople suffering from chronic joint pain, the hydrothermal vent, the countless pieces of information he gathered, and other seemingly irrelevant situations or elements. Lloyd jumbled, assembled, and compounded all everything until they materialized into one business idea. This business would be used for his goal, that is, mobilizing countless merpeople to discover the site of the Jewel of Truth. Ultimately, it would lead Lloyd to find a way to stop the restoration of destiny.

“I think it’ll be a big hit if I open a jjimjilbang here,” Lloyd said at last.
