# A Lucky Accident (1)

---

Hmm?

Having arrived at the construction site before anyone else in the morning, Lloyd was getting ready for the day when he felt something strange.

What the heck? What’s wrong with my heart?

Something felt off. His heart was racing without any particular trigger, and it strangely felt cold, as though someone threw a scoop of ice cream inside him.

What is this?

It wasn’t serious, however. Just faint enough for a dull person to miss it. But Lloyd wasn’t dull. He owned the manaheart of an intermediate-level sword expert. He detected even the smallest and faintest changes in his heart.

Strange. This has never happened to me.

Lloyd closed his eyes and examined his heart, focusing on his senses. With that, his pounding chest felt even more clear.

So, it’s not my heart… It’s the manaheart in my heart moving on its own.

His manaheart was moving the cold mana on its own. But that was only for a moment. Right now, the cold energy was going away.

What was that? Should I ask Javier later?

Since Javier was more of an expert than him when it came to the manaheart or the mana circle, Lloyd decided to ask him after today’s job.

I kind of want to ask him immediately if I can.

Except that Lloyd couldn’t. He was sorry about it. Javier wasn’t with Lloyd. He was training with the queen and set to stay there all day as her sparring partner to help improve her fighting skills.

While I’m asking, I should also get him to inspect my mana circle.

Lloyd put that on his to-do list. The timing was good since he feared that his manaheart skill would increase to a high level and give him sword master syndrome along with horrible insomnia. So, there was a need to get it checked to find out if his manaheart was leveling up without his knowledge.

Insomnia? No, thank you.

His shoulders quivered at the thought, and now, the inexplicable cold sensation in his manaheart was completely gone. A little later, a group of laborers started to arrive at the site one after another.

“You guys are late!” bawled Lloyd when he saw them. “You should come on time to prepare your work tools and join the morning meeting, don’t you think?”

Lloyd’s reproach hastened their steps, and the site immediately bustled with vibrant activities. Lloyd and the laborers began to move around busily. The Heart of Winter inside the control chest did too.

♣️

Thump… Thump…

The Heart of Winter was stored in a chest specially designed to control its capacity for construction. And now, it was beating. It was pounding like the heart of a human in the presence of a lover. It was thumping like the person who met their life partner. This resonation was unprecedented and like never before. As it picked up a strange and rare sense of familiarity from a human, it could not help but resonate and vibrate. It aligned its rhythm to the person’s heartbeat.

Its faint ego posited a theory. Perhaps, this was the day its creator, Ice Dragon Tyrannus, had once predicted in the distant past when the Magentano Kingdom was founded. It wondered if Ice Dragon Tyrannus created it and presented it to the founding king, Mikael, for this reason.

Thump…Thump…

It probably was the case, the Heart of Winter thought. This meeting had to be fate, not a coincidence. How could it be otherwise? This was the first time it had ever met a human whose manaheart possessed similar qualities to that of a dragon. The Heart of Winter never imagined that encountering an entity like this would be possible.

Thud… Thud…

The Heart of Winter quietly quivered in the chest and sent a message to the human’s manaheart as it shyly resonated.

♣️

Ding dong.

Hmm?

Lloyd had been engrossed in the excavation project. He triple-manablasted the ground to dig the vertical shaft, sent Ppodong to expand and flatten it, and assigned laborers to clear the rocks and dirt before plastering its walls with temporary concrete. He also took part in shoveling and even supervised the whole process. As such, he couldn’t focus on anything else. But when a message floated in front of his eyes out of the blue, he stopped.

What is this?

He looked at the message with dismay.

[Manaheart Skill Option ④: Half-Human Half-Dragon has been activated.]

[Your mana, which coincidentally came to possess the same unique quality seen in a dragon’s heart, is attracting the attention of a dragon-like creature. This mysterious dragon-like creature is considerably curious about you, and one small encounter will be enough for it to shower you with explosive favor.]

What?

Lloyd wiggled his brow.

A dragon-like creature? Something like that is nearby me? And it’s even curious about me?

Lloyd hurriedly looked around, but all he could see were the fantastical creatures like Ppodong, laborers, and royal magicians managing and monitoring the chest of the Heart of Winter.

Is there a dragon among them?

Lloyd soon banished the thought.

The message said a dragon-like creature, not a dragon.

So, there was a creature akin to a dragon. The message confused Lloyd, and he had no clue as to what it meant.

Tsk. Things have been strange since earlier.

Something peculiar has been taking place since this morning. His manaheart produced chilly air out of nowhere, and now, a message appeared to tell him that a dragon-like creature was curious about him. None of this had ever happened to him before.

Could it be the Heart of Winter? I don’t think so. It’s a magical item, so it can’t be related to the dragons. Tsk. Oh, whatever.

Unable to find any answer, Lloyd pushed the thought away and decided to focus on his present task. The vertical shaft construction couldn’t be done carelessly, so Lloyd figured he should focus on the site before anything else.

Nothing is more dangerous than losing focus at a construction site.

That was the nature of construction sites. Even if everything looked like it was in place and safe, even if you were a seasoned worker with decades of experience, a moment of lost concentration would lead to an accident. Lloyd had witnessed such unfortunate accidents a few times before.

I’ve seen smaller accidents that resulted in a back or shoulder incident. Otherwise, I saw a grinder blade bounce off as it cut through a block of wood before. The worst accidents are… Well, it happened to the man whose family was abroad… Tsk. Let’s not dwell on it.

Lloyd didn’t want to continue his sudden thought as it saddened him. Anyway, his point was that construction sites were filled with danger.

We’re excavating the vertical shafts.

He was already digging 32 feet under the ground. The plasterers were dangled in the air as they plastered temp concrete on the walls, like the skyscraper window cleaners and painters. Therefore, it was imperative for Lloyd to keep a close eye on the job because they could get hurt otherwise.

Lloyd had the duty to responsibly supervise the progress, his keen eyes never missing anything. As he spent every day, alert and vigilant, focusing on the job, he soon forgot about the message. The construction developed smoothly, and eventually, the shaft’s depth reached 147 feet. Next, Lloyd instructed Javier to bore several horizontal holes, about eleven inches in diameter, beside the vertical shaft toward the palace. By this time, Javier had free control over his manablast. He could determine its length and diameter.

“Carefully and position it correctly,” instructed Lloyd. “Follow the angle I marked for you. If you swerve even a little, your mana blast will strike or penetrate the piers of the palace. You know that can’t happen, right?”

“Of course.”

Kaboom! Each time Javier’s sword glinted, it produced hundreds of yards-long horizontal holes that passed beneath the palace foundation. After a few days, dozens of such holes were bored. Next, Lloyd inserted customized metal pipes into some of them and connected one side of each to the refrigerant supply pipe, which was then attached to the Winter of Heart chest. The royal magicians were the ones operating the chest.

Whoosh… Under the magicians’ control, the chest reacted and slowly opened up, revealing the Heart of Winter. And it started to emit frigid air, the volume of it kept well under control.

Craaack… Crack! The chilly air produced frost around the chest and the refrigerant supply pipe.

Craaack! The white frost soon spread to the metal pipes at a fast speed and started to harden the moisture inside the soil around the pipes.

Great. Everything’s going according to my plan.

Lloyd clenched his fists as he observed the freezing ground with his surveying skill.

The Heart of Winter is really cold. It lives up to its title as a divine treasure of our kingdom.

It wasn’t like an ordinary cold freezer. Its temperature was hundreds of degrees in the negative. The amazing part was that Lloyd wasn’t employing its full capabilities.

At this rate, I should be able to freeze the entire foundation under the palace within just a few days. Well, theoretically, though, I shouldn’t freeze the ground at such rapid speed.

Supposedly, this method was to be conducted slowly due to the way water took up more space when it was frozen. If the ground froze too rapidly, all the moisture in the ground would expand. That would cause the ground to protuberate and considerably damage the structure.

And so, typically, the ground is frozen over several months, as slowly as possible, while ensuring that the protuberation is as controlled as possible. So, it’s only done in construction sites that have enough time and space.

But time was a luxury to Lloyd. He didn’t have several months to waste, so he opted for the slightly drastic measure, which was inserting only a handful of pipes for freezing among all the holes Javier had dug.

I left the rest of them empty. That will prevent the ground from being uplifted. The vacant holes will cover the expanded size.

Lloyd did the calculation already. The vacant holes will make up for the holes that will expand once frozen. Thanks to this clever idea, he could freeze the entire palace ground within a few days, and there wasn’t much elevation in the ground. Once Lloyd checked that all was good, he moved on to the next part of the construction.

“Okay!” shouted Lloyd. “Bangul will chomp up the dirt first, followed by Ppodong, who will flatten the ground and follow behind. The rest of you guys will transfer the rocks and dirt. Blacksmiths! What did I tell you to make out of Bangul’s rebars?”

“Rebar fixtures!”

“Yes, indeed. I am sure you’ve familiarized yourselves with the measurements.”

Everyone got to work. Now, they started to dig a horizontal tunnel from the vertical shaft they had dug before, heading toward the palace. As for this, Lloyd utilized the shield method, which he once used to construct the bituminous coal mine back in his fiefdom. This time, however, it was more developed and larger in scale. Everyone became one and energetically excavated the horizontal tunnel, which was designed to be about twenty feet in diameter.

In the process, Lloyd frequently made use of the rebar supports to prevent the tunnel from collapsing, and afterward, he inserted lock bolts on all sides and plastered concrete on them. The concrete played the same role as the short concrete in the New Austrian Tunnelling Method (NATM) by being the additional layer to the tunnel inside. Moreover, the ground around the tunnel was extremely stable because it was frozen. The ice and soil naturally supported the weight surrounding the tunnel. This was also one of the core principles of NATM. Lloyd’s careful consideration and application design created a synergy and ensured the tunnel excavation was smooth sailing. The tunnel’s distance increased as a result.

Five days. Ten days. 15 days. Time passed and the tunnel finally reached its destination: 16 feet below the isolated piers where the tauranga was. There, Lloyd altered the direction of the excavation.

This time, we’re going upward.

Now, it was time to dig upward from the bottom to the top until they would be able to take out the tauranga from the side of the pillars. Everyone became one in boring the upward tunnel, clearing all the stones and rocks, and flattening and reinforcing the vertical passage. The tunnel moved upward along the isolated piers. And just like that, everyone believed the construction would occur without any accidents. It was stable in everyone’s eyes. But on the same morning they started this part of the project, an accident took place. An obstacle nobody foresaw. A lucky accident that would become a key stepping stone to Lloyd’s future development.
