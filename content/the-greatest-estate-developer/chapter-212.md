# Bones More Precious than Treasures (2)

---

Woah. This is just nuts.

Lloyd almost clapped his mouth over with his hand when he looked inside the wide open doors. A pile of dragon bones. A tremendous pile. It was nothing like the dinosaur fossils exhibited for display in museums. Putting the bones all together, much like the museum style, would seem to render a 655 feet tall figure. The skull alone was as large as a city bus. But there was something else that caught Lloyd’s attention, and it was neither the size nor the scale of the ginormous dragon bones.

Three horns.

This dead dragon had three horns. Technically speaking, there were originally four, but the one on the left temple had snapped off. So, there were two on the head and one on the right temple. It looked quite familiar.

Of course. I’ve seen it a sickening amount of times.

That dragon’s head and wings appeared in the design with the grim reaper. It was suspicious-looking as it was buried with the undead Mastodons near the Frontera fiefdom. It was thick on the ground in Namaran, where Lloyd had pursued after Cannavaro. And… in the novel as well, it was the pattern the secretive character used beside Queen Magentano the tyrant. The dragon head in the pattern looked just like what Lloyd was seeing right in front of him. It had a broken horn in the left temple and three other upright horns. It was the same as the dragon in the design he saw.

It’s like they copy-pasted the dragon.

Lloyd was sure of it. This dead dragon and the design he saw then looked the same.

Confident, Lloyd said, “Your Majesty, I have something that I’m curious about.”

“Ask,” allowed the queen. “Yes, Your Majesty. Did Your Majesty say that these dragon bones are the spoils you have acquired after subduing the warlocks?”

“Indeed.” She then let out a chuckle, slightly flashing a bit of pride in her smile. “They had it hidden in the deepest part of their laboratory and fought very hard to protect it.”

“Do you mean the remnants of the warlock, Your Majesty?” asked Lloyd for clarification.

“Yes. They looked like they were trying to protect a precious treasure. I presume it was the variety of potions, strong shamanistic tools, and many other goods that were extracted or processed from these bones. Much like the black jewel you excavated back in Namaran.”

“Do you know about them, Your Majesty?” Lloyd said in a surprised tone.

The queen smiled wryly before saying, “I heard it when Cannavaro confessed. The thing is, the man repeated his words five times even when I did not ask him to. I suppose that is how wretched he felt for being robbed of his stones.”

“…”

“Anyway,” she went on, “I was only able to acquire it after killing the last leader and the rest of the others. In all honesty, I was shocked when I first laid my eyes on this.”

The queen’s eyes held a glint of satisfaction as they fixed on the bones. Lloyd thought it natural.

I mean, we’re talking about dragon bones here.

Dragon bones themselves were high in value and also a powerful source of magic for extracting mana, much like what those dark magicians did. Otherwise, they could be processed to create battle gear. Given their priceless value and rarity, it was only natural for the queen to candidly express her satisfaction with procuring an entire dragon head.

Moreover, the queen may be unaware of this, but if my guess is correct, she just made a remarkable accomplishment by suppressing the dark remnants.

Was it because she merely eradicated the remaining warlocks? Or because she acquired a pile of precious dragon bones? No. From Lloyd’s perspective, her feat held more significance and meaning behind it. More precisely, she seemed to have exposed the dark secret that lay hidden in The Knight of Blood and Iron.

I’m almost certain. The horns of the dragon head. The dragon head grim reaper symbol. They’re the same. What else could that mean?

It proved that the warlocks were the dark powers that used the symbol and were responsible for burying the Mastodons near the Frontera fiefdom. And… one of them had been a parasite that stuck to the queen in the original novel.

But, wow, it was all resolved with this one incident.

The identity of the parasitic official, who never left the queen’s side, was hidden in veils even when part one of the novel ended. Every reader expected this secret to be revealed in the sequel, but it never came out as the author stopped midway.

So, I was quite bothered by it. I couldn’t pinpoint who these guys were even after reading the novel. I was afraid that they might suddenly attack me in the future.

But now, the queen had taken care of them in one clean sweep. It felt like a terrible migraine was forever gone and like someone had paid off his debt for him. The more he chewed over the fact, he felt a greater desire to bow down in front of the queen.

She must not know what kind of extraordinary feat she achieved.

Lloyd suppressed his laughter. This was good. The pests were now taken care of. He didn’t even have to lift his finger in the process. This incident was akin to a mosquito plunging into the water in a sink and dying on its own without the help of insecticide. The enemy was now gone, and he passionately welcomed this turn of events.

Concluding his thoughts, Lloyd asked, “So, does Your Majesty wish to construct a large garden using these dragon bones?”

“Indeed. I am thinking of assembling the dragon bones to make it look like it’s bowing down to a statue across from it.”

“By statue, do you mean the statue of Your Majesty?”

“That is right. This is why it is convenient to work with you,” she complimented with a nod. “You see through my thoughts without any explanation from me.”

The queen smiled in a satisfying manner and continued to speak.

“You may wonder as to why I am using these precious dragon bones to decorate the garden and create a sculpture. Is it simply vanity? No. There is a more important reason.”

“Is it,” put in Lloyd, “to exhibit the power of the House of Magentano to the outside world, Your Majesty?”

“Did you notice that already?”

“Yes, Your Majesty, a little bit…”

“Your guess is correct.” She paused before continuing. “At this juncture in history, is there any power that can fight against me head-on in the Lorasian Continent? No. Is there someone that can dare overpower my military, wealth, or any other area of power? No.”

Her voice was proud and equally confident. Lloyd nodded in agreement. It was true. One could objectively and meticulously attempt to go over her words, but one would conclude that it was fairly factual. That was how prosperous the House of Magentano was now. In the 21st century, they would be comparable to Russia in a world without the United States. It wasn’t an understatement to say that the Magentano was one step away from becoming a hegemony that ruled over the Lorasian Continent.

Though the sultan’s kingdom is its rival, much like China is to the United States.

But they were on good terms now, which meant there was no real threat at this point.

So now, the queen must have judged that the time is ripe to elevate her power once and for all. Consolidate the nobles in the kingdom. Raise her global status. Preemptively botch any other countries’ plans to dare challenge her, much like the sultan did with the Monster Domino operation.

The construction would be beneficial in many ways, socially and diplomatically. Come to think of it, Lloyd remembered that a similar instance took place on Earth before.

This happened during the heyday in France when Louis XIV ruled over the country. He mobilized massive funds to advertise the wealth and power of the House of Bourbon. He ordered the expansion and construction of his palace and garden. This eventually became the Versailles. And that part of history was taking place in this world with Queen Magentano. She was revealing her demands with a gaze as solemn as that of Louis XIV.

“I am thinking of expanding the back garden of the main palace,” she said, making one of her many demands. 

Yes, my dear client.

“Moreover, I want the expansion of the back garden to cover more than 800 hectares, and the path that will be laid out in the garden should be over 12 miles along with a waterway that stretches over 18 miles.” Why, yes, my dear client.

“More than 200,000 beautiful multi-colored trees must be planted, and the main garden area must have the dragon confidently blowing across from my sculpture.”

Why, of course, my dear client.

“There is one more very important aspect.”

Just say the word, my dear client.

“The dragon and my sculpture should be surrounded by a waterway that’s wide enough for 50 people to freely travel by ship. It would be best to show the view of the ship entering through the waterway from the Magena River, and finally wading out into the opposite waterway to exit the garden.”

Please save me, my dear client.

There was no end to her demands and requests. Moreover, they were very detailed. Queen Magentano was the type of client who clearly knew exactly what she wanted.

Haah.

Although Lloyd was burdened by her meticulous demands, he managed to remember every one of them. To some degree, a part of him even welcomed her demands.

This type of client is surprisingly easy to work with.

Just then, Lloyd’s mind traveled back to his laborious days in South Korea. One time, he was called in to work on a private residential house. The owner was a hard client to deal with. But it wasn’t because he maliciously abused Lloyd or used his money and power as leverage against him and the others.

That man was a hesitant, wishy-washy, and indecisive but good-natured man.

Lloyd realized for the first time then that the most frustrating and troubling type of client to work with was the good-natured but indecisive ones who didn’t know what they wanted.

I didn’t believe it at first, but it really was the case.

The owner was definitely something. At first, he said that anything was fine. As long as it looked good, all was okay. He nicely went along with anything at the initial phase of the renovation. But as the project progressed…

He slowly started to change.

His demands started increasing. He slowly became more ambitious in his vision. Why?

It was because he didn’t know what he wanted at the start of the project.

And so, the owner only came to figure out his needs midway.

“Oops! Now that I see it, I’d like the tiles in the first-floor entrance to be gray, not white.”

“I’m sorry, but can we do this door in another style? Not this one?”

“I’m seeing it now, and it looks like we should get rid of the shower booth.”

Surely, it would have been relatively fine if the project had ended that way. Lloyd and his team did reflect on the changes with a good heart. But the project wasn’t finished with such a beautiful ending.

“Oh, seeing it now, I think I like the very first one best. Let’s go with the first one.”

Eventually, the house had to be redone all over again.

Dammit. Just thinking about it pisses me off. I worked my ass off and gave him what he wanted, and he what?

Stuck in his memory, Lloyd clenched his fists. So, in a way, he felt a rush of relief as he looked at Queen Magentano. At least she wasn’t such an indecisive person.

“So, Your Majesty,” asked Lloyd for a final go, “Do I simply have to include everything Your Majesty has mentioned in the construction?”

“Indeed. Combine all the elements I mentioned without leaving anything out. Do not worry about the cost and labor as I will provide more than enough. How does that sound, Young Master Frontera?”

“I think it is splendid,” replied Lloyd.

“Is that so?”

“Without a doubt, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd, hanging his head lower.

“Good. Then I presume you already have a reward in mind?”

“That is also without a doubt, Your Majesty.”

“Do tell.”

The queen and Lloyd had each other completely figured out. Each knew that either one of them wasn’t an easy opponent. So, they didn’t need to mince their words. Since the queen had laid out all her requirements, it was naturally time for Lloyd to make his own demands.

Lloyd Frontera. You could have returned home to relax, but instead, agreed to take up a construction project as large as this without putting up a fight. So, I shall grant you one request even if it seems a bit excessive.

Her gaze deepened as it was fixed on Lloyd. She regarded him as a useful talent since he was quick-witted and clever. But now, she felt a sense of discomfort about taking advantage of his capabilities unlike before.

Tsk. I never thought he would dare side with the sultan so shamelessly.

The queen never imagined a day would come when she would have to butter up Lloyd to do a job for her. She licked her lips in bitterness.

Sounding politely, Lloyd said, “With your permission, I shall make my request, Your Majesty.”

Being left with no choice but to work since it was too early to reject her orders, Lloyd decided to get compensated with something very valuable. He would make the best out of the deal. For example, ask for something luxurious as dozens of special full-set armors along with a two-liter bottle of a legendary potion.

“I, Lloyd Frontera, ask for a labor exemption period of ten years once the construction is completed.”

“What…?” The queen’s eyes shook. Her eyebrows were lifted.

It was a request that went beyond her comprehension. Her surprise soon turned into annoyance. Disobedience for the next ten years? Even you cannot dare make such a request! The moment the queen was about to rebuke him for his impertinence, Lloyd quickly added, his voice sounding shameless.

“Should Your Majesty find my request unfavorable, I ask for a large chunk of the dragon bone instead of the ten-year labor exemption.”
