# Smack the Back of the Head and Run (2)

---

Clung! Getting smacked in the back of the head was annoying. The simple sensation of your head bumping into something was distasteful in the first place. And it was particularly the case if that smack reminded you of the moment of your death.

…!

A triple mana blast flew out of nowhere and knocked the Bone Dragon’s skull. As it reeled from the impact, Targa gritted his teeth in its body. He had been intoxicated for a second from obtaining the sheer power of the Bone Dragon, feeling as though he became a god. But all of a sudden, his mood turned sour. The moment of his death came to his mind.

It was like this that time…

He had been beheaded. The back of his head landed on the ground with a thud and rolled away. How chilling it was when his head rolled on the ground! Targa desperately wanted to forget that sensation now that he was in his new body. But he couldn’t.

And he was reminded of it just now because of the bastard who left a sharp blow to his head.

The Bone Dragon lifted its head, and Lloyd came into its view. And when it saw him fly with the parrotbill, it was reminded once again of what happened.

Namaran.

Targa’s botched plan in Namaran came to his mind. He had planned to summon Hell’s Knight by offering the people there as a sacrifice. One month was enough time for Hell’s Knight to wipe out the kingdom and the major cities and forces. But he had failed. And because of who?

That bastard.

It must have been right after his plan failed. He had received a report from an informant he planted in Namaran. It mentioned that someone excavated and stole all his Noctanium, the black stone he processed with the mana extracted from the bone of the Bone Dragon. Moreover, he was told that someone on a parrotbill slaughtered the warlocks on the day of the event. And when Hell’s Knight woke up, the same man killed it alongside a knight with silver hair.

Lloyd Frontera.

Targa recalled his name. But Namaran wasn’t the only time that the man interfered with his plans.

He also blocked the assassination of the queen.

The poison he used was acquired after an immense struggle on his part. And it had been even more difficult to recruit Sir Kyle, the sword master. When Targa acquired both, he pushed forward with his scheme, convinced his plan was perfect and would bring chaos to the House of Magentano. But it ended in failure. And the fault lay in Lloyd Frontera.

If it weren’t for you…

All of his schemes would have succeeded. At least, things wouldn’t have ended up in absolute disaster like now. Queen Magentano would have either died or become crippled. Her throne would have toppled, and her control over the kingdom’s regions would have weakened. Hell’s Knight would have awakened amid that instability, and the 500 undead Mastodons would have caused an uproar near the Frontera fiefdom. Hell’s Knight and 500 undead Mastodons. The two would have been enough to wipe out an entire kingdom. On top of that, Targa had one last final touch in mind that would vouch for the success of his aspirations: a plague that turned people into zombies and the cure for it.

If I succeeded…

An innumerable number of people would have been zombified. The curse would have spread like an epidemic, and since it was a curse finished with a special procedure, even the high temple officials’ blessing couldn’t treat it. The only method of cure was the medicine produced by the mana extracted from the Bone Dragon’s bone. In other words, Targa made his plan, so that he’d be the only one who could cure the zombies. He’d emerge as a messiah after the fall of the royal family, the whole region was set aflame, and the whole kingdom became zombified. That was his plan.

If things took place that way…

He could have become the king. An emperor even. But now, it was impossible. He was dead. Though he could run riot, he was no longer a human. And there was no way to go back to being a human.

Now, I’m nothing but a monster.

He had wanted to rise above his position and rule over the world. How he had yearned to be the object of fear and respect! He had dreamed of discarding his discriminating label of warlock and becoming a dictator remembered all throughout history!

But I can’t anymore.

Even if he held the power of a god now, his wish couldn’t be achieved anymore. All his plans failed, and now, he was nothing but a monster.

It’s all because of you.

Fire burned in his eyes as he glared at Lloyd. Murderous desire rose in him. That emotion brought a change to the Bone Dragon. No, it brought a big change to Targa inside the Bone Dragon.

I’m going to kill you. I don’t know about the others, but you’re dead.

Kaboom! The Bone Dragon spread its wings. There was no more awkwardness in its gait or fluttering. The body of the dragon he was in no longer felt unfamiliar, and Targa didn’t even think about taking more time to adapt. He simply decided to fly upward, rip apart, destroy, and chomp down on everything in sight. He’d burn and trample on everything until it became a pile of ash and his rage was quenched.

Boom! Kaboom! The Bone Dragon leaped high. One jump. That alone propelled it 2600 feet off the ground. Then it spread out its wings. The space between its empty joints was set ablaze and harnessed an explosive power with the flaps of its wings.

Thwack! Just once. Its wings fluttered once and pushed on the surrounding air. One flap of its wings, and the air swirled around it. The air was explosively pushed away and compressed, creating a shockwave that sent the dragon’s 656-foot body flying straight forward for hundreds of yards.

Fwoosh! The Bone Dragon zoomed forward in a flash, and his large skull became the peak that overlapped the airwave. It overlapped, amplified, and finally exploded in a burst of shock.

Wham! A powerful sonic boom was created as the air condensed around it. The windows of the palace and nearby buildings got shattered. Everyone in the garden covered their ears and collapsed.

“What the… That’s nuts!”

Lloyd, too, wanted to collapse. He stared behind, goggle-eyed. The Bone Dragon was hurtling toward him as it neared the distance, and it looked like it was hell-bent on knocking him out!

How can it be so fast?!

Lloyd was gobsmacked. His plan was to run away, and that was why he smacked its nape with the triple mana blast to provoke it to be on his heels. But it never occurred to Lloyd that it would pursue him at a shocking speed and fling itself at him so thoughtlessly and deathly.

Maybe it’s the type of dragon that hates getting smacked in the head?

But none of that mattered right now. Because right now, even in that split second of thought, the Bone Dragon flapped its wings once more. A sonic boom commenced as air moved around and water vapors appeared.

Wham! The distance between them became worryingly close.

I’m going to get caught.

Five seconds? Four? No, less than that. What could happen to Lloyd if he got caught?

Well, no point in wondering! It’d be the same as a puppy getting crashed from behind by a fully-speeding tank.

Every bone of his would be broken. Lloyd’s eyes trembled when he imagined it. The response he gave was immediate.

“Ggoming! Down!” yelled Lloyd.

“Ggoming!”

Ggoming, too, had been feeling the charge of a dangerous figure from behind, so he immediately closed his wings as soon as he heard Loyd’s shout. He then did his specialty in flying, a sudden drop.

“Ggomiming!”

Ggoming lowered his head, shot downward like a nimble sparrow, and let gravity drop him vertically.

Argh!

The headwind blew like a storm, and Lloyd held on as he grabbed the saddle.

Could it still be following me?

Lloyd threw a glance behind.

It’s following me…!

The color drained from Lloyd’s face. The Bone Dragon was right behind him, its lowered head and closed wings much like Ggoming. It was nimbly swooping down, hot on his heels!

“Come on… Ggoming! Move left!” bawled Lloyd in desperation.

“Ggoming!”

At Lloyd’s urgent yelling, Ggoming turned his head to the left and rotated like a nail screw 720 degrees counterclockwise. And he then swiveled 90 degrees to the left, using the twist as his propulsion. That got him in horizontal flight mode. Right after that, the Bone Dragon did exactly the same and maneuvered exactly the same flight like a swallow with its giant body. What was more, it even stretched its giant neck!

Chomp! The Bone Dragon chomped the very space Ggoming was at a second ago, and its large teeth almost bit his tail.

“Ggoming! Ggomiming!”

Ggoming cried out urgently, hurrying Lloyd to come up with something as he couldn’t do anything more and was just seconds before getting caught.

“I know! I know!”

Lloyd’s forehead broke out in a cold sweat, and he was forced to admit that he had planned it too comfortably on this one.

I made a mistake this time. I should have run away without looking back, without giving a hoot about the others.

Lloyd didn’t care if people hurled insults at him for being a coward. They may call him trash, but Lloyd didn’t care. What mattered more was staying alive.

But, come on! Why did I come back to save Javier and the queen?!

Lloyd regretted the decision he had made ten minutes ago.

Am I really going to die here?

Lloyd was at a loss. He felt dejected. Ggoming was flying his best, and Ggoming was the best mobile asset Lloyd had. But that Bone Dragon showed no signs of that awkwardness in its movement that was evident a while ago. It was likened to a supersonic fighter jet or a rocket. It was flying at a scary speed, tearing up the whole air. And not with its tremendous body, it boasted outrageous mobility that of a sparrow. No, it was more fitting to say that the Bone Dragon was a skyscraper flying like a dragonfly.

I can’t run away from it.

Not a single method to lose this dragon, who was well-skilled in flying, came to Lloyd’s mind. The mere possibility of it was hard to imagine. But Lloyd didn’t give up.

No. I’m going to die if I give up. So think. Work, my brain!

Lloyd desperately racked his brain as he led Ggoming to perform various acrobatic flights and came close to losing his life at the attack of the Bone Dragon. He didn’t want to die here. He had to make it out of here alive, come hell or high water. The desire to live increased when he recalled the comfortable life he had dutifully toiled for in the Frontera fiefdom.

And so, he beat his brains for his life, scavenging every piece of information stashed in his head. The setting of the novel. The history he had changed. The littlest events took place a few months ago. And at last…

Oh, hold on.

Something came up. Lloyd lifted his head and swiveled.

I need to check first!

There was no time to lose. The Bone Dragon was charging at full speed with its giant body. Starting right into the monster, Lloyd inwardly shouted…

Surveying!

He tensed his eyes and glared at the Bone Dragon.

Bzzt!

[Scanning will begin.]

Every part of its body came into view along with the familiar message. But Lloyd didn’t study its whole body.

But I don’t have time for this!

His gaze darted to the lower part of its body. In between the frenzied chase, Lloyd spotted its tail that appeared from time to time. The giant tail was constantly moving left and right to ensure balance in its flight.

So, please..! Come on! Show yourself!

Lloyd’s desperate eyes ran along its tail from the end, from the thinnest part to the middle part that thickened in size. And at last, around one-third of its tail…

Found it!

A ray of hope appeared in Lloyd’s eyes. He found it. He could see it. The chunk of tailbone he was looking for. To be more exact, it was…

A fake tailbone!

The area Lloyd was looking at. A section of its tailbone was different from the rest. To be more exact, it was not a bone. It was granite. Why? The reason was simple.

Because you see, I took the actual bone. That was my payment for working for the queen!

A few months ago, around the time the Termina Garden project took off, he informed the installation expert in charge of the dragon project in advance that the chunk of the tailbone was his and that it must be left aside.

Then I totally forgot about it because I was so busy with the garden and cleaning up today’s mess.

But now that he remembered and checked, the chunk of its tailbone was missing from the skeleton and filled with a rock.

What’s more, we used granite to cut the material cost!

Lloyd recalled how the palace wanted to use the expensive backbone of the emperor whale. Though it was not as fancy as the dragon bone, it boasted remarkable strength and was lightweight. But there was one problem.

It was too expensive.

It wasn’t just expensive. It was obscenely expensive, the kind that increases the material cost a whopping amount for no useful reason. So Lloyd himself stopped the palace by convincing them that no one was going to study the dragon bone with a magnifying glass. People would go wow on their cruise ship. So what must be done instead was sculpting the rock to have similar color and texture as the dragon bone. The rock needed to be strong just enough to ensure that the skeleton won’t fall apart, and granite will do those jobs just fine. He argued and set his foot down.

That was how I lowered the cost of materials!

Though the money came from the palace, he didn’t want to waste the money on something unnecessary. That was why he stubbornly protested against the possible squandering of material costs. At that time, it never occurred to Lloyd that it would be this helpful. No, it never, ever occurred to Lloyd that the rock would become the only weakness he could capitalize on. An evil smile appeared on Lloyd’s smile.

“Excuse me! I have an idea!” Lloyd yelled at the people behind him. “But you know, we fail, and this will be our graveyard! So! Any last words?!”

“…”

No response came. So, Lloyd turned around and looked back.

Dang. What’s wrong with their faces?

The queen and Javier put on overly dramatic faces. They looked like they were ready to die in a grand but daunting battle.

The sight of their faces made Lloyd let out a snort. The two appeared to be extremely tense, and since nothing looked like his words would be deafened out, Lloyd decided to sober them up. That was the only way they’d give their ears to his plan. With a resolved heart and determination, Lloyd prepared a load of truth bombs.

“Then I’ll go first!” yelled Lloyd. “Javier! It was fun to be with you, but I hate you! You boast every day that you get a lot of love letters. But you know what? You’re single just like me! And Your Majesty! No. Noona!”

“What…? Me?” gaped the queen.

“Yeah, Noona! You too! I hate you a little too! You always excitedly exploit me!”

“Did you dare call me, Noona, just now?” asked the queen defiantly.

“Who cares about that when we’re going to die anyway soon?”

“You-”

“Anyway! Now it looks like you all are relaxed a little!”

Lloyd cut off the queen and shouted aloud again.

“From now on, listen to what I say, and please follow me! This one is, you see! We succeed, and we’ll be able to live! And forgive me for calling you noona a while ago! And exempt me from labor for ten years! And really, just if… If I catch the Bone Dragon! Give all the bones to me! Do you agree?!”
