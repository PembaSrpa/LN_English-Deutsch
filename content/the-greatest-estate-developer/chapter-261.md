# Do You Know the Jewel? (2)

---

“Are all people on land like this?” grumbled Rotorua as he stomped his feet in frustration. “How can you have so little faith in others? Now open your ears and listen to me. I will prove that I am not lying about the Jewel of Truth.”

“Sure, sure,” drawled Lloyd, his face bored.

Ignoring his curt attitude, Rotorua started speaking, “The Jewel of Truth is not merely a fictional item that exists in legends.”

Lloyd picked his ear. “Sure, so what?”

Rotorua gazed into Lloyd’s eyes. “It certainly existed at one point in time.”

Lloyd picked his ear. “Sure, so what?”

“T-technically speaking,” Rotorua stammered, “It was certainly in the sea during the second mythical age, that is, the time when spring and summer were the only seasons that existed in the world.”

“Hmm. Is that so?” Lloyd made a curious face.

“That is the truth. Undoubtedly.”

After a pause, Lloyd said, “But, um, so?”

“It really did exist at one point,” emphasized Rotorua.

“Um, so?” asked Lloyd again, fidgeting his fingers.

“Hmm, um, but it disappeared later on. No, it collapsed!”

“Hmm, I see,” said Lloyd without looking up.

“…“

A strong yearning to strike Lloyd overwhelmed Rotorua as his face turned red with anger. His fists unconsciously clenched. If only he could. If only it was harmless, he wanted to hammer the back of this spiteful human just once. But Rotorua mustered a miraculous amount of patience and remembered a lesson from his childhood.

Patience. Patience. I just have to be patient. They say that patience is the secret behind cultivating pearls. It is time to exercise patience now.

It was Rotorua’s first time meeting someone who was interested in the Jewel of Truth. That person was this human, Lloyd. Thus, Rotorua couldn’t ruin this precious opportunity by his momentary feelings of anger. Controlling his tumultuous rage, he spoke.

"Haah… I will cut to the chase. The Jewel of Truth was not a small object.”

Lloyd lifted his brows. “If it wasn’t, what was it?”

“It was a building,” Rotorua paused before continuing for effect. “The Jewel of Truth was a building that was as much of a treasure as even the shiniest pearl under the sea. Huge, magnificent, and majestic. Though, of course, it fell down at the end of the mythical age.”

“Hmm,” mused Lloyd.

“Do you trust me now?” inquired Rotorua as he expectantly looked straight into Lloyd’s eyes.

“Hmm, I’m not sure.” Lloyd’s tone reflected his hesitation.

Rotorua frowned.

“The Jewel of Truth was not a small object but a building. Well, that’s something anyone can think of with a little effort.” Lloyd tapped the side of his head.

“I am not lying,” protested Rotorua indignantly, “It is real. Based on some of the records my ancestors excavated and after putting together various interpretations and deductions of artifacts, the Jewel of Truth was a building without question. I am sure of it. However, it was not just any building. And it surely was not one that merely looked beautiful on the outside.”

“Then,” asked Lloyd, “What was it?”

“It was a magic circle.”

“A magic circle?”

Rotorua nodded. “Every component of the building, that is, from the foundation to the floor, pillar, and ceiling, everything was magically structured. It was one gigantic magic circle.”

“Hmm,” mused Lloyd as he stroked his jaw. “So, to sum everything up, the Jewel of Truth was a magic circle in the form of a giant building?”

“Exactly.”

A faint hopeful smile appeared on Rotorua’s face. Rotorua sensed Lloyd was starting to believe and show interest in his words. His story seemed to be getting through to the man, and at last, Lloyd looked like he trusted him. Rotorua swelled with joy and excitement. From that moment on, he began to reel off in full force, his mouth moving like a tube man in a storm.

“It is said that the foundation, floor, pillars, and roofs have been designed according to the flow of magical power. As such, the entire building resembles something close to an intelligent life form.”

“An intelligent life form?” repeated Lloyd. This was a surprise.

“A magical entity that can think, reason, and draw logical reasons on its own.”

“Hmm.”

Lloyd slightly lifted his brow as he listened. Rotorua’s explanation reminded him of a concept he was familiar with back in Korea.

Is this something similar to AI?

Lloyd wondered if that building called the Jewel of Truth, which was just one magic circle, could be…

So it was essentially an AI supercomputer, but one that operates with the power of magic.

The building was a supercomputer, but its operation engine didn’t come from science and technology. It was powered by magic. This thought soon shed light on what kind of structure it was to Lloyd. And as he continued to hear Rotorua’s explanations, he was able to gain a better understanding of the Jewel of Truth.

“Literally speaking,” explained Rotorua, “The Jewel of Truth was a building with magical intelligence. It was astute and wise. It was capable of offering clear answers to even the most complicated questions, which ranged from trivial personal matters to dilemmas of great importance that could change the world. The Jewel of Truth provided the most accurate and wisest answer by considering all the truths and facts. And-”

“But,” Lloyd chimed in, stopping Rotorua to deal with an important matter, “You say it collapsed out of the blue and disappeared?”

Rotorua nodded, his eyes downcast. “Yes. According to past records, they say that a terrible catastrophe shook the entire world on the last day of the mythical age, and in the wake of it, all the living souls in existence waged a war of survival against each other. The Jewel of Truth was devastated during this time and…”

“It got destroyed?” Lloyd finished Rotorua’s words.

“Probably. It is assumed that this was done by those who feared the jewel would fall into the hands of their enemies.”

“No surprise there,” said Lloyd as he nodded. Lloyd, too, agreed that an all-knowing magical AI supercomputer could make an extremely functional and intimidating piece of weaponry to any holder. It’d be a source of absolute confidence. And during precarious times when everyone was biting each other to survive, that’d be even more true.

Whoever last used the Jewel of Truth must have destroyed it, so no one else can have it and use it themselves.

Lloyd figured he’d have made a similar choice if he were in the same place. When his train of thought reached that far, Lloyd felt his knees weaken with a sense of emptiness.

Anyway, this means that the Jewel of Truth does not exist in this world anymore.

It didn’t exist. The incredible smart building that used to exist during the mythical age. Lloyd could see that it had been a mind-blowing creation at the time but was ultimately destroyed. It was no longer in this world. In other words…

Tsk. All my effort was for nothing.

He had taken a tedious trip down to this kingdom. But for what? To discover a nonexistent item. It looked like he had wasted his time.

“I see. Then I will take my leave now,” Lloyd said, ready to take off.

“Hmm? What do you mean?” Rotorua, who had been excitedly talking about the Jewel of Truth, stopped, and he shot an incredulous look at Lloyd. “Didn’t you say you can’t trust me? But that you would go find the jewel if I convinced you otherwise?”

“Sure,” acknowledged Lloyd casually, “I did. But then you just told me it was destroyed.”

“I did.”

“That’s why I’m leaving. What’s the point of searching for the destroyed jewel? I won’t be able to use it anyway.”

It really was the case. According to Rotorua, the Jewel of Truth wasn’t just a small device but an awe-inspiring building, a supercomputer magic circle. That was what had collapsed. So what good was there in excavating the remaining pieces? It wouldn’t be useful anyway.

“You see, I was searching because I want to use the jewel. But it’s destroyed, you say? And given that it was such an intricate magic circle and building, it’ll need to be perfectly restored for it to operate,” pointed out Lloyd in annoyance.

“T-That’s right,” Rotorua acknowledged.

“Right? Plus, we won’t be able to restore the building since the remnant traces would be thousands and thousands of years old. Am I wrong?”

“On that part, you are wrong,” Rotorua said grimly.

“Excuse me…?” When Lloyd saw Rotorua shake his head very firmly, he lifted his brow. “I’m wrong? Then is there a way to restore the Jewel of Truth?”

“Of course there is.”

“How can you be so certain?” said Lloyd, almost in a demanding tone.

“It’s because there is a blueprint of the jewel.”

“There is a blueprint?”

“Yes.”

“Where is it?” Lloyd’s heart started to pound.

Who would’ve thought? There was a blueprint!

Then he wouldn’t have to scavenge around for the jewel. He only needed to thoroughly go through, analyze, and study the blueprint before constructing the building. But Rotorua’s next words killed his excitement.

“The land where the jewel collapsed. One record says that the blueprint was carved into the land the building once stood on,” replied Rotorua.

Lloyd kept listening without saying anything.

“So we must find the land where the jewel sat. I am hopeful that once we find it, the jewel can be restored to its original state. My ancestors were all just as hopeful as me. That is why we devoted our entire lives to this search for hundreds of generations.”

Tsk. Then we still have to find the site the jewel was at.

Lloyd licked his lips.

But still, I guess it’s a relief that it wasn’t all for nothing?

That seemed to be the case after he listened to Rotorua. Though the jewel was destroyed and long gone, its blueprint could be recovered once the site of the building was found. Therefore, with the blueprint, the jewel could be rebuilt one way or another.

Luckily, construction is one thing that I’m familiar with.

Lloyd started to see a ray of hope, and at the same time, he was glad to have doubted and felt Rotorua out.

This merman told me almost everything he knew after feeling wronged by my suspicions.

Rotorua had reeled off everything he knew, allowing Lloyd to conveniently and happily acquire information about the jewel in a breeze. Simultaneously, he was able to recognize Rotorua’s sincerity.

At the very least, I know he isn’t a scammer.

Rotorua had an embittered look on his face that screamed, “I’m innocent!” but any scammer could easily feign the emotion well enough. So it wasn’t his expression that prompted Lloyd to ascertain Rotorua’s innocence. It was the messages that kept appearing in front of his eyes.

Ding dong.

[Merman Rotorua is feeling anxious and disappointed over your snobby attitude and lack of willingness to trust him.]

[Your likeability with Rotorua has decreased by two points.]

[Current RP with Rotorua: +14]

[Even though your relationship with a major character has slightly worsened, your RP remains unchanged and will not be forfeited.]

[Current RP: 2,482]

See? My likeability status with him has already been activated.

A faint smile spread across Lloyd’s face as he looked at the messages. His past experiences confirmed the knowledge that his likeability with another character didn’t always reward him with RP. The system opened only when the other party was deeply invested in Lloyd. In other words, the likeability message meant only one thing.

It’s proof that he’s resolved to win my trust and get close to me.

Such a resolution couldn’t be established with lies and wouldn’t be harbored by a scammer. The system wouldn’t have been activated, either, in the first place. Thanks to this, Lloyd was now able to trust Rotorua.

I was able to get an important confirmation, thanks to my suspicions.

His smile slightly deepened. He was now able to trust Rotorua and work together with him. This was critical for Lloyd. In a situation related to stopping the restoration of destiny, that was even more so the case.

I’ve got only three years with me. The count will be the first. And then everyone is going to die one by one. Time is running out, and I can’t waste any by being deceived and fooled.

It was imperative for Lloyd to work with someone he could completely trust. There couldn’t be any time wasted from getting foolishly scammed or deceived, and that was why Lloyd intentionally raised his suspicions. He proceeded with great caution when engaging with Roturua and didn’t hide his doubts. He, instead, used his doubts to pressure the merman, which eventually allowed him to confirm the latter’s sincerity.

“So, if what you are saying is true, I just need to find the location of the jewel, right?” asked Lloyd meaningfully.

“You are right. That is exactly right.” Rotorua’s face lit up at Lloyd’s question. “Then do you trust me now? Are you going to join me in excavating the jewel?”

“Um, maybe…” responded Lloyd with hesitation.

Disappointment filled Rotorua’s eyes. “What do you mean?”

“I would first like to hear about your excavation method,” said Lloyd, “You said that your family has been working tirelessly for hundreds of generations on this search.”

“That is right.”

“But despite such extensive years, you have not found the land yet.”

“T-that’s right,” Rotorua acknowledged.

“So it’s only natural for me to have some doubts.” Lloyd looked straight into Rotorua’s eyes for emphasis.

“Doubts?”

“What inefficient method has your family been using to fail in finding a piece of land for hundreds of generations?”

“That’s-”

“That’s what?” put in Lloyd.

“We simply dug to the best of our abilities.” Rotorua scratched the back of his head.

“Dug?”

“We simply dug. That’s really all.” Rotorua’s grew red, probably due to Lloyd’s hard stare. “We made a shovel out of clams and dug around. Everyday. Without fail.”

“Wait.” Lloyd gaped, aghast. “You don’t mean you scoured around digging through the bottom of every ocean in the world?”

“Oh, my ancestors did that, but…”

“And now?”

“Through some research, we concluded that the land is near the Arctic Ocean. So we stopped trying to dig up all the oceans in the world.”

“Instead, you’re digging around the seabed here. Alone. Is that right?”

“Yes.”

Lloyd was at a loss for words. Now, he had the answer as to why the merman’s family had failed to find the land even after clinging to this mission their entire lives.

They’re like the epitome of inefficiency.

This entire sea, no, this Arctic Ocean alone was vast, and Rotorua was digging through its ground all by himself? In short, this was a matter of probability. Lloyd found himself clicking his tongue.

“Tsk. That’s why you haven’t found it,” Lloyd commented under his breath.

“What is that?”

“Nothing,” dismissed Lloyd, “Don’t mind what I said. Let’s go then.”

“Go?” said Rotorua, following suit. “But where?”

“To the last site you worked at.”

“Oh, does this mean you will join the excavation?”

“No. I’m not joining.”

“Then what is it?”

“I’ll finish the excavation in a blink of an eye.”

Lloyd smiled confidently as he pushed Rotorua to lead the way. He thought it was time to put an end to this inefficient, dead-end practice of shoveling the seabed. The reason was simple.

Surveying.

Bzzt! As soon as Lloyd arrived at the excavation site, he activated his surveying skill and meticulously examined the surrounding area, covering the entire seabed within his skill range. And just like that, he began to scan the topographical data of the ground up to 16 feet beneath him.
