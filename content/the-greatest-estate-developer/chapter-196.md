# Young Master is a Top Gun (1)

---

Crack! Turtle Neck held out his hands and made a finger heart. Behind him appeared some more skeleton soldiers. They all followed their leader and shot a heart at everyone else. This was their way of expressing their joy of meeting Lloyd, their savior, for the first time in a while.

Whew… It was terribly difficult getting here.

Turtle Neck’s third cervical vertebrae clacked. Just then, the long journey to get here crossed its mind. It must have been the following morning after Lloyd coldly left the fiefdom with the envoys.

“Boss!”

It happened in the morning when everyone woke up. They had been living in accordance with the construction schedule of the humans and the biorhythm marked in their marrows. It was around the time when they were soaking themselves under the sun, all dazed and blanked out, after opening their coffin lids. One of them raised its hand high, suddenly swaying its fingers and writing words in the air. A human would have had difficulty catching the meaning. But the rest of the skeletons understood in a breeze the meaning of its gesticulation. It was saying that it felt empty.

The rest of the skeletons nodded their skulls.

“Right. Me too.”

“I feel the same. For some strange reason, I am agitated.”

“I feel like the wind is blowing through my ribs.”

“But it always has been like that.”

“Oh, come on. I’m saying that’s how I feel. You have no say when you have a smaller pelvis than me.”

All the skeletons wrote something in the air. Some nodded. Some quarreled. But everyone expressed a similar sentiment. Later, Turtle Neck wrote a message in the air after observing them.

“I think I know why.”

“What is it? Do you know why, boss?”

“Tell us quickly.”

The rest of the skeletons stared at Turtle Neck like baby birds staring up at their mother. Turtle Neck clacked its lower jaw and laughed without any noise.

“It’s because Lloyd left us.”

“Oh! Ah…”

The rest of the skeletons nodded their skulls, and each wrote what they had to say.

“What must we do, boss?” one of them wrote hurriedly.

“Should we bear this emptiness until Lloyd comes back?”

“I don’t want to. I feel like I’ll develop osteoporosis at this rate.”

“Right. I can’t live like this.”

“Let’s do something!”

Their suppressed emotions burst out of them. The longing for their master and lifesaver, Lloyd. The emptiness due to his absence. They yearned to fill the hole in their hearts and find a solution. Turtle Neck struggled for some time to console its team.

“Come on now. Everyone, calm down. Getting all worked up is not healthy for your bones. You will lose your gloss, and your bones will age.”

“Then what do we do, boss?”

“I can’t say for sure. But I have an idea.”

“Tell us now!”

Everyone stared at Turtle Neck’s fingertip, and at last, it wrote its opinion in the air.

“We could go find Lloyd.”

“Oh! Oh!”

“But on one condition.” Turtle Neck wrote.

“Condition?”

“Yeah. I think it would be best if only a select few go find him.”

“Why? Why is that?”

Everyone cocked their skulls. Turtle Neck continued to write.

“Think about it. Lloyd is our master, so he isn’t afraid of us. And the people in this fiefdom heard from him about us, so they’re okay with us. But what about the others?”

“…”

“The moment they lay their sight on us, they will panic and go berserk. They will run away in fear or come attack us. Is that what you all want?”

“No, it’s not.”

“Therefore…”

Turtle Neck began to move faster.

“We must stay low and away from people’s sight as much as possible. But if 200 of us move all at once, the chance of being seen gets higher. That is why we should choose a few of us to look for Lloyd.”

“Are we going there to help Lloyd’s work?”

“Yes.”

Turtle Neck nodded once again.

“Lloyd should be constructing something somewhere. I don’t know why, but I just can feel it. He was always like that. But it hardly matters if he isn’t. Anyway, let us pick the elite among us. About 20. Only they will go find Lloyd and help him out. How does that sound?”

“What about the others?”

“We can’t do anything about them. They will have to be content with cheering on those who are selected to go.”

Turtle Neck adamantly expressed its opinion. There was no opposition. There was about a ten percent chance for a skeleton to be selected. If one got selected, it was set to meet Master Lloyd. If not, it was set to stay unde the sun as its bones grew dry. But there wasn’t any other option. After all, their leader, Turtle Neck, was alway right.

"Okay. I’m in!”

“Aye!”

“Let’s hurry, and pick who will go!”

Everyone agreed. The process of selecting the team was simple and swift.

“Okay, group into 20 and pick a leader. The leaders will do a fair game of rock, paper, scissors!”

“Paper!”

After several rounds and many groans, 20 skeletons were selected at last. They were Turtle Neck, Frozen Shoulder, and 18 other members.

“I leave the command to you Square Jaw while I am gone.”

“Aw… Okay, say hi to Lloyd for us, boss.”

“I will.”

Just like that, Turtle Neck, Frozen Shoulder, and 18 other skeleton soldiers picked up a shovel and left the Frontera fiefdom in the middle of the night. They didn’t get lost during travel as they were bound to Lloyd by the Zombie Domination. Thanks to it, they could feel where Lloyd was as they moved.

“To the east!”

“Let’s goooo!”

They climbed the eastern mountain range. The journey wasn’t smooth sailing. A host of wild animals and monsters coveted their oily and nice-looking bones, particularly the pack of wolves that kept pestering them as they drooled. But the skeletons defeated them and safely climbed the eastern range until a wasteland without a drop of water appeared. The journey then turned out easy afterward. Why? They didn’t need any water in the first place.

“Wahaha! It’s so nice to bathe in the sun all day!”

They didn’t need any food or water. It went the same even if the desert spanned in front of them. This environment was way more comfortable because now, there was only a little risk of being seen by humans. Just like that, they traversed the desert. Sometimes, they had to travel past the oasis or villages. Even then, it wasn’t a problem. They simply spent their afternoon lazing around the dunes or pits. Even if people passed by, they simply clicked their tongues in pity, thinking that these skeletons must have belonged to those who perished after getting lost in the desert. With such cover and disguise, they kept traveling. There was one time when Lloyd’s location suddenly changed.

“Ugh? Lloyd just passed over us!”

“What? Really?”

“I saw it! It was Lloyd! He flew away riding Ggoming!”

There was a stir among them for a while. The location of Lloyd they felt in the eastern desert suddenly changed to Magentano, but the fuss lasted only a while. From the western to eastern desert, Lloyd flew over them again From then on, they dutifully continued treading their path until today all safe and well after a long journey, no one left behind. Of course, Lloyd, too, was feeling the movement of the skeletons due to his skill.

Oh, you precious little skeletons.

Lloyd swelled with pride and contentment as he gazed at Turtle Neck, Frozen Shoulder, and 18 skeleton soldiers that dared all the way to come here.

They don’t demand food or fancy lodging. They don’t get tired. They’re skilled. Moreover, they don’t even need to get paid! My, how can I not like you?

And not enough with that, they made it all the way here to lend a hand. How could Lloyd not cherish them? such a sentiment wasn’t shared by the rest of the rebels that looked at the Skeleton Corps.

“Eh?! Eh?”

“What is that?!”

Termes, the leader of the rebels, and the rest of the members were taken unawares. The appearance of Turtle Neck prompted them to raise their shovels and pickaxes by reflex. Hadn’t Lloyd interfered at the right time, they would have charged at the skeletons.

“Woah, woah,” said Lloyd as he motioned with his hands. “Everyone calm down.”

Lloyd grabbed Termes’s shovel, and in a nonchalant face, he quickly turned around. “Don’t be surprised. These friends of mine are very obedient,” said Lloyd.

“Those skeletons obey you…?”

“Yes.”

“Wait, are you a warlock?”

“Oh, no. I’m not.”

Lloyd’s smile turned more playful. “I have a backstory to this. Do you want to listen?" 

"Oh…”

Termes and the others flinched in hesitation at Lloyd’s question, and their faces turned pale all of a sudden. It was because their minds flew back to the time when Lloyd talked for more than an hour when they kidnapped him. Termes shook his head quickly.

“No, no,” Termes said. “I don’t think I need to hear your explanation.”

“Right?” agreed Lloyd. “We are close enough to skip those formalities.”

“You are right. Yes, yes, of course,” said Termes as he plastered a hurried and awkward smile on his face. Lloyd flashed a satisfied smile and said, “I am glad that you trust me. Anyway, they don’t bite. They won’t beat or hurt you. They are dutiful and nice, so they are obedient to me.”

“Is that so?”

“Yes, you see, they are regular workers of my fiefdom.”

More than that, they were star workers. With Lloyd’s introduction, Turtle Neck, Frozen Shoulder, and the rest of the skeletons carried their shovels on their shoulder bones with pride. Now that their appearance was explained, Lloyd spoke.

“Anyway, it’s a relief that the talent to penetrate the water vein of the waterway came just in time.”

Lloyd spoke and grinned, tapping Turtle Neck’s shoulder. Everyone’s eyes widened in surprise.

“Wait, are you saying that the skeleton soldiers will break the last vein of water?”

“Yes. That way, no one will die.”

It was obviously the case. Breaking a vein of water in the Qanat was extremely dangerous. How dangerous? The chance of dying was 100%. In other words, anyone who tried was guaranteed death. So, that was the reason why this step of construction was given to the oldest laborers in the ancient Middle East. No, it wasn’t given per se. They volunteered to give their lives away to provide the young ones and their descendants with clean water. But here? That wasn’t necessary.

“Turtle Neck and the others here will be assigned the vein water task.”

“I-is that okay for them?” mumbled Termes.

“Yes, well. But we should at least install a net downstream of the waterway. That way they don’t lose their bones.”

“…”

Everyone nodded, slightly overwhelmed. Just like that, the last operation was done. Several layers of nets and frames were installed downstream. Next, Turtle Neck, Frozen Shoulder, and the skeleton soldiers were brought into action.

“Okay, do you see the guideline direction from here?” asked Lloyd. “Just dig along the line.”

Crack! The skeletons nodded their skulls as they held up the shovels and pickaxes. Then they restlessly smite and dug bedrock. A long while later, at last, the vein of water exploded.

“…!”

Boooom! Water gushed out and its terrifying pressure disassembled the skeleton soldiers’ bones within a nanosecond, sweeping them away with a fierce current. Thankfully, every single joint was caught in the net. Even after that, the water continued to flow along the waterway.

Fwoosh! Past underground tunnels of the vast and desolate deserts, through the exactly calculated path, the water arrived at the destination. The end of the Qanat. The clean water flowed from the filtration plant that sat in a corner of Kandara. The water had melted from the permanent snowline in the mountain 23 miles away from the city.

“It’s water! There is water flowing out!”

People of Kandara shouted in excitement as the stream of water filled the filtration plant in no time. In truth, they didn’t have much faith in Lloyd before and thought that he was nothing but the sultan’s stool pigeon. That was until the rebels came to clear the misunderstanding, telling them how Lloyd was actually a noble from a kingdom far west and came to solve the drought. They enlightened the people on what was happening. But still, people remained skeptical about the part of drawing water to their homes. Drawing water from a distant mountain? They thought that it was impossible. A false hope. Such doubt dominated their hearts. After all, this place was abandoned even by their ruler, the sultan himself. The city administrator had long left this city to save his own life as well. And what? A young noble from a foreign country would come to help out? Everyone became united to not expect too much as it will only bring bigger disappointments. They were preparing themselves for the possibility of getting disappointed one day.

But it turned out they were wrong. Lloyd’s bravado turned out to be true. Everything that he said happened. The cold water in their hands. The freshwater the city’s mothers, daughters, fathers, and sons drank in relief proved so.

It’s a relief.

Lloyd smiled in satisfaction as he gazed at the people who now had an infinite source of water amid the drought. But he didn’t explicitly shout in happiness like the people. It was too early to dance in joy. He still needed to check the other destinations of the waterway.

The waterway of Qanat doesn’t end here. I need to check them all.

Lloyd recalled the construction contract he had signed with the sultan. The contract promised to solve the drought in the entire Kandahar region using the Qanat. But this Kandara city was merely the central city of the region. It wasn’t the entire region. And so…

That’s why I didn’t blindly close all the rebel’s tunnels in the first place.

Lloyd only blocked the unnecessary areas after doing some careful calculations and running a simulation. And so, the waterway was designed to even flow into the small cities and villages of Kandahar region like spider webs or blood vessels.

“Where to?”

“To the small cities and villages.”

The people there won’t know yet about the existence of the Qanat. So, Lloyd needed to personally fly with Termes and explain to the people that clean water is now available through the rebels’ underground tunnels and that they only have to draw water for drinking and farming. Lloyd would then be able to tell the sultan that he fulfilled the duties in the contract.

“Let us go then.”

From then on, Lloyd traveled to the small cities and villages in the region, and it took well over three days. He enlightened the people in detail of the Qanat and how to use and manage it. The moment the final village was visited, a rewarding message appeared on Lloyd’s eyes.

Ding dong.

[You have accomplished the great feat of connecting miraculous streams of water in the middle of the arid desert with your exceptional wits, bold negotiation skills, and accurate and persistent construction method.] 

[The people of Kandahar region who have suffered from the prolonged drought praise you very much.] 

[You have earned a new title.]

[A new title <Top Gun from the West> has been created.]
