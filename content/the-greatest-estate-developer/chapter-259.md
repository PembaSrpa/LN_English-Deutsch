# The Internal Affairs of the Mermaid Kingdom (3)

---

“It must be very concerning for you that the gates to Hell opened over the sea without any warning,” Lloyd shamelessly said.

The frown on her face deepened. “Concerned? Of course, I am. Furthermore, before that incident happened, the Arctic Ocean was the most peaceful ocean in the whole world.”

“What do you mean by that incident?” inquired Lloyd with curiosity.

“The Bone Dragon invasion.” Her voice sank as though the event still haunted her.

“Eek.”

Lloyd almost shot out a gush of seawater as he breathed then, but he managed to mask his shock by shutting his mouth. The queen continued to speak with a sigh.

“It happened not too long ago. The peaceful sea was thrown into chaos. It was definitely an invasion. An invasion by the Bone Dragon, an unprecedented existence.”

“The Bone Dragon…”

Lloyd gulped dryly. An appearance of the Bone Dragon in the Arctic Ocean. Moreover, it had happened recently. It had to be Yongyong. There was no other possibility.

I opened Hell Gate about the time the queen experienced all this. It opened up right here in the sky. And Yongyong, who was lingering around the Arctic Ocean, crossed over to Hell through it and helped fight the Lava Giant. It matches. The story matches perfectly.

Lloyd unwittingly shrugged. His conscience was prickling, and a sense of crisis overwhelmed him. He wondered what would happen to him if the queen found out he was responsible for sending Yongyong here.

There’s no doubt that she would execute me.

He wouldn’t be able to get out of here in one piece. It was true regardless of if he was under Javier’s protection. Javier wouldn’t be able to endure for long without the help of magic in this deep sea. Lloyd became more convinced when he saw the muscular build of the mermaids. Even as Lloyd kept dryly swallowing in anxiety, the queen continued to speak.

“I cannot go over the details, but the Bone Dragon invaded our territory. It even made the Gigatitan and emperor whales run away in chaos.”

“So were the Mermaid Corps employed?” Lloyd maintained his cool.

“Of course.” Could she be recalling the event of the day? Her gaze grew icy. “We attacked day and night. It was a bloody fight. We were fighting for days when the Hell Gate opened up in the sky.”

Lloyd felt a pang of guilt as he listened.

“It appears that our attacks took a toll on the Bone Dragon,” she went on. “The moment it saw the gate open, it frantically ran off into the gate.”

Lloyd had a lot to say, such as how Yongyong only flew into the gate because it was happy to pick his smell, but he zipped his mouth shut.

“Long story short, the gate has remained open in the air after that incident. So, I am troubled as the hot air from Hell constantly flows into this world.”

“That… is really problematic.” Lloyd smiled wryly. It indeed sounded like a pickle on the queen’s part.

The gate is allowing heat to enter here… Hmm. That must have increased the temperature in this area. Now that I think about it, the title I received from Hell didn’t even get activated when I got here.

Just then, the title “Frozen Feet, Frozen Hands” he had received from Hell came to his mind.

It activates when I set foot in areas with annual temperatures below 32°F.

But the title didn’t activate when he arrived in the Arctic Ocean. In other words, the annual temperature wasn’t falling below 32°F.

Hmm… I wonder if the Arctic is experiencing an unexpected bout of global warming right now.

Lloyd was troubled. Unwittingly, he’s become a culprit for environmental destruction. But he didn’t know how to fix that.

The King of Hell wants to keep the gate open like it is now.

So the gate was unlikely to close any time soon. It was to remain open as it is for the unforeseeable future. This bout of global warming was expected to last here.

Tsk.

Even though Lloyd didn’t choose this place on purpose, he nonetheless felt apologetic for the polar bears and merpeople in this area. However, he soon fixed the expression on his face.

If she finds out that I did it, I’m dead,

The uproar with Yongyong and the Hell Gate. All these incidents were connected with Lloyd, so there wasn’t any good for him to have the queen find that out.

I should try to survive before anything else. I have to be alive to be able to help them out and fix this problem.

And it would be even better if the mermaids somehow solved this problem with the Hell Gate on their own before he did. Thinking so, Lloyd shamelessly justified himself and empathized with the queen.

“Haah,” he sighed, “I understand. You must be very concerned.”

“I am,” said the queen, her voice heavy. “In particular, the glaciers are melting fast. My head is about to explode, hearing the complaints and protests of various creatures. I did not know being a ruler would be this difficult. It would be easier to jump rope with 20 emperor whales for 12 hours straight without breathing.”

“I-is that so…” murmured Lloyd.

“Yes, sincerely. If only I could find the culprit behind this mess. I would lay a hard punch into their gut, taking out all of the stress and anger that has been building inside me.”

Lloyd blanched at the warning, his face going pale as a sheet.

“Why do you look like that?” the queen said after observing Lloyd’s face. “You look pale. Are you still not used to breathing underwater?”

“Nothing. Just for a second… I was…” Lloyd stopped.

“You were what?”

“This is random, but the honeypots I’d left behind at home just came to my mind,” Lloyd said carefully, inwardly thinking that he should enjoy his honey before he passed away. To Lloyd’s relief, it appeared that the queen didn’t catch on.

“Honey? I have heard that it’s a sweet produce made on land. I wish to get a taste of it when I have the chance,” she said. “Anyway, you can go now. I will permit you to go around the kingdom as I have said earlier.”

“Thank you so much!”

Lloyd quickly bowed and disappeared from the queen’s view. He feared she’d start to notice something if he unnecessarily hovered around longer. As soon as he removed himself from her presence, he let out the breath he’d been holding in until now.

“Haah…”

I made it, Lloyd said to himself. Just then, he felt a prickling gaze coming from someone beside him. He turned and stared at Javier with a bitter smile.

“Why are you looking at me like that? You aren’t questioning my conscience or something with that look you’re giving me, right?”

“Why else?” Javier replied, almost in a rebuking tone. “It’s impossible for you to say that you have nothing to do with the Hell Gate’s appearance and Yongyong running amok here.”

“Well, you’re right. But what can I do? I have to survive before anything else. Didn’t you see the abs on the queen back there?” Lloyd sighed.

“I did see it.”

“And yet, you have the heart to say that? Must I get crushed by her muscular embrace for you to be satisfied?”

“Yes.” Javier gave a thumbs up.

“What? Seriously?” said Lloyd, shooting a look at Javier.

“Yes, those are my honest feelings,” Javier announced confidently.

“So, you would really feel better after seeing me get crushed to death?”

“For anyone who abandons their conscience and integrity can only live a life of a coward.”

“Why, yes, thank you. I’ll live a long life as a coward.”

Seeing that Javier wasn’t answering, Lloyd chuckled.

“But Master Lloyd.” When Javier spoke again, his face was grim.

“Yeah.”

“Is there really a reason you had to come all the way here to find the supposed Jewel of Truth?” asked Javier.

“Um, maybe?” Lloyd shrugged, and he thought to himself that the moment had finally come.

Okay. Yeah, it’s about time that he starts to ask questions.

Lloyd stole a glance at Javier walking beside him. He couldn’t know. The terrible events that would land on the Frontera family. The death of Count and Countess Frontera and Julian. The utter downfall of the family. This world was going through the same events as the novel.

That’s the effect of the restoration of destiny. Goodness.

Lloyd shuddered in annoyance as he thought about it. He had worked hard to change the dreadful plot at the start of the novel. There was no end to the amount of blood, sweat, and tears he went through until now!

But it will eventually end up being for nothing. That’s all because of the moronic restoration of destiny or whatever it’s called.

Lloyd thought the whole situation was unfair. But he couldn’t sit down and wail about it. He had to do something to prevent this damned catastrophe from happening. He had to find a way to stop it. And that was why he needed to find the Jewel of Truth which held the answer to any question it was asked.

But I can’t tell this man everything.

The original history. The altered storyline. The return of destiny to its initial path. Lloyd would have to uncover his identity to explain it all to Javier, including that he wasn’t the real Lloyd. That he was a fake from another world who took the place of the real person. Everything would need to be revealed.

I can’t do that.

Lloyd shook his head immediately. Now was the time to lie. And there was a perfect lie he had prepared for a situation like this.

“Um, you see, the thing about the Jewel of Truth,” Lloyd said as he licked his lips before pausing. And in a nonchalant voice, he said the remark he had prepared beforehand. “It’s a form of insurance.”

“Insurance…?”

“Yeah.” Lloyd nodded, feigning his conviction. “You know how I’m waking the dragon king up every morning? I heard it from him by chance.”

“That the jewel is here in the kingdom?” inquired Javier.

“Yeah. I heard it’s a powerful jewel.”

“What power does it carry?”

“It has the answer to even the most difficult question in the world.” Lloyd continued to walk as he talked.

“An answer?”

“Yeah. There’s a chance of encountering a difficult problem as you go through life, right? For example, the Monster Domino effect. Do you remember it? The swarm of locusts that chased us.”

“I do remember,” said Javier with a nod.

“There’s no guarantee that such a thing will never happen again.” Lloyd looked straight into Javier’s eyes.

“So, what you are saying is, Master Lloyd, you are looking for the jewel in preparation for a disaster that will fall on the fiefdom?” summed up Javier.

“That’s right. That is exactly right.” Lloyd nodded casually. And he continued to lie. “Life is long. You never know what calamities will come. But what if we have the jewel in our hands before they hit us? I’ll be able to safely overcome them, at least once. So? Don’t you think it’s a pretty good form of insurance?”

“Truly. It is.”

“That’s what I thought,” said Lloyd as he brought his hands to his back.

“Yes,” said Javier.

“But I’m surprised that you’re nodding to what I say without a fight. What’s going on?” Lloyd said with a smile.

With a straight face, Javier looked at Lloyd. “I believe that even the most messed up man can speak something right once in a while.”

“What?” gaped Lloyd.

“So, acknowledging and encouraging the messed-up man when he says the right thing is a beautiful consideration a person like me can show.”

“Woah,” replied Lloyd with utter disbelief.

“This is the first time in a while that I heard you say something right. Good job, Master Lloyd.”

“Wow.” Lloyd stopped walking. “I see you’ve stooped down to attacking people with compliments now.”

“Like I always say,” replied Javier as he followed suit, “I’ve never been violent toward you, Master Lloyd. I do not plan on it in the future, either.”

Lloyd turned and continued to walk. “Tsk. You know, I wouldn’t hate you so much if you didn’t talk back.”

“Thank you for hating me.”

“Then should I keep it up from now on?” asked Lloyd.

“Thank you in advance for that as well.”

“Gratitude comes in advance now?”

“If I could, I wish to offer mine all at once.”

“Do you not want to offer it in parts?”

“Yes. That way I will not have to thank you in the future.”

“Wow! You know what? You be the young master. I’ll be the guard,” Lloyd exclaimed in a high-pitched voice.

“Okay now, Sir Frontera.”

“Hey.” Lloyd looked genuinely offended.

“Please forgive me,” Javier said, backing down immediately.

“Yet, you’re still smiling.”

“Since it was indeed amusing.”

“Fine, if you were pleased. What else can I ask for more,” chuckled Lloyd. And he thought to himself…

Whew.

Javier bought his seemingly believable lie. Lloyd was actually worried that his reasoning, that he was here to find the jewel in preparation for a future disaster, would not be persuasive enough.

But for now, it worked. I’m off the hook for a while.

Lloyd inwardly sighed in relief. Meanwhile, Javier stole a glance at Lloyd with a serious gaze and thought to himself…

It’s a relief.

Javier’s reaction was all an act, but Lloyd bought it. He was actually worried about whether he would be able to hide his knowledge of the whole situation.

But Master Lloyd, no, that fraud doesn’t have a clue that I know. He mustn’t know yet that my reaction was an act, and I’m just playing along to his hardly convincing excuse. I’m off the hook for a while.

Then Javier would be able to protect the fake in relief. And help out what the fake was up to. Just like that, Javier smiled meaningfully. The two then got out of the palace premise and passed through a few strands of passages before entering the city that looked like a giant grape.

Woah. I’m craving salted pollack roe.

The image of the city, thousands and tens of thousands of dozens-foot large eggs stuck to each other, stimulated Lloyd’s appetite. The city made him miss the food back home. The salted pollack roe, which was his favorite, was the biggest craving of all.

Whew. Pour a bowl of bibimbap with wild chive soy sauce and throw in some salted pollack roe and top it with perilla oil and sesame seed and seaweed… Wow. I’m going nuts.

A pang of nostalgia assaulted Lloyd’s mind, making him salivate. But he knew he had better things to do than space out.

First, I’ll look for the jewel.

Lloyd resolved and looked around the city district of the Mermaid Kingdom, his gaze filled with determination. Countless muscular merpeople were swimming around.

One of them must know about the jewel for sure!

Lloyd hoped so. After all, that was the legendary jewel of the kingdom. A mythical item, a precious one, even if that was the item of the past. He figured that they should know one or two things about it.

So, I’ll be going around asking as many merpeople as I can. Then I can put all their answers together and find some hope. Or, at least, I’ll be able to get a clue as to how I can track the jewel.

That was what Lloyd intended to do. Plus, he was confident. No biggie. A simple task. So, he approached merfolks, brimming with confidence. But sometime later, Lloyd started to receive the same treatment as the cults that go around soliciting people on the streets.
