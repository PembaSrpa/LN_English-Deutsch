# The Membership For the Dragon King’s Support (1)

---

After a preparation period of one month and four months of construction, it all resulted in the first-ever railroad in Hell. The report on Hell Express 666 was stellar.

“So, there were no issues during the test drive?” asked the King of Hell.

“Yes, my lord.”

Lloyd had entered the Hell Castle a while ago after finishing the test run and politely knelt down before the king.

“After inspecting the overall operation for the last time, the average speed of the train on a stable drive was about 21 mph. Therefore, it will take about seven hours for a train to transport the souls from here to the edge of Hell.”

"Seven hours, huh,” mused Hellkaros.

“That includes the time it takes to load and unload all the souls.”

“Good,” replied Hellkaros with a smile. “Then I can construct more of the trains you tested today.”

“That is right, my lord,” said Lloyd, “Moreover, I constructed the drains so the lava will seep out around the bed without overflowing and damaging the tracks.”

“A drain, I see.”

“From now on, the remaining demons should be placed in even intervals to carefully observe the amount of lava flowing nearby, check for clogged drains, and study the state of the tracks.”

“Then they can conduct repairs when a problem arises?” asked the king.

“Indeed.”

“Hmm, then what if something unmanageable occurs?”

“…”

“Can I summon you then?” suggested the king.

Instead of answering right away, Lloyd looked up at the King of Hell, feeling slightly bitter. If only he could, Lloyd wanted to grab the king by his collar and shout, “You are an awful client!” for asking whether he would travel down here again to conduct a repair. But Lloyd decided to opt for a more constructive method to make the most out of this situation.

Plastering a salesman’s smile on his face, Lloyd said, “Of course, but with a fee.”

“You are heartless,” blurted the king.

“We did not have an agreement on free follow-up maintenance when we signed the contract. Plus, you do not enjoy receiving free services,” pointed Lloyd.

“Hmm. You are right, yet…”

“As long as you offer sufficient compensation, I will answer your calling.”

“Sufficient compensation, huh.”

“Yes.”

“Are you suggesting that what’s considered sufficient will differ based on the situation at hand?”

Lloyd didn’t answer on purpose.

“You are,” said the king, getting his message.

“It cannot be helped as our circumstances and market prices fluctuate every day.”

Lloyd didn’t deny the king’s comment as it was unnecessary. He simply laughed it off without shame, but it wasn’t to dismiss the king’s request. It was his desperate effort to prevent receiving any unreasonable demands or calls from him.

Obviously! What would happen if I agreed to repair the track in advance? That king would call me anytime he wants or needs me!

Lloyd could already envision it in his head. The king would call him for simple repairs involving bad tracks, a clunky train, overflows of mana, or unstable beds. It was obvious that the king would summon Lloyd for tasks that he could solve on his own by examining the issue and putting the demons to work for a bit.

Why would he still call me then? Because that’s convenient for him!

What would happen if Lloyd provided the after-service once, twice, and so on? What would happen if his continued favors became the norm? He’d eventually become an official pushover, the kind that responds to every summon. That would be how the king viewed him. Lloyd wished to prevent that from happening.

Of course. I’ll be busy enjoying a comfortable and relaxing life in retirement. Why should I take a business trip to Hell?

He had been able to exonerate himself from being a mindless construction robot for the queen after the longest time. If he wasn’t careful, he was at risk of becoming one in Hell. Sensing the impending crisis, Lloyd made a frantic effort.

“That’s why I apologize, but I cannot be readily available to come here at any time. I have other work to do,” said Lloyd.

“You mean I would need to compensate you for coming down here despite your busy schedule?”

“Yes.”

“But then it will be your choice to decide whether you will come down here based on the compensation I offer. Am I right?”

“I apologize again, but that is right.”

“Hmm.”

The King of Hell sent a meaningful gaze, and Lloyd endeavored to keep smiling as he felt a pang of guilt in his heart.

Wait a second. The king isn’t going to sentence me to life imprisonment in Hell in the future just because he’s offended or anything, right? That can’t possibly be his decision.

Of course, it was not. It was impossible. The king was the fairest man in Hell who only made rational judgments without letting his personal feelings get in the way. That quality of the king was what Lloyd banked on while exhibiting such shameless behavior. Soon, Lloyd’s gamble was proven to be a success.

“Good. Although a part of me finds you despicable, it looks like the one who is holding all the cards in this matter is you, Suho Kim.”

“Thank you for seeing it that way…”

Lloyd sighed in relief. Thankfully, it worked, and Lloyd was able to be saved from being summoned to Hell for continuous maintenance. He could now turn down repair requests without worry. And so, Lloyd was able to receive his payment with a heart full of satisfaction.

“Now, it is time for me to pay you for the construction. Let me check the terms and agreements in the contract. Let’s see,” the King of Hell said before reading the contract. “It was to release the soul of Dragon Anticus locked up in Hell.”

“Yes, it was,” agreed Lloyd.

“Good. I will give you your compensation right away.”

Hellkaros swished his pen a few times, and he held out a paper at a demon, which hurried off with the paper. When it returned, it held a fist-sized half-opaque marble in its hand. The King of Hell handed the marble to Lloyd.

“Here. Take this.”

“Could this be the soul of Anticus?”

“To be more exact, it is a plate that temporarily holds the soul of the dragon,” explained the king.

When Lloyd peered into the marble, he did see something slightly move inside. Lloyd figured it was the soul of Anticus.

“The moment you put your mana into the marble, you will be able to release the soul right away,” the King of Hell further explained. “You would be freeing it once and for all. But do not think of doing that here. If you do, the soul will become trapped in Hell again.”

“You mean I should open the lid when I get home, so to say.”

“Indeed.”

“I see. Thank you.”

Lloyd took the soul-containing marble with care and put it in his backpack. And then, he suddenly looked up at the king. The question that had been taking up a corner of his heart but which he never dared show came to his mind.

“Oh, excuse me. Can I ask you something?”

“What is it?”

“Do you know why I possessed the body of Lloyd Frontera?”

All these times, from the very start to this moment, Lloyd wanted to know. He was curious. But the King of Hell shook his head as soon as the question was uttered.

“No, I do not know.”

“Is that so…”

“However, I can only tell you that this is a rare occurrence that happens once every hundreds of years.”

“Do you mean the way my soul crossed into another world?”

“Yes. The dimensions are not completely separate from each other after all. Though they appear distant, they’re able to influence one another due to the giant halo that envelops them.”

“Oh, yes…”

“I presume that the interruption of the gravity force that took place between the halo of dimension was affected by a fluctuation of mana. Much like how energy-filled particles from the sea of Dirac trigger the process of pair production.”

“Oh, yes, yes…”

“Do you comprehend what I am saying?”

“Please forgive me…” said Lloyd in almost a whisper.

“Tsk.”

Lloyd wondered why he was feeling guilty.

The King of Hell smiled bitterly. “But Suho Kim, you are not asking about something that someone else in your place would easily want to know.”

“My king?” asked Lloyd, taken back. “What could you mean?”

“I am talking about your body in your original world.”

“…“

Lloyd shut his mouth. He was curious, sure, but…

"I am curious,” Lloyd replied. “But I do not really want to find out.”

“Why is that?”

“It will not affect me in any way even if I found out.”

Lloyd’s smile turned bitter. It was true. Sure, he was curious, but he didn’t want to know. Nothing was going to change now, even if he did. It’d only satisfy his curiosity and nothing else. No, to be really honest, he was reluctant.

I’ve had enough of my past.

His memories of the past were overshadowed by the immense hardship he faced, no matter how hard he tried to romanticize them. So, some intermittent recollection of his past was enough. He didn’t need to go the extra mile and find out what happened to his body in his world. And besides, he never wanted to go back. Could it be that the King of Hell noticed his feelings? The king gave a chuckle.

“Is that so? I see.”

“Thank you. I just need to know if my body is alive.”

“It is alive. Happy?”

“Yes,” Lloyd replied, “That is enough.”

Lloyd didn’t necessarily want to learn in detail how his body was doing when his soul crossed into this world. It was enough to know that it was doing well.

“I suppose it is time for you to leave,” said the king, “I will then open the Hell gate for you to arrive where you left to come here.”

“Do you mean the Termina Garden in the Magenta capital?”

“Yes.”

Bzzt! At one simple gesture of the king, the Hell gate opened up at once. Through the gate, Lloyd saw a glimpse of the dim night of the garden, which was still a mess.

“Time moves differently in this world with the human world. Your five months here is only about five days in the human world,” explained the king. “Do go back now.”

“Thank you, my king.”

A sense of relief rushed through Lloyd. He was afraid that he would need to use the Hell gate that led to the polar region, the one he had opened up to beat the Lava Giant. However, thanks to the kindness of the King of Hell, he was saved from venturing through the polar region.

“Then I shall prepare a few things and go back right away.”

Lloyd hurried, telling himself that he must return before the king changed his mind. After giving a deep bow, he moved and brought Javier, fantastical creatures, and the Bone Dragon, all awaiting outside Hell Castle. Then they all passed the Hell gate. Lloyd stepped in first with his fantastical creatures. Next was Javier, followed by the Bone Dragon, who took apart its joints. Once they crossed inside, they were welcomed back with the night breeze of the human world.

“Whew…”

It was cold. Though it wasn’t winter, the brush of the ordinary wind chilled his skin as he was used to the heat from Hell. But he didn’t have the time to revel in the fresh air and grass on the ground as he had to immediately scan the surroundings as soon as the gate closed. To his relief, it was late at night. No one was around.

Lloyd called, “Yong Yong?”

Clack!

The Bone Dragon, or Yong Yong, responded in its disassembled state.

Lloyd said, “Since people will panic if they find out about you here, hide somewhere. Over there. That’s east.”

Creak!

“Keep flying, and you’ll see a giant mountain range. That’s called the eastern mountain range. Hang around there for a while. Be friendly with the orcs if you bump into them.”

Creak?

“Good boy. If you behave, I’ll introduce you to your friends in the Skeleton Corps. Okay, off you go.” Creak!

Yong Yong gave a firm answer and flew toward the east, and it was only when it was gone that Lloyd was relieved.

“Whew.”

That was when the fresh wind of the human world finally felt real to him. He had spent almost five months in Hell, but that was only five days in this world. That comforted Lloyd a little.

I’ll turn 30 next year. Haah…

But that was delayed by a few months, thanks to the different rates of time in the two worlds. The catastrophe of becoming 30 had been delayed by a few months. Moreover, he had returned from Hell, safe and unharmed.

Now, it’s time to claim my rewards.

He hadn’t taken a trip down to Hell for no reason. There was something he could gain from it. An undeniably beneficial reward. That was the sole reason for his travel. And the time had come now to receive his award for traveling to Hell.

I’ve got lots of mess to clean up. Like the garden showcase that ended up in a disaster because of the Bone Dragon. Or saving the face of the queen. But I’ll push those issues aside for now since it’s not related to me.

If he did, it would just be unnecessary meddling on his part. He could offer his help when he had the time and resources. The job belonged to the queen and the palace. So, Lloyd resolved to prioritize himself.

“Okay, let’s go first,” said Lloyd.

“Where are we going?”

Javier seemed joyful to be back from Hell. He looked bright and happy as he took in fresh air.

“Somewhere dim and shady.”

“…”

“Somewhere inconspicuous. We should release the soul of the dragon.”

That was the only way to attain membership for the Dragon King’s support. It was the most important task, something that had to be done as long as they were alive even if the world collapsed.

Lloyd hurried his steps, slipping out of the still-messy Termina Garden. Then he and Javier passed the city district and the Llojavi suspension bridge, jumping over the wall of the capital with ease into the remote, unpopulated woods. He soon settled in an appropriately vacant lot.

“No one’s around, right?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes. Aside from the twelve rabbits, one fox who has her eyes on one of them, two owls, and other small animals, no one is here,” Javier reeled off.

“Good.”

Since Javier was a grandmaster, he could be trusted. With his confirmation, Lloyd brought the marble out of his pocket and activated the Asrahan Core Technique. Then he inserted his amplified mana into the marble.

Fwoosh! A flash appeared in the half-opaque marble. With that, the creature coiled up inside opened its eyes, and rushed out of the marble. And without giving any time for Lloyd to say anything, it shot up like a shooting star into the night sky. And then, with one more flash, it became a star in the Milky Way and disappeared.

Whoosh. The flurry of cold wind blew to dishevel his hair. Lloyd waited for a second, rolling his eyes and glancing at the sky where the ancient dragon just flew up and back at the empty marble in his hand. He found himself gulping.

It can’t be.

Did that dragon do a bunk without a word of gratitude or the provision of the promised reward?

No way. No.

The dragon had made a vow that its soul would vanish if it failed to fulfill its promise. However, he wondered if the dragon still ran away and broke their promise as soon as it was freed.

But… this is concerning…

There weren’t any words of appreciation or a cheesy, grandiose celebration for him. With one big flash, the dragon disappeared into the sky. Now, Lloyd was left with nothing but the cool breeze blowing at him. He couldn’t help but feel anxious, wondering if this was what it felt like to be scammed by a second-hand dealer.

“Master Lloyd.” Right when Lloyd was about to entertain those thoughts, he heard Javier call him with two taps on his shoulder.

“It looks like we have an unexpected guest.”

A guest?

Lloyd wondered what Javier could mean. The latter said back there that no one was around. So, how did this guest get here? Lloyd turned to the side Javier was pointing at. Then he found out. There really was an unexpected visitor. He was…

The Dragon King?

Goggled-eyed, Lloyd stared at the Dragon King.
