# My Responsibility (2)

---

Fwoosh! A flash of light dropped straight down at lightning speed with a thundering roar. It aimed for his head, but Javier reacted with nonchalance.

His foot took a half step to the side, avoiding the blow by the edge of a knife, and he held out his sheath simultaneously. Swish.

A slow, relaxed stab. It was a simple attack, so common that one would yawn at the sight of it. But Queen Magentano panicked when she received his counterattack.

“Hmph!” Her eyes widened in fright as his sheath dominated her vision.

He’s blocking all the directions around him.

Even though Javier had his sheath extended toward her abdomen, she noticed the various changes to its motion. She felt it. She sensed it. She may attempt another blow, but the sheath would change the path and neutralize her. Thus, her next move should be to retreat and avoid it.

Tsk!

The queen quickly stepped back, pulling her sword back and adding flexibility to it.

Whoosh! Fwish! Her sword glinted as it rotated twice in front of her, and a ray of red aura appeared in its glint. Seven strands of the aura extended like a whip and wrapped Javier’s sheath. No, it almost did but failed.

Slash! The sheath instantly disappeared. The seven strands of the queen’s aura were wrapped around nothing but empty air. Javier’s sheath vanished before she could even respond to its stabbing.

“…!”

Queen Magentano’s eyes trembled for a second. Just then, she heard Javier’s voice from her left.

“Your Majesty’s counterattack against mine was sharp and quick. It was excellent. However, if I may point out a potential point of improvement, Your Majesty still has the tendency of overreacting to your opponent’s movements and relying on your technique.”

“Argh!”

She didn’t notice him coming from the side. Astonished, she yanked her aura to the side, and it splashed so powerfully to grind any material in its way. The violence of her aura enveloped Javier’s whole body. And yet, he looked composed. His sheath moved with equal composure.

“It goes the same now, Your Majesty. If I were you…”

Thud! Twang! Boom! His sheath moved smoothly and with ease as if it was just swaying fruit flies away. All seven strands of her aura bounced off at his movement. The more amazing part was that Javier’s sheath was bare. It didn’t have anything that remotely resembled an aura. He went on to instruct, “I would have responded like this, Your Majesty.”

Fwoosh! With that, Javier’s ordinary sheath transformed into a calamity. It was like a sweeping tsunami ready to engulf everything in its proximity.

I’m going to die.

That was what the queen’s instincts told her. She felt frightened. Slicing? Stabbing? Striking? Words fell short of describing his blow. “Pulverize” would be the most fitting word if she had to put a name to it. And yet, she didn’t let herself be intimidated. She instead reacted with greater ferocity.

“Argh…!”

She pulled back the seven strands of her aura and compounded them into one, summoning a giant, sword-shaped aura. She confronted Javier’s sweeping blow head-on, and the two overwhelming powers collided.

Boom! A shock wave swept the whole area. Queen Magentano stood her ground, so desperately and earnestly, to win and save herself from being shoved and flung away. She fully opened her manaheart, squeezing out all the strength in her body, and at last, she managed to endure his attack.

“Excellent, Your Majesty. Once you master the movement of your senses and instincts instead of your techniques, you will be able to progress further.”

The storm subsided. The tsunami disappeared. The chaotic space of oppressive sword auras quietened. And now, with the impact of the auras all gone, she saw Javier grinning with a glow of satisfaction. His sheath was still touching her sword. At that moment, something came to her.

She realized that she would never be able to surpass this man, even if she gave her best throughout her life. She wondered if this was what it felt like to be stuck in the presence of a genius. But she, too, often received compliments for being a genius. Could it be that there was another level of genius among geniuses?

This is too unfair.

A bitter smile appeared on her face. But she didn’t allow herself to wallow in self-hatred and shame.

If I can’t surpass him, then I only have to strive to be like him.

And eventually, her path would appear. She didn’t need to work hard to become better than him. It would be enough for her to focus on taking her own path. That was how she would find life’s answers and destinations that solely belonged to her. As such, now was the time for blood, sweat, and tears. Not comparisons.

“What impudence,” said Queen Magentano. “I will worry about how I will progress further. Focus on keeping a hold of your sheath.”

Clatter! She pushed her sword forward. Javier happily fought back. And at the same time, he was amazed.

Her Majesty has completely surpassed the level of an ordinary sword master.

She wasn’t this strong back then when she went up against the Bone Dragon. Recently, as Javier helped her train, he got the feeling that her growth speed was no joke.

People often think that being a sword master is the end goal for all sword users.

No, she had surpassed that. The level of a grandmaster was only a theoretical concept. As such, it was widely believed by the public that a sword master’s development couldn’t be furthered after this stage. This contributed to the custom of comparing the period of one’s sword master status to decide who was stronger. The years of being the sword master and operating the aura sword, people normally decided which sword master was stronger based on them. But the queen… She was different. She was like himself. Beyond the evaluation and consciousness of the public.

She is still developing and at a fairly fast speed, too.

The pure obsession and passion over the sword. The belief that her development translated to the growth of the kingdom’s military power. Javier wondered if those were the secrets behind her remarkable advancements.

Then I shall help her as much as I can.

Javier resolved inwardly that he’d help her. He wouldn’t change his mind even if she surpassed him. And if she did become stronger than him, he’d gladly congratulate her for it. With such resolution in his head, he parried her sword. Just then…

“…”

A strange sound of an explosion came from somewhere. It came from a distance. Inside the cave. Near the construction site Lloyd was supervising. Confusion rushed through Javier.

Could it be a mana blast?

He thought so at first. Could it be that Master Lloyd used the mana blast? But did he have any reason to bore a hole? But soon, he concluded that couldn’t be it.

It sounded different from a mana blast.

The explosion sounded much stranger, and his senses mysteriously picked up on a cold sensation. Despite the long distance, Javier could feel it without a doubt. There was a strange, destructive flow of mana coming from somewhere. It was strange and also dangerous. Javier immediately withdrew his sheath.

“Forgive me, Your Majesty,” said Javier solemnly to the queen.

“Eh…?”

Thwack! The queen was shoved forward when Javier suddenly pulled away his sheath.

Quickly finding her balance, she said, “Forgive you? What do you mean? For what?”

“I have to make an urgent visit, Your Majesty.”

“Urgent visit?”

“Yes, Your Majesty. It appears that something has happened to Master Lloyd. I will provide a better apology after I return.

Please excuse me.”

“Hold on!-”

The queen cried out in a hurry, but Javier was already gone. She was left alone.

“What is this…”

She was flustered. How could he vanish in thin air when the training was going well? Without her permission? But it wasn’t something she’d become angry over. She didn’t even berate Javier’s rudeness. Instead, a look of worry came over her face.

He said that something appeared to have happened to Lloyd.

Could it be that Javier felt something she didn’t? Could it be that his grandmaster-level senses detected there was danger looming over Lloyd?

“Sir Maldini!” Something was wrong. She called the commander of the royal knights. “Declare martial law across the capital within the next hour. All citizens are to stay in their homes or a designated evacuation site. All knights and military forces should be prepared for an emergency alert.”

“Your Majesty…?” Sir Maldini, the commander of the royal knights, reacted in a daze, possibly because the order came too suddenly. But the queen paid no attention to his timid response.

“Effective immediately. And as for you, lead the royal guards and get to the excavation site for the palace repair. As quickly as possible.”

Javier had fled immediately with a pale look on his face. That was proof of how serious this matter was. As such, she couldn’t just sit back. Once she uttered the order, she leaped and started to make her way to the construction site.

♣️

Whoosh! Lloyd’s body dropped down as if he was on a bungee jump and also relentlessly like a waterfall. He was diving into the bottom of the shaft. His eyes glinted.

Now!

He held up his shovel and stabbed it into the air.

Claaack! The shovel’s head sparked as it scratched the wall until his drop speed slightly decreased. And his outstretched hand got a grip of the ladder.

Chuck! Although his shoulder jerked at the sudden weight of his body, he was overall fine. He was able to endure it well enough, and it wasn’t the time to let such a menial pain strain him. He then threw his body into the corridor and started to run as soon as he landed.

“Ha! Ha! Ha!”

The air was frigid. Lloyd could feel his body temperature drop. He wondered how cold this place was. Minus 22? 55? Or worse? He didn’t know. His face and hands felt numb even when he was protecting his skin with the Asrahan Core Technique. In particular, the pain became the worst when he moved past the refrigeration room where the Heart of Winter was.

Arggh!

Is this what it feels like to wrap his whole body with liquid nitrogen? Lloyd ran through the thick cloud of frigid air which was determined to paralyze his body. He went further into the end part of the cave, where the laborers were.

“S-S-S-ir… L-L-Loyd? Wh-what… I-Is going… O-O-On?” The leader of the group, whose face was now pale as a sheet, asked as he stuttered. There was fear on his face. Lloyd felt relieved when he saw him.

Thank goodness.

They were alive. Everyone was holding up somewhat. Lloyd quickly scanned his surroundings and realized the secret behind their survival despite the lethally cold temperature.

It’s because of Ppodong.

Ppodong had all twenty of them in his embrace, protecting them with his size and fluffy hair. It was all thanks to him that they didn’t freeze to death. The same went for Bangul.

“Bangul! Bba-bangul! Bangul!”

Bangul, having no fur to warm her, was tightly stuck to Ppodong as she shivered from head to tail. The sight immediately flooded his heart with remorse.

I’m sorry. I got scared and nervous about coming here for a moment.

He had hesitated for a second. Had second thoughts about coming down here. Apologetic, he encouraged, “Have you been waiting for long? It’s all right. Everything is going to be all right.”

“Ppodong!”

“Bangul!”

“And mister,” Lloyd called the leader of the group. “I’m sure you’re very surprised, but you should know what happened.”

Lloyd briefly walked him through what had happened. A look of shock appeared on the leader of the group. “Are… w-w-we g-going to d-d-die then…?”

“No, that will never happen,” said Lloyd adamantly.

“Tt-then…”

“We have to get out of here. We must hurry. However, we’ll never safely make it past the refrigeration room if we try to go out all at once. So I will carry you one by one.”

“…”

“Don’t worry,” Lloyd comforted. “Don’t look at me like that. Can’t you see how I’m fine despite the fact I came all the way here? Hey, over there. You look the worst out of everyone. Come over here, please. Here, cover yourself with this blanket. And put on the overall. Quickly. I brought the biggest one.”

Lloyd then carried one of the men in Ppodong’s embrace and activated the Asrahan Core Technique at the same time, sharing most of his warmth with the man on his back.

Argh.

As soon as some heat left his body, his skin ached as if they were being lacerated. But Lloyd kept moving.

It’s okay. It’s just pain. I just have to save enough mana to keep me from getting frostbitten. I won’t die. I have my title with me.

He endured his pain with such belief. And hiding his now bluish lips, he smiled at Ppodong.

“Ppodong,” called Lloyd.

“P-Ppodong!”

“I’ll be right back. Keep embracing them until I come back. Bangul, too.”

“Ppodong!”

“Bangul!”

Lloyd jumped into action as he heard their response. There was no time to waste. Stalling any longer would only put them in more serious jeopardy. He needed to hurry.

I can do it!

Lloyd ran. But at the same time, he was sorry that he couldn’t use Bangul’s Volcanic Explosion.

There’s only one way out and in for this cave. If I use the volcanic explosion thoughtlessly, the cave will collapse from the sudden change of pressure. Even if it remains intact, people won’t. They’ll be receiving the full blow of the explosion in this compact space.

Busted gut. Instant death. As such, Lloyd eliminated the option to use the Volcanic Explosion against the coldness from the list.

This is the only method I’ve got. It’s going to be too late by the time the rescue team gets here.

Lloyd sprinted as he resolved such, passing the refrigerant room. Lloyd could feel that the man was terribly shivering. His back, his arms. The more Lloyd felt him shivering, the more warmth he shared with the man. And this made Lloyd feel colder.

Argh…!

It was as if his whole body was being slashed by a knife. As if his ears were being torn. Even then, he kept running. Past the refrigerant room until he arrived at the bottom of the vertical shaft. He clambered up the tunnel through the ladder, which felt tediously long, and finally walked out to the ground. Lloyd then carefully placed the man down.

“Here! Blankets! Now!”

The men on the ground rushed to the man. Lloyd immediately turned back when he saw them come, leaving the sunlight and throwing himself down to the vertical shaft again. Past the refrigerant room. Into the end of the cave. He carried another man on his back, shared his warmth, and endured the frigid air until he reached the ground again. He passed the man to the others, only to fling himself back into the cold. This process repeated itself.

Three. Four. Five. The longer he withstood the cold, the more lives he saved. And yet, his body started to drain at lightning speed. The Asrahan Core Technique needed to be running in full swing, so he could share the heat and protect the man on his back. Every bit of mana in his manaheart had to be squeezed, so he could give it to the man. That was the only way an ordinary man could survive amid the terrorizing cold. And Lloyd was glad to do it.

But it came with a price. Since he had to give most of his mana to the others, he had to rely solely on his physical muscle and stamina to carry an adult man, run through the stifling cave, and climb the deep vertical shaft. First, he rescued one magician and six knights. Then, the laborers. Lloyd carried, ran, and climbed with big men on his back. In a severe condition that was rougher than the Arctic Ocean.

Huff, huff! “Ha…!”

Soon, the color started to drain from his face. Beads of sweat froze before they could fall to the ground. Even the act of removing those ice particles burdened him. Lloyd bit his lips.

A little more.

He couldn’t give up just because he was tired. This accident took place at the site he was in charge of. So, everyone’s safety was his responsibility. He must be responsible till the end. Lloyd mechanically sprinted again and again with that thought alone. He even used the Energizer and Latent Power Surge, which eventually got him to save 19 men. And now, only one more was left. He was passing the refrigerant room to save the leader, the last man, when he reached his limit.

Just a little more effort is all I need.

But his legs refused to move. Even when the Asrahan Core Technique was still on the energy conservation mode, his stamina had plummeted. His body was fighting against him. His legs didn’t budge.

Dammit. Please. Just a bit more…

Lloyd whacked his thighs, begging them to move just a little more. Just one last. Ppodong and Bangul can be downsized and carried out. Lloyd hit again and again, pleading and praying they would budge. And each time he did so, his heart pounded and thumped.

Boom! Boom!

The rampageous Heart of Winter thumped violently, matching Lloyd’s desperate heartbeat. And now, the Heart of Winter opened its eyes as it felt Lloyd’s existence with greater clarity. A thought crossed its mind. Why not become one with the human?
