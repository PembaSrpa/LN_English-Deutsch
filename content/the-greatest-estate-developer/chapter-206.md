# A Secret Plan to Stop Forced Labor (1)

---

“Miss Sheherazade?” Julian asked frightfully.

Julian was quietly sitting on a bench in the hallway because he couldn’t sleep. His home felt oddly unfamiliar for some reason. Also, all sorts of concerns pestered his thoughts. So he came to rest on the hallway bench, just to sit there. The summer night air flowing through the window opposite the bench pleased him, and the soft, distant whirring noise of insects tickled his ears. He stayed there with his eyes closed, feeling refreshed. That was when he heard something. There was a soft creaking from a door, followed by a soft thud of footsteps. And later, he met eyes with Sheherazade, who was looking around the dark hallway lest someone see her or she wakes the others up. Julian’s bunny-like eyes opened in surprise.

“Hmm? What is going on?” asked Julian in a daze.

Julian had blurted out the question in confusion. However, he wondered if it was a dumb one as Sheherazade was coldly staring at him with a leaden face.

“…”

But she didn’t say anything. She simply fixed her toneless eyes on him. Julian wondered if his silly question irritated her.

Tensing his neck slightly, he quickly added, “Um, I’m sorry.”

“About… what?” asked Sheherazade at last.

“Excuse me?” Julian asked in surprise.

“What are you sorry about?”

“Oh…”

Sheherazade was still wearing a straight face. Julian was glad that she was now speaking, but he was taken aback by her question. Could it be that she was really angry? But about what? And what was he apologizing for? Julian racked his brain to find a solution, but his clever mind, whose excellent performance as evidenced by his academic achievements, wasn’t clicking at the moment for some reason. It grated and squeaked like an unoiled wheel.

“Just. I’m just sorry,” Julian spoke after trying to think of a response. He gulped and looked up.

“…”

“For sitting down here, and um, catching you off-guard,” added Julian as an afterthought.

Sheherazade didn’t speak.

“If I really surprised you there, I apologize. I’m sorry.”

“…”

Sheherazade considered her feelings. She was surprised but not angry. And she had nothing to feel sorry for this man and was quizzical about how she should respond to his behavior. Part of her felt flustered, too.

I never expected to bump into him here.

It never crossed her mind. She was too spaced out up until a moment ago and too emotionally drained. Even the sword master syndrome couldn’t keep her attention laser-focused on everything. So, she didn’t catch his presence.

“Um, hmm, actually, I was having a hard time falling asleep,” Julian prattled. “So, I was just getting some fresh air. I never expected you to come out like this… and surprise me… Um, you know, I would have made some noise had I known.”

“I knew you were here,” tonelessly replied Sheherazade. “I wasn’t surprised.”

“Really…?”

“Yes.”

For some reason, she lied. Several times even. It was an odd and unsettling situation. This bunny-looking man had the power to make her lie.

“But what is that bag you are carrying-”

“Lunch box,” she immediately muttered.

Scheherazade suddenly felt a rush of heat, and her face burned red. She had given a half-hearted response to just get past the situation. But now that the words had come out of her mouth, she thought it sounded too strange.

Does he… see me as a lady who packs a lunchbox to go out in the middle of the night because she can’t get any sleep?

She hoped that wasn’t true. But this bunny-like man simply believed her outrageous answer.

“Oh, I see,” Julian accepted coolly.

No…

“You must have been very hungry.”

No, it’s not that…

“Actually, I was getting a little hungry myself, hehe.”

I’m not going to share! I mean, don’t believe what I said, she told herself. Julian was beaming so innocently at her that she grew solemn seeing him smile.

“Then I will excuse myself if you don’t have anything else to say.”

She quickly moved past Julian. That had been her plan at least, but she wasn’t able to.

“Um, I actually have something to say…”

“Excuse me…?”

Julian’s words stopped her dead in her tracks. She turned around and found Julian still flashing a smile at her.

“If you are going for a walk, then, shall we walk together?”

“Of course, I refuse-”

“I won’t ask you to share your food with me,” promised Julian.

“…”

“I actually have been wanting to go on a walk. But my vision is a bit poor at night because I’ve spent too much time reading books. So, I often trip over a jagged stone when I walk alone at night.”

“…”

“So, I will just follow you from behind.”

“…”

I should just lose him, she told herself. Sheherazade wordlessly turned around and walked down the hallway. She heard Julian’s footsteps following her. Not minding at all, she passed the main chamber, opened the door, and stepped out into the garden. The footsteps of Julian following behind her grew slightly more hurried. But she didn’t care, regardless. She simply strode across the garden, distanced herself from the annex, and stood in front of the wall.

“Wait, hold on-”

Jump! Ignoring Julian’s voice, she quickly crossed over the wall in one leap.

“…”

Now, he won’t follow me, she said to herself. Feeling relaxed and alone at the same time, she started to walk ahead. Just then…

“Argh…!”

Thump! Julian rolled and fell down to the ground from the wall she had just jumped over.

“Ugh, wait for me,” groaned Julian.

“…”

“This is so painful.” He dusted off his clothes.

“…”

“Do you plan on asking me whether I’m hurt?” he asked as he looked at Sheherazade.

“Not at all…”

Deciding to ignore him again, she was about to turn when an unexpected question stabbed through her heart.

“Are you still… in pain?”

“Excuse me?” Sheherazade broke off. It was he who hurt himself after clumsily jumping over the wall. So, why was he asking whether she was still in pain? Why was he, she wondered to herself, staring at her with eyes full of concern?

“You’ve been like this since earlier.”

Julian’s voice sounded like a whisper and continued to speak carefully.

“At dinner earlier… with my family.”

“…”

“You seemed to be in a lot of pain since then,” Julian said.

“…”

“So, um, actually… Here. Take this.”

Limping to his feet, Julian searched for something in the inner pocket of his vest that he wore over his pajamas and held it out to her. It was a vial.

“It’s digestive medicine. I figured you had an upset stomach… from all the greasy food at our dinner today…” Julian scratched his head. “Um, the medicine is quite effective. However, it’s extremely sour and bitter, so, um, I prepared this for you as well.”

He searched his pocket again, and this time, there were two round red pieces of candy wrapped in wax paper.

“It will be bearable if you suck on this candy right after taking the digestive medicine.”

“…”

Sheherazade stared at him dazedly, as well as the digestive medicine, the candy, and his hands.

He’s bleeding.

His hand was slightly scraped. Probably from the fall a little while ago. It must be quite painful, she thought. But his face didn’t show any signs of pain. He only continued staring at her with eyes full of guilt and concern.

“Pray, are you not a fan of strawberry-flavored candy?” wondered Julian aloud in a careful voice.

Sheherazade didn’t respond. She simply had her eyes pinned on Julian.

“Um, if it’s not that, then, I’m sorry for lying earlier. I honestly did have trouble sleeping and was idling around because of that, but, um, I was more worried about you, Miss Sheherazade…” Julian trailed off, suddenly confessing.

“…”

“So,” he went on, this time more bravely, “I stayed in the annex hallway just in case as I was idling around anyway. I wanted to give you what I prepared if I heard you were awake. Um, and also…”

“Did you tell another lie?”

“Yes…”

His bunny eyes flinched. And with widened eyes, he spoke.

“When I said I wanted to go on a walk and my vision was poor at night…”

“Those were lies as well?”

“Yes…” confessed Julian.

“Was it just to give me this?”

“Mhm… Yes.”

“I see you are very good at making excuses.”

“I’m sorry…”

“It is all right,” she said. “I lied too.”

“Excuse me?”

His eyes grew round again. Sheherazade laughed for the first time that night at seeing his gaze looking up at her.

“I don’t have a lunch box with me,” she said.

“Oh…” he gaped.

“This bag isn’t a lunch box. It’s a travel bag.”

“Wait a minute,” Julian paused. “Are you leaving?”

“I am.”

“…”

“Are you not going to ask me why?”

“Yes, I won’t.” Julian shook his head, and with a gaze that had grown deeper, he looked up at her. “If I do, I might be forcing you to give me an answer. Then you would be reminded of the painful reasons you have, Miss Sheherazade.”

“…”

“I don’t want to do that. Now, take this. Here.”

“…”

The vial that contained the medicine. The wax-paper-wrapped candies. She took them in a daze, and soon, became struck with a thought.

“Hold on…”

She then opened a wax paper wrapper and tossed a piece of candy in her mouth. She opened another one and put it in Julian’s mouth. And she clasped the wax paper and rolled it up, grabbing his hand and pressing his palm with it.

“Ouch…” Julian agonized.

“Hold on,” she said.

“Ugh, it stings.” Julian scowled.

“Stop pretending. You didn’t even know that you were hurt until now.”

“I didn’t know, yes, but it still stings when you press like that.”

“Stay still.”

Sheherazade couldn’t just leave him like this. The last thing she could do was stop this man’s hand from bleeding. That would lessen the guilt and regret inside of her heart. She pressed Julian’s hand against the paper harder. A look of dismay appeared on his face.

“Um, Miss Sheherazade?”

“Yes.”

“The candy. You shouldn’t have eaten it now.”

“…”

“The medicine is extremely sour and bitter, you see.”

“…”

That’s what he is worried about right now? It was probably because of this thought that she blurted out the following question by impulse.

“Why are you so worried about me?”

“Excuse me?” Julian gaped.

“I am asking why you have been so particularly worried about me since a while ago.”

She was curious and confused. She had to hear his answer to leave without any regrets. That was how strange Julian’s behavior was. He was like a baby bird worrying about its mother, nervously following Sheherazade everywhere she went. There had to be a special reason, she figured. So, she decided to find out. When she heard him speak, Sheherazade stopped breathing for a second.

“No reason.”

“…”

“There is no particular reason. Really, I am just worried,” replied Julian.

“…”

“I just idled around because I was worried about you and brought these for no particular reason… Since you are leaving, I now feel…”

“Sad?” she put in.

“Yes…” confessed Julian.

“Why?”

“I don’t have a reason for this either.”

“…”

No reason. He really had no special reason. Sheherazade bit her lips, tearing up. She had led a hard life until now. Up to this day, people needed a reason to care about her. At first, she couldn’t wrap her head around how Julian could worry for “no reason”. Then she was momentarily confused about how that was even possible. She wondered what those words meant and why they felt so foreign to her. And at last, as she found the answer to both questions, her suppressed resentment melted down the front she put on without a single trace.

“You see, I…”

From then onward, like she was bewitched by something, Sheherazade stopped holding back and expressionlessly shared her life story as though she was talking about someone else. She was still pressing his wounded hand. Her words were toneless, dry, and objective.

Her eyes started to get wet to her dismay. Nonetheless, Julian did nothing but occasionally give a silent nod. He never asked if she was okay or said she went through a lot. He simply met her eyes, quietly standing in front of her, and listened as the night passed by.

By the time the morning rolled around, Sheherazade realized at last that she had been keeping Julian up all night. Her desire to leave this place was now gone. There wasn’t any particular reason. She just wanted to stay by Julian’s side.

Ha! Look at those two. It’s nice to be young.

Though it was early summer, the morning was hot, not warm. The season was harsh as it raised the hackles of a pure, innocent single man, Lloyd. With his chin propped against the wooden fence of the manor, he grumbled and gazed in the distance. A man and woman were walking along the pathway under the manor’s hill. It was Julian and Sheherazade.

“Hmm.”

Lloyd scowled, recalling the events of a few days ago. It was probably about five days ago. The night Julian returned home, gloom hung over Sheherazade’s face as she joined their family dinner. It looked like she was about to dash off to her kingdom. But the next day, her face changed altogether.

It was the face of a completely different person. And she started to stick around Julian all day.

What could have happened that night? What could have taken place that explains this happy, romantic atmosphere around them? But Lloyd wasn’t curious about it.

That isn’t important.

Sure, he was a bit miserable about still being single. But there was something more important than his love life right now.

My retirement plan.

Not just any kind. It was a plan that would deal with the danger that he knew was coming to ruin his retirement.

Of course. If I sit idly like this, I’ll never be able to live the life of Riley. The queen won’t leave me alone. I’m sure of it.

Lloyd could vouch for it. Looking back, his accomplishments were too great. From solving the financial crisis in his fiefdom to managing physical, administrative, and pathological crises, he performed extraordinary feats that went well beyond everyone’s expectations, eventually earning him the queen’s complete trust. Lloyd bit his lips as he had the thought.

No. At this rate, I won’t be able to live in peace for the remainder of my life. If I let things continue as is, I’ll end up like Hwang Hee, the minister who passed away after being exploited by King Sejong for his entire life.

Lloyd didn’t want that. He wanted to avoid that fate at all costs. He already did more than enough work up to this point. Now, he wanted to take a break and be promised a peaceful retirement. Thus, live the rest of his life leisurely and relaxing.

To do that, I need to come up with a plan.

Lloyd had a feeling he would need a plan before, but he couldn’t think of one. He couldn’t devise a good plan until he now saw Julian and Sheherazade happily flirting with each other like two lovebirds. That was when he was hit with the ingenious plan to ensure his beautiful, work-free retirement.

It’s doable. As long as everything goes to plan, I can do it. I will be able to come up with a rough idea of how to prevent the queen from treating me like a slave.

Just then, a mischievous smile spilled out of him.

“Mwahaha. So, what I will do is…”

With a greasy smile on his face, Lloyd gazed down at those flirty birds in the path. And he reviewed the broad plan he just came up with.

“I’m going to be your cupid, Julian. Thanks.”
