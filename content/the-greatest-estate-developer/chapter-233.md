# Demons Must Be Abused (1)

---

“What? Master Singer Lloyd Frontera is at the center of Hell?”

About ten demons were relaxing in a lava bath in the capital region near the Hell Castle. Among them, one demon created more stress for its parents today as usual. Another one was the cause behind the deepening wrinkles of its parents. The gathered demons placed an immense burden on their parents through various acts of evil. Normally, they boasted about their depravities and exploits to each other, but today was a bit different. There was a more refreshing topic for them to discuss.

“Lloyd Frontera?”

“Wait, the rumored man of Hell’s Singer?”

“I heard Sir Giolexius passed out listening to his singing.”

“Hahaha. How could he not?” The one-horned demon, who brought the news to the group, grinned. “You all felt it. How the gate of Hell opened up.”

“Oh, yes, I did.”

“Then you must have felt that very man we are praising set his foot inside Hell.”

“That I did, too. But you’re saying he’s at the center of Hell?”

“Yeah.” The one-horned demon nodded. “I guess you’re slow to the news? The King of Hell announced it this morning.”

“Is that actually true?”

“Of course.”

“Really?”

“Do you really think I’d lie about an announcement from the king?”

“Well. You wouldn’t.”

“Of course not. So, trust me.”

“Good. I will trust you. So, let’s go hear the master sing! To the edge of Hell!”

“But I just said that Lloyd Frontera is at the center of Hell,” the one-horned demon corrected.

“That’s why we’re heading to the edge.”

“Why?”

“Think in my shoes. Will you trust every word you say if you were me?”

“…”

“Let’s go! To the end!”

All of the demons chilling in the lava bath jumped to their feet and set on the journey to the edge after packing up. But this instance didn’t take place only here. All sorts of demons learned about Lloyd’s news near the castle, on the side of a nameless volcano, and in Purgatory. And everyone began to travel to the end of Hell with one purpose in mind.

He is said to be the master in singing. And everyone praises him. So I’ve got to check how awful his singing is. And if his singing is even slightly different from the rumor…

The demons vowed to hurl insults at him. Nitpick even the slightest fault. Lay into him to the point he’d want to die. They resolved to destroy Lloyd’s reputation as the master of singing because he was better than all of them for no reason. A man unqualified like him must be brought to the ground to make them feel better even a little. They decided to pull his hair and make him fall.

Wahaha. Get ready.

The demons evilly waited for the opportunity to come. Lloyd Frontera? Hell’s voice? The singer of the century? Their stomachs flipped every time they heard those rumors. They couldn’t wrap their heads around why a man, who seemed to be mediocre just like them, should be respected and praised. They were much better than a puny man like Frontera. It was just evident that this moronic world didn’t recognize their talents. All they needed was one chance, and they could be more famous than this Lloyd. That was the only explanation behind their misfortune and lack of fame. Some day, they’d rise above their current position and show their true talents. But first, it required dragging down this undeservingly respected man and making one thing right in their idiotic world. All of them had the same thought, each engulfed by a wave of jealousy, competitiveness, and envy. Over 30,000 unemployed demons rushed to the end of Hell.

When Javier saw the spectacle of demons gathering around, he gasped, “Is… this why you asked the King of Hell to cooperate?”

“Yeah, of course.” Lloyd nodded.

“Did you see this from the start?”

“Yeah, of course.”

Standing on top of a sharp rock, Lloyd shrugged as he looked down at the 30,000 demons assembled below the rock.

Perfect.

This was beyond his expectation. It was a great success. He smiled satisfyingly like a farmer during harvest season, as he looked at the crowd.

“Think about it,” Lloyd continued, “where are we?”

“Hell.”

“How do you think demons would react when they hear the news of a famous and acclaimed man?”

“Did you think they’d be jealous and envious?” asked Javier.

“Yeah, you are correct.” Lloyd chuckled. “I figured that they’d run down here to slander me. The result is this.” Lloyd pointed at the plain.

Woooo! 30,000 demons responded when they saw Lloyd’s gesture. Their rough cry filled the plain. First, they yelled out whatever they wanted until sometime later, their shouts evolved into one unified cry.

“Sing a song! Now!”

Their urge was threatening, and their gaze was hostile. The energy in the demon crowd grew fiercer. Lloyd smiled meaningfully as he looked at them from above. He cupped his hands together and pressed them to his mouth. Then activated the Asrahan Core Technique. The sound of his dry cough was amplified about ten times louder than normal through his hand trumpet and rang aloud across the plain.

“Hmm! Hmm! Ehem!”

The moment the noise hit their ears, the loud clamoring in the plain deafened as every demon shut their mouth. Tens of thousands of gazes focused on Lloyd. They waited in anticipation for Lloyd to sing and hoped to nitpick his skill. When their desire and anticipation reached their peak, Lloyd put on a mischievous smile.

“Come on, did you guys gather here because you thought I’d sing for you?”

“…”

Everyone flinched. Lloyd continued to speak in a nonchalant manner. 

“But I don’t want to sing for you!”

“…”

“Why should I do that?” Lloyd raised his hands, his palms looking up.

“…”

“Any strong reason why I should sing for you?” Lloyd’s voice was now brazen.

“That’s nonsense!” One of the one-horned demons yelled, provoked. “We traveled all the way to this place! To hear you sing!”

“Sure, but so?”

“And you won’t sing? That’s not making any sense!” The demon cried out again.

“Oh, yes, it does.”

“What…?”

“Why should I sing just because you all gathered here?” Lloyd asked back with a smug smile.

“B-but still!” the same demon replied, not knowing what to say. “The King of Hell made the announcement that you’ll sing here!”

“Yeah. But I just canceled it.”

“…”

“You guys are so pathetic for gathering up here just to hear a song that won’t help your life at all. Don’t you think so? Everyone must have a lot of free time.”

“And besides, did I make a promise that I’d do it?” defended Lloyd. “No, I didn’t. So, why are you demanding for me to sing? I decide whether I want to sing or not. That’s my decision. Don’t you agree?”

“So, I’m not singing,” announced Lloyd.

“No! But still!”

“You think this is unfair?”

“Of course!” One of them yelped.

“Then let’s just say I scammed you all.”

“…”

“Oh, wait, will that make you feel worse?”

“…”

Rage boiled in their hearts. They glared up at Lloyd, their gaze holding a glint of anger. If they could, they wanted to rip this human into pieces. But that wasn’t possible because of the silver-haired knight by his side, who had taken down the commander of the 1st Corps, Hell’s Knight. Come at him, and ordinary demons like them would get slaughtered even if there were thousands or tens of thousands of them. Knowing this, Lloyd laughed at the demons shaking in anger.

“Is there something else I can help everyone with? Or can you all just not bring yourself to leave? I’m going to leave now to do something much more important than entertaining this crowd here. If you have a problem with it, then fight me. Or else, piss off to where you came from and honor your parents.”

“…”

The demons silently seethed in anger. Leaving them alone, Lloyd walked down to the other end of the rock. Javier, who was waiting for him down here, frowned at Lloyd. His face showed signs of concern, which was unlike him.

“Um, Master Lloyd.”

“Eh? Why?”

“You rallied the demons in one place with the cooperation of the King of Hell,” he reminded, his voice still concerned.

“Yeah, uh-huh.” Lloyd nodded, picking up his ears.

“Then why did you treat them like that?”

“Hm?”

“We are not getting anything by treating them so heartlessly and coldly.”

“You think so?” Lloyd asked.

“Yes, I thought… that you would win their hearts with that awful singing of yours.”

“Then I’d use them as my workers?”

“Yes, so I am worried.”

“What are you worried about?”

“That they would hold hostile feelings against you and try to disturb our work.”

“Yeah, they definitely will.” Lloyd clapped his hands.

“Excuse me?”

“I said, they will definitely do it.”

“Then why did you still-”

“Do what I did?” put in Lloyd.

“Yes.”

“So that they’d disturb my work. That’s how I’ll get things moving.”

“Excuse me?”

“There’s no time to explain. Let’s get to work. Follow me.”

Lloyd motioned to Javier, who was still in a state of confusion. He took them to an area near the edge of Hell that he had eyed in advance. It was an open space with a boiling lava pond on one side.

“Okay, time to shovel.”

Thwack! Lloyd handed over a foldable shovel after bringing it out from his backpack. And he grabbed another one. Javier cocked his head to the side.

“Are you going to dig here?” asked Javier.

“Yes.”

“But for what?”

“Hunting.”

“Excuse me?”

“Hoards of hunting game will barge in this place for a few days.”

“Hunting-”

“Don’t worry about it. The hunting games will provide us with the key material for this project.”

“…”

Javier stared at Lloyd with a strange gaze, thinking to himself that Lloyd was truly a mysterious man he couldn’t understand. He even wanted to look into this young master’s head if he could. The smile Lloyd made as he looked at Javier was equally mysterious. It was as if he had already foreseen the future.

“Don’t look at me like that.”

“…”

“I’m not a prophet, and none of my ancestors were a fortuneteller. What I’m doing purely came from racking my head and thinking ahead.”

“…”

“So, come and dig now.”

“Yes.”

Javier couldn’t put a finger on what was going on and what was to happen. But he could suppose one thing. 

Master Lloyd must know a lot more about Hell than other people.

Javier had the thought on his way to meet the King of Hell and saw Lloyd conversing with him. And right now, he could feel Master Lloyd knew something about Hell that no one else did.

But…

Whatever that was, he decided to trust his young master. At least he wasn’t the type of person to harm others. He held a history of devoting himself to the fiefdom and the people around him. So, Javier decided to trust him once more and dutifully followed his instructions.

“Do I have to dig according to the construction guideline?”

“Of course.”

Bzzt! In no time, the Construction Guideline was marked on the ground in detail. From that moment, Javier and Lloyd dug over and over again without ever getting exhausted. Javier’s dug with the power of a grandmaster. On top of the two, Ppodong and Hamang joined their efforts, so they were able to dig up a massive cave in only a few hours. Overall, the cave was shaped like a large gourd. The diameter of the opening was about 32 feet, and the slope of the passage that led from the opening was about 60 degrees in angle. At the end of the passage, which extended about 320 feet, sat a huge room. The depth, width, and height were 262, 656, and 164 feet, respectively. Lloyd then built a cozy tub at the center of the room and filled it up with the help of Hamang.

“Hamang! Hamamang!”

Slurp! As luck would have it, there was a boiling hot spring nearby, and Hamang gulped the hot spring as he blew on it as if he was drinking a cup of hot coffee. Then he carried the water from the hot spring into the tub. When it was done, Ggoming was next to take action.

“Ggoming,” called Lloyd.

“Ggoming?”

“Do you like taking baths?”

“Ggoming!”

“I see, you don’t like them very much.”

“Ggomiming!”

“Hmm, but you know what? I’ve been seeing fleas in your feathers lately.”

“Ggoming?”

“You should wash yourself, right?”

“G-ggoming!”

“Yes, good. Thank you for making a good choice. Time to dive in.

"Ggoming…!”

Splash! Ggoming jumped into the hot spring. At first, he seemed to loathe it, but when the water soaked his body, the muscles around his face relaxed in ease.

“Ggo, Ggomiming…”

“Are you enjoying yourself?” asked Lloyd.

“Ggomiming…”

“Good, I’m glad. Since you like it so much, let’s leave you in there for around ten hours.”

“Ggoming…?”

“That’s how we can make a good stock.”

“…”

From then on, Ggoming stayed in the hot spring for ten consecutive hours. The water didn’t even get cold as the ground was full of heat. As a result, his prolonged stay in the water caused his feet to unreservedly swell. And soon, thanks to Ggoming’s sacrifice, the hot spring water turned into parrotbill stock.

“Ehem, that smells good.”

Lloyd carefully scooped the hot spring water, making sure that he didn’t spill a single drop. Then he sprayed it all around the entrance and passage of the cave. At last, Lloyd smiled with a glow of satisfaction, revealing all of his teeth.

“Whew, it’s done.”

A cave instead of a glass bottle trap. Parrotbill stock instead of chicken stock for bait. With that, everything was all set. He was ready to acquire a bulk of essential materials for the construction in one go. This special material that didn’t melt or bend under the lava was meant to stabilize and support the surface of the railway’s subbase and was used to build rails and ties on top of the layer at the same time. Now, the time had come to hunt lava centipedes who would bring countless pissed-off demons to where he was.
