# New Level (2)

---

Splash! Ten minutes ago. Submerged in the swirling river, Javier was dying. Opening his eyes proved to be a struggle, but when he did, it was all blurry. Nothing could be made out properly. Even the touch on his skin felt strange. Something felt cold, wet, and bubbling.

River water.

Javier thought to himself. Finally, he was somewhat conscious. He figured that he was under the Magena River after being thrown out hundreds of yards away from the fighting scene.

The tail… moved.

The tail had been definitely sliced from its body. Javier was certain that he got the dragon for good when it was immobilized. He was certain that he could defeat it by smashing its skull.

But I let my guard down too much.

He was an idiot for that. His mistake was being convicted of a victory that was yet to be his. It never occurred to him that the snapped tail would move on its own to sweep him away. But Javier soon stopped, believing that he was only giving out excuses. It was time to face reality.

I must go back.

He grew urgent. There was no time to think in here. He had to return and stop the dragon. But… his whole body had lost strength. He couldn’t even lift a finger.

“Cough!”

Brrr! He coughed with great struggle, and the remaining air in his chest bubbled out of his throat. Resultantly, his body plunged further down into the lower portion of the river. Deep down to the very bottom.

“…”

He couldn’t breathe. And as the consciousness he painstakingly clutched onto started to fade, he repeated the names that kept popping up in his mind.

My Lord… And Young Master Lloyd.

Javier vowed to protect them in the face of tribulation and even death. He promised himself to keep them from harm until his last breath. But it looked like he wouldn’t be able to fulfill that promise.

Please forgive me.

Against his will, a scoff escaped him. The vow was grand, but he was not. He was doomed to miserably drown at the bottom of the river. How pathetic, he berated himself. He hated dying this way and leaving Lloyd alone in danger. That is, he wanted to protect his young master. Was it because he was the son of the lord and successor of the fiefdom?

No.

That wasn’t it. It used to be in the past. But now, Javier had somewhat realized that there was another reason. But it wasn’t anything grand. He didn’t want to see Lloyd die or get hurt. He wanted Lloyd to laze around and grow old, much like the wish that the latter expressed from time to time. He wanted to remain as a knight by Lloyd’s side until his wish was achieved. That was it.

So…

He urged his body, begging his system to move. He tensed up his whole body in utter desperation, yelling at his broken arms, legs, and body to move as it was time to get out of this water and go back to fight. He urged, scolded, hurried, and cornered himself. But later, his heart stopped beating.

No…

That was it. His consciousness was cut, and his whole body became limp. The bottom of the river was cold and dark. With his eyes open, sank to the bottom, his body followed the slow current of the water, and just like that, he met his death. As his heart stopped beating, his blood stopped flowing. The flow of blood in the carotid artery to his brain came to a halt. Once his oxygen was cut off, his brain cells screamed, and every cell in his body struggled. His nerves, fasciae, muscles, and marrows begged for oxygen as they insisted they could move some more. This couldn’t be the end. They resisted defiantly in the face of the inevitable death. Every fiber in his system begged to keep his body alive. It was a miserable last-ditch effort. A pathetic act of begging. And now that his oxygen supply was cut off, they started to absorb other resources outside of oxygen. Fortunately, there was a good resource. Mana.

Ziiing… Even when his body was dying away, Javier’s heart held a large volume of mana. It was the manaheart of a sword master, and the three-strands circle that wrapped it. The traces of voluminous mana circulation remained in his body like a historical record of his past. Then his history recalled his memory. The traces rekindled the circulation and mana thumped in his body.

Ziiing… The circle gradually started to move again. Each organ in the body yearned for mana, and the circle instinctively moved to satisfy the desperate demands. It provided mana to every corner of the body, filling up the depleted blood vessels and stimulating the struggling body.

Ziiing! The circle’s rotation stepped up its speed. It squeezed out every mana left in the manaheart to avoid the incoming death. It then amplified and poured out everything it held to all of Javier’s body. With that, the manaheart was emptied. And yet, the circle continued to spin, squeezing out more without holding anything back. It continued until even the manaheart itself was gone.

Ziiing! The circle’s violent rotation went beyond its limit and melted the manaheart, Javier’s manaheart beginning to fade away like cotton candy in the spring and spilled ink, like a sweeping storm. It melted and smeared into all his cells, equipping each with a manaheart. Before this moment, the manaheart was only a large lump sitting in his heart. But now, it was different. He had 60 trillion somatic cells and 60 trillion manahearts. Multiply both, and that was the amount of its resonance. All of the processes formed a network along the blood vessels, its distance reaching over 62,137 miles. It dispersed, disseminated, and amplified without holding anything back. Finally, everything amounted to one big explosion.

“…!”

He was silent. Javier had no time to be aware of anything. He didn’t know how his eyes had opened, how he had gotten out of the river. How he’d gotten here. It was a mystery even to him.

Soon, Javier made sense of things. He woke up to find himself out of the river and back on the battlefield. Before he knew it, he had his back against Lloyd and Queen Magentano, with the Bone Dragon hurtling toward them. Gazing up at the dragon, he could see it.

I can feel it plainly.

The blowing wind. The difference in air density. The mana pool loaded in it. The borders of the minute mana whispered. The way his sword should be wielded was engraved in his heart. The place he wanted to focus his strength came into his view. So, he did. He raised his sword and wielded it sideways. So casually. Nothing special. He just wielded his sword the way his heart told him to. And when he opened all of his manahearts, the somatic cells in his whole body reacted. The manaheart as many as the somatic cells burned, creating a resonance that reached infinity. It was then amplified into the three-stranded circle. And just like that, he sliced a portion of the world.

…!

Likewise, there was no sound. The sword ray glinted in the split second he blinked. That was enough.

Slice. Lines formed across the body of the Bone Dragon. One, two, three, five, ten, 100, and 1,962. The 1,962 strands of aura rushed into the air. No, it broke through it with a sharp noise. The body of the Bone Dragon met the same fate.

…!

The Bone Dragon was shattered into thousands of pieces and defenselessly scattered in various directions. Thud! Twang! Boom!

It was like the whole area was struck with a salvo of dice-sized bone pieces. But Javier gave no mind to the dragon anymore. After he put the sword into his ordinary sheath, he turned and checked on the people he had saved.

“Your Majesty, are you well?” asked Javier.

“Y-Yes… As you can see…” murmured Queen Magentano with a dazed look on her face. She couldn’t believe what spectacle she had just witnessed.

What was that just now?

It was beyond her comprehension. The shocking sight was too much for her to understand in mere seconds. But soon, she realized that there was something more important than that.

“Oh, more than that, here.” She spoke as she put down Lloyd she was carrying. And she took a step back.

“Master Lloyd,” called Javier as he approached Lloyd. “Are you awake?”

“Yeah…” Lloyd’s voice was raspy.

“Are you okay, Young Master?”

“Do… I… look okay in your eyes…?” A faint smile appeared on Lloyd’s face.

He was far from okay. Really. Every part of his body was sore and in pain. He felt like he had at least broken a few bones. It looked like he also suffered a concussion. He kept dry-heaving, which was torturing him to no end. It was so severe that he wanted to commend himself for achieving the miracle of not passing out. Even though it was painful, Lloyd was able to put on a smile nonetheless.

This rascal is really something.

The sight of Javier brought a smile to his face. Javier didn’t die. He was safe. Completely fine. And that spectacle of taking down the Bone Dragon?

He has done it. He’s now a grandmaster.

Lloyd could sense it. Javier’s aura was split into thousands of strands when it slashed through the entire space. The Bone Dragon was chopped like pieces of radish in a second. That spectacle was rather familiar in Lloyd’s eyes as he had read the novel. That part never failed to overwhelm him, even after reading the same passage many, many times. Thankfully, Lloyd was able to ease his mind.

It’s a relief.

Actually, Lloyd had been worrying that Javier’s growth was stagnant. He was afraid that by completing the Asrahan Core Technique and teaching Javier about the mana blast, he had taken away his chance for self-growth. Part of him was bothered that his teaching poisoned Javier’s growth. But today, he was relieved of that burden right away.

And we’ve overcome this crisis.

Feeling relieved, Lloyd let out a breath of ease. Meanwhile, Javier placed his hands on Lloyd’s shoulders.

Ziiing… Lloyd felt his powerful mana streaming into his body from the shoulders. He even felt Javier kindly spinning the mana circle for him. A comforting mana slowly traveled throughout his body.

Ugh…

He wondered if this was what it felt like to rest after a day of work on a luxury massage chair but not before hitting the sauna and a bowl of warm soup. Lloyd almost moaned but stopped just in time. And soon enough, the emergency treatment was over.

“Whew…”

Lloyd snapped his eyes open. Now feeling light, he sat up. There wasn’t any pain anymore. The parts that he had thought were either broken or fractured had been completely healed. Lloyd turned to face Javier, who treated him.

“Hey, you,” called Lloyd.

“Yes, sir.”

“You’re not expecting me to thank you for what you did, are you?” Lloyd lifted his chin.

“Of course not.”

“Really?” asked Lloyd again, narrowing his eyes.

“Yes,” Javier assured. “You are not that kind of person, after all.”

“You know me well. You were about to tell me that I am a petty, vengeful person and whatnot, weren’t you?”

"You know me well,” said Javier with a smile.

“Yes. I know you very well. I know what you have accomplished too,” hinted Lloyd

“Did you… figure it out already?” Javier’s voice contained mild disbelief.

“How can I not?” Lloyd scolded and pointed with his chin at the thousands of shattered pieces of the Bone Dragon that lay on the ground. “You just did that with one stroke of your sword. So, how can I not? It was the Bone Dragon, for Pete’s sake.”

“…”

“Anyway, congratulations.”

“Thank you.”

“Thank you?” huffed Lloyd

“Yes.”

“Are you thanking me because you know what I’m congratulating you for?”

“Well, of course, it is because I have become a grand-”

“Grand what?” put in Lloyd.

Javier stopped and said nothing afterward.

“I don’t give a hoot about your grand… whatever that is,” huffed Lloyd. “But hey, I just saw how smoothly you chopped everything into pieces.”

“Excuse me?” Javier retorted.

“You chopped it to pieces. That.” Lloyd chuckled and pointed to the Bone Dragon’s skeleton. “The pieces are all uniform in size with a sleek surface. Very nice. Nice indeed. Now, you’re going to make a fine granite cutter. It’ll be easy to cut them up into thousands of pieces of blocks and tiles, right?”

“…”

“This is what you call ‘mass production’,” Lloyd said proudly. “Standardization. Regularization of size. And flawless quality guarantee. So you just have to slice things like that from now on. I’ll do the selling. The profit will be a ratio of nine to one. Of course, I’m going to have 90 percent of the profit, while you take the ten. Got it?”

“…”

“You’re going to be rich too, man.” Lloyd congratulated

“Ha…”

Finally, Javier could not repress his laughter anymore. Lloyd was smiling as he tapped his shoulders with force. This young master was making a corny joke right now, and Javier could see from his eyes that Master Lloyd was rejoicing. Not only because he had reached a new level in swordsmanship but also because he was safe. Javier felt pure joy in Lloyd. And the feeling was mutual for Javier.

I am glad that you are safe, Master Lloyd.

There was no need to speak it out loud. Just a silly joke was enough. Javier’s desire to protect Lloyd, and Lloyd’s heart to cherish and worry about Javier. The two understood each other without needing any words, and that was enough. It was enough, even if they outwardly hurled insults at each other.

“Is that so? Then congratulations, too,” said Javier.

“Congratulations? For what?”

“Since you are alive, you can always be made fun of by me whenever you want.”

“Ha,” said Lloyd defiantly. “Are you seriously going to make fun of me for being 30? Do you really think that will have any effect on me?”

“No.” Javier shook his head.

“Then what?”

“30-year-old…”

“Stop it,” Lloyd broke in.

“…virgin.” Javier ignored it and finished it.

“I said, don’t say it.”

Just like that, Lloyd chuckled like a fool and got up with the support of Javier. That was only when things became real to him.

I’m alive.

Javier was helping Lloyd get to his feet, and the Bone Dragon’s remains were sprawled all across the area. The unbelievable event started to feel real now. He finally took in that he had gone through a life-or-death experience. That was when he was able to relax a little bit. Just then, unexpected messages appeared in front of him.

Ding dong.

[Your Zombie Domination skill has spotted a new entity that can be dominated.]

[Object: Bone Dragon]

[Status: Critical Condition]

[The Bone Dragon, which is originally strong, is unexpectedly in a critical condition. Therefore, the mana in it is almost depleted. Due to this, the condition to dominate with your basic domination skill has been satisfied temporarily. Moreover, once you succeed in dominating, the object will never be freed without your permission.]

[Would you like to dominate the Bone Dragon?]

[YES / NO]

Lloyd exclaimed “Jackpot” in his head after reading the messages, and his eyes widened.
