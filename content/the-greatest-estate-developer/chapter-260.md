# Do You Know About the Jewel? (1)

---

“Excuse me, have you ever heard of the Jewel of Truth?”

“…”

The 840,928th egg was at the center of the strange but humongous city in the Mermaid Kingdom, composed of hundreds and thousands of eggs. It sat at the deepest part of the Arctic Ocean. The residents of this area, otherwise known as the “Red Egg,” found themselves encountering an eccentric foreigner.

“The Jewel of Truth?” The mermaid who heard the question bulged her pecs, wondering what the heck this human was saying. She stared intensely at the stranger, Lloyd, who awkwardly swam in place before flashing a smile.

“Oh, please don’t think of me strangely,” Lloyd explained. “I just heard an interesting rumor from the human world. I came all the way down here because I heard that there is a remarkable treasure called the Jewel of Truth.”

“Hmph,” puffed the mermaid, “That’s an ancient rumor.”

“Excuse me?”

“That’s such an old story…” The mermaid sent a baffling gaze at Lloyd, and she was already about five gills away from him.

“I only heard of it in passing when I was a child. Sorry.”

“Oh, please wait a minute…!”

The mermaid dashed away from Lloyd before he had any chance to stop her, her gills working faster than earlier.

“Haah,” Lloyd sighed.

This… This feels weird.

Their reactions were slightly different from what he expected. Originally, since the Jewel of Truth was one of the precious jewels of the kingdom and existed in their myths and legends, he assumed that the merpeople would be familiar with it. As such, he anticipated easily getting an answer from any passing merperson.

Of course, I didn’t expect all their answers to be useful. I thought that once I put them all together, I’d be able to find some sort of clue or lead. But this is just…

Their reaction was the complete opposite of his expectations. Almost all the merpeople reacted cynically to his question. Some were even…

“Excuse me, do you know anything about the Jewel of Truth?” Lloyd asked a merperson passing by.

“No, I don’t. I really don’t,” the mermaid swayed in irritation.

“Do you really not know?”

“Oh, don’t hold me back. I have to swim. It’s not like you’ll take responsibility if I lose my muscles because you stopped me from swimming.”

This kind of blatant disrespect toward him was a common occurrence. In other words, this was…

I’m being treated like those cult members who poach people in public!

Lloyd sighed at length. Surely enough, everyone’s reaction told of his existence to them. They acted like they were being held back to answer a useless question in their busy schedule. They blatantly showed their annoyance. Still, even that could be considered a mild reaction compared to others.

“My, Ma'am, where are you so busy going?” Lloyd asked another mermaid.

“Hmm? What’s a human from above water doing here?”

“Oh, well, the thing is, I am looking for a treasure called the Jewel of Truth here. And you appeared at a perfect time. I can’t believe I ran into such a wise-looking mermaid like you. Could you spare me a few minutes to answer a few questions-”

“No,” the mermaid rejected flatly.

“…”

“I’m already going nuts from my aching bones. I don’t give a damn about your questions.”

“Um, but still-”

“Still, my foot. You young, dumb human. You’re a scammer, right?”

“Excuse me?” Lloyd stared, aghast.

“How can the Jewel of Truth exist in our day and age? I’d have entertained you if it was 10,000 years ago when the seasons of fall and winter didn’t exist. You’re bringing up crap that’s older than the dentures of my great, great, great grandmother. It’s not something a human child like you should care about.”

“…”

“So, young man. What are you really up to? What kind of scam are you trying to pull off, asking around about the Jewel of Truth and whatnot?”

“Um…”

“Answer me now!” snarled the old mermaid. “Do you want a spanking from me?!”

Flare! The mermaid grandmother growled menacingly. Though her back was slightly curved, her forearms looked terrorizing. She seemed strong enough to easily snap five quarter dollars in half as her regular morning workout. Lloyd was in dismay.

Haah. I don’t know what to tell her.

Lloyd wanted to cry. All this time, he was receiving icy stares from passing merpeople, and some of the elderly nearby even tried to spank Lloyd when he acted in the slightest ill-mannered way.

“Eek! Please forgive me! I am sorry!” Eventually, Lloyd bowed several times and scooted off. And as he caught his breath in the corner of an alley, a surge of bitterness arose in him.

Wow. This is just nuts.

Dumbstruck, a snort escaped him.

This feels like I’m going around Seoul asking people where I could find a wolf pretending to be an old lady in Little Red Riding Hood.

That was how they reacted. The Jewel of Truth of the Mermaid Kingdom. Now, Lloyd could sorely feel what an ancient treasure it was.

Should I give up and go back?

He considered going back home and clinging onto Dragon King Verkis, who had played him. That looked like a better course of action. Another option was traveling to Hell and asking the King of Hell about how he could stop destiny from restoring, even if it came with a price. But he soon shook his head.

No. I went out of my way to come all the way down here!

It was too much of a waste to pack up and go back. He felt that he should stick around for about a day or two longer. If nothing useful was found, then he would go back. From that moment on, he acted more shamelessly as he went around questioning anyone he could find despite the cold treatment and disrespect he received from the merpeople.

“Excuse me? Have you ever heard of the Jewel of Truth?”

“…”

“There! Over there with the awesome biceps! Are you interested in the Jewel of Truth?”

“…”

“Oh, you have the most beautiful gill among the mermaids I’ve seen today. Anyhow, have you ever heard of the Jewel of Truth?”

“…”

Not a single one of them reacted nicely. They all treated him like how Koreans reacted to cult members on the streets. That is, with a gaze that said, “What in the bullerap are you talking about?” Then walking away after ignoring him. But Lloyd was relentless. He was neither disappointed nor discouraged. The cold disregard and disrespect were something he experienced often back in Korea when he was shabbily dressed and at the bottom of the social pyramid. Experiencing that again was nothing to fuss over at all. It was nothing new. On the contrary, he just became more brazen.

Whatever. If they’re going to regard me as if I’m a cult member, then I might well just act like one.

At last, Lloyd made the decision. Since he couldn’t avoid it, he would go all out. It was time to embody a cult recruiter and draw everyone’s attention.

“Excuse me, ma'am. My, you look so kind! You look like someone who received an immense amount of positive energy from the Jewel of Truth!”

“…”

“Fellow over there? Don’t your shoulders feel heavy or doesn’t your neck feel tense? Goodness, I’m looking at you now, and your ancestors are on your back crying. They’re saying that you should find the Jewel of Truth at all costs.”

“…”

“Hmm, I don’t know if I can tell you this when I’m just seeing you for the first time, but tell me, aren’t you going through a hard time recently? If you receive energy from the Jewel of Truth, everything will go well for you. I don’t usually tell this to people, but I’m making an exception just for you.”

“…”

Of course, despite this change of strategy, the merpeople still showed the same cold reaction. But there was one thing that was now different.

Great. They are gathering around!

Lloyd discretely clenched his fists. Could it be that the rumor about him and his weird ways went out? Could it be that they started to hear about the human nut-job? They were gathering around in groups and whispering as they watched Lloyd. Some scoffed, while some erupted into laughter. The atmosphere of the crowd was that of people watching a fascinating or silly scene. Lloyd, however, felt no shame despite all this.

Great. This is much better than before.

Now, he was starting to grow more hopeful. It wasn’t that they were responding now. It was only that he succeeded in gaining traction and gathering a fairly large audience.

This is a matter of probability, so to speak.

The chances of getting an answer would increase if he could gather hundreds of merpeople at once instead of asking one of them at a time.

Though Javier is embarrassed on my behalf.

Javier had been blushing crimson since a while ago. He couldn’t even lift his head up. Could it be that he was ashamed of Lloyd? Was he trying to signal to everybody that this human was a stranger to him? Whatever the reason was, his shame was evident. He couldn’t get himself to move far away but timidly stayed five steps away from him. Lloyd grinned.

So, the game is on.

From then on, Lloyd talked louder and asked questions about the Jewel of Truth, so that every merperson gathered nearby could hear him. He shamelessly shouted in full force, hoping that any interested merperson would speak out. That was what he was doing when…

“Excuse me, are you really curious about the Jewel of Truth?”

For the first time, a merman came and asked Lloyd a question!

“Eh?” Shocked, Lloyd stared at the merman.

It was a young merman with eye-catching light blue hair. But his looks were a bit unique.

He’s not that muscular for a merman.

Unlike the common muscular build, this merman was different. He had the body of a track athlete, slim. And he was sending a serious gaze at Lloyd.

“Actually, I’ve heard you speaking since a while ago. I mean the questions you’ve been asking other merpeople.”

“You’ve heard my questions?” asked Lloyd in surprise.

“Yes.” The merman nodded slightly. And in a much more serious voice, he said, “So, I would like to have a serious conversation with you about the Jewel of Truth. Do you mind if we move somewhere else? Somewhere less crowded.”

“I’d like that,” Lloyd pleasingly agreed with a nod.

Yes, this was the moment he had been waiting for all this time. He and Javier followed the slim merman. They all swam away until they arrived in a less populated area.

The merman turned and said, “It is nice to meet you. My name is Rotorua.”

“Rotorua?” Lloyd repeated the name instead of greeting himself as he was still in a daze.

“That’s right. My mother got the name from the most famous mermaid of the mythical age. She did so in the hopes that I would retrieve the most precious treasure of that time period, the Jewel of Truth.” Merman Rotorua spoke with a grave look on his face.

Lloyd lifted his brow. “You’re looking for an artifact of the mythical age? The Jewel of Truth?”

“Yes.” Rotorua nodded. “To be honest, my family has devoted all our lives to unearthing the Jewel of Truth for several generations. They have been doing so for thousands of years. But we have not succeeded yet. Then recently I heard the news of a human swimming around asking strange questions.”

“It was about me,” pointed Lloyd.

“That is why I hurriedly swam over here.” A faint smile bloomed Rotorua’s face which had remained serious until then. It manifested the surprise and admiration he felt earlier.

I never expected to meet a human who believes in the Jewel of Truth.

He was more than pleased about it and couldn’t be any happier. The reason for his happiness was simple.

Even the merpeople have nearly forgotten about the Jewel of Truth. However, it’s the most regrettable part of our heritage that we have lost.

Rotorua has believed for the longest time that it must be retrieved. That was why he devoted his whole life searching for it. But he never got any of his folks to cooperate with him. Instead, he was mocked all this time for being unmerman-like. A true merperson did not fuss over the artifact. He was told a proper merperson should care about swimming and building more muscles instead of going out searching for a boring jewel. Rotorua had been lashed out and slapped down all this time. But today, he met someone who was on the same page as him. The Jewel of Truth was something that even merpeople overlooked.

Yet, he finally met someone who was as desperate to find the treasure as him! But it was not a merperson but a human! Deeply moved, Rotorua grabbed Lloyd’s hands as his shoulders shivered in excitement.

“So, I would like to ask for a favor if it is not too much from a stranger like me.”

“What favor?” asked Lloyd.

With his eyes glimmering in sheer earnestness, Rotorua asked, “How about you and I work together to find the Jewel of Truth?”

Rotorua didn’t care if his expedition partner wasn’t his kind or a different race. That didn’t matter as long as he had a companion to search for the jewel with. He was merely happy to work with someone like him. Just then…

“Hmm,” mused Lloyd. “I don’t know.” He cocked his head to the side and sent a doubting gaze at Rotorua before saying, “The thing is, I don’t know how much I should trust you.”

“What…?” His words flustered Rotorua. This human man was looking for the Jewel of Truth. So Rotorua naturally presumed that his request would be accepted instead of receiving such a snobby and reluctant reaction.

“Is it not?” Lloyd continued to voice out his doubt. “Put yourself in my shoes. The others are busy ignoring me in annoyance, and yet, you came on your own to find me. How should I know whether you have something up your sleeves? Am I supposed to happily take your offer without a second thought?”

“Oh, what are you talking-”

“I’m saying,” put in Lloyd, “What if you’re a scammer with bad intentions? Don’t you think it’d be easy for you to prey on my desperate, innocent heart, which has been tattered after running around for an answer but to no avail?”

“H-hey. I’m not the kind of merperson that preys on someone’s desp-”

“Then prove it,” demanded Lloyd as he gazed into Rotora’s eyes.

“…”

“You said your family has been searching for the jewel for generations.”

“I did.” Rotorua frowned, trying to make sense of this whole situation. “But what of it-”

“So, prove that you’re not a scammer.”

“How?”

“Well, how else?” Lloyd said, his voice dripping with humdrumness. “It’s easy. Tell me everything about it. I know a lot about it on my part. I’ll cross-check your knowledge with mine, and I’ll be able to judge if you’re scamming me or being genuine.”

“I-is that how much you can’t trust me?”

“Tsk. You know, you’re free to go if you can’t prove yourself.”

Lloyd then wheeled around. At the sight, Rotorua was provoked. But anxiousness soon overcame his anger.

No!

This was nearly his first time meeting someone who was deeply invested in the jewel. He didn’t want to lose this human. Rotorua hurriedly held out his hand and grabbed Lloyd’s shoulder.

“Then you better listen to me carefully. You see, the Jewel of Truth has been passed down since the mythical age of the Mermaid Kingdom…”

From then on, Rotorua began to reel off everything about the jewel, antsy and annoyed at the same time. He missed the evil smile that hung on Lloyd’s face as he tried to prove his innocence. It never occurred to him that Lloyd, whose back was against Rotorua, was smiling like a man who succeeded in catching a fish with his bait.
