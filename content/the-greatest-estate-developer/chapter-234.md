# Demons Must Be Abused (2)

---

Fwoooosh! Hiss! 200 legs rippled and whisked the lava, and the centipede went up to the ground and made its long body move forward.

Splash! A giant lava centipede came out of the lava lake. Its body was 98 feet long and about 3 feet wide. Truly, this centipede was a huge one, and its temper was extremely foul.

Swish! Hiss! It let out a deathly sound the moment it set foot on the ground, ready to bite and rip apart anything bothersome in sight with its huge jaw. However, contrary to the angry centipede, the demon on the centipede’s back grinned in contentment.

Wahaha. This is what I’m talking about!

The one-horned demon flashed an evil smile, and a surge of conviction rose in its heart.

“A thick shell that can’t be melted by the hottest lava! A jaw that can penetrate the sturdiest of armor! And the strength to crush a boulder in one stomp! We have gathered 30,000 creatures who possess all these features! Mwahaha!” the demon cried out triumphantly.

“Wahahahaha!” another joined in its cry.

“Die, Lloyd Frontera!”

“Let’s end him, the master singer!”

“Hell’s Singer? I will give you a taste of Hell!”

“Arghh!!”

At the one-horned demon’s shout, the rest of the 30,000 demons followed suit, and 30,000 centipedes beneath them hissed. 30,000 demons and lava centipedes. They created a stately atmosphere as they charged forward. The entire lava pond turned black as they marched, crushing and flattening the basalt boulders with their movements. All the creatures of Hell whose homes resided in their pathway hid themselves in fear.

It made sense. Demons were undoubtedly an unnerving force, but the lava centipedes were ghastly creatures, predators in the ecosystem of Hell. Their shell was so thick and hard that not even hot lava could melt it down. And their body reached from 65 to 98 feet in length. On top of everything, no creature in the lava pond could dare put up a fight against them due to their foul temper. Yet the demons had gathered up 30,000 of these horrifying predators. It wasn’t an understatement to say they had brought with them the entire centipede population to Hell.

But I don’t care!

The one-horned demon leading at the front of the march of 30,000 centipedes thought to itself.

He insulted us! How dare he insult us demons when he’s just a human!

Its fist violently shivered at that thought. The demon felt a pang of shame. It had tried to nitpick the human and leave a dent in his reputation as the master singer. It had rushed to the edge of the Hell full of anticipation and dreams. But when it got there, the demon realized Lloyd had tricked all of them, asking why he must sing for the crowd. And he even acknowledged his hustling, bestowing a blessing for them to honor their parents instead of wasting their time with him.

That was more insulting!

Insulted. Humiliated. The mockery was something it could never forget. It wanted revenge. That was why it cooperated with the other demons and spent over 15 days rallying together all the lava centipedes in hell!

“What do we want?!” the one-horned demon roared.

“To humiliate Lloyd Frontera!”

“Or give him death!”

“Let’s end him!”

The one-horned demon cried out first, and 29,999 demons answered in unison. All the demons were convinced of one thing.

We’ll win this fight! No matter what!

After all, the legion of 30,000 lava centipedes was more than enough to take Lloyd down, even if he had that extremely powerful knight standing next to him.

Even Hell’s Knight, who commands the Corps in Hell, can only fight about 1,000 centipedes by itself!

That was the case for Giolexius, better known as Hell’s Knight, whom Lloyd and Javier defeated. Giolexius was the commander of the 1st Corps, which meant it was considerably stronger than the other knights in Hell. There was one time it faced 1300 lava centipedes by itself. That was its limit. Given that, 30,000 lava centipedes would easily destroy them, even if Lloyd Frontera sang an awful song and Javier Asrahan wielded his powerful sword.

Hahaha. Even if they had taken down Hell’s Knight, they’re still humans in the end. How strong could they possibly get during that short time? There will be no mercy for them. I’ll sit back and enjoy the show as those bugs beg for their lives!

Kaboom! At last, the 30,00 demons and lava centipedes arrived at the edge of Hell, and their eyes eagerly searched for the despicable human who caused them great humiliation. But…

“Where is he?”

Lloyd was missing from the scene. They scoured the area and searched for him in every direction around them. They even poked their heads into volcano craters, and yet, Lloyd Frontera was still nowhere to be found.

“Could it be that he ran away?” One of them postulated.

“No way!”

They were feeling flustered when…

Hiss? All of the lava centipedes flinched and shuddered in unison. Soon, they jerked their heads up and shook their antennas.

“Eh? What are they doing?”

The one-horned demon cocked its head. It considered the centipede’s behavior insignificant at first. But that turned out to be untrue. Gradually, the centipedes stopped listening. They began to ignore all of the demon’s orders and move on their own.

“Eh?! Eh?” The demon squealed.

It yelped at one of them to stop, but it was no use. In no time, the centipede dashed toward one direction with its antenna moving in alert, as though a tasty prey was waiting there. It aggressively charged forward in full force. Swish! Swoosh!

“Eek? Ack! What’s wrong with you?!”

The one-horned demon was flustered. But the situation didn’t end there. All the other centipedes nearby were the same, escaping the demon’s control and dashing toward a single place. The reason behind their sudden change was simple. A mouthwateringly appetizing scent was coming from this specific direction.

Hiss! Their antennas trembled in sheer excitement but didn’t know why. It was that smell. The scrumptious smell stimulated their instincts. It was like the smell of chicken stock! As soon as the scent enamored their noses, their whole body quivered. They yearned to soak themselves in this smell! Thus, they dashed in search of the slowly intensifying aroma, crossing a plain of volcanic ash and crushing any boulders in their way until they discovered a cave.

Hiss! They were certain. The rich aroma of stock was coming from a deep underground cave in this direction. The voices of the demons that were leading the centipedes fell on deaf ears.

“Hey! Stop! I said, stop! Hey!”

Hiss! They made for the cave right away, ignoring the demons’ yelling. As they crawled further down, their antennas became captivated by the aroma of stock that intensified the deeper they went in. In response, they ran forward in a frenzy. There was no way for them to stop. And finally, the moment they entered the innermost part of the cave, something came into view.

“Ggoming?”

It was a giant parrobill taking a bath, the bird’s feathers seemingly expanded from soaking in the water for several long hours. Besides the bird, there was a human who grinned as he scooped the hot bath water into a bucket.

“See? I told you they’d come.” The centipedes looked toward where the human directed his words, and they saw a silver-haired knight sitting down without a trace of surprise in his gaze. He was simply staring back at them with keen eyes like a butcher looking at a new slab of meat. Then they saw his hands move slowly. He was sharpening a sword on a whetstone.

Hiss…? The centipedes shuddered at the unexpected turn of events.

The hunt was a success. All 30,000 centipedes were taken care of, and every one of the 30,000 demons was captured. He used a very systematic process to make this happen.

Hiss? The moment the lava centipede at the very front flinched, Javier’s sword flashed in the air. With that, the centipede’s final fate was set in stone.

Slash! Scores of aura strands enveloped the bug’s entire body, immediately cutting and dissecting its back, stomach, antenna, and legs in equal parts. The inside of the shell was chopped up into a smaller size that was easily transportable. This whole process took only an exact 0.2 seconds. By the time the rays of aura subsided, the lava centipede was neatly diced into pieces and ready to be put away. As for the one-horned demon on its back, Lloyd took care of it. He only needed to say one thing.

“Lower your stupid head.”

“Yes, Master Lloyd.”

Drop! Reading the room in a flash, the demon pelted to the corner and shoved its head against the ground, hands behind its back. It cried while consoling itself that at least the horn jutting from its forehead made it easier to find balance while its head was pressed into the ground. Meanwhile, the hunt continued.

Hiss?! When the next centipede entered the cave, drawn inside by the smell of parrotbill stock…

Slashh! Javier’s aura flashed in the air without fail.

Thwop! The remaining demon dropped its head to the ground just like the first one. Meanwhile, Ppodong became busy with moving the centipede shells and soft tissue pieces expertly dismembered by Javier to ensure the cave would remain clean and the hunt would go on smoothly. He transferred the centipede remains to the exit opposite the entrance. And just like that, the hunt continued for the rest of the day. The demons outside tried to stop the centipedes when they figured out what was going on, but it was no use. Completely blinded by the intoxicating aroma of parrotbill stock, the bugs bolted right into Lloyd’s trap without any care.

They entered the trap one after another, and Javier cut into them in consecutive order. Through this process, it took them a total of eight days to complete their hunt. All 30,000 centipedes were methodically dismembered into equal-sized pieces and put away. Their remains were stored in Lloyd’s makeshift supply warehouse. Every single one of the 30,000 demons was captured alive, and they all kneeled across the vast plain of volcanic ash.

“Mwhahahaha.”

“…”

“Hahaha. Mwhahahahaha.”

“…”

Lloyd’s eerie laughter filled the land. The demons swallowed dryly as they heard the ominous noise coming from him.

What’s going to happen to us now?

Everyone was filled with anxiety. It was understandable, considering the demons never imagined that the 30,000 lava centipedes they had ambitiously rallied would powerlessly get sliced into pieces. They didn’t foresee that Lloyd would set such a trap for them either. The fact that the silver-haired knight with him had risen above the status of a sword master was an even greater surprise to them. Not a single one of the demons ever thought they would be captured alive as they were currently.

Ha. My life as a demon. I should’ve led a trashier life.

The one-horned demon heaved a big sigh. It would’ve lived a more evil life had it known that this was how everything would end. It should have committed more wrongdoings without leaving behind any regrets instead of doing a half-assed job. But life was short and regret always came too late.

“Hey, you there,” called Lloyd.

“…”

Step. Lloyd approached the one-horned demon, and he looked down at it.

“I heard you incited all of these guys to move,” added Lloyd.

“…”

The one-horned demon kept its head low.

“So, isn’t it only right for you to be punished?” Lloyd spoke with a slightly raised voice.

“…”

Punish me or do whatever you want. You’re going to eventually kill me anyway. Why keep wasting your breath? The demon waited for its incoming death. But what Lloyd said afterward caught it by slight surprise.

“From now on, you won’t be able to die even if you want to. I’ll abuse you until you beg me to kill you instead." 

"Excuse me…?” the demon gasped and lifted its head in sheer confusion. Then it saw. It saw the smile of an evil employer who was hell-bent on abusing his workers in the most merciless and cruel way possible.

“Excuse me, my ass,” Lloyd huffed. “You’re going to work. I’m holding you captive right now.”

“…”

“So, you guys are my slaves from now on. Do you not understand what’s happening?”

“Um… then…”

“Yeah, ask your question,” allowed Lloyd.

“Are you not going to kill us?” the demon asked hopefully.

“Why would I kill you?” Lloyd tilted his head and beamed, wondering why on earth it was asking such a foolish question. “Don’t you agree that it’s a loss for me if I kill those I can abuse to near death?”

“So, why would I kill you? I can’t be bothered to, and it’s unnecessary.” Lloyd shook his head.

“…”

“If you have a death wish, then die from overwork. That’ll be the only way you can die from this moment onward.”

“…”

Kill me now instead, thought the one-horned demon as a shiver went down its shoulders. However, Lloyd’s smile was completely devoid of mercy, and correspondingly, there wasn’t an employment contract between them either.

I’m dealing with demons here.

Obviously, Lloyd wouldn’t be creating a contract. There also wouldn’t be any proper treatment or wages given to the workers. The reason was simple.

These guys are quite literally demons. If I treat them with kindness, they’ll take advantage of me. That’s the way of life in Hell.

Being kind caused more harm than good. Following the rules brought trouble to your door. Those were the rules of thumb in Hell. And since Lloyd was in Hell, he decided to do what demons do. He vowed to try his best to adhere to local customs this time around.

And my plan worked perfectly.

It wasn’t just a simple success. It was a grand success. Spreading a rumor through the King of Hell. Humiliating the demons after they gathered around him. Lloyd expected them to react poorly to his plan.

It’s just the nature of demons. They always pay back their grudges. And if the one that brought their wrath is someone superior to them, there’s no way they’d just let them off without a fight.

Lloyd predicted that they would pour all their efforts and resources into getting revenge. His prediction was on point. The demons rushed here with the strongest creature they could control in Hell, the lava centipede.

The lava centipede made a short appearance in the novel.

As the name suggested, these centipedes lived in lava ponds. And so, their shells didn’t melt in lavas. Not only that but since their shells were big and pleasing to look at, Lloyd believed they would become a key material in building the railroad tracks.

Moreover, there are 30,000 of them. This will be enough.

The foundation for the railroad. The railroad bed is on top of the foundation. And the railroad tie and rail that will be installed along the bed. Lloyd was able to obtain every material required to build those from the centipede. As luck would have it, he held plenty of experience when it came to catching centipedes. Where? In the military. How? By becoming a dutiful SCV of the administrative NCO.

Ha. Thinking about it pisses me off to this day.

At that time, the NCO was interested in healthy foods, and that could probably explain the two-week centipede hunt. He had to become a hunting specialist when the sergeant heard about the health benefits of drinking alcohol infused with centipedes.

I learned from that experience. Pour chicken stock into a plastic bottle, and just wait until the centipedes crawl into it. Then zap! Seriously, I was like a ginseng digger or a pest controller.

Anyway, thanks to his experience, Lloyd was able to successfully catch and kill lava centipedes and acquire 30,000 demon slaves. As he delved more into his accomplishment, the corners of his lips rose into an evil smirk.

“By the way, just to clarify, the stuff about safety first and whatnot? You can forget it. And obviously, there’s no wage or additional stipend. Got it? If you have a problem, try breaking through Javier’s watch and run away,” Lloyd growled.

“…”

The one-horned demon and the others thought inwardly that this human was quite charming, in the sense that he was evil.

Ugh… I can’t fall for him… But he’s respectable!

Now, as Lloyd treated the demons, he was transformed into an unprecedentedly evil employer. And before he knew it, 30,000 demons were deeply impressed by his merciless attitude.
