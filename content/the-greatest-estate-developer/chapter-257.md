# The Internal Affairs of the Mermaid Kingdom (1)

---

“You won’t freeze to death in the water since you’re a grandmaster, right?”

“…”

“Right?” said Lloyd.

“…”

What are you talking about, Master Lloyd? Javier shot an icy glower at Lloyd, understanding immediately what he was about to do.

“You can’t be thinking of using me as bait after suggesting for us to fish out of nowhere, could you, Master Lloyd?”

“Yeah. That’s correct.” Lloyd waggled his brows.

Javier didn’t respond.

“Um, are you happy that you got the answer in one try?”

“…”

I’m not happy. Rather, I wanted to trample you for just a second, Young Master. But Javier couldn’t express his sincere emotion because Lloyd hurriedly explained his plan.

“Hey, even though you may think I’m joking around here, you know this situation is serious, right?”

“I am aware of that.”

“That’s why.” Lloyd’s stare suddenly grew serious. “This won’t do. This ship won’t be able to endure if it continues to be attacked like now. Besides, those mermaids don’t look like they’ll give up any time soon.”

“I agree with you,” seconded Javier seriously.

“So, I need you to get in the water.”

“By being a bait, what plan do you exactly have on your mind?”

“A very scary bait.” Lloyd smiled scarily. “I never meant to fight back, but this won’t do. So, we just need to return one proper blow. One that won’t hurt them but send them the message to back off. You can do that, right?”

“Are you saying we can buy time to escape while they back off from the ship?”

“That’s right. How does it sound?”

“I think I can do that.” Javier nodded. He quickly understood Lloyd’s message. With one powerful and menacing counterattack, he would temporarily draw the mermaids back. In return, their frenzied attacks would be put to a halt and earn them some time.

“Great,” said Lloyd, “And this would be our best option if you can avoid getting hurt in the process.”

“I will try my best, Master Lloyd.”

A faint smile of satisfaction spread across Javier’s face. This was what he liked about fake Lloyd. Though there were times Lloyd acted like a nutcase, the man cared about his safety. However, realizing it wasn’t the time to think about this matter, Javier jumped into action.

“The rope. Please tie it to the mast.”

“Okay.”

Javier tightly tied the rope he received from Lloyd around his waist, and Lloyd tied its other end to the mast. Everything was set.

“Now, I will jump into the sea. The moment I lay a counterattack, please pull up the rope right away.”

“How will I know that you made a counterattack? Will you give me a separate signal?” asked Lloyd.

“No,” replied Javier, “You will know right away without one.”

“How?” Lloyd asked again.

Javier scoffed and then said, “Because I will overturn the sea.”

With that, Javier jumped up to the rail and threw a glance behind at Lloyd before diving in.

Splash! As soon as he plunged into the water, his body became wrapped in bitter cold, and Javier felt an icy sensation that seemed to be freezing his skin, muscles, and blood vessels. But Javier didn’t mind at all. He activated the Asrahan Core Technique to its full limit and reverberated all the manahearts that were woven into the cells of his body. He then ran a sweeping survey across the sea in a ferocious gaze, ready to roar, and noticed hundreds of mermaids rushing toward the ship. Javier was able to predict their incoming trajectory and movements before preparing to send a sweeping blow that would not hurt them but make them retreat.

I only have one chance.

Javier’s eyes shone much brighter than the cold blue sea. He gripped the hilt of his sword, harnessing his power. But soon, before he did anything, all the mermaids froze in place. Could it be that they sensed his dangerous energy with their almost wild instincts? The mermaids all ceased attacking the ship. Instead, with fright in their eyes and astonishment on their faces, they intensely stared at Javier. The latter lifted his brow at their abrupt change.

Did they figure out my plan?

It appeared so, indeed. But Javier didn’t care as he had already identified the location and line of trajectory for every single mermaid at sea. Now, he just had to sweep them hundreds of feet away by drawing his sword. But right when he was about to pull his sword out, one of the mermaids staring at Javier raised her hand. She then pointed at Javier’s face with a slight blush and a trembling voice like a girl who had fallen in love for the first time, she murmured something along with everyone else.

“You pass.”

Swoosh. At once, the sea grew quiet. There weren’t any mermaids aggressively swimming around them anymore. The surrounding glaciers stopped clashing against each other. The only sound was from the quiet waves on the surface of the water. Javier flinched as the sea grew quiet. He grabbed the hilt of the sword he was about to pull. His eyes shone coldly.

“…”

Pass? What were they saying all of a sudden? What kind of trickery was this? Did they predict his counterattack already? Or were they buying time to ambush him later?

I cannot lower my guard.

Javier thought so. But at that moment, one of the mermaids that stopped attacking the ship swam over to Javier. Her manner was extremely careful compared to a while ago. When she was about 15 feet away from him, she asked, “Beautiful human. What is your name?”

“…”

Javier didn’t answer. He couldn’t actually because he wasn’t a mermaid. He didn’t have the ability to talk underwater. Realizing this, the mermaid nodded.

“Oops. I forgot. Humans don’t know how to talk underwater like us. I would like to have a conversation with you. Do you mind if we go above water for a while and talk?”

Javier simply stared without saying anything.

“It is all right. You passed. We won’t attack you.” The mermaid grinned as she opened her palms to Javier.

That wasn’t all. She even gave a strict order to the others.

“Everybody back off.”

With one command, the entire Mermaid Corps withdrew and loosened their siege. This sight relieved Javier a little, so he swam up to the surface without a word.

“Whew…”

Javier breathed out in between the splashing waves. A moment later, the mermaid surfaced as well, finally allowing Javier to take a proper look at the face of the individual who came forward as the representative of the mermaids.

“Nice to meet you. I am the commander of the third underwater guard team of the Mermaid Kingdom.”

“I am Javier Asrahan, a knight serving Count Frontera.”

“Javier Asrahan?”

“Yes,” affirmed Javier.

“Oh! Even your name is pretty.”

“…”

Javier frowned, his forehead wrinkling. The mermaid right in front of him and the others below were all acting the same way. They were blushing as they looked at him. Javier could easily presume why. He was used to this.

“Did you mean that my face has been approved?” asked Javier impassively.

“Oh! How did you know?” exclaimed the mermaid.

“I am familiar with this situation,” Javier nonchalantly spoke as if it was obvious. “I have been at the center of attention everywhere I went throughout my life for my looks, which have been received positively by women. So I am not entirely unfamiliar with this situation. However…” Javier’s gaze slightly grew cold as he looked at the mermaid. “Despite my dashing face, you are the guard who protects this territory. From my understanding, we need your permission and approval to enter your kingdom.”

“Um, you are right,” seconded the mermaid nicely.

“I also heard that you must sing with great sincerity and care to gain approval. Am I correct?”

“Yes, that is roughly correct.”

“But I did not sing, you see,” Javier pointed out, fixing his gaze on her.

“It does not matter,” said the mermaid casually.

“It does not matter…? Wait-”

“Singing is not the only way to touch someone’s heart.”

Javier frowned, not understanding what she was saying.

Reading his face, she added, “Who cares about singing when your face already has inspired us to give you our approval?” Her eyes glimmered very brightly as if what she said was obvious.

For some reason, Javier was overwhelmed by her eagerness.

“Then, do I really have permission to enter your kingdom? Just based on my appearance?” asked Javier.

“Of course. Didn’t you see how all my sisters gave their approval?”

“I see… Then could you give me a second?” asked Javier.

“Why? For what?”

“I would like to share the news with my company onboard.”

Javier fleetingly glanced toward the upper deck of the ship, and the mermaid commander shrugged. It was a silent agreement from her.

“I appreciate your kindness. Well then.”

Javier then jumped out of the water and got to the deck of the ship. Conveniently, the captain, crew, and Lloyd were gathered in the area to watch over the situation.

“What the? What happened? Why did they stop attacking when you didn’t even fight back?” asked Lloyd, who came to Javier before anyone else.

Shaking his hair dripping with seawater, Javier replied, “I believe I have attained their approval.”

“You what? You received their permission?” Lloyd frowned.

“Yes,” affirmed Javier.

“How?”

“They said my face had their approval.”

“…”

“Actually,” continued Javier, “When you think about it, it is nothing very surprising. I should have jumped into the water instead of having you sing, Master Lloyd, in the first place. Then the ship would not have been attacked like this.”

“…”

“Anyhow, what a relief it is that I have acquired approval from them in this way at least.”

“Yeah,” Lloyd huffed sarcastically, “It’s such a great relief that you’re handsome. Isn’t it? Hahahaha.”

“Yes. It is a big relief that I was able to prevent a destructive situation brought about by your hopeless singing with my handsome looks.”

“Yeah. What a relief, really. Oh, I’m so happy. Ahaha…”

“Master Lloyd.” Javier stared at Lloyd with a serious look.

“That’s right. What.”

“Please stop crying.”

“Shut up,” blurted Lloyd in denial. “My eyes are just dry from the salty sea breeze.”

“Anyway, you should first talk with the mermaid representative below,” suggested Javier with a smile.

“I was about to,” huffed Lloyd as he wiped his eyes with his sleeve and stuck his body out of the ship railing. When he looked down, he saw the mermaid in question.

Wow. Her muscles are unreal.

Amid the splashing waves, there was a fellow, no young lady, whose body was swaying in the water. Her muscles were nothing to joke about. They were bulky and brawn, and Lloyd realized again something he had felt when he built the mermaid statue in Port Cremo. The mermaid’s muscles appeared to be literal weapons that came from intensely training for 24 hours every day in the water their entire lives.

It’d be a sight to behold if I brought her to a gym alongside Orc Arosh.

The mermaid’s physique seemed to be on par with the orcs, whose bodies Lloyd had examined up close. But now wasn’t the time to admire her muscles. He cleared his throat.

“Hmm! Ehem! How are you? My name is Lloyd Frontera,” announced Lloyd aloud.

The mermaid commander simply stared back at Lloyd. Her eyes were marked with terrible boredom.

“I just heard from my personal knight, Sir Asrahan. I was told, ‘Thanks to your generosity, Sir Asrahan has gained your approval and praise for his beauty.’”

“Yes. And…?” the mermaid retorted.

“First, I would like to express my gratitude for your generous and wise decision.”

Lloyd smiled like a salesman. Could it be that worked? The same smile hung on the mermaid’s face.

“Gratitude?” she puffed. “We should be the grateful ones. We were able to enjoy a view of such an unimaginable beauty like him. It looks like today is our lucky day.”

“My,” replied Lloyd gleefully, “Thank you very much for saying so. Today appears to be a lucky day for us as well.”

“Lucky? What do you mean it is a lucky day for you?”

“Why else? I came to meet someone with such a generous and wise insight,” groveled Lloyd.

“Generous and wise insight, you say? But I just described what is beautiful to be beautiful. Nothing else.”

“Ahaha. Is that so? I see you are honest as well.”

“Who cares. Anyway, what business do you have with us?”

The mermaid commander’s eyes now turned sharp as they looked up at Lloyd.

She continued to speak. “Speaking of, it is slightly strange. We were only touched by Javier Asrahan’s beautiful appearance, and we allowed his visit to the kingdom. So it is a mystery why you would come out and gain our affection. Do you have any business with us?”

“Oh, I definitely have.” Lloyd’s face lit up.

“What is it?”

“As I have said briefly, I receive protection from Javier Asrahan. We came here together.”

“So?”

“Since you allowed his visit to your kingdom, I ask that I come along-”

“No.”

“What…?”

“I said no.” Her rejection was clear-cut and sharp. With a sterner gaze, she asked, “What touched us was the beautiful face of Javier Asrahan. So why should we allow your visit to the kingdom when you have no connection with his appearance?”

“Oh… That’s…” Lloyd gulped dryly.

Come on…

Lloyd was secretly flustered. It never occurred to him that he’d be treated this coldly. At last, he understood what was going on.

She’s discriminating against me for my looks!

It looked like Javier was the only one who was permitted to enter. There didn’t seem to be a great reason for it either.

He’s good-looking. That’s the only reason. Fine. Sorry that I’m not good-looking. Sorry that I look like this! Hmph.

Lloyd was resentful and envious. He began to tear up from his anger directed at all the handsome men in the world. But he couldn’t just sit back. He elbowed Javier’s side and whispered, “Hey.”

“Yes,” Javier casually answered when he was called

“Back me up,” whispered Lloyd.

“How should I back you up?”

“Anything. Just make sure that I can visit their kingdom too.”

“Understood, Young Master.”

Javier nodded and stepped forward out of the railing. Lloyd waited with bated breath for what Javier might say. He prayed that the latter would offer some strong words and persuade the mermaids to give him permission to visit their kingdom.

Please!

While Lloyd prayed so, Javier began to speak.

“Commander? I have a favor to ask you.”

“Oh! You do?! What is it?”

Contrary to the conversation with Lloyd a moment ago, her voice was now sweet and gentle as she answered Javier. Even her eyes glimmered. Lloyd gave a snort when he saw the difference in her attitude. Meanwhile, Javier and the mermaid continued to converse.

“I would like for you to approve Master Lloyd’s visit to your kingdom,” gently said Javier.

“Of course. Since you are asking, I will grant it.”

With great ease and as if it was the obvious course of action, the mermaid commander flashed a big grin and nodded.
