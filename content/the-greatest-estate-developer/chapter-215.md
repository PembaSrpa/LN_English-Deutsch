# The Grand Completion Ceremony (1)

---

“Okay, ladies and gentlemen, please look over here. If you pull this lever, the main waterline opens up and drains the water.”

Cluck! As he explained, the floodgate official pulled the lever, and the pulley inside the floodgate turned with a metallic noise. The main waterline opened to lower the water level as the water drained out.

“Eh? It’s real! The water is going out!”

“Goodness gracious. What machine is this?!”

The small cruise ship waiting at the floodgate descended as the water level lowered, and the passengers. onboard looked around the inner floodgate with marvel in their eyes. The inside looked much like a giant square water tank. Once the water level lowered to reveal the sides, there appeared a host of relievo-styled sculptures. It told the story of a vigorous dragon in the distant past. An acting monarch was elected by the dragons, who in the later years was surrounded by power-hungry, greedy rebels. The whole capital was set ablaze by the war the rebels stirred. One heroic knight appeared at that moment to save the aged monarch and drive the rebels away. People cheered fervently, and he married the princess of the monarch, eventually declaring this land as the capital “Magenta” and starting a new era.

“That… Isn’t that the legend about our kingdom’s founding?”

“That knight appears to be Sir Mikael, the heroic founding king of our kingdom.”

“I am certain it is him from the large flower on the helmet.”

“That flower was symbolic of the promise he made with a dragon in his youth, right?”

“Yes. That is why this flower is still used as the symbol for the throne.”

A piece of the Kingdom’s origin story engraved in the floodgate left a strong impression on the passengers, and their faces swelled with pride at the sight of the unexpected spectacle. They took pride in being the citizens of a kingdom that was growing in prosperity. Their feelings of pride peaked when they were taken to the center of the garden.

“D-dragon…”

“Is that a real dragon skeleton?”

“It looks like it is. Then is that… a sculpture?”

“A sculpture? Where?”

“Right in front of the bowing dragon.”

“Oh… It’s really there!”

“I think that’s Her Majesty.”

“It looks like the dragon is kneeling before her.”

“Spectacular…”

There was a dragon bowing down its head, and Queen Magentano proudly stood right in front of it. The masterpiece struck a balance between stateliness and harmony. The sheer size and power overwhelmed the beholders, and since the waterway was designed to make passengers look up at the sculpture and dragon, the overwhelming sensation left a more dramatic effect. They even felt like they’d become a part of the scene, watching history be made. Their unity in the dramatic moment boosted the passengers’ pride in the kingdom. Still feeling overwhelmed, they got off the dock, which was connected to the venue for the ceremony. The noble passengers attended the ceremony at the height of their pride for the kingdom. Of course, Lloyd, who was also among the bustling nobles, felt the same. His national pride was at its peak too.

How can buffet food be this delicious? Hurray for the House of Magentano! Big hurray for the queen!

The banquet hall sat at The Magentano Sanctuary, located at the center of the garden. It couldn’t be any more luxurious. It was even so for Lloyd, whose banquet standards had become considerably high. This one appeared to have reached the peak of luxury.

This is a lot flashier than the time I broke the sultan’s bank in Ahinsya.

Truly, all sorts of cuisines were served. Moreover, everything was scrumptious and literally melted on his tongue as it offered his taste buds the wonder of nature, much like the premium-grade beef he used to buy with his meager salary. And the best part? It was all free!

“So Master Lloyd,” Javier asked expressionlessly, “Do you like these kinds of food?”

“Yeah, of course,” answered Lloyd as he ate a forkful of mouthwatering seafood pasta he didn’t know the name of. And he asked back, “Of course I do. It’s free. What’s not to like?”

“…”

“What are you doing?” Lloyd went on. “Eat up. If you still don’t get it, then just put it in your head. In an event like this, food is what you remember.”

“I do not-”

“Feel like eating it?” put in Lloyd.

“No.”

“But why?” There was genuine curiosity in Lloyd’s voice.

“To be honest, I am skeptical about whether it’s really necessary to waste so much money on this extravagant food,” Javier spoke as he looked at the food stacked high on Lloyd’s plate. “It is excessively rare. Unnecessarily high-end. Inefficient and expensive. The money that was spent on your plate of food alone could buy a large amount of rice and bread.”

Lloyd noticed the faint glint of complaint in Javier’s eyes. He smiled.

“Aha,” said Lloyd, “I suppose you mean that giving up a small portion of this extravagant meal could help feed more hungry people in the capital, right?”

“Yes,”

“Well,” Lloyd said with a nod, “you’re not wrong, but what can we do?”

“What do you mean what can we do?” blurted Javier.

“It’s not like the food you refuse to eat will be distributed to the people.”

“…”

“And we didn’t spend our money on this,” Lloyd reminded. “This banquet was prepared and hosted by the palace. That is why they prepared food befitting the status of this event.”

“But such a luxurious event-”

“Is absolutely necessary,” broke in Lloyd.

Javier paused. Lloyd gave a quick scan around the banquet hall.

“Will you look over there?” Lloyd pointed to one area. “They’re the nobles from the palace, right?”

“Yes, it appears so.”

Pointing to another group this time, Lloyd asked, “Then what about them?”

“They look like foreigners.”

“You’re right. Probably ambassadors from nearby nations. Can’t you see?” Lloyd paused before resuming. “Events like these are a necessity, especially right after the completion of a grand and majestic construction funded by the kingdom.”

“Why is that?” Javier asked innocently.

“This place itself is a large, carefully crafted show window.”

Lloyd surveyed the garden thoroughly constructed with sheer magnificence. Javier frowned a little.

“By show window…”

“Yes, all of this is a show.”

“You cannot possibly mean that this is all a show to flaunt our power to foreign kingdoms.”

“Of course it is.” Lloyd grinned. “Do you think anybody can build a garden like this? The mere fact that construction of this size was finished without any difficulty proves our kingdom’s wealth. It’s proof of our overflowing capital, showing the entire world that this kingdom is capable of mobilizing and supporting an equally strong military.”

It was true. In other words, this project was not sham or overly excessive despite what it looked like. It was an indirect and clear yardstick to demonstrate the power of the royal family.

It was historically no different on Earth. The Versaille was similar to the Termina Garden. The Peterhof Palace and Sanssouci Palace commissioned by Peter the Great and Friedrich II, respectively, were also close examples. So, in a way, this was a sort of showcase, a remarkable event that displayed the power and wealth of the royal family both domestically and internationally.

“So, enjoy yourself,” Lloyd suggested. “This event and amount of extravagance is a necessity.”

“Um, but…”

“Oh, wow. Is that the special coconut from Ahinsya?”

Flinch, Javier, who was about to grumble, stopped. The special coconut from Ahinsya. The sweet and sour taste automatically came back to him.

“I got you.” Lloyd smugly smiled.

“…”

“Look at that. I knew you’d chow down once you found something you like,” pointed Lloyd.

“You are wrong,” denied Javier with a defiant look. “I was just tensing my body to hold back a sneeze.” “Really?”

“Yes.”

“Really?” Lloyd waggled his eyebrows.

“Yes.”

“Then can you swear that you will never lay your hands on the coconut even when it’s right in front of you?” “Of course, I won’t-”

“My, looks like there’s a real one here.”

Lloyd immediately snatched a plate from a passing servant. The plate, in fact, did have a special coconut placed right on top of it. The smile on Lloyd’s face turned more evil.

“Then I will enjoy my drink.”

“…”

Javier’s face froze in a flash. That gave Lloyd another smile but not because he was able to make fun of Javier. 

Those reactions just now definitely suit Javier.

A knight who was always sided with the weak. A hero who pursued justice over immediate power. Lloyd suddenly recalled Javier’s appearances throughout the original novel.

Yes. Right. I forgot because he’s with me all the time, but he was always like this.

Javier preferred a conversation in front of a bonfire to a luxurious banquet. He preferred to use the money for an extravagant meal to buy dozens of bread loaves for people in need. He didn’t hesitate to act and didn’t come to regret his decisions. This aspect of his personality remained unchanged even when he became the grandmaster in the novel.

If he really wanted, he could have attained all the power and wealth he wanted. If he really made an effort, he could easily have had his own kingdom.

But Javier didn’t.

He’s really quite the man.

Lloyd picked up the coconut with a grin and held it out to Javier.

“No need to stiffen your face like that. Eat it.”

“…”

“I won’t judge.” Lloyd held it out some more. “Eat it, I tell you.”

“…”

“Hey, eating comes first before swearing,” Lloyd insisted.

“I never swore.” At last, a wry smile appeared on Javier, and he was about to receive the coconut when… 

Clack! Crush!

“…!”

An abrupt and blaring noise bellowed, and Lloyd wheeled around in surprise.

“Woah.”

The dragon skeleton that decorated the central garden and was assembled like a dinosaur fossil displayed in museums was now missing a skull, which had fallen to the ground.

What is happening?

Lloyd was flustered. That shouldn’t have been able to fall off so easily. He had overseen the installation himself. An intricate wiring of metal chains and support fixtures tightly held the pieces together.

I suppose it’s good that no one got crushed under it at least?

But for an accident like that to occur during a showcase event. Lloyd clicked his tongue to himself and watched the scene. There was a stir among the people in the hall. Queen Magentano was now up from her seat and giving orders to the people around her. Two people were being summoned to her, and it was the sculptor in charge of installing the sculpture and dragon skeleton and Viscount Nort who oversaw the garden construction.

Whew. My dear viscount is going to have to write a thick report on this one. Lloyd felt pity while watching the back of Count Nort as he restlessly squirmed in front of the queen’s presence. How could he not?

That man was a bit strict but nice to me.

The viscount was willing to do the majority of the design and construction work. Thanks to that, Lloyd could just focus on building the waterway and floodgate. It really made his job easier to do during the entire construction.

Plus, Viscount Nort even cried in overwhelming happiness when the construction ended.

Lloyd recalled how Viscount Nort called it the greatest masterpiece of his life. He had sobbed and wept that he could die happy now as his name would be remembered in the kingdom’s history of construction. An accident like this during the completion ceremony was completely unexpected.

Tsk, tsk, tsk. This is why you should never be too willing to bear responsibility.

A part of Lloyd felt sorry for the man, but another part of him was relieved. It really gladdened him that he didn’t decide to supervise the whole construction.

“Master Lloyd.” Javier tapped Lloyd’s shoulder twice as he called.

“Eh?”

“I have something to tell you.” Javier’s voice was grim.

“What is it?” demanded Lloyd. “Do you want some more coconuts?”

“It is not that,” refused Javier. “It’s about that dragon skeleton over there.”

“The skeleton? What about it?”

“Something feels a bit off.”

“…”

Javier’s expression and gaze were stern, grim, and serious. The situation felt oddly nostalgic. Lloyd briefly racked his brain and was able to quickly recall why.

Oh, right. It was at Cremo. There was a completion ceremony there too.

That completion ceremony celebrated the completion of the mermaid statue. And the Gigatitan swept over the banquet hall. Javier was the first to sense danger before anyone else. As Lloyd continued thinking, he was gripped with an uneasy feeling.

“What is it now?” blurted Lloyd. “You’re not going to say something’s wrong with the dragon skeleton, are you?” “Yes, I was about to tell you that.” Javier was dead serious. Lloyd’s heart sank.

Oh, come on! This was why completion ceremonies were the worst. Come to think of it, Lloyd said to himself, it was at the completion ceremony of the suspension bridge when the queen was almost assassinated as well. This was too unlucky for it to be a mere coincidence. Could it be that completion ceremonies were the root of evil? Javier continued to speak as this outrageous thought went on in Lloyd’s head.

“I have been feeling this since a while ago, but the mana in the skeleton is faintly moving.”

“What? The mana is moving?” asked Lloyd in an incredulous tone.

“Yes. Don’t dragon bones naturally hold a small amount of mana?”

“They do, but the mana shouldn’t be flowing. It’s dead.”

“Yes. That was definitely true until just earlier. But right after the skull fell off…”

“It started to flow?” asked Lloyd in an incredulous voice.

“Check it for yourself, Master Lloyd.”

“…”

It dawned on Lloyd that the Asrahan Core Technique was a skill that specialized in mana detection, absorption, and employment. Moreover, Javier was the inventor and leading expert of the technique. So, it was impossible for him to be wrong.

As the uneasy feeling in his stomach spread out in him, Lloyd rotated his triple circle. Ziiing!

As the Asrahan Core Technique began to work, he could feel the flow of mana around him. He picked up the powerful mana belonging to the queen from the other side and the mana stirring among the people at the unexpected event. There was also a natural one present in the drifting wind. And….

“Dammit.”

The moment Lloyd picked up a mana flow in the dragon skeleton, shivers ran down his spine. He was sure of it. Javier was right. There really was mana flowing inside the dragon skeleton. What was more, the flow was getting stronger even while he could feel it. The mana was aggressive and explosive.

Gulp. So, something was behind this. A terrible event that no one expected was about to implode in this location. The moment Lloyd realized it, the moment his bad feeling solidified into conviction…

Dammit! I hate these stupid completion ceremonies!

Lloyd immediately got to his feet without a second of hesitation. It was beyond him what was going to happen. But he had to evacuate people first. He was convinced that he’d regret it otherwise. So, he threw his hand into his inner pocket and brought out Bibeong to send everyone far, far away from the danger they weren’t aware of yet.

“Bibeong!” called Lloyd.

“Bibeong?”

“It’s been stifling to forcefully stay small since the construction ended, right?”

“Bibebeong? Bibeong?”

“No, you must have felt stifled, I’m sure. So, eat this.”

“Bibeong?”

Bibeong had been eating a blue sunflower seed every day while he was in the capital. Lloyd held out a red sunflower seed to him.

“Do you trust me?” Lloyd asked.

“Bibeong…?”

“Better yet, do you trust Bangul?”

“Bibeong!”

The mere mention of her name made him nod hard.

“Great,” Lloyd complimented. “Then eat this, and do as I tell you.”

Lloyd fed Bibeong a sunflower seed and hurled him far away where there weren’t any people.

Poof! “Bibeong!”

A 328-foot beaver appeared in a flash, and he let out a roar in one corner of the hall and started to stomp and trample the ground. The effect was immense.

“Arghhh!!”

A piercing shrill of a noble lady became the whistle that caused all the panic-stricken guests to swiftly evacuate, prompting a mass exodus.
