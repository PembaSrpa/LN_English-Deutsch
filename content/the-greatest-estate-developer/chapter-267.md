# Distinguish Yourself (1)

---

Moments of realization often came without any warning. There were many of these moments throughout history. For instance, when Archimedes shouted, “Eureka!” after discovering the principle of buoyancy, when Galileo Galilei discovered the moons orbiting around Jupiter, or when someone found a 50-dollar bill in their coat after a year. Big and small discoveries emerged at the most unexpected times. And today. Lloyd, too, experienced this same phenomenon.

Oh…?

Lloyd opened his eyes wide. It had been two months since he supervised the construction site. Every day, hundreds of merpeople were mobilized to sweep and search the seabed of the Arctic Ocean. But nothing was found even with Lloyd’s surveying and subterranean scanning skill as they diligently removed countless layers of mud. They couldn’t find a single artifact, let alone the location of the jewel. As he was starting to grow tired, something began to emerge.

It can’t be.

He wasn’t very far from the Mermaid Kingdom, and this location looked nothing special on the outside. It was just a vast plain under the sea. But as soon as the merpeople cleared the mud, his surveying skill caught something beneath the ground.

There’s a stratum, but this looks too sleek. It’s almost like the structure was man-made.

When that realization hit him, Lloyd yelled, “Wait! Everybody stop working!”

The busy shoveling came to a halt. Lloyd removed everyone at the site, and he furrowed his brows, putting his eyes into focus. He carefully walked toward the area where the artificial stratum was. Once he got close, he was able to get a more detailed view.

I’m sure of it. That looks like a stratum, but it’s not that. This is some sort of foundation constructed by man.

About 10 feet below the cleared seabed, there appeared a strange-looking foundation. It had a perfectly square shape as if someone had poured cement on the ground. In particular, the upper area was excessively sleek to the point that it seemed unnatural. So, that could only be…

I think it’s the foundation of a building. But doesn’t seem to be made of rebar concrete. Hmm, I wonder if this is the result of stacking flat and smooth rocks together.

It appeared so. His heart started to race.

He said that the Jewel of Truth was a building.

Perhaps that really could be the foundation of the Jewel of Truth. It seemed that the two months they spent on excavation weren’t meaningless after all.

If we’ve found the location of the jewel’s site, then the most important part is what we do from here on out.

Lloyd’s eyes became focused. And he became more mindful of his movements with the shovel.

Psh! Crack! He dug the seafloor. Heavily at first. But around the time the foundation was about to reveal itself, he moved with even more caution like he was scooping up a grain of rice. And for the final sweep, he used his hands to dust the top portion of the foundation. Sometime later, he finally succeeded in exposing most of the top portion without any damage.

“This is the real one…” murmured Lloyd as he rose.

Lloyd instantly knew now that he could see the foundation up close. This wasn’t a natural formation. It was a foundation built by someone with a clear purpose in mind.

This could be the foundation for the Jewel of Truth.

Lloyd’s eyes became more focused. Now that he confirmed it was a man-made structure, it was time to check whether it was truly a part of the Jewel of Truth.

“Um, Mister Rotorua?” called Lloyd aloud.

“Yeah? Yeah?!” Rotorua, who was nervously observing Lloyd from afar, immediately swam to Lloyd’s side when he was called.

“What do you think this is?” asked Lloyd as he pointed at the ground.

Rotorua observed before he finally said, “I have no idea, but…”

“But what?” hurried Lloyd.

“If this is the one we are looking for, there should be a symbol somewhere on this foundation. The records tell us so.”

“What symbol?”

“One that looks like a starfish with sharp edges.”

“I see.” Lloyd and Rotorua started dusting every surface in search of the symbol. Several minutes later…

I found it!

Indeed, there it was. At the corner of the foundation was a starfish symbol as big as the palm of his hand. Yet to be satisfied with the finding, Lloyd’s gaze continued to move across the surface of the structure.

Of course. There’s more I need to do than just discover the location of the jewel.

Lloyd wasn’t an archeologist. Discovering the Jewel of the Truth site and discussing its academic values and whatnot were entirely irrelevant to Lloyd. What he needed was the Jewel of Truth that could be used immediately, not just its foundation structure.

I have to find the blueprint.

Lloyd turned and said, “Um, Mister Rotorua?”

But when Lloyd turned around, to his surprise, he found Rotorua wailing and crying his heart out.

“Stop crying and look at me,” demanded Lloyd.

Rotorua nodded and tried to control himself, only to give way to overwhelming emotions again. “I… It’s just… I feel overwhelmed…” He sniffled heavily in between his words.

Could it be due to the joy of finding the site of the jewel at last? Rotorua was weeping his heart out as he clapped his face with his hands, like someone who won their first lottery ticket amidst drowning in crippling rent and loans. Lloyd eventually let Rotorua have his moment until he calmed down.

“Ehem. Hmm! I am sorry for the sudden display of emotions,” Rotorua finally said in an embarrassed voice. He wiped his eyes.

“Don’t be,” comforted Lloyd, “I fully understand you. But the thing is, didn’t you say that the blueprint would be here at the site?”

“Oh, I did.”

“Then where is it?”

The blueprint was the most important thing here. Finding the site location was only half of the job. The Jewel of Truth could only be restored to its original shape with a blueprint. Only then could Lloyd ask the jewel about how to stop the restoration of destiny.

Without it, everything would have been a waste.

But there was nothing in sight that remotely looked like a blueprint. Nervousness started to creep up on Lloyd. Rotorua, on the other hand, was unperturbed.

“Oh,” Rotorua said in a casual voice, “The blueprint is here. The record says that it is right here.” He then pointed at the starfish symbol. “The record about the symbol was discovered by one of my ancestors in the sixteenth generation of my family. According to it, the blueprint is said to be hidden in the starfish symbol.”

“In this symbol?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes. All it needs is just some pressure… Let’s see.” Rotorua dropped to a crouch and started to run his hands over the symbol before he pressed the center of it.

Clack.

And then…

Ruuumble! Like a morning alarm, the foundation structure started to vibrate with great force, which caused a resonation in the seawater around them. The tremors gradually traveled farther and farther across the ground.

“Oh, wow.”

Lloyd clenched his fists at the magnificent resonation shaking the entire sea. This grand effect could only mean that he succeeded in finding what he was looking for. An ember of hope was ignited in his heart. But at the same hour, miles away from the excavation site, a mysterious resonation began to occur in the stomach of the Kraken, the gigantic octopus that protected the eggs that contained the whole kingdom.

***

Vooooooo!

“K-Krurung?”

The Kraken was utterly shocked. How could it not? It was in the middle of a peaceful nap, going about its day like usual, when a violent vibration assaulted its stomach.

“Krung? Krurung?”

It was flustered. Its stomach knotted up, but it didn’t know why. It didn’t know that an object was lodged in between the villus of its fifth stomach. The object resonated in line with the vibration of the jewel’s foundation from miles away. The Kraken was utterly clueless about what was happening and seriously dismayed.

“Krururng! Krung! Krung!”

It coughed aloud and even violently shook its body. Still, the forceful and sudden tremors didn’t lessen. No. It only grew more tumultuous. It was a stifling feeling as if its stomach and intestines were tangled in a knot.

“Krurung!”

The Kraken’s screams grew louder. The strong yearning to move violently and writhe up and down rushed over the octopus.

That seemed like a good way to alleviate the excruciating and splitting pain inside it. The Kraken deliberated until it reached a conclusion. It must not move mindlessly. Moving the mountainous body that wrapped the eggs of the Mermaid Kingdom would only inflict severe damage to the place. As such, the Kraken frantically struggled to endure the pain.

“K-Krurung!”

It roared. It flexed its stomach to suppress the churning. But later, it reached the point where it couldn’t endure the sensation any longer.

Vooooooo! The resonation in its fifth stomach became fiercer. The vibration was so fierce and terrifying that its desperate will couldn’t contain the octopus any longer.

“Krurung!”

At last, its body, which was over several miles long, convulsed reflexively to the pain. Soon, the eggs that contained the city of the merpeople scattered in all directions after being separated from the octopus’s tentacles that were wrapped around them.

***

Boooom!

“Eh…?” Queen Kiakora stopped dead in her tracks. She was deliberating on the matter of the Hell gate opening up in the sky and ways to solve the problem when the egg that contained the palace was hit with a great force. Thump! Boooom!

“What is happening?”

A look of dismay came over her face. The whole palace tilted to one side. No, she realized that it was not the palace but the whole egg that contained the palace that was thrown off balance. Just then…

“My queen! The outside of the egg! Something terrible has happened!” One of the servants hurriedly swam to her side, and in an urgent voice, she added, “The Kraken! It has started to go berserk!”

“What?!” shrieked the queen. “The Kraken?”

“Yes, Your Majesty! We do not know the cause. It was said that it screamed several times and started to writhe its entire body and go berserk!”

“How could this be happening…?” The queen furrowed her brows. The report was hard to believe. The Kraken was an old friend of her people. It had been with the merpeople for several generations, long before she hatched out of her egg.

The Kraken was hatched on the same day as the most respected queen Houhora and they grew up together. It even fought with us shoulder to shoulder in the expeditionary battle in the desert during our punitive expedition against the dragon, Kaisertus. It has been protecting us ever since then for generations. And now it is going on a rampage?

She couldn’t wrap her head around this. Was it betrayal? A sudden awakening of its wild instincts? That made even less sense.

There must be a reason.

The Kraken would never run amok without a reason. It wasn’t the kind to harm the race this thoughtlessly. Having drawn such a conclusion, the queen ordered in a calm voice, “I see. But since it appears it is not safe here, everyone must evacuate. Now.”

“I will get to it right away, Your Majesty!”

At her command, the servants around her sides and the guards darted out of the palace. They swam faster than they ever had in their entire lives. Once they swam out of the egg, they were able to get a full view of the situation.

“The eggs of the kingdom are…”

They were all scattering away. Like pollack eggs thrown into a fast current of water, hundreds and thousands of eggs that had been held tightly together were being scattered away, each drifting, falling, rolling, and crashing. There was only one cause for this.

“Krurung!”

The Kraken was roaring and going berserk. It was violently wielding its eight legs that used to grip the eggs into one tight case and twisting and turning its body that extended over a few miles long. Its terrible writhing caused the eggs to disperse across the sea and be sucked into a violent current of water.

Whoosh! Just then, one of the eggs was caught in the current hurtled in the queen’s direction. With a diameter of over 650 feet, it was a pretty big one among the city eggs.

“My queen!”

“Duck!” The servants on both of her sides shrilled in panic, but the queen didn’t listen to them. Although she was slightly dazed by the overwhelming and unexpected violence of the Kraken, she soon recovered from her shock.

“Hmph!” She gave a rough snort, and a glint of resolution appeared in her eyes as she glared at the egg flying in her way. Anaconda-thick veins bulged in her forearm, biceps, triceps, and delts, and soon her chest, stomach, and back popped as though an armor was put on her. And she immediately hurtled toward the egg.

“As if I could turn a blind eye and swim away when the land of my people is flying at me!” She opened her arms wide and took the brunt of the egg with her pecs.

Boooom! Having received the full force of the 656-foot egg loaded with buildings, her body was shoved about 320 yards backward. Even so, she kept moving her fins at an explosive speed and with incredible power.

“Argh… Ah… Aaargh!!”

Rumble…! The veins all over her body now thickened like a python, but still, the egg was relentless, doggedly shoving the queen back with its ferocious speed and weight.

“Arggh!”

The queen’s face reddened, and right at the moment when the thought of being crushed to death crossed her mind…

“My queen!”

Suddenly, she heard someone shout beside her. She reflexively looked at her side as her body trembled from head to toe. And then she saw who it was.

“I may not be much, but I will help you!!! Argh!!!”

It was the man from the land. Lloyd Frontera. He was at her side, stopping the egg with both hands. He looked desperate to provide aid to her, and his whole body screamed, “I am going to help you!”

“Human?” she panted, “Why? But you are a foreigner.”

“If you’re asking me why I’m helping out! My answer is that I have no reason!”

“No reason?”

“Yes, Your Majesty! "What reason do you need to help someone in crisis?!” Lloyd yelled louder. His response tugged at her heartstrings. Of course, she was left completely in the dark about one thing. She had no idea that Lloyd was pretending to help by barely touching the egg. She was unaware that Lloyd was faking it. She was clueless that, even amid this unexpected turn of events, Lloyd had a scheme in mind.
