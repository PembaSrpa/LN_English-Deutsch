# The Unexpected Encounter (1)

---

Occasionally, there were moments when you were aware that you were in a dream. For example, moments like now when Lloyd was confronted with this unworldly yet vivid entity.

“Are you Lloyd Frontera?”

“…”

Lloyd blinked several times and lifted his head. Is this what it would feel like to talk with a burning Mount Everest? A fiery giant was standing in front of Lloyd. Its whole body was set ablaze with large flames, and from the despairing height near the clouds, it was glowering down at Lloyd with fire-filled eyes.

What kind of dream is this?

But since it’s a dream, I at least won’t die in the hands of that fiery giant, Lloyd told himself. As a sense of relief washed over him, Lloyd relaxed his shoulders.

“Yes, I am Lloyd,” he uttered coolly.

“Liar,” accused the fiery giant.

“…”

“Suho Kim. You thought I wouldn’t be able to see through you?”

“Oh, that's…”

Lloyd was left speechless for a second. He wondered if the fiery giant was his conscience. Could that be the reason it was trying to take advantage of his guilt and push away the real Lloyd? But thankfully, this did not seem to be the case.

“I am the Master of Hellfire who sees through the core of all souls.” Hellkaros, the Watcher of the 19 Purgatories and the King of Hell, continued its introduction, its voice booming like thunder and rumbling like an earthquake.

“I am also the Master of Giolexius, the knight of hell you defeated.”

“Oh,” yelped Lloyd, “do you mean Hell’s Knight?”

“Indeed.”

Rumble…! Hellkaros, the King of Hell, stooped down like an inclined mountain peak. Lloyd lifted his head up, resisting his desire to flinch his shoulders in fright.

Looking straight at Hellkaros, Lloyd asked, “Is that why you came over to my dream?”

“Yes, you are right,” agreed Hellkaros.

“You’re not going to ask for compensation, are you? We were mutually at fault, and it was actually self-defense.”

“Self-defense…?” mused the King of Hell. “Mutually at fault?”

“Yes.”

Lloyd nodded, and he started to explain in detail.

“Your knight and I were bent on ripping each other apart. So, the winner can’t be blamed for hurting the other. Do you not think so?” argued Lloyd proudly.

“Do you think I am here to quibble about who’s to blame?” scoffed the King of Hell.

“Then why did you come to see me?”

At Lloyd’s question, Hellkaros let out a snort of laughter, hot globs of fire spurting out of its mouth.

“To observe the qualities of a man suitable for Hell.”

“What?”

What nonsense was this now? Lloyd cocked his head, and the King of Hell continued to speak.

“From the moment you fought against Giolexius, I have been watching you. I noticed a quality of yours that sets you apart from the others,” announced Hellkaros.

“What quality is that?”

“You are petty,” the King of Hell began without a second of hesitation. “To an unimaginable level. You are as rancorous, cheap, and spiteful as a mosquito that sucks blood in the middle of the night only to hide from angry humans under their bed.”

“…”

Come on, no need to hurt me with your words! A spike of depression rose in Lloyd. Hell’s King, however, continued to diss him with its fake compliments.

“But more than that, you are careful like a rat who always plans out her escape. Moreover, you are so sly I want to set you as an example to other devils in Hell. There is also the singing. Your singing that drains anyone’s strength the moment they hear you and makes them wish their ears will stop working. That great singing that made even me, the King of Hell, regret living to hear it.”

"What’s wrong with my singing…?” Lloyd’s voice faltered.

“I wanted to play it on repeat 24 hours every day for thousands and thousands of years.”

“‘Why?”

“That would bring more painful agony to the souls that have fallen to Hell.”

“Wait a minute,” said Lloyd, shaking his head. “You don’t plan to broadcast me in Hell, right?”

“Why wouldn’t I?” The King of Hell shrugged.

“…”

“I am jealous of your talent and voice for singing. Moreover, on top of your singing, you are capable of mocking and persecuting the devils that oppose me. So, how does it sound? Lloyd, no, Suho Kim? How about you trust me and spread out your wings in Hell?”

Wings in Hell…! In Hell…! Hell…!

The thunderous words from Hell’s King rattled the whole space in Lloyd’s dream. It wasn’t a simple shout. Its voice reverberated deep within him, rattling the very core of his soul. Lloyd struggled not to get crushed by the violent sound waves and finally opened his eyes.

“…”

Chirp! Chirp! The early summer morning was filled with singing birds. The white curtain in one corner of Lloyd’s bedroom swayed. A ray of sunlight streamed out and scratched Lloyd on the cheek. He felt the warmth of the sun and thought to himself…

That was one weird dream.

To heck with the King of Hell. And it what? Wanted to broadcast his singing in Hell? That would make the evil souls further writhe in agony.

How frugal it is for a ruler of Hell to want to use me.

A scoff escaped Lloyd as he thought about the dream he had just had. The fact that it felt so real made him feel worse.

There’s no need for a silly dream to be so vivid.

Lloyd shuddered as he rose from his bed. The truth was, he couldn’t push aside the dream entirely. He still remembered the message that popped in front of him right after he took down Hell’s Knight. The following messages had undoubtedly appeared in front of his eyes then.

[The King of Hell is impressed by your tremendous accomplishment. The King of Hell has lost his corps commander but now has his eyes on your skill, tact, and potential.]

“…”

Tsk. Stop thinking. It’s a silly dream and nothing else. Even if the King of Hell is interested in me, there’s no way it’d chase after me for my singing. Lloyd assured himself that such a thing would never happen. Something like that was impossible in real life.

Lloyd loudly forced out a cough. “Hmm! Hmm! Ehem!”

Soon, someone reacted from the other side of the door.

“Master Lloyd, are you awake?”

“Yeah, come on in.”

Emily, beaming with a glow of satisfaction, entered with a tray of breakfast. She was the first to greet Lloyd on most mornings.

“You don’t have to ask, Young Master. I didn’t wait for long today as well,” Emily said preemptively.

“Ah, would you get mad if I told you I wasn’t planning to ask that?” asked Lloyd, his voice dripping with playfulness.

“That depends on how you act, Master Lloyd,” said Emily, amused.

“Understood,” said Lloyd. “I was going to aggressively question you.”

“Hehe. The soup is good today. Please try it.”

“Yes, thank you.”

Lloyd lifted the spoon and thought, to Hell with Hell! I’m going to enjoy this peace for the rest of my life. Resolving such, Lloyd enjoyed every bite of his soup.

Has it been five days now?

Sheherazade. The daughter of the sultan who traveled across the desert to acquire his lullaby. It had been five days since Lloyd demanded she enlist in the Engineering Corps in exchange to record him.

She should have reached a decision now.

Sheherazade seemed to be lost in thought for the past five days. Watching over her, Lloyd figured that she would make a decision soon and also that her choice wouldn’t hurt him regardless of what it was.

It’s obvious. If she chooses to enlist in the Corps, then I’ll have someone who possesses the talent of a sword master on my side. If she rejects my offer and goes back to the sultan’s kingdom, her progression to become a sword master will be significantly delayed.

Lloyd wouldn’t lose anything either way.

What will she decide then?

The soup had cooled to a perfect temperature. Finishing the remaining soup in one big gulp, Lloyd felt a rush of curiosity and anticipation at the same time.

“Whew…”

How would she describe her feelings at the moment? Half dumbfounded and half dismayed. That was how Sheherazade defined her emotions. She heaved a big sigh and looked up.

Chirp! Chirp! There were birds flying above the trail in the woods. In between them, she also noticed the clear sky and white clouds of early summer. What a truly beautiful place this is, she marveled to herself.

But staying in this foreign land for five years?

A sense of loss gripped her. Must I go that far to obtain one recording? Must I invest five years of my life in it? And how would my father react if I made such a decision? He would go berserk.

How could he not, considering who she was? The sultan’s daughter. The princess of a powerful kingdom. Though, of course, she was the daughter of a concubine and not the queen, and though she was only one of the sultan’s dozens of daughters, none of this lessened her princess status in any way. That was why she was frustrated.

How would His Majesty react if I spent five years working in a foreign kingdom’s Engineering Corps after leaving Ahinsya without his permission… Whew, I can’t even imagine it.

There would certainly be an outburst of chaos. But she couldn’t even ascertain how bad it would be, and that was a bigger concern for her.

But I must become a sword master.

This was the most definite way to get acknowledged by her father. Once she became one, everything would change.

It seems worth a try if I consider my time here as an investment toward that goal.

She needed the lullaby. Without it, her insomnia would make her life hellish again and it’d become that much harder to focus on sword training. Her growth would be slowed, putting her far away from the path to becoming a sword master.

Bear my father’s wrath and give up five years of my life, or safely go back to Ahinsya and tolerate insomnia? I suppose it boils down to these two choices.

Sheherazade let out another big sigh. Just then, she stopped dead in her tracks. She heard a sigh similar to hers just over a nearby bush.

“Haah…”

“…”

Whom could it be? She wondered to herself. This part of the path was quite remote from the Frontera fiefdom. She heard that residents and hunters didn’t travel this far. That was why she decided to take a stroll here. It looked like a good place to have time for herself.

“…”

Sheherazade walked over in that direction and soon found the person who had sighed a moment ago.

A young boy? No, he doesn’t look like a child.

Her gaze traveled over the bush, and there was a baby-faced man, whose height was only about 5'6. He had curly orange hair and round freckled cheeks. His eyes looked exceptionally smart but soft like a rabbit’s. His short height and small figure made him look even more innocent.

Who is that?

Sheherazade narrowed her eyes. He looked suspicious. There wasn’t anyone else except her this deep in the woods, and yet, here was a boy who looked like he could barely throw a punch. It didn’t make sense. Furthermore, this boy was surrounded by a luxurious carriage and what seemed like his companion’s luggage.

Hmm, what could have happened?

The stranger didn’t appear dangerous at first glance. The boy’s posture and gestures didn’t show any signs of swordsmanship. Just then…

“Master Julian!”

A man came sprinting from the other end, panting and out of breath. Collecting his raspy breath, the man stopped in front of the boy.

“Whew, ha… Have you been waiting for long?” The man breathed heavily in and out.

“No. I’m all right. What happened to the boulder?”

“About that, Young Master, I believe it will be difficult to get rid of it by today.”

The man shook his head as he wiped the sweat on his forehead. The boy, who was called “Julian,” looked dismayed.

“Really? That is a problem.”

“Please forgive us. We are trying our best to clear it out, but…”

“What if I join you?” volunteered Julian with a smile.

“Eh?! Absolutely not! Please do not say that. You do not have to do that for us. How could we dare ask for your help with this kind of work, Master Julian?”

“Um, I’m alright with it,” Julian said in a casual tone. “I would just have to roll my sleeves up, clear the soil, and push the boulder away.”

“T-that is exactly why this job is for us.”

“Is that so…?”

“Yes.”

The man nodded his head resolutely. Julian’s shoulders dropped like a dog soaked in the rain.

“I still feel sorry,” Julian went on with a contrite look on his face. “If I didn’t graduate second in class at the academy, I wouldn’t have received this carriage from the palace. We could have simply walked from here if it weren’t for this carriage. That would have been less work for you all.”

“Haah, not at all. Please do not speak that way.” The man swayed his hands. “You graduated with honors, and we are honored and proud to serve someone like you. Moreover, this carriage is a precious one bestowed by the palace. It is also a token of honor given to those who graduate in second place.”

“But still, it’s just a carriage-”

“Please do not say that. We are really all right. It is tradition for graduates to return home riding a carriage bestowed by the palace if they were given one.”

“But no one expected a sudden downpour to destroy the road,” Julian argued feebly.

“But it wasn’t you, Master Julian, who ruined the path. No need to be so critical of yourself. Sure, the ruined mountain path is muddy, and the boulder that blocked the road is quite sizable, but it is all right. We can clean it up in one day with a bit of effort.”

Clapping in realization, Julian said, “How about we send someone to the fiefdom to tell them about this situation? They shouldn’t have to go too far.”

“That would be unnecessary,” the man protested again. “Please think of our pride as well. We can take care of it. That is why we are escorting you.”

“How about then I lend a hand-”

“Please wait here comfortably. There is no need to feel sorry for us.”

The man grinned in satisfaction. He was covered from head to toe in mud. Despite that, he appeared to be genuinely happy to serve Julian. The conversation between the man and Julian left a strong impression on Sheherazade, who was observing them from a distance.

“…”

As she eavesdropped on their conversation, something came to her mind. During her stay in the manor, she overheard conversations between the maids and servants several times. They were about the second son, Lloyd’s brother. She heard he was quite clever, graduating second in his class at the royal academy. And so, he was set to proudly return home in a few days.

That boy, no, that guy, must be the second young master.

He looked much younger than she imagined him to be. And it seemed he had found himself in a pretty sticky situation.

Indeed, there was a big downpour two days ago.

It had been a while since there was a heavy downpour. And so, a portion of the road seemed to have collapsed in the rain. That was why the carriage from the palace couldn’t pass through.

This might be my chance.

Sheherazade racked her brain. The destroyed mountain path. The boulder was difficult to remove. Julian and the others, all of whom were stuck here because of this. What if she helped him out?

If I did, it would be easier to ask for a recording from that man, Lloyd Frontera. I’d be doing his brother a favor after all.

She would gain an advantage in their negotiation. So certainly, this was a clear opportunity for her.

All right. I will help him out.

Sheherazade weighed her options and rose from the bush and walked over to him conspicuously.

“Excuse me,” she called to Julian.

“Ahhh!” shrieked Julian as he jumped.

“…”

“Y-you got me really good there. Who are you?”

Sheherazade must have come forward too suddenly. Julian’s eyes went wide in sheer fright. Seeing him, Sheherazade thought to herself that the man looked like a fluffy baby bunny.
