# Singing with a Genuine Heart (1)

---

What the hell did you just say?

Lloyd hardly swore. This was true no matter what horrible or sickening situation he was in. It was simply something he didn’t like to do. He grumbled to himself instead of cursing. But the moment just now was an exception. As soon as he heard Captain Felipe’s serious and gentle words, Lloyd was almost triggered to grab him by the collar and shake him several times. The reason wasn’t hard to understand.

Singing? Did you say singing? A beautiful singing voice?!

If there was one skill that he sucked at, it was singing. Lloyd was very good at evaluating himself, so he had a keen perception of his limitations. Singing was a notable weakness of his. He had no expectations for himself to be able to sing a beautiful melody. To him, it was more realistic for a newly-enrolled engineering student to make a semiconductor than for him to sing well.

But what? Sing?

Laughter spilled out of him. He was too dumbfounded. But he couldn’t give up like this. The restoration of destiny needed to be stopped, and that required obtaining the legendary jewel rumored to be in the Mermaid Kingdom.

“But, um, must I really have to sing?”

“Hmm?” Captain Felipe cocked his head to the side.

Lloyd stared at him in desperation as if he was grabbing a branch in the river amid the sweeping current or searching for some lifting insoles before going out on a surprise blind date. Banking on his hope, he asked, “Isn’t it? Do I have to sing? I should only prove that I’m not an enemy or an intruder. For example, through dancing or whispering.”

“Hmm, I don’t know. I have never tried any other method. I was drowning in water at the time and didn’t have time to think. Also, dancing and whispering? Why would you have to resort to such methods? You already have the most foolproof method that has been verified by me, singing.” Captain Felipe shrugged.

“…”

Thinking to himself that this aforementioned verified method wasn’t going to be possible, Lloyd wanted to cry. But he couldn’t abandon hope just yet. So he asked, “Um, then, will any song do?”

“Any song?” There was curiosity and dismay in Captain Felipe’s voice. “What do you mean by that?”

“So, um, I am asking if it’s okay if this friend over here sings instead of me.”

Lloyd pointed to Javier beside him. Captain Felipe’s gaze flickered over to Javier and came back to Lloyd. He shook his head. “I don’t think so.”

“Why not?” whined Lloyd.

“I heard it directly from the mermaid that saved me.”

“What did they say?”

“That it responds to the song personally sung by the one in dire need. So a song sung by someone other than the one who has business with the kingdom will not convince the mermaids,” said Felipe firmly.

“Then, I have to be the one to sing no matter what?”

“That appears to be the case.”

“…”

“What’s wrong? Is there a problem?” asked Captain Felipe.

“…”

Yes, there are a lot of problems. Lloyd smiled as he stared at the captain. It was the smile of someone who has given up on everything.

“I am quite cautious about telling you this,” interrupted Javier on Lloyd’s behalf, “But Master Lloyd cannot sing.”

“He cannot sing?” asked Captain Felipe.

“Yes,” affirmed Javier.

“How bad is it?”

“Have you heard of the rumor back in Namaran, the city of trade?”

“Oh, yes, I have. How the Hell’s Knight made a fuss there.”

“Yes,” acknowledged Javier, “And Hell’s Knight writhed in pain after hearing him sing.”

“Writhed in pain, you say? Why?” Genuine curiosity flashed across Felipe’s face.

“…”

“It cannot be!” A look of shock came on the captain’s face when he realized the meaning of silence.

“Your guess is right,” affirmed Javier.

“Ehem… Hmm!” Captain Felipe cleared his throat. “His singing is so horrible that it can even torment the Hell’s Knight from the underworld?”

“Simply put, the noise of nails scratching on a chalkboard would be more melodious. Otherwise, a parasite living inside your intestine will be better at singing than Master Lloyd.”

“Whew, I see now.”

Stop it, you dimwits… I’m right here. Haah.

Lloyd heaved a long sigh, wiping off the drops of tears starting to form in his eyes. Just then, the captain tapped his shoulder twice and spoke with the most generous and easygoing expression on his face in the world.

“It’s okay. Singing, well… It doesn’t matter if you’re not good at it.”

“Excuse me?”

“It looks like you’ve misunderstood me.” Captain Felipe went on, “When I spoke of melodious singing, I wasn’t referring to a singer with great technique or voice.”

“Then what did you mean?” Lloyd looked at Captain Felipe for understanding.

“Sincerity and genuineness.” The captain’s gaze turned serious. “In fact, I didn’t sing well when I was in the water as well. How could I? Think about it. I was plunged into the icy water, and my voice was rough and coarse after having screamed too many times for the distancing ship to come back. What do you think my singing would’ve sounded like?”

“Um, not very good,” supposed Lloyd.

“You’re right. It was the worst.” Captain Felipe chuckled. “My awful singing wasn’t something a sound mind could bear. The tone was off, and my voice was shaky and off-key. I didn’t expect the mermaid in front of me to like my singing, which was done in a dire state of desperation as there was nothing else I could do.”

“Then I suppose the mermaid thought kindly of your singing nonetheless.”

“Yes,” replied the captain, whose smile deepened. “At first, it cocked its head and stared right at my eyes. That was when I felt it. It was paying attention to my singing, realizing from my desperate cry that I’m no enemy or an intruder.”

“Your sincerity and genuineness were conveyed to the mermaid.”

“That’s exactly it,” replied the captain, tightening his grip on Lloyd’s shoulder. “So, you don’t have to be discouraged just because you can’t sing. That only tells me about your vocal technique. What you need to do here is sing genuinely and sincerely.”

“You are saying that my singing can work well enough.”

“Speaking from experience, yes.”

“Thank you.”

At last, Lloyd was able to smile, and his heart felt lighter. It turned out the captain, who was speaking from experience, was right.

Right. I jumped to the conclusion too fast. It’s a mermaid, not a human. It wouldn’t be strange for them to have different singing criteria than humans. There’s no need to worry right now. I just need to show them my sincerity.

Now, he was confident. This was the first time he ever had that sort of emotion toward his singing. That probably was the reason why he managed to ask the captain the following.

“Um, excuse me, captain,” called Lloyd after seeing Captain Felipe was about to leave.

“Yes? Do you have anything else you would like to ask?”

“Yes. What is your recent schedule for a voyage?”

“My sailing schedule, you mean?”

“Yes. Since we’re talking about this matter anyway, I’d like to make contact with the mermaids in the Arctic Ocean, but if possible, I’d like to receive help from a seasoned sailor like you.”

Lloyd revealed his plan in earnest. He had to go look for the Mermaid Kingdom no matter what, and that required him to go to the Arctic Ocean. If this was the case, he reasoned that it was better to travel via ship cruised by an experienced captain.

I could fly with Ggoming or Yongyong for comfort. But I don’t think that’s what I should do this time.

Ggoming was a parrotbill, so he wasn’t suitable for a long-hour flight. Even more, he needed to rest several times a day when things got too tiring for him. But taking a break was impossible in the northern sea.

It’s too cold and also vast with no land in sight. There are glaciers, but I don’t think that would let us take a proper break.

At the same time, flying with Yongyong didn’t sound that comfortable.

Sure, it won’t get exhausted like Ggoming. But it’s too big and intimidating. The mere presence of Yongyong alone will be a great threat to the mermaids.

It would wreck the mood around them and ruin everything. What Lloyd needed was to engage with the mermaids with an earnest heart, and taking Yongyong would only give the impression that Lloyd was an intruder from the very beginning. Lloyd didn’t want that to happen.

“And you will receive a generous payment from me. Could you travel with us?” suggested Lloyd.

“Hmm, does that mean you will pay to reserve the entire ship?”

“Indeed.”

“Not to mention, the journey is to the Arctic Ocean. Tsk. Are you asking me this even when you know that it’s off-season for hunting emperor whales?”

“And that is exactly why I am pleading even harder.”

“Hmm.” Captain Felipe pondered. “I shall have to bring back my crew on break at the moment, and the payment should be at least doubled, given there will be more icebergs than usual to make the trip that much more dangerous.”

“It is all right,” said Lloyd, “I have a lot of money.”

“Really?”

“Yes,” said Lloyd as he brought out a bag and put it on the table. The bag made a thick, thudding noise, and the table creaked. The captain’s eyes shook slightly.

“…”

“You can look at what’s inside,” added Lloyd.

“Ehem…!”

The captain glanced and there, inside the bag, were dazzling pieces of jewelry and treasure Lloyd had taken from the dragon cave. The wrinkles around the captain’s eyes, the mark of his turbulent years, wiggled.

“If you’re saying this a retainer, then the total payment will be-”

“Five times more than what’s in the bag,” said Lloyd.

“So are we leaving today?” Captain Felipe didn’t hesitate anymore.

“Thank you, captain.”

And surely, money talked. Everything from that point on proceeded very smoothly, the preparation for the journey was done at lightning speed. The captain had summoned all the crew members on vacation to his ship within one day, and though they grumbled at first, they all hurriedly scrambled to get on board when the captain offered them an exorbitant amount of money.

Similarly, he was easily able to prepare the food and water for the trip, thanks to the help of Count Cremo, who still wanted Lloyd to be his son-in-law. He generously provided everything needed for the expedition as if he was trying to put Lloyd in his debt.

And just like that, Feliconia managed to depart Cremo before sunset and set out on the restless journey toward the Arctic Ocean. The weather was clear, and the wind was gentle. The waves were calm, and the current was stable.

This is a relief. I was worried that I’d get seasick.

A sense of relief rushed through Lloyd. To be honest, he had been on a ship just once when he rode a cruise ship in the Han River during his childhood. So he was slightly worried.

It’s been the first time I’ve been on a whaling ship. I was so worried that I’d vomit everything I’d eaten.

But thankfully, it didn’t happen. The sail was smooth. And finally, on the 20th day of the expedition, Captain Felipe told Lloyd, “We have arrived at the destination.”

“This is the place?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes. We’re in the Arctic Ocean. Though technically speaking, we are just at the entrance.”

Lloyd looked around. The deck was spotlessly clean, and over the ship rails and in between the thick cold air that formed each time he breathed, the dark sea spanned without end. Glaciers floated around here and there, and the whole scenery was typical of the Arctic Ocean documentaries Lloyd had seen on TV.

The captain continued, “So I think you should sing here.”

“Really? Sing here?” asked Lloyd, uncertain.

“Yes. I don’t know if you can feel it, but there are already mermaids around us. They have been increasing in number after starting to follow us yesterday.” The captain glanced at the sea.

“Don’t ask me how I know. I can only guess from observing the movement of the birds and sardines. Anyway, a fair number of mermaids are surrounding our ship now. So it’s time that we let them know we’re not intruders.”

“You speak as though we might get attacked otherwise,” said Lloyd.

“Precisely.” The captain smiled bitterly. “They are very closed off. It has been decades since they last took me in, and that was only for a short while. And so, it’s time that you appease them with your singing. Otherwise, we’ll be marked as intruders and either get attacked or banished from the ocean.”

“Whew… All right.”

Lloyd glanced at Javier briefly as he listened to the captain. Javier affirmed the latter by slightly giving a nod. The mermaids were indeed enveloping the ship, which he knew based on his acute senses as a grandmaster. So Lloyd knew it was time for him to act.

“Ehem… Hmm!”

Lloyd let out a few dry coughs as he calmed down, and he took a step forward toward the edge of the ship, his heart thumping. The sharp mast stood tall. Lloyd stood on the bow spirit, which was the most conspicuous area for the mermaids down the sea. It was a conducive place to express his genuineness and sincerity through his singing.

Whew.

Lloyd took a deep breath, calming his heart. And he reflected on his honest feelings then.

I want to save everyone. The count. The countess. Julian. Sheherazade. I’d hate to see the queen with a paralyzed arm. And to that end… I have to convey my sincerity to the mermaids here.

Lloyd gazed down at the dark sea. The mermaids must be observing him from there. Lloyd wished his sincerity would be felt so they would no longer be hostile. Lloyd dared to wish so.

Carefully and genuinely, in a trembling voice, he sang. While still lacking technique and in an unstable tone, he instilled sincerity from the bottom of his heart. He hoped and yearned that his honesty would lower their guard and hostility.

Sometime later, the mermaids laid a merciless salvo of attack against the Feliconia Ship.
