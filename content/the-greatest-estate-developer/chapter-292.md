# Reckless Driving is Bad (3)

---

Four days passed. All the while, a strange rumor went around among the centaurs in the Stone and Wind tribe.

“Honey, did you hear about that human?” a centaur wife asked her husband.

“Human? Who?”

“The one who arrived here a few days ago.”

“Oh, the man with the red speeding ticket?” asked the male centaur. “What was his name? Lloyd Frontera?”

“Yes, that’s right,” she affirmed. “Lloyd Frontera. He told me he’s going to give a lecture.”

“A lecture?”

“Uh-huh.”

“About what?”

“I think it’s an in-depth lecture about speeding or whatever.”

“Are you serious?” He lifted his brow.

“Yes. Honey, you were getting anxious that your hind leg isn’t as strong as it used to be.”

“Hmm, yes,” he acknowledged. “I fear my stamina isn’t as great as before.”

“So…” she said as she clapped her hands. “How about you go and listen to the lecture?”

“Do you think it will help?”

“Of course. You have nothing to lose by attending. While you’re at it, take the kids with you.”

“Why?”

“You should know how important early education is these days,” she said strongly.

“Hm, you know what? You are right,” he agreed. “The man earned a red speeding ticket as a mere human. He must know the secret to getting faster, right?”

“I’m sure he does. I hope our kids become great foals that get red speeding tickets.”

“Of course,” he said. “You are wise, indeed.”

At the same time…

“Boss! We have a situation!” A young centaur charged into a stable.

“What? What is it?” His boss asked in surprise.

“Well, do you remember the human who got a red speeding ticket? He’s giving a lecture.”

“On what?”

“They say it’s going to be about something called speeding!” The young one’s voice sounded frantic.

“Ha…” the boss centaur sighed. “Are you sure?”

“Yes.”

“That’s a big problem.”

“Isn’t it? I’m afraid his galloping method will be similar to ours, and he will teach it to others.”

“I agree. That tactic should remain a secret amongst ourselves.”

“Should we apply for a patent?” asked the young one carefully.

“Hmm… When’s the lecture?”

“The day after tomorrow.”

“Tsk. It’ll be too late by then. We won’t have enough time to get a review,” said the boss.

“Then what do we do?”

“Let’s go to the lecture. We should listen to what that human has to say about this speeding matter.”

“Yes, boss. Do you want us to put our names on the attendance list?”

“Yes, put everyone down.”

“I must have heard wrong. All of the members?” The young centaur was surprised by his boss’s eagerness.

“Of course. The galloping members and the maintenance team as well. Even if his theory is different from ours, I’m sure we can glean one or two lessons from him.”

“Yes, I see, boss.”

A similar conversation also took place among the younger centaurs. The rumor didn’t stop there. It spread further outside of the Stone and Wind tribe and across the plain.

“Chief! The human who received a red speeding ticket from the Stone and Wind tribe is giving a lecture!”

“This is our chance. The human’s theory of speeding must be different from ours, so we’ll no doubt learn something new from him!”

“Let us go!”

“Faster now!”

“Quickly now!”

“Nothing can stop us!”

A total of 21 centaur tribes, including the Rock Wild Grass, Deer Cloud, Mushroom and Creek, and Dew and Stake tribes, all spread across the various parts of the plain, flocked to the news of Lloyd’s lecture. The crowd of centaurs was as thick as the clouds in the sky, and everyone came regardless of their age or gender. There were couples with kids, elderly who claimed to be as healthy as a horse, and foal centaurs who wished to become a champion of reckless racing in the future. Within a few days, the Stone and Wind village was packed with centaurs who came to listen to Lloyd’s lecture.

The following morning rolled around. At last, the lecture titled “What is Speeding?” started.

“Ehem, thank you all for gathering. My name is Lloyd Frontera.”

Lloyd greeted the group of over a thousand centaurs gathered at the meadow near the Stone and Wind village. He slightly shifted to show his back as he spoke with a booming voice charged with mana. His red speeding ticket was proudly stuck behind him. Soft exclamations of admiration spilled among the centaurs.

“It’s real… It’s really the red speeding ticket.”

“It’s glorious to see it with my own eyes.”

“Mother, I’ve never seen anything like that.”

“Make sure to get a good look, sweetie. Promise me you’ll grow up to become a great adult who earns hundreds of those tickets, got it?”

Whisper! Clamor! The awe-filled murmuring filled the area, and the gazes fixed on Lloyd were full of admiration. Lloyd remained silent for a second until the noise subsided.

When everyone seemed to have calmed down, he spoke again. “Yes, nice to meet you. I am the human who received a red speeding ticket. I am sure you have come here because you are curious about the secret behind my running ability. That is right! What do I mean by speeding? From now on, I would like to have a meaningful time with you by having an in-depth discussion about what speeding is. Shall we begin with a round of applause?”

Clap! Clap! Clap! Lloyd was natural on the stage with the centaurs. A smile of contentment appeared on his mouth.

“Yes, clapping makes you feel good, doesn’t it? That’s how important blood circulation is. Now, allow me to start the actual lecture. What is speeding? I am sure that this is a question all of you constantly ask yourself every day without fail. Am I correct?”

Nod! Everyone nodded. It was true. What was speeding? How could they gallop faster? What should they do to race faster than their competitors? Those concerns were on their brain every second of the day. It was something that stayed on their mind for the rest of their lives. Lloyd prepared his lecture being well aware of this fact. With a big, broad smile on his face, he resumed speaking.

“To tell you the conclusion first.. Speeding is the most foolish thing you could ever do.”

Everyone’s eyes widened in surprise. Their eyes said, “What are you talking about?!” But Lloyd didn’t stop there and relentlessly continued his lecture.

“Running faster? Sure, it’s good. Refreshing. Exhilarating even. But look. Think about it. Galloping anywhere you please and bragging about your speed… Don’t you think it’s a bit immature and childish?”

“…”

“In the past few days,” Lloyd went on, ignoring their shocked silence. “I’ve seen how many of you behave during my stay here. A lot of you race at full speed, even inside the village. Very recklessly. You all thoughtlessly charge forward regardless of whether you are at a crossroad between stables, an alley between tents, or a play area for young foals. I did witness countless accidents, both big and small. There were also several times an accident almost happened.”

“…”

“Will that do?” Lloyd’s voice was now accusatory. “So what if you can run fast? Your raging speed brings nothing but trouble. It just hurts innocent centaurs and yourselves as well. Do you know what we call someone like that in the human world? A nuisance.”

No one said anything. But the silence was rather cold, and so were the gazes fixed on Lloyd. They were colder than Lloyd expected. A bitter smile appeared on his face as he recalled his days in South Korea.

Korea has more reckless drivers than you’d expect too.

There were quite a lot, actually. It was common for drivers to move forward without stopping at an intersection, even when the pedestrian light turned green. Some even squeezed through crossing pedestrians. On roads without traffic lights, it was common for cars to ignore pedestrians who were on the crosswalk first. Only a handful of cars stopped for people to pass by.

Not only that, a lot of drivers park in no parking zones or near the fire hydrants or pedestrians. It’s more uncommon for people to speed than to not. They only slow down when there are cameras around and hit the gas as soon as they’re cleared. I was guilty of that, too.

Before joining the military and his family went bankrupt, there was a time Lloyd drove his father’s car to Busan after getting his driver’s license. That was when he learned that the 60 mph speed limit on the highways was only for show around cameras. Everyone drove over 70, 80, 90 mph when there was nothing watching them. It was the same even inside cities.

The speed limit of 30 mph was ignored by everyone. But even then, that’s on the mild side of things. Most people aren’t truly reckless drivers and just go slightly above the speed limit. But some… Ha.

The thought alone prompted Lloyd to sigh. He occasionally encountered a reckless driver. There were some who risked their lives to tailgate him on the road at full speed. Others speedily tailed the car ahead to cross an intersection before the light turned red. There were drivers who even went on a transcontinental journey from one end of the road to the other by incessantly switching lanes. At times, there were people who also threw cigarette butts out their windows. In other instances, there were drivers who honked and turned on their high beams just because the car in the front was moving a little slow.

Those instances were blatant examples of reckless driving.

The problem was that these violations weren’t rare. They happened quite often. This reality was shocking. Lloyd couldn’t help but wonder what those drivers were thinking inside their car. And Lloyd held the same sentiments now.

The centaurs are similar. They just aren’t aware of their recklessness because that’s the norm for them. Reckless galloping is a part of their culture. They just can’t accept the notion that different areas need to have different speed limits.

Their sole focus was galloping at an outrageous speed. Safety and whatnot were not even a consideration. Lloyd figured that he had to change that. And so, Lloyd started to openly provoke the centaurs.

“If one has common sense and reason and knows how to be considerate of those around them, one would know where it is appropriate to race and where it is not. No one deuces anywhere they please. The same goes for galloping. One must be able to distinguish where to run at full speed and where to restrain themselves. Do you know what we call those who are incapable of doing so?”

“…”

No one gave an answer. Amid the even more frigid silence, Lloyd spoke, his voice ringing clearer.

“Airheads.” Lloyd’s voice slightly turned up to emphasize the word. “That’s the word. Unfortunately, that description fits a lot of your behaviors. You recklessly ran even when there were children around. You galloped across intersections, being inconsiderate of others, and only regretted it once you collided with someone and fell to the ground. What is that? Certainly not the proper way to gallop.”

“Then how do you want us to gallop, two-legged smartass?”

At last, a voice of opposition erupted from the crowd. It belonged to a young centaur who stood out from the rest. He was about 1.2 times bigger than the rest, and his hooves were thick and robust. His muscles moved as if they had been sculpted by an artist. The white, silky fur that covered his whole body was a remarkable sight to behold. Even his face was good-looking.

“Yes,” Lloyd acknowledged him. “Your name is?”

“Komanchi. Three-time champion of this plain.” Komanchi’s voice sounded intimidating and confident.

“A champion? Are you the fastest centaur here?”

“Yes. In addition, I am the future head chief who will lead all the centaurs on this land one day.”

Komanchi then turned his back toward Lloyd. Five red speeding tickets were plastered on his back. He said, “I suppose that is enough of an introduction about me. Anyway, I would like to ask you as you seem to be on the high horse. How does the best galloper with intelligence and thoughtfulness run? I would like to pick your smarty brain on that.”

“I see. That’s a good question. The answer is simple. It is to only race inside designated areas without causing harm to others.” Lloyd held up his hands, palm facing up toward the sky.

“Are you saying that we must limit our right to freely gallop?” Komanchi demanded.

“That is not a right but an act of disturbance.”

“It’s our tradition!” Komanchi protested.

“And a bad one.”

Feeling ever more frustrated, Komanchi growled, “Can you prove that arrogant statement of yours?”

“How do you want me to prove that I am right and take responsibility for my statement?” Lloyd’s voice, on the other hand, was calm.

“Race with me!” Komanchi, all worked up now, thwacked his chest several times. And he bawled, almost declaring, “I don’t think you have any idea about us since you’re a human, but we don’t listen to slowpokes. So prove that you’re faster than us!”

“Hmm, but I already received a red speeding ticket though.” Lloyd pointed to his back.

“That was the work of your hamster, not yours!” Komanchi thrust his finger in the air at Lloyd. I’ve heard from my cousin, the patrol commander. You did the race while riding a hamster! So I cannot accept that. It’s pathetic. Use your own legs and run with the wind to prove you are a fast runner!“

"You’re saying that you’ll acknowledge my words if I race with you alone and win. Am I correct?”

“That’s right!”

“Mister Komanchi. Are you challenging me now?”

“What else do you think I’m doing?!” screamed Komanchi as he thought to himself that there was simply zero chance for him to lose in the race against the scrawny two-legged slug. Smiling confidently, he was determined to humble this conceited human in front of everyone.

Lloyd, too, flashed a big smile.

That human fell right into my trap! Komanchi thought proudly to himself.
