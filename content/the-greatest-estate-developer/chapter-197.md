# Young Master is a Top Gun (2)

---

Ding dong.

[A new title <Top Gun from the West> has been created.]

Woo!

A series of messages appeared in front of Lloyd’s eyes, and he secretly clenched his fists. He held a bounty of successful experiences and earned various accomplishments and titles from them. So this time, he had also been expecting a decent tribute to come out.

Rather, I’d be upset if these messages didn’t appear before me anymore.

The construction took over a whopping four months, which meant that he had to give up his spring season in this scorching and arid desert. As such, it would be only natural to receive a reward. Lloyd’s eyes rolled down in anticipation.

[Top Gun from the West]

[Title Level: Regional Hero Tale]

There’s no water. We’re thirsty. The Sultan abandoned us. Even the administrator fled away. No help came our way. That seemed to be it. That seemed to be it without change. All may perish, but nothing will change. Everyone gave up. That was the reason. When a young master from a neighboring country woke us up When he gave us water stressing it was coming from him, What a strange man he is, we thought. What a creepy man he is, we thought. We never knew then. That this young master will never give up on us. That he would toil covered in the sand all day when the sultan and administrator abandoned us. So, what happened now? Allow me to ask you a question first. My dear customer, would you like some tea?

[Compliment Effect: You have become the lifesaver and hero of the entire region by solving the chronic drought that plagued the Kandahar region. Your tale will spread to all corners of the desert. The shamans of desert tribes in the world bless your name with gratitude and respect. Thanks to it, you will not experience dehydration in any situation in the regions of the complement influence.]

[Title Region: All desert regions. All areas whose annual average climate is 104 °F and above.]

[Active Period of Title: 300 Years.]

[A title’s powers can be activated for 24 hours in the region for which the compliment was given. Moreover, time and region can expand or shrink depending on your actions.]

[Monthly CP Earned from the Title: 4]

[Current CP: 614]

Wow.

Lloyd exclaimed in pure amazement after reading the title description. He couldn’t help it.

I’m immune to dehydration?

The title only worked in hellish deserts and other regions where the climate went above 104 °F (40 C°) on average. It would have been better if it included all regions instead of only the deserts but was still a tremendous skill. Dehydration. It was rare to find any other symptom that could take one’s life away so easily.

Of course. We die when we lose about 12% of the water from our bodies. Furthermore, dehydration is not uncommon.

Vomiting and diarrhea from a nasty illness. Not drinking water after sweating much. Or getting lost in the desert. Any of those events could cause dehydration in the body. But now, Lloyd was immune to it, and he wouldn’t die in the desert because of it at least.

Not only in the desert, though. I mean, I could fall in hell out of great misfortune, but even then, I’d be okay.

But, well, it was apparent such a thing wouldn’t happen. Lloyd shrugged his shoulders and proceeded to focus on the joy of earning a new tribute. However, his joy didn’t end there. Aside from the tribute message in front of his eyes, more praises and tributes continued to appear.

“Mister! Water!”

Right after Lloyd returned from teaching people in the small provinces how to use the Qanat, someone shouted at him as he entered the downtown. The voice belonged to a five or six-year-old child, who came sprinting toward Lloyd. And the boy held out the wooden cup to Lloyd.

Slosh! Half of the water splashed out, soaking Lloyd’s pants.

“Eek?!”

Lloyd was surprised by the sudden splash, but he didn’t blame the boy because the boy’s eyes glimmered so brightly.

“Water!”

“You want me to drink it…?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes!”

“Is this mine?”

“Yes!”

The boy, whose blushed cheeks were full like a dumpling, continued to speak, his dark eyes glimmering all the more.

“Mom said! You gave Selim water!” the boy cried out.

“Oh, your name is Selim?”

“Yes!”

“Then Selim, are you gifting this water to me?”

“Yes! Mom said you must be thirsty too!”

Lloyd took the cup, which was almost empty, and slowly drank from it, feeling several gazes watching him. Countless people of Kandara were gathered and looking at him. Some with a content gaze. Some clapping. Each and everyone was expressing their gratitude and favor in their own way.

I just did what I had to do to achieve my goal, which was to start the talks and prevent war with the sultan kingdom, Lloyd thought to himself. He started this project to ensure a comfortable life for himself and no one else. So, he was unsure if he deserved this kind of treatment.

It’s strange. This feeling.

Lloyd was shy. At the same time, he was ashamed and apologetic. He particularly felt that way toward Termes and the rest of the rebels.

“You know… You should have been the ones to be applauded and praised by the people. Not me.”

Later that night, Lloyd expressed his feelings to Termes.

“Since you all have conducted various activities against the sultan who gave this region up. You funded and bought water for the people in need and endlessly worked to restore the wells abandoned by the regional administrators who ran away.”

Lloyd was telling the truth, but they actually did more than that. The rebels struggled hard to convince the regional emirs of the redistribution of the prosperity and wealth concentrated in Ahinsya and argued that the scattered regional military power must be brought together to press the sultan. They tenaciously clamored to get help from the emirs.

“But we weren’t able to provide water to the entire region like now,” Termes chuckled as he turned to Lloyd.

Termes was in traveling clothes now. But he wasn’t alone. The rebel executives and their families were all wearing robust traveling clothes. They loaded a handful of items needed for a long camelback journey as they were defecting to the Frontera fiefdom tonight.

“It is true that we tried,” said Termes. “But we failed to be as successful. So, you don’t have to apologize. We should be grateful instead.”

The corners of Termes’s lips pulled further up before continuing. “You gave the people in this region a new life. Not only that, you gave us a new land in your fiefdom where we can start anew. Everything you said when you were abducted all came true.”

“Termes,” said Lloyd.

“Thank you. You are our lifesaver. So…”

Flop. Termes got down on his knee, and the rest of the rebels did the same. Everyone then politely bowed their heads.

“I, the son of Murat Termes, and the people of Damieta Termes vow unchanging allegiance to you, Lloyd Frontera, until we breathe last.”

Woah.

There was no time to stop them. Termes got down on his knee in a flash and vowed his allegiance to Lloyd. Simultaneously, another message appeared in front of his eyes.

Ding dong.

[Damieta Termes has opened his heart to you.]

[The rebel leader of the sultan kingdom, Damieta Termes, vowed his allegiance to you.]

[Damieta Termes will forever be grateful to you and gladly follow you until your death.].

[From now on, you can increase likeability and earn RP from him.]

[50 RP has been rewarded for forming a new connection with another human.]

[Current RP: 2,755]

[Your likeability with Damieta Termes has increased by 11 points.]

[Current RP with Damieta Termes: +61]

[You have earned 77 RP by improving your relationship with a character.]

[Current RP: 2,832]

[Moreover, the likeability of the men and women of Damieta Termes, who vowed allegiance with him, will be included in the average likeability of all the Frontera fiefdom residents.]

Woah.

A title, likeability, and RP? Is it my birthday or something? Lloyd quickly pulled himself together, forcing down the corners of his lips that were pulling up to his ears. And laboriously ignoring Turtle Neck’s message that real allegiance comes after death, Lloyd looked at Termes and the rest of the rebels.

“Thank you, everyone…”

When someone expresses or hands out something with a genuine heart, you must do the same. Or you must pretend to act so. That’s the polite thing to do. So Lloyd accepted Termes’s vow of allegiance in a serious expression, gaze, and voice.

“I will do my best to not let you all down as well,” said Lloyd solemnly.

“Lord…”

“The night will be cold. Be safe on your way back to Frontera fiefdom.”

“Yes, my lord,” said Termes. “We will leave first.”

“Yes. Turtle Neck?”

Crack! At Lloyd’s calling, Turtle Neck stepped forward fast.

“From now on, I want you and Skeleton Corps to guide them. You can do a good job, can’t you?” Crack! Turtle Neck nodded its skull energetically. It and its team had already once traversed the desert, and such experience allowed them to completely master which road was risky and safe for humans. Moreover, Termes and the rest of the rebels had been born and raised in the desert. The reliable guide that was the Skeleton Corps. The travelers native to the deserts. The two were a wonderful combination. So, there wasn’t anything to worry about regarding their journey to the fiefdom. And just like that, the Skeleton Corp, Termes, and the rest of the rebels set out on their long journey. Lloyd finally sighed in relief as he felt a weight taken off his shoulder.

“Whew.”

Lloyd was used to receiving compliments from the messages, but it was a slightly different scenario to get praised in person by real people. It was uncomfortable and made him shy. He even felt embarrassed to a degree. His heart was eased when he shamelessly went around and showed off his charitable actions to the city’s people to get cooperation from the rebels.

But now that the stage is set, it’s really embarrassing to hear real praises. I’m not making any sense.

A wry smile escaped Lloyd, and soon, he headed back to his lodging again to get ready to leave for Ahinsya the next day. But when he got there, the string of praises that agonized Lloyd continued. The next up in line was surprisingly a member of the delegation the sultan had assigned to him.

“I sincerely respect you, Master Lloyd.”

“…”

What’s the deal with this man now? Lloyd wondered as he stood in the hallway of his room. Lloyd stared at the head of the delegation with a gutted face.

“Actually, I knew this from the beginning,” confessed the delegation head with a serious look on his face. He seemed to be early in his 40s. “How else could I not? That you are constructing with the help of the rebels here.”

“Oh… Did you?” mumbled Lloyd.

“Yes,” the delegation head said as he nodded. “We, too, have eyes and ears. So we contemplated about this matter whether we should stop and report you to the sultan that you joined hands with those that oppose him. But we didn’t. Actually, we thought we shouldn’t.”

“Why is that?” asked Lloyd.

“Since completing the Qanat you are working on will stabilize the lives of the people in this region, which will benefit the sultan in the long run.”

“I suppose,” replied Lloyd, “you saw turning a blind eye is a way to be loyal to the sultan in the grander scheme of things?”

“Business-wise, yes. But personally, no.”

“What do you mean about the latter?” asked Lloyd, intrigued.

“I personally came to respect you.”

…Cough!

“I respect you, Master Lloyd.”

“Ah… I understand. You know, you can let go of your hand…”

“Can I hold them for ten more seconds? I want to tell proudly tell my son about how I held the hands of a wise man once I get back to Ahinsya.”

“Suuure…”

He got goosebumps all over his body. It was more than toe-curling. His hands, feet, neurons, and every hair and cell in his whole body curled in embarrassment. But Lloyd held up, pushing himself with sheer willpower, and endured the uncomfortable and shiny gaze this middle-aged man was shooting at Lloyd. Making things worse, Lloyd even signed the autograph when the latter held out a piece of paper.

Oh, crap. Why is it that I keep being liked by smelly old men? Come on.

Case in point, Lloyd had a group of father-in-law candidates who wanted him as their son-in-law. It was also the case for the men who bore their hearts out so easily and told him that they respect him. The more he thought about it, the deeper he was plunged into sorrow.

With the same thought in his mind, Lloyd and the others left Kandara the next day while the people cheerfully saw them off. And for several days, Lloyd lived through the coldness of the desert at night and rode camelback for several days until he arrived at Ahinsya, the capital of the sultan’s kingdom. But Lloyd’s popularity didn’t dwindle here. The most powerful man among the father-in-law candidates was waiting in the palace.

“How come you are not my son-in-law…?”

Sultan Samarkhan, with a fiery gaze and gritting teeth, demanded Lloyd when the latter went to the palace to give a report on the Qanat construction.
