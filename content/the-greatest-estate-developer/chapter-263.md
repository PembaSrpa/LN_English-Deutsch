# Mermaids Love Jjimjilbangs (2)

---

Jjimjilbang. A popular location that was loved by most Korean people. A space where they could enjoy a tender boiled egg with a sip of ice cold sikhye, a sweet rice beverage, washing away all their prolonged stress and exhaustion. Lloyd, too, had been to a jjimjilbang in South Korea. Just once.

It was around the time I was working nonstop on construction sites.

There was a period when he was blessed with an endless amount of work. He made daily visits to construction sites without taking a single break between them. His daily wages piled high in his bank account, and he was elated. But in return, it took a serious toll on his body.

I didn’t have any time to relax and unwind. Even after finishing my work at construction sites, I had to head home and study.

Though he was on leave due to his next semester’s tuition fees and was free from the stress of schoolwork, he couldn’t sit back and idle away. He had a goal to graduate, get a license, and land a decent job. Then liberate himself from the sickening, heavy financial shackles weighing down on him. And finally, start an ordinary family and live a normal life without any large troubles or worries. That was the ultimate goal he had back in South Korea.

To others, it was an ordinary and modest goal, but to him, it wasn’t. He was desperate and despaired over his goal, so he barely rested to that end. He had at least seven nose bleeds every week. Not only that, his nutrition was also quite poor, having relied only on rice, noodle, and kimchi provided by his room rental for free. And thus, it went without saying that his face was yellow as if he had serious jaundice.

And one day, when fatigue was cruelly weighing him down, right before he crawled back home after one of his construction jobs, Lloyd’s coworker called him.

“Suho, do you want to go to a jjimjilbang after work if you have no other plans?”

But, of course, Lloyd shook his head and turned down the offer. It was the only choice he had. He was penniless. And going to a jjimjilbang? That was an unimaginable luxury. Thinking that he might as well spend the money on noodles and a few more canned tunas instead, he awkwardly smiled and said he was busy rather than saying he was broke. He was too embarrassed to say the truth. He told his friend to go alone and refused. But his friend said…

“Hey, you’re going to get sick if you keep living like this. I’m paying. We’re going, you bastard.”

Lloyd jumped at the chance. Free! How nice! And when he arrived and saw the place for the first time, his jaw dropped. A red clay room. A diamond seawater room. And there were many other rooms with all sorts of different themes. The whole facility was beyond what Lloyd had expected. And there were the boiled eggs! The savory flavor was more delicious than he could have ever imagined. Lloyd wondered if those eggs were the same as the ones he had always eaten before. And then what about the iced sikhye he drank with the eggs? His eyes welled with tears of happiness as he took his first sweet sip. Having always drank nothing but lukewarm tap water, which only the heavens knew when it was last cleaned, sikhye tasted like nectar blessed by the gods.

Ah, those were good times.

And as he left the jjimjilbang, he resolved to himself that he would frequent this place once every week when he got a job and lived a decent, humane life. Lloyd had made a firm resolve in his heart. But unfortunately, he wasn’t able to keep this promise.

Getting a job? Ha. I was brought here before I could get one.

Lloyd finished off his trip down memory lane with a bitter smile. And he looked beside him. People he newly met. There was Javier, who was lifting his brow like someone out of a beautiful painting. And Rotorua, looking up at Lloyd with his head tilted to the side. The two of them were eyeing him with curiosity in their eyes. Lloyd smiled.

Oh, they must have never heard of a jjimjilbang.

Come to think of it, that was the case. Lloyd cleared his throat.

“Hmm. Ehem. You must have never heard of it until today, right?” asked Lloyd.

“You are correct,” affirmed Rotorua grimly. “Um… What is it about the place that makes you so certain it’ll be a success here? And what does it have anything to do with unearthing the jewel’s site location?”

“Yes.” Lloyd brought his hands together. “To simply answer your two questions, I want to give you a yes.”

“What on earth is a jjimjilbang?”

Lloyd sensed impatience in Rotorua’s voice. “It is a place where you heat your body with hot water or steam.”

“Heat my body?” Rotorua was almost rooted to the spot at the suggestion.

“Oh, of course, but not like how we heat up food.” Lloyd chuckled before he went on with his explanation. “I mean, it’s where you warm up your body but not until you get burned. The heat is supposed to relax your muscles and release tension.”

“Hmm,” mused Rotorua, “I know of something similar to a jjimjilbang. It is not that we merpeople do not enjoy hot massages.”

“Really? That is new.” Lloyd was glad that the concept was familiar.

“Indeed. Though it is illegal.”

“What do you mean illegal?” asked Lloyd, interested.

“Exactly what I mean,” replied Rotorua, “It seems as though you thought of this jjimjilbang after looking at the hydrothermal vent, but actually, we merpeople enjoy secretly getting a massage in these sorts of locations.”

“Secretly?”

“Yes, since it is dangerous.”

“Dangerous, you say…”

“The water over there is too hot,” pointed out Rotorua. “It can be an adequate source of heat at a reasonable distance and good position. However, that is hard to determine.”

Lloyd nodded. “I see what you mean.”

“Right? But the bigger problem is that the water is not very clear.”

“Are you talking about the toxic substance?”

“Toxic substance, you say. That’s probably right. If you drink the water in the area by accident, you start to become dazed, struggle to breathe, and lose consciousness in an instant. So, it is very dangerous.”

“Hmm… I see. It would be extremely dangerous for you to be floating around unconscious with extremely hot water gushing out beside you.”

“Yes. It makes it more than likely for you to get a severe burn. If you are unlucky, you might die. So, that is why it is forbidden to warm yourself at the hydrothermal vent.” Rotorua’s eyes, which were fixed on Lloyd, were filled with doubt. “But do you plan on constructing a facility for that? The queen will not allow it. No, even if she does, it will not be easy to have the merpeople overcome their fear and reluctance. Have you thought about that?”

“Yes, I did.” Lloyd nodded, thinking that it was a simple matter. “That is a good point. But all I need to do is provide an adequate amount of heat control and block the toxic substance from entering the jjimjilbang facility. These two things will be the main focus of this construction project.”

“So, is there a way for us to manage this?”

“Of course,” Lloyd replied confidently. He was certain of it.

Of course. This isn’t something I’d joke about or half-ass.

There were no second chances. It was essential to conserve time and energy in the process of discovering the jewel’s site location, constructing the jewel with the blueprint, and finally finding a method to stop the restoration of destiny. Only the heavens knew how much more time and effort Lloyd would have to pour in to execute the method he found. Considering this point, he couldn’t carelessly handle any of the tasks or processes that needed to be completed

Do I think the merpeople will welcome my jjimjilbang with open arms and love when I build the facility? I might as well give up on this project with such naive expectations.

Lloyd had carefully thought through his entire plan. The safety measures in the jjimjilbang. The elements that would charm the merpeople, draw them in, and make them fall in love with it. And how he’d use that love to his advantage to achieve his ultimate goal of forcing a work contract onto the merpeople. In no uncertain terms, Lloyd had a plan.

“So,” continued Lloyd, “Please lead me.”

“Lead you? To where?” Rotorua looked at Lloyd.

“Is there a cave or something like that nearby?” Lloyd asked without answering the question.

“There is.”

“How far is it from here?”

“Hmm, around a distance of five Gigatitans.”

“Five Gigatitans…” pondered Lloyd, “That would be about 400 yards away.”

“That should be about it from your standard measurement. Do you want me to take you there?”

“Yes.”

“…”

Rotorua stared at Lloyd. He thought that this human was a bit unique. No, weird would be a better word to describe him. At first, he had scoured the city, claiming to search for the Jewel of Truth. Eventually, the man excitedly met with him for that purpose. Then he seemed to become suspicious of him. When his suspicions were resolved, Lloyd declared he’d build a jjimjilbang or whatever it was, as if that would solve everything. Furthermore…

Plus, there is the silver-haired man beside him. He has an interesting attitude as well.

Rotorua’s gaze then moved to Javier, whom he pegged to be working for this human named Lloyd. The man wasn’t stopping his master’s actions or asking any questions either. He continued maintaining his stoic composure even after hearing Lloyd’s ridiculous suggestion to build a jjimjilbang. It was as if he had complete trust that everything would work out just by following Lloyd’s plan.

Hmm.

Rotorua gulped. Something didn’t sit right with him, and the thought that these two guys were swindlers crossed his mind. The rumors had it that land humans were sly and cunning creatures who were willing to commit even the most heinous crimes for gold or money.

But…

Rotorua shook his head. It was his first time meeting anyone who was looking for the Jewel of Truth. Moreover, at the same time, he was slightly getting tired of this whole expedition.

I was stuck.

He had devoted over 20 years to this job but found no success. The fear of spending his life meaninglessly digging dirt at the bottom of the sea until he died of old age just like many of his ancestors, including his grandfather and father, suffocated Rotorua at times. He wondered if he’d also eventually pass down the mission of finding the jewel to his son before passing away.

I don’t want that.

He was scared and didn’t want to lead such a futile life. But if he continued as he was now, that seemed to be his inevitable future. However, he encountered an unexpected source of help today.

I might not find an opportunity like this again.

An explorer looking for the Jewel of Truth other than him. Rotorua decided to put his hopes on this human. Resolved, he nodded at Lloyd and said, “Good. I will take you to the cave. Follow me.”

Rotorua led the way. The cave was not very far from where they were.

“Oh.” Lloyd’s eyes shone as he arrived and admired the cave entrance.

This is nice.

It was much better than what he expected. The entrance was vast, about 22 feet wide and 32 feet high.

What about the inside?

Lloyd entered the cave with the help of Javier’s aura to light up the place. The inside turned out to be better.

Wow. This looks like a karaoke room!

The cave stretched 820 feet long. The main corridor at the center extended in an almost straight line from the entrance to the exit, with small and large rooms attached on all sides. The smallest one was about the size of a college dorm. The biggest one was close to the size of a university auditorium.

This is amazing. It’s perfect. Just perfect.

Lloyd’s heart pounded as he surveyed the inside as the whole space was unbelievably perfect for actualizing his jjimjilbang blueprint.

Looking at what’s here, I can say that a chunk of the construction has already been completed. Foundation work? There’s hardly much left to do. I’ll just smooth out some rough edges, divide the sauna rooms according to their themes, place down pipes for the hydrothermal vents, add some convenience facilities, and et voila!

This construction didn’t require much effort from him. From that moment on, Lloyd hit the ground running, going in and out of the cave for four days straight and surveying the inside structure. He simultaneously engrossed himself in surveying the entire place, concentrating on the areas and points that required repairs, reinforcements, and the exit and entrance channel for the hydrothermal vent. Lloyd thought over, planned, and designed every element of the construction. Meanwhile, he excitedly made sure Javier was put to work as well.

“Hey, Javier,” called Lloyd.

“What work do you plan on giving me now, Young Master?” grumbled Javier curtly.

“Ha!” puffed Lloyd. “Can you already tell what I want just from hearing your name?”

“You slaved me around too many times.” Javier chuckled. “By this time, I cannot tell whether I am your personal guard knight or a practical piece of construction equipment.”

“Yeah? You can’t tell yet, huh?” quipped Lloyd as he wiggled his brows.

“You couldn’t be…” Javier became goggle-eyed. A look of disbelief came over him.

“You are a knight. Have some pride,” said Lloyd as he smiled. “How could I so shamelessly think of you as a piece of construction equipment?”

“Do you… mean it?” said Javier, relaxing his muscles a little.

“Of course I do. You are a knight. Our fiefdom’s most honorable knight, Sir Javier Asrahan.”

“Hmm,” reflected Javier and squinted his eyes.

“What? What? What is it? Why are you glaring like that when I’m being nice.”

“I am wondering whether you are up to something.”

“Up to something?” blurted Lloyd, offended. “I’m not up to anything.”

“Do you really mean it?”

“Of course. Sir Asrahan, what’s wrong? Do you feel embarrassed about being properly treated as a knight? Is that it?”

“That is not it.”

“It’s not, right?”

“No.”

“Then time to get to work.” Lloyd then put his arm around Javier’s shoulders. “Do you remember the hydrothermal vents from earlier? Near them, there should be some long, thin shells that look like bamboo.”

Javier turned and shot Lloyd an I-knew-it look.

“Those are called giant tube worms. Collect as many of their outer shells as you can for me. I just need the ones that are thicker than your thighs. Also, make sure to leave out any of them that are cracked or chipped,” instructed Lloyd.

“…”

Seeing that Javier wasn’t reacting to him, Lloyd asked, “By chance, do you not want to do this job, Sir Asrahan?” Finally, Javier clicked his tongue, lashing out inwardly at Lloyd for sweet-talking him into being treated as a piece of construction equipment.

Haah… You are not even the real Lloyd.

Regretfully licking his bitter lips, he turned, returned to the hydrothermal vent, and buckled down to collect the shells. But not without rage.

While Javier was busy with his task, Lloyd preoccupied himself with constructing the jjimjilbang. For this project, he utilized the water pipe construction method. Since the facility was built in the deep sea, Lloyd employed all the knowledge he had to make sure there wouldn’t be any malfunctions. With that resolve, he finished everything from determining the pipe thickness, installing the pipe conduit, employing the water pipe branching method, calculating the area of exposure, and performing the installation method. The veins in his eyes popped out as Lloyd stared at the design blueprint. Each day, Javier became faster and more adept at collecting the giant tube worms’ shells. Five days later. And at last.

Great.

Lloyd gazed at the perfected design blueprint with a glow of satisfaction. At last, the moment to lure the merpeople into a labor contract negotiation using an incredible, first-ever construction of a seabed jjimjilbang was nearing.
