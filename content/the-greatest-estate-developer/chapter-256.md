# Singing with a Genuine Heart (2)

---

Splash! Ice-filled waves crashed down with a gruesome noise and hit the flank of Feliconia, the gigantic whaling ship. The ship, which had overcome countless tribulations and raging storms, staggered. Icy water flooded over the upper part of its poop deck, soaking Captain Felipe in the process.

“Hard over! Port!” yelled Captain Felipe.

“Hard over!” repeated the boatswain, relaying the captain’s instruction to the rowing room beneath the deck. The rowing sailors’ back muscles moved in response like waves of water. As the experienced captain smoothly turned the wheel, the huge ship swerved to the port, and the rowing room yelled in accordance with the changing angle.

“Port! Let fall! Four! Starboard! Give way together! One!”

“Whew! Ha!”

The rowing sailors synchronized their breaths, but the rhythm of their rows started to shift. The portside sailors stroked once every four breaths, while the starboard sailors did so with every breath they took. The different movements of the oars immediately led to a change in the ship’s movement. Splaaash!

The front of the ship drastically swerved to the left like a stirring eel, its giant body whipping left and cutting through the surrounding waves. Right after the ship took a sudden left turn, a series of muscular individuals jumped out of the water like dolphins. But they weren’t dolphins. Splash! Plash! Splash!

“Acck!”

“We missed it!”

Their muscular torsos glimmered in the seawater, and below that, they had a fin with scales that brilliantly reflected against the light. They were mermaids. During the split second they jumped above the water, some stared at the Feliconia, shuddering in anger that their majestic attack had failed. But they didn’t give up easily. This only marked the beginning of their attack.

“Sisters! Do not lose the intruder!”

“We protect our sea with our own hands!”

“Break the keel in one go!”

Over 500 mermaids charged at the ship as they swayed their fins at full speed from the deepest part of the sea. And they charged forward with sheer strength and tried to ram their bodies into the lower part of the ship.

Crash! Boom! Thump! Although the ship was performing well in its maneuvering, it failed to dodge the violent ambush from the hoard of mermaids. The hull heaved at each of their attacks. An ominous thudding noise came from the ship. The look on Captain Felipe’s face paled slightly. But Lloyd’s face looked more urgent than that of the captain.

“Wait, captain?!” bawled Lloyd, who was terrified by the sudden shift of the atmosphere right after the mermaids carried out their violent ambush. He almost fell off the bowsprit and dove into the sea.

“Singing! I thought that was enough! I thought being sincere and genuine was enough!” Lloyd screamed at the captain, his voice dripping with sheer bitterness. He had come down and crawled through the deck, which was rocking from the ship’s drastic maneuvers and mermaids’ attacks.

You said it’d work! You said I only have to sing sincerely with all my heart!

So he did. And with all of his heart. There was seriousness, warmth, and desperation in his singing. He wanted to save the count and countess, Julian, and so many others. He had sung, direly asking for the mermaids to offer him a little help and signaling that he was no enemy or intruder.

“Then why?! Why is this happening?!” ranted Lloyd, still feeling embittered.

Captain Felipe wheeled around at that. “Hey! I told you to sing!” He shot a dumbstruck glance as he busily steered the wheel. “My gracious! Why is this happening, you ask? Can’t you tell? I told you to sing! With all of your heart!”

“Yes, you did!” shouted Lloyd. “That’s why I sang!”

“That… was what you call singing?” Captain Felipe froze and cocked his head to the side as though he was saying, “Are you for real?” And then he started, which was dead serious and grim that Lloyd almost broke down and burst into tears. Unperturbed, the captain went on, “Come on! You should have just sung a normal song instead of making my ears bleed with that awful sound! Why did you even bother with such a poor attempt? Tsk!”

Lloyd was speechless.

“It’s not too late now. How about giving it a proper try this time?” suggested the captain.

Still, Lloyd said nothing.

“Hey! What are you doing?! Stop crying and say something!”

“…”

Lloyd wanted to burst into tears and ask what the captain would do in his place. He was getting beaten to the bone from the verbal attacks coming at him with every passing second. Lloyd forced himself to hold back his tears and changed the subject.

“Tsk! I won’t sing! Let’s just get out of here since it looks like I’ll need to use another method.”

That seemed to be the best option. The singing approach the captain suggested seemed to be a failure since it made them look like a threat instead of opening the mermaids’ hearts. So, right now, the best they could do was run away.

Then I’ll have to look for another method. Really, if I really have no other choices, I’ll bring Yongyong and invade the kingdom.

If no method worked. If he couldn’t be friends with the mermaids, it seemed he would have to consider using brute force.

But that’s my last resort. Right now, escaping this place safely comes first.

Lloyd anxiously observed the situation. It wasn’t looking good.

Clang! Crash! Boom! Noises crept up from the bottom of the ship nonstop. It looked like the mermaids were trying to bore a hole in the bottom of the ship by ramming against it, and each attempt created a fierce vibration on impact.

I’ve got a bad feeling about this.

Lloyd’s face slightly darkened. Truly, the situation was bad, and it was going to get worse.

Even though this ship is huge, it won’t be able to hold on forever.

The whaling ship was manufactured for the sole purpose of hunting emperor whales. This meant that the ship carried a fair number of oars and a sail that ensured a safe escape from a pod of whales even while transporting hundreds of tons of whale carcasses. The design also gave the ship explosive maneuvering capabilities despite its huge size. Furthermore, since the ship had to endure the frantic effort of whales to break free from a harpoon, it was designed to be incomparably more durable than an ordinary ship.

But we are up against a terrible opponent.

They were up against mermaids. The most powerful creatures in the sea. The aqua tyrant that made even emperor whales tremble in fear. That was what mermaids were.

It is said they’re exceptionally strong. Their strength is rumored to equal the strongest of the orc warriors. Moreover, there are hundreds of these creatures coming at us at once… If we allow their attacks to continue, the bottom of the ship will eventually break.

Of course, the ship would last for a while even if there was a hole on the bottom of it. The vehicle was designed to withstand the attacks of emperor whales. It wouldn’t sink from a few holes and a bit of flooding.

But what if we get unlucky and the keel snaps?

The keel was the spine of the ship. If it broke, it was over for them. The partition structure of the vehicle that prevented flooding would be useless the moment its keel broke. The real problem was that the mermaids were strong enough to break the keel.

Right now, the ship has just barely been able to avoid any direct attacks on its keel with some impressive maneuvers, but there’s no guarantee that this luck will continue.

Lloyd objectively observed the situation. It was bad.

And it’s not like we can fight back.

Technically, he could. He could order Bangul to use his volcanic discharge or have Hamang create a whirlpool by absorbing water and spitting it back. Or he and Javier could set off their mana blast into the sea.

But that’s too dangerous.

Lloyd shook his head. It was dangerous not to them but to the mermaids.

I need their help. What if I fight back and hurt the mermaids? Then that’d be the end. It’d be a complete deal breaker.

From then on, he’d be their archenemy. Any chance of convincing or persuading them would disappear, much like a cup of instant ramen that had been spilled on the ground.

Then there’s only one way left for us.

At last, Lloyd made a decision. He shouted at the captain, “Captain! How long do you think we can continue on like this?”

“Continue on? What do you mean?” replied Captain Felipe aloud.

“I mean, stay in one piece without sinking!”

“If we’re lucky, all day!”

“And if we’re unlucky?”

“One second!”

“…”

So, in short, the whole thing rested on luck. Gripping himself together, Lloyd said, “Then I’ll try to strike a negotiation with them.”

“Negotiate? In our current situation?” Captain Felipe asked.

“Yes,” replied Lloyd. “Though I don’t expect it to work!”

“Are you trying to buy us time?”

“They might employ less attacks on us while they listen to me speak! So, do you understand what to do when I start negotiating with them?”

“I see. Escape instead of evading.”

It was vital for them to get out of this sea. Once that happened, there would be fewer glaciers around them, and the ship would be able to move freely without the fear of crashing into one. They would have an increased chance of escaping without harm. Like the experienced sailor he was, Captain Felipe understood Lloyd’s intention in a flash and nodded. A satisfied smile appeared on Lloyd’s face.

Okay. Let’s give it a try.

Lloyd loosened the muscles around his mouth and went down to the ship’s poop. And he stuck his body out of the edge, the icy waves breaking in real-time, and he gave a mana-loaded shout down below.

“There! Mermaid sisters! I actually have a question!”

His shouting made its way into the waves, reaching the ears of the mermaids throwing their bodies at the ship.

Lloyd shouted once more, “I know that I upset you all with my strange and terrifying singing, but you see! I’m curious! What can I do to appease your anger?!”

Your anger! Your anger! Your anger…

His yelling spread more ferociously, further and deeper into the sea, at last bringing the mermaids to stop their attacks for a second. Everyone’s gaze moved beyond the sea and fixed on Lloyd, whose body was protruding out of the ship. They became livid.

“That’s him!” one of the mermaids shouted. “Yes! That’s the guy who sang!”

“That wasn’t singing! It was a curse that used sound waves!”

“My ears are still bleeding from the noise!”

Every single mermaid’s eyes burned with rage, and they soon became hellbent on catching and ending the human in their view. They would sew his mouth shut to make sure that he wouldn’t defile the sea with his disgusting singing and screeching ever again. The Mermaid Corps resumed attacking after cementing their spirits with burning anger and determination. Swoosh!

“Eek…?”

Just then, Lloyd spotted one mermaid barreling up below the sea and winding up to punch him. Lloyd fell back in surprise, but it was too late.

Shoot! The fist was coming too quickly at him, and Lloyd saw the massive forearm and shoulder muscles attached behind it. And he also saw the burning eyes of his attacker. It was evidently the eyes of a warrior.

She’s going to kill me!

But he couldn’t do anything. The fist was flying toward him with unexpectedly great speed and power, and his reaction to it was pathetically slow in comparison. The moment he had this realization, he became certain.

I’m going to get punched.

What would happen if she hit me? Lloyd wondered. He would get a fractured jaw at the very least.

If I’m unlucky, I might have to eat nothing but porridge for the rest of my life.

He didn’t want that to happen, but there was nothing he could do. At that moment, all he could do was extract mana from his manaheart and amp up his mana with the Asrahan Core Technique. And as a result, avoid shattering his jaw by slightly raising his defensive capabilities.

Ugh. Please!

Lloyd clamped his eyes shut, hoping that it wouldn’t be too painful. Just then…

Baaam! He registered a loud banging noise coming right below his jaw. He opened his eyes in fright. Then he saw.

Javier?

Javier, whom Lloyd didn’t notice coming, had his hand outstretched, blocking the punch with his palm.

“Hmph!”

The mermaid became wide-eyed when her punch was blocked. In response, she instantly twirled her body to wield her muscular tail, which was thicker than a log of wood. It swung with a sharp noise, and Lloyd feared that a mere brush would at least break every bone in his body. But it was nothing to Javier.

“It is dangerous. Please step back.”

Javier held out his hand and shoved Lloyd back before projecting his shoulder and bumping against the mermaid tail. The impact sent the mermaid flying back. Whack!

“Ack!”

The muscular mermaid sucked in a breath as she was sent off dozens of feet away. She crashed into the sea but swam down, unharmed from the blow. That was when Javier’s gaze went back to Lloyd.

“Are you all right, Master Lloyd?”

“Um… Yeah…”

Lloyd, who was thrown to the ground, nodded in a daze. He was unconsciously rubbing his jaw, and chills ran down his spine. In all seriousness, it was very dangerous a second ago. He might have gotten badly injured had Javier not intervened.

Dammit.

Lloyd shuddered when his attempt to buy time by negotiating failed.

I need to find a way out by any means possible.

Otherwise, this ship could really sink. He and Javier could escape with Ggoming, but Captain Felipe and his crew would be sacrificed.

I don’t want that.

He shook his head hard, racking his brain to find a solution. The situation continued to worsen nonetheless.

Crash! Clack! Creak! Crack!

As the mermaids flung their bodies at the ship and created a thumping noise, the ship began to gradually make a creaky noise that definitely signaled something worse.

“I fear that the ship is starting to topple by the impact,” said Javier, feeling nervous. Something terrible was about to happen.

I should save Master Lloyd first if the ship sinks.

Master Lloyd must be protected, come hell or high water. Even if he wasn’t the real one, now that he has become the pillar of the family, he was more important than the real one and required the utmost protection. At all costs. Regardless of any unreasonable situations, he shall be protected and guarded. Javier cemented his resolution when…

“Hmm. I’ve got no choice, given the situation. Javier. We need to fish for a bit,” announced Lloyd.

“Pardon? Fish?” Javier tilted his head at Lloyd’s words. Fish? Now? What could he mean? But there was no bait. How? And at last, Javier saw Lloyd hoisting a rope with a shameless smile on his face before the latter nonchalantly gave him an unbelievably absurd demand.

“You’ll have to be the bait. You won’t freeze to death in the water since you’re a grandmaster, right?”
