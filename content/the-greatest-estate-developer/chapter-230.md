# Along with Money: Hell (3)

The main gate of Hell. Underneath the sky blackened with smoke, two pillars of blazing fire shot up, and a long passage led in between them. At the end of it, there was a skull that was as big as a mountain. It was the home of the King of Hell, as well as the center of the land, the Hell Castle. Demon Pagenti, a resident of Hell, smiled evilly as it pointed to the castle.

“That is not the Hell Castle,” Pagenti said.

“Yeah? All your words are lies, aren’t they? You rotten piece of a bastard,” Lloyd scolded.

“Hehe, did you think I’d thank you if you told me that?”

“Who cares if you thank me or not?”

“Hahaha, is that so?”

Demon Pagenti blurted out rude remarks one after another. However, contrasting its words, it cordially bowed to Lloyd. How could it not? The day before, when it met Lloyd for the first time, it was beaten up to a pulp. It could never forget what happened the other day.

It hurt like hell, dammit!

Pagenti was simply going its way, hungry after lazing around in the magma sauna, its home. It had come out of its home to pester and eat lost souls nearby. That was when a large parrotbill landed beside it and the human riding the bird suddenly gave the demon a blessing.

What was it he said? That I would have to practice filial piety toward my parents if I don’t answer his question properly?

This was a blessing that anyone in Hell would deplore and lament as soon as they heard it, the kind that insulted a demon’s personality to the core.

Practice filial piety to my parents?!

What kind of life did one have to live to be able to spit out such awful words? And did Pagenti seem like an easy enough target for Lloyd to give such a blessing without a second thought? Demon Pagenti burned in rage and revealed its fangs right there, rightfully so as a resident of Hell. It jumped at Lloyd with determination to exterminate the rude man and throw him alive into the flaming pits of Hell. But before it could succeed, the demon was slammed across its cheeks. A clear and melodious sound rang across the whole world.

Wham! The metal shovel struck down at lightning speed. Pagenti found its head jerking to the side when the flat shovel head whacked it. That was it for Pagenti. The whole world grew dark, and it passed out. When the demon opened its eyes, it found its attacker, Lloyd, grinning at it.

“Hello?”

“Wh-what’s…”

Pagenti was about to ask what just happened, but before it could do so, the shovel struck down once more.

Wham!

Once again, a clear, melodious sound rang aloud and the world darkened. That was when it started. Pagenti woke up. Lloyd grinned. And before Pagenti had the time to say anything, it received another strike. This pattern repeated ten more times. And on the 11th strike, Pagenti finally made a realization. So, it bowed down flat on the ground as soon as it woke up.

“Please forgive me. Stop it, please!” Pagenti begged earnestly. But Lloyd simply grinned with a glow of satisfaction.

“Then let’s end it with one last strike!”

“…!”

There couldn’t be anyone more heinous than this person, thought the demon. Even after Pagenti’s sincere begging, Lloyd’s shovel continued to fly toward it. When Pagenti felt its head get shoved to the side by another blow, amid losing consciousness, it felt a shaft of despair and thought inwardly that it had been a while since it met such a deeply evil individual. He was truly a respectable man. The demon teared up in sincere admiration. And so, when Pagenti opened its eyes again, it was already prepared to pledge its loyalty to Lloyd. From that moment, Pagenti became Lloyd’s dutiful tour guide.

“Hahaha, do you plan on flying to the twisted mountain, Master Lloyd?”

“Mhm,” drawled Lloyd.

“My, how wise you are. Yes, that’s right. That’s the Hell Castle.”

“Is it? If that’s not the castle, I’ll fry you in magma as a reward. How does that sound?” growled Lloyd. 

“Please don’t.”

“Then? Where is it?”

“The truth is, that twisted mountain… Haah, it’s a mirage.” At last, Pagenti revealed.

“Tsk. My suspicion was correct. Then there’s got to be the real one somewhere, right?” asked Lloyd.

 "Yes,“

"Where is it?”

“…”

“Do you want to die?”

“To be honest, the Hell Castle is at… Over the lava lake… Blah blah blah… Past the western ash pile… Yada yada… Over the skeleton bin… That’s where it is.”

“Wow. And you planned on lying, pretending you don’t know?”

“Yes,”

“Was it worth it?”

“Yes.”

“How about now that you spilled the truth?”

“I am in agony…”

“Tsk. It must be just so hard to tell the truth.”

“Yes… ” Pagenti replied, choking up.

“It’s okay. Cheer up, you bastard. Once you’re done guiding me around, I’ll insult you as a reward.”

“Do you really mean it?”

“Of course.”

And so, the conversation took off in such a manner for the past day, and Pagenti finally succeeded in guiding them to the real Hell Castle.

Wow, not lying for a day was an ordeal.

Of course, Pagenti did lie at times. But it told the truth more often than not, making Pagenti feel a pang of guilt in its conscience. But it held up because it was no other than Lloyd, the notorious “Hell’s Singer.” He was the very person who destroyed the eardrums of Sir Giolexius the commander of the 1st Corps. And guiding this heinous man would bring honor to three generations of its family. Demon Pagenti turned around to look at Lloyd, feeling a sense of pride.

“Um, since my guide ends here-”

“Are you asking for an insult?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes!”

Demon Pagentani’s eyes glimmered. An insult from the Lloyd? Pagentani wished to permanently ingrain his words in its ears or frame them on a wall. But Lloyd destroyed Pagentani’s excitement in one moment.

“I don’t want to,” dismissed Lloyd.

“Excuse me…?” Pagenti’s eyes widened.

“Wait, did you expect me to keep my promise?” Lloyd taunted.

“Ha, t-that means-”

“The promise I made with you means less to me than a chewed-up piece of gum.”

“Oh! Oh! Oh!” exclaimed Pagentani.

“Did you get it…?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes…”

“Then piss off. I don’t want you in my sight.”

“Thank you so much!” Demon Pagentani ran away, its face beaming with a smile.

Lloyd sighed as he looked at the back of it.

Goodness, demons…

Being insulted enthralled them. And being lied to along with it, had them rolling on the ground in excitement. 

Seriously, they’d love internet forums in Korea.

It was a haven for slander and insults. The very place where users put down each other on a regular basis and threw insults at one’s personality. Though the atmosphere was different for each website, users’ trash-talking and humiliating people was generally a daily occurrence across Korean social media. But here in this place called Hell, it seemed everyone would be able to easily adjust to the internet.

Well, anyway,

Lloyd stared at the main gate of Hell Castle with a shrug. He had finally arrived here after taking advantage of one demon. So now…

“You cannot be meeting the King of Hell like this,” interrupted Javier, who had been silently following behind Lloyd all this time.

Lloyd turned and looked at Javier, his face saying, “Why bother to ask something so obvious?”

“I am,” Lloyd spoke with a grin.

But Javier’s face stiffened. “I believe your plan is too dangerous and careless.”

“Dangerous and careless?” Lloyd cocked his head to the side. “Why?”

“He is the King of Hell.”

Lloyd, however, ignored him and moved toward the entrance. He soon pointed out, “The King rules over Hell. We have no way of knowing what power he holds. Therefore, your safety is in question. That is, even if I cultivated a new level of power.”

“Do you mean you’re nervous even though you’re now a grandmaster?” asked Lloyd.

“Yes,” Javier admitted without any bit of shame in his voice.

“And that’s because you don’t know what kind of person he is?”

“Yes.”

“In that case, it’s fine,” assured Lloyd.

“Excuse me?”

Javier flinched. Lloyd shrugged.

“You may not know this, but the King of Hell is the least dangerous fellow in all of Hell.”

“I am not sure what you mean…”

“Don’t worry about it. I have a plan, so just relax and follow me.”

Lloyd chuckled in spite of himself. The thing was, he could always summon an exit gate from Hell if things really got dangerous and transport them back to the Lorasian continent. He could return to his ordinary life and give up on this mission without any regret. There wasn’t much pressure on him. So, Lloyd relentlessly walked forward without any hesitation as he entered the main castle, as though he was strolling around the Frontera manor. Just then, a passage from the novel naturally came to him.

There was a mention of the King of Hell in The Knight of Blood and Iron.

The King of Hell. The ruler of Hell. The commander ruling over all Hell’s Knights. Most would envision him to look so destructive when they hear those titles. But in reality, he was very different. Lloyd continued to walk as he recalled that part, and he made his entrance to the main palace of Hell Castle.

Since Lloyd wasn’t a soul yet but a living person with flesh, he was a stranger in this realm of the world. So his appearance brought surprised murmurs from the guards, who whispered to themselves if that man was indeed the master singer Lloyd Frontera. Lloyd ignored and continued to stride to the center of the castle, finally toward the throne of the King of Hell. Lloyd paid his respect to the king, who was sitting on a throne that looked much like an office chair. And the desk was wide but bare, without any decorations. Piles of paper took up the desk, and the king looked like he’d soon drown under the paper heaps. Pale and fatigued, the king fidgeted his rimmed glasses and muttered as he looked at the one paying respect to him. There was annoyance in his gesture.

“Haah, what now when I’m so busy…?” the King of Hell murmured.

The King of Hell. The one who could see through the core of anyone’s soul. The master of karma, and the watcher of the 19th purgatory. He was Hellkaros, the King of Hell.

“Haah,” grumbled Hellkaros, “For what reason have you barged in here when I am swamped with an endless amount of work? Can you not see that I have been unable to get off work for the past 670,000 years?”

“Please forgive me, the ruler of Hell,” said Lloyd respectfully. “Lloyd Frontera from the ground pays his respects to the king.”

“Respect, my foot. Kim Suho. We’ve met, haven’t we?”

The King of Hell’s question stung Lloyd, who flinched when he heard his real name. And Lloyd cast a glance at Javier to check for any change in the latter’s expression. Thankfully, it looked like Javier didn’t know yet the real meaning of “Kim Suho.” Trying to look natural and undisturbed, Lloyd looked up at the king.

“Yes, we have met,” answered Lloyd. “You appeared in my dream.”

It was true. The giant in fire. This king had once appeared in Lloyd’s dream in all of his stateliness. He had considered it a lively silly dream back then, but now, it turned out that the dream was real.

“I am truly guilty for paying a visit to you amid this time when you are busy giving verdicts in Hell. May I then get to business right away?”

“Of course. Hurry up.”

The King of Hell swayed his hands as if he couldn’t be bothered. All this while his gaze went back to the paper, his one hand grasping the pen and signing documents. With his other hand, he was flipping the papers as he even stamped them. Yes. This was the real image and essence of the King of Hell.

He’s a workaholic. A judge that judges hundreds, billions, and trillions of dead souls. He’s piled under a mountain of work, so he punished the others only with formal documents and the rule of law.

Just then, Lloyd recalled a part in The Knight of Blood and Iron that spoke about the essence of the King of Hell. He was said to never take part in direct combat. And he killed no entity. Documents and judgments. Those two weapons were the only ones he wielded to determine the fate of the others.

In other words, the work that required any use of force was left to Hell’s Knights and their corps, while he glued himself to the desk of never-reducing paper works to exact judgment on people. That was what the novel said about the King of Hell. And so, he had the least chance of posing any harm to Lloyd so long he wasn’t set for an official judgment in Hell. There was zero chance of offending the king during the conversation and dying or getting hurt. And so, literally speaking, this king was the safest brother here. That was the reason why Lloyd was able to maintain such composure about meeting the king.

Really, the scarier one is Hell’s Knight, not the King of Hell.

But there was Javier to save Lloyd from Hell’s Knight, for Javier has become a grandmaster. So long five or six Hell’s Knights don’t jump at him at once, he’d be able to put up a fight. But if things really seemed like Javier would be outnumbered and cornered in danger, Lloyd could always use the exit gate out of Hell and run away to the ground. He had done the math and concluded that he’d be safe even if he went to Hell. And it was primarily due to that conclusion and conviction that made Lloyd accept Anticus’s request.

And so, now that he had met the King of Hell at last, it was time to go back home after finishing his business quickly. Go back home, get an audience with the Dragon King Verkis, and enjoy the benefits of being in the Dragon King club. Resolving such determination in his mind, Lloyd wet his lips and spoke aloud the clever comment he had prepared for this moment.
