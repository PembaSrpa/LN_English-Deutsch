# Distinguish Yourself (2)

---

“What reason do you need to help someone in crisis?!”

“…”

Lloyd’s unexpected response surprised Queen Kiakora, and she clamped her mouth shut. She fell into a daze.

This human. Who is he?

Just then, the warnings of the previous generations of queens came to her mind. Do not trust the land humans. They are absolute opportunists. If something involves money or benefits them, they don’t even think twice about betraying their friends and anyone else. But then, at the prospect of another gain, they’ll team up again. They are an incorrigible existence. That was what all of her ancestors had advised.

But this human…

He seemed slightly different from what she was told. Even though they were faced with an extremely precarious situation, this human named Lloyd Frontera immediately rushed and stood by her side to hold up the gigantic egg with her. This situation and undeniable fact brought a flood of emotions to her heart.

“All right. Human Lloyd Frontera. I will accept your help.”

“Thank you so much!” shouted Lloyd, recognizing a slight tremble in the queen’s voice. He wondered if it was because she was straining to use all of her strength or because she was filled with gratitude toward him. Whatever it was, a proud smile hung on Lloyd’s face.

Great. That’s one big crisis avoided. At least for now.

Lloyd quickly stole a glance at the queen. Red-faced. Pumped muscles. She was giving her all to stop this gigantic ball of an egg. Whereas he was… standing next to her with his arms also outstretched to push against the egg. However, more precisely, he was only pretending as he just touched the egg with his hands and let out groans of struggle. Of course, there was a reason behind Lloyd’s actions.

Of course. I don’t know what exactly happened, but the monster started to go wild after I discovered the foundation of the jewel. It was at the same time as when the vibrations began.

Just then, Lloyd’s mind flew back to the previous events. He finally discovered the foundation of the jewel. Rotorua fidgeted it around to activate the blueprint. It was a success. And then came a rumbling noise and powerful vibration. The water around the foundation fiercely trembled. Lloyd was very certain of it. He had found the Jewel of Truth. He was happy. But his happiness was short-lived.

The Kraken went on a rampage.

Although he was several miles away from the Mermaid Kingdom, Lloyd got a vivid look at the Kraken in a frenzy. It was because of the subtle glow in the eggs that made up the Mermaid Kingdom.

The Kraken roared, and it started to dance as if it was going mad.

In the wake of that, the eggs were crushed into pieces and scattered across the water. The sight was akin to a portion of salted pollack roe falling apart from someone’s clumsy attempt at using chopsticks.

And I dashed here as soon as I caught sight of what was happening.

His instinct tingled as soon as he saw the Kraken going rampant. The resonance from activating the foundation of the jewel was related to what was happening at the moment. The timing was too perfect for Lloyd to dismiss it. It was very likely he might be held responsible for the Kraken’s savage behavior and the Mermaid Kingdom’s possible demise.

It was Rotorua who activated it, but I, too, am responsible since I supervised the job. So I can’t turn a blind eye now.

Did he feel a sense of responsibility? Fixing what was wrong? Neither of the two reasons was his reason for helping.

Like I care about taking responsibility! I just don’t want to get killed by the queen, who’ll for sure throw a punch to my gut once she finds out I’m the one behind this mess!

That scenario was plausible enough. What if a future investigation revealed that the cause behind this chaos was the excavation of the jewel’s lot? The merpeople would demand Lloyd to be held liable. If that happened, he’d have no excuses for his guilt.

Obviously, I won’t be able to exonerate myself. I was the one who ran the jjimjilbang and mobilized the merpeople to the site. And hundreds of merpeople were there when the jewel was discovered. They’ll act as witnesses. This means that I can’t bury this incident even if I want to.

So what would happen if things settled down at the current flow of events and the time came to clarify who needed to be held responsible? Lloyd believed justice would be served, and he would take the full blow.

That’s why I’m buttering her up in advance! I’ll pretend to actively offer my support to resolve this mess and gain some merit! This way, I’ll earn some brownie points from her too!

That would minimize the amount of criticism he’d receive down the road and allow him to avoid getting his gut-punched by the queen. And at the least, he wouldn’t be banished from the kingdom.

I can’t be banished after finally finding the jewel’s lot.

He had discovered the jewel after a great length of struggle and couldn’t get himself kicked out of this place. When he thought about all the time and effort he’d assiduously invested until now, he had to do something to avoid getting expelled from this kingdom. After making some calculations, Lloyd yelled, “My queen! Please push a little harder! On my count!”

“Argh!” Queen Kiakora groaned. “On your count?!”

“Yes! So, push harder when I tell you! Push!”

“Argh!” Her veins were enlarged

“Push!”

“Gargh!” Lloyd shouted while pretending to flex his muscles, cheering the queen on. It was similar to the sudden surge of strength that occurred when a personal trainer at a gym supported someone using a bell bar to get in another rep or as if a supernatural force was helping someone lift a 44-lb weight at the gym. The egg started to slow down at last.

Grrrr…

“We’re doing good! A little more! One!” Lloyd bawled.

“Arggh!” she boomed.

“Push!”

“Gargh!!”

Boom!

The queen’s pecs and triceps inflated as if they were ready to burst. And at last, the tyrannous egg stopped, proving once again the monstrous strength and majestic nature of the queen, the leader of the incredibly ripped race of merpeople.

“Ha… Ha…” panted Lloyd, wiping his forehead for effect. “Your Majesty, are you all right?”

“Of course,” replied the queen as she looked back at Lloyd. Her face was still red. She placed her pot-like rough hand on Lloyd’s shoulder. “I was this close to being crushed into the ground. Thank you. It could have been dangerous without your help.”

“Whew!” panted Lloyd still. “Do not be, Your Majesty. I merely supported you with my small amount of strength.” Lloyd pretended to collect his breath. It was a relief.

One egg was stopped. Moreover, it appeared that the queen hadn’t seen through him.

Great. I earned some brownie points from her for now.

But he needed much more. Much more brownie points, and more good impressions. That would be the only way he could use the jewel without getting lashed out by the merpeople.

That means I’ll have to accomplish something greater to solve this problem.

Lloyd’s calm gaze raked over the Kraken. Its trunk was so large, it was impossible to determine the size. Similarly, its legs were much too thick and long to estimate the measurements.

“Krururung!”

At another roar, the whole sea shook. Each time it twisted its body, more eggs bounced off in all directions. Lloyd felt a pang of remorse that was as bitter as a cup of espresso.

This chaos looks impossible to resolve.

That was how different the scale of the commotion was. And yet, the courage among the merpeople was also on a level beyond Lloyd’s expectations.

“Sisters! Rise!”

“Sister! Take my hand!”

“Brother! Trust me and grab me tight!”

Every merperson acted with composure, despite that they were unexpectedly thrown into this unprecedented commotion by the Kraken, the guardian of their city. They weren’t in a state of terror. Instead, they took charge of the situation, uniting as one.

“Everyone! I’m sure we all saw the example set by the queen! Let us follow her lead!”

“Wooo!!!”

Hundreds of merpeople teamed up to save the flying eggs and rescued the merpeople floundering inside them. The rescued individuals recuperated soon and joined in on the ongoing effort. And within 30 minutes, the merpeople safely managed to save thousands of eggs and their residential areas, placing all of them at the bottom of the sea. Then they encircled the frenzied Kraken together. However, they didn’t resort to violence right away.

Of course! The Kraken is our friend!

Even though its anomalous behavior damaged their houses and scattered tens of thousands of eggs, which translates to back-breaking labor and exorbitant money for reconstruction, the merpeople regarded the sea monster as their friend. They believed that this was an honest mistake and that there must be a reason behind its troubling behavior. And so, they agreed that it had to be appeased first. They gathered around, and in the most genuine manner, they sang, forming a choir of merpeople. Their melody softly caressed the sea and comforted the Kraken. Lloyd, who was seeing this, became keenly aware of one thing.

“They can’t sing, either!” Lloyd yelled, which came out inadvertently. He thought it was unfair.

What the heck! Their tune and rhythm are completely wrong! They didn’t have any right to judge me for being a bad singer!

Truly, their singing was horrendous. If they were brought to karaoke in South Korea, they would have enough energy to make the tambourines and flashing lights in the room seem dull. Anyone listening to them would want to divert their attention toward something else, such as a leaflet, remote controller, or a poster with the top song chart on the wall. But before Lloyd could voice out his bitterness, he was interrupted by the queen.

“They cannot sing, huh?” mused Queen Kiakora. “To your human ears, does our singing sound bad?”

“Oh, eh? That’s…” mumbled Lloyd, rebuking himself for forgetting the queen nearby.

“But you just shouted that they cannot.” Her gaze bore into Lloyd.

“Ahaha,” Lloyd quickly worked on his face to hide his emotion. “To be honest, I said so because I felt a sense of camaraderie.”

The queen cocked her head to the side. “Camaraderie?”

“I, too, am considered tone-deaf in my world.”

“Are you now?” Her tone indicated she wasn’t very convinced.

“Yes,” said Lloyd with a nod.

“But you seemed very upset a moment ago.”

“Yes. I couldn’t help but remember the judgmental gazes I received from others in the past..” Lloyd observed her face, and before the queen could say anything, he butt in as he pointed at the Kraken. “Oh, look! It looks like the singing isn’t comforting the monster!”

It was true. At first, the song seemed to calm the monster down, but that was only for a second.

“Krurung! Krung!”

Could it be that something else was bothering the monster? It twisted its body like a snake and roared, this time moving even more uncontrollably than before. The mermaid queen’s face darkened.

“The Kraken… refused our request for reconciliation.”

Could she be feeling the same rejection you feel when your trusted friend refuses to handshake you? There was sadness in her hard and stern gaze.

“Human Lloyd Frontera, step back.” Her voice was cold and emotionless.

“Pardon? What do you plan on doing?” asked Lloyd.

“Plan, my foot. Now that the Kraken has refused, we must fight.”

“Fight? With that thing?”

“Yes,” the queen replied with a wistful look on her face. “Our friend is going mad. We tried to reason with it, but that didn’t work. What should come next?”

“Um,” observed Lloyd, “Roll up your sleeves and suppress it?”

“Yes. That is my plan.”

Fwipp! As soon as she was done talking, she moved her fin for some powerful dolphin kicks, creating a whirling storm around that sucked everything, including Lloyd.

Ack!

It felt as though he was being thrown into a dryer. Lloyd was only able to recover his position after Javier came to the rescue. Meanwhile, the queen was already miles away from him.

“Kru-rururung! Krung!” Its roar terrorized the deep sea.

The Mermaid Corps formed a file to fight against their friend. In minutes, the Kraken and the Mermaid Corps, who both used to be friends, were about to clash. Why? All because Lloyd had activated the foundation of the jewel. But he wasn’t the type to feel guilty about it. To be technically correct, he didn’t have the time to feel guilty.

I can’t let a fight develop like this. If the jewel’s foundation gets destroyed… Then I’m screwed!

Lloyd became a bundle of nerves as he imagined it happening in his head. He knew that this concern was very likely to happen.

The jewel’s foundation is just a few miles away from where we’re at.

It wasn’t actually that close, at least from Lloyd’s perspective. But from the Kraken’s perspective, it sure was.

The Kraken can reach the lot by outstretching just one of its legs just like when someone reaches for the TV remote control in front of them from their sofa.

With just one stretch of its leg, it would be able to touch the jewel’s lot. And if that happened, the foundation of the jewel would easily break like a potato chip under a pair of dress shoes. It would get crushed in one blow. And along with it, his solution to find a way to stop the restoration of destiny from plaguing his life would get crushed.

That can’t happen!!

Lloyd shook his head as he let his imagination fly.

I have to prevent a clash between them. But what should I do? What am I supposed to do?

Lloyd’s clenched fists started to get sweaty. He racked his brain for a solution, going over the events in his head. The Kraken started to go on a rampage after the resonation flew out from the jewel site. And it wouldn’t listen to the merpeople’s soft words of persuasion and restraint. Now, the Mermaid Corps was seconds away from laying an all-out attack. Seeing them, Lloyd cudgeled his brain, observing the situation for any clues, even a small one. And at that moment…

“Kru-rururung! Krung… Krugh! Krung!”

Tumble! The Kraken’s whole body gave a strange, sudden jerk as it roared within the merpeople’s net. And then it let out a familiar noise at the end of its roar.

Buuurp!

“…”

Just then, a thought crossed Lloyd’s mind.

“Wait, did it just burp?”

Ding! A light bulb lit up in his head! The burping, writhing, and commotion. The Kraken’s behaviors started to piece together in his head, and from then on, he had a realization. He saw where the octopus’s legs were tapping itself and the silent gesture it made as it writhed. And the seemingly intentional burping. When he put everything together, he recalled a familiar experience he had. At last, he found the answer.

Hold on. Does the Kraken have an upset stomach?!
