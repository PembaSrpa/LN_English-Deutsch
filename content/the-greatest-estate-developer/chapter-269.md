# How to Treat an Upset Stomach (1)

---

An upset stomach. It was a painful experience everyone had at some point in their lives. It came at the most unexpected moments without any warning. Your stomach suddenly churned before making you tap your midsection in a desperate attempt to facilitate digestion and whatnot. Lloyd, too, experienced this before.

It was during a high school field trip. Dammit.

The mere recollection of the event made him shudder. It must have been the first night. He was about to hang out with his friends after dinner when his stomach started to feel weird. At first, he didn’t think much about it. But his bowel situation grew worse, making him nauseous and break out in a cold sweat. His clueless friends teased him for getting so pale. That was when Lloyd figured that something was wrong, that he had to do something. But more than that, he wanted to burp.

That was why I looked for a fizzy drink.

He heard that drinking a fizzy drink wasn’t a good way to soothe an upset stomach. It could worsen the pain. But Lloyd didn’t know this back then. All he wanted was to chug down a drink and let out an almighty burp to relieve his stomach. And as luck would have it, he found a cider in his friend’s bag. Popping the lid of the sixteen-ounce bottle, Lloyd guzzled the drink, only to sputter it out.

It was soju.

Soju in a cider bottle. It was one of the contraband goods his friend trafficked away from the teacher’s radar. It was too late by the time Lloyd realized. Though he spat the alcohol out, he had taken several sips already. Soju on top of the upset stomach. His first ever drinking. From then on, he felt a clawing sense of nausea in his stomach, as if a nuclear bomb had been dropped there. Lloyd vomited and retched through the night, feeling dizzy and groggy. Thus, the mere recollection of his first-ever school trip brought that sense of despair back to his present self. But thankfully, his moment of recollection didn’t last long, thanks to the question that interrupted his thoughts.

“So, the Kraken is acting up because it has an upset stomach?”

Lloyd was jostled out of his trance when his ears picked up the question, and he looked at the source. The mermaid queen was there. Deep furrows between her brows. And she was staring at Lloyd with a grim and flustered gaze.

“Oh, that’s right,” nodded Lloyd, and it was then he refocused on his present situation. The Kraken had gone rampant. The Mermaid Corps stood by to lay a forcible attack on it. He had been anxious about the possible damages to the jewel foundation. His realization that something was off about the monster. The octopus dry heaved and retched. It even rubbed its stomach with its legs. These were the typical behaviors of a human with an upset stomach.

Once it became clear to Lloyd, he had hurriedly swam to the queen and shouted at the latter, who was about to order the merpeople to charge forward. He begged her to wait as the Kraken might be in trouble. They needed to find a solution other than having an armed fight. He stopped her and managed to hold off the attack for some time.

“An upset stomach?” the queen inquired as she stared gravely at Lloyd. “That means it is suffering from indigestion. Is this true?”

“It appears so, indeed,” Lloyd said.

“What is your proof of that?” she demanded.

“I have been watching the Kraken retch while it roared. I also spotted it dry heaving a few times. Furthermore, look at how it rubs its stomach with its legs while writhing and then retches again. This seems like a typical case of an upset stomach, don’t you think?”

“Hmm… I do not know. We don’t get upset stomachs.” The queen pumped her pecs each. “Can you not relieve them with muscular strength?”

“Um,” Lloyd hesitated, “The stomach muscles are involuntary and can’t be moved at will.”

“Who cares?” puffed the queen. “All you need to do is to press your stomach with your abs.”

Lloyd was utterly speechless.

Might as well say that you can use your brain by flexing the muscles on your scalp!

However, Lloyd couldn’t voice his thoughts out loud. It wasn’t really because he was scared of getting punched. Rather, he was rather afraid to see her brazenly answer that it could be done. Her answer would surely make anatomists foam at their mouths. Lloyd shook off his dismay by shaking his head.

“No, haah, anyway,” insisted Lloyd, “I believe this is a case of an upset stomach from what I see. You may not be familiar with it, but humans like us get an upset stomach from time to time.”

“Do you think that the Kraken has an upset stomach based on your experiences?” considered the queen gravely.

“Indeed, that’s correct.”

“Hmm. Is that so?” Thankfully, it appeared that the queen, though still half in doubt, was considering Lloyd’s opinion. This was a golden opportunity, but her doubts could quickly turn into full-blown suspicion.

Sensing it, Lloyd immediately said, “Yes, so it will be better to confirm this possibility first.”

“Check it?”

“Yes. You must confirm first before trying to respond.”

“But how do we confirm it? Do you have a plan in mind?”

“I do.” Lloyd nodded. Surely, there was. He had thought of one before he desperately swam to stop the queen’s order to charge forward. “Is there a food or snack the Kraken likes?”

“Hm, there is. But why do you ask?”

“If you give me that information, you will be able to confirm what is happening.”

“How?” The queen was confused as to where Lloyd was getting at.

“If it does not have an upset stomach, it will still react violently to your snack.”

“And what if it has an upset stomach?” Her gaze grew icy.

Lloyd replied confidently, “At first, it will stop and show interest in your snack as usual. But soon, it will retch and turn away, only to throw another fit.”

“Then,” summarized the queen, “The key is whether it goes on rampant right away or after showing initial interest. Am I right?”

“Yes, that is a precise summary,” conceded Lloyd with a smile.

“Great. Let us do it.”

“Do you plan on trying it yourself, my queen?”

“Who else will do it if not me?” The queen looked down at Lloyd with a relaxed smile unique to the powerful. “Who can get close to the savage Kraken? Me. Who can swim the fastest in this sea and dolphin-kick without being swept away by the current? Me. Lastly, who can best endure the blows from the Kraken? Me.”

The queen pounded her chest muscles as if her answer was obvious. “Should I send out a frail merperson who is weaker than me instead? One of my vulnerable people? Not in a thousand years. I must step forward for a matter like this. That is the responsibility and duty of someone on the throne.”

“Oh? Okay…”

“Or did you mean to test it out yourself?”

“No.” Lloyd blurted at lightning speed. Thankfully, it didn’t look like she thought about sending him there, for she merely chuckled. And then something came out of her pocket.

Noticing that it was a shiny light green marble, Lloyd asked curiously, “What is that?”

“A marine diamond,” replied the queen, her voice completely relaxed.

“I have never heard of it before.”

“If you take a regular diamond to the deepest part of the sea and squeeze in with all your strength for ten days in that terrifying water pressure, it becomes this unique jewel. Even I can only make a few stones a year. That is how exhausting the process is.”

“Th-Then it must be extremely expensive,” commented Lloyd, gulping as he spoke.

“That’s right. It probably is one of the most expensive stones in the world. That is why the Kraken loves it so much.”

“…” Lloyd almost told her that he’d be her Kraken instead of that gigantic octopus head. That was how marvelous the marine diamond was.

“Then I will go confirm what is happening. Everyone! Standby!”

The queen then swam toward the Kraken as it continued throwing a fit.

“…”

Was it in great pain and discomfort? Was the human right about it having an upset stomach? The queen felt apologetic.

If the human is right, then it only means that I, who have spent my entire life by your side, had a harder time understanding you than that mere man.

The queen wondered if she had gotten arrogant as she was always with the Kraken and became used to its presence. Had she failed to notice the agony it was in that even strangers could easily see? What if, out of her ignorance, she tried to suppress her precious friend by force? The queen swam over to the Kraken’s side, reflecting on her actions.

“Kru-rururung!”

The Kraken let out a cry, which was now more sorrowful when it felt her near. Their eyes met. She instantly knew.

The Kraken is sorry for all this.

The Kraken’s gaze was filled with remorse. At the same time, it was filled with pain and dismay.

Is what the human said really correct?

Biting her bottom lip, she inched closer to the Kraken and held out her palm with the marine diamond on top.

“Kraken? Do you want this? This is your favorite snack,” the queen carefully said.

Thump! Thump! She hoped that the human would be right. She prayed that her friend would react the way the human said it would. She yearned direly in her heart. Some seconds later, the Kraken froze.

“Krung?” Sniff! Sniff! Sniff! The octopus stopped its violent fit and brought its head closer to the diamond before smelling it. Some seconds later…

“Krung! Kruru… Blargh! Krung!”

It violently retched, even spitting out a bit of stomach acid. And then it resumed its frenzy.

Whoosh! Because of its gigantic size, a small movement from the Kraken covered a wide distance.

“…!“

The queen closely avoided its leg by dolphin-kicking herself away from the spot. At last, she confirmed Lloyd’s suspicion.

Ha… That human was right.

Even on her way here, she had been half in doubt. But upon confirming it herself, he was right. The Kraken showed initial interest in her offering but soon retched. It even burped and spat out stomach acid. The confirmation brought a sense of relief to her heart.

It’s a relief. The Kraken had a reason for this behavior.

She couldn’t have been more shocked at first. The Kraken had never caused such a commotion like the one today throughout the history of the Mermaid Kingdom. It was always a peaceful and gentle creature. So the entire situation threw her into a pit of confusion. That was until now, when she confirmed the reason for its strange behavior was an upset stomach. This joyful realization lifted the weight of confusion from her shoulders. Now, she only had to devise a solution for the issue, and a ray of hope that this unprecedented situation could be handled appeared. Now that the cause behind the incident was found, she wasn’t as helpless anymore.

But…

As the queen fiercely swam away from the Kraken, sadness came to her eyes.

I am sorry, my friend.

She had been ignorant of her friend’s pain. Instead, in her dismay, she almost suppressed it with force. A twinge of guilt for not recognizing her friend’s sadness and difficulty gripped her. Simultaneously, she resolved that she would never let the Kraken be in pain again. She was ready to care for and dispel all the suffering it was going through. There wouldn’t be any challenges or hurdles that could stop her from that goal. Come hell or water, she was ready to relieve the pain of her friend.

Whatever the cost is!

Several drops of tears flowed along the current that appeared as the muscular queen swam by. Hiding her reddened eyes, she came back and asked Lloyd, "You saw the Kraken’s reaction, didn’t you?”

“Yes, of course,” answered Lloyd with a nod. The queen now felt a swelling sense of confidence in this human, who used to be nothing but a frail and scrawny figure in her eyes. She used to even feel pity toward him for having to live with such a weak body. But now, it was different. She couldn’t consider him to be any more reliable. The smile on his face. The confident gaze in his eyes. It all exuded a sense of trustworthiness.

“Do you have a solution to relieve its upset stomach?”

“Very well, I do,” Lloyd answered right away as if he had been waiting for her to ask.

The queen’s face lit up, and she thought to herself that he indeed had a plan. Everything was all good. She didn’t have anything to worry about from now on. Her precious friend wouldn’t be in pain anymore, and nothing would ever hurt it again. She was relieved. Confident. At that moment, she heard Lloyd casually state the solution he thought of.

“The best thing to do when you have an upset stomach is to mercilessly prick your fingertip to let the blood out!”
