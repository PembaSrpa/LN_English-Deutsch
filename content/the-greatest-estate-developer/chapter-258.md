# The Internal Affairs of the Mermaid Kingdom (2)

---

“Of course. Since you are asking, I will grant it.”

With great ease and as if it was the obvious course of action, the mermaid commander flashed a big grin and nodded.

That’s it?!

That really was it. That concluded Javier’s request and gained the mermaid guard commander’s approval. There was nothing else expected from him. Javier didn’t even have to use flowery words while making his request or provide any explanations. Nor did he have to desperately expound his situation or give a solid promise in return. It was all left unsaid. Even the reaction of the mermaid guard commander was simple, as though that was the way it should be. She didn’t bother saying anything more and skipped the entire negotiation process.

Dammit. I can do my best, but I’ll never compare against a handsome bastard…

Lloyd berated his life as a growing sense of insecurity gripped him. If they used his usual method, he’d have needed to go through the tiring process of making countless plans and preparations in order to gain permission to visit the Mermaid Kingdom. But when Javier took charge, the whole process became unnecessary.

What the heck? What the heck was that? Please help us. Yes, I will help you since it’s coming from you, Sir Asrahan. Tee-hee. And that was it?

Lloyd felt bitter. The very core of his soul was dismayed. He jumped into the sea, received the clam necklace for visitors, which enabled underwater breathing, talking, protection from water pressure, decompression sickness prevention, and defense from cold, and was safely guided into the deep part of the sea. And throughout all of it, he had tears saltier than the sea running down his face while he followed Javier.

Well, still, I got what I wanted at the end of the day.

Even though the process was unexpectedly uglier than he thought as he sorely learned how perfect Javier was down to his pores, Lloyd achieved exactly what he wanted.

At last, I’m entering the Mermaid Kingdom.

Lloyd clenched his fists and looked around. He saw Javier swimming by his side and the dozens of muscular mermaids guarding them, as well as the sunlight streaming down from the surface of the water like a curtain. The scene looked like something from an incredible dream.

Ha. Is this why he said he wants to reincarnate as a whale?

Just then, Lloyd remembered the Ghost Frontera, how he wanted to be born as a whale again and swim freely across the vast sea. Lloyd could understand what he was talking about.

Meanwhile, they ruthlessly ventured into deeper water. Darkness crept in until all the sunlight vanished around them. Their view became completely pitch black. Just then, Javier moved his hand.

Bzzt…! The aura at his fingertip lit their surroundings like a lantern, allowing Lloyd to swim further in the pitch darkness. And after they traveled about 6,500 to 9,800 feet down the water, or maybe deeper than that, they arrived at the Mermaid Kingdom.

An octopus?

The first thing that came into Lloyd’s view was a humongous octopus. But not just any kind. The trunk of its body stretched over a few miles long, and he couldn’t picture how gigantic its spread-out legs would be. The largest creatures he had seen until now, the Gigatitan, Bibeong, and the Bone Dragon, would look like a sparrow beside this octopus. Now, it was embracing something that looked like grapes.

Is that… an octopus egg?

At first, Lloyd thought it was the octopus egg he had seen in a TV documentary a few times. But upon closer look, he realized it wasn’t.

It’s a city.

Its brood case was completely empty. No, it was filled with countless buildings.

I wonder if each egg acts as a single living quarter.

In other words, the Mermaid Kingdom was structured like a bunch of grapes embraced by a giant octopus. They were hundreds of square feet large and thousands in number. Could it be that it noticed Lloyd’s admiration?

“It’s all right,” said the mermaid guard commander as she neared Lloyd. “The octopus doesn’t bite. It’s the baby of our kingdom.”

“…”

Their baby? How? Lloyd had a lot of things to say, but he kept silent as they were not kind words. Then they slipped by the trunk of the octopus, safely passing its watchful eye that was as big as a football field. And they entered one egg that served as the entrance to the city before passing and crossing dozens of eggs at the guard commander’s guidance.

“This is where our queen resides. And it’s a rule that visitors must greet the queen and state their business here. And Sir Javier Asrahan?”

“Yes,” said Javier.

“Please have a nice stay here,” said the mermaid with much gentleness.

“Thank you.”

“No, I should be the one to thank you. Well then.”

The guard mermaid had been extremely uptight and cold when she addressed Lloyd. But she was sweet and gentle to Javier. Lloyd scoffed at her.

Tsk. Lookism can go to hell!

He wanted to leave the kingdom as soon as he could because of the unfair treatment he received at the very least. Lloyd hurried his steps and trudged into the audience room of the queen. The queen was massive and muscular.

Lloyd bowed and said, “I express my infinite gratitude for granting us permission for today’s visit. My name is Lloyd Frontera, a human, and I am honored to be in your presence, my queen. Moreover, I traveled all the way here in search of the Jewel of Truth rumored to be here in the Mermaid Kingdom.” Lloyd quickly revealed his reason for visiting here and waited for her response.

The Jewel of Truth must be an incredibly precious jewel here too if I follow the words of the dragon king. So it’s likely that they won’t easily give it out or let me use it. They might set some big conditions. For instance, demanding some sort of help for the kingdom or a large payment. I’m sure that’s what will happen.

That probably was the case. Lloyd was certain that he’d have to negotiate.

Here comes the important part. I need to figure out what the queen wants and lacks.

The start of negotiation would begin with understanding the needs of another party and capitalizing on their deficits. This strategy promised big returns while minimizing the price of the exchange. Lloyd was sure he’d be able to pull it off this time as well. So he put his mind into focus and carefully waited for the queen’s reaction. But the queen’s reaction was nothing like he had expected.

“What?” gasped the queen in dismay. “The Jewel of Truth? I did not know there was still someone naive enough to believe in that these days.”

“Excuse me…?”

The unexpected laughter from her made Lloyd lift his head without thinking and meet her gaze. At that moment, he felt the meaning of her wide smirk. There was something strange about her smile.

What’s that reaction?

An uneasy feeling gripped Lloyd. That wasn’t the reaction he expected.

This feels like seeing a foreign journalist coming to the Incheon airport and revealing his purpose of visiting Korea. To hold the interview with the bear who became a human after eating garlic and mugwort. That’s the way the queen is looking at me. She has the same gaze as that Korean person staring at the foreign journalist.

At first, Lloyd denied it. There was no way, he said. But it turned out to be true. He observed her, and she was indeed looking at him like that.

No way… The Jewel of Truth can’t be a fictional treasure. It can’t really only exist in legends, can it?

Inexplicable anxiety crept up in Lloyd. Legendary objects and treasures existed everywhere across the world in any region. There was Cheonbuin, the three divine items of the Dangun myth, and Gan Jian and Moye from the Warring States Period in China. The Kurikara of the Akalnatha in Buddhism. The Excalibur of King Arthur. The dragon-slaying Ascalon in Christianity. Gungnir of Odin in Northern Europe. Brahmastra of the Indian myth. And there was the case of overpackaged snacks in Korea that appeared to be full but were empty when opened. By and large, all of these legendary items had one commonality. They existed only in the realm of imagination and didn’t exist in real life. Even if they did exist, they did so a bazillion years ago. In other words, they were impossible to find.

Gripped with a bad feeling, Lloyd asked, “May I ask a question to the queen of the great merpeople?”

“Ask,” allowed the queen.

“What you just told me, my queen, about my naivety for believing in the Jewel of Truth… Did you mean that it existed only in stories?”

“What else?”

Although Lloyd’s question was full of nervousness, the queen gave her answer as if that was obvious. She then chuckled and sent a dumbstruck and pitiful gaze at Lloyd.

“You are right,” she said. “The thing about the Jewel of Truth you wish to find is something that I cannot say does not exist. But if you ask me whether I can find it, well. Instead, I want to ask if you belong in a different time period.”

“You mean…”

“The Jewel of Truth exists only in legends and myths even to us,” she continued.

“Agh.” Lloyd choked on some seawater in shock. His uneasy feeling was right.

Dammit! So, it doesn’t exist in real life?

Lloyd was hopeful until he revealed his visiting reason to the queen. But after seeing her reaction, it appeared that this Jewel of Truth was an item of ancient times.

“Um,” went on Lloyd, “Please allow me one more question. Did it ever exist in real life?”

“Um, maybe?” There was uncertainty in her voice.

“What do you mean?” asked Lloyd.

The queen shrugged. “It should be real, I think. But as I told you, perhaps in the mythical ages.”

“Mythical ages…” mumbled Lloyd.

“A shame, isn’t it? You should have been born earlier if you wanted to find it. Don’t you think?”

“…”

Lloyd was utterly speechless. Hearing her, he realized that it wasn’t just the past. It was far back in the mythical ages. So, when he estimated the period through the chronicle mentioned several times in the novel…

It was at least 10,000 years ago.

Lloyd wanted to pass out. The Korean equivalent was even before the time when Dangun founded the Gojoseon. There was no chance of locating an ancient item that hadn’t been seen by the merpeople at the bottom of this vast sea since the mythical ages.

It’s not like I’m an archeologist or anything similar.

Lloyd heaved a sigh. Everything turned bleak for him.

The queen spoke, her voice regretful. “I am sorry that you had to travel all the way down here for such a hopeless answer. But where did you hear the story about the Jewel of Truth? The story is hardly known by those outside of us.”

“Oh… That’s…” Lloyd lifted his head. Berating the very person who taught him about it, Lloyd replied, “From Dragon King Verkis.”

“The dragon king?” shrilled the queen. “Ha. He should be well enough to know about that. And the truth about how it only exists in myths. It appears that you have been played. Is that right?”

“It seems I was in all respects…” Lloyd smiled bitterly. She was right. He had been played by Verkis.

Verkis must have tricked me, so he can send me away. That way I wouldn’t badger him anymore.

Now, Lloyd realized what Verkis did to him. Verkis intentionally gave a cursory, half-hearted answer to get rid of him because he wouldn’t stop pestering the former with questions. And since Lloyd had no way of checking the truth of his claim, he traveled all the way down here thinking it was real.

Haah. What should I do now?

Truth be told, Lloyd wanted to fly back to the Frontera fiefdom at this very moment and grab Verkis by the collar and shake him. But he had put too much effort into coming all the way here.

Plus, she didn’t say that it was all fake.

Maybe. Perhaps. A trace of it could be discovered. Did he think so because he couldn’t move on? It didn’t matter. He had to stay here for a few days and dig around before he left this kingdom.

“My queen of the great mermaids. May I be granted one favor?” said Lloyd politely.

“A favor?”

“Yes. May I be permitted to stay a few days here to search for the Jewel of Truth?”

“Hmm.”

“Please.”

Lloyd looked at her earnestly. The queen looked at him annoyingly. At last, she nodded.

“Well, fine. You won’t be harming the kingdom with that request anyway.”

“Thank you so much!”

“There is no possibility that you will find anything, but in case you do, it only benefits us. Let me make one thing clear. We will not give assistance to your search. Right now, there’s an issue troubling us that needs our attention instead of this miscellaneous matter of yours,” said the queen.

“Pardon? An issue?” Lloyd asked again.

The queen frowned. “Yes, something like that. To summarize, the Gate of Hell suddenly appeared in the Arctic Ocean.”

“…”

Flinch! The back of Lloyd’s neck tightened.

“By any chance, do you know what caused it to open? Did you hear any rumors about it from the human world?”

“No. I didn’t.”

“Really?”

“Yes.” Lloyd’s conscience pricked his heart. But he quietly ignored it and nodded at lightning speed. Deciding to shamelessly layer his face with a thick plate of titanium, he spoke.
