# A Prison Called Lullaby (3)

---

“Whew…”

Inside the reception room of the Frontera manor, Count Frontera sighed at length, but it didn’t necessarily lighten his heart. Acutely feeling so, he turned to the side and gave a look to his wife.

“What about Lloyd?”

“I called him.”

Countess Frontera answered him through her gaze. And their eyes toward each other slowly moved to the front, where a foreign woman was nonchalantly sitting across from the table.

“Whew…”

He sighed again at the sight of her. Count Frontera mused in agony on the possible things Lloyd did and went through in the sultan’s kingdom for the woman to say those words. And so, the count dearly wished for Lloyd to quickly return home and change this awkward atmosphere. Thankfully, his wish came true soon.

Knock knock knock. Someone knocked on the door. It was Lloyd. At last.

“You wanted to see me?”

“Come on in.”

Count Frontera jumped to his feet and personally opened the door himself, studying his son’s reaction.

“Eh?”

Lloyd stopped as he entered when he saw her. His eyes widened slightly. Count Frontera wondered if it was that of surprise. Or was it because of something else? But soon, the count was able to spot something while observing Lloyd. He caught his son’s faint smile and the words he uttered to himself under his breath.

“I guess this is your final choice.”

“…”

Final? This choice? The count pondered what they could possibly mean. With a mildly anxious heart, he took his seat and asked Lloyd to do the same.

“Have a seat.”

“Yes.”

Lloyd sat on the sofa. But his seating choice was strange. He didn’t choose to sit beside the count or the woman. He sat in between them and looked from one side to the other.

“You must have called for me because of her, right?” wondered Lloyd aloud at the count.

“Yes… I did.”

A sense of relief soon came to the count when he saw how his son was barely shocked or flustered by the woman’s presence. Lloyd didn’t seem to have caused trouble in the sultan’s kingdom.

But I cannot relax just yet.

The count told himself that he knew nothing about what had happened yet. For that reason, he slowly opened his mouth to speak while trying his best not to hurt his son’s feelings.

“The thing is… Um… My dear Lloyd, can you honestly answer your old man’s question?”

“Yes. Of course,” assured Lloyd with an eased expression.

“Then, do you know this woman?”

“Yes, we know each other.”

“Then… did you, um, cause any trouble for her?”

“Trouble?” mused Lloyd. “I can’t say.”

Lloyd then chuckled. He was enjoying breakfast when he was called here out of the blue, but he wasn’t angry. He was rather happy. Sheherazade finally came to him, and now, he could tie the future sword master down to his fiefdom. Satisfaction swelled in him like a farmer staring at a well-ripe harvest. But there was one small thing that he was curious about.

“Did the woman say I caused any trouble for her?”

What could Sheherazade have possibly said? What did she say to make the count and countess fidget in their seats earlier?

She must have said something off.

Sheherazade was sitting calmly. But the count and the countess couldn’t stay still. It looked like they had a concern that was bothering them but couldn’t muster the courage to say it out loud.

Lloyd plastered on the smile of a dutiful son. Then in a reassuring voice, he said, “I think there is a misunderstanding here. Please tell me, and I will clear it up.”

“Hmm…”

The count and countess looked at each other and exchanged a troubled gaze. The latter nudged the count to speak, and he nodded with a look of reluctance. Lloyd thought that the count looked like he had taken a bullet for her. Just when he thought so, the count opened his mouth, his cheeks slightly red.

“Um, how should I say this…? You see, um, this lady here says…”

His face burned red.

“You see, um, she said she can no longer fall asleep without you.”

There. He said it out loud.

My goodness. How embarrassing this is…

Count Frontera lifted the teacup as he nibbled his lips. Countess Frontera held his hand tight and sent a gaze that seemed to say, “Good job, dear.” As for Lloyd…

“Oh, that’s true,” tonelessly admitted Lloyd.

Pfft!

The count spat out the tea, and Lloyd shrugged.

“It is true. She can no longer go to sleep without me. But she left out all the details, so it’s not what you think it is.”

Cough! Cough! “Meaning? What do you mean?” The count spluttered out.

Lloyd handed him a handkerchief and said, “She means my lullaby. You know, right? How I recite a lullaby to Sir Asrahan.”

“…”

They knew about the lullaby. Somewhat. The story of how Javier secretly had miserable insomnia until he was cured by Lloyd’s recital of vast knowledge was a secret shared only with the count and countess. Now that the coughing fit had subsided, the count pointed at Sheherazade.

“Wait, does she have…”

“Yes, something similar to what Sir Asrahan had,” clarified Lloyd.

“Is that why you sang a lullaby to her? Just like you did with Sir Asrahan?”

“Yes. Thanks to me, she was able to sleep soundly at night. But the insomnia must have crept back now that I returned home. Didn’t it?”

Lloyd’s question was directed to Sheherazade, who nodded after staying silent until now.

“You are right.”

The corners of Lloyd’s lips rose.

“Then that cheesy and creepy remark about how you can’t sleep without me at night must have been intentional.”

“Of course.”

Sheherazade nodded again and looked straight at Lloyd.

“I figured it was the quickest way to see you.”

Indeed. Sheherazade actually arrived at the Frontera fiefdom the day before. Her plan yesterday was extremely simple. Meet Lloyd as fast as possible and record his lullaby in her expensive magic orb.

I meant to return to Ahinsya straight away after achieving my goal.

Of course, that part, going back to Ahinsya, didn’t change even right now. Her sole focus was on the lullaby. She only needed to obtain that. It wasn’t a particularly difficult task either. She had a magical device that cost as much as a mansion but could record about 20 minutes of sound. And so, it could be used to record Lloyd reciting his lullaby. She only needed to set up a clandestine meeting with Lloyd. Away from everyone’s gaze, her visit would remain secret. It was simple. She would just quietly approach Lloyd to request a recording. However, it turned out she was wrong.

The reality was a bit different.

Sheherazade shook her head as she thought about the previous day. Right after she arrived, she realized that there was one big flaw in her plan.

Javier Asrahan. It was his fault.

That silver-haired knight had stuck to Lloyd all day! So, it was impossible to get close to Lloyd at all. And she couldn’t think of a way to outwit the sword master’s senses. Heck, the mere act of observing them was risky itself. As such, she scrapped her plan altogether and decided to approach Lloyd head-first. After racking her brain all night, she came to the manor during the early morning and let out an explosive remark that garnered everyone’s attention. And here she was, finally meeting Lloyd face-to-face.

“Tsk, what a foul temper you have.”

Lloyd’s smile became wryer. He turned to the count and countess, who now seemed aware of their misunderstanding. There was a look of ease on their faces as they listened to his conversation.

“So,” Lloyd asked Sheherazade, “I suppose you want my lullaby?”

“Yes.”

“And you are not thinking of taking me back to your kingdom.”

“Of course not.”

Sheherazade put on a straight face and fished a plum-sized transparent orb from her pocket.

“This is a magical recording device. It can record about 20 minutes of sound.”

“So, you plan on recording my lullaby, huh?” mused Lloyd.

“Yes.” Sheherazade nodded and looking straight into Lloyd’s eyes, she added, “I am not asking for much. Just 20 minutes. One lullaby is all I want.”

“Really?” said Lloyd as he stroked his jaw.

“Yes.”

“Hmm, but too bad.”

Lloyd grinned. He slyly cocked his head to the side and spoke.

“Nothing is free in the world.”

“…”

“Did you really think that I would hand over my lullaby for free? How naive of you.”

“But I came all the way here-”

“Come on. I didn’t call you here. Why does it matter to me how long it took you to come here?” huffed Lloyd.

“…”

Sheherazade was speechless. Lloyd made sense, sure. But she wanted to punch his face out of spite. That was when he brought something out of his pocket as he smiled even more nonchalantly. He opened the article up on the table.

“What is it?” asked Sheherazade.

“It’s an employment contract,” informed Lloyd.

“Excuse me?”

Sheherazade couldn’t believe her ears just now. She thought she heard it wrong at first. An employment contract? Why bring that out now? Regardless of her confusion, Lloyd sounded completely nonchalant as he continued to speak.

“I thought you wanted to record my lullaby,” queried Lloyd, almost teasingly. “Isn’t it to improve your sleep? That’s why you traveled this far. Right?”

“Yes, that’s right, but…”

An unsettling feeling swirled in her. She nodded, but her eyes were filled with distrust. Her uneasiness only continued to grow, and just then, Lloyd spitefully started to smile.

“Then you have to pay for it. So, please sign here.”

“…”

No way! She rolled her eyes down, her heart sinking, and studied the contract on the table. Words of despair crawled out of her mouth in spite of herself.

“Enlistment in the Civil Engineering Corps? Participate in various constructions for the Frontera fiefdom and work in compliance with the given regulations? Wait, are you saying I should do this?” asked Sheherazade, shell-shocked.

“Yes,” replied Lloyd matter-of-factly. He nodded and smiled as if she asked an obvious question. “Stay and work here for just five years.”

“…”

“Then I will record your lullaby.”

“…”

What the? Sheherazade gritted her teeth. To this, Lloyd lifted his brows.

“I know,” he went on, “who you are. But think about it. Do you think that you are being forced into something that doesn’t suit you? You know that is terribly incorrect.”

“Wrong thinking? How?”

“It can’t be that you think you’re above shoveling and pickaxing, right?”

“Well, that’s…”

“Do you really think so?”

“…”

Sheherazade hurriedly clamped her mouth. Her heart sank. She had not given much thought about the matter. It hardly even crossed her mind. But now, she realized that she vaguely had those thoughts. The contract stated that the unit was mainly tasked with digging the ground for construction. And the suggestion to enlist in that unit meant she’d have to shovel and pickaxe at the worksite. And who was she? The princess of the sultan kingdom.

That’s ridiculous.

The mere suggestion itself was blatantly offensive and rude. But she couldn’t be forthright about it because of Lloyd’s strange manner of talking.

“Please think about it. Do you think shoveling is too low for you? Is it a job that’s only fit for peasants? You can’t be suggesting that this kind of physical labor should be looked down upon, right?”

“…”

“These are necessary jobs for our livelihoods,” Lloyd said proudly. “A construction cannot be done without a shovel. Without it, there are no paved roads or bridges that allow us to cross rivers. But that’s not all. That sword you hold, your jewels, and so many tools that you enjoy are produced using the minerals unearthed with a shovel and pickaxe.”

“…”

“Despite all of this, you can’t be saying that this job is beneath you. Right?”

“Of course-”

“Right?”

“No…”

At last, she agreed with Lloyd. All of his words made complete sense. She couldn’t find a way to refute him. But she wasn’t going to sign the contract so easily.

“How about I pay for it instead?” suggested her.

“Excuse me?”

“Instead of signing the contract, I’ll give you money. A generous amount at that. What do you think?”

“I don’t need money,” rejected Lloyd coolly, not even batting an eye.

“…”

“My fiefdom has enough money now. The sultan gave us more than enough along with silk and pieces of pottery.”

“…”

“So, you should sign the contract.”

“…”

Sheherazade stared at the contract with a despairing gaze. In the end, there were two choices for her. Return home and live with insomnia. Or throw away her pride and sign a five-year work contract. She would have to carefully make her decision. Nibbling her lips, she hesitated. Lloyd chuckled.

“All right,” said Lloyd, sensing her hesitation. “It must be difficult to come up with an answer right now. I’ll give you some time to think.”

“You will let me do that?”

“Yes, stay here for a few days as our guest, and think it over.”

“…”

She welcomed the suggestion as she thought it was too cruel of Lloyd to ask for an immediate answer. So, she hurriedly nodded. That marked the beginning of her contemplation. She pondered about it, absolutely engrossed with the choices in front of her, through her stay in the Frontera manor. Even when she ate decent meals, lay against the cushion of a VIP room, and strolled all day around in indecision, she grimaced, a frown never leaving her face.

And so, that must be how it happened. On the fifth day of her stay, she decided to take a stroll farther out as her heart felt heavy with frustration. She was sighing and walking along the solitary path in the woods outside the border of the fiefdom when she bumped into an unexpected figure.

“Eh…?”

The second son of Count Frontera. Sheherazade unexpectedly met Julian Frontera, who was coming back home.
