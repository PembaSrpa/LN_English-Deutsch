# A Prison Called Lullaby (1)

---

“Bibeong!”

The booming alarm in the lake of the eastern mountain range echoed through the land. The morning began in the Frontera county at Bibeong’s voice. Lloyd’s eyes flew open as well.

“Haha.”

His bedroom came into view as soon as he opened them. It was not fancy like the annex hall in the royal palace or the reception hall of the sultan kingdom. But it was cozy and familiar. Lloyd dreadfully missed this bedroom during the last six months of his stay in the sultan’s kingdom.

“Master Lloyd, are you awake?”

As soon as he made the sound, a careful voice spoke from the other end of the door. It, too, was familiar, and he was glad to hear it.

“Yeah, come in.”

The door opened up slowly, and Emily, a maid, poked her head out and beamed at Lloyd.

“I brought you breakfast.”

“Thank you. Have you been waiting long?”

“No, just a little.”

“Oh no,” remarked Lloyd. “You must have waited for a long time.”

“No, I really didn’t wait for long. It is all right,” she hurriedly said.

Lloyd was even happy to smell the breakfast on the tray Emily brought. A light bean stew with shredded chicken, a wheat loaf of bread, two sausages, a soft-boiled egg, a chunk of goat cheese, and a cup of warm milk. It was a modest breakfast for a noble.

However, this was Lloyd’s favorite breakfast meal.

This is a buffet compared to my days in the room rental.

Lloyd didn’t like to have an overly fancy breakfast. It placed a burden on his stomach and heart. So, this was just enough.

“Thank you. It looks great,” thanked Lloyd with a smile.

“I’m glad, Young Master. I’ll come to take the dishes once you’re done eating.”

“Yeah.”

“Oh, right.”

Emily stopped when she was about to leave after putting down the tray. She turned Lloyd’s way and beamed at him again.

“It’s a relief that you got back safely.”

“Sure…”

Emily then scooted out of the room before Lloyd could reply. He stared for a moment at the door. Is this what being a popular kid felt like? This feeling of being welcomed by everyone. He didn’t dislike it. No, to be honest, he liked it.

That’s why I love it here.

A smile escaped from Lloyd. Just then, he was hit with a memory of when he first came here. As though he was a monster or an annoying pest, everyone he met gave him a cold gaze. Their gazes from them revealed their disdain and desire to avoid interaction and stay away from him. But now, those gazes changed into a warm, hearty welcome. And who did it? Lloyd himself.

I worked hard until now.

There wasn’t any grand reason behind their change of attitude. It was the fruit of his hard work and endeavor. So, from now on, he wanted to live the rest of his life tasting the sweet fruits of his blood, sweat, and tears.

I can do it.

He had solved countless issues during his time here. He paid off the county’s debt, prevented the Monster Domino effect, accommodated the refugees, prevented an epidemic, raised a generous amount of funds, and avoided war with the sultan kingdom.

All those issues were taken care of. By who? Me. So, it’s time to relax now. Farewell to my sickening days of work. I’m going to spend the rest of my life lazing around like a landlord. I just need to stay clear of the queen. I’ll have to think of a plan to make sure she won’t call me.

Lloyd lifted his fork with his new resolution. He then started to enjoy his modest breakfast, savoring his first day of retirement. In the morning, he went out for a stroll. The warm and bubbly sunlight of the early summer pleased him. On the way, he met a couple of welcoming faces. The rebels of the sultan kingdom, who defected and were now residents of the Frontera fiefdom.

Termes and his rebel executives.

“I am glad that everyone arrived safely.”

They shared with him that they had come here about five days earlier and that they managed to safely cross the desert and secretly pass the border at the protection and guidance of Turtle Neck and others, even though the journey was slowed by their families. After that short chat, Lloyd’s first day in retirement continued to go by without a hitch. Whereas his morning was filled with wonderful people, his afternoon was filled with something even better, gift packages.

“What… is all this?”

About a little past lunchtime, dozens of carts trundled into the Frontera fiefdom, all of which were packed with large chests. The carts were so full that it looked like their wheels would pop off. Count Frontera’s jaw dropped at the sight. Just then…

“Mister Lloyd Frontera? Are you here?”

A man who was leading the cart in the very front yelled as he stood at the gate. Lloyd rushed at his calling.

“Here! On my way!”

Lloyd swiftly accepted the packages with great excitement. Once he signed the receipt, the workers immediately started to lower the chests off the carts.

“Lloyd, what is all this?” Count Frontera stuttered.

A satisfying smile hung on Lloyd’s face. He answered, “My packages. No, the delivery of a valuable shipment.”

“Shipment?”

“Yes.”

Lloyd nodded and enumerated the items that made the count’s ears twitch in disbelief.

“Pottery, silk, carpets, spices, and what else did I order? Anyway, they’re all specialty products of Ahinsya, the capital of the sultan kingdom.”

“Ahinsya’s… specialty products?” stuttered the count.

“Yes.”

“Who in the world sent these to us?” There was still a tremor in the count’s voice.

“I did,” said Lloyd proudly.

“Huh?”

“I sent them here while I was in Ahinsya,”

The smile on Lloyd’s face deepened, and he briefly narrated the story of how he splurged the sultan’s money.

“And so, I hired a cargo ship and commissioned these shipments.”

“To come here after passing through Port Cremo?”

“Yes, Come to think of it, I really made the right choice. I must have picked a good ship, I was worried that the goods wouldn’t arrive on time because of the long distance.”

“…”

Lloyd beamed nonchalantly at the count, who was too dumbstruck to speak. The mountain of shipments was filled with pottery, silk, and other specialty items from the east?

“Then, what do you want me to do?”

The count swallowed as he pulled himself together. Lloyd was quick to reply as though he had been waiting for the question,

“Please look for merchants in the capital, Cremo, Namaran, and other areas nearby. Of course, it doesn’t matter if we can’t sell everything at once.”

“You are right.”

The count nodded, He could see the impressive amount of products with just a glance, Could they all be sold at the same time? He thought it was unlikely.

It would be nice if one buyer could purchase all of this. But even a fairly large merchant won’t have enough money to buy everything.

Moreover, there was a limit to the demand for specialty goods, especially high-end ones. This meant that there was a fixed pool of consumers who could purchase these products. And so, it was impossible to sell them all at once.

“I see what you mean. You can leave this job to your father.”

“Of course. However, it doesn’t matter if we slowly sell everything bit by bit, so please don’t overwork yourself.”

“Very well.”

The count nodded as he patted Lloyd on the shoulder. Lloyd could sense that the count was proud. A smile appeared on his face. He then received the items and asked the Skeleton Corps to stack them up in the storage. That took a whole day.

“Woah. I finally feel alive. Right?”

Lloyd asked behind as he walked out of the storage. Javier, who was silent for the whole day, was there. But strangely, he didn’t answer the question given to him.

“What’s wrong? Did something happen?”

“…”

There was something off about his facial expression. It was the face of a person who stepped on dog poop with their new, expensive shoes that still had their tag on. Javier’s gaze grew mischievous as he looked at Lloyd.

“Tsk. I can tell something happened. Am I right?”

“…”

“What is it?” demanded Lloyd. “Did I do something wrong again?”

“Yes.”

An answer finally. The corners of Lloyd’s lips rose up.

“I did something wrong? What is it this time?” Lloyd questioned with hands in the air.

“…”

“Seeing how you’re still silent, it looks like you’re really upset-”

“Master Lloyd, your lullaby. I did not know it was free.”

Under the yellowing dusk, Javier cocked his head to one side. Rough. And he glowered at Lloyd.

“I believed that your lullaby was an expensive service, considerably so.”

“What do you mean?”

“It is about the woman.” Javier finally got to the point.

“Sheherazade?” queried Lloyd.

“Yes.”

Javier nodded. His glare grew more ferocious.

“Recall the first day we arrived at Kandara. This is what you told me right after you sang a lullaby to Miss Sheherazade. That your lullaby is not free. That I am ignorant of how much you plan to rip her off.”

“Yes, I did,” Lloyd said matter-of-factly.

“Then what is this?”

“What is?”

“You did not make that woman pay for anything.” There was stifled anger in his voice.

“You think so?” Lloyd asked.

“Yes.”

As the two walked, Javier continued to speak bitterly.

“It can’t be that you’re simply counting the help you received from the rebels while she was asleep as her payment?”

“Of course not.”

“Then what did you receive from her?” Javier, walking briskly, demanded an answer.

“Her future.”

“What…?”

The nonchalant remark from Lloyd made Javier pause. Lloyd turned his way with a meaningful smile on his face.

“Let me say this once again. My lullaby? It’s expensive. I’m holding her future as security.”

“What do you mean…?”

Javier tilted his head in confusion, trying to make sense of what he had just heard.

“Do you possibly mean that she will sacrifice her future for your lullaby?” assumed Javier.

“Yeah, roughly.” Lloyd nodded his head. “She has amazing talent. You should know since you fought with her. Do you agree with me?”

“Yes.”

"More precisely, give me your feelings on her skills.”

“She can be a sword master in 20 years.”

“Yes, I think so too.”

Lloyd grinned. It was true. Javier’s judgment was correct, and it was how the novel had described Sheherazade. Her talent was on par with Queen Magentano, and she was set to become a sword master in 20 years.

But she fails to fully develop her talent.

The reason was simple. She died before that happened.

Cornered by the rebels, she fought to protect the sultan until the end. Hoping even in the last moment that her father would acknowledge her. She risked herself even when she could have retreated.

And so, she died in vain and never got the chance to reach her full potential. But now, the story had changed due to the Qanat Lloyd built.

The rebellion is not going to happen anymore. The Qanat solved the drought in the Kandahar region. People are no longer dissatisfied. So, the sultan won’t be surrounded by the rebels anymore, and Sheherazade won’t die protecting her father.

In short, Sheherazade would live. She would live a stable life until she became a sword master in 20 years.

“So.”

Lloyd spoke, his voice full of finality.

“There’s a person who will become a sword master within the next 20 years just across the border in a country that could potentially become our enemy one day. Moreover, she’s the daughter of the sultan. She might wield her sword toward us in the future. Do you think it’s right to leave her as she is?” demanded Lloyd.

“Um…”

"We have to make a move.”

“How?”

“We can bring her over to our side, or nip her potential in the bud if we can’t have her.”

Lloyd’s smile became slightly more vicious.

“Sure, right now, we’re on good terms with the sultan. But there’s no guarantee that will be true in ten or twenty years in the future. The sultan kingdom has the potential to be our enemy. That’s why I sang a lullaby for her every night while I was there.”

“You conditioned her…”

“Oh, did you realize that already?” said Lloyd as his brows lifted up.

“You did the same thing to me,” stated Javier.

“Yes, you are correct.”

Lloyd shrugged, then continued to speak.

“She listened to my lullaby for over four months. During that time, she was able to peacefully sleep at night. But now, my lullaby was suddenly taken away from her. She must be terribly flustered since she can’t sleep anymore.”

It was true. She was probably tormented by the abrupt return of her insomnia.

“She probably regrets ever learning about my lullaby. Why? She knows what she’s missing now. She’s discovered the comfort it brings. So, she can never go back. She’s changed from when she could overcome sleep deprivation with sheer will.”

“You… killed her willpower, which was a weapon that would defeat her sword master syndrome,” concluded Javier.

“Yeah.”

Lloyd nodded as if he was making a declaration. It was true. This principle could be applied to anything. It was hard to tolerate discomfort knowing it could be resolved. Once someone experienced what it was like to own a remote controller, they couldn’t live without it when watching TV. A family could be happy with an electric fan until they felt the cool air of an air conditioner. There’s no going back. The same was the case for furnaces, cars, elevators, the internet, and smartphones.

That’d be even more accurate in regard to sleep which directly affects the body.

Sheherazade, who was suffering from sword master syndrome, wouldn’t be an exception. Previously, she would have endured the constant torture from her insomnia, thinking she had no other choice. She would have gradually increased her swordsmanship level through training amid such a handicap. However, she found out about Lloyd’s lullaby and the tremendous comfort it offered.

“So, she can never go back,” assured Lloyd. “She won’t be able to forcefully push herself like she used to. I mean, she could come close using an extraordinary show of power, but that’s going to take a very long time.”

“Do you mean that we can expect a delay in her progress as a sword master?” asked Javier.

“Yeah. That’s if she doesn’t come here.”

“Are you serious?”

“Uh-huh.”

“Master Lloyd, you are..” Javier paused, holding back.

“Go on. You don’t have to hold back,” permitted Lloyd.

“…”

The corners of Lloyd’s lips rose as he looked at Javier. It was probably his expression that prompted Javier to blurt out the worst insult he knew that had been suppressed inside him until now.
