# Demons Must Be Abused (3)

---

Hell strangely reminded Lloyd of Seoul. It was especially so now as Lloyd flew around the area on Ggoming’s back.

Survey.

[Scanning will begin.]

Lloyd’s gaze traveled down, and beneath the wide-spread Ggoming’s wings, he saw the whole land span out.

“…”

Of course, Hell was different from Seoul. There were active volcanos erupting everywhere with pools of ketchup-like lava spilled out of their sides. The air surrounding him was full of heat. One glance around and the whole landscape didn’t have any resemblance to the capital of Korea.

Yet, there is one similarity. It’s that it’s hard to breathe.

Volcanic ash flew everywhere in the air, reminding Lloyd of Seoul’s fine dust. And the musty scent of sulfur reminded him of the gas from a car engine exhaust. But then, of course, this place was 30, 40, or possibly 50 times more deadly and pungent than Seoul.

But it’s making me a bit nostalgic.

Lloyd wondered whether the woman in the first-floor gimbap restaurant in his room rental was doing well. Was the manager, who used to pick on him from time to time but took care of him, still struggling to pass the civil service examination?

Ah. Stop thinking about it.

Any further thoughts would just bring strange emotions to him. He wondered if he was starting to lose it as he was romanticizing this terrible place with a twinge of nostalgia. Pulling himself together, Lloyd focused on surveying.

The terrain of the land isn’t too rough to my relief, ignoring the lava.

He was currently surveying a plain here. The topography of Hell was surprisingly sleek. There weren’t any excessively steep or jagged mountains. It was hard to find sharp cliffs and basins as well. Instead, the most common terrain was a flatland abundant with volcanic ash.

It must be due to all the volcanic activity.

There wasn’t any time for the mountains to become taller. The volcanoes erupted and crumbled as soon as they showed signs of growing. The basins and valleys, even if they existed, were quickly filled with lava and volcanic ash. Because of these reasons, the overall terrain was flat. It seemed the construction would go fairly smoothly, thanks to it.

We don’t have to dig any tunnels through mountains to lay the railroad or bridges over the valleys.

Just one straight railroad on the flatland. But what about hundreds of lava ponds intermittently spread throughout the whole place?

Just have to evade them with skill.

Just like that, Lloyd surveyed the flatland of Hell, never forgetting to look 16 feet underground as well. He meticulously studied the areas with relatively lesser volcanic ash and without lava influx. And he connected them in one route, which extended gradually from the edge of Hell to the Hell Castle, reaching 68 miles in total. And then he came back to the edge and arrived at the camp where he imprisoned the demon slaves.

Haah.

Lloyd hopped off Ggoming’s back and shook his head. He had missed sleep for three days straight, having to tightly tense his eyes and focus on surveying with the help of the Asrahan Core Technique. Although he was able to acquire the data for the entire route of the railroad, Lloyd felt a heavy weight of fatigue as if his eyes were soaked in cement.

"You are here,” said Javier when he saw Lloyd.

“I am here. Can I have some water?”

“Here it is.”

Lloyd gulped down the lukewarm hot spring water Javier held out. Just then, he became curious. “Hey.”

“Yes.” Javier looked at Lloyd.

“I hope you didn’t desperately sit out here all day until I came back.”

“Please do not say such chilling words,” snapped Javier with a slight frown. “Of course, I did not.”

“Then how did you come out knowing I arrived?”

“I just felt it,” Javier replied.

“You felt it?” Lloyd arched his brow.

“Yes, since about five minutes ago.”

“Could you mean…”

“Yes, you are most likely right,” affirmed Javier. “I heard Sir Ggoming’s fluttering his wings.”

“…”

“That is how I realized you returned and thus came out to escort you.”

“…”

“Why are you not saying anything?” Javier’s gaze was fixed on Lloyd. He was confused.

“…”

“Master Lloyd?” Javier called.

“Yeah,” Lloyd blurted, blinking.

“What are you doing? You are staring at me without saying anything.”

“Oh, did you not hear my voice?”

“Excuse me?” Javier retorted.

“I was mumbling something very softly. I was badmouthing you.”

This time, it was Javier who didn’t respond.

“You didn’t hear what I said, right?”

“I do not wish to know what you said about me. However…”

“However what?” hurried Lloyd.

“At the very least, I know that those ill-spoken words contain your envy toward me.”

“What did you say?”

“I am taller than you,” stated Javier with a smug.

“…”

“And as for my face? There’s no comparison between us.”

“…”

“Swordsmanship, too.”

“Moreover, I am a better singer than you.”

“Wait!” Javier’s provocation invited Lloyd to stop him right there. “But I’ve never seen you sing before up to now.”

“No matter what, even if I lose my vocal cords or sing with my toenail, I am confident that I can sing better than you.”

“…”

And Javier smirked, looking straight into Lloyd’s eyes.

“…”

What is this feeling? Lloyd wondered. He felt like he just fired a plastic bullet, only to be shot back with an explosive cannonball. Lloyd shook off this growing feeling of shame with a wry laugh.

“Well, anyway, how were the demons while I was gone?” Lloyd asked, regaining composure.

“They were relatively well-mannered.”

“Really?” That was a surprise, Lloyd thought as he chuckled. “I expected them to come up with all sorts of tricks and schemes to get out of here.”

“Of course,” affirmed Javier, “they tried the very first day you left to survey.”

“They did?”

“Yes, there was one that tried to sneak out of the camp, and another attempted to raise a riot by stirring up the other demons.”

“So?” Lloyd asked. “Were you able to suppress them all?”

“Yes.”

“You didn’t kill them, did you?”

“No. However…” A faint smile spread across Javier’s face. “I had a little fun with them.”

“You had fun with them?” Lloyd lifted his brows.

“Yes, I had been curious about what style of swordsmanship was popular in Hell.”

“…”

“I was very curious, but not anymore.”

“…”

What a terrifying and dreadful bastard. Lloyd inwardly paid tribute to the demons who became Javier’s training partners and were endlessly abused.

“Good job,” Lloyd sincerely encouraged Javier, “Keep abusing them.”

“Are you serious?”

“Of course I am. Set the order right when you have the chance.”

Javier seemed pleased to hear that. “For how long should I do it?”

“Until I am done with designing, and we start the construction.”

“Understood.”

From that day onward, Lloyd busied himself with designing while Javier focused on abusing the demons. As days progressed, the planning sheet for design became jam-packed with construction plans. And the demons were put to intense training at Javier’s command. This prompted the demons to make an earnest prayer in their hearts.

Ha… Ha! Whew! Ha… Please finish the construction design or whatever it is faster!

At Javier’s command, they rolled forward. And rolled backward. When they moved the wrong way, they were forced into a fierce one-on-one sword fight with the grandmaster.

And so, Lloyd gradually completed his design as the demons sweated buckets from their raid training. He then drew up an overall track plan and conducted a field survey following what he wrote. After drawing the track plan on a 1/50,000 scale, he worked on the cross-section plan after cutting the track every 164 feet. Lastly, he conducted the conclusion survey, and the final process, and then drew another track plan. This time, it was 1/1,000 in scale. That was the basic design process, and it was comparatively simple as the train wouldn’t stop at any station.

I’m done.

After organizing the cross-sectional track plan in 65 feet intervals, he smiled contently. The design was finally finished, but it wasn’t the time to celebrate. With the completed plan, it was time to jumpstart the construction. So, Lloyd knuckled down with the railroad construction, and his evil instinct as an employer shined out of him. 

“Over there! Group 11! I can see your shovel! Group 13! Didn’t I tell you to dig up a new lava path before the day is over?! Group 14! Why are you taking a break when I never permitted you one?”

This construction project involved building a roadbed at the edge of Hell. A roadbed was a sort of passage that was built for railroads to be laid out on. It was a common sight to see trains traveling on slightly elevated ground instead of ordinary land. That was a roadbed, to put it simply.

Roadbeds are important. They serve as the basis for all railroads and should be well-built. That way, a railroad can stay stable and won’t get waterlogged or sink down.

Lloyd chose to use sand piles for most of the roadbeds. It was an inevitable choice given the lava-filled environment of Hell.

It’s an obvious choice. There’s no way to know when the lava will overflow.

Of course, the centipede shells that would be used for the railroad didn’t melt. But as for the ground underneath, that was another problem.

To give a description, this area is similar to a swamp. The difference is the flowing lava in the ground, which is much stickier than water.

What would happen if the lava flowed over the railroad? It could lose a rail or tie from the lateral pressure as a result. Even if that didn’t happen by pure luck, the lava covering the railroad could also become a problem by solidifying. In any case, an uncontrolled lava flow would wreak havoc and halt the train operation.

So, the roadbed has to be sufficiently elevated.

Lloyd elevated the bed as high as he could to ensure that it would prevent most flows of lava from reaching the railroad. To his relief, Lloyd had plenty of material to build the bed. The volcanic ash was piled all around the area.

“Hey! I can see your shovels! What did I tell you about flattening volcanic ash with your shovels?” bawled Lloyd.

“Spray on the hot spring water until it gets sticky. Then tap down!”

The demons in the construction site yelled in unison.

“You told us to tap as fast as we can until our shovels turn into a blur!”

“Yes, that’s right!” Lloyd cried out. “I told you. Tap! Tap!”

“Yes!”

“Then why am I seeing shovels?”

The demons didn’t know what to say to that.

“Tap until your shovels can’t be seen!” screamed Lloyd. “Tap until your eyelashes and nose hairs sway! Tap down until your shoulders fall off!”

“Yes, Master!” The demons yelled. Everyone was on the verge of collapsing from exhaustion. But Lloyd didn’t give any mercy to them. No, the more tired they became, Lloyd pushed them to work even harder. Of course, they didn’t get to get off work either.

Huff, huff! “H-help me…”

“Argh… I am thirsty… I want a glass of strawberry lava juice…”

“Uh… When did we start working?”

“About ten days ago, I think…”

“And we never took a single break? Is this for real?”

“It’s real… I think…”

“Just kill us now.”

“Eh, die alone, leave me out of it…”

“What did you say?”

The demons, at times, lamented their own misfortune. Otherwise, they let off steam by lashing out at each other. But all of them worked diligently, following Lloyd’s instructions without any complaint. Why?

“Whew, but you know that human, Lloyd Frontera. I feel a bit drawn to him the more time I spend experiencing his evilness.”

“What?” a demon gasped. “Are you nuts?”

“Come on. That’s the truth. Who would have known he’d work us so hard like this?”

“Well, that’s true, but-”

“Hey, that’s why you’re not even trying to run away.”

“Hey!” the accused demon shouted defensively. “I tried once and almost got killed!”

“So what? It’s not like you to stop after just one near-death escape.”

“…”

“See? You do have a good impression of him.”

“What do you mean? I made a good impression? On him?”

“Yeah.”

“What impression?”

Lloyd’s fan arched his brows. “You want to learn his evil and sly ways of life. Like I do.”

“…”

“Ha… To be honest, I don’t want to do this. Dammit, I’m a demon for Pete’s sake. I hate admiring a human’s evilness and pettiness and wanting to learn from him. But…” The grumbling demon heaved a sigh, “I still have to learn. A great demon is always prepared to learn.”

“Wow, that was a long excuse,” accused its comrade.

“You can say I’m making an excuse, but I don’t care. As long as I can become a demon that properly dishonors their parents.”

“…”

“Eh, what’s the point of this conversation? Let’s shovel now. Whew! Crap!”

“You’re right. Crap!”

Similarly, most demons knew in their hearts that Lloyd was prettier, slyer, eviler, and more stubborn than themselves. And so, they all decided to learn from this opportunity and stay by Lloyd’s side doing construction work for as long as possible. They would observe, study, reflect on, and copy his actions to become a more wicked demon. Their hearts burned with the desire to develop themselves and gave all to their shoveling.

“Hey!” shouted Lloyd. “Stop chatting and use that energy to shovel!”

Encouraged by Lloyd’s merciless nagging and frightened by Javier’s watch, they constructed roadbeds one section after another. About three months later, a sturdy 68-mile-long bed was built from the edge of Hell. The number of slopes and curves was kept minimal. Each car wasn’t meant to travel on more than three elevated slopes, and each slope was to be less than 1.8 miles long. Everything was designed in accordance with Lloyd’s careful and deliberate plan.

Good.

In no time, the 68-mile roadbed started to show its final outline. Lloyd clenched his fists as he looked down at it from the sky. The construction was going better than he expected. It was all thanks to the hard work of 30,000 demons, all of whom almost never slacked off. Interestingly, these demons were more cooperative when he treated them harshly and without any show of mercy.

Perverts.

Lloyd landed Ggoming, smiling wryly. His smile widened with a streak of greater satisfaction when he knuckled down to inspect the bed constructed today.

It’s flawless.

It was near perfection, precisely meeting all of his size and durability requirements.

The project will hopefully continue as it is now. Once the bed is completed, it’ll be time to build the tracks.

Then the construction would be over. Next, he would simply inform the King of Hell how to maintain and operate the railroad before receiving his payment. With that, he’d finally be able to liberate the soul of Anticus. 

Then I’ll be able to obtain Dragon King Verkis’s support.

A lifetime warranty for the Bone Dragon. A promise of endless treasure. Even one extra life on top of everything. It all culminated to create the perfect conditions for him to enjoy and experience the best of life.

So, let’s work harder to end the construction in a success.

Calming his pounding heart, Lloyd rounded off the bed inspection. And in his excitement, he didn’t see the desolate-looking ghost wandering near the flatland-where Hell’s first railroad was being constructed-as it looked at Lloyd in sheer panic.

What the heck? That’s… me?!

The lost ghost. The former hooligan of the Frontera barony. The man whose body was suddenly stolen without warning one day. The dead soul who became a resident in Hell. The ghost of Lloyd Frontera’s eyes snapped open when he looked at Lloyd.
