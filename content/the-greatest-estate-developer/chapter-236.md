# The Pleading Hooligan (1)

---

Lloyd Frontera, the hooligan, was a mess. His personality. Behavior. Attitude. Not a single attribute of his was worthy of praise. He destroyed himself and those around him, slacking off and behaving recklessly at any given chance. He was always preoccupied with his thoughts about the Frontera fiefdom, a boring and hideous province. He pitied his fate of being born and rotting away in such a place. And so, he was always angry. He was enraged by the lack of his family’s status and his unfulfilled greed. The anger, disappointment, and frustration inside the young master were taken out on everyone around him. He hurled insults at maids and physically assaulted servants. He mistreated his parents and the rest of the family.

And he drank. Getting intoxicated made him feel good. It was the only source of consolation in his life. So, the young master kept drinking more. That day was no different. He drank himself into a stupor at the pub. As he continued drinking, he felt a shaft of rage.

What happened after was unclear. Did he destroy everything? He vaguely recalled toppling over, throwing around, and stomping on the tables, chairs, and everything else he could lay his eyes on. The pub owner seemed to have teared up, choking in his tears to please stop while he was at it. But he must have wrenched the man off. How that man shuddered and flinched in fear when Lloyd tossed a glare and lifted his fist high! How entertaining the sight of that useless man was! Just like that, as Lloyd walked back home, he giggled as the scene replayed in his head. And then, when he got home, he flung himself into bed and fell asleep. That was his last memory. The last memory he had inside his physical body.

And afterward… That bastard…

The ghost of Hooligan Lloyd blinked, his eyes shaking. And he tossed his vacant gaze to the sky, where his flesh was. He couldn’t forget the morning of that day. How could he forget? The moment he became the ghost against his will? The moment when the very bastard that stole his body woke up and got out of bed?

…

On that very morning, the bastard skillfully struck up a conversation with Javier like everything was normal. As if it wasn’t enough to steal his body, the man even pretended to be him. The sight flipped his lid. No, actually, Ghost Lloyd was scared. Flustered and afraid. The young master couldn’t wrap his head around why his body was stolen and he became a ghost. There was nothing he could understand or do about it.

Yes. He was powerless. But he wanted to get his body back. So, he stuck next to the thief 24/7, never leaving his side as the thief slept, ate, and whatnot. Ghost Lloyd resorted to every attempt to get his body back. He yearned to drive away the fraud who was pretending to be him and restore his possession. But it was impossible. All his attempts failed. It was a fruitless endeavor even when the fraud passed out after defeating the Gigatitan in Cremo and waking up to reunite with his parents.

…

Ghost Lloyd was certain that he’d succeed when the fraud was in an unconscious, vegetative state. But even then, it was a failure. Not enough with that, he had to watch the fraud hug his parents who had run up to him in tears. He despaired seeing their concerned expression and eyes full of worry for the fraud’s wellbeing as their fingers trembled.

…

He felt dismayed, hopeless, defeated… and regretful for his crooked ways of life. That was when Ghost Lloyd realized. There was no excuse for him. Throughout the year, he glued himself to the thief of his body, and Ghost Lloyd saw how the latter worked. How earnest and desperate the man was. How purposeful his actions were. Ghost Lloyd was able to observe him for longer and more closely than anyone else. And so, he couldn’t make an excuse even when he wanted to.

I… lived blaming everything on my poor family status.

But he was different. The man possessed the same body as him, along with his family’s low status and crippling debt. But he acted like it was nothing and even as if he had gotten lucky with these conditions. He desperately worked hard to make money, so much so that Ghost Lloyd wondered if such an endeavor was possible for a human. Sometimes, the fraud put himself in harm’s way, changing the reputation Ghost Lloyd had tanked to the bottom by being a wretched hooligan.

Ghost Lloyd knew that all of these accomplishments would be unimaginable for him. He couldn’t give out any excuse because he saw it with his own eyes. So, he accepted defeat. As he saw his parents hug the fake Lloyd in tears, he thought to himself that it was all for the better, that his body was stolen, and that there was nothing else he could do. And just like that, he left and came to the underworld, mixing in with the dead. Then he fell to Hell and went to the Hell Castle, only to be kicked out without receiving a sentence. The reason was simple.

I’m neither dead nor living.

His body was still alive, although taken away, so he wasn’t dead. However, he wasn’t alive either, as he didn’t possess a living body. In other words, he was a spiritual refugee, a ghost without a home country. A buoying ghost that was neither living nor dead. A ghost that didn’t belong to any world. That was what he was. And so, from then on, he lived and spent his time in the corner of Hell since that was the only way. He was scared of demons, as his soul would be exterminated if they got to him. So, Ghost Lloyd lived in apprehension, fearing that he might be seen by demons.

For how long? There was no way of knowing. Hell didn’t have changing seasons. Thus, he simply hid every day to survive, bearing every moment in Hell. But he didn’t have many regrets as he regarded his current life as a fitting punishment for his crooked ways in the past. But now? For the first time, he regretted the decision to come down to Hell as he saw fake Lloyd standing in front of his eyes.

I gave up my body to him. Yet, that idiot already died and came here?!

This place was Hell, and Ghost Lloyd saw his thief here! Too soon!

That scumbag!

Ghost Lloyd teared up, a sudden onrush of regret washing through him. He shouldn’t have given up his body too soon. Eventually, his patience snapped.

“Hey!” shouted Ghost Lloyd and rushed toward Lloyd. “Since you’re already dead, it’s time to die in my hands now, you scumbag!”

Ghost Lloyd ran up to Lloyd and threw a punch. Lloyd felt flustered.

“What is this…?” Lloyd was inspecting the roadbed, and when a dead soul appeared out of nowhere and wielded a fist, he wondered if it had lost its mind.

How dare a mere dead soul attack me when even demons cower in my presence?

Swish. Lloyd easily shifted his feet. Normally, he just chose to avoid fighting. But he, too, was on the verge of being a high-level sword expert, which meant that he could easily dodge the fist of a dead soul. Launching a counterattack was easy for him as well.

Thwack! Lloyd stretched out his hand after avoiding the attack.

Slap! Slap! Slap!

Lloyd slapped the dead soul, whose face jerked to the side along with a sharp sound. But that wasn’t the end of his attack.

Thwack! When the dead reeled, Lloyd used his shoulder to push the former’s back as he hugged its knees. And he pulled, letting his weight pin on the dead.

“Eh?! Eh?”

When Ghost Lloyd fell to the ground, Lloyd immediately worked to push the ghost’s stomach with his right knee while his left leg and his right hand pressed down on both of its legs. And with his left hand, he clenched its throat. Hooligan Lloyd was completely suppressed.

Woah. What is this now?

Lloyd let out a snort after suppressing Ghost Lloyd without breaking a sweat, wondering if it was a resident in Hell that was against the indiscriminate development of the railroad. Did he need to act as an evil corporate developer again? Lloyd entertained this silly thought for a while before getting a proper look at the dead soul’s face. But when he checked its face properly, he flinched.

“Hmm?” Lloyd gasped and added, “Lloyd. Frontera?”

“Did you finally notice me, you bastard?”

“…”

It was really Lloyd Frontera. Though he was having trouble accepting the sudden turn of events, the dead soul glowering at him as it flailed under his weight was the hooligan, Lloyd Frontera. His face had somewhat faded away and even became half-transparent. However, the living Lloyd instantly recognized by instinct who it was.

What is happening? How am I supposed to react to this?

For a second, Lloyd was completely thrown off balance. Meeting the hooligan Lloyd Frontera was the very last thing he expected to happen. He wasn’t merely surprised. Lloyd felt as if he was caught red-handed in a crime. It was understandable considering that he was meeting the very person he unintentionally stole his body from. The ghost was the source of guilt for Lloyd. But soon enough, he pulled himself together. There was no need to be flustered. First, he had to figure out what was going on. He collected his thoughts and fixed his expression before speaking.

“Why are you here?” blurted Lloyd.

“That’s my question, you bastard,” Ghost Lloyd shot back.

Of course. The Ghost Lloyd didn’t give a kind response. Instead, its gaze turned hostile.

“How did you die and come here so early?”

“Me?” asked Lloyd with a frown. “Are you asking if I’m dead?”

“Yes.”

“I’m not dead at all.”

That was when it struck him that Ghost Lloyd had misunderstood what happened.

“I’m not dead,” blurted Lloyd.

“What?”

“I said I’m not dead.”

“Cut the bullcrap. Then why are you here in Hell if you aren’t dead?!”

“I was asked by a dragon to come here, and I’m building something for the King of Hell. I’m working here,” said Lloyd matter-of-factly.

“Eh…?”

“Yes. Consider it a business trip.”

“…”

Confusion rushed through Ghost Lloyd. Asked by a dragon? Building something for the King of Hell? A silly question came out of its mouth against its will.

“You. What in the world have you been doing with my body?”

“Nothing much. Just been trying to live diligently.”

“…”

“Then why are you here?” asked Lloyd this time.

“What do you mean, why?” snapped Ghost Lloyd. “It’s because of you! I was living an ordinary life until you did this to me! Got it?” His nostrils flared.

From then on, Ghost Lloyd screeched and screamed, sharing the story beginning from the day its body was taken away. It complained, protested, and shouted. It mourned its berating life in anger, pleading, and begging.

“So please… help me. Come on! I don’t want to be here. I’m sick of being neither dead nor living. Please… Get me revived or something!”

“…”

“It’s because of you. I’m neither dead nor living because you took away my body. So why should you be the only one living a good life? Come on! What did I do wrong?”

“…”

“Take responsibility for what you did! Do something for me!”

“Whew…”

Lloyd heaved a sigh he had been holding in. Hooligan Lloyd was still pinned to the ground, and Lloyd slightly pitied the guy after he heard the former’s story.

He’s become a soul that’s neither dead nor living.

So, Ghost Lloyd didn’t belong either in the outside world or the underworld. Living Lloyd never knew that it’d be roaming around in such a state,

It’s in a completely stateless condition.

Lloyd grasped the current situation after organizing his thoughts. He was flustered. Sure, he pitied Hooligan Lloyd and felt bad for it. But…

“How am I supposed to help you? Do you have any ideas?” Lloyd’s voice was now soft.

“That’s for you to find out!” shrieked Ghost Lloyd.

“…”

“That’s what my servants and maids did for me at home! I didn’t have to say it out loud. They all worked and did things for me! Don’t you think that’s the obvious thing to do?!”

“No, it’s not.”

Another sigh came out of Lloyd’s mouth. Sure, he sorely felt bad for Hooligan Lloyd, but he figured that the latter hadn’t gotten rid of its foul nature.

“First, listen to me carefully.”

Lloyd looked straight into Hooligan Lloyd’s eyes.

“First of all, you are asking me for a favor. So, don’t shout or throw a tantrum. You are not a baby.”

“…”

“Two. Let’s decide our nicknames.”

“What?”

“Nicknames. Don’t you know what that is?”

“I know, of course. But why should I do that? I’m Lloyd. You’re a fake,” Hooligan Lloyd protested.

“But I’m Lloyd, too.”

“Bullcrap! You’re nothing but a fake.”

“Then do you plan on keeping calling me a fake?”

“Of course.”

“Then I won’t help you.” Lloyd shook his head resolutely.

“What?”

“It’s offensive.”

“…”

Hooligan Lloyd swallowed, and that was when it hit Ghost Lloyd.

Right. I forgot he was a spiteful and petty bastard.

That was what Ghost Lloyd had observed from watching this fake for almost a year. And after having a conversation with him, it could even more clearly sense how petty the other was. Forcefully hiding the dismay in its voice, it asked, “T-then what are you saying we should do?”

“Let’s do rock scissors paper. The winner will be called Lloyd. The loser is Frontera. How’s that?”

“That doesn’t make any-”

“Rock…” put in Lloyd.

“Hey, you out of your mind?”

“If you don’t play, you lose. Paper, scissors…”

“Oh, come on!”

“Shoot!”

Swoosh! Two Lloyds held out their hands. Lloyd chose scissors. Hooligan Lloyd chose paper. A satisfied smile played across the former’s face.

“Great. Since I won, I’ll be called Lloyd.”

“Wow. This is nuts. It wasn’t enough with my body; you’re stealing my name too?”

“At least I’m calling you by your last name, Frontera.”

“Dammit. Then let me go now,” muttered Frontera. Lloyd’s gaze turned wistful as he watched Frontera.

Haah. I feel quite sorry for him.

It wasn’t on purpose that he took this man’s body. He never intended to in the first place. The event was a mere accident. But it was undeniably true that the unintended incident had completely changed his and the hooligan’s destinies.

I’ve been on a winning streak while this bastard turned into a ghost. Tsk.

Though nothing was by design, Lloyd still felt apologetic. But, of course, it wasn’t just 100% guilt. There were things that he thought he should be credited for.

The thing is, had I not taken away his body… He’d have died in the corner of a pub about 15 months after…

That was the destiny Lloyd Frontera met in the novel, something that would have happened without a doubt if his body was not taken away. But it wasn’t only Lloyd who was slated to meet a tragic death.

The baron, baroness, and Julian too.

The entire family was set to either kill themselves or get killed. That would have happened had it not been for himself. And so, technically speaking, he was a savior to the family of Lloyd Frontera.

Haah. I kind of want to call it even.

That was Lloyd’s honest thought. He wondered if it was necessary to help out this hooligan. Despite that, he was bothered. A part of him was pricked with guilt and so was his conscience. So, Lloyd felt that he couldn’t just ignore the issue, particularly when he thought of the count and countess, who were always kind to him.

I pretended to be their son all along. So, if I overlook the trouble their real son is facing and turn a blind eye, then I’d be doing an injustice to them.

When his thoughts reached there, he was able to make a decision.

“Hey, Frontera,” Lloyd called, “Listen carefully. I get what you’re going through and what you want. But right now, I’m not in the place to help you right away.”

“What? You scumbag, I’m going-”

“Stop cursing,” Lloyd put in, “and listen to me until I finish. I’m swamped with work right now. The construction for the King of Hell is in full swing. So, let me just wrap up what I’m doing first. Neither of us knows how I can help you anyway.” Lloyd looked at Frontera meaningfully.

“You are right, but…”

“That’s why I’ll try to search for ways to help you while I’m finishing the construction. Okay? So, get in my inner pocket and stay quiet.”

“In your inner pocket?” Frontera asked in confusion.

“Yeah.” Lloyd chuckled. “You can become smaller since you’re a ghost, right?”

“I could if you let me go.”

“So, shrink yourself and stay in my pocket for the time being.”

“Why should I do that?”

“I’m giving you the chance to be safe until I’m done with this construction. Or what, do you want the demons to get you and be exterminated?”

“…”

“You now get why I’m making this suggestion?”

“Haah. Dammit. I get it. So let me go first,” grumbled Frontera.

Lloyd freed Frontera, who downsized his own form. Lloyd introduced Frontera to his friends in his inner pocket.

“Hey, Ppodong. Bangul. Hamang. Ggoming. This is Frontera. Don’t hit or pester him. But be nice, okay?" 

"Ppo? Bang? Ha? Ggo?”

Ppodong, Bangul, Hamang, and Ggoming were enjoying a nap in Lloyd’s inner pocket. The four fantastical creatures widened their eyes at the sight of Frontera, who largely possessed the same appearance as Lloyd. The ghost sparked their curiosity. What about Frontera? He was flabbergasted.

“Ack!!! What are these fur balls?!” yelped Frontera.

“Well, they’re your friends for now. You said you stuck by me for a year or so. Then you should have seen all of them except maybe Ggoming,” drawled Lloyd.

“Hey!” shouted Ghost Lloyd. “What does that have to do with anything? Why do I have to be with these things!" 

"Don’t like it? What, you want me to chuck you to the demons then?”

“…”

“That’s worse, right?”

“…”

“Then be nice and get along. Don’t make them hate you for no reason.”

Lloyd then smiled in a meaningful way because it looked like they were already disliking Frontera. The four of them were glowering at Frontera for calling them “these things” a while ago. But Lloyd paid no attention to it.

“Okay, time to go in.”

“H-hold on!”

“Closing the button now.”

“Hey!” Boing! Lloyd shoved Frontera into his inner pocket and closed the button. Later, he heard a ruffling noise coming from his pocket. It looked like the four of them were very friendly.

Haah. I bought some time for now.

Lloyd sighed at length. To be honest, he didn’t have any idea how he could help out the ghost..

But at least I didn’t make any promises. I just said I’d search for ways I could help.

That was his escape. In case he fails to give any help, he can always say, “I tried, but I couldn’t find ways to help you out.”

And for the time being, it’s better to keep him hidden. That way, Javier won’t find out about him.

Javier didn’t have any suspicions about Lloyd’s identity. So, what would happen if he saw the ghost of Lloyd Frontera, the real one? What if he learns the truth? Lloyd couldn’t imagine what his reaction would be. As such, he decided it would be best to keep Frontera hidden for now. Right then…

“Master Lloyd? Why are you sighing so heavily?”

Right when he thought so, Javier’s voice came close behind him without any warning. It happened much too unexpectedly for Lloyd. His heart jumped out of his chest at Javier’s sudden question.
