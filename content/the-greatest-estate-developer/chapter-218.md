# What the Queen Carries (2)

---

I am a queen.

She lifted her gaze. Under the night sky, the Bone Dragon stood tall like a giant mountain range. An indomitable wall. An impregnable fortress. Could this be what mayfly feels in the presence of a giant?

What a useless worry.

Queen Magentano smiled, and next, she leaped high. Fwoosh!

A second later, there was a crater on the spot she jumped from.

Boom! The Bone Dragon’s foreleg stabbed the ground, leaving a destructive hole that was 32 feet deep and 82 feet wide upon impact. Rock debris scattered all over the air.

“Hmph!”

Queen Magentano brandished her sword in the air, her sword aura splitting into hundreds of strands that wrapped around her body. Hundreds of rock shards flew at her at a subsonic speed and clashed with her aura. 

Clang! Clang! Clang! Sparks flew off from the hundreds of strands wrapped around her. She then broke through the fiery sparks to hurtle toward the Bone Dragon.

Exhale!

As she let out a rough exhale, her manaheart roared, and at the same time, her sword began to change. 

Whoosh! The long sword in her hand cried out like a beast, revealing the fangs it had been hiding. Along with a red flash of light, it released an aura that was nearly ten times larger than its blade. Ultimately, the sword created from the aura had a blade that was about 20 inches wide and 13 feet long. The destructive aura slammed down like an ax, targeting the foreleg of the dragon.

Clatter! She couldn’t even make a scratch on the Bone Dragon’s skeleton before. But now, it was different.

I did it!

The sword aura created a third of an inch-long crack. A spurt of dark mana power came out from the slightly dented bone.

It wasn’t much but it worked.

Her eyes danced with hope. The situation differed from a while ago when none of her attacks worked against the monster. She managed to leave a dent, even if it was a small one, in the Bone Dragon’s foreleg. This meant only one thing. If she fought a little harder. If she fought a little better. Then, she could leave more significant damage.

I’m not asking for anything grand. Just one leg. Then the royal troops will be able to put up a fight afterward. I can do it!

Whoosh! Queen Magentano’s manaheart roared more ferociously. Now her movement turned rougher and heavier, much like a muscular predator or beast that could snap their prey’s neck in one bite. She shifted all her strength against the ground.

Boom! Anything she touched splintered on contact, rocks broke apart and boulders cracked around her. She aggressively walked forward. And with a strong repulsive force, she jumped, her whole body jetting out like a cannonball. Then she concentrated every bit of her massive strength on her sword aura.

“Arggh!”

Crash! With more brute force than technique, Queen Magentano slammed down on the dragon’s wrist bone. She hit, slashed, and jabbed him. Then, she repeated the process more fiercely each time. 

Boooom! She hurtled toward him five times, which ensued five consecutive explosive attacks. Now, the dent on its carpal bone was slightly deeper. Just then, the Bone Dragon brushed off its wrist.

“…!”

Thwack! Queen Magentano, who was getting ready for the next attack, was grazed by the dragon’s movement, sending her to fly 30 yards away. She barely recovered her balance and managed to land safely on the ground. “Argh…!”

She dry-heaved right after she hit the ground. It was just a small graze. And she had even softened the impact by blocking the blow with her aura sword. But it nearly killed her.

How can it…

Her eyes trembled. Even now, her stomach was churning, and her manaheart struggled to recover from the blow. She suddenly felt bitter.

I had trained so hard, yet…

She didn’t take a proper day of rest ever since someone tried to assassinate her. She obsessively spent everyday pushing herself to extreme limits for the sake of training. It was more strenuous than the time she had trained to rise to the level of a sword master. And it made her strong, all right. She could easily win a fight against her past self and Sir Kyle, the man who betrayed her. And that sense of pride had advanced into conviction when she defeated the remaining warlocks.

I thought I had done enough. But…

But she discovered a new height of power that she could never reach. And that was this indomitable monster, Bone Dragon, and it was looking down at her. A shaft of despair gripped her as she looked back up.

I cannot fight it.

In any case, she thought that she could leave a little damage to the dragon and earn enough time for the royal troops to prepare for battle. After all, it was something only she, a swordmaster, could do. That was why she felt the need to take action and was confident of her success. At least, that had been her thought process.

But I was mistaken.

Grit! Queen Magentano clenched her teeth. And she looked up at the Bone Dragon now charging toward her way. Its mouth was open wide, and she raised her aura sword at it. Just then…

Kaboom! A storm of mana flew in one straight line and hit the side of the Bone Dragon’s face.

Boooom! Its head was jerked to the side, and the sudden attack managed to stall its hurtle for a short moment. And at that second, she heard a voice.

“Your Majesty, speed over strength. Aim sideways instead of headfirst. I dare to offer my assistance, Your Majesty.”

“…!”

Queen Magentano could see it. There was someone in between the clouds that covered the full moon by half. Someone was hurtling through the air, and he landed on the Bone Dragon’s backbone and gave a hard stab. Another destructive explosion. The man allowed the force of the explosion to send him flying away, and soon enough, he landed on the queen’s side.

“Please forgive me, Your Majesty, but please move over there.”

“…!”

It was Javier Asrahan, the silver-haired knight. He was gesturing with his gaze, and Queen Magentano reacted right away. She charged in the direction Javier was pointing to, and the giant mouth of Bone Dragon chewed the very place she was in a second ago.

Crack!

“…”

She dodged and started to counterattack. With Javier joining her, the moment marked the combined attack of two sword masters, a rare event to see in history.

“Your Majesty,” Javier called.

“Yes.”

“May I say a few words?”

“Yes, but drop the formalities, given our current situation. Be as brief as possible,” ordered the queen as she avoided a string of attacks from the Bone Dragon.

“Yes, Your Majesty,” replied Javier as he ran beside her. “Please don’t hold back and jump as high as you can.” “How about you?”

“I will be its distraction.”

“Distraction?”

“Meaning I will distract the dragon from attacking you. Excuse me, Your Majesty.”

Fwish! Javier disappeared like the wind, and he was already several feet away from her. A light appeared. Kaboom! A mana blast appeared like a bolt of lightning and struck the very center of the dragon’s backbone. Although it failed to leave any cracks, it was enough to minutely shake the Bone Dragon’s ginormous body. That alone was enough as it gave a slight stagger to the trajectory of the front leg that was coming down on her.

Wham! Its front leg hit the ground after failing to land on Alicia.

All right. I understand his plan.

A ferocious glow appeared in her eyes when she understood Javier’s intent. From that moment on, her giant aura sword leaped like a beast, crisscrossing the front and rear legs of the dragon and leaving a scratch on them. But the Bone Dragon couldn’t attack back because of Javier.

Kaboom! Unlike the queen, who could attack only close by, Javier had the mana blast with him. Each time he held out his sword, the mana blast jetted out dozens of feet through the air, striking the gap between its joints each time. And its giant body staggered each time. The reason was simple. Javier capitalized on the vulnerable joints of the body parts that received the weight of its movement. Javier attacked its opposite elbow and wrist when it wielded its foreleg. When it staggered back, Javier attacked its knees and pelvis. And when it attempted to straighten up, he aimed at the back. Each attack was piercing, and the giant body lost balance and reeled, missing its aim at Queen Magentano by a hair’s breadth.

And then the queen would raise her vigor and charge at it without hesitation. She danced with her aura sword while roaring like a ferocious lioness. Meanwhile, her heart sped up by a notch.

This is working. I can do it.

Her heart pounded in hope. She slowly began to see a chance to win. Bone Dragon looked formidable in every sense of the word when she was alone. It was an undefeatable monster. But now, with Javier, she was able to return the dragon’s punches. At the very least, she was able to do something that could be called a fight. 

My attack is working!

Her strike didn’t leave great damage. It only left a third of an inch-long crack. That was even after she used all her strength. But if she hit the same spot a hundred times, the crack would become 40 inches long.

40 inches. That’s all we need. The dragon will eventually collapse from its aggressive moments if I drive a 40- inch hole in its wrists and ankles that hold its massive weight.

The key to fighting a giant mammal was hitting a weight-bearing part of its body. Queen Magentano figured that it would be the same case for Bone Dragon, so she obstinately focused on the wrist connected to its front legs. She threw one blow after another. And finally, she saw a chance for her hopes to turn into reality

A little more. Just a little!

Crash! Slash! Crash! Backed by Javier’s support and barely avoiding the Bone Dragon’s attack, she whacked the same spot on its body over and over again. Her smile started to grow with her hopes. A faint chance of victory emerged. That was only until Bone Dragon flew high into the air.

Kaboom! Its grand wings spread out without warning. After one rough flap, it was off the ground, right before Queen Magentano could land her hundredth strike. It abruptly swooped into the air. The dragon flew almost 1,000 feet off the ground with a flap of its wings. Queen Megantano lifted her head, her heart swelling with disbelief. It was carefreely flying in the air where she couldn’t reach it. And now, it was leisurely looking down at her.

“This cannot be happening…”

She wondered if this was what a colony of ants felt like after failing to capture an eagle with great effort. She wondered if she was in a nightmare.

How am I supposed to fight something like that?

As her gaze darted to the sky and at the Bone Dragon, a despairing sense of helplessness overwhelmed her as if she had been blocked by an impenetrable wall.

That’s it. Keep on despairing. You will feel helpless… Just like how I was…

The Bone Dragon opened its wings, and as it flew up toward the night sky, it gazed down at Queen Magentano, left on the ground by herself. And the soul in its body, Warlock Targa, thought to himself…

I’m sure I had a similar expression while I was beheaded.

She was looking up at his way, and there was helplessness in her face. It was written with abjection. It was likened to the look of a person in the face of a disastrous natural disaster. That was especially joyful to see for Warlock Targa.

You should be looking at me like that. It’d have been very unfair if I didn’t get to see you making that face.

Targa thought it was unfair. He thought so the moment he was killed in her hands. The warlock was forced to give up on his body in the final moment when his disembodied neck rolled over on the ground. An inevitable choice had to be made. He didn’t want everything to end like this. So, he transferred his soul to the dragon skeleton stored inside the laboratory. It was his last resort or final act of desperation, to put it one way.

He hadn’t had high hopes to begin with. His chance of success was less than ten percent. But it worked. His soul was transferred into the skeleton of the dragon. That was the moment he gave up on being a human. From then, there was a long period of waiting. He used the time to wait for his soul to settle into the dragon skeleton. It took a few months. And finally, the day had come. Today. Targa opened his eyes to a new body, and the result was better, beyond his expectation.

I didn’t expect to hold this much strength.

He turned out to be very strong. Though he wasn’t as powerful as the actual Bone Dragon, a simple imitation was all that he could manifest, still, he was powerful enough to toy the queen like a bug. What was more, he could do so even when he wasn’t used to the body at all!

I’m done warming up now.

Targa smiled ferociously, determined that this was the end of the monotonous attack with forelegs. It was time to get real and toy the queen hard. He focused on the alienating sensation of fluttering the wings. That got his body to fly higher in the air. The grand garden and the palace behind it, the city district, everything felt like a toy, a city of the bugs. And at the same time, he even felt he had become omnipotent. In a word, he felt like he was…

I feel like I’ve become a god.

He was thrilled. Everything seemed doable. He wanted to crush every creature that stood in his way.

Queen Magentano in particular. How dare you behead me. You will pay the price for your actions. I will slaughter you until you’re at the edge of death and make you watch the capital completely collapse in flames. 

The soul of the Bone Dragon, Targa, smirked in an evil way. Now, the fluttering of wings felt natural. The time has come to knuckle down and smash things. The time for enjoyment has come. The time to break and kill everything in sight.

Wahaha!

It lowered its giant head and closed its wings for a dive. But when it was about to, an unexpected creature took a nosedive at it first.

“Ggoming!”

Swooosh!

Huh…?

A white form zoomed down through the murky clouds like a bolt of lightning. It was chubby and had wings. A round head and clear eyes. So this was…

A parrotbill?

But it was larger than a human. Its shape felt familiar. Just then, Targa recalled the story he had heard right after their schemes failed in Namaran. A report from a guy said that there was a man who attacked them on the parrotbill. The man’s name was…

Lloyd Frontera?

Targa remembered. That was when Lloyd yelled as he dove with Ggoming and threw something toward it. 

“First, take this!”

Fwish! Something was flying his way. Targa saw an ordinary beaver flying toward his way. He felt flustered.

What now? What is this Lloyd doing by hurling that beaver in this situation? Right when Targa was dumbstruck…

Boom!

The beaver suddenly turned gigantic, his body grew to be 328 feet tall. And swaying his merciless belly, Bibeong pulled the dragon into a hug right away.

“Bibeong!”

Eh?!

Bibeong got it in his embrace in mere seconds. And now, Bibeong laid a body-press attack against the Bone Dragon midair 8,200 feet off the ground, pressing it with his 3,000-ton body.
