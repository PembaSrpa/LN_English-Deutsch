# Bones More Precious than Treasures (1)

---

“Please do me a favor and hold on for just a little longer everyone.”

Slash! As Queen Magentano murmured to herself, the wooden sword in her hand terrorized the air, as though it was engraving a lightning bolt or slashing into the empty space. Ultimately, it rushed straight ahead and struck the shield of a nearby knight.

Boom! Though it was only a wooden sword strike, a booming noise akin to a fired canon blared out. The blow left a fist-sized dent in the shield made of five layers of metal sheet. The royal knight holding the shield was not left unharmed.

“Argh…!”

He was hurled 26 feet from where he stood along with his crushed shield.

“Urghh…”

The knight writhed in pain like a caterpillar stuck in the sand. He felt his stomach turn and was drained of all his energy. However, he wasn’t upset since there were other victims aside from him.

Thwaaack! Clang! Thwack!

Each time Queen Magentano wielded her wooden sword, several knights shot into the air in all directions like a pinball. Of course, the knights didn’t just defenselessly stand still.

An intermediate commanding knight yelled, “Gather up and form a close formation!”

The roughly 70 knights left in the hall busily moved in unison. 40 of them gathered in close formation, holding out a thick tower shield in front of themselves as they marched forward. The speed at which they moved wasn’t slow, even though they were wearing a few hundred pounds of armor, as every single one of them was a high- level sword expert.

Clack! Click! Clack!

The heavily armored and dense wall of shields pushed against the queen. Meanwhile, the rest of the 30 knights surrounded her, moving like a delicately running cogwheel. Then they lay a heavy, fierce siege from the rear as they quickly changed their bearing and locality in rotation. But soon, they were destroyed. Kaboom!

“Ack!”

The moment Queen Magentano flew open her eyes, the knight that wielded his sword at her was thrown 16 feet in the air.

W-what the…

A look of bewilderment appeared in the knight’s eyes. He was certain that he got her, only to find himself flying in the air by the time he came to his senses. Of course, his sword had escaped from his grasp. He couldn’t even lift his finger from the shock that ran down his whole body. And in the next moment, however, he received a mental shock that hit him harder than the physical blows he received.

Kaboom! Clang! Pow! Clung! Thump!

“…!”

His comrades started to float in the air, too. Everyone was flailing their arms in midair. It was an unseemly sight to behold. They all hung at the same altitude of 16 feet as though the queen had intentionally chosen that height. And each of them gaped wordlessly like a goldfish out of the water and exchanged frightened glances.

“You too?”

“Me too.”

Then came the drop. Bam! Thud! Thump!

“Ack!”

“Eek!”

“Argh!”

Eight knights that were floating in the air hit the ground at almost the same time. Even after that, the queen didn’t stop brandishing her wooden sword.

“I asked you all to do me a favor and hold on a little longer.”

She hurtled toward the close formation of shields, and like a lion pouncing into a flock of sheep, she ripped the formation apart in mere seconds.

Baaam! She threw one blow after another, never striking her sword upward. And yet, the knights that were hit by her sword catapulted into the air. She continued storming forward, and the knights shot upward like a strong gush of fountain water.

“Yet, why can you not carry on? Hm?” the queen calmly scolded.

Slam! Pow! She didn’t even use her aura and only wielded her ordinary wooden sword and natural strength. She merely struck at the most appropriate timing and angle and with just the right amount of strength. But the rest of the royal knights collapsed in front of her, unable to endure the attacks for even a second. The queen moved as if she was using this training to let out her frustrations. Even then, however, the tumultuous emotions reflected in her eyes burned fiercely, and refused to calm down.

Lloyd Frontera.

The name came to her mind as she wielded her wooden sword. She had intended to use his talent for a noble purpose. But now… Ha! She was more dumbstruck the more she thought about him. A part of her was even disgusted.

Is that how much he hates helping me out?

Lloyd was practically an open book to Queen Magentano. She could undoubtedly tell that Lloyd was refusing to use his talent and wanted to rest for the remainder of his life. And the marriage between the Frontera family and the sultan’s family must have been pushed forward for that goal of his. She was sure about it.

Lloyd Frontera must have intervened without question.

Queen Magentano didn’t even need to look into it. Lloyd must have wanted to two-time her with the sultan using this marriage. The queen was regretful about it. She truly was. But what Lloyd did wasn’t a crime per se that granted her the right to lay into him.

It is Julian Frontera, the second son of the Frontera family, who got married. He is not set to inherit the fiefdom.

Moreover, this marriage was the perfect publicity stunt to taut to her people, especially at this juncture. 

The marriage happened just in time when the sultan and I struck a peace treaty. The marriage between the second son of the Frontera county, which receives unwavering support from the royal family, and the daughter of the sultan. It’s an amazing symbol of friendship between us and the sultan kingdom.

Going beyond just good, one would have wondered if the queen and the sultan had planned for this to happen on purpose. And that was why Queen Magentano gritted her teeth more.

Lloyd must have felt his chance had come. He must have thought he had now earned the chance to befriend the sultan without getting hated. What a sly man he is.

Wham! The wooden sword continued its explosive and emotional dance, and the innocent human punching bags, the royal knights, collapsed to the ground like dominos. Queen Magentano continued her train of thought.

It must probably be because he is chary of staying under my influence. Through this marriage, he is throwing a modest, quite elusive message, a protest, that he can get closer to the sultan if he wishes to and will stick to the latter if I treat him badly. That is why I find him more disgusting but cute.

Grit! She clenched her teeth. Lloyd Frontera. She wanted to kick Lloyd’s gut for being detestable. She wanted to harass him the more he struggled to tear himself away from her. She longed to drown him in work the more he fumbled to escape, assign him to lifelong labor and watch him flounder in misery, and watch him wretchedly beg in tears for a break.

“Agh…!”

Boom! A knight was hurled 16 feet away as he clutched his stomach. That was the last of it. Her insolent gaze swept over the surroundings. Not a single knight was standing near her.

“Tsk.”

She wanted to stretch her body a little more. Dealing with the knights hadn’t even made her break a sweat. Recently, ever since she ramped up her level once more, training with the knights wasn’t enough for her. Around the time she was growing dissatisfied…

“Your Majesty, Lloyd Frontera has arrived,” an official reported brightly in the corner of a training.

The corners of her lips rolled up ferociously. The timing was perfect. She was enticed by this opportunity to harass Lloyd.

Suppressing the strong desire, she said, “Let him wait.”

After dropping the wooden sword, she walked out of the training hall and she took a swig of a glass of cold water. When she passed the long driveway and garden, she spotted Lloyd down on one knee.

“Lloyd Frontera is honored to be in the audience of Her Majesty,” announced Lloyd when he sensed her presence.

“…”

The queen felt a sudden impulse to slap the back of her head as she stared at his brazen actions. He would helplessly fall and roll to the ground. That sight would be exhilarating to watch, she thought to herself. But then, just barely holding back her impulse, she spoke in a formal tone.

“Follow me.”

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

Queen Magentano swiveled and made for the outside of the secondary palace where the training hall stood. She passed the garden that led to the main palace, Lloyd obediently following behind her. He studied her very carefully, and the conversation between them resumed only much later.

“I see. Seeing that you have come here, you have decided to accept my request,” she stated coolly.

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty,” replied Lloyd with feigned humility.

“Gratitude, my foot.”

“…”

“I will not,” she went on, “beat around the bush after calling you here when you want to rest. I will cut to the chase. I am sure you are well aware of how I subdued the remnants of the warlock recently, yes?”

“Yes, Your Majesty, I have heard about it.”

Lloyd nodded as if it was true. About four days ago, right after Julian’s wedding ceremony, Count Ventura gave him an update as he shared the queen’s request for him.

So, while I was building the Qanat in the sultan’s kingdom… the queen achieved a brilliant feat.

Count Ventura shared that the interrogation of Cannavaro had ended. The man was said to have confessed everything when the palace laid a salvo of mind-altering and truth spells on him. The result was highly satisfactory. From the location, number, and plan of the remnant members, she was able to acquire almost all their classified information.

And she jumped into action right after, I heard.

Queen Magentano didn’t even dawdle for a second. As soon as she was done with analyzing the information, she mobilized and led her armed forces to execute the punitive operation, eventually ambushing the enemy’s cave like a bolt of lightning without giving them any time to react.

“I am glad that you have heard already,” said Queen Magentano as she kept walking. “Then you must know that I slaughtered every single one of them, yes?”

“Certainly, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd.

What the queen was saying was true. It was a clear fact that she had wiped out all their souls.

And in the process, she was said to manifest a sword skill that is much stronger than before.

According to Count Ventura, at the last portion of the battle, the queen personally beheaded the leader of the warlocks, showcasing an aura more iridescent than an ordinary sword master in the process. Thanks to her, the forces suffered less damage.

The queen has grown to be stronger than in the novel.

It was a mystery to Lloyd how much stronger she had gotten. But he knew that it was by a considerable increase.

In the novel, she loses an arm and turns into a tyrant.

After she lost her arm, she overlooked training. But now, it was a different story.

I suppose the assassination attempt terribly motivated her.

That thought crossed Lloyd for a second. It didn’t seem far-fetched since he always found her coming out of training whenever he requested an audience with the queen after the assassination incident. In short, this meant that she was subjecting herself to intense training every day.

I pity the royal knights.

Those who were involved in the daily training of the queen could have the lowest job satisfaction in the whole kingdom. Lloyd was quite sure of it.

I guess they’re the victims of the changes I created in history. Tsk.

Lloyd licked his lips, feeling slightly apologetic toward the royal knights. Meanwhile, the queen continued to speak.

“Anyway, I acquired an unexpected bundle of spoils after subduing the enemies. That is why I called you.” “Are they related to me, Your Majesty?” Lloyd asked, intrigued.

“If I want them to be, they are. After all, I plan on entrusting you with a construction project that will make use of this.”

“Construction project… Your Majesty?” Lloyd’s voice trembled.

“Indeed.” She nodded. “Why? Do you not want to do it?”

“Um…” Lloyd was about to answer when he clamped his mouth shut.

The queen paused her walk before he realized it. She was staring at Lloyd with a fierce gaze, which seemed to say, “You better pick your next words very carefully.”

Gulp. As soon as Lloyd saw her gaze, he had a realization.

She is aware of my plans already.

The queen was something indeed. Lloyd realized this once more.

I just sought my chance after observing the countless turn of events.

In the end, he succeeded in pairing Julian and Sheherazade. He was now able to two-time both the queen and sultan without being blamed by the other. And thanks to that, unilateral “orders” from the queen were changed into “requests.” Or so Lloyd thought. But now, he realized….

I guess it’s too early for that right now.

It was too early for him to bluntly reject the queen and express his unwillingness to do the work she requested of him.

It’s the case even if I’m holding the Magentano Carry title.

The title granted a powerful benefit, but it wasn’t absolute. Lloyd’s behavior could alter the valid period of the title, like, for example, right now.

If I upset her now, it’s over.

A powerful hunch came over Lloyd. If he behaved cluelessly and stubbornly, the title “Magentano Carry” that liberated him from any suspicion of treason could be shortened or completely eliminated. As the bad feeling gripped Lloyd, he decided to act like an elusive cockroach that sneaked around a kitchen sink at night. In other words, he immediately changed his attitude and began sincerely groveling at her.

“I, Lloyd Frontera,” said Lloyd in a grave tone, “have been waiting day and night until Your Majesty assigned me to a new project. Please grant me any task of your wish, Your Majesty.”

“Truly?” asked the queen, her voice grave.

“Truly, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd, stressing his words.

“Really?”

“Certainly, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd.

“How naturally the eldest son of the Frontera family spews lies out of his mouth.”

“…”

“As if anybody in this world likes to work.”

“Uhm, that is because-”

“Enough. I am aware of your true feelings already.” Her voice was now softer. “What purpose and reason you had when you cajoled your brother into marrying the sultan’s daughter. Thus, I would like to give you a special reminder.”

“…”

Queen Magentano’s eyes held a glint of something mysterious.

“I like you,” the queen revealed. “You please me. Therefore, I will not be careless and incessant toward you. However…”

She looked at him a tad more sharply.

“Do not get too close to my enemy. Do not indiscreetly test my favor. Am I clear?”

“I shall keep that in mind, Your Majesty.”

Lloyd nodded earnestly so much that his upper spines hurt. That was only when her eyes relaxed.

“As I thought, you catch on quickly.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

“I do not need your gratitude that you so easily speak of every time you are out of things to say.”

“…”

“Anyway, since you know where I stand, it is now time to reveal my assignment to you. This way.”

Queen Magentano led the way again, and Lloyd followed her. Past the garden, into the main palace, and after numerous corridors and stairs later, they finally arrived. They were at the palatial repository that stored a host of palatial treasures. Queen Magentano smiled meaningfully as she turned and faced Lloyd.

“Are you ready to see the spoils?”

“Yes, I am, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd.

What could she possibly want to show him? And what job is she planning on entrusting him with? The queen motioned while Lloyd was lost in thought. The royal knight moved and opened the sturdy door, revealing, at last, the spoil stashed inside.

“I order you to create a large garden that will reflect the global power of my palace using that.”

The spoil, which the queen was pointing at with a smile on her face, was no other than piles of bones from a dragon whose body stretched over 655 feet in length. Lloyd recognized this as soon as he laid his eyes on it.
