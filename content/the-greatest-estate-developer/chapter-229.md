# Along with Money: Hell (2)

---

Fwoosh! They were in an unknown place inside Hell. Floating in the air, the Hell gate was spinning like an angry beast while spluttering out fire. Suddenly, something else was spluttered out. It was Lloyd and Javier.

“Oops…!”

Lloyd closely managed to recover balance and land safely without crashing down. But he soon winced and withdrew his foot.

“Ouch! It’s hot!”

Sear! The rocks on the ground were soft and half-melted from the heat. Lloyd’s shoes were slightly scorched on the bottom. 

Wow.

Coming to terms that he had entered the fiery pits of hell, Lloyd surveyed his surroundings. Just then, the events he had experienced right before his arrival to Hell struck his mind.

“Is this it, Lloyd Frontera?” asked the queen. “Do you plan to go to Hell now?”

“Yes, Your Majesty,” affirmed Lloyd.

“How heartless of you. I was hoping you would leave after helping me clean up this mess.”

“I will leave Bibeong behind, Your Majesty,” he said.

“Is that so?” asked the queen.

“Yes, Your Majesty.” Lloyd then hung his head down without hesitation, which prompted the queen to make a wry smile. There was slight wistfulness in her expression.

She shrugged before adding, “Well. It was the dragon that asked for your help, so it is only sensible that you want to take care of it first. Thankfully, there weren’t many critically injured people or deaths considering the size of the incident.”

It really was the case. Given that the Bone Dragon had torn this place apart, the damage wasn’t substantial. Apart from the royal magician killed by the dragon early on and some royal guards, there was almost no casualty.

“It is all thanks to you,” the queen said.

“That’s-”

“No,” she broke in before Lloyd could finish. “No need to deny my words. It is all thanks to you who shocked the visitors into fleeing by bringing out a giant beaver.”

“…”

"If you hadn’t taken action, countless of them would have wound up in the tragedy and got killed. I truly appreciate you for setting an example and for your valiance,” said the queen.

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

“Tsk. You and your empty words.”

“…”

“Every time you say these things, my hatred toward you soars through the roof and I wish to send you to Hell. However, through a method different from the one you are about to take.”

“…”

Lloyd smiled awkwardly, hoping to himself that the queen would talk without the sword in her hand. To be honest, Lloyd, too, knew where she was coming from. Lloyd was a reliable, trustworthy, and astute subject who lived without any greedy ambitions. It wasn’t easy to come across such a great underling. No, the mere discovery of such a talent was a miracle. And so, she would never want to lose him. That was why the queen felt wistful. She was wistful as Lloyd had grown too strong for her to order him around like before. That was why she was making these sharp jokes.

Haha, but it’s kind of ridiculous how I’m calling myself a great talent.

Lloyd thought so because, in the first place, he was just an ordinary guy who had slightly more knowledge of this world than other people here. His astuteness and ability to read the room? Those were helpful only because he was slightly more knowledgeable about the circumstances and future of this world, which he had gotten from reading The Knight of Blood and Iron. And so, Lloyd never overestimated himself. Instead, he resolved to keep a low profile.

I can’t become cocky and act recklessly just because things are going a bit smooth for me. That behavior led many people to fall to their demise throughout history.

The same went for him, who now had the Bone Dragon and Javier, a grandmaster, under him. Even though he was on the crest of a wave and held massive power, he could potentially get brutally assassinated against his luck. Such was life. There wasn’t any guarantee that it wouldn’t happen. And so, Lloyd told himself long before that he must never antagonize the queen. And his mind hadn’t changed.

“Your Majesty, I can only come back to your side if I go to Hell through the method of my choice.” Lloyd’s voice was cool and convincing.

“Is that so…?”

“Yes, Your Majesty,” answered Lloyd.

“But you see,” reasoned the queen, “even if I behead you, I have a feeling that you will come back to life after tricking the demons in Hell.”

“I am overly flattered, Your Majesty,” said Lloyd, lowering his head.

“Are you?” asked the queen, arching her brow.

“Yes, Your Majesty.”

“Your sly behavior keeps making me want to test your words,” she growled playfully.

“…”

“Do not worry,” she said, her voice softening. “I am pulling your leg.”

“I am overwhelmed with gratitude, Your Majesty.”

“If you really do, make sure to come back safely to my side again.”

“I will try my utmost best, Your Majesty.”

Yes, Lloyd wanted to come back. That he was sure about. He didn’t want to stay any longer than he had to in a place like Hell. After their conversation, Lloyd lent his helping hand to the cleanup by bringing Bibeong to the site, who was limping in the wake of the 3,000-ton body-press attack. There was some convincing and coaxing in the process of leaving him to the queen. And then, Lloyd took Ppodong, Bangul, Hamang, and Ggoming in his inner pocket. As for the Bone Dragon, he ordered it to be on standby.

“Quietly hide yourself somewhere nearby.”

Creak? The Bone Dragon cocked its giant head like a large, meek puppy trying to make out its owner’s words. A smile erupted from Lloyd’s mouth.

“The people here won’t react well if they see you. They’ll be terrified. You don’t want that to happen, do you?” asked Lloyd nicely.

Clack! Clack! The Bone Dragon nodded its head earnestly.

Lloyd said, “That is why I want you to hide near the capital until I return from Hell. Away from people’s sight. Better if you submerge yourself in the sea without the presence of any humans.”

Clack!

“It’s all right,” cooed Lloyd, “You’re not going to die. You don’t even breathe, do you?”

Clack!

“If monsters like the Gigatitan try to harass you, just flick them with your foreleg. You’re stronger,” assured Lloyd.

Clack!

“Yes.” Lloyd nodded. “I’ll be back quickly. I promise.”

For some reason, Lloyd got the feeling that the Bone Dragon he acquired from the Zombie Domination skill had a new personality of a giant puppy. Finally relieved by the promise, the Bone Dragon flew up in the direction of the sea north of the capital. As for Lloyd, he roughly cleaned up the mess from the incident and left the rest to the queen.

“I’ll leave now.” With that, he stepped toward the Hell gate and crossed to Hell with Javier.

“Whew…”

He had been lost in his reverie just for a while, and yet, his nose was already prickling.

The whole place reeks of rotten eggs.

There was a pungent, rotten stench that pricked their nostrils. It was the smell of sulfur. Their view was equally unpleasant with volcanos everywhere within Lloyd’s sight. Lava spilled out along the large and small mountain ranges like a milk carton bursting from the side. Grass? Shrubs? Green trees? They did not exist here. The whole sky was gray with smoke. Could it be the reason why?

“Ehem. Ah-choo! Cough!”

It was Javier, who kept coughing beside Lloyd. It seemed that he wasn’t used to the smoky, polluted air. However, Lloyd was completely fine.

Well, this is similar to the air in Seoul.

To be specific, the air was as murky as the streets in Hongdae, Seoul on the days with the highest levels of fine particulate matter.

I went around without a mask then, too, because I had no money for them.

Lloyd had no way of avoiding exposure to air pollution even if it was harmful to his body. Some days were better than others when Lloyd managed to pick up some anti-dust masks from construction sites. But on days he failed to get them, he had to bear the terrible air and bank on having a healthy respiratory system. Thankfully and ironically, his vigorous respiratory training back in South Korea made his alveoli shine and reveal their worth in Hell.

“Tsk. This is enough to make you cough?” Lloyd smirked pitifully at Javier.

“Ehem…!” Javier waved his hands. “That is not! Ah-choo!”

“My, my. I thought you said you’re a grandmaster.”

“…”

“And yet, you can’t handle this?”

“Ah-choo…!”

“Tsk. You’re like a weak indoor plant that’s only ever breathed fresh air.”

“…“

Lloyd smirked. Even if one was a grandmaster, it would take time to adjust to breathing polluted air. He broke out in laughter at the discovery of his single point of superiority over Javier. But that wasn’t his only good news.

Ding Dong!

There was a sudden noise of alert, and unexpected messages cascaded in front of Lloyd’s eyes.

[Your title <Top Gun from the West> has been put into effect.]

[You are now in a region with an average annual temperature of 104 degrees Fahrenheit. "Top Gun from the West” has been activated. You will never get dehydrated under any circumstances during your stay here.]

Yay.

Satisfaction danced in Lloyd’s eyes when he read his messages.

That explains why I haven’t felt overly warm since a while ago.

When he first arrived in Hell earlier, he didn’t think it was particularly hot given the volcanoes and magma surrounding him. He didn’t sweat much, either. It all turned out to be thanks to his title.

I made the right choice to build the Qanat for the sultan in the desert. Wow. Never did I expect to use my title like this.

Lloyd had no idea that he’d be coming down to Hell back then. He never even imagined he’d come to a monstrous neighborhood that was 104 Fahrenheit year-round. He merely thought he obtained yet another title and moved on. To him, it just meant he’d be safer in desert areas. But now that he was in Hell, there wasn’t a better title he could have.

“Hey, are you hot?” asked Lloyd.

“I am fine, ah-choo, ehem!”

“But you’re sweating buckets.”

“It’s bearable,” assured Javier.

“Tsk. You’re a weakling.”

“…”

"Seriously, knights these days just don’t have any mental fortitude. Not an ounce of it.” Lloyd shook his head regretfully.

“…”

Lloyd looked inside his inner pocket. “Everyone all right?” he asked.

“Ppo! Bang! Ha! Ggo!” Ppodong, Bangul, Hamang, and Ggoming nodded happily inside his pocket.

“How is the temperature?”

“Ppo! Bang! Ha! Ggo!”

“It’s all right? But Ppodong and Ggoming look a little hot.”

“Ppo! Ggo!”

Like Lloyd observed, Ppodong and Ggoming seemed to be having a tough time as they were covered with fur, unlike Bangul and Hamang whose body was sleek and hairless.

“Then why don’t you guys step outside instead? And Ggoming?”

“Ggoming!” exclaimed Ggoming.

“You take this.”

“Ggomiming!”

He fed a red sunflower seed to him, and he hopped on Ggoming’s back with Javier. As for Ppodong, who was panting from the heat, he got on Lloyd’s shoulder.

“Now, we fly!” ordered Lloyd.

“Ggoming!” exclaimed Ggoming.

Soaring across the sky in Hell, Lloyd was able to look down on the surrounding area. It thankfully allowed him to see the spot he could fly toward.

That must be it.

Beyond the horizon, there stood a strangely shaped volcano. It wasn’t anything like an ordinary mountain. To analogize, it looked like a demonic churro. And that churro-looking mountain was bubbling with lava. At the sight, he suddenly recalled reading a descriptive passage about Hell from the novel.

The novel said that the Castle of Hell, where the King of Hell lives, had an outline of a person writhing in agony. And at the top, the King ruled over every soul that fell in here.

The mountain in the distance perfectly fit that description. And so, there was only one conclusion he could make.

That is the place.

That was where the King of Hell resided. And so, the soul of the ancient dragon Anticus had to be entrapped there.

“Ggoming!” called Lloyd. “Let’s fly there, how about it?”

“Ggomiming! Ming!”

Now that their destination was set, he didn’t have to go on an unnecessary adventure and travel by foot. Thinking so, Lloyd encouraged Ggoming to fly, and the unfamiliar view of Hell sped by beneath them. One hour. Two hours. Three hours. Five hours. And nine hours passed by.

We’ve flown this much, and why does it feel like we’re not getting close?

Lloyd clutched the back of his neck in distress after spending almost half a day flying. They weren’t getting closer to the twisted mountain where the King of Hell was at. No matter how many times Ggoming fluttered his wings, even when Lloyd was sure they were flying toward the mountain and when the ground beneath him sped by, for some strange reason, the mountain wasn’t getting closer. Not at all.

This is nuts.

There had to be a reason. A reason why he couldn’t get close to the mountain. But Lloyd had no clue. Nothing changed even after he rummaged through the novel’s content in his head. He couldn’t recall any mention of a phenomenon like this one. Just when he was feeling a bit hopeless, he spotted something.

Hmm?

Lloyd, who was gazing around in dismay, spotted something within his vision. And he called Javier behind him and said, “Hey, Javier. Your vision is better than mine, right?”

“Yes, probably,” answered Javier casually.

“Then do you see that?” Lloyd pointed to an area on the ground.

Knitting his brows, Javier focused his gaze there. “I see it. It looks like a human.”

“Right? And am I correctly seeing that the person’s skin is a little off?”

“Yes, you are correct. Their skin is bright red like the color of blood. And their head… has a horn on it.”

“A horn?” Lloyd cocked his head as if he hadn’t caught that.

Javier replied, “I am sure of it. There is a horn jutting out of them.”

“Then that means-”

“It is a demon, a demon living in Hell,” Javier finished.

“You’re absolutely right. Bingo. What perfect timing. I just needed to ask for some directions,” said Lloyd.

“Wait, do you plan on approaching the demon?” Javier frowned.

“Of course. I have a grandmaster with me, so why should I be afraid of that demon?”

After giving a nonchalant response, Lloyd told Ggoming to fly down toward the demon in their distant view. Like a driver asking a passerby for directions after circling around in a strange place, so naturally, Lloyd got Ggoming to land near a demon.

“Hey!”

The demon flinched in surprise at the sudden landing of a creature and called. Lloyd then put on a benevolent smile toward it and said, “You better answer me what I’m about to ask, or else you’ll practice filial piety to your parents.”

“…!”

The demon’s eyes burned in rage when it heard a blessing from a complete stranger.
