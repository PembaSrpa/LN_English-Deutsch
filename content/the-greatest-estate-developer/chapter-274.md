# A Shameless Demand (2)

---

“Your Majesty. I ask you to… permit me to pull out your palace from the ground.”

“What…?” Widened eyes. Speechless voice. Lloyd, however, remained calm despite the queen’s reactions. A part of his mind flew back to what happened fifteen days ago.

I, too, was flustered.

It was right after the Kraken’s upset stomach settled down. Lloyd was thrilled about getting his hands on the blueprint for the Aotearoa, the Jewel of Truth, and was about to jump into action until something stopped him.

Then I learned that the key materials for construction are spread out in various parts of the world and have to be collected for the Jewel of Truth to function properly.

The blueprint enlightened Lloyd that tauranga was used to build the stylobates found in Magenta. It was used to build the Palace of Magenta. And that needed to be pulled out.

I abandoned everything there and flew here.

Lloyd recalled treating the Kraken and finding the foundation of the Jewel of Truth, which was thought to be only a legend. This merited him plenty of recognition from the merpeople. He became a star. Everywhere he went, the merpeople cheered, and he was showered with a plethora of gifts. One of them was lifetime access to the Mermaid Kingdom.

In exchange, I had to hand over the jjimjilbang’s operational rights to the queen.

However, Lloyd hardly looked upon it as a loss. In the first place, the jjimjilbang was built to acquire laborers set to be mobilized in the excavation project. But now that he had dug what he wanted, Lloyd was certain that managing its operations, repairs, and maintenance would only slow down his plans. Generously handing over the jjimjilbang amidst the pleasant mood and working on his public image appeared to be more beneficial for him in the future.

After all, Lloyd would need the merpeople’s help once he returned for the reconstruction. He’d have to use the merpeople for labor again anyway. So, earning plenty of brownie points from them while he had the chance sounded like a much better choice. That was why he donated the jjimjilbang without any hesitation, inviting explosive applause from the merpeople society at large.

Plus, I’ve become an honored citizen of the Mermaid Kingdom and earned the right to enter the kingdom without any restrictions for the rest of my life. There’s also the magical necklace that protects me under the deep sea and the VVIP membership to jjimjilbang.

That wasn’t the end of it. A likeability system was opened between him and the merpeople. He was now capable of earning RP by winning their hearts.

Well, I should say that I’ve become friends with the entire community of merpeople.

Thanks to the friendship, Lloyd was promised unreserved assistance in his future reconstruction of the Jewel of Truth. And yet, nobody pressured him to give his hand in rebuilding the demolished city. They assured him he could stay out of it since he was an honored guest.

Anyway, I’ve attained everything I could from there.

Just like that, he left the Mermaid Kingdom and was even escorted out by the queen. Her lightning-fast speed of swimming easily got them from the Arctic Ocean to the sea near Port Cremo, where Lloyd timely reunited with the Feliconia Ship on a nearby shore and paid off his remaining balance for using the ship. Then, he hopped on Ggoming before bidding farewell to the crew and flew all the way to Magenta, where he headed to the palace at once and requested the audience with the queen. Those were the series of events that Lloyd went through for the past fifteen days.

Haah. Tsk. Sure, I made the right call by coming here, but look at how cold her eyes are.

Lloyd felt bitter for some reason. The queen’s eyes fixed on Lloyd were filled with burning intensity. At first, they were flustered, but now, she was shooting a stone-cold look at him with a straight face. But Lloyd understood her.

It’s the same as randomly coming up to the queen and asking if I could take apart the palace.

Will she grab me by the collar? Or punch me in the gut? Lloyd was starting to feel anxiety creeping up in him when she spoke.

“Whew,” sighed Queen Magentano, “You are always like this.” She gave a chuckle. “Truly nasty. How do you always fluster me like this every time? But your request. That was not a joke, wasn’t it?”

“It was not, Your Majesty,” Lloyd answered seriously.

“Do you wish for me to permit you to pull out my palace from the ground?”

“Yes, Your Majesty.” Lloyd didn’t deny it. This was a critical moment, and the timing was perfect. She wanted to know his intentions.

“Not a common house or building but the palace? I suppose your outrageous request is backed by a reason I shall accept?”

“Yes, of course, Your Majesty.”

“Tell me.”

Feeling the cold gaze of the queen, Lloyd replied, “The palace was built using the ancient material called tauranga.”

“So?”

“I wish to have my hands on the material.”

“For what reason do you need it?”

Lloyd paused for a second before speaking. At last, he looked up at the queen with a serious face and said, “Please forgive me for being unable to reveal the purpose in detail. However, I can dare ascertain that you and the royal family shall not be harmed by this.” His answer was solemn and genuine.

Really, I am not trying to harm her or the royal family. It’s rather the opposite. I’m helping them. After all, she’ll be one of the victims of the restoration of destiny.

Just then, the future he glimpsed through the Ending Spoiler came to his mind. The queen’s left arm would become paralyzed. Though it didn’t get cut like in the novel, her arm suffered from paralysis as destiny fell back to its original track, which was just as bad as not having it.

In particular, since she’s a swordmaster, losing one arm is… awful. Nothing could compensate for that.

As such, to prevent the tragedy from happening, he had to attain tauranga, which could only happen if the palace was lifted from the ground. Lloyd bore his heart out to the queen but omitted talking about the restoration of destiny because it’d inevitably lead to a discussion of his identity from South Korea and the novel.

I should be ambiguous about that part while still making sure that she feels my sincerity. I need to create an atmosphere that’ll incline her to trust me.

Lloyd thought so. He believed her. He knew that the queen was way more intelligent than him, who only knew a few more things about the future. He decided to trust her excellent political instinct. And she didn’t disappoint.

“Ha.” She scoffed at last, her smile soon turning bitter. “You really are… a nasty person.”

“Forgive me, Your Majesty.”

“Not at all. I can’t not trust you. Anyway, if you wanted to, there are many ways you could hurt me and the imperial family. You would not even have to go as far as visiting me and making a request like now. How could I not trust your words of assurance?” Queen Magentano spoke, almost in a self-mocking manner. She could only trust him. It was a simple matter.

Lloyd Frontera. He is already powerful enough. Well beyond my sphere of control.

It was really true. The Bone Dragon. Grandmaster Javier. Two of them were loyal to Lloyd. There were more.

The Sand and Steel Orc Tribe.

Queen Magentano remembered the report from her informant. It said that the Frontera family had a blood alliance with the barbarous ore tribe composed of Herculean warriors, which was on her list of potential threats in the eastern wasteland. Tying blood alliance with the orcs meant the Frontera family was part of their family. In other words, the orc tribe coalition across the world would raise their weapons and wage war at the request of the Frontera family.

Bone Dragon. Sir Asrahan. Orc tribe. These three alone allowed the Frontera fiefdom to surpass the power of the royal family already. More than that, if he wanted to, he could establish a new dynasty in a blink of an eye.

Queen Magentano thought that Lloyd wouldn’t have resorted to this complicated method if the latter desired to hurt her or the imperial family. Making the tedious trip down here? Requesting with a cap in hand? None of that was necessary. He just needed to wield his power.

As such, it should be better to trust the intention of his request.

She concluded that giving him full trust was a better way to solidify her relationship with him and politically benefit her and the imperial family. Wrapping up her thoughts, she spoke. “Good. Since I have never doubted your allegiance, I shall continue that pattern this time as well.”

“Words fall short of my gratitude to you, Your Highness,” Lloyd answered.

“Tsk. You and your gratitude,” tutted the queen, recognizing his repetitive expression. “You only visit me when you want something.”

“Forgive me, Your Majesty.”

“And you ask for forgiveness when you are out of things to say.”

Lloyd clamped his mouth shut, feeling exposed.

“But there is no need for the embarrassed look on your face. After all, I heeded to your request of pulling out my perfectly fine palace from the ground. I only ask you to avoid damaging the palace in the process.

"Of course, Your Majesty,” Lloyd promised firmly. “I will carefully carry out the construction so that no unnecessary damage will occur.”

“While the necessary damages will occur, right?” She gave him a sharp look.

Lloyd decided not to respond to her.

“It is all good,” she went on, “I already granted your request, so I can understand that much at least. I shall be announcing to the public that the regular maintenance on the palace will be done with a special method this time.”

“Yes, Your Majesty.” And with that, Lloyd rose and was about to leave when the queen stopped him.

“But first, I believe we have discussed only half of the matter. What do you think?” She looked at him sharply.

“Oh,” said Lloyd, “Do you mean the solution for your worries?”

“Indeed.”

“Do you wish to hear everything in detail right at this moment?” Lloyd pointed his finger to the ground.

“Of course. Continue on.”

“Yes, Your Majesty. If that is the case, may I have permission to whisper it in your ears?”

“Come closer.”

At her permission, Lloyd carefully walked closer to her desk and leaned forward, whispering something very softly into her ears.

“So, the plan is… In order to revive the damage done to the royal family due to the Bone Dragon… A shocking method is called for… Even if officials around you oppose… Everything will be resolved…”

Lloyd spelled out his scheme to the queen, trying to be as detailed as possible. Meanwhile, as she listened, her eyes changed.

“…”

What kind of madman was he? She dazedly stared at him. But not for long. A quick decision was made. “Good. Do as you say.”

“Words fall short of my gratitude to you, Your Majesty.” Lloyd’s confident smile spread around the office.

Fifteen days passed. During these days, the Magentano royal family was busy making preparations for the celebration of the reconstruction of the Termina Garden, which had taken over several months.

“Tsk, tsk. As if this event will change anything,” a foreign ambassador tutted as he arrived at the venue.

“You could not be more right, Hahaha.”

On the night of the ball, carriages carrying diplomatic ambassadors and prominent figures residing in the capital arrived at the garden. The facial expressions of the guests waltzing out of the carriages were subtle. There was a subtle derision on their faces, which was directed to the House of Magentano.

“This is simply between us,” the same ambassador whispered to his aide, “The House of Magentano has been parading their power by increasing their military spending and whatnot, but don’t they seem surprisingly unstable?”

“What do you mean, ambassador?”

“Shh,” ordered the ambassador. “Lower your voice. How long have you been working as my aide?”

“About two years, my lord,” replied the aide.

“Tsk,” he grunted. “That must account for your incomprehension. Listen carefully. If I were Queen Magentano, I would not hold a banquet like this after the incident caused by the bone dragon. I would instead hold a large-scale military parade.”

“A military parade?”

“Yes,” the ambassador nodded, his eyes shining. “What happened during the bone dragon’s commotion? Did you forget how miserably helpless the queen was? Everyone at the banquet clearly saw it. That is why I am saying this. What is the purpose of ramping up military power if she does not plan to use them for an event like this? She ought to boast her strength in front of everyone.”

“Are you saying that she would be able to recover the authority she lost from the previous incident that way?”

“Yes!” The ambassador nodded earnestly. “This banquet will not change anything. And yet, the queen holds the same banquet in the same format as before to reclaim her lost glory. Tsk, tsk. What is the point of possessing great strength? When you cannot even take advantage of it? Do you not agree?”

“Oh, yes. I think so too.”

It wasn’t that the mockery was present only between this ambassador and his aide. Almost every guest present at the banquet was gossiping among themselves about the futility of this event in making things right. It was nothing but another invitation for mockery. They chitchatted and scorned the queen for being ignorant and losing her political sensibilities, most of them doubting and looking down on the House of Magentano for organizing this banquet. Thus, blinded by their arrogance and disdain for the kingdom, they had no clue that deep into the dark woods, the extraordinary and jaw-dropping host of the party was getting ready with Lloyd.

“So,” reminded Lloyd, “You have to do well tonight. Got it?”

Clack! It was the Bone Dragon dressed in a gigantic bow tie and tuxedo. Yongyong clacked in grave determination to entertain the guests.
